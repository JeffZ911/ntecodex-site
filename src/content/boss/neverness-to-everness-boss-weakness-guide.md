![Boss Stats](/img/neverness-to-everness-boss-weakness-guide/inline-1.webp)


The combat system in Neverness to Everness (NTE) revolves around the Lane Combat mechanic, where boss resistances and the state of their Break Meter dictate the flow of battle. Unlike traditional action RPGs, bosses in Hesperia do not just have static HP pools; they possess a secondary "white bar" known as the Break Meter. Reducing this meter to zero triggers a "Broken" state, significantly increasing the damage the boss receives and pausing their attack patterns.

Boss scaling is tied directly to your Appraisal Level. As you progress through the main story and increase your world rank, bosses in high-risk commissions will gain "Anomaly" status. This status grants them increased HP and specific elemental resistances that can only be bypassed by triggering specific reactions like Discord or Shatter.

## Attack Patterns

![Attack Patterns](/img/neverness-to-everness-boss-weakness-guide/inline-2.webp)


Success in Neverness to Everness depends on recognizing visual cues, specifically the "Red Shine." This flash appears on a boss's weapon or limb exactly 0.5 seconds before a parryable attack lands. Executing a "Critical Riposte" during this window deals massive Break damage and often interrupts the boss's current phase.

### Visual Cues for Unblockable vs. Parryable Attacks
*   **Red Shine:** Indicates a Parryable attack. Successful timing triggers a Lane Shift follow-up.
*   **White Flash:** Indicates a standard attack that can be dodged for a "Perfect Dodge" slow-motion effect.
*   **Purple Aura:** Indicates an Unblockable Anomaly attack. You must move out of the highlighted "Fluctuation Zone" immediately.

### Arachne Phase Guide: Puppets to Spotlights
The Never-ending Arachne battle is divided into three distinct phases. In Phase 1, she remains stationary, using her front legs to sweep the arena. At 70% HP, she enters Phase 2, summoning "Puppet" adds that must be cleared to lower her damage reduction. 

Phase 3 (below 40% HP) is the most dangerous. She retreats to the ceiling, and the arena is bathed in moving spotlights. Standing in a spotlight for more than two seconds will freeze your character in place, leaving them vulnerable to her "Web Drop" execution. The movement pattern of these spotlights follows a clockwise rotation, requiring you to constantly shift lanes to stay in the dark zones.

### Headless Rider: Chasing the Phantom Bike
The Headless Rider’s primary threat is his "Phantom Drift." He will circle the player at high speeds, leaving a trail of Incantation fire. If you stay in the center, he will eventually charge with an unblockable bike slam. The strategy here is to use long-range characters like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) to chip away at him until he attempts a "Wheelie Smash," which is a parryable move signaled by a bright Red Shine.

### Swallowtail: Managing Mid-Air Hovering Patterns
Swallowtail is a high-mobility flyer. Its primary pattern involves hovering just out of melee range and firing Scorch-infused feathers. You must wait for its "Dive Bomb" animation. When the boss pulls its wings back and glows bright orange, it is about to strike. A successful dodge here allows for a "Vita Bud" counter, which pulls the boss down to ground level for 10 seconds.

### Nestbound Bird: Melee Strike Timing
The Nestbound Bird is a heavy-hitter that relies on Physical damage. Its attacks are slow but cover a wide area. Its most dangerous move is the "Triple Peck," a three-hit combo where only the final hit is parryable. Many players make the mistake of trying to parry the first hit; instead, dodge the first two and save your stamina for the third hit's Red Shine.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

![Step-by-Step Strategy](/img/neverness-to-everness-boss-weakness-guide/inline-3.webp)


The core of NTE bossing is the "Lane Rotation." Because you can have multiple character phantoms active on the field simultaneously, the goal is to stack elemental applications to trigger Discord as quickly as possible.

### Step-by-Step: The 'Boss Executioner' Rotation Flow
1.  **Initiate with Incantation:** Start with Fadia’s Skill to apply the Incantation mark.
2.  **Apply Chaos:** Switch to Adler and use his "Abyssal Burst" to create a Chaos field. This triggers the "Stain" reaction, dealing initial DoT.
3.  **Trigger Discord:** Switch to Daffodil (your Breaker). Her Psyche-infused heavy attacks will interact with the existing Incantation and Chaos marks to trigger **Discord**.
4.  **The Break Window:** Once the Break Meter is empty, switch to your Main DPS (Nanally or Baicang).
5.  **Ultimate Dump:** Activate all Ultimates during the "Broken" state to capitalize on the 200% damage multiplier.

### Shredding the Break Meter: Reaction Priority
Prioritize the Discord reaction above all else. While Shatter is useful for Physical bosses, Discord provides a flat reduction to all boss resistances. If you are struggling with a boss's defense, ensure your team has at least one Psyche and one Chaos character to facilitate this.

### Mastering the Critical Riposte
The Critical Riposte is not just a defensive move; it is a massive DPS gain. When you see the Red Shine, do not spam the attack button. Wait for the audio cue—a sharp "ping" sound—to press the Parry button. A successful riposte restores 20% of your character's Energy and deals [information unavailable] Break damage.

### How to Contain Anomalies Post-Battle
After the boss's HP reaches zero, an "Anomaly Core" will appear. You must interact with it using your "Appraisal Scanner." Failing to do this within 30 seconds will cause the boss to respawn with 15% HP in a "Frenzy" state. Always keep your scanner keyed to a quick-access slot.

### Survival Tactics: Fadia's Damage Redirection
If you find yourself caught in a boss's ultimate attack, switch to Fadia. Her passive ability, "Mirror of Hesperia," redirects 40% of incoming damage to her phantom, allowing your Main DPS to stay healthy. This is vital for the Headless Rider's "Supernova" phase where the entire arena deals ticking damage.

## Loot

![Loot](/img/neverness-to-everness-boss-weakness-guide/inline-5.webp)


Defeating bosses in Neverness to Everness is the primary way to obtain S-Rank Arcs, which serve as the game's high-tier weapons. These drops are divided into weekly rewards and commission-based rewards.

### S-Rank Arcs: Where to Find Raging Flames and Reality Refuge
*   **Raging Flames (S-Rank Scorch Arc):** Dropped by Swallowtail in the Industrial Zone.
*   **Reality Refuge (S-Rank Psyche Arc):** A rare drop from the Beat King.
*   **The Ichi-daime (Nanally's Signature):** Obtained via the limited-time banner, though upgrade materials are dropped by The Never-ending Arachne.

### Weekly Boss Materials: Passive Upgrade Priority
Weekly bosses like Arachne provide "Character Pixels" and "Anomaly Essences." These are required to level up your character’s Passive Talents beyond Level 6. It is recommended to prioritize upgrading your Main DPS's "Executioner" talent first to increase damage against Broken enemies.

### Anomaly Hunting: High-Risk Commission Rewards
Completing high-risk commissions (Appraisal Level 50+) grants high-tier Modules and Cartridges. These items provide flat stat boosts to your vehicle and character. Look for "Gold-Grade" Modules which offer unique set bonuses, such as the "Hesperia Speedster" set that increases Break damage by 15% after a Perfect Dodge.

### Exclusive Currencies: Fons and Anuweth
*   **Fons:** Earned from defeating bosses in the "Realm of Greed" (Mammon instances). Used to purchase S-Rank Arc shards from the urban black market.
*   **Anuweth:** Obtained from "Beyond the Rails" anomaly challenges. This currency is used to buy rare cosmetics and vehicle skins.

### Weekly Reset Checklist for Boss Rewards
1.  **Clear The Never-ending Arachne (3/3):** Highest priority for Character Pixels.
2.  **Defeat 5 Field Anomalies:** Ensures a steady supply of Mid-tier Modules.
3.  **Complete 2 High-Risk Commissions:** Required for S-Rank Arc upgrade materials.
4.  **Claim Daily Appraisal Rewards:** Provides the "Fons" needed for long-term weapon progression.

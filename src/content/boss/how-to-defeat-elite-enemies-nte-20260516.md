To "Break" an enemy, you must deplete this gauge using a combination of heavy attacks, elemental reactions, and successful counters.

## Attack Patterns

![Attack Patterns](/img/how-to-defeat-elite-enemies-nte-20260516/inline-2.webp)


Success in NTE combat is not just about damage output; it is about reading the visual cues provided by the enemy. Elite enemies use telegraphs that dictate whether you should dodge, parry, or retreat.

### Mastering the Critical Dodge

When an enemy is about to perform a standard heavy attack, a Red Flash Indicator will appear on their weapon or limb. Timing a dodge exactly when this flash occurs triggers a "Critical Dodge." This slows down time for a brief moment and allows your character to perform a Critical Riposte—a high-damage counter-attack that deals massive Break damage. 

For many players, the Critical Dodge is the primary method of maintaining momentum against aggressive bosses like the Headless Rider, whose multi-hit combos can otherwise overwhelm a team's shielding.

### The Art of Parrying

Certain Elite attacks are marked by "Double Red Circles" that appear on the player's UI. These circles will slowly converge. To execute a successful Parry, you must activate your defensive skill or swap characters at the exact moment the two circles overlap. 

A successful Parry does not just negate damage; it causes a massive "Stagger Shockwave" that can deplete up to [information unavailable]% of an Elite's Break Gauge instantly. Failing the timing, however, usually results in a Guard Break, leaving your character vulnerable for several seconds.

### Boss-Specific Gimmicks: Rhythm and Environment

Some bosses introduce unique mechanics that break the standard combat loop. The Beat King is the most prominent example, utilizing "Music Note" patterns. During his "Performance Phase," icons appear on the screen that require specific player inputs to avoid massive area-of-effect (AoE) damage.

**Beat King's 'Music Note' Sequence Types**

*   **Blue Note (Jump):** The boss sends a shockwave along the ground. Jumping over it prevents the "Vibration" debuff.
*   **Red Note (Dodge):** A targeted projectile that must be dodged sideways.
*   **Yellow Note (Basic Attack):** A prompt to hit the boss's summoned speakers to reflect soundwaves back at him.

### Phase Transitions

Upon reaching the 50% HP threshold, most Elite enemies enter a "Frenzy" or "Enraged" state. This transition is usually marked by a massive elemental explosion that pushes players back. During this second phase, Elites unlock enhanced movesets. For example, Mammon will begin summoning "Greed Totems" that heal him over time, while the Headless Rider gains increased movement speed and fire-trail effects on his dashes.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

![Step-by-Step Strategy](/img/how-to-defeat-elite-enemies-nte-20260516/inline-3.webp)


Defeating an Elite is a four-phase process that relies on managing your team's cooldowns and the Esper Cycle meter.

### Phase 1: Setup and Shielding

At the start of the encounter, your priority is survivability. Use characters like Adler or Fadia to establish a defensive perimeter. Adler’s shielding abilities provide a buffer against the initial flurry of attacks, while Fadia can draw aggro to ensure your squishier DPS characters aren't targeted. During this phase, focus on observing the boss's patterns and landing safe hits to build up your Esper Cycle meter.

### Phase 2: The Shred Window

Once your meter is full, transition into the "Shred Window." This is where you trigger the "Discord" reaction. By combining Nova and Scorch elements, you create a Discord effect that significantly reduces the enemy's Break resistance. 

**The 'Discord' Reaction Loop:**
1.  Apply **Nova** status using a support character.
2.  Switch to a **Scorch** character to trigger the explosion.
3.  Follow up with a **Psyche** attack to maximize the resulting stagger.

### Phase 3: The Stagger Window

When the Break Gauge is finally depleted, the boss will slump over. This is the "Stagger Window." Switch to your Main DPS, such as [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte), and unleash her "Underboss" ultimate. Because the boss is immobile, you can land every hit of her high-commitment combo. This is the time to burn all stored cooldowns and Esper energy.

**Optimal Nanally Burst Rotation:**
*   **Step 1:** Deploy Daffodil's "Breaker Field" to lower the enemy's defense cap.
*   **Step 2:** Trigger Nanally's Skill 1 to gap-close.
*   **Step 3:** Activate Nanally's Ultimate ("The Ichi-daime" style).
*   **Step 4:** Finish with a Heavy Attack to trigger any remaining elemental procs.

### Phase 4: Rotation Reset

After [information unavailable] seconds, the boss will recover its Break Gauge and perform a "Wake-up Attack." Do not get greedy. Back off, swap back to your sustain characters (Adler or Edgar), and begin managing the Esper Cycle meter to prepare for the next window.

Elites drop various "Thread" materials used to upgrade Arcs and Esper abilities. High-Risk Anomalies are the only consistent source of Arcane Thread and Magic Thread outside of the premium shop. You will also receive Esper Cores, which are required for character level uncapping.

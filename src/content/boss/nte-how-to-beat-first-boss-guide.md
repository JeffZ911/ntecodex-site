# Neverness to Everness First Boss Guide: How to Defeat Picture Frame and Taygedo

Successfully completing the Prologue in Neverness to Everness (NTE) requires players to defeat two distinct boss encounters: the Picture Frame Anomaly and the formidable Taygedo. While the Picture Frame serves as a mechanical introduction, Taygedo represents the first significant difficulty spike that tests your understanding of character swapping and defensive timing.

## Boss Stats

![Boss Stats](/img/nte-how-to-beat-first-boss-guide/inline-1.webp)


The first boss encounter occurs during Prologue 1 inside the Eibon Antique Shop. The Picture Frame is a "Concept Anomaly," a manifestation of urban legends that haunts the shop. Defeating it transitions the story into Prologue 2, where you face Taygedo, a high-mobility threat that requires a more aggressive approach to the Break system.

### Picture Frame (Tutorial Anomaly)
The Picture Frame is designed to teach you the fundamentals of the "Break" and "Parry" systems. It has relatively low health but high defensive windows. You cannot brute-force this fight; the game forces you to engage with the yellow parry circles and red dodge flashes to deal significant damage. It is stationary for much of the fight, making it a predictable target for learning your starter trio's skill rotations.

### Taygedo (First Major Story Boss)
Taygedo is a vastly different encounter located in the streets near the antique district. Unlike the Picture Frame, Taygedo moves constantly, utilizing dashes and leaping attacks. This boss introduces the "Enrage" mechanic, where its aggression increases once its health drops below 50%. You must manage your Esper Gauge effectively here to ensure you have enough energy for a full burst cycle once Taygedo enters a Staggered state.

### Break Meter (Stagger) Thresholds
Both bosses feature a white "Break Meter" located directly beneath their primary HP bar. Depleting this meter is the only way to deal massive damage.

*   **Picture Frame:** Requires approximately 3 successful Parries or 2 Critical Ripostes to Break.
*   **Taygedo:** Requires sustained elemental pressure. A full rotation of Mint’s "Thunderous Whirlwind Slash" combined with Zero’s support skills is typically needed to deplete the meter twice during the fight.

## Attack Patterns

![Attack Patterns](/img/nte-how-to-beat-first-boss-guide/inline-2.webp)


Understanding the visual language of NTE is the difference between a quick victory and a repetitive defeat. The game uses a color-coded system to signal how you should respond to incoming threats.

### Identifying Red and Yellow Cues
When an enemy prepares an attack, a flash of light will appear on their model. 

*   **Red Flash:** This indicates an "Unblockable" attack. You cannot parry these moves. You must use a Critical Dodge (dash just before impact) to avoid damage. A successful Critical Dodge triggers a brief period of slowed time, allowing you to counter-attack.
*   **Yellow Enclosing Circle:** This indicates a "Parryable" attack. A yellow ring will appear and shrink toward a center point. You must press your Basic Attack or Skill button exactly when the ring overlaps the center point to execute a Parry.

### Phase Transitions and Enrage Timers
The Picture Frame does not have a formal Phase 2, but it will increase the frequency of its "Super Duper Ultra 360" spinning move as its HP lowers. This move covers a wide area around the boss and is best avoided with a backward dash rather than a parry.

Taygedo, however, enters an "Angry" state at 50% HP. During this transition, Taygedo lets out a shockwave that pushes your characters back. In Phase 2, Taygedo’s dash attacks are often followed by a secondary swipe, meaning a single dodge is no longer sufficient. You must perform a double dodge or a dodge followed by a parry to stay safe.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

![Step-by-Step Strategy](/img/nte-how-to-beat-first-boss-guide/inline-3.webp)


The core combat loop in Neverness to Everness follows a specific rhythm: **Dodge/Parry -> Break -> Burst.** Following this sequence is the most efficient way to clear both the Picture Frame and Taygedo.

### Mastering the Break Meter
The Break Meter is your primary objective. While the boss is "Active," they take significantly reduced damage. To deplete the white bar:
1.  **Perform Critical Ripostes:** After a Perfect Dodge, your next Basic Attack becomes a Critical Riposte. This deals massive "Stagger Damage" to the Break Meter.
2.  **Use Break-Heavy Skills:** Mint is your primary "Breaker" in the early game. Her Anima-based attacks are designed to chip away at the meter faster than Zero or Adler.

### Executing Perfect Parries
For the Picture Frame, wait for the yellow circle. Do not spam your attack button. If you click too early, you will take the full hit. If you click too late, you miss the window. The timing is generous, but the "Super Duper Ultra 360" can bait you into dodging when you should be standing your ground for a parry on the follow-up slam.

### Managing the Esper Gauge
The Esper Gauge (located at the bottom of the screen) fills as you deal damage and perform defensive maneuvers. This gauge allows you to swap characters and trigger "Esper Cycles." 

Against Taygedo, do not use your Ultimate abilities immediately. Wait until the Break Meter is fully depleted and the boss is "Staggered" (lying on the ground). During this window, swap through your entire team to trigger the **Blossom** reaction. Blossom occurs when Cosmos and Anima elements interact, creating an explosion that deals [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-24] percentage of the boss's max HP as bonus damage.

**Combat Loop Flowchart:**
*   **Engagement:** Start with Zero to build initial Esper energy.
*   **Defense:** Watch for Yellow/Red cues. Execute Parries to lower the Break Meter.
*   **Break:** Once the meter is empty, the boss is immobilized.
*   **Burst:** Swap to Mint, use "Thunderous Whirlwind Slash," then swap to Adler to consume Karma stacks for a finishing blow.

## Recommended Team

![Recommended Team](/img/nte-how-to-beat-first-boss-guide/inline-4.webp)


The game provides a "Starter Trio" that is specifically tuned to handle these first two bosses. While gacha pulls can provide alternatives, the synergy between Zero, Mint, and Adler is more than enough for the Prologue.

### The Starter Trio Synergy
*   **Zero (Element: Cosmos | Role: Support/Battery):** Zero is the engine of your team. His primary role is to fill the Esper Meter quickly. Use his skills at the start of the fight to ensure your other characters have the energy needed for their more powerful moves.
*   **Mint (Element: Anima | Role: Breaker/DPS):** Mint is the most important character for the Picture Frame and Taygedo fights. Her "Thunderous Whirlwind Slash" deals high multi-hit damage, which is perfect for shredding the Break Meter.
*   **Adler (Element: Incantation | Role: Main DPS):** Adler relies on building "Karma" stacks. As you attack with Zero and Mint, Adler builds energy in the background. Swap to him last during the Staggered window to unleash his high-damage Skill, which consumes all Karma stacks for a massive multiplier.

## Loot

![Loot](/img/nte-how-to-beat-first-boss-guide/inline-5.webp)


Defeating these bosses is the gateway to the mid-game systems of Neverness to Everness. The rewards are a mix of character progression materials and currency for the gacha system.

### Guaranteed Story Rewards
The Picture Frame and Taygedo are part of the main questline, meaning their rewards are fixed for all players.
*   **Hunter EXP:** A large amount of experience to raise your account level, which is required to unlock further story chapters.
*   **Fons:** The primary gold currency used for upgrading Arcs (weapons) and purchasing items in the Helleana shops.
*   **Fabricated Dice:** Usually rewarded after Taygedo, these are used for pulls on the Standard Resonance or limited banners like "The Ichi-daime."

### Unlocking the Anomaly Hunt
Defeating Taygedo marks the end of the combat tutorial phase. Once he is defeated, you unlock the **Anomaly Commissions** system. This allows you to revisit boss-style encounters throughout the city to farm for specific upgrade materials. You will also gain access to the **City Tycoon** system, which allows you to manage businesses in Helleana to generate passive Fons income.

### Resource Management Tips
Do not spend all your Fons on low-level Arcs immediately after the fight. The materials dropped by Taygedo are better saved for 4-star Arcs you might pull from your first 10-pull using the Fabricated Dice. Focus on upgrading Mint’s level first, as her Break potential scales with her base stats, making future Anomaly Hunts much easier.

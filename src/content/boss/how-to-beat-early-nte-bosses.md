# How to Beat Early NTE Bosses: A Complete Strategy Guide

Survival in the early hours of Neverness to Everness (NTE) hinges on your ability to read the battlefield and exploit the "Break" system. While standard enemies might fall to basic combos, bosses like the Picture Frame and the Never-Ending Arachne possess dense shields that require specific elemental interactions to shatter. Understanding the rhythm of the city’s supernatural side is the difference between a successful containment and a costly defeat.

## Boss Stats

![Boss Stats](/img/how-to-beat-early-nte-bosses/inline-1.webp)


The early game introduces three distinct types of threats: tutorial anomalies, commission-based targets, and story milestones. Each boss has a specific elemental profile that dictates which team members will be most effective. In NTE, damage is secondary to the **Stagger Bar (White Bar)**. This meter sits directly beneath the boss's health and represents their posture; once depleted, the boss enters a "Broken" state, taking significantly increased damage for approximately 4–5 seconds before the bar recovers.

### The Picture Frame (Tutorial Boss)
Encountered during the Prologue in the subway and Baihui Alley, the Picture Frame serves as the primary introduction to the Breaking mechanic. As an Object-type anomaly, it uses paint-based projectiles and heavy slams. It is notably weak to **Anima** damage, making characters like Mint or [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) ideal for this encounter. The fight focuses heavily on learning the "Parry" and "Critical Riposte" systems, as the boss frequently broadcasts wide, telegraphed swings.

### Bopp (Anomaly Commission)
Bopp is the target of the "Lightspeed Tagger" Anomaly Commission found south of the Iris Bridge. While Bopp appears early (Level 16), high-risk versions of this fight scale up to Level 39 and beyond. Bopp moves erratically across the arena, often leaving graffiti traps that deal area-of-effect (AoE) damage. Defeating Bopp is essential for unlocking the "Old Skateboard" furniture material for your apartment.

### Never-Ending Arachne (Episode 3 Milestone)
Located at the Ebisu Auction House in the New Herland District, the Never-Ending Arachne is the first major wall for many players. She utilizes a two-phase combat structure involving puppet summons and high-speed slash combos. Despite her Chaos-aligned nature, she is vulnerable to **Chaos** damage, meaning that utilizing a Chaos-heavy team (such as one featuring Daffodil) is the most efficient way to shred her Break meter.

## Attack Patterns

![Attack Patterns](/img/how-to-beat-early-nte-bosses/inline-2.webp)


Recognizing visual cues is vital because many NTE bosses utilize "Red-Glint" attacks. These indicators signal that an incoming strike can either be parried for massive Break damage or dodged to trigger a Critical Riposte.

### Recognizing Red-Glint Indicators
*   **Circular Warning Symbol:** When a red or yellow circular ring appears and begins to shrink, it indicates a Parry window. Timing an attack exactly when the rings overlap will trigger a Parry, dealing the highest possible Break damage.
*   **The Red Flash:** A sharp red glint on the boss’s weapon or limb usually precedes a heavy strike. If you dodge at the exact moment of impact, you trigger a **Critical Riposte**, granting a brief window of invincibility and a powerful counter-attack.

### Boss-Specific Cues Checklist
*   **Picture Frame:**
    *   **Hyper Vortex:** The frame spins rapidly, pulling the player toward the center. Watch for the paint droplets to turn dark before the final explosion.
    *   **Heavy Slam:** The frame stands upright and glows red. This is a primary Parry opportunity.
*   **Bopp:**
    *   **Graffiti Burst:** Bopp stops moving and shakes its body. This precedes a 360-degree AoE burst that leaves slowing paint on the ground.
    *   **Skateboard Dash:** A linear charge signaled by a bright trail. Dodge sideways to trigger a Riposte.
*   **Never-Ending Arachne:**
    *   **Phase 1 Puppets:** The boss remains off-stage while puppets dance. Watch the floor for circular ground pulses; these waves must be jumped over or dodged through.
    *   **Phase 2 Slash Combo:** Arachne performs a 3-to-4 hit slash sequence. Only the final hit typically carries a Red-Glint, so do not attempt to parry the initial swings.

### Elite Anomaly Patterns: Headless Rider and Beat King
Outside of the main story, you will encounter Elite Anomalies like the **Headless Rider** and **Beat King**. The Headless Rider focuses on high-speed vehicular combat patterns, requiring you to dodge linear charges. The Beat King, found in the "Rhythm Master" commission, uses sound-wave pulses that require rhythmic dodging to avoid being stunned.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

![Step-by-Step Strategy](/img/how-to-beat-early-nte-bosses/inline-3.webp)


The core of NTE combat is the **Lane System**. Unlike traditional swap-based RPGs, switching characters in NTE leaves a "phantom" of the previous character on the field for several seconds. This allows their elemental attributes to linger and combine with the incoming character’s attacks, creating powerful reactions.

### Triggering the Discord Reaction
The most effective way to beat early bosses is to trigger the **Discord** reaction. This specific interaction is designed to shred Break bars.
1.  **Apply Incantation:** Use a character like Adler to apply the **Scorch** (DoT) effect.
2.  **Apply Chaos:** Swap to a character like Daffodil to apply Chaos energy.
3.  **Apply Psyche:** Use a character like Haniel or Fadia to trigger the **Nova** (delayed burst) effect.
When Scorch and Nova overlap on a single target, the **Discord** reaction occurs, melting the boss’s Stagger Bar significantly faster than raw damage alone.

### Optimal Boss Combat Rotation
To maximize efficiency, follow this "Trigger -> Anchor -> Finisher" flowchart:
1.  **Primary Trigger:** Start with **Zero** or **Adler** to apply an initial elemental mark (Cosmos or Incantation).
2.  **Anchor:** Swap to a support like **Haniel** to provide ATK buffs and add the Psyche element to the lane.
3.  **Finisher:** Switch to your main DPS (like **Nanally** or **Chiz**) to trigger the final reaction and unleash Ultimates only once the boss enters the **Broken** state.

### Specific Strategy: Defeating the Never-Ending Arachne
To defeat Arachne at the Ebisu Auction House:
*   **Manage the Puppets:** In Phase 1, do not focus on raw damage. Instead, time your dodges against the puppet waves to build your Ultimate meter.
*   **Save the Burst:** Do not use Ultimates when her health bar is grayed out or during her teleportation animations.
*   **The Parry Window:** In Phase 2, wait for her to teleport close to you for her red-glint slash. Successfully parrying this hit will often deplete the remainder of her Break bar instantly.
*   **Execute Threshold:** If you are using **Baicang**, remember that his Ultimate can execute bosses when they fall below 5% HP, bypassing the final sliver of their health.

### Best F2P Team for Beginners
For players starting out, the combination of **Zero (Main Character)**, **Edgar**, and **Haniel** is highly effective. 
*   **Zero** provides reliable Cosmos damage and acts as your primary field presence.
*   **Edgar** is currently the only dedicated A-rank healer, making him essential for players still learning boss patterns.
*   **Haniel** provides a massive 20% ATK buff through her skill and Ultimate, significantly boosting Zero’s damage during Break windows.

### High-Impact 'Discord' Reaction Team
For those who have pulled from the standard S-Class selector, the **Fadia and Daffodil** duo is the current meta for bossing. **Adler** (available for free through story progression) completes this team by providing Incantation shields. This team focuses entirely on triggering Discord to keep the boss in a permanent state of stagger.

### Support Roles: Healing vs. Shielding
While **Edgar** provides direct healing, **Adler** is often preferred for early bossing because his shields scale off **DEF**. This allows you to ignore minor chip damage while maintaining your attack combo, which is vital for building the Esper Cycle meter. **Fadia** offers a middle ground, redirecting team damage to herself and providing high survivability for high-complexity encounters.

## Loot

![Loot](/img/how-to-beat-early-nte-bosses/inline-5.webp)


Defeating bosses is the primary way to progress your characters’ "Esper Abilities" and obtain signature gear.

### Key Boss Drops and Their Uses
*   **Dress Sleeves of Vanity:** Dropped by the Never-Ending Arachne. This S-Rank consumable is required for upgrading the passives of characters like **Haniel** and **[Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte)**.
*   **Tomorrow Paint:** Obtained from Bopp. Used to craft Anomaly Furniture and upgrade the "Old Skateboard" item.
*   **Character Pixels:** This is the primary "stamina" currency for boss rewards. You can spend **60 Character Pixels** up to three times per week on major bosses to receive triple rewards.
*   **Legendary Arcs:** Exploration bosses and high-risk commissions (like the Level 45 Headless Rider) have a chance to drop Legendary Arcs, which function as the primary equipment for enhancing base stats.

### Weekly Boss Reward Structure
The weekly reset is the most important time for progression. You should prioritize your 60-pixel spends on the **Never-Ending Arachne** first, as "Dress Sleeves of Vanity" are the most significant bottleneck for mid-game character ascension. 

### City Tycoon Rewards: Chiz and Her Weapon
By progressing the **City Tycoon** management system, you can unlock **Chiz** for free. Chiz is a unique rhythm-based DPS who manages a resource called "Grain." Her signature weapon can be farmed by defeating the **Beat King** anomaly. This makes her one of the strongest F2P-accessible characters for tackling the later stages of the Episode 3 milestones.

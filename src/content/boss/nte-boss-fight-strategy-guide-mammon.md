## Boss Stats

![Boss Stats](/img/nte-boss-fight-strategy-guide-mammon/inline-1.webp)


Mammon serves as the primary antagonist of the Realm of Greed, an endgame combat mode that players can access through the use of Anomaly Furniture. Specifically, you must interact with the Blind Mammon plushie located in your customized apartment to enter this instance. Unlike standard street-level anomalies found in the open world, Mammon is designed to test the limits of your team synergy and mechanical execution.

The boss features a massive health pool that scales significantly based on the level of your Blind Mammon plushie. At lower levels, the fight serves as a basic gear check, but at Level 10 and beyond, Mammon becomes a battle of attrition. One of the most defining characteristics of this boss is his exceptionally high stagger resistance. Most heavy attacks from standard Espers will fail to interrupt his animations, meaning players cannot simply "un-ga bun-ga" their way through his health bar.

Mammon’s movement speed is deceptive. Despite his hulking frame, he possesses quick recovery frames and can close gaps across the arena in a fraction of a second. His elemental affinity is tied to the Fons/Greed attribute, but he remains highly susceptible to the "Stain" reaction. Triggering Stain requires the combination of Lakshana and Psyche attributes, which significantly lowers Mammon's defense and increases his damage taken.

To unlock Mammon, you must first complete the "Urban Legend: The Golden Plushie" side quest available in the Hethereau business district. Upon completion, you receive the Blind Mammon Anomaly Furniture. Placing this item in your home allows you to "Resonate" with it, transporting your team to the Realm of Greed.

The difficulty is not set by a menu but by the level of the plushie itself. You level up the plushie by feeding it Covetous Coins, which are dropped by Mammon himself. This creates a progression loop where you must defeat weaker versions of the boss to strengthen the furniture and unlock higher reward tiers.

## Attack Patterns

![Attack Patterns](/img/nte-boss-fight-strategy-guide-mammon/inline-2.webp)


Understanding Mammon’s telegraphs is the difference between a successful clear and a total party wipe. His attacks are designed to punish players who panic-dodge or stay too far away.

### Straight-Line Lunge
This is Mammon’s most frequent gap-closer. He will crouch slightly, gold energy swirling around his feet, before launching himself across the entire arena. The hitbox for this move is exceptionally long. If you attempt to dodge backward, the tail end of the animation will likely catch you as your invincibility frames (i-frames) expire. The only reliable way to avoid this is the 45-degree side dodge.

### Ground Slam
Mammon raises both fists high above his head, creating a massive circular AoE (Area of Effect) centered on his position. This attack deals heavy physical damage and can one-shot squishier DPS characters like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) if they are caught in the center. You must either time a Critical Dodge to trigger a slow-motion window or immediately sprint to the outer edge of the arena.

### Deceptive Recovery
This is the "noob-trap" of the Mammon fight. After a Ground Slam or a heavy overhead swing, Mammon appears to stay still for a second. However, he often follows this up with an immediate 360-degree swipe. Players who over-commit to their combo during this supposed "opening" will be knocked back, losing their DPS momentum.

### Stagger Resistance and the Break Meter
Unlike smaller enemies, Mammon will continue his attack chain even while taking heavy damage. He only stops when his white "Break" meter (located under his HP bar) is fully depleted. Until that meter is gone, do not expect your attacks to flinch him.

### Phase 2: Enraged Greed (Below 30% HP)
Once Mammon’s health drops below 30%, he enters an Enraged state. His skin takes on a darker, metallic hue, and his attack frequency increases by approximately [exact value pending official confirmation]. During this phase, he begins chaining his lunges into slams without any recovery window. This is the most dangerous part of the fight and requires players to save their Ultimate abilities to burst him down quickly.

### Attack Priority List
1.  **Parry:** The overhead fist slam can be parried by characters with a counter mechanic (like Adler), dealing massive posture damage.
2.  **Dodge:** The Straight-Line Lunge must be dodged; it cannot be parried by most characters.
3.  **Outrun:** The Ground Slam AoE is best avoided by simply moving out of the circle unless you are confident in your Critical Dodge timing.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

![Step-by-Step Strategy](/img/nte-boss-fight-strategy-guide-mammon/inline-3.webp)


Success against Mammon relies on a specific rhythm: dodge, apply elements, break, and burst.

### Mastering the 45-Degree Dodge
When you see the gold flash indicating a Straight-Line Lunge, do not move away from Mammon. Instead, dodge diagonally toward his hip. Because the lunge moves in a narrow but long rectangle, dodging toward the side and slightly forward puts you behind Mammon’s active hitbox. This positioning allows you to begin your counter-attack while he is still finishing his dash animation.

### Optimizing the Esper Cycle for Maximum DPS
The most effective way to drain Mammon’s HP is to maintain the "Stain" reaction. 
1.  **Start with Lakshana:** Use a character like [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide) to apply the Lakshana status.
2.  **Switch to Psyche:** Swap to your Psyche applicator (such as Haniel) to trigger the Stain reaction.
3.  **Maintain the Debuff:** Stain lasts for a limited duration. You must rotate your characters constantly to keep the defense shred active.

### Breaking the Guard
Characters like Daffodill are essential for this fight because of their high "Impact" stat. Daffodill’s skill set is specifically tuned to deplete the Break meter. Use your DPS characters to chip away at HP, but as soon as Mammon prepares a heavy attack, swap to Daffodill to deal massive posture damage. Once the Break meter is empty, Mammon will enter a "Downed" state for 8 seconds, taking 50% increased damage.

### Handling the Level 99 Justice Executioner Variant
For players who have maxed out their Blind Mammon plushie, a secret variant called the "Justice Executioner" may appear. This version of the boss has an additional mechanic where he summons "Greed Sentinels" that heal him. You must prioritize the Sentinels before returning your focus to the boss, or the fight will become an infinite loop of healing.

## Recommended Team

![Recommended Team](/img/nte-boss-fight-strategy-guide-mammon/inline-4.webp)


Building a team for Mammon requires a balance of elemental synergy and role specialization. You cannot rely on a single character to carry the entire fight.

### The Meta 'Stain' Team (Premium)
This is currently the highest-performing team for clearing the Realm of Greed at Level 10.
*   **Nanally (Main DPS):** Her "Underboss" summon provides consistent off-field pressure. While you are busy dodging lunges, the Underboss continues to deal damage. Equip her with her signature weapon, the Plasma Arc, for maximum burst.
*   **Sakiri (Buffer/Lakshana):** Sakiri provides the necessary Lakshana application while buffing the team’s overall attack speed.
*   **Haniel (Support/Psyche):** Essential for completing the Stain reaction. Her ultimate also provides a small amount of crowd control to slow Mammon’s recovery.
*   **Daffodill (Breaker):** The best character for depleting the Break meter. Her heavy hammer swings ignore a portion of Mammon’s stagger resistance.

### Best F2P Team for Early Greed Farming
If you haven't pulled the premium units, this team can still clear up to Plushie Level 5 with decent gear.
*   **Esper Zero (Main DPS):** Reliable physical damage and easy to play.
*   **Adler (Shielder):** His shield can absorb the chip damage from Mammon’s swipes, making the fight more forgiving.
*   **Edgar (Healer):** Mammon deals significant damage even through shields. Edgar’s consistent healing keeps the team above the 70% HP threshold required for certain passive buffs.
*   **Haniel (Buffer):** Even in F2P teams, Haniel is highly recommended for her Psyche application.

### Arc & Cartridge Recommendations
For Nanally, focus on Arcs that increase "Critical Rate" and "Anomaly Damage." The Plasma Arc is the best-in-slot, but if you don't have it, any S-Rank Arc with a focus on Lakshana scaling will suffice.

## Boss Stats

![Boss Stats](/img/nte-how-to-beat-elite-enemies/inline-1.webp)


Every elite enemy and boss in NTE operates on a dual-bar system. The red bar represents their total Health Points (HP), while the white bar directly beneath it is the Stability Layer, commonly referred to as the Break Gauge. Understanding how these two bars interact is the foundation of high-level combat.

### The Break Gauge Explained

The Stability Layer acts as a massive damage reduction shield. As long as an elite enemy has any white gauge remaining, they possess high poise, meaning your attacks will not stagger them or interrupt their casting animations. Furthermore, elites take significantly reduced damage while their Break Gauge is active. 

Your primary objective in the first half of any encounter is to "Break" the enemy by depleting this white bar. Using heavy attacks, specific Esper skills, and successful parries are the most efficient ways to chip away at this layer. Once the gauge reaches zero, the enemy enters a "Broken" state, loses all damage resistance, and becomes immobile for a short duration.

## Attack Patterns

![Attack Patterns](/img/nte-how-to-beat-elite-enemies/inline-2.webp)


NTE uses a visual telegraph system that rewards players for reactive gameplay. Learning to distinguish between a standard dodge opportunity and a parry window is essential for survival.

### Mastering the Parry Circle

The most important mechanic for beating bosses is the Parry Circle. During certain heavy attacks, a "Double Red Circle" (similar to a rhythm game interface) will appear over the boss. Two concentric circles will close in toward a center point. 

*   **The Red State:** While the circles are red and closing in, do not attack.
*   **The Yellow State:** The moment the outer circle overlaps with the inner circle, it will flash yellow. This is your window to press the basic attack or skill button.
*   **The Result:** A successful parry completely negates incoming damage, deals massive Break damage to the enemy, and instantly refills your Esper Cycle Meter to 100%.

### Identifying Unparryable Fury Attacks

Not every attack can be countered. When an elite enemy glows with a deep, pulsating red aura, they are entering a "Fury" state. These attacks are unparryable and often cover a wide area of effect (AoE). 

Common Fury attack indicators include:
*   **Deep Red Aura:** The enemy's entire model glows red.
*   **Ground Markers:** Large red circles or rectangles appearing on the asphalt.
*   **Sound Cue:** A high-pitched screech or mechanical hum preceding the strike.

Against Fury attacks, you must use a Perfect Dodge. Timing your dodge right before impact triggers a Critical Riposte, allowing you to slow down time (similar to a "Witch Time" mechanic) and land several free hits.

### Learning Boss-Specific Tells

Each boss has a unique "signature" move that can wipe an unprepared team.

*   **Beat King:** This boss uses rhythm-based note patterns. It will summon speakers that pulse with energy. You must destroy the speakers to prevent a city-wide AoE blast that ignores a portion of your defense.
*   **Mammon:** Known for deceptive lunge speeds. Mammon will often pause mid-charge to bait an early dodge before following through with a wide-area ground slam. Wait for the yellow flash of the Parry Circle rather than reacting to the initial movement.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

![Step-by-Step Strategy](/img/nte-how-to-beat-elite-enemies/inline-3.webp)


The optimal combat flow in NTE revolves around the "Build, Break, Burst" cycle. Following this four-phase strategy ensures you maximize your damage output during the limited window when the boss is vulnerable.

### Phase 1: Meter Building

Start the fight by using your characters' basic attacks and E-skills to build your Esper Cycle Meter. Do not use your Ultimates yet, as they deal significantly less damage while the boss's Stability Layer is active. Focus entirely on fishing for parries. A single successful Parry Circle timing is worth more than ten basic attack combos in terms of meter gain.

### Phase 2: Triggering Discord

Once your meter is charged, begin rotating your characters to trigger the Discord reaction. In the current meta, the Discord reaction—achieved by combining Incantation, Chaos, and Psyche elements—is the fastest way to shred a boss's Break Gauge.

**The Optimal Combat Loop:**
1.  **Engage:** Apply Incantation (e.g., Adler's skill).
2.  **React:** Switch to a Chaos character (e.g., Daffodil) to trigger a "Resonance" effect.
3.  **Discord:** Switch to a Psyche character (e.g., Fadia) to finalize the Discord chain. This creates an elemental explosion that deals massive white-bar damage.

### Phase 3: Maximizing the Stagger Window

When the Break Gauge hits zero, the boss will slump over and a "Broken" notification will appear. This window typically lasts only 4 to 5 seconds. This is the only time you should unleash your Ultimates. 

To maximize damage during the stagger:
*   Use any ATK-buffing skills (like Haniel's support field) first.
*   Chain your Ultimates starting with your sub-DPS and ending with your main DPS.
*   If using [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte), ensure her "The Ichi-daime" buffs are active before the boss hits the ground.

### Phase 4: Defensive Rotation During Recovery

After the stagger window ends, the boss will perform a "Recovery Burst," knocking back nearby characters and instantly regenerating its Stability Layer. The boss often enters an aggressive phase immediately after recovering. 

Use defensive skills to survive this transition. Adler’s shield or Fadia’s redirection skill can prevent your squishier DPS characters from being one-shot during the boss's counter-attack. Once the boss settles back into its standard pattern, return to Phase 1.

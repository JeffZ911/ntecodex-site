# Neverness to Everness Weekly Boss Strategy: Defeating the Never-Ending Arachne

The Never-ending Arachne serves as one of the primary hurdles for players looking to advance their characters into the late game of Neverness to Everness. As a "Breached" entity, this boss ignores standard physical stagger, forcing players to rely on elemental reactions and precise parries. Successfully clearing this weekly challenge is the only way to obtain the high-level ascension materials required for the game’s top-tier Hunters.

## Boss Stats

![Boss Stats](/img/nte-weekly-boss-fight-strategy-arachne/inline-1.webp)


The Never-ending Arachne is a High-Risk Anomaly that players will encounter within the New Herland District. Unlike standard street-level anomalies, this boss utilizes a dynamic level scaling system. This means the boss's health pool, attack power, and defense values adjust based on your current Hunter Level, maintaining a steep difficulty curve regardless of when you choose to engage it during your weekly reset.

### Location and Unlock Requirements

To face the Never-ending Arachne, you must first progress through the main storyline of Hesperia. Specifically, you need to complete the Episode 3 story mission titled "Deal?" and reach a minimum Hunter Level of 14. Once these conditions are met, the boss arena becomes accessible in the northern section of the New Herland District. 

While you can enter the arena and practice the fight as many times as you wish for free, claiming the rewards requires 60 Character Pixels. You are limited to 3 reward claims per week, making it essential to maximize the efficiency of every successful run.

### Elemental Weaknesses and Resistances

Arachne is classified as a "Breached" entity. This classification means she possesses a unique defensive layer that makes her nearly immune to stagger from standard Physical attacks. To effectively deplete her Stagger Meter (posture), players must utilize Anima and Psyche Esper reactions.

| Damage Type | Resistance Level | Effectiveness |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Physical | High | Low Stagger / Standard DMG |
| Anima | Low | High Stagger / High DMG |
| Psyche | Low | High Stagger / High DMG |
| Cosmos | Medium | Standard DMG |
| Incantation | Medium | Standard DMG |

The boss is particularly vulnerable to the "Resonance" reaction, which occurs when Anima and Psyche elements interact on the target. This reaction not only deals significant damage but also applies a temporary slow effect, which is vital for managing her high-speed movements in Phase 2.

## Attack Patterns

![Attack Patterns](/img/nte-weekly-boss-fight-strategy-arachne/inline-2.webp)


Understanding the choreography of the Never-ending Arachne is the difference between a clean clear and a team wipe. The fight is split into three distinct phases, each introducing a new environmental hazard or mechanical check.

### Phase 1: Managing the Puppet Waves

During the "Puppet Stage," Arachne herself remains perched high above the arena, shielded and untargetable. Instead of fighting the boss directly, you must deal with her summoned puppets. These puppets do not move traditionally; instead, they remain stationary and emit high-speed circular waves from their feet.

The primary threat here is the density of the waves. If you are hit, you take moderate damage and suffer a brief knockback, which often pushes you into a second wave. The goal in this phase is to use these waves to your advantage by performing "Critical Ripostes"—dodging through the wave at the exact moment of impact to trigger a counter-pulse that damages Arachne’s overhead shield.

### Phase 2: Mastering the Slash Combos

Once the shield is broken, Arachne descends to the floor for "The Duel." Her movement speed increases significantly, and she begins utilizing a variety of melee strikes. Her most common pattern is a 3-4 hit slash combo. The first two hits are fast and horizontal, while the final hit is a heavy overhead slam.

You can identify the overhead slam by a distinct red warning flash on her weapon. This is your cue to either perform a perfect dodge or, if using a character with high parry frames like Andrew, execute a counter. 

**Phase-Specific Threat Levels:**
*   **Puppet Waves:** Low Damage / High Disruption
*   **3-Hit Combo:** Medium Damage / High Speed
*   **Overhead Slam:** High Damage / Guard Break Potential
*   **Dash-Thrust:** Extreme Damage / Massive Stagger Window

### Phase 3: Navigating the Spotlight Mechanic

When Arachne reaches 40% health, the arena transitions into "The Spotlight." The entire stage goes dark, and two beams of light appear. One beam follows your active character, while the other follows Arachne. 

This phase introduces the "Frozen" debuff. If your character stays outside of the player-focused spotlight for more than 3 seconds, they will be instantly Frozen. While Frozen, you cannot move or use skills, leaving you completely defenseless against Arachne’s teleporting strikes. The boss will frequently attempt to dash away, tempting you to leave your spotlight to chase her. You must resist this and wait for her to come to you.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

![Step-by-Step Strategy](/img/nte-weekly-boss-fight-strategy-arachne/inline-3.webp)


Defeating Arachne requires a disciplined approach to cooldown management and positioning.

### How to Force a Stagger Break

To end the fight quickly, you must focus on the Stagger Meter located beneath the boss's health bar. Standard attacks do very little to this bar. To force a Break, you should follow this rotation
1.  Apply the Anima status using Mint’s rapid dual-blade strikes.
2.  Switch to a Psyche-type character to trigger the Resonance reaction.
3.  Look for the overlapping warning rings during Arachne's Dash-Thrust attack. This is a "Parry Window." If you hit your skill button when the rings overlap, you deal massive posture damage, often instantly breaking the boss if her meter is already partially depleted.

### Optimal Ultimate Timing

Do not use your Ultimate abilities as soon as they are available. In Neverness to Everness, bosses take 200% increased damage while in a "Broken" (staggered) state. Save your highest-damaging Ultimates and your Trio reactions for this window. 

When the boss breaks, she will fall to one knee for approximately 8 seconds. This is the only time you should dump all your Esper energy. If you use your Ultimates while she is mobile, you risk her teleporting away mid-animation, wasting your most valuable resources.

### Avoiding the Phase 3 Freeze

In the final phase, stamina management becomes the most important factor. The "Death Circle" AOE is a massive attack that covers roughly 60% of the arena floor. If you have been spamming dodges to stay inside your spotlight, you may find yourself without enough stamina to sprint out of the Death Circle. 

Instead of dodging to move with the spotlight, use standard movement and save your dodge specifically for the red-shine attacks Arachne launches from the darkness. If you see the "Frozen" timer ticking up on your UI, immediately prioritize repositioning over dealing damage.

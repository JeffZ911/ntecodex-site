## Attack Patterns

![Attack Patterns](/img/nte-boss-strategy-guide-list/inline-2.webp)


NTE bosses follow specific behavioral loops. Learning these patterns allows you to transition from reactive dodging to proactive counter-attacking.

### Never-Ending Arachne: Three-Phase Breakdown

The Never-Ending Arachne is a complex encounter that tests your ability to manage multiple targets while tracking a high-mobility boss.

1.  **Phase 1 (100% - 70% HP):** Arachne stays grounded, using her front limbs for wide horizontal sweeps. She frequently fires silk projectiles that apply a "Webbed" debuff, slowing your Esper's movement. Watch for the silk threads appearing on the ground; these indicate where she will leap.
2.  **Phase 2 (70% - 30% HP):** Arachne retreats to the ceiling of the rift, becoming untargetable by melee attacks. She summons three "Lesser Weavers" that must be defeated to bring her back down. During this time, she will rain down venomous ichor.
3.  **Phase 3 (Below 30% HP):** The boss enters a frenzy. She teleports rapidly behind your active character. This phase introduces the "Grand Cocoon" move, a massive AoE that deals lethal damage unless you hide behind the pillars generated by Nova reactions.

### Morphix: Melee vs. Ranged Stances

Morphix is a shapeshifting anomaly that alternates its combat style based on your distance from it.

*   **Melee Mode:** Morphix takes a quadrupedal form, charging at the player with high-impact headbutts. These attacks have a distinct "Red Shine" tell just before impact, providing a perfect window for a Critical Riposte.
*   **Ranged Mode:** Morphix shifts into a stationary turret-like form, firing Remora-infused projectiles. These projectiles track the player, requiring consecutive dodges or the use of a shield Esper like Haniel to negate damage.

### Beat King: Mastering the Rhythm Gimmick

The Beat King encounter in Illusion Town functions differently than other bosses. The arena pulses with a rhythmic beat that matches the background music.

*   **On-Beat Attacks:** The Beat King’s shockwaves always land on the "heavy" beat of the track. If you dodge exactly on the beat, you gain a "Rhythm Buff" that increases your next attack's damage by [information unavailable].
*   **The Drop:** When the music reaches its crescendo, the Beat King performs a screen-wide explosion. To survive, you must stand within the "Safe Zones" that appear as illuminated dance tiles on the floor.

### Nestbound Bird: Aerial Maneuvers

The Nestbound Bird spends roughly 60% of the fight in the air, making it a difficult target for ground-based Espers like [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide).

*   **Dive Bomb:** The bird circles the arena twice before diving. This attack is parryable but requires precise timing.
*   **Feather Barrage:** It launches a volley of Cosmos-aligned feathers. These feathers stay lodged in the ground and explode after 3 seconds.
*   **30% HP Cutscene:** Upon reaching 30% health, the bird triggers a scripted sequence where it attempts to fly away. You must use a grapple mechanic or a long-range Esper ability to bring it down, or the fight resets.

### Parryable vs. Unblockable Boss Tells

*   **Red Shine:** Indicates a Critical Riposte opportunity. Successfully countering this deals massive posture damage.
*   **Blue Aura:** Indicates a grab attack. These cannot be parried and must be avoided using a dash.
*   **Purple Glow:** Indicates an ultimate move. These usually require environmental interaction (like hiding behind cover) to survive.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

![Step-by-Step Strategy](/img/nte-boss-strategy-guide-list/inline-3.webp)


Efficiency in NTE combat is measured by how quickly you can force a boss into a "Broken" state.

### Breaking the Posture Bar

The white bar beneath the boss's health is the Posture Bar (or Break Bar). Once depleted, the boss is stunned for several seconds and takes increased damage. To break a bar quickly:
1.  Initiate with a Chaos-aligned ability (e.g., Daffodill’s skill).
2.  Follow up with Psyche or Incantation attacks to trigger the **Discord** reaction.
3.  Use heavy attacks or "Break" specialized skills to finish the bar.

### Maximizing the Esper Cycle Window

When the boss is "Broken," you enter the Esper Cycle window. This is the time to dump all your Ultimate abilities. The optimal sequence is:
*   Apply a defense shred or elemental resistance debuff (using a Support Esper).
*   Switch to your Main DPS (e.g., Hathor or [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)).
*   Execute a full combo, ensuring you trigger at least two elemental reactions during the stun duration.

### Defensive Maneuvers: Critical Dodge and Riposte

A "Perfect Dodge" (dodging just as an attack lands) slows down time for 2 seconds, allowing for a safe counter-attack. However, the **Critical Riposte** is more valuable for boss fights. When you see the Red Shine, use your Esper's "Counter" skill (usually bound to the Skill key + a directional input). A successful Riposte deals 20% of the boss's total Posture Bar in a single hit.

### Specific Boss Tactics

*   **Serenetti:** This boss creates illusions of itself. The real Serenetti is the only one that casts a shadow on the garden floor. Focus your attacks there to avoid wasting cooldowns on clones.
*   **Headless Rider:** This boss moves too fast for standard combos. Use **Vita Buds** (consumable items or specific Esper skills) to apply a consistent Scorch DoT. This wears down his health even while he is kiting you around the highway.
*   **Black Tome:** Below 50% HP, the Black Tome attempts to apply a "Control-Lock" debuff that freezes your character switching. To avoid this, you must destroy the three floating pages that orbit the boss when they begin to glow purple.

### Anomaly Classification: Understanding R-3707

In the lore of Neverness to Everness, R-Class anomalies are entities that have "breached" from the supernatural plane into the physical urban environment. R-3707 is a sentient, distorted piece of art that uses ink and spatial manipulation to attack. Because it is an R-Class entity, it possesses a high resistance to standard physical attacks unless its Break bar is depleted. During the fight, you will notice a white bar underneath the primary red health bar; this is the Anomaly’s stability. Depleting this bar is the only way to deal significant damage.

### Environmental Hazards: The Baihui Alley Distortion

The fight takes place inside a subway carriage that has been warped by the anomaly’s presence. The "Spatial Distortion" effect means the arena is narrow and linear. This limits your lateral movement, making the boss’s "Gilded Rush" particularly dangerous. You cannot simply run to the side; you must either time a perfect dodge or utilize the parry mechanic. Additionally, the flickering lights of the carriage serve as a subtle cue for the boss’s teleportation—pay attention to the environment, as the screen will often glitch or distort right before the boss moves behind you.

## Attack Patterns

![Attack Patterns](/img/how-to-beat-first-boss-nte/inline-2.webp)


R-3707 relies on a mixture of long-range projectiles and sudden, high-speed lunges. Recognizing the color-coded indicators is the difference between a quick victory and a team wipe. The game uses a visual signaling system: Yellow for parryable attacks and Red for unblockable ones.

### Phase 1: The Subway Ambush

During the first 50% of its health, the Picture Frame Anomaly is relatively passive, focusing on keeping the player at a distance.

*   **Ink Projectiles:** The boss launches globs of black paint that track your movement. These deal minor damage but can stagger your character, leaving you vulnerable to a follow-up rush.
*   **Gilded Rush:** This is the most important attack to learn. The boss will glow with a bright yellow aura and charge across the carriage. This is your primary Parry opportunity.
*   **Canvas Burst:** If you stay in melee range for too long, the boss will pulse with ink, dealing AoE damage in a circular radius.

### Phase 2: The Enraged Canvas

Once the boss’s health drops below 50%, it enters an enraged state. The carriage distortion intensifies, and the boss becomes much more mobile.

*   **Spatial Snap:** The boss teleports instantly. It often disappears and reappears directly behind the player. This is almost always followed by a Red Shine attack.
*   **Red Shine (Unblockable):** When the boss glows red, you cannot parry. You must use a dodge (Shift or right-click) to avoid the hit. These attacks deal massive damage and can knock Mint out of her attack animation.
*   **Frame Trap:** The boss slams into the ground, leaving behind a pool of ink. Stepping on this pool significantly reduces your movement speed and prevents you from sprinting for three seconds.

### Identifying Parry vs. Dodge Indicators

The combat UI provides clear signals for when to act. When the yellow ring overlaps the boss, it is the cue to use a Basic Attack or a Skill to trigger a Parry. Successful parries deal massive "Break" damage to the boss’s white stability bar. Conversely, when the boss flashes red, the "Parry" mechanic is disabled. You must time your dodge to trigger a "Perfect Evasion," which slows down time and allows for a counter-attack.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

![Step-by-Step Strategy](/img/how-to-beat-first-boss-nte/inline-3.webp)


The key to winning this fight is not raw damage, but rather the systematic depletion of the boss’s Break bar. Once the white bar is gone, the boss enters a "Broken" state, taking 50% increased damage and remaining stationary for several seconds.

### Mastering the Break Bar

Focus all your efforts on the white bar. Mint’s Dual Blades are specifically designed for high-frequency hits, which chip away at this bar faster than heavier, slower weapons. Use Mint’s Skill whenever it is off cooldown, but only after you have successfully parried an attack. A parry grants a temporary "Stagger Bonus," making your subsequent hits deal 20% more Break damage.

### Executing the Perfect Esper Cycle

Neverness to Everness uses a "Switch-In" system called Esper Cycles. During the fight, you will notice the portraits of your off-field characters ([Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide) and Esper Zero) pulsing with light. This indicates that their "Entry Ability" is ready.

1.  **Start with Sakiri:** Use her to apply her ATK buff to the team.
2.  **Switch to Mint:** Immediately after Sakiri’s buff, swap to Mint to begin the assault on the Break bar.
3.  **Trigger the Break:** Once the white bar is nearly empty, use Mint’s heavy attack to shatter the boss’s defenses.
4.  **The Burst Window:** Once the boss is "Broken," swap back to Sakiri for a quick buff, then back to Mint to unleash her Ultimate (Burst). This ensures the Ultimate benefits from the 50% Break damage bonus and Sakiri’s ATK increase.

### Managing Spatial Distortions

The Baihui Alley Subway carriage is a cramped space. If the boss uses the "Frame Trap" ink pools, do not try to fight inside them. Lure the boss to the other end of the carriage. Since the boss teleports frequently, it will eventually follow you to a clean area. Saving your stamina is vital; do not spam the dodge button. Only dodge when you see the Red Shine or if you miss the timing for a Yellow Parry.

### Why Mint is Essential for the First Boss

Mint is the cornerstone of the early game meta. Her Anima-attribute Dual Blades allow for a rapid-fire playstyle that excels at building the Break bar. Her Skill, [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-17], allows her to dash through enemies, which is perfect for repositioning when the Picture Frame Anomaly attempts to corner you in the subway carriage.

### Optimizing Sakiri’s Buff Rotation

Sakiri should not spend much time on the field during the R-3707 fight. Her primary purpose is to trigger her "Resonance Buff" upon entry. When you see her portrait glowing, switch to her, use her Skill once, and then immediately switch back to Mint. This ensures that Mint’s high-speed attacks are always empowered by Sakiri’s ATK bonus.

### F2P Team Alternatives

While the trial team is the most efficient way to clear this boss during the story, players who have participated in the "Strange Encounters" banner early might have access to Daffodil. Daffodil is a specialized "Breaker." If you find yourself struggling to deplete the boss’s white bar, replacing Esper Zero with Daffodil can speed up the fight significantly. Daffodil’s heavy attacks deal massive poise damage, though she lacks the mobility that Mint provides for dodging the "Spatial Snap."

Beating R-3707 is the prerequisite for unlocking the "Anomaly Hunt" activity. This allows you to revisit previous boss locations to farm for better gear and ascension materials.

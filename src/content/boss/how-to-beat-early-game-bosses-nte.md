## Attack Patterns

![Attack Patterns](/img/how-to-beat-early-game-bosses-nte/inline-2.webp)


Mastering the telegraphs for these bosses is the difference between a 2-minute clear and a team wipe. Each boss has a specific "Critical Riposte" window where a perfectly timed dodge or counter-attack deals massive Break damage.

### Mammon's Phase 1 vs Phase 2 Patterns
Mammon’s primary threat is the "Deceptive Lunge." The boss will pull back its gilded arm, holding the pose for [exact value pending official confirmation] seconds before striking. Early-game players often dodge too early, getting hit by the active frames of the lunge.

When Mammon reaches 50% HP, it enters Phase 2 and triggers the "Covetous Aura." This ground slam covers a wide radius. You must look for the gold-tinted cracks appearing on the floor; these indicate the safe zones. During this phase, Mammon gains a ground slam that can stun-lock characters with low poise.

### Morphix's Mobility and Ambush Cues
Morphix uses the "Shadow Leap" sequence to disappear into the environment. When the boss vanishes, look for the shimmering distortion near the trees or urban structures of Emerald Hill. Morphix will always reappear behind the character currently on the field. To counter this, wait for the red glint on Morphix’s eyes before dodging sideways—not backward—to stay within melee range for a counter-strike.

### Beat King's Rhythm Game Mechanics
Unlike other anomalies, the Beat King forces a perspective shift. Mid-fight, the camera locks, and music note prompts appear on the screen. 
1.  **Blue Notes:** Single tap to negate damage.
2.  **Yellow Notes:** Hold to charge your Resonance Meter.
3.  **Red Notes:** Must be dodged using the directional input indicated on the note.
Perfectly hitting these notes accelerates the Beat King's Break Gauge, leading to a "Performance Crash" where the boss takes 200% increased damage.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

![Step-by-Step Strategy](/img/how-to-beat-early-game-bosses-nte/inline-3.webp)


Execution in NTE is about the "Break and Burst" rotation. You cannot simply chip away at a boss's HP; you must deplete the Break Gauge to create a window for your high-damage Espers.

### How to Cheese Mammon's Ground Slams
The most effective way to handle Mammon is the "Stain" strategy. By combining Psyche and Lakshana Espers, you trigger a Damage-over-Time (DOT) effect that ticks even while Mammon is performing his long "Covetous Aura" animations.
*   **Step 1:** Apply Psyche status using Hathor.
*   **Step 2:** Switch to a Lakshana-aligned character to trigger the Stain reaction.
*   **Step 3:** While Mammon slams the ground, stay at mid-range and use ranged Incantations to chip the Break Gauge.

### Mastering the Beat King Rhythm Phase
To handle the Beat King’s ranged notes, use the urban terrain in Illusion Town. The concrete pillars and abandoned kiosks can block the "Sonic Wave" projectiles if you position yourself correctly. This allows you to ignore some of the smaller notes and focus on charging your Ultimate for the stagger window.

### Breaking Morphix's Shadow Stealth
Morphix’s "Tail Swipe" is a 360-degree attack that occurs immediately after it exits stealth. The only way to avoid the stun-lock is to maintain a position directly behind its hind legs. If you are caught in the "Tail Swipe," switch to a character with high defense like [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide) to absorb the hit without losing your combo multiplier.

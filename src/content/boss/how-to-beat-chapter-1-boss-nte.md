### Understanding Type 2 Anomalies

Type 2 Anomalies in Neverness to Everness are characterized by their "Structural Armor." Unlike standard street thugs or minor glitches, MANISH possesses a white Break Gauge located directly beneath its health bar. While this gauge is active, the boss takes significantly reduced damage and cannot be staggered by standard attacks. Type 2 entities also tend to have "Phase Transitions" triggered by health thresholds, usually at 60% and 30%, where they gain new projectile types or increased mobility.

### Why Cosmos Damage is Essential

The Cosmos attribute is the natural counter to the "Glitched" state of Type 2 Anomalies. Characters like Chiz or Edgar utilize Cosmos energy to bypass the standard damage mitigation of MANISH’s film-reel armor. When you deal Cosmos damage, you aren't just lowering the boss's HP; you are dealing 50% more damage to the Break Gauge compared to other elements. This allows you to reach the "Stun Window" much faster, which is the only time the boss is fully vulnerable to Ultimates.

Film - MANISH uses a mix of long-range projectile harassment and sudden, high-speed physical lunges. The arena in the Film Orbit Studio is relatively small, which makes its "Film Compression" zones particularly dangerous if you don't manage your positioning.

### Phase 1: Ranged Harassment

During the first 40% of its health pool, MANISH focuses on keeping you at a distance. It will frequently use Flash Film to force you to move, followed by a burst of film-strip projectiles. The key here is not to spam your dash. The Flash Film spikes have a slight delay after the visual indicator appears. If you dash too early, you will be caught by the eruption at the end of your animation. Success in Phase 1 is defined by triggering "Critical Ripostes" off the Flash Film to chip away at the Break Gauge.

### Phase 2: Aggressive Teleportation

Once MANISH drops below 60% HP, it enters a more erratic state. The boss will start using the Vending Machine Teleport more frequently. During this move, it launches heavy projectiles that can stun-lock your character. The "Plunging Attack" also becomes more frequent. This is your biggest opportunity; the plunging attack is the only move with a guaranteed Parry window. If you miss the Parry, you will likely lose at least 30% of your character's health. If you land it, you deal massive Break damage and usually force the boss into a downed state.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

![Step-by-Step Strategy](/img/how-to-beat-chapter-1-boss-nte/inline-3.webp)


Beating MANISH is a test of patience rather than raw damage. If you try to "ungabunga" through its attacks, the boss will simply teleport away or crush you with a vending machine.

### Mastering the Critical Riposte

When MANISH uses the Flash Film spikes, wait for the audio "ping" that occurs just before the spikes emerge. If you dodge at this exact moment, time will slow down slightly—this is a Critical Dodge. Immediately press your Basic Attack button to perform a Critical Riposte. This move deals a fixed percentage of Break Gauge damage regardless of your character's level. Repeating this three or four times is the fastest way to get the boss to its first stun.

### The Parry Window

The Plunging Attack is telegraphed by a red circle that follows your character. Stand still. If you keep running, the circles may desynchronize, making the timing harder to read. Wait for the two circles to overlap and turn bright yellow. The moment the color changes, hit your attack button. You don't need to be facing the boss perfectly; the game's auto-target system will assist the Parry as long as your timing is correct.

### Managing the Break Gauge

Once the white bar under the boss's HP is gone, MANISH will collapse. This is your "Stun Window." You have approximately 8 seconds of free damage.
1.  **Swap to your Main DPS** (e.g., Chiz or Esper Zero).
2.  **Activate your Support buffs** (e.g., [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide)'s damage field).
3.  **Dump all Ultimates.**
4.  **Trigger an Esper Cycle.** If you use a Cosmos skill followed by an Anima skill, you trigger the "Blossom" reaction, dealing a massive burst of area damage that ignores the boss's defense.

### Positioning

Stay near the center of the studio. The edges of the Film Orbit Studio are littered with debris that can snag your character's hitbox during a dash. During the Vending Machine phase, staying closer to the boss actually makes dodging easier, as the projectile spread is narrower near the point of origin.

### Combat Sequence for Maximum Break Efficiency

*   **Step 1:** Bait the Flash Film spikes and perform a Critical Riposte.
*   **Step 2:** Switch to a Cosmos character (Edgar or Chiz) to deal sustained attribute damage.
*   **Step 3:** Watch for the red circles; land the Parry to deplete the remaining 30% of the Break Gauge.
*   **Step 4:** Deploy Sakiri’s ultimate for the damage multiplier.
*   **Step 5:** Spam Chiz’s "Stellar Blade" skill until the boss recovers.

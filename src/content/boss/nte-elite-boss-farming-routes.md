## Boss Stats

![Boss Stats](/img/nte-elite-boss-farming-routes/inline-1.webp)


Elite Bosses in NTE are designed to remain challenging throughout your progression. Unlike standard mobs found in the streets of Hethereau, these entities scale dynamically with your Hunter Level. This means that as you grow stronger, the bosses gain increased HP and Attack, but the quality of the loot they drop improves accordingly. At Hunter Level 40, you can expect these bosses to possess health pools between 450,000 and 600,000 HP.

### Understanding Level Scaling
The scaling system in NTE ensures that farming remains relevant regardless of your current power level. When you enter an Anomaly's fluctuation zone, the boss's level is set relative to your current Hunter Level. This prevents early-game bosses from becoming trivial and ensures that the rewards, such as Beetle Coins and EXP guides, scale to meet your current upgrade costs.

### The Break Meter Mechanics
Every Elite Boss features a secondary bar beneath their health known as the Break Meter. This white bar represents the boss's poise and resistance. By dealing damage—particularly through elemental reactions like Discord—you deplete this meter. Once the bar is empty, the boss enters a "Broken" state, becoming staggered and taking significantly increased damage for a short window. Managing your team's rotation to trigger a Break during your main DPS unit's burst window is the key to sub-five-minute clears.

## Attack Patterns

![Attack Patterns](/img/nte-elite-boss-farming-routes/inline-2.webp)


Success in Elite Boss farming is as much about defense as it is about offense. NTE utilizes a high-action combat system where telegraphs are your primary source of information. Ignoring these cues often leads to a quick team wipe, especially in high-risk Anomaly Hunts.

### Critical Dodge vs. Parry Windows
There are two main defensive mechanics to master:
*   **Critical Dodge:** When an enemy's attack flashes red, timing your dodge perfectly will trigger a "Critical Dodge." This slows down time slightly and allows your character to perform a powerful counter-attack, often restoring a portion of your stamina or energy.
*   **Parry Windows:** Certain heavy attacks are preceded by closing red circles. If you use a skill or a heavy attack just as the circles overlap, you perform a Parry. This deals massive damage to the boss's Break Meter and can interrupt their entire combo chain.

### Boss-Specific Signature Moves
Each boss has a unique "gimmick" that can disrupt your farming rhythm if you are unprepared.

*   **Headless Rider:** This boss is unique because the fight often begins as a high-speed chase. He utilizes a spectral scythe for wide-reaching 3-hit combos. His most dangerous move is the "Spectral Charge," where he disappears and reappears for a high-speed strike. You must be in a vehicle to initiate this encounter at the correct time.
*   **Beat King:** This boss turns the battlefield into a rhythm game. He emits AoE sound waves that pulse in time with the background music. During his "Rhythm Slam," you must time your parries to the final beat of the musical phrase to avoid massive physical damage.
*   **Sea Prisoner:** Primarily found in the Nautili Tunnel, this whale-like entity uses the environment to its advantage. It will dive "underwater" (into the ground) and re-emerge with a massive slam. Watch for the bubble traps; if you are caught, you are immobilized for several seconds, leaving you vulnerable to a follow-up dash.
*   **Nestbound Bird:** This aerial boss spends a significant amount of time out of reach. It fires feather projectiles in a fan pattern and will occasionally telegraph a "Talon Strike." This dive attack has a long wind-up but can one-shot lower-HP characters like [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide) or Daffodil.

### Checklist: Common Visual and Audio Cues
*   **Red Flash:** Immediate Critical Dodge window.
*   **Closing Red Circles:** Parry opportunity.
*   **Blue Lock-on Line:** Indicates a high-speed charge is targeting you (common with Headless Rider).
*   **Rhythmic Thumping:** Indicates the Beat King is about to launch an AoE sound wave.
*   **Ground Ripples:** Signal that the Sea Prisoner or Serenetti is about to emerge from below.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

![Step-by-Step Strategy](/img/nte-elite-boss-farming-routes/inline-3.webp)


To maximize efficiency, you should follow a route that minimizes teleportation and utilizes the game's time system to spawn time-gated bosses.

### Route 1: The Urban Midnight Run
Start your run in the **Bridge Crossings** district.
1.  **Time Setting:** Use the in-game clock to set the time to 00:00 (Midnight).
2.  **Headless Rider Spawn:** Travel to the stretch of road between Davidia Avenue and Fox Window Street while inside a four-wheeled vehicle. The Headless Rider will spawn behind you.
3.  **The Chase:** Follow the Rider into the tunnel to transition into the boss arena.
4.  **Beat King Transition:** Immediately after defeating the Rider, teleport to the **Fiscus Avenue** waypoint in Illusion Town. The Beat King is located in the nearby park. Ensure you have the "Rhythm Master" commission active to trigger the spawn.

### Route 2: The High-Rise & Coastal Loop
After clearing the urban bosses, head toward the more vertical and coastal regions.
1.  **Miguel District:** Teleport to the central Miguel District tower. Use the elevator to reach the 4th floor to challenge the **Black Tome**. 
2.  **Serenetti Outskirts:** Exit the tower and head toward the outskirts of the district to reach **Serenetti Manor** in the Unheard Shores area. You will find Serenetti in the greenhouse.
3.  **New Herland Finale:** Teleport to the **Nautili Tunnel** entrance. Drive through to trigger the **Sea Prisoner** (Deep Blue Sorrow Anomaly). 
4.  **Watchtower Climb:** Finally, head to the **Pink Paws bank** in the New Herland District. Climb to the rooftop or use a glider from a nearby skyscraper to reach the **Nestbound Bird**.

### Optimizing Travel Time with Vehicles
While teleportation is useful, some anomalies only trigger if you are driving. Always keep a high-performance car like the Novis ST-X 950 in your quick-summon slot. Using a car is mandatory for the Headless Rider and Sea Prisoner triggers, as motorcycles or two-wheelers will not activate the chase sequences.

## Recommended Team

![Recommended Team](/img/nte-elite-boss-farming-routes/inline-4.webp)


The current meta for Elite Boss farming revolves around the "Discord" reaction. This reaction is specifically designed to shred the Break Meter of elite enemies, allowing you to bypass their high HP pools by keeping them in a constant state of stagger.

### Top Tier: The Discord Execution Comp
*   **Main DPS: Baicang (Incantation).** Baicang is the premier boss-killer in NTE. His kit revolves around "Power Words" (Bless, Silence, Objurgate) that he detonates during his Ultimate, Judgment of Autumn.
*   **Buffer/CC: Sakiri (Incantation/Support).** Sakiri provides essential buffs to Incantation damage and helps gather smaller adds if the boss summons them.
*   **Support: Haniel (Psyche).** Haniel is the backbone of the team, providing healing and applying the Psyche element required for the Discord reaction.
*   **Reaction Enabler: Daffodil (Chaos).** Daffodil is essential for triggering the complex "Trio" reactions. Her Chaos application interacts with both Baicang and Haniel to create constant Scorch and Nova procs.

**Why it works:** When you apply Scorch (Incantation + Chaos) and Nova (Psyche + Chaos) to the same target, you trigger **Discord**. This reaction instantly deducts a large percentage of the boss's Break Meter.

### Budget Friendly: F2P Anomaly Hunters
If you haven't pulled the S-Rank units yet, this team can still clear all Elite content:
*   **DPS: Zero (Cosmos).** The protagonist has an "Instant Cycle" mechanic on their skill that allows for rapid character swapping.
*   **Healer: Edgar (Cosmos).** Provides reliable healing and helps maintain the Cosmos resonance.
*   **CC: Sakiri (Incantation).** (Available via the Standard Board selector or early pulls).
*   **Sub-DPS: Skia (Anima).** Useful for triggering Blossom reactions with Zero to deal consistent AoE damage.

### Key Arc Synergies
*   **Raging Flames (S-Rank):** Best for Baicang or any Plasma-based attacker. It increases ATK and provides a blue flame visual effect for your motorcycle.
*   **Good Boy's Grand Adventure (A-Rank):** An excellent support Arc for Sakiri that increases the team's Break Intensity.
*   **Song of the Whale (S-Rank):** Ideal for Anima characters like Jiuyuan or [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte), providing HP recovery upon breaking an enemy.

### Team Rotation for Maximum Break Meter Shredding
1.  **Start with Haniel:** Use her Support Skill to apply Psyche.
2.  **Swap to Daffodil:** Use her skill to apply Chaos, triggering the **Nova** reaction.
3.  **Swap to Sakiri:** Use her burst to gather and buff.
4.  **Finish with Baicang:** Use his skill to apply Incantation. This interacts with Daffodil’s Chaos to trigger **Scorch**. Since the boss already has Nova/Psyche/Chaos, the overlap triggers **Discord**, instantly breaking the boss.
5.  **Unleash Ultimate:** Once the boss is Broken, use Baicang’s Ultimate to execute.

### Daily vs. Weekly Reward Caps
While you can fight these bosses as often as you like for practice, "containing" the anomaly to receive rewards requires **Stamina** (or Fons). Most Elite Bosses cost 40-60 Stamina per claim. Weekly bosses, which are higher-difficulty versions of these Elites found in the "Beyond the Rails" mode, have a limit of 3 reward claims per week but offer significantly higher chances for S-Rank Modules and Annulith.

The first major hurdle in Neverness to Everness (NTE) arrives during Episode 0: "Unforeseen Yet Foretold." As you navigate the neon-soaked streets of Heta City, you are introduced to the reality-bending threats known as Anomalies. The first true boss encounter is Anomaly R-3707, widely referred to by the community as the "Picture Frame" due to its ornate, gilded appearance and central void. While this serves as a tutorial for boss mechanics, underestimating the Picture Frame can lead to a quick defeat for unprepared Espers.

### Understanding the Break Mechanic

Below the boss's primary HP bar is a secondary white meter known as the Break bar (or Stagger bar). Until this bar is depleted, Anomaly R-3707 takes significantly reduced damage from all sources. Depleting this bar is your primary objective during the first half of the fight.

The Break bar is most effectively reduced by:
1.  **Perfect Dodges:** Successfully dodging an attack at the last moment.
2.  **Elemental Resonance:** Swapping characters to trigger reaction effects.
3.  **Heavy Attacks:** Specific skills labeled with high "Stagger" values.

Once the bar reaches zero, the boss enters a "Nullification" state. It will fall to the ground and remain motionless for several seconds, during which all damage dealt to it is increased by a massive multiplier.

## Attack Patterns

![Attack Patterns](/img/how-to-beat-first-boss-picture-frame-nte/inline-2.webp)


The Picture Frame relies on a mix of wide-reaching physical strikes and projectile-based anomaly energy. Learning the visual cues for these moves is the difference between a smooth victory and a frustrating retry.

### Phase 1: The Static Guardian

During the first 50% of its health, the Picture Frame is relatively slow. It focuses on teaching the player how to time their dashes.

*   **Gilded Charge:** The boss’s frame begins to glow with a sharp red light. After a 1.5-second delay, it lunges forward in a straight line. To counter this, dash to the left or right just as the boss starts to move.
*   **Glass Shard Volley:** The boss hovers slightly higher and begins to vibrate. It then fires a cone of jagged glass shards toward the player. This attack has a long range but a narrow spread. Staying close to the boss and circling behind it is the most effective way to avoid damage.
*   **Anomaly Pulse:** A circular ripple appears on the ground beneath the boss. After a short delay, it releases a shockwave that knocks players back. This move is designed to prevent you from "face-tanking" the boss.

### Phase 2: Enraged Anomaly

Once Anomaly R-3707 falls below 50% HP, it enters an enraged state. Its frame cracks, and the void in the center glows more intensely.

*   **Super Duper Ultra 360:** This is the boss's most dangerous move. It begins spinning its frame rapidly while moving toward the player. The radius is wide, and getting caught in it can deplete a character's health in seconds. You must move away immediately when you see the boss tilt its frame horizontally.
*   **Fractured Reality:** The boss summons smaller, non-targetable picture frames that fire lasers from the periphery of the arena. You must keep your camera active to track these secondary threats while focusing on the main boss.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

![Step-by-Step Strategy](/img/how-to-beat-first-boss-picture-frame-nte/inline-3.webp)


Beating the Picture Frame requires a disciplined approach to combat. You cannot simply overpower it with raw stats at this stage of the game.

### Mastering the Perfect Dodge

The "Critical Riposte" is the most important skill to learn for this fight. When you see the red flash on the boss's frame, wait for the split second before the hit connects to press the dash button. If timed correctly, the world will slow down, and your character will gain a temporary attack speed buff.

During this slow-motion window, your attacks deal increased Stagger damage. Aim to trigger at least three Critical Ripostes during Phase 1 to quickly bring the boss to its first Break state.

### Maximizing Damage During the Break Window

Once the Break bar is empty, the "Nullification" window begins. This is when you must unleash your full rotation.

1.  **Switch to Koko:** Use her support skill to apply any available buffs or shields to the party.
2.  **Switch to Mint:** Immediately use Mint’s Burst (Ultimate) ability. Her high-frequency strikes capitalize on the boss's downed state.
3.  **Elemental Synergy:** If you have built up enough energy, perform an "Elemental Switch" mid-combo. Swapping between characters during the Break window triggers a Resonance explosion that deals a flat percentage of the boss's health.

**Optimal Combat Flow:**
Dodge Attack -> Deplete Break Bar -> Switch to Support (Buff) -> Switch to DPS (Burst) -> Repeat.

## Recommended Team

![Recommended Team](/img/how-to-beat-first-boss-picture-frame-nte/inline-4.webp)


For the first encounter in Episode 0, your team is largely predetermined by the story, but understanding how these characters interact is vital for success.

### The Tutorial Duo: Mint & Koko

*   **Mint (Main DPS):** Mint is your primary source of damage. Her Arc (weapon) allows for rapid strikes that are perfect for building up the Stagger meter. Her mobility is her greatest asset against the Picture Frame’s Gilded Charge. Focus on using her standard attack string followed by her "Cosmic Slash" skill whenever the boss finishes an attack animation.
*   **Koko (Support/Sub-DPS):** Koko provides the necessary survivability for the fight. Her primary role is to deploy shields that can absorb the impact of the Glass Shard Volley if you fail to dodge. Furthermore, Koko’s elemental affinity interacts with Mint’s to create "Anima Resonance," which speeds up the depletion of the Break bar.

### Early-Game F2P Alternatives

If you are revisiting this boss in a challenge mode or later side quest, you might have access to other characters.

*   **[Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte):** If you were lucky enough to pull Hotori early, her "Temporal Sovereign" abilities are a hard counter to the Picture Frame. She can effectively freeze the boss in place, making the Super Duper Ultra 360 move completely harmless.
*   **[Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte):** Using Nanally allows for a ranged approach. By staying at a distance, you can ignore the Anomaly Pulse and focus entirely on dodging the Glass Shard Volley.

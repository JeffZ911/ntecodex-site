## Attack Patterns

![Attack Patterns](/img/how-to-beat-first-boss-neverness-to-everness-20260523/inline-2.webp)


The Picture Frame fight is divided into three phases, each introducing a new set of lethal mechanics. The boss utilizes puppets and environmental manipulation to keep the player at a distance.

### Phase 1: The Puppet Dance

During Phase 1, the Picture Frame remains stationary in the center of the arena while summoning "Gilded Puppets" to attack.

*   **Ripple Pulse:** The boss emits a blue circular shockwave from its base. You must time your jump or dash to avoid the pulse, as getting hit results in a "Heavy" stagger that leaves you open to puppet follow-ups.
*   **Puppet Spin:** One or more puppets will charge toward the player while spinning. A red glint will appear on the puppets just before the impact. This is your cue to perform a Critical Riposte. Success will instantly destroy the puppet and deal massive Stagger damage to the main boss.

### Phase 2: Melee Aggression

Once the boss reaches 70% HP, it enters Phase 2. The frame itself begins to move, floating across the arena to engage the player directly.

*   **Gilded Slashes:** The boss performs a 3-to-4 hit melee combo using the sharp edges of the frame. Unlike the Puppet Spin, only the final hit of this combo has a red shine. You can parry the initial hits, but they require precise timing without the visual aid.
*   **Frame Slam:** The boss rises high into the air and slams down. The impact creates a localized explosion of Anima energy. Dash backward twice to exit the impact radius.

### Phase 3: The Spotlight Mechanic

At 30% HP, the arena darkens, and the boss enters its most dangerous state.

*   **Ethereal Spotlight:** Two spotlights appear on the arena floor. One follows the boss, and one follows the player. You must stay within the player-focused spotlight. If you step into the darkness for more than 3 seconds, you will receive the "Frozen" debuff, locking your character in place.
*   **Quantum Drift:** The boss teleports rapidly around the arena, leaving behind afterimages. It concludes this sequence with a heavy overhead slam that can one-shot lower-level characters.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

![Step-by-Step Strategy](/img/how-to-beat-first-boss-neverness-to-everness-20260523/inline-3.webp)


Defeating the Picture Frame requires a balance between aggressive DPS and mechanical patience. Do not try to rush the fight, as the boss's high defense makes raw damage inefficient until you achieve a "Break" state.

### Managing the Stagger Bar

Your primary goal in Phases 1 and 2 is to deplete the white bar located beneath the boss's HP. This is the Stagger Bar. Using Zero's (MC) Anima skills is the most effective way to build the Esper Cycle meter while chipping away at this bar. Once the meter is full, wait for the boss to finish an attack animation before unleashing your Esper Cycle to force the "Break" state.

### Mastering the Critical Riposte

The Critical Riposte is the most important mechanic for the first boss. When you see the red glint during the Puppet Spin or the final hit of Gilded Slashes, press the attack button exactly as the hit connects. A successful Riposte triggers a slow-motion window where your team deals 200% increased Stagger damage. This is the safest way to transition the boss between phases.

### Surviving the Phase 3 Darkness

Positioning is everything in Phase 3. The boss will attempt to lure you out of your spotlight by teleporting to the far edges of the arena. Resist the urge to chase it. Wait for the boss to teleport back to you. If you are forced to move, use characters with high mobility like Daffodill (if available) to traverse the dark zones quickly.

**Pre-fight Checklist:**
*   Ensure Edgar is at least Level 8 to unlock his primary healing skill.
*   Equip the "Reality Refuge" Arc on Chiz for increased Cosmos damage.
*   Consume a "Heta Energy Drink" (Consumable) for a 5% boost to Esper Cycle regeneration.

## Recommended Team

The best approach for the Picture Frame is a team that focuses on Cosmos damage and sustainability. Since this is an early-game fight, most players will rely on the F2P (Free-to-Play) roster.

### The Best F2P Starter Comp

*   **Zero (Sub-DPS / Anima):** Zero is essential for generating Esper Cycle energy. Use her skill to clear out the puppets in Phase 1 quickly.
*   **Haniel (Support / Lakshana):** Haniel provides the elemental base for the Remora reaction. Her ultimate buffs the team's attack power, which should be saved for the boss's "Break" window.
*   **Edgar (Healer / Cosmos):** Edgar is the safety net. His Cosmos-aligned healing skill not only restores HP but also contributes to the Stagger Bar depletion. Use his skill during the transition between Phase 2 and 3.
*   **Chiz (Main DPS / Cosmos):** Chiz is your primary damage dealer. Once the boss is in the "Break" state, swap to Chiz and unleash her full combo to capitalize on the Cosmos weakness.

### High-Tier Alternatives for Faster Clears

If you were lucky during your initial pulls on the starter banner, you can optimize this team:

*   **[Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) (S-Rank):** Replace Chiz with Nanally. Nanally’s follow-up damage is significantly higher and can bypass some of the boss's Phase 2 defense.
*   **Daffodill (Chaos):** If you have Daffodill, her "Phantom Step" mechanic makes the Phase 3 Quantum Drift much easier to handle. Her Chaos element is also highly effective against the Picture Frame's Anomaly type.

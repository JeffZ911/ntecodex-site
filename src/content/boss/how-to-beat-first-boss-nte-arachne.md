### How to Unlock the Never-Ending Arachne

To face the Never-Ending Arachne, you must progress through the main storyline until you reach Episode 3, titled "Deal? deal." This chapter involves an undercover operation at the Ebisu Auction House in the New Herland District. After the story encounter is resolved, the boss becomes a permanent fixture in the bottom right side of the New Herland District map. You can then fast-travel directly to the Ebisu Auction House entrance to initiate the fight for weekly rewards.

### Weekly Reward Limits and Reset Times

As a Weekly Boss, the Never-Ending Arachne follows the standard NTE server reset schedule. You can spend 60 Character Pixels to claim rewards up to three times per week. It is highly recommended to save these attempts until the end of your weekly play session, as higher Anomaly Ranks (unlocked by increasing your Hunter Level) significantly improve the quality and quantity of the drops.

## Attack Patterns

![Attack Patterns](/img/how-to-beat-first-boss-nte-arachne/inline-2.webp)


The Never-Ending Arachne fight is divided into three distinct phases, each requiring a different tactical approach. While the first phase is largely about crowd control and positioning, the final phase introduces environmental hazards that can result in an instant freeze if ignored.

### Phase 1: The Puppet Master's Stage

In this opening phase, the Arachne herself remains on an elevated stage, out of reach of your melee attacks. She acts as a conductor for a troupe of puppets that occupy the arena floor.

*   **Circular Waves:** The dancing puppets periodically stomp or strike the ground, sending out high-speed circular shockwaves. These waves move outward from the center of the arena. You must time your dashes to pass through these waves.
*   **Spinning Puppet:** Watch for a specific red warning flash on a female puppet. She will begin to spin toward the player like a top. This move tracks your movement, so a single dodge is often insufficient; you must continue moving laterally until the animation ends.
*   **Critical Riposte Opportunity:** Successfully dodging the shockwaves near a puppet allows you to trigger a Critical Riposte. This is the primary way to deal damage to the boss's "proxy" health bar in this phase.

### Phase 2: Close Quarters Combat

Once the puppets are cleared, the Arachne descends into the arena. This phase focuses on traditional hack-and-slash combat and introduces the Break Bar mechanic.

*   **Multi-Hit Slash Combo:** The boss performs a sequence of three to four slashes. A critical detail here is that the game does not show a red warning flash for every hit in the combo. If you see the first swing, expect the subsequent strikes and prepare to dodge or parry.
*   **Lock-On Lasers:** The boss fires lasers along the ground while simultaneously emitting shockwaves. This attack covers a large area and is best avoided by jumping or staying at mid-range.
*   **Parryable Attacks:** Periodically, the boss will telegraph a heavy attack with a distinct yellow or gold shine. This is your cue to use a Parry. A successful parry will not only negate the damage but also deal massive "Break" damage to her stability bar.

### Phase 3: The Spotlight Finale

When the boss drops to approximately 30% to 40% of her maximum HP, the arena darkens, and the third phase begins. This phase is characterized by restricted movement and high-frequency teleportation.

*   **The Spotlight Mechanic:** Two spotlights will appear on the floor—one following the boss and one following the player. You must remain within your designated spotlight. If you stay in the darkness for more than a few seconds, your character will be frozen in place, leaving you vulnerable to the boss's follow-up attacks.
*   **Teleporting Strikes:** The Arachne will teleport around the arena, often appearing just outside your spotlight to fire projectiles or initiate a dash attack.
*   **Final Parry:** Toward the end of this phase, the boss will usually attempt one final, heavily telegraphed parryable attack. Landing this parry is typically the killing blow, as it completely depletes her remaining Break Bar.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

![Step-by-Step Strategy](/img/how-to-beat-first-boss-nte-arachne/inline-3.webp)


Beating the Never-Ending Arachne requires a balance of aggressive damage and disciplined stamina management. Because the fight can be lengthy at higher Anomaly Ranks, efficiency is key.

### Maximizing Stagger Damage

The fastest way to end the fight is to trigger a Stagger state by depleting the boss's Break Bar. In Phase 2, prioritize parrying over dodging. While a "Perfect Dodge" provides a brief window for a Critical Riposte, a "Perfect Parry" deals significantly more damage to the Break Bar. Characters with high "Solid-type" damage or those who specialize in Chaos-type attacks (like Daffodill) are particularly effective at this.

### Dodging the Never-Ending Waves

Stamina management is the most common cause of failure in Phase 1. Many players panic and spam the dodge button when multiple puppets spawn shockwaves simultaneously. Instead, watch the rhythm of the music and the puppets' movements. Time your dash to coincide with the wave's contact. This ensures you have enough stamina left to follow up with a Critical Riposte and clear the puppets quickly.

### Managing Character Pixels

Since the boss costs 60 Character Pixels to claim rewards, you should only spend them if you actually need the Dress Sleeves of Vanity for your current team. If you are early in the game and haven't unlocked the ability to use these materials yet, it may be more beneficial to spend your Pixels on character EXP or Ascension resources until you reach the appropriate Hunter Level.

## Recommended Team

![Recommended Team](/img/how-to-beat-first-boss-nte-arachne/inline-4.webp)


Your team composition should focus on exploiting the boss's Chaos weakness while maintaining enough utility to handle the multi-hit combos of Phase 2.

### Best F2P Team Composition

*   **Zero (S-Rank/Cosmos):** As the protagonist, Zero provides reliable damage and is likely your most leveled character during the first encounter. Their Solid-type damage is essential for chipping away at the Break Bar.
*   **Mint (A-Rank):** Mint is a story-given unit that offers excellent synergy. Her ability to trigger Esper Cycles helps maintain damage during the boss's teleportation phases.
*   **Adler (A-Rank):** Adler excels at maintaining pressure. When paired with Zero, Adler can trigger frequent "Blossom" or "Nova" effects, which are useful for clearing the puppets in Phase 1.

### Why Hathor is the MVP for Arachne

If you have pulled **Hathor (S-Rank)**, she is the definitive counter to the Never-Ending Arachne. Hathor's kit revolves around gaining power stacks when she is targeted by multi-hit attacks. Because the Arachne's Phase 2 combo consists of 4 rapid hits, Hathor can reach her maximum damage potential almost instantly. Additionally, her signature Arc, "Raging Flames," increases her Redirect Skill and Ultimate damage, allowing her to punish the boss heavily after a successful parry.

### Alternative: Nanally (S-Rank)

For players who pulled on the limited banner, **[Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)** is a powerhouse. Her scaling is high enough that she can often bypass the boss's defensive mechanics through raw DPS. While she doesn't exploit the Chaos weakness as effectively as Daffodill, her ability to maintain "Infinite" state damage makes her a viable primary DPS for this encounter.

## Loot

![Loot](/img/how-to-beat-first-boss-nte-arachne/inline-5.webp)


Defeating the Never-Ending Arachne is the primary method for obtaining high-level upgrade materials in the early-to-mid game.

*   **Dress Sleeves of Vanity:** This is the "Gold" drop from the boss. It is a mandatory material for upgrading the passive skills and ultimate abilities of S-Rank characters like Haniel and [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte).
*   **Beetle Coins:** The primary currency used in the Hethereau district for purchasing items at the various urban simulation shops.
*   **Magic Thread / Arcane Thread:** Used for crafting and upgrading Arcs (weapons).
*   **Fabricated Dice:** These are occasionally dropped and are used in the "City Tycoon" gacha board system to earn extra resources.
*   **Elite Hunter Guides:** These provide a significant amount of Esper EXP, helping you level up your secondary team members quickly.

### Farming Dress Sleeves of Vanity

Because you are limited to three reward claims per week, you should prioritize characters that provide the most utility to your current roster. For example, if you are using Haniel as your primary support, focus your Dress Sleeves of Vanity on her passive abilities to increase the team's overall survivability.

### Other Notable Anomaly Drops

In addition to the primary loot, the boss also contributes to your Anomaly Investigation rank. Increasing this rank unlocks further districts in Hethereau and provides one-time rewards like Annuliths (premium currency) and S-Rank Arc selectors.

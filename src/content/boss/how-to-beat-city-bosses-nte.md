## Attack Patterns

![Attack Patterns](/img/how-to-beat-city-bosses-nte/inline-2.webp)


Success in Neverness to Everness combat relies on visual literacy. Most city bosses utilize a standardized set of indicators to signal their most dangerous moves. Recognizing the difference between a dodgeable attack and a parryable one is the difference between a quick victory and a team wipe.

### The Rhythm of the Beat King

Beat King is a unique encounter in Illusion Town that incorporates a rhythm-based mechanic into its offensive phase. 

*   **Rhythm Notes:** During the "Encore" phase, Beat King summons floating musical notes around the arena. Players must strike these notes in the order they glow to prevent a city-wide shockwave. Successfully clearing these notes deals massive Break damage to the boss.
*   **The Drop:** When Beat King raises its scepter, it will slam the ground three times. The first two slams are signaled by a **Red Flash**, requiring a dodge. The final slam is signaled by a **Double Red Circle**, which is the window for a Parry.

### Black Tome's Binding Chains

Found in the New Herland District, Black Tome focuses on immobilization and area-of-effect (AoE) denial.

*   **Chain Bind:** The boss targets the active character with purple circles. If caught, the character is immobilized for 3 seconds. You can escape early by switching to another character in your party.
*   **Chaos Vortex:** Black Tome pulls players toward its center. This move cannot be parried and must be avoided by sprinting or using a movement skill like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)’s dash.

### Mammon's High-Stagger Resistance Cues

Mammon is a heavy hitter that relies on "Array Slams."

*   **Array Slam (Ground Slam):** The floor will glow with a golden hue before Mammon strikes. The timing is delayed; you must wait for the glow to reach its maximum intensity before dodging.
*   **Shockwave:** Following a slam, a shockwave ripples outward. Jumping is often more effective than dodging for this specific mechanic to preserve stamina.

### Nestbound Bird's Aerial Dives

This boss utilizes the verticality of the New Herland rooftops. It will frequently fly out of reach of melee characters.

*   **Dive Bomb:** Signaled by a sharp screech and a **Red Flash**. A successful Critical Dodge here allows for a Riposte that grounds the bird.
*   **Feather Rain:** A projectile attack that covers the arena. Using Cosmos attacks during this phase is the most effective way to force the boss back to the ground.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

![Step-by-Step Strategy](/img/how-to-beat-city-bosses-nte/inline-3.webp)


The combat in Neverness to Everness follows a specific "Break and Burst" loop. Efficiency is determined by how quickly you can cycle through your characters' Esper abilities to trigger reactions.

### Phase 1: Building the Esper Meter

At the start of the fight, your primary goal is generating Esper Cycle energy. 
1.  **Basic Attack Strings:** Use your Attacker (e.g., Jiuyuan) to land consistent hits.
2.  **Skill Usage:** Deploy Elemental Skills as soon as they are off cooldown. This generates energy for the entire party.
3.  **Positioning:** In urban environments, use the terrain. If fighting near buildings, you can use wall-runs to avoid ground-level AoE attacks from bosses like Mammon.

### Phase 2: Shredding the Break Meter

Once your Esper Meter is full, focus entirely on the white Break bar.
*   **The Critical Dodge:** When you see a **Red Flash**, dodge at the last possible moment. This triggers a "Critical Riposte," a high-damage counter-attack that deals significant Break damage.
*   **The Momentum Parry:** When the **Double Red Circle** appears, use your Parry skill (default [information unavailable]). A successful parry instantly fills a portion of the Esper Meter and deals a massive blow to the boss's stability.
*   **Elemental Reactions:** Swap between characters to apply different elements. For example, applying Anima followed by Cosmos triggers a [information unavailable] reaction that shreds the Break Meter faster than raw damage.

### Phase 3: The Stagger Burst Window

When the Break Meter reaches zero, the boss enters a Staggered state.
1.  **Ultimate Dump:** This is the only time you should use your Ultimate abilities. The boss takes [information unavailable]% increased damage during this window.
2.  **Esper Cycle Reactions:** Continue swapping characters to maintain elemental pressure.
3.  **Reset:** Once the boss recovers, its Break Meter will refill. The boss often gains a temporary "Enrage" buff immediately after recovering, so play defensively for the first 10 seconds of the next cycle.

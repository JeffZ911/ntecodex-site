# How to Beat Phantylia the Undying in Honkai: Star Rail: Complete Boss Guide

Phantylia the Undying serves as the climactic encounter of the Xianzhou Luofu main story arc and remains one of the most mechanically demanding Echo of War bosses. Unlike earlier bosses that rely on pure damage, Phantylia utilizes a unique "Prana-Siphon" mechanic that permanently reduces your team's maximum health until cleansed. To farm her advanced Trace materials efficiently, players must balance offensive pressure with high-frequency cleansing.

## Boss Stats

![Boss Stats](/img/how-to-beat-phantylia-hsr/inline-1.webp)


Phantylia is a three-phase boss found in the Scalegorge Waterscape. Each phase represents a different Path—Abundance, Destruction, and finally, a combination of both. Her stats scale significantly across the six difficulty levels of the Echo of War.

### Phantylia Weaknesses and Resistances

Phantylia possesses a consistent weakness profile across all three phases, making specific elemental types far more effective at breaking her Toughness.

*   **Primary Weaknesses:** Lightning, Wind, Imaginary.
*   **Damage Resistances:** Phantylia has a 20% resistance to Physical, Fire, Ice, and Quantum damage.
*   **Debuff Resistances:** She has a 50% resistance to Frozen and Entanglement. This makes Freeze-based strategies (like those using March 7th or Gepard's Skill) unreliable for stalling her turns.

### Phase Transitions and Elemental Shifts

The fight is divided into three distinct HP bars. When a bar is depleted, Phantylia transitions immediately, often resetting her summons and changing her attack style.

1.  **Phase 1 (Abundance):** Focuses on Wind DMG. She summons Abundance Lotuses that interfere with your Skill Point (SP) economy.
2.  **Phase 2 (Destruction):** Shifts to Lightning DMG. She summons Destruction Lotuses that apply the Prana-Siphon debuff.
3.  **Phase 3 (The Undying):** Combines both elements and summons both types of Lotuses. She gains the ability to use Imaginary DMG for her ultimate attacks.

## Attack Patterns

![Attack Patterns](/img/how-to-beat-phantylia-hsr/inline-2.webp)


Understanding the Lotuses is the most important part of the Phantylia fight. She rarely acts alone, relying on these summons to cripple your team's resources and health pools.

### Phase 1: The Power of Abundance

In this phase, Phantylia uses the "Worldly Lotus" (Yellow). These flowers have a specific mechanic called "Samsara."

*   **Abundance Lotus (Yellow):** These lotuses use an attack that drains 1 Skill Point from your team. If you do not destroy them quickly, you will find your characters unable to use their Skills, leading to a collapse in healing or damage output.
*   **Death Throes:** When an Abundance Lotus is defeated, it triggers a "Samsara" effect, which restores 3 Skill Points to your team.
*   **Phantylia’s Attacks:** She primarily uses single-target and AoE Wind attacks. Her "Yi Wood" attack hits a single target, while "Rain of Lustre" deals Wind DMG to all targets.

### Phase 2: The Path of Destruction

The difficulty spikes in Phase 2 as Phantylia summons Destruction Lotuses (Purple). These flowers do not target your Skill Points; instead, they target your survival via "Prana-Siphon."

*   **Destruction Lotus (Purple):** These lotuses apply the Prana-Siphon debuff to your characters.
*   **Prana-Siphon Mechanic:** This debuff reduces the target's Max HP by 40%. On your health bar, this appears as a greyed-out section that cannot be healed. If a character is hit twice, their Max HP can be reduced even further, making them susceptible to being one-shot.
*   **Weakness Protection:** Destruction Lotuses are "Weakness Protected" by default, meaning you cannot reduce their Toughness until they enter the "Bloom" state.
*   **Havoc State:** After a Lotus uses "Chaos," it enters the Bloom state. If it is not Weakness Broken before its next turn, it unleashes "Havoc," a massive Lightning attack that ignores a portion of the target's DEF.

### Phase 3: The Cycle of Rebirth

Phase 3 is a chaotic combination of the previous two. Phantylia summons two Abundance Lotuses and two Destruction Lotuses.

*   **Arbor Apotheosis:** This is Phantylia’s ultimate move. She restrains a target and deals massive Imaginary DMG to the entire team. This attack is often preceded by her lotuses applying Prana-Siphon, making it extremely lethal if your team’s Max HP is reduced.
*   **Metamorphosis:** She will frequently cycle through summoning new lotuses as soon as others are destroyed.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

![Step-by-Step Strategy](/img/how-to-beat-phantylia-hsr/inline-3.webp)


Success in this battle depends on how you handle the "Cloudhymn Roar" assist and your cleansing priority.

### How to Handle the Lotus Summons

In Phase 1, you should prioritize killing the Abundance Lotuses. Since they have low HP and grant 3 Skill Points upon death, they are essentially "SP batteries." Using an AoE character like Jing Yuan or Serval can keep these lotuses cleared while still damaging Phantylia.

In Phase 2 and 3, the priority shifts. You cannot always kill the Destruction Lotuses immediately due to their high HP and Weakness Protection. Instead, wait for them to "Bloom." When they Bloom, they reveal their Weaknesses. This is the moment to use Ultimates or the Cloudhymn Roar assist to Break them. Breaking a Destruction Lotus prevents the "Havoc" attack and stops them from applying Prana-Siphon for a turn.

### Cleansing the Prana-Siphon Debuff

Prana-Siphon is the primary reason for team wipes. You must bring at least one character capable of "Cleansing" debuffs. 

*   **Priority:** Always cleanse your Sustain (Healer/Tank) first, then your Main DPS.
*   **Timing:** Do not wait for the grey bar to disappear naturally; it won't. Use your healer's Skill (like Natasha, Lynx, or Luocha) to remove the debuff immediately.
*   **Passive Cleansing:** Characters like Huohuo or [Fu Xuan](/characters/fu-xuan-best-relic-build-hsr-20260513) (at E0 with her Skill active) provide excellent protection against these debuffs by preventing them or cleansing them automatically at the start of a turn.

### Optimizing the Cloudhymn Roar Assist

During the story version and the Echo of War version of this fight, you have access to a special "R" button (on PC) or an icon on the screen representing Dan Heng • Imbibitor Lunae's power. This is the "Cloudhymn Roar."

*   **Charging:** The Roar charges as you deal damage and perform actions.
*   **Effect:** It deals massive Imaginary DMG to all enemies on the field and deals significant Toughness damage.
*   **Best Use Case:** Save the Cloudhymn Roar for Phase 3 when Phantylia has four lotuses on the field. Using it when the Destruction Lotuses are in their "Bloom" state will often Break all of them simultaneously, protecting your team from a massive damage spike.

## Recommended Team

![Recommended Team](/img/how-to-beat-phantylia-hsr/inline-4.webp)


Because Phantylia hits multiple targets and applies Max HP debuffs, a "Solo Sustain" setup requires a very well-built healer. For players still gearing up, a "Double Sustain" (Healer + Tank) approach is safer.

### Best F2P Team for Phantylia

This team uses free or easily accessible 4-star characters that align with Phantylia's weaknesses.

*   **Main DPS:** Serval (Lightning AoE is great for clearing lotuses).
*   **Sub-DPS:** Dan Heng (Wind DMG for single-target pressure on Phantylia).
*   **Sustain (Healer):** Natasha or Lynx (Both can cleanse Prana-Siphon with their Skills).
*   **Sustain (Tank):** Fire Trailblazer (Provides shields and taunts to redirect single-target hits).

### Top-Tier Premium Team Compositions

If you have access to 5-star characters, the fight becomes significantly faster.

*   **The Wind Blade:** Blade, Bronya, Pela, Luocha.

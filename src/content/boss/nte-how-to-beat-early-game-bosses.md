# NTE Early Game Boss Guide: How to Beat the First Major Anomalies

The combat system in Neverness to Everness (NTE) relies on a sophisticated "Esper Cycle" that rewards players for timing their dodges and managing a boss's Break bar. Unlike standard action RPGs where you can simply mash attack buttons, the early game bosses act as skill checks to ensure you understand the interaction between Cosmos, Anima, and Incantation elements. This guide covers the three primary threats you will face in the first few weeks of play: the Picture Frame tutorial boss, the open-world brute Bopp, and the first weekly challenge, The Never-Ending Arachne.

Understanding the elemental profiles and level requirements of these Anomalies prevents unnecessary wipes. Each boss has a specific "Break Resistance" value that determines how much "Break Intensity" your characters need to stagger them effectively.

### Picture Frame: The Tutorial Gatekeeper
The Picture Frame is the first Anomaly you encounter during the opening sequence in the Hekate City Art Gallery. It serves as a controlled environment to teach you the "Critical Riposte" mechanic. Because it shares the Cosmos element, using Zero’s basic attacks will trigger resonance effects quickly, making this a short encounter. Its level is fixed to the tutorial scaling, so you cannot be under-leveled for this fight.

### Bopp: The First Open-World Challenge
Bopp is a massive, gorilla-like Anomaly that typically spawns in the industrial districts and construction sites of Hekate. Unlike the Picture Frame, Bopp has no fixed level; it scales based on your World Level, usually ranging from Level 15 to 25 in the early game. Bopp lacks a specific elemental affinity but takes significantly increased Break damage from Anima-aligned characters like Mint.

### The Never-Ending Arachne: Your First Weekly Boss
Located within the "Gilded Theater" instance unlocked at the end of Episode 3, The Never-Ending Arachne is a significant step up in difficulty. It is an Incantation-element boss, meaning it is highly resistant to fire-based or psychological-status attacks but vulnerable to the "Hex" and "Blossom" reactions triggered by Cosmos and Anima combinations. You should aim for a Hunter Level of at least 30 before attempting the higher difficulty tiers.

## Attack Patterns

![Attack Patterns](/img/nte-how-to-beat-early-game-bosses/inline-2.webp)


Success in NTE combat is dictated by your ability to read visual cues. The game uses a standardized flashing system to tell you when to dodge and when to counter-attack.

### Recognizing Red Flash vs. Yellow Shine
*   **Red Flash:** This indicates an Unblockable Attack. When a boss's eyes or weapon glints red, you must perform a "Critical Dodge." Timing this perfectly slows down time and fills a portion of your Esper Meter.
*   **Yellow/Gold Shine:** This indicates a "Parryable Attack." If you use a Skill or a Heavy Attack the moment the yellow shine appears, you perform a "Critical Riposte," which deals massive Break damage and interrupts the boss's combo.

### Bopp's Stomp and Charge Sequence
Bopp relies on heavy physical momentum. His primary rotation involves:
1.  **Industrial Stomp:** Bopp raises his right fist. A yellow shine appears. If you parry this, he falls forward, exposing his head for extra damage.
2.  **Rubble Charge:** Bopp scrapes the ground and rushes toward the player. This is preceded by a red flash. You must dodge sideways; dodging backward will often still result in being hit by the lingering hitbox of his shoulders.
3.  **Anomaly Burst:** When Bopp reaches 50% HP, he emits a circular shockwave. This move has [exact startup frames pending official confirmation], but the visual cue is a darkening of the air around his feet.

### Arachne's Puppet Wave Phase
The Never-Ending Arachne is a multi-phase fight that tests your crowd control.
*   **Triple Slash Combo:** Arachne uses her front legs to swipe three times. The first two swipes are standard, but the third swipe always triggers a yellow shine window.
*   **Puppet Wave:** Arachne retreats to the ceiling and summons four smaller "Stage Puppets." While these puppets are active, Arachne is immune to damage. You must clear the puppets to force her back down.
*   **Silk Trap:** A red flash move where she shoots webbing at the player's feet. If caught, you are immobilized for 3 seconds, usually followed by a high-damage plunge attack.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

![Step-by-Step Strategy](/img/nte-how-to-beat-early-game-bosses/inline-3.webp)


The core combat loop in NTE is: **Stagger -> Break -> Burst.**

### Mastering the Break System
Every boss has a white bar beneath their HP. This is the Break bar. Dealing damage with the boss's elemental weakness depletes this bar faster. Once the bar is empty, the boss enters a "Broken" state, taking [information unavailable]% increased damage and remaining motionless for several seconds.

**The Stagger-to-Burst Combat Loop:**
1.  **Setup Phase:** Use Mint (Anima) to apply the "Lush" status to the boss.
2.  **Reaction Phase:** Switch to Zero (Cosmos) to trigger a "Blossom" reaction, which deals high Break damage.
3.  **The Riposte:** Wait for the Yellow Shine and use Adler’s Skill to trigger a Critical Riposte.
4.  **Burst Window:** Once the boss is "Broken," swap to your primary DPS (like Chiz or [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)) and unload your Ultimate abilities.

### Phase 1: Clearing the Puppets (Arachne)
During the Arachne fight, do not focus on the boss while she is on the ceiling. The Stage Puppets have very low Break resistance. Use Adler’s Incantation circles to group them together, then use Mint’s Anima swirls to finish them off. This saves your Esper Meter for when Arachne returns to the floor.

### Phase 2 & 3: Sustaining DPS and Parrying
In the final phase (below 30% HP), Bopp and Arachne both gain "Enraged" status, increasing their attack speed. At this point, stop chasing raw HP damage. Focus entirely on landing Critical Ripostes. Each successful Riposte grants you a "Resonance Stack," which increases your final damage output during the next Break window.

For early game players, the "Starter Trio" is surprisingly viable for clearing all content up to the end of Episode 3.

This team focuses on the "Blossom" reaction (Cosmos + Anima) to shred Break bars.

### Premium Alternatives: Incorporating Nanally or Daffodill
If you were lucky enough to pull on the debut banners, you can significantly speed up these fights. 
*   **Chiz (Cosmos):** Currently considered the best free S-Rank for early bosses. Her "Singularity" skill has the highest Break Intensity in the game, allowing her to break Bopp in nearly half the time it takes Zero.
*   **Nanally (Anima):** Her banner, "The Ichi-daime," provides a powerful DPS who excels at aerial combat. This makes her the perfect counter for The Never-Ending Arachne, as Nanally can stay in the air to avoid the Silk Trap and Puppet Wave ground effects.
*   **Haniel (A-Class):** Essential for players struggling with survival. Haniel provides a "Radiant Shield" that absorbs Physical damage, which is incredibly helpful against Bopp's high-damage stomps.

### Recommended Arcs (Weapons) for Early Bossing
Arcs provide the passive stats needed to survive boss encounters. For the early game, focus on Arcs that increase "Break Efficiency."
*   **For Zero/Chiz:** Look for Arcs that boost Cosmos DMG or provide Esper Meter regeneration on hit.
*   **For Mint:** Use Arcs that scale with Max HP to increase her healing potency.
*   **For Adler:** Arcs that increase the duration of Incantation fields are preferred.

## Loot

![Loot](/img/nte-how-to-beat-early-game-bosses/inline-5.webp)


Defeating these bosses is the primary way to advance your characters and your "City Tycoon" rank.

### Essential Upgrade Materials
Bosses in NTE do not just drop gear; they drop "Character Pixels," which are a currency-like material used to "reconstruct" character levels. You must spend a specific amount of "Energy" (the game's stamina system) to claim these rewards after the fight.

| Item Name | Rarity | Primary Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Dress Sleeves of Vanity | Epic (Purple) | Talent leveling for Incantation characters. |
| Fabricated Dice | Rare (Blue) | Random stat rerolls on Arcs. |
| Beetle Coins | Common | Currency for the Hekate Anomaly Shop. |
| Fons | Common | General currency for car parts and city upgrades. |

### Weekly Boss Reward Limits
The Never-Ending Arachne can be defeated multiple times, but you can only claim the "High-Density Loot" (which includes the Dress Sleeves of Vanity) three times per week. These rewards reset every Monday at 04:00 server time. It is highly recommended to use all three attempts on Arachne early on, as her materials are required for the Level 40 Ascension of most characters.

### Using Fons for City Tycoon Upgrades
While boss drops are mostly for combat, the Fons you earn from Bopp and the tutorial missions should be reinvested into your Hekate properties. Upgrading your business storefronts increases your passive Fons income, allowing you to buy high-end Arcs and consumables from the city vendors without having to grind boss encounters repeatedly.

By focusing on the Break system and utilizing the elemental synergies between Zero, Mint, and Adler, you can overcome the early game hurdles of Neverness to Everness with minimal frustration. Pay attention to the yellow and red flashes, manage your Esper Cycle, and ensure your team is leveled appropriately before heading into the Gilded Theater to face the Arachne.

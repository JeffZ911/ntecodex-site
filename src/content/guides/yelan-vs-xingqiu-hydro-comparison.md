## Detailed Comparison

![Detailed Comparison](/img/yelan-vs-xingqiu-hydro-comparison/inline-3.webp)


While both characters use an Elemental Burst that triggers coordinated Hydro attacks with the active character's Normal Attacks, the nuance of how those attacks function—and what they provide—varies significantly.

### Hydro Application and Elemental Gauge Theory

In Genshin Impact, "Hydro Application" is not just about hitting the enemy; it is about how many "units" of Hydro are applied to maintain an elemental aura. This is where Xingqiu often outperforms Yelan, particularly at Constellation 6 (C6).

Xingqiu’s Elemental Burst, *Raincutter*, fires waves of rain swords. At C6, these waves follow a 2-3-5 pattern. Because of how Internal Cooldown (ICD) works, the 5-sword wave applies significantly more Hydro. Furthermore, Xingqiu possesses "Orbital Rain Swords" that circle the active character. When these swords touch an enemy, they apply Hydro independently of the Burst's coordinated attacks. This makes Xingqiu the most reliable enabler for Hu Tao, ensuring she always Vaporizes her Charged Attacks.

Yelan’s Burst, *Depth-Clarion Dice*, fires three shots per wave. While fast, it follows standard ICD rules (applying Hydro every 3 hits or 2.5 seconds). Without her Constellation 2 (C2), which adds an extra Hydro arrow every 1.8 seconds, Yelan can occasionally struggle to keep up with extremely fast Pyro applicators. If you are running a solo Hydro enabler for a Vaporize team, a C6 Xingqiu is mathematically more consistent than a C0 Yelan.

### Personal Damage vs. Team Buffing

Yelan is a "selfish" Sub-DPS in terms of damage—she wants the glory, and she usually earns it. Her damage scales entirely off Max HP, meaning she completely ignores ATK-based buffs like Bennett’s Burst or the Noblesse Oblige set. This is actually an advantage in modern team building, as it frees up Bennett for the other side of the Spiral Abyss. Her A4 passive, *Adapt With Ease*, provides the active character a damage buff that starts at 1% and increases by 3.5% every second, capping at 50%. This is a massive "Universal DMG" multiplier that affects all types of damage.

Xingqiu scales with ATK, which means he benefits from standard team buffs. While his personal damage is lower than Yelan's in a vacuum, his C2 reduces the Hydro RES of enemies by 15%, which benefits both his own damage and any other Hydro units on the team. In terms of raw numbers, a well-built Yelan will almost always out-damage a well-built Xingqiu, often by a margin of 20% to 35% depending on her weapon.

### Defensive Utility and Interruption Resistance

This is the category where Xingqiu remains unbeaten. His Elemental Skill, *Guhua Sword: Fatal Rainscreen*, creates Rain Swords that provide:
1.  **Damage Reduction:** Usually around 40% to 45% based on his Hydro DMG bonus.
2.  **Interruption Resistance:** This allows characters like Yoimiya or Wanderer to complete their attack strings without being knocked back by enemies.
3.  **Healing:** When a sword breaks or expires, it heals the active character based on Xingqiu’s Max HP.

Yelan offers zero defensive utility. She is a "glass cannon" support. If you use Yelan in a team without a shielder like Zhongli or a dedicated healer, you must be much more proficient at dodging. For many players, the "comfort" of Xingqiu is worth the slight loss in total team DPS.

### Weapon and Artifact Flexibility

Both characters favor the *Emblem of Severed Fate* 4-piece artifact set, as it converts their high Energy Recharge (ER) requirements into Burst damage. However, their weapon choices differ:

*   **Xingqiu:** Almost exclusively tied to the *Sacrificial Sword*. This allows him to cast his Skill twice, effectively doubling his energy particle generation and ensuring his 80-cost Burst is always ready. *Favonius Sword* is a secondary F2P option.
*   **Yelan:** Has much more variety. Her signature 5-star bow, *Aqua Simulacra*, provides a massive 88.2% CRIT DMG. For F2P players, the *Favonius Warbow* (given for free during the Monstadt Archon Quest) is her best-in-slot 4-star option because it solves her energy issues while providing high utility for the team.

### The Impact of Constellations

The "Yelan vs. Xingqiu" debate changes drastically based on Constellations.

*   **Xingqiu C6:** This is his "Gold Standard." It increases his damage, provides energy restoration, and maximizes his Hydro application. A C6 Xingqiu is widely considered a "hidden 5-star."
*   **Yelan C1:** This is arguably the best C1 in the game. It gives her an extra charge of her Elemental Skill, which solves almost all her Energy Recharge problems and allows her to run a damage-oriented weapon like *Aqua Simulacra* or *Slingshot*.
*   **Yelan C2:** This constellation specifically addresses her Hydro application gap, adding an extra 140% HP-scaling Hydro hit every 1.8 seconds. At C2, Yelan officially surpasses C6 Xingqiu in almost every metric, including application.
*   **Yelan C6:** [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-13] regarding any recent nerfs or adjustments, but historically, her C6 turns her into a Main DPS capable of clearing Abyss floors in seconds by turning her Normal Attacks into "Mastermind" arrows.

## Recommendation

![Recommendation](/img/yelan-vs-xingqiu-hydro-comparison/inline-4.webp)


Choosing between them isn't always about who is "better," but about who fits the current needs of your roster.

### Best Scenarios for Yelan

1.  **Speedrunning and High Investment:** If you want the fastest possible clear times in the Spiral Abyss, Yelan’s damage and ramping buff are essential.
2.  **Overworld Exploration:** Her Elemental Skill, *Lingering Lifeline*, is the fastest way to move across the map, making daily commissions and farming much less tedious.
3.  **Teams with Existing Shields:** If you are already running Zhongli or Layla, you don't need Xingqiu’s damage reduction. Yelan is the clear winner here.
4.  **Double Hydro Core:** She is the perfect partner for Xingqiu.

**Top 3 Yelan Teams:**
*   **Hyperbloom:** Alhaitham / Nahida / Kuki Shinobu / Yelan
*   **Raiden Overvape:** Raiden Shogun / Xiangling / Bennett / Yelan
*   **Mono Hydro:** Kokomi / Furina / Kazuha / Yelan

### Best Scenarios for Xingqiu

1.  **F2P / Low Investment:** Xingqiu is a 4-star who has appeared in the Paimon’s Bargains shop and numerous events. Getting him to C6 is much cheaper than pulling multiple Yelan copies.
2.  **Vaporize Reliability:** If you play Hu Tao or Diluc and find that you aren't "vaping" every hit, Xingqiu is the more consistent enabler.
3.  **Fragile Main DPS:** If you play characters like Yoimiya who are easily interrupted, Xingqiu’s poise bonus is a massive quality-of-life improvement.
4.  **National Team Variants:** He remains the backbone of the "National" core (Xiangling/Bennett/Xingqiu).

**Top 3 Xingqiu Teams:**
*   **Classic National:** Xiangling / Bennett / Kazuha / Xingqiu
*   **Hu Tao Vape:** Hu Tao / Zhongli / Albedo / Xingqiu
*   **Nilou Bloom:** Nilou / Nahida / Baizhu / Xingqiu

### Why You Should Run Both: The Double Hydro Meta

The strongest team-building strategy in the current meta is often not choosing between them, but running them together. This is known as the "Double Hydro" core.

**The benefits of Double Hydro are immense:**
*   **Hydro Resonance:** Increases Max HP by 25%. This directly buffs Yelan’s damage, Xingqiu’s healing, and the HP pools of characters like Hu Tao, Furina, or Zhongli.
*   **Reduced ER Requirements:** Because they both generate Hydro particles, they can "battery" each other. This allows you to build less Energy Recharge and more CRIT/HP stats on both characters.
*   **Unstoppable Application:** With both Bursts active, no amount of Pyro or Dendro can overtake the Hydro aura, ensuring 100% reaction consistency.

**Top 3 Double Hydro Teams:**
*   **The "Funerane":** Hu Tao / Yelan / Xingqiu / Zhongli
*   **Yoimiya Double Hydro:** Yoimiya / Yelan / Xingqiu / Yun Jin
*   **Hyperbloom Core:** Nahida / Kuki Shinobu / Yelan / Xingqiu

Ultimately, Yelan and Xingqiu are two sides of the same coin. Xingqiu provides the foundation and safety for your team to function, while Yelan provides the ceiling and power to crush the hardest content. For a well-rounded account, you should aim to build both.

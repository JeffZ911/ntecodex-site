The combat in Neverness to Everness revolves around the manipulation of Esper energy, a supernatural force utilized by the city’s residents to combat anomalies. Unlike traditional elemental systems that rely on simple rock-paper-scissors counters, NTE utilizes an "Esper Wheel" where the positioning of elements dictates how they interact. Success in Hethereau’s toughest commissions depends on your ability to cycle through your four-character party to trigger specific reactions while managing the enemy’s Break bar.

### The Esper Cycle System Explained

The Esper Wheel is a circular diagram where elements are linked to their immediate neighbors. When you swap characters and use skills, you generate "Cycles."

*   **Duo Cycles:** Triggered by swapping between two neighboring elements on the wheel. Common reactions include **Nova** (Psyche + Chaos), **Stain** (Chaos + Incantation), and **Remora** (Anima + Cosmos). These provide immediate burst damage or localized debuffs.
*   **Triple Cycles:** These occur when three characters of neighboring elements are swapped in a specific sequence. The most notable are **Charge** (Anima/Cosmos/Lakshana), which focuses on massive energy regeneration, and **Discord** (Chaos/Incantation/Psyche), which is the primary tool for shredding boss defenses.

### Mastering the Parry and Dodge Counter

While Normal Attacks and Skills gradually fill your Esper Meter, the fastest way to reach a full burst state is through the **Instant Fill** mechanic. By timing a Parry (typically tied to specific character skills or universal defensive frames) or a Perfect Dodge, you gain a massive chunk of Esper energy. Characters like Hotori excel at this, as her kit rewards precise timing with enhanced counter-attacks that contribute significantly to the Stagger bar.

### Understanding Break Intensity

Every elite enemy and boss possesses a white "Break" bar beneath their health. Once this bar is depleted, the enemy enters a Staggered state, taking significantly increased damage and remaining immobile for several seconds. 

**Break Intensity** is a hidden stat on Arcs and Cartridges that determines how much "weight" your attacks carry against this bar. The **Discord** reaction is specifically designed to maximize Break damage, making it essential for fights against high-armor anomalies.

### Optimizing the Block Grid (Tetris System)

Each Cartridge Block has a shape (L-shape, T-shape, Square, etc.). Your "Console" has a limited grid size that expands as you level up your City Tycoon rank. To maximize your stats, you must:
1.  **Prioritize Main Stats:** Consoles and Modules carry primary stats like Elemental DMG or CRIT Rate.
2.  **Fit the Pieces:** You cannot overlap blocks. Sometimes, a lower-rarity block with a better shape is more valuable than a high-rarity block that prevents you from completing a 4-piece set bonus.
3.  **Substat Priority:** For most DPS, the priority is CRIT Rate > CRIT DMG > ATK% > Cycle Intensity. For Breakers, prioritize Break Intensity above all else.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/nte-combat-mechanics-guide-builds/inline-5.webp)


Efficient combat in Neverness to Everness is about the "Glow." When a character on your bench is ready to trigger a Cycle, their portrait will glow. Swapping at this exact moment is the key to maintaining high DPS.

### Standard DPS Rotation for a 'Nova' (Psyche + Chaos) Burst Team

1.  **Opener:** Start with your Psyche character (e.g., Sakaki). Use their Skill to apply an Azure mark.
2.  **The Swap:** Wait for the Chaos character's portrait to glow. Swap immediately to trigger **Nova**.
3.  **The Burst:** Use the Chaos character's Normal Attack string until their Skill is off cooldown.
4.  **The Ultimate:** Once the Esper Meter is full, wait for the boss to be at 50% Break bar.
5.  **The Finish:** Trigger the Ultimate, then swap back to Psyche to restart the cycle.

### Managing the Esper Meter During Combat

Do not waste your Ultimates on trash mobs unless necessary. In boss fights, save your energy for the **Stagger Window**. When a boss breaks, they take nearly double damage. This is when you should chain your Triple Cycles and dump all stored Ultimates. If you find your meter filling too slowly, focus on landing **Parry** counters rather than just spamming Normal Attacks.

### Advanced Quick-Swap Techniques

Advanced players use "Animation Canceling" by swapping characters mid-Skill. For example, Mint’s Skill has a long animation; if you swap to Nanally halfway through, Mint will finish her attack off-field while Nanally begins her own string, effectively doubling your active DPS for a short window.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/nte-combat-mechanics-guide-builds/inline-6.webp)


**How do I get free S-Rank Arcs?**
You can obtain S-Rank Arcs like **Reality Refuge** and **Raging Flames** by completing Anomaly Commissions and progressing through the City Tycoon milestones. These require significant time investment but are guaranteed rewards.

**What is Cycle Intensity?**
Cycle Intensity is a stat that scales the damage and effectiveness of Duo and Triple reactions. High Cycle Intensity makes reactions like Nova and Discord hit significantly harder, regardless of your base ATK.

**Does the 50/50 exist in NTE?**
Yes, Neverness to Everness features a 90-pull guaranteed system for limited character banners. If you do not pull the featured character on your first S-Rank hit, your next S-Rank is guaranteed to be the featured one.

**Can I use any Arc on any character?**
You can equip any Arc, but the **Arc Skill** (the unique passive) will only activate if the Arc Type (Plasma, Solid, etc.) matches the character's requirements. Always check the character's profile to see their compatible Arc types.

**What is the best way to fill the Esper Meter quickly?**
The most efficient method is the **Parry** mechanic. Successfully parrying an enemy attack provides a much larger boost to the Esper Meter than a full string of Normal Attacks.

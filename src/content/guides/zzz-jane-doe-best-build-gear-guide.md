## What a Jane Doe main actually needs

![What a Jane Doe main actually needs](/img/zzz-jane-doe-best-build-gear-guide/inline-2.webp)


To maximize the potential of a Jane Doe build, you must understand the technical demands her kit places on your hardware. She is not a "set and forget" character; she requires active, precise management.

### High Polling Rate and Input Precision
Jane Doe's Passion State involves rapid multi-hits. During her Basic Attack: Salchow Jump, she strikes enemies multiple times in a fraction of a second. A mouse with a 1000Hz+ polling rate ensures that every single click is registered by the game engine during these high-APM bursts. If your mouse has a low polling rate, you might find that some inputs feel "mushy" or fail to trigger her EX Special Attack at the exact moment her energy bar fills.

### Low Response Time for Visual Cues
Consistently triggering Jane Doe’s Perfect Dodge is the difference between an average build and an optimized one. ZZZ uses visual flashes to indicate when an enemy is about to strike. A monitor with a sub-3ms real-world response time is required to see these cues clearly. On slower screens, the "ghosting" effect can blur the flash, causing you to dodge too early or too late, which resets your Passion Stream accumulation.

### Lumbar Support for the Long Grind
Farming the best Jane Doe build requires repetitive clears of Routine Cleanup stages to get the Fanged Metal set. Static posture for 4+ hours during these farming sessions requires active spinal support. Without a chair like the [TITAN Evo](https://secretlab.co/products/titan-evo-2022-series), players often develop "gamer lean," which leads to neck strain and reduced focus, ultimately slowing down your clear times.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/zzz-jane-doe-best-build-gear-guide/inline-4.webp)


Not every player needs to rush out and buy a full suite of new hardware. If your current setup meets certain thresholds, the gains from upgrading may be minimal.

*   **Existing 144Hz IPS Monitors:** If you already own a 144Hz IPS monitor, the jump to 165Hz or 180Hz is marginal for ZZZ gameplay. While the LG 27GP850-B is excellent, it won't fundamentally change your Jane Doe performance if you are already above the 120Hz mark.
*   **Controller Players:** If you play ZZZ exclusively with a controller, the high-end mouse and keyboard recommendations will not impact your Jane Doe performance. Instead, focus your budget on a controller with hall-effect sensors to prevent stick drift during intense combat.
*   **Ergonomic Basics:** Don't buy a $500 ergonomic chair if your current setup already allows your feet to rest flat on the floor and your monitor is at eye level. Proper desk ergonomics often matter more than the brand of the chair you sit in.

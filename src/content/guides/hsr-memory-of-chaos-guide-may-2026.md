# Honkai: Star Rail Memory of Chaos Guide: How to Clear Stage 12 (May 2026)

## Overview

The Memory of Chaos (MoC) remains the ultimate test for any Trailblazer’s roster. As of May 2026, the mode continues its six-week rotation within the "Treasures Lightward" system, alternating with Pure Fiction and Apocalyptic Shadow. The current cycle, titled "Grand Finale," is heavily tailored toward the high-frequency combat style introduced in Version 4.1, specifically benefiting characters who can act multiple times within a single turn or trigger frequent follow-up attacks (FUA).

To secure a full 36-star clear, you must complete Stage 12 within 10 total cycles (remaining cycles ≥ 20) while ensuring all four team members survive both halves. The aggressive AI scaling in the latest patches means that traditional "glass cannon" builds are increasingly risky; survivability via high-HP supports or high-frequency shielders is now a requirement rather than a luxury.

### Understanding the 'Grand Finale' Turbulence

The "Grand Finale" mechanic revolves around "Punchline" points. Every time an ally performs an attack—including Basic ATKs, Skills, Ultimates, and Follow-up Attacks—they accumulate 1 Punchline point. In Stage 12, these points can stack up to a maximum of 50. At the start of every new cycle, the Memory Turbulence triggers, dealing massive True DMG to all enemies based on the total number of accumulated points.

Because the damage scales exponentially with the number of stacks, teams that can reach the 50-stack cap early in the cycle will see the most significant benefit. This makes characters like Ashveil, who can trigger multiple follow-up attacks per action, the undisputed kings of this rotation.

### Stage 12 Boss Analysis: Zandar and Pollux

Stage 12 features a daunting lineup of bosses that require specific elemental coverage and mechanical knowledge.

| Node | Boss | Recommended Elements | Key Weaknesses |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Node 1** | First Genius, Entelechy, Zandar | Lightning, Ice, Quantum | High Toughness, Summon-heavy |
| **Node 2** | Pollux, The Sunken Star | Physical, Imaginary, Wind | High DMG Burst, CC Vulnerable |

**Zandar (Node 1):** Zandar operates in two distinct modes: Evolution and Disputation. In Evolution Mode, he summons Titankins that buff his speed and damage. In Disputation Mode, he shifts focus to "Black Tide" summons. The key to defeating Zandar is triggering his "Antinomy" state by breaking his summons' Toughness, which applies a permanent 50% Vulnerability debuff to Zandar himself.

**Pollux (Node 2):** Pollux is a massive single-target threat that utilizes "Starfall" mechanics to target the ally with the highest current HP. While he deals devastating damage, he is highly susceptible to Weakness Break. Characters like Mydei or the Remembrance Trailblazer are essential here to keep Pollux locked down and prevent him from reaching his "Nova" state.

### The Ashveil FUA Core

This team is designed to break the "Grand Finale" Turbulence. Ashveil marks an enemy as "Bait," and every time Sunday or Tribbie attacks that target, Ashveil launches a follow-up. Tribbie’s "Numinosity" state adds additional Quantum damage to every hit, which helps shred Zandar’s Toughness bar. Sunday acts as the primary battery and action-advancer, ensuring Ashveil never runs out of "Gluttony" stacks.

### Break Meta: Remembrance Trailblazer Synergy

The Remembrance Trailblazer (Ice/Remembrance) has become a staple for Node 2. Their ability to summon "Mem" adds a fifth actor to the turn order, which significantly boosts the Punchline stack count. When paired with Castorice, the team can effectively "freeze" Pollux’s action bar through repeated Weakness Breaks and the "Dissociation" status effect.

## Rotation

The difference between a 4-cycle clear and a 10-cycle fail often comes down to how you manage your Ultimates and Skill Points (SP).

### Maximizing the 'Punchline' True Damage

To maximize the "Grand Finale" effect, do not use all your Ultimates at once. Since every attack counts as a stack, you should space out your Ultimates to ensure you are always building toward the 50-stack cap. If you reach 50 stacks early in a cycle, save any remaining offensive Ultimates for the start of the *next* cycle to get a head start on the new stack count.

### Handling Zandar's Form Shifts

When Zandar enters **Evolution Mode**, he will summon two Titankins. Do not ignore them. Killing a Titankin grants your team the "Antinomy" buff, which restores [information unavailable] Energy and increases DMG dealt by 30%. In **Disputation Mode**, Zandar becomes much more aggressive. Save your sustain's Ultimate (Aventurine or Hyacine) specifically for his "Query: Prime Mover's Outcome" attack, which deals distributed damage across the entire party.

### 0-Cycle Opening Rotation for Ashveil Teams

1.  **Technique:** Use Ashveil’s technique to enter battle with 3 Gluttony charges.
2.  **Ashveil Turn:** Use Skill on the elite/boss to apply "Bait."
3.  **Sunday Turn:** Use Skill on Ashveil to advance his action.
4.  **Ashveil Turn:** Use Basic ATK (recovers 1 SP due to "Bait" mechanic).
5.  **Tribbie Turn:** Activate Ultimate to create the "Zone" and trigger Ashveil’s FUA.
6.  **Sunday/Robin:** Activate Ultimate *after* the DPS has finished their turn to provide a full team action advance.

## FAQ

### Unlocking and Reset Schedule

**How do I unlock Memory of Chaos?**
You must clear Stage 15 of the "Last Vestiges of the Towering Citadel" (the permanent Forgotten Hall) to gain access to the MoC floors.

**When does MoC reset?**
MoC resets every 6 weeks on a Monday at 04:00 server time. It alternates with Pure Fiction and Apocalyptic Shadow, meaning there is always an endgame mode refreshing every two weeks.

### Stellar Jade and Shop Rewards

Clearing all 12 stages with 36 stars rewards a total of 800 Stellar Jades. Additionally, you earn "Jade Feathers," which can be traded in the Priceless Jewels shop for Light Cone Manifests, Self-Modeling Resin, and Star Rail Passes.

| Milestone | Rewards |
| :--- | :--- |
| **3 Stars (per stage)** | 60 Stellar Jades + 20,000 Credits |
| **Full 36-Star Clear** | 800 Stellar Jades + 600 Jade Feathers |

### Troubleshooting Low Damage Output

If you are failing to clear within the cycle limit, check your **SPD tuning**. Most players fail because their supports are too slow to generate the Turbulence stacks. Ensure your Harmony units are at 143+ SPD. If survivability is the issue, swap your ATK% sphere for an HP% sphere on your supports; a dead support provides zero buffs.

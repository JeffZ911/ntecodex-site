## Overview

![Overview](/img/clara-best-light-cone-hsr-build-guide/inline-1.webp)


Clara is a 5-star Physical character following the Path of Destruction. Unlike traditional damage dealers who rely on high Speed to take as many turns as possible, Clara thrives on the enemy's turn. Her kit is built entirely around Svarog’s "Mark of Counter," a mechanic that triggers a powerful follow-up attack whenever Clara or her allies (under the effects of her Ultimate) are struck by an opponent.

Because her primary damage output is reactive, Clara’s build priorities differ from most Destruction units. She requires a delicate balance of survivability to withstand incoming hits and high offensive stats to ensure Svarog’s retaliation hits hard. Management of "Aggro" (Taunt Value) is the defining factor of a successful Clara build; if enemies do not target her, her damage output drops significantly.

### Trace Priority
1.  **Talent (Because Svarog's Counter is the main DMG source)**
2.  **Ultimate (Essential for DMG reduction and "Enhanced Counters")**
3.  **Skill (Strong AoE clear for marked enemies)**
4.  **Basic ATK (Lowest priority, used only for SP generation)**

### Clara's Unique Counter-Attack Playstyle
Clara’s gameplay revolves around the Talent "Because We're Family." When Clara is hit by an enemy, Svarog immediately launches a counter-attack dealing Physical DMG equal to a percentage of Clara's ATK and marks the target with a "Mark of Counter." This makes her exceptionally strong against enemies with frequent multi-hits or AoE attacks. Her Skill, "Svarog Watches Over You," deals damage to all enemies and doubles the damage dealt to those with a Mark of Counter, though it removes the mark upon use (unless you have her first Eidolon).

### Why Aggro is Clara's Most Important Mechanic
Aggro determines the likelihood of a character being targeted. As a Destruction unit, Clara has a base Taunt value of 125, which is higher than Hunt or Erudition characters but lower than Preservation units. To maximize Svarog's counters, you must inflate this number through her Ultimate, specific Light Cones, or teammate abilities. Without high aggro, Clara becomes a passive observer rather than a carry.

## Best Weapons

![Best Weapons](/img/clara-best-light-cone-hsr-build-guide/inline-2.webp)


The Light Cone selection for Clara has evolved significantly since the game's launch. While her signature weapon remains a solid choice, newer options designed for follow-up attackers have pushed her damage ceiling even higher.

### Top 5-Star Light Cones for Clara

**1. Dance at Sunset (Yunli's Signature)**
This is currently the undisputed Best-in-Slot (BiS) for Clara. It provides a massive 36% Crit DMG boost and, crucially, greatly increases the wearer's chance to be attacked. When the wearer is attacked, they gain a stack of "Firedance," which increases Follow-up DMG by 36% for 2 turns. This solves Clara's aggro issues while providing the specific damage buffs she craves most.

**2. Something Irreplaceable (Clara's Signature)**
Clara’s own signature weapon is a powerhouse for sustain. It increases ATK by 24%. When the wearer defeats an enemy or is hit, they immediately restore HP equal to 8% of their ATK and receive a 24% DMG boost until the end of their next turn. This is the best choice for high-difficulty content like Gold and Gears or Simulated Universe where Clara’s survival is under constant threat.

**3. On the Fall of an Aeon (Herta Shop)**
The premier F2P option. At Superimposition 5 (S5), this Light Cone provides a 16% ATK increase every time the wearer attacks, stacking up to 4 times (64% ATK total). Additionally, when the wearer inflicts Weakness Break, their DMG increases by 24% for 2 turns. While it lacks the aggro or Crit stats of premium cones, the raw ATK stat is massive.

**4. The Unreachable Side (Blade's Signature)**
This provides 18% Crit Rate and 18% Max HP. When the wearer is attacked or consumes their own HP, their DMG increases by 24%. Since Clara is hit constantly, she maintains 100% uptime on the DMG buff, making this a very consistent stat-stick.

### Best F2P and 4-Star Light Cones

If 5-star options are unavailable, Clara can still perform well with 4-star Destruction cones, though the gap in base stats is noticeable.

*   **A Secret Vow:** Provides a flat 20% DMG increase (at S1). If Clara’s current HP percentage is lower than the enemy's, she gains an additional 20% DMG boost. Since Clara takes hits frequently, she often stays at a lower HP percentage than healthy bosses.
*   **Under the Blue Sky:** Increases ATK by 16%. When the wearer defeats an enemy, their Crit Rate increases by 12% for 3 turns. This is excellent for Wave-based content like Pure Fiction.

However, the signature weapon's healing provides a "comfy" factor that the F2P option lacks. *Dance at Sunset* outperforms both by roughly 12-15% due to the increased frequency of counters from the aggro boost.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

![Best Disks (Artifacts)](/img/clara-best-light-cone-hsr-build-guide/inline-3.webp)


Selecting the right Relics for Clara involves maximizing her Physical damage and ensuring she has enough Crit consistency to make every Svarog swing count.

### Best Relic Sets for Physical DMG

**Champion of Streetwise Boxing (4-Piece)**
This remains the gold standard for Clara. The 2-piece set grants 10% Physical DMG. The 4-piece set increases the wearer's ATK by 5% every time they are hit or attack, stacking up to 5 times (25% ATK). Since Clara is designed to be hit, she reaches max stacks almost instantly.

**Longevous Disciple (4-Piece)**
A strong alternative if you are struggling with Crit Rate. The 2-piece grants 12% Max HP, and the 4-piece grants 8% Crit Rate for 2 turns when the wearer is hit or consumes HP (stacks twice). This provides a total of 16% Crit Rate with near-permanent uptime on Clara.

**The Ashblazing Grand Duke (2-Piece Mixed)**
While the 4-piece set of Grand Duke is inconsistent for Clara (as Svarog's counters are single-hit or low-hit count), the 2-piece set provides a flat 20% Follow-up DMG boost. Pairing 2-piece Grand Duke with 2-piece Streetwise Boxing is a highly efficient "2+2" build for those with poor substat luck on full sets.

### Planar Ornaments: Duran vs. Salsotto

**Duran, Dynasty of Running Wolves**
The current best-in-slot for Clara when she is paired with other follow-up attackers like Topaz or Aventurine. It grants a stack of "Merit" whenever an ally launches a follow-up attack. Each stack increases Follow-up DMG by 5% (max 5 stacks). At 5 stacks, it also grants 25% Crit DMG.

**Inert Salsotto**
The classic choice. It provides 8% Crit Rate and, if the wearer's Crit Rate is 50% or higher, increases Ultimate and Follow-up DMG by 15%. This is more self-sufficient than Duran and better if Clara is the only follow-up unit on the team.

### Recommended Main Stats and Substats

Clara is one of the few DPS units where **Speed is a detriment**. If Clara is too fast, she cycles through her Ultimate's "Enhanced Counter" stacks and buff durations too quickly. You want her to be slow so that the 2-turn duration of her Ultimate lasts through as many enemy turns as possible.

*   **Body:** Crit Rate or Crit DMG (Aim for a 1:2 ratio)
*   **Feet:** ATK% (Avoid Speed boots)
*   **Sphere:** Physical DMG%
*   **Rope:** ATK% or Energy Regeneration Rate (ERR)

**Substat Priority:** Crit Rate/DMG > ATK% > HP% > DEF%.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/clara-best-light-cone-hsr-build-guide/inline-5.webp)


Mastering Clara requires understanding the timing of her Ultimate, "Promise, Not Command." This ability reduces damage taken by Clara and enhances the next two counters launched by Svarog. These enhanced counters trigger even if an *ally* is hit, and they deal splash damage to adjacent targets.

### Ultimate Timing and Energy Management
The most common mistake is using Clara's Ultimate during her own turn. To maximize the 2-turn duration, you should ideally use her Ultimate **immediately after her turn ends**. This ensures she keeps the damage reduction and enhanced counter stacks for the longest possible duration of the enemy's action cycle.

**Energy Regeneration Breakdown:**
Clara has a 110 Energy cost.
*   **Skill:** 30 Energy
*   **Basic ATK:** 20 Energy
*   **Counter-Attack:** 5 Energy
*   **Being Hit:** 5 to 15 Energy (depending on the enemy attack)
*   **Ultimate (Enhanced) Counter:** 5 Energy

With Tingyun’s Ultimate (50 Energy) and a few hits from enemies, Clara can comfortably maintain a 2-turn or 3-turn Ultimate rotation.

### Skill vs. Basic Attack Priority
In most scenarios, Clara should use her Skill every turn if Skill Points (SP) allow. Her Skill deals significant AoE damage and benefits from the "Mark of Counter." However, if you are running an SP-hungry support like Bronya or Sparkle, Clara is perfectly capable of playing SP-neutral (alternating Skill and Basic) or even SP-positive, as the majority of her damage comes from Svarog's out-of-turn counters.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/clara-best-light-cone-hsr-build-guide/inline-6.webp)


**Should I use Speed or ATK boots on Clara?**
ATK boots are almost always better. High Speed causes Clara to "run out" of her Ultimate buffs and Robin’s buffs too quickly. By staying slow, she remains under the protection of her damage reduction for more enemy attacks.

**Is her signature Light Cone worth buying from the Starlight Exchange shop?**
If you already have *On the Fall of an Aeon* at S5, the upgrade to *Something Irreplaceable* is marginal. It is generally better to save your 600 Undying Starlight for Star Rail Special Passes or Bronya's signature Light Cone. However, if you struggle with Clara dying in high-level content, the healing from her signature is very valuable.

**Does Clara work with March 7th?**
Yes, March 7th provides a shield that increases aggro. However, be careful: March 7th’s Ultimate has a high chance to Freeze enemies. Frozen enemies cannot attack, which means Clara cannot counter. In high-level play, Lynx is a superior partner.

**What is Clara's best Eidolon?**
Her E1, "A Tall Figure's Back," is her most impactful. It prevents the "Mark of Counter" from being removed after Clara uses her Skill. This turns her Skill into a permanent high-damage AoE nuke, significantly increasing her DPS in multi-target scenarios like Pure Fiction.

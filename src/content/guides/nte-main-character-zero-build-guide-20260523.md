# Neverness to Everness Zero Build Guide: The Best Main Character Build

Zero serves as the anchor for most early-game and meta-level team compositions in Neverness to Everness (NTE). While often viewed as a "starter" unit, Zero possesses a unique mechanical advantage that makes them indispensable even in high-level endgame content: the ability to force elemental reactions through instant meter generation.

## Overview

![Overview](/img/nte-main-character-zero-build-guide-20260523/inline-1.webp)


Zero is classified as a Cosmos-attribute Main DPS or Rotation Accelerator. Their primary utility lies in their ability to manipulate the Esper Cycle, the core combat system of NTE. Unlike other characters who must spend significant time on-field to build their meter, Zero can bridge the gap between different elemental phases with a single button press.

### Zero's Role in the Meta
In the current version 1.0 meta, Zero is frequently utilized as a "Quick-Swap" DPS. Because their damage is heavily front-loaded into their Redirect Skill and Ultimate, they do not require the extended field time that characters like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or Baicang might need. Instead, Zero enters the field, triggers a massive reaction, drops their Ultimate, and swaps out to allow supports to reset.

### Understanding the Insta-Cycle Mechanic
The core of Zero’s kit is the "Insta-Cycle" capability tied to their Redirect Skill, **Appraise and Engrave**. While most characters generate Cycle Energy through basic attacks and standard skills, Zero’s Redirect Skill has a fixed Cycle Rate of 100. This means using the skill instantly fills the Esper Meter, allowing for an immediate character swap to trigger a powerful reaction like Blossom (Cosmos + Anima) or Remora (Cosmos + Lakshana).

### Zero Skill Breakdown

*   **Basic Attack: Appraisal (Severance):** A sequence of up to five sword strikes. While not the primary source of damage, the final hit provides a minor boost to energy regeneration.
*   **Redirect Skill: Appraise and Engrave:** Zero performs three weapon strikes followed by an aerial Esper Cannon blast. This skill deals AoE Cosmos damage and instantly fills the Esper Meter to 100%.
*   **Ultimate: Divide by Zero:** Zero executes a wide-range blade dance that hits multiple times. This is the primary burst tool, benefiting significantly from Cosmos DMG and ATK buffs.
*   **Support Skill / QTE: Rift Blossom:** Triggers when Zero is swapped in during an active Esper Cycle. Zero slashes the area for two instances of AoE Cosmos damage.
*   **Passive: Appraiser:** Restores HP to Zero (equal to 50% of base ATK) whenever the active character gains Ultimate Energy.

## Best Weapons

![Best Weapons](/img/nte-main-character-zero-build-guide-20260523/inline-2.webp)


In Neverness to Everness, weapons are known as "Arcs." Zero requires Arcs that prioritize ATK, CRIT stats, and Cosmos DMG. Because Zero relies on their Ultimate for burst, Arcs with Charge Efficiency are also highly valued.

### Top Tier: S-Rank Arcs

**Day Off (Best-in-Slot)**
The "Day Off" Arc is widely considered the best weapon for Zero, particularly because it can be obtained for free. It provides a massive 30% ATK boost and features the "Eclipse" passive. When Eclipse is active, every enemy defeated restores 6 Ultimate Energy (up to 5 times per 300s cooldown). This allows Zero to chain Ultimates more effectively during mob encounters.

**The Rain That Shook the World (Alternative S-Rank)**
This Arc is the premier choice for players looking to maximize Zero's personal damage. It provides a 30% increase to Cosmos DMG for both the Redirect Skill and Ultimate. Additionally, it increases Zero’s Cycle Intensity by 36 for 15 seconds after casting a Redirect Skill, which directly scales the damage of the resulting Esper Cycle reaction.

**Ready-Ready (Plasma-type Alternative)**
While primarily associated with Nanally, "Ready-Ready" is a viable S-rank alternative for Zero due to its high base ATK (570 at max) and 24% CRIT Rate substat. Its passive increases Basic Attack and Critical Riposte damage by 15% after using a Redirect Skill or Ultimate.

### Budget & F2P: A-Rank Alternatives

**Fluff of Fearlessness**
For players who have not yet completed the quest for "Day Off," "Fluff of Fearlessness" is the strongest A-rank option. It grants a reliable ATK boost for 10 seconds after casting an Ultimate. While its scaling is lower than S-rank Arcs, it is easy to refine to Mixing Level 5 (T5) through standard gameplay.

### How to Obtain the "Day Off" Arc for Free
The "Day Off" Arc is a reward for the hidden quest **"Naked Ghost Tour of the City."** To start this quest, you must find the anomaly named Eclipse at the BaC Headquarters (Area M10) during the morning hours (set time to 08:00). You will then follow a 6-step chain involving specific locations like the Film Orbit Studio roof and the Tikal Bridge. Completing the final dialogue at BaC Headquarters grants the S-Rank Arc Disk.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

The gear system in NTE consists of Cassettes (Disk sets) and Driver Blocks (Tetris-style stat modules). Balancing these two systems is critical for reaching Zero's damage ceiling.

### Optimal Cassette Set Bonuses

**Lost Radiance (Recommended Set)**
The "Lost Radiance" set is the gold standard for Cosmos DPS units.
*   **2-Piece Bonus:** Increases Cosmos DMG by 10%.
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** After casting an Ultimate, the wearer ignores 25% of enemy DEF for 20 seconds.
This set perfectly aligns with Zero's rotation, as you typically cast the Ultimate right before your main burst window or before swapping to another character to continue the assault.

**[Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-23] (Crescent Set)**
While some early testers mentioned a "Crescent" set for Cosmos damage, current meta data prioritizes Lost Radiance for its superior DEF penetration. If the Crescent set is found to provide Light-based ability damage or stacking ATK buffs, it may serve as an alternative for specific hybrid builds.

### Driver Block Sub-stat Priority
The Driver Block system allows you to slot Tetris-shaped pieces into your character's "Console." For Zero, you should prioritize Type III modules (3-block pieces) to maximize stat density.

1.  **Cosmos DMG Bonus:** Essential for scaling all of Zero's active abilities.
2.  **CRIT Rate / CRIT DMG:** Aim for a 1:2 ratio (e.g., 60% CRIT Rate to 120% CRIT DMG).
3.  **ATK%:** Necessary for the base scaling of the "Appraiser" passive healing.
4.  **Cycle Intensity:** Increases the damage dealt by Blossom and Remora reactions triggered by Zero.

## Team Comp

![Team Comp](/img/nte-main-character-zero-build-guide-20260523/inline-4.webp)


Zero is a versatile unit that fits into almost any team requiring a Cosmos anchor. However, they shine most in teams that utilize the **Blossom** (Cosmos + Anima) or **Remora** (Cosmos + Lakshana) reactions.

### The "Full Cycle" Meta Team
This team is currently the highest damage-per-second (DPS) composition in version 1.0.
*   **Zero (Main/Quick-Swap DPS):** Triggers instant cycles and provides Cosmos burst.
*   **Nanally (Anima Sub-DPS):** Provides massive off-field damage and benefits from Zero's Blossom triggers.
*   **[Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide) (Incantation Support):** Provides crowd control and ATK buffs.
*   **Haniel (Psyche Battery/Buff):** Generates energy for the team and applies Psyche debuffs to enemies.

### Best F2P Team for Zero
This team uses characters given for free or easily obtained early in the story.
*   **Zero (Main DPS):** Core damage dealer.
*   **Mint (Anima Sub-DPS):** Enables the Blossom reaction.
*   **Adler (Incantation Shield/Support):** Provides "Blessing" shields and defensive utility.
*   **Haniel (Free Support):** Provided via story progression to act as the team's primary buffer.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/nte-main-character-zero-build-guide-20260523/inline-5.webp)


To maximize Zero's potential, you must respect the internal cooldowns of the Esper Cycle and ensure you are utilizing the "Insta-Cycle" at the correct moment.

### Standard Combat Opener
1.  **Start with Haniel:** Use Haniel's Skill to apply the Psyche attribute and generate initial energy.
2.  **Swap to Sakiri/Adler:** Deploy Sakiri's "Hold Skill" to gather enemies or Adler's Skill to provide a shield.
3.  **Enter Zero:** Use Zero's Redirect Skill, **Appraise and Engrave**. This instantly fills the Esper Meter.
4.  **Trigger Reaction:** Immediately swap to an Anima character (like Nanally or Mint) or a Lakshana character (like Skia) to trigger the Esper Cycle reaction.
5.  **Unleash Ultimate:** Swap back to Zero when their Ultimate is ready to cast **Divide by Zero** during the reaction window for maximum damage.

### Maximizing Burst Windows
The "Reaction Window" lasts for several seconds after an Esper Cycle is triggered. During this time, enemies often take increased damage or are affected by status ailments like Remora's slow. Always aim to have Zero's Ultimate available during these windows. If you are using the **Lost Radiance** set, you should cast the Ultimate first to trigger the 25% DEF ignore before following up with the Redirect Skill.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/nte-main-character-zero-build-guide-20260523/inline-6.webp)


**How do I unlock Zero?**
Zero is the default protagonist of Neverness to Everness. You receive them automatically after completing the opening prologue in the Miguel District.

**Is Zero's gender changeable?**
Yes. At the start of the game, players can choose between the male or female version of the Investigator. This choice is purely cosmetic and does not affect stats or skills.

**Can Zero use other elements?**
As of version 1.0 (May 2026), Zero is locked to the Cosmos attribute. While the story hints at further "Anomaly Adaptations," there is currently no system for switching elements.

**What is the best free weapon for Zero?**
The "Day Off" S-rank Arc is the best free option. It is obtained through the "Naked Ghost Tour of the City" quest chain.

**What is the "Esper Cycle"?**
The Esper Cycle is NTE's elemental reaction system. When you fill the meter and swap characters, you trigger a reaction based on the elements of the characters involved. Zero's ability to fill this meter instantly makes them the fastest character for cycling through these reactions.

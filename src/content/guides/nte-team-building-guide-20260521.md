## Overview

![Overview](/img/nte-team-building-guide-20260521/inline-1.webp)


A standard team in Neverness to Everness consists of four characters, each filling a specific niche to ensure the squad can handle both mob clear and boss stagger windows. Unlike traditional role-based RPGs, the "Main DPS" in NTE often relies heavily on the "Glow Swap" indicators triggered by their teammates to reach peak efficiency.

### The Esper Cycle Explained

The core of NTE combat is the Esper Cycle. As you deal damage with Basic Attacks and Skills, you accumulate Cycle Energy. Once a character’s energy is full, their icon on the right side of the screen will glow. Swapping to a glowing character triggers an "Entrance Skill" that often applies unique elemental debuffs or provides immediate burst damage. Maintaining a high "Cycle Rate" stat on your supports is essential for keeping this loop active, as it allows for near-constant invulnerability frames during swap animations.

### Core Combat Roles

Successful team compositions generally follow a structured role distribution:

*   **Main DPS:** The primary field-time consumer who deals the bulk of the damage during "Stagger" windows.
*   **Sub-DPS/Battery:** Characters who provide off-field damage or generate energy for the team’s Ultimates.
*   **Break Specialist:** Espers with high "Break Intensity" stats designed to deplete the enemy's white Stagger bar.
*   **Support/Sustain:** Characters focused on healing, shielding, or providing massive stat buffs to the Main DPS.

### Understanding Break and Stagger Windows

Enemies in NTE possess a Break Gauge. While this gauge is active, they take significantly reduced damage and resist most hit-stun. Break Specialists like [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide) are vital for shattering this gauge. Once broken, the enemy enters a "Stagger" state, taking [information unavailable] increased damage for a short duration. This is the "Punish Window" where you should unload your Ultimates and highest-damage Arcs.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

![Best Disks (Artifacts)](/img/nte-team-building-guide-20260521/inline-3.webp)


The gear system in NTE is divided into the "Console System," which uses Cartridges (Set bonuses) and Modules (individual stat pieces). Modules come in various geometric shapes—similar to Tetris pieces—and must be fitted into a limited grid on the character's Console.

### Understanding Cartridges vs. Modules

Cartridges provide the "Set Bonus." You can activate a 2-piece Epic bonus or a 4-piece Legendary bonus. Modules are the individual pieces you slot into the Cartridge grid. Each Module has a "Type" (II, III, or IV) which determines its primary stat. For example, Type II Modules are the only ones that can roll Critical Rate as a primary stat.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/nte-team-building-guide-20260521/inline-5.webp)


Maximizing your damage in Neverness to Everness requires a strict adherence to the "Glow Swap" timing. Missing a swap window results in a significant loss of Cycle Energy and reaction damage.

### Standard Combat Loop

1.  **Setup:** Start with your Support/Sustain character. Use their Skill to apply initial buffs or shields.
2.  **Element Priming:** Switch to your Sub-DPS. Use their Skill to apply an elemental aura (e.g., Anima or Incantation) to the enemy.
3.  **The Reaction Loop:** Watch the character icons. As soon as your Main DPS icon glows, swap them in. This triggers the Entrance Skill and initiates a reaction like Blossom or Scorch.
4.  **Field Time:** Perform the Main DPS combo until their Skill is off cooldown. Use the Skill, then look for the next Glow Swap indicator (usually the Break Specialist).

### Maximizing Stagger Damage

When the enemy’s Break Gauge is nearly empty, ensure your Main DPS has their Ultimate ready. Once the "BREAK" text appears on screen:
1.  Swap to your Support to apply any "Damage Taken Increased" debuffs.
2.  Swap immediately to your Main DPS.
3.  Activate the Ultimate.
4.  Spam high-damage skills until the gauge refills.

### Energy Management and Ultimate Timing

Do not use all four Ultimates at once. In NTE, Ultimates provide a brief period of time-dilation. Spacing them out allows you to manipulate the boss’s attack patterns and gives your Skill cooldowns time to reset. If you are running a "Liquid" Arc, your goal is to funnel all energy particles into your Main DPS to ensure their Ultimate is available for every Stagger window.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/nte-team-building-guide-20260521/inline-6.webp)


**How does the gacha system work for new characters?**
Neverness to Everness features a 90-pull "No 50/50" system for limited character banners. If you reach 90 pulls on a banner like "The Ichi-daime" (Nanally's debut banner), you are guaranteed the featured S-Rank character.

**Where is the best place to farm Cartridges?**
Cartridges and the Modules needed to power them are found in Rabbit Hole Anomaly Zones. These zones rotate daily, offering different elemental sets. You will need Carrota Coins to claim rewards from the final chest.

**Which free characters are worth high investment?**
Haniel and Esper Zero remain top-tier even into the late game. Haniel’s crowd control is unrivaled in mob-heavy floors, while Esper Zero’s versatility allows them to fit into almost any elemental reaction team.

**How do I get better gear drops?**
Gear rarity is tied to your Hunter Level and Appraisal Level. Increasing your Hunter Level through story quests and side activities unlocks higher difficulty tiers in the Rabbit Hole, which eventually drop S-Rank Legendary Cartridges.

## Overview

![Overview](/img/nte-leveling-guide-beginners/inline-1.webp)


Progression in Neverness to Everness (NTE) is bifurcated into two distinct tracks: your account-wide Hunter Level and your world-facing Appraisal Level. While your Hunter Level represents your total experience gained from missions and exploration, your Appraisal Level dictates the difficulty of the world, the quality of loot drops, and the maximum level cap for your Espers and Arcs.

### Hunter Level vs. Appraisal Level

To progress effectively, you must understand that Hunter Level experience is the fuel, while Appraisal Level milestones are the gates. You gain Hunter Level EXP by completing Episode missions (main story), Spinoffs, and daily tasks in the Exploration Guide. Once you reach specific Hunter Level thresholds, you must complete an Ascension Quest to raise your Appraisal Level. Raising this level is mandatory to continue strengthening your team, as it unlocks higher level caps for your characters.

| Hunter Level | Appraisal Level | Esper/Weapon Max Level | Key Unlock / Note |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 10 | 1 | 20 | Initial World Rank |
| 20 | 2 | 30 | Ascension Quest Required |
| 30 | 3 | 40 | Unlocks full Cartridge/Console loop |
| 40 | 4 | 50 | Ascension Quest Required |
| 45 | 5 | 60 | Higher Anomaly Zone rewards |
| 50 | 6 | 70 | Ascension Quest (Endgame prep) |
| 55 | 7 | 80 | Maximum world difficulty |

### The City Tycoon System

The City Tycoon system is NTE’s robust life-simulation backbone, unlocked via the "Good Morning, Hethereau" quest where you interact with Chiz at the Pink Paws Bank. Unlike combat, which uses "Character Pixels" (daily stamina), City Tycoon uses "City Stamina," which resets weekly. 

Prioritizing this system is essential for beginners because it provides a massive stream of Fons—the currency required for high-level upgrades. At Tycoon Level 4, you unlock the **Cafe by Origen**, which generates passive income. By reaching Tycoon Level 18, you receive the S-Rank character **Chiz** for free, followed by her signature weapon at Level 21.

### Stamina Management: Anomaly Zones

Your primary combat stamina is called Character Pixels. You should never let these cap out. Spending Pixels in Anomaly Zones or against World Bosses like the Headless Rider or Black Tome provides the materials needed for "Ascending" your characters. In the early game (Hunter Level 1–29), focus your Pixels on guaranteed upgrades: character levels, Arc levels, and skill levels. Do not heavily farm Cartridges (artifacts) until you reach Hunter Level 30, as the drop rates for Legendary-tier gear are significantly higher at that milestone.

## Best Weapons

![Best Weapons](/img/nte-leveling-guide-beginners/inline-2.webp)


In Hethereau, weapons are referred to as **Arcs**. These are categorized into five types: Plasma, Solid, Gas, Liquid, and Synthesis. Matching the Arc type to an Esper’s preferred style is critical because it ensures the character can utilize the Arc's unique passive effects and stat synergies.

### Top Tier S-Rank Arcs

S-Rank Arcs are the gold standard for damage and utility. Many of these can be obtained for free by defeating specific Anomaly Bosses for the first time or through City Tycoon progression.

### Best F2P Arcs for Beginners

For players who haven't yet secured limited Arcs from the Scarborough Fair gacha, several high-value options are available through gameplay. The **Contemplative Cat** is the best free option for Cosmos characters like Chiz or Zero, as it scales directly with your wealth in the City Tycoon system. 

Another excellent starter choice is **Good Boy's Grand Adventure**, which provides a massive 45% ATK substat and increases Charge Efficiency, making it perfect for supports who need to cast their Ultimates frequently. If you are using Haniel, the **Blow up the Crowd** Arc is obtainable from the "Rhythm Master" Anomaly Commission in Illusion Town and provides excellent off-field buffs for your main carry.

### Arc Refinement and Scaling

Scaling an Arc involves two processes: Leveling and Mixing. Leveling increases the base ATK and substat, while Mixing (refining) requires duplicate copies of the Arc to enhance the percentage values of its unique passive. You can obtain duplicates for many free S-Rank Arcs, such as **Eternal Waltz** and **Song of the Whale**, by spending Tri-Keys in the Arc Selection shop found in the Mall.

## Best Disks (Cartridges)

![Best Disks (Cartridges)](/img/nte-leveling-guide-beginners/inline-3.webp)


The gear system in NTE revolves around **Cartridges**, which are slotted into a **Console** grid. This is not a simple equipment screen; it is a Tetris-like puzzle where you must fit different shaped **Modules** to activate set bonuses.

### Understanding Cartridges and Modules

A Console consists of a grid and a central Cartridge that defines the set bonus. Modules come in various shapes (L-shape, Z-shape, T-shape, etc.) and must be placed onto the grid to satisfy the Cartridge's activation requirements. For example, a 4-piece set bonus might require you to fit four specific modules into the grid without overlapping.

### The Console Grid System

NTE is remarkably player-friendly regarding stats; all four sub-stats on a Module are visible immediately upon acquisition. You do not need to level a piece to see if it is "trash." When building your Console, prioritize the following stats:
1. **Elemental DMG Bonus** (matches your Esper's element)
2. **Crit Rate / Crit DMG** (for DPS characters)
3. **ATK%** (universal scaling)
4. **Break Intensity** (for Breaker characters like Daffodill)

## Team Comp

![Team Comp](/img/nte-leveling-guide-beginners/inline-4.webp)


The core of NTE combat is the **Esper Cycle**, a system of elemental reactions triggered by swapping characters when their meter is full. Teams should be built with a Main DPS, a Breaker to shred enemy stagger bars, and two supports to facilitate reactions.

### The Hypercarry Meta: Nanally & Zero

The current Patch 1.0 gold standard is the **Hyper Nanally** team. This composition focuses on the **Blossom** reaction (Cosmos + Anima).
*   **Main DPS:** Nanally (Anima)
*   **Sub-DPS/Support:** Hotori (Cosmos)
*   **Support:** Jiuyuan (Anima)
*   **Healer/Utility:** Mint (Anima)

In this team, Hotori provides time-stop utility and triggers Blossom, which generates "Vita Buds" that fire homing projectiles at enemies. Nanally then enters her "Ichi-daime's Authority" state to deal massive sustained damage.

### Best F2P Starter Team

For players relying on the initial roster, the **Zero-Edgar-Haniel** core is incredibly resilient.
*   **Main DPS:** Zero (Cosmos)
*   **Breaker:** Daffodill (Chaos) - *Obtainable from the S-Class Selector.*
*   **Support:** Haniel (Psyche)
*   **Healer:** Edgar (HP/Physical)

This team utilizes the **Nova** reaction (Chaos + Psyche) to debuff enemies and the **Discord** trio reaction (Chaos + Psyche + Incantation) if you can flex in an Incantation character like Baicang.

### Elemental Reactions (The Esper Cycle)

Reactions occur when you swap from one element to another with a full Esper Meter.
*   **Blossom (Cosmos + Anima):** Spawns Vita Buds that fire follow-up attacks.
*   **Scorch (Incantation + Chaos):** Applies a 15-second Damage-over-Time (DoT) effect.
*   **Nova (Chaos + Psyche):** Applies a debuff that explodes for Mental Damage after 5 seconds.
*   **Discord (Trio: Chaos + Psyche + Incantation):** Requires both Scorch and Nova to be active. It dramatically accelerates the depletion of the enemy's Break meter.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/nte-leveling-guide-beginners/inline-5.webp)


Mastering a rotation in NTE is about managing the "swap-in" triggers to maximize the uptime of your Main DPS's strongest state.

### Nanally: The Underboss Loop

Nanally's damage peaks when her "Underboss" is active.
1.  **Setup:** Use Hotori’s skill to build the Esper Meter and apply Cosmos.
2.  **Trigger:** Swap to Nanally to trigger **Blossom**.
3.  **Authority:** Use Nanally’s Redirect Skill (*Colucci Howling Technique*) to enter the **Ichi-daime's Authority** state. This grants a 30% Crit DMG bonus and allows her to walk on walls.
4.  **Burst:** Activate Nanally’s Ultimate to summon the **Underboss**. For the next 6 seconds, the Underboss will mirror every attack Nanally makes, effectively doubling her damage output.

### Hotori: Time-Stop and Momentum

[Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-21 regarding the specific "Replay" mechanic name], but her core loop involves her **Non-Closed Timepiece**.
1.  Build energy for the Timepiece using basic attacks.
2.  Activate her Ultimate to slow time to a near standstill.
3.  Execute katana strikes to build **Momentum**.
4.  Once Momentum is maxed, a massive Cosmos DMG burst occurs, ending the time-stop. This burst should be timed to hit just as your Main DPS is ready to swap back in.

### Quickswap Energy Management

Always cycle through your supports to apply debuffs like **Stain** (Psyche + Lakshana) before committing to a Main DPS burst. The Stain reaction increases the Psyche and Lakshana damage the enemy takes by 20%, which is vital for amplifying the damage of characters like Haniel or Hathor.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/nte-leveling-guide-beginners/inline-6.webp)


### Rerolling Guide

Rerolling in NTE is recommended if you are aiming for a specific high-tier support like **Jiuyuan** or the premier breaker **Daffodill**. The process takes approximately 20 minutes. However, because the "Scarborough Fair" gacha system lacks a 50/50 mechanic—guaranteeing the featured S-Rank like Nanally at 90 rolls—rerolling is less stressful than in other titles.

### Fons and Currency Farming

Fons are primarily farmed through the **City Tycoon** system.
*   **Cafe Management:** The most reliable passive source.
*   **Pink Paws Heist:** A high-yield activity unlocked at Tycoon Level 11.
*   **Safe Cracking:** Found during world exploration in various Hethereau districts.

### Endgame Content: Beyond the Rail

Once you reach Hunter Level 50, you unlock the highest difficulty of the **Anomaly Zones**. This is where you farm Legendary (Gold) Cartridges. The ultimate challenge is the **Beyond the Rail** mode, a multi-stage combat trial that requires two fully built teams. Focus on building a "Discord" team for one half and a "Blossom" team for the other to cover all elemental resistances.

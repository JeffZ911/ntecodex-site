# Best Budget IEMs for Gacha Grinders Under $100

The Truthear x Crinacle Zero:RED is the best overall for neutral sound and long-session comfort. For maximum detail, the Simgot EA500LM is the top technical performer under $90.

Mobile and PC gacha players seeking high-fidelity audio for 4-8 hour daily sessions on a budget under $100 need hardware that balances technical resolution with physical comfort. Whether you are farming materials in the rainy streets of Hesperia or optimizing your character's gear, the right pair of In-Ear Monitors (IEMs) provides a level of immersion that standard wireless earbuds cannot match.

### TRUTHEAR x Crinacle Zero:RED
*   **Price:** $55
*   **Rating:** 4.5/5
*   **Best For:** Neutral sound and long-session comfort

The Truthear Zero:RED is a refined successor to the original Zero, developed in collaboration with renowned audio reviewer Crinacle. This model features a dual dynamic driver setup, utilizing a 10mm driver for bass and a 7.8mm driver for mids and highs. This separation ensures that low-frequency effects, such as the engine roar of a customized vehicle in Neverness to Everness (NTE), do not muddle the clarity of character dialogue.

**Pros:**
*   The dual dynamic driver (10mm + 7.8mm) configuration provides clean bass separation without bleeding into the midrange.
*   Includes a 10-ohm impedance adapter for optional bass boosting, allowing players to customize the impact of supernatural explosions.
*   The smooth treble response prevents ear fatigue, which is essential for 6-hour grinding sessions during limited-time events.

**Cons:**
*   The large 6.7mm nozzle diameter can be uncomfortable for players with smaller ear canals.
*   A bulky shell design means they protrude significantly from the ear, making them less ideal for side-sleepers or those who play while lying down.

**Verdict:**
The Zero:RED utilizes a dual-driver crossover that mimics the performance of much more expensive monitors. Its 17.5-ohm impedance makes it easy to drive directly from a phone or tablet during mobile grinding sessions without requiring a dedicated amplifier.

### Simgot EA500LM
*   **Price:** $89
*   **Rating:** 4.7/5
*   **Best For:** Technical detail and build quality

For players who want to hear every nuance of the Unreal Engine 5 environment in NTE, the Simgot EA500LM is a top-tier choice. It features a Lithium-Magnesium dome diaphragm, a material typically reserved for flagship headphones, which offers exceptional transient response. This means fast-paced combat sounds remain crisp and distinct even when multiple Esper abilities are triggering simultaneously.

**Pros:**
*   Lithium-Magnesium dome diaphragm provides exceptional transient response for sharp combat audio.
*   Three pairs of swappable tuning nozzles allow users to customize the sound signature between balanced and more aggressive profiles.
*   The all-metal construction feels significantly more premium than plastic competitors in the sub-$100 bracket.

**Cons:**
*   A bright upper-midrange peak can be fatiguing during high-volume gameplay, especially during repetitive farming tasks.
*   Heavy zinc alloy shells may cause "ear-drag" or sagging if the cable cinch isn't tightened properly.

**Verdict:**
The EA500LM is a technical powerhouse featuring a second-generation DMDC driver. Its ability to resolve fine details in complex JRPG soundtracks makes it the best choice for players who prioritize audio clarity over pure bass impact.

This is particularly useful for identifying subtle audio cues in NTE, such as the sound of a Nanally ability charging up in the heat of battle.

**Pros:**

**Cons:**

**Verdict:**
The Dioko is one of the most affordable ways to experience planar magnetic technology. While its ergonomics are polarizing, the 14.6mm driver provides a level of transparency that dynamic drivers in this price tier struggle to match.

## What a gacha grinder actually needs

![What a gacha grinder actually needs](/img/best-budget-iems-for-gacha-grinders-under-100/inline-2.webp)


Grinding in Neverness to Everness involves repetitive tasks, from daily commissions to farming Anomalies for upgrade materials. This type of gameplay places specific demands on audio gear that a competitive FPS player might not face.

**Fatigue-free tuning** is the most critical factor. In games like NTE, high-pitched sound effects from menus or specific Esper abilities can become piercing over time. IEMs with a smooth treble response, like the Truthear Zero:RED, allow you to play for hours without experiencing "ear bleed" or headaches.

**Ergonomic stability** is equally important. Gacha gaming is often a "secondary" activity—you might be playing on your phone while commuting or relaxing on a couch. The shells must be lightweight and secure enough to stay in place. A heavy metal shell like the EA500LM provides a premium feel but might require more frequent adjustment if you are moving around.

Lastly, **Sensitivity** determines how loud the IEM gets on your device. Most modern smartphones have limited power output. An IEM with a low impedance (typically under 32 ohms) ensures that the bass remains punchy and the volume is sufficient even when using a basic phone dongle.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/best-budget-iems-for-gacha-grinders-under-100/inline-4.webp)


While these budget IEMs offer a massive improvement over standard phone speakers or cheap earbuds, they aren't for everyone. If you already own a pair of mid-range open-back headphones, such as the Sennheiser HD 560S, these IEMs will likely feel like a side-grade. Open-back headphones generally offer a much larger "soundstage," making the world of Hesperia feel wider and more open.

Furthermore, do not buy these if you require Active Noise Cancelling (ANC). While IEMs provide passive isolation by physically plugging your ear canal, they cannot block out the low-frequency drone of an airplane engine or a noisy bus as effectively as wireless sets like the Sony WF-1000XM5. If your grinding happens in extremely loud public environments, ANC might be more valuable than pure audio fidelity.

Finally, if your ears are physically sensitive to pressure, "in-ear" monitors may cause discomfort regardless of the nozzle size. In these cases, sticking to over-ear headphones or bone conduction sets is a better path for long-term ear health.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need a special adapter to use these IEMs with my phone?
Most modern smartphones have removed the 3.5mm headphone jack, so you will likely need a USB-C or Lightning to 3.5mm "dongle" adapter. For these budget IEMs, the standard Apple or Samsung dongles provide plenty of power and clean audio conversion.

### Are IEMs better than gaming headsets for Neverness to Everness?
Yes, in terms of pure audio quality and detail per dollar, IEMs typically outperform gaming headsets under $100. They provide better passive noise isolation and a more accurate soundstage, which helps in identifying the supernatural Anomalies found throughout Hesperia.

### How do I make my IEMs last longer during daily grinding?
The best way to extend the life of your IEMs is to store them in a hard case when not in use and avoid pulling on the cable. Since all the models recommended here have detachable cables, you can easily replace the wire if it begins to fray or lose connection over time.

### Can I use these IEMs for voice chat in NTE?
Most of these IEMs come with a standard cable that does not include a microphone. If you plan to use voice chat while playing with friends, you may need to purchase a separate "tripowin" cable with an inline mic or use a dedicated desktop microphone.

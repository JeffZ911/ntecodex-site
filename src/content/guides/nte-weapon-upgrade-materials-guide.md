## Overview

![Overview](/img/nte-weapon-upgrade-materials-guide/inline-1.webp)


In Neverness to Everness (NTE), weapons are referred to as Arcs. These Arcs are categorized into five distinct types: Plasma, Liquid, Gas, Solid, and Synthesis. Each type aligns with specific Esper roles and elemental affinities, dictating how damage scales and which resonance bonuses trigger during combat. For instance, Plasma Arcs generally favor high-frequency attackers, while Liquid Arcs often provide utility or critical damage modifiers.

The progression system for Arcs is divided into three primary paths. Enhancement is the standard leveling process using experience materials. Ascension is the process of breaking through level caps at specific intervals. Finally, Mixing allows players to consume duplicate Arcs to improve the "Mixing Intensity," which directly upgrades the weapon's unique passive skills.

### Arc Upgrade Currencies

To facilitate these upgrades, you will need a variety of currencies and specialized materials. While some are earned through general exploration, others are tied to specific daily or weekly activities.

| Currency | Primary Use | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Beetle Coins** | General Arc leveling and Ascension | Daily Commissions, City Tycoon activities |
| **Shell Credits** | Universal growth currency for all systems | Combat encounters, Side quests, Shops |
| **Annulith** | High-tier material processing | Weekly Wealth Board, Specialized Anomaly Hunts |
| **Tri-Keys** | Unlocking Arc Research Program rewards | Annulith processing, Limited-time events |

### Level Cap Thresholds

Arcs cannot be leveled indefinitely from the start. You must increase your Appraisal Level (player rank) to unlock the ability to ascend your weapons past certain milestones.

*   **Level 20:** Requires Appraisal Level 10.
*   **Level 40:** Requires Appraisal Level 25.
*   **Level 60:** Requires Appraisal Level 40.
*   **Level 80:** Requires Appraisal Level 55 (Current Launch Cap).

### How to Enhance Arcs with Dye
Dye is the primary experience material used to level up Arcs. It comes in various rarities, with higher-tier Dye providing significantly more experience per unit. To farm Dye efficiently, you should focus on the Kaleidoscope stage located within Houdinii's Magic Stage. This repeatable challenge scales with your Appraisal Level, offering better rewards as you progress. Higher-level players should prioritize the "Prismatic Prism" difficulty for the best Dye-to-Stamina ratio.

### The Arc Ascension Process
Once an Arc reaches its current level cap, you must perform an Ascension to continue leveling. This process requires a combination of Beetle Coins and specific boss or mob drops. The Bubble Can Factory is the essential farming location for these materials. Each Arc type typically requires a specific "Can" variant (e.g., Plasma Cans for Plasma Arcs). Rare materials like Golden Appleseed are also required for S-Rank Arcs at higher Ascension tiers.

### Mixing Intensity: Strengthening Passive Skills
Mixing is the NTE equivalent of refinement. By feeding a duplicate copy of an Arc into the main weapon, you increase its Mixing Intensity (up to level 5). Each level of Intensity boosts the percentages found in the Arc's passive description. For S-Rank weapons like Ready-Ready, high Mixing Intensity is often the difference between a standard DPS and a top-tier meta build, as it can significantly increase Boss damage multipliers.

### Weapon Type Synergy (Plasma vs. Liquid)
Plasma Arcs are designed for "High-Output" scenarios. They often feature passives that trigger on hit counts or energy expenditure. Liquid Arcs, conversely, focus on "Flow" and "Precision," rewarding players for dodges, parries, and specific elemental reactions like Blossom. Using a matching weapon type grants a 10% bonus to the Arc's base ATK and HP stats, making it highly recommended to match types whenever possible.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

![Best Disks (Artifacts)](/img/nte-weapon-upgrade-materials-guide/inline-3.webp)


In Neverness to Everness, the artifact system is known as Cartridges. Each Esper can equip a set of Cartridges, which are further customized with Modules. Modules come in different "Geometries" (shapes), and fitting them into the Cartridge grid is a mini-game that determines your final stat spread.

### Top Cartridge Sets for DPS

*   **Fireflies and the Forest:** This is the premier set for both Anima and Plasma damage dealers.
    *   **2-pc Bonus:** Increases Anima/Plasma DMG by 15%.
    *   **4-pc Bonus:** When dealing damage to an enemy with an elemental status, CRIT DMG is increased by 8% for 10 seconds, stacking up to 7 times (56% total).
*   **Urban Echo:** A versatile set for sub-DPS characters that focuses on Energy Recharge and Support Skill damage.
    *   **2-pc Bonus:** Energy Recharge +20%.
    *   **4-pc Bonus:** Using a Support Skill increases the active character's ATK by 20% for 12 seconds.

### Module Optimization and Geometries
Modules are the individual stat sticks you slot into your Cartridges. They are categorized by Type (1, 2, or 3).
*   **Type 1 (Square):** Usually focuses on flat HP and ATK.
*   **Type 2 (Circle):** Usually focuses on DEF and Elemental Resistance.
*   **Type 3 (Triangle/Diamond):** These are the most valuable, containing CRIT Rate, CRIT DMG, and Elemental DMG % main stats.

For a main DPS like Nanally, you should prioritize Type 3 modules that overcap CRIT Rate if her passive requires it, or focus entirely on CRIT DMG if you are using an Arc like Ready-Ready that provides sufficient base stats.

### Recommended Substat Priority
1.  **CRIT Rate / CRIT DMG:** Maintain a 1:2 ratio for optimal damage.
2.  **ATK %:** Essential for scaling the base damage of Arcs.
3.  **Break Intensity:** Important for characters meant to shatter enemy defenses.
4.  **Energy Recharge:** Vital for characters with high-cost Ultimates like Hathor.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/nte-weapon-upgrade-materials-guide/inline-6.webp)


### Where can I find Golden Appleseed?
Golden Appleseed is a rare Ascension material required for S-Rank Arcs level 60 and above. You can obtain it by crafting it at the Alchemy Bench using lower-tier seeds or by purchasing it from the Hunter Exchange once you reach Tycoon Rank 6.

### How do I get more Tri-Keys for the Arc Research Program?
Tri-Keys are earned primarily through the weekly Wealth Board challenges. You can also obtain them by processing high-purity Annulith at the Research Terminal in the main hub. They are essential for unlocking the "Research" tiers of your Arcs, which provide permanent stat boosts.

### Can I use a Plasma Arc on a Liquid Esper?
Yes, any Esper can equip any Arc type. However, you will lose the "Type Synergy" bonus, which grants a 10% increase to base stats. Additionally, many Arc passives only trigger on specific elemental damage types, meaning a Plasma Arc's passive might not work at all on a Liquid Esper.

### What is the max level for Arcs?
As of the launch version (v1.0), the maximum level for Arcs is Level 80. This requires Appraisal Level 55 and the final Ascension tier materials found in the deep levels of the Bubble Can Factory.

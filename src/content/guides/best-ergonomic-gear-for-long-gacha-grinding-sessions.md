## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/best-ergonomic-gear-for-long-gacha-grinding-sessions/inline-4.webp)


While ergonomic gear provides significant benefits, it is not always a mandatory purchase. If you currently experience zero pain or stiffness after a 4-hour session, your current desk and chair geometry is likely already optimized for your body type. Ergonomics is highly individual; what works for a 6-foot player may not be necessary for someone who is 5-foot-2.

Furthermore, medical history plays a role in these decisions. You should not buy a standing desk if you have a history of varicose veins or plantar fasciitis without consulting a doctor, as prolonged standing can exacerbate these specific issues. Additionally, avoid high-end monitor arms if you use a lightweight 24-inch monitor. A simple stack of books or a basic riser often achieves the same ergonomic result for free, provided the center of the screen remains at eye level. The goal is the [neutral posture](https://www.logitech.com/en-us/products/mice/mx-vertical-ergonomic-mouse.910-005447.html), not the price tag of the equipment used to achieve it.

## What we didn't include and why

![What we didn't include and why](/img/best-ergonomic-gear-for-long-gacha-grinding-sessions/inline-5.webp)


In our research, several popular "gaming" products were intentionally omitted because they do not meet strict ergonomic standards. Racing-style gaming chairs were the first to be cut. Many of these chairs use "bucket seat" geometry that pushes the shoulders forward and rounds the upper back, which is counterproductive for long-term spinal health. Instead, we focused on tools that allow for a wide range of motion and support the natural curvature of the spine.

We also excluded high-button mice like the Razer Naga. While these are popular for MMOs, the dense thumb-grid can cause significant thumb fatigue in games like Neverness to Everness, which don't strictly require 12-button rotations. For NTE players, the focus is more on character switching and Duo/Trio Reactions, which are better served by a mouse that prioritizes wrist health over button density. Finally, budget gas-spring monitor arms were avoided. These cheaper alternatives often lose tension within 6 months, causing monitors to tilt downward during intense gameplay, which defeats the purpose of maintaining a consistent eye level.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How does the ZZZ Lighter morale stack affect wrist health?
Lighter’s mechanics require players to manage Morale stacks that trigger a Morale Burst at 80+ points. During this burst, the player must hold or rapidly click the basic attack button to perform a flurry of punches. This high-frequency input can lead to wrist fatigue, making an [ergonomic vertical mouse](https://www.logitech.com/en-us/products/mice/mx-vertical-ergonomic-mouse.910-005447.html) essential for players who main this character.

### Can a standing desk help with Neverness to Everness grinding?
Yes, a standing desk like the FlexiSpot E7 Pro allows you to switch between sitting and standing, which prevents blood pooling in the legs during long Esper Cycle sessions. This movement helps maintain alertness and reduces the pressure on the lower back that typically accumulates after several hours in a static seated position.

### Why is monitor height important for gacha games?
Long sessions of navigating urban environments like Hethereau or managing gacha boards in The Fair often lead to "gamer neck" if the monitor is too low. Using a monitor arm to keep the top third of the screen at eye level reduces the strain on the cervical spine, preventing chronic neck and shoulder pain.

### What is the best way to reduce eye strain during late-night NTE sessions?
Using a light bar like the BenQ ScreenBar Halo provides bias lighting that reduces the contrast between the bright screen and a dark room. This, combined with the high CRI > 95, ensures that the game's colors remain vibrant without the glare that causes ocular fatigue and headaches.

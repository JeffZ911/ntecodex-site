# Ayaka vs Ganyu: The Ultimate Cryo DPS Comparison Guide

Deciding between Kamisato Ayaka and Ganyu requires an understanding of how their damage is delivered. While both utilize the Cryo element to dominate the Spiral Abyss and the new Stygian Onslaught endgame mode, their mechanical execution and team requirements differ significantly. This guide breaks down their stats, optimal builds, and performance in the current 2026 meta.

## TL;DR Verdict

![TL;DR Verdict](/img/ayaka-vs-ganyu-dps-comparison/inline-1.webp)


Kamisato Ayaka remains the premier choice for players who prioritize "nuking" enemies. Her ability to dump over 4,000% [damage scaling](https://ntecodex.com/faq) into a five-second window makes her the undisputed queen of Freeze teams, especially when paired with modern supports like Furina or the recently released Escoffier. If you want a character who can delete a boss's health bar before it can move, Ayaka is your pick.

Ganyu, conversely, is the pinnacle of versatility. While her Freeze teams have faced challenges due to un-groupable enemies in the Natlan era, her Melt compositions have seen a massive resurgence thanks to Mavuika. Ganyu offers a "no-cooldown" playstyle where her primary damage comes from her Level 2 Charged Shots, making her immune to the Energy Recharge (ER) droughts that can cripple Ayaka.

## Detailed Comparison

![Detailed Comparison](/img/ayaka-vs-ganyu-dps-comparison/inline-3.webp)


To understand who provides more value to your roster, we must examine their damage windows and how they interact with the game's increasingly complex elemental systems.

### Playstyle and Mechanical Difficulty

Ayaka’s gameplay revolves around her unique alternate dash, **Kamisato Art: Senho**. Emerging from this dash infuses her sword with Cryo and grants a 10% Cryo DMG bonus. Her primary loop involves dashing to apply Cryo, using her Elemental Skill (**Kamisato Art: Hyouka**) to buff her Normal and Charged attacks by 30%, and then unleashing her Burst. On mobile and controllers, Ayaka is generally more comfortable because her Burst, **Kamisato Art: Sousetsu**, creates a Frostflake Seki no To that moves forward and locks onto enemies, requiring minimal manual aiming.

Ganyu utilizes a "Sniper" playstyle. Her Level 2 Charged Shot, the **Frostflake Arrow**, blooms upon impact to deal massive AoE Cryo damage. This requires the player to remain aimed for approximately two seconds per shot. In the high-mobility environments of Natlan, Ganyu is extremely vulnerable to being interrupted. Without a shield, her DPS drops to near zero if she is staggered during a charge. Consequently, Ganyu's mechanical difficulty is higher, as players must master "aim-canceling" and positioning to maximize her output.

### Burst Damage vs. Sustained DPS

The core of the "Ayaka vs Ganyu" debate is front-loaded vs. sustained damage. 

**Ayaka's Burst Scaling:** At Talent Level 10, her Burst deals 20 hits (19 cutting hits at 202.14% and one bloom hit at 303.21%). This results in a total multiplier of roughly **4,143.87%** over 5 seconds. This is one of the highest burst ratios in the game. However, if the enemy moves out of the Burst or is a boss that cannot be frozen, the damage can drop significantly if the Seki no To passes through them.

**Ganyu's Charged Shot Scaling:** At Talent Level 10, a single Frostflake Arrow and its Bloom deal a combined **622.08%** damage. While this is lower than Ayaka's Burst, Ganyu has no cooldown on these shots. In the time Ayaka takes to recharge her 80-cost Burst, Ganyu can fire 8 to 10 shots, totaling over **6,000%** scaling. Ganyu wins in long, multi-wave fights where Ayaka might find herself with an empty energy bar and a fresh wave of enemies.

### Elemental Reactions and Team Synergy

In 2026, the introduction of characters like **Mavuika** and **Escoffier** has refined their respective best-in-slot teams.

*   **Ayaka (The Premium Freeze Team):** Ayaka’s best performance currently comes from a team consisting of **Ayaka, Furina, Shenhe, and Escoffier**. Furina provides the Hydro application and massive DMG buffs, while Shenhe provides the flat damage "Quills." Escoffier has emerged as a top-tier support who can manage the "Bond of Life" mechanics often required by Fontaine-era weapons like **Finale of the Deep**, while providing additional Cryo shred.
*   **Ganyu (The Burn-Melt Team):** Ganyu has shifted away from the classic "Morgana" (Ganyu, Diona, Mona, Venti) because modern enemies often resist Venti’s vacuum. Her strongest team now is **Ganyu, Mavuika, Nahida, and Zhongli**. Nahida and Mavuika maintain a constant "Burning" aura on the enemy, allowing Ganyu to "Reverse Melt" every single Frostflake Arrow Bloom for a 1.5x damage multiplier. Because Ganyu’s Bloom has no Internal Cooldown (ICD), she can Melt every shot, leading to astronomical numbers.

### Weapon and Artifact Scaling

For Ayaka, the **Mistsplitter Reforged** remains the gold standard, offering 44.1% CRIT DMG and up to 40% Elemental DMG bonus. However, the Fontaine craftable sword **Finale of the Deep** is a monstrous F2P option. At R5, it provides a massive ATK buff but requires a healer to clear the "Bond of Life" it generates. If you can clear the bond, it often outperforms the Inazuma-based **Amenoma Kageuchi** in pure damage.

Ganyu’s signature **Amos' Bow** is still excellent for her Charged Shots, but **The First Great Magic** (originally Lyney's signature) has become a competitive alternative due to its high CRIT DMG and ATK% buffs based on team composition. For F2P players, **Prototype Crescent** remains the best craftable bow if you can hit weak points, while **Hamayumi** is a more consistent "generalist" bow for Melt Ganyu since it buffs Normal/Charged attacks when her energy is full.

### Performance in the Spiral Abyss

In the current Spiral Abyss, Ayaka is the "Boss Killer." If a floor contains a high-HP target like a Coppelia or an Aeonblight Drake, Ayaka’s ability to stack buffs and unleash her 4,000% scaling Burst is unmatched. She thrives in windows of opportunity.

Ganyu is the "Chamber Clearer." In floors with multiple waves of enemies spawned at opposite ends of the arena, Ganyu can stand in the center and snipe them down. Her Elemental Burst, **Celestial Shower**, also provides a 20% Cryo DMG bonus to active characters within its field, allowing her to function as a support for Ayaka in "Dual Cryo" teams—a strategy that has gained traction in the Stygian Onslaught mode where multiple teams are required.

## Recommendation

![Recommendation](/img/ayaka-vs-ganyu-dps-comparison/inline-4.webp)


Both characters have aged remarkably well, but your choice should be dictated by your existing roster and preferred platform.

### Pull for Ayaka If...
*   You already own **Shenhe** or **Furina**.
*   You play primarily on mobile where aiming a bow is frustrating.
*   You prefer a "set up and execute" playstyle where you spend 15 seconds buffing and 5 seconds dealing 90% of your damage.
*   You need a reliable melee unit for the **Stygian Onslaught** endgame.

### Pull for Ganyu If...
*   You have **Zhongli** or a high-investment **Layla** to provide a shield.
*   You want a character who can function as a Main DPS, a Sub-DPS, and a Support.
*   You enjoy the "Melt" reaction and have **Nahida** or **Mavuika** to enable consistent Pyro application.
*   You want an easy time with overworld exploration, as Ganyu can clear camps from a distance.

### The Future of Cryo DPS in Genshin Impact

As we progress deeper into the Natlan era, Cryo as an element is evolving. While newer units like **Wriothesley** offer a different flavor of Cryo DPS (focused on Normal Attacks and HP fluctuation), Ayaka and Ganyu still hold the highest potential for raw Cryo application and reaction damage. The introduction of "Nightsoul" mechanics in Natlan has primarily benefited Pyro and Hydro units, but the consistent high multipliers on Ayaka and Ganyu ensure they remain "future-proof" against power creep.

If you are looking for vertical investment, Ayaka’s **C2** (which adds two smaller Frostflake Seki no To) and **C4** (which shreds enemy DEF by 30%) are among the strongest constellations in the game for speed-running. Ganyu’s **C6** (which makes her Frostflake Arrows instant after using her Skill) transforms her into a burst-DPS comparable to Ayaka, but it requires a much higher investment.

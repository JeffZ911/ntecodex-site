# Ben Bigger Best Build Guide: The Ultimate Fire Defense Agent

Ben Bigger is a Fire-attribute Defense Agent who excels at mitigating damage and providing powerful shields to his team. Unlike other Agents who rely solely on dodging, Ben's kit revolves around his ability to stand his ground, block enemy strikes, and unleash devastating counters. Because his shield strength and damage output scale directly with his Defense (DEF) stat, building him effectively requires a specific focus on survivability that doubles as offensive scaling.

## Overview

![Overview](/img/ben-bigger-best-build-zzz/inline-1.webp)


Ben Bigger serves as the primary tank for the Belobog Heavy Industries faction. His playstyle is distinct due to his "Counter Block" mechanic, which allows him to negate incoming damage and retaliate with a heavy physical blow. Beyond his personal survivability, Ben is highly valued for his Core Passive, which grants a significant Critical Rate buff to any ally currently protected by his shield. This makes him an exceptional support for DPS units who struggle to reach their Crit Rate caps.

### Skill Priority
When investing resources into Ben’s skills, prioritize his defensive utility and the buffs he provides to the team.

1.  **Core Passive (Guardian):** This is his most important skill, as it dictates the strength of the Crit Rate buff provided to shielded allies.
2.  **Special Attack:** Upgrading this increases the damage of his parry and counter-attacks, as well as the damage absorption of his block.
3.  **Chain Attack / Ultimate:** These skills provide massive shields to the entire party and should be leveled to ensure team-wide safety.
4.  **Basic Attack:** This is the lowest priority, as Ben should rarely be on the field performing basic strings unless his Special Attack is on cooldown.

### Ben Bigger's Strengths and Weaknesses

**Strengths:**
*   **Unique Parry Mechanic:** He is currently the only Agent who can nullify damage through a block rather than a dodge.
*   **Team-Wide Buffs:** Provides a rare Critical Rate buff through his Core Passive.
*   **High Burst Counter:** His counters deal significant Fire damage and can quickly stun smaller enemies.
*   **Synergy:** Has unique "Joint Attack" animations and enhanced multipliers when paired with Koleda Belobog.

**Weaknesses:**
*   **Slow Mobility:** His movement and attack speed are significantly slower than most other Agents.
*   **Energy Dependent:** Requires Energy to maintain high uptime on his EX Special Attack for maximum shielding.
*   **Niche Role:** As a Defense Agent, he may lower the overall team DPS in time-trial content compared to a dedicated Stun or Support Agent, unless his buffs are utilized perfectly.

### Core Mechanics: The Follow-up Counter
Ben’s identity is tied to his Special Attack: Fiscal Punch. When you press the Special Attack button, Ben enters a blocking stance. If he is hit during this stance, he automatically triggers a counter-attack.

However, the "EX Special Attack: Cash Flow Monitoring" is where Ben truly shines. When he has enough Energy, his block becomes much stronger. If he successfully blocks an attack during the EX Special version, you can follow up by pressing the Special Attack button again to trigger a massive "Follow-up Counter." This move deals heavy Fire damage and grants a shield to all team members. The strength of this shield is based on a percentage of Ben's DEF, making it the primary reason to stack DEF% on his equipment.

**Bunny Band** is another strong contender. While its secondary stat is DEF, its passive focuses on HP and ATK. This is better suited for a "hybrid" Ben build where you want him to stay on the field longer, though it is generally less efficient than Big Cylinder for pure support.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/ben-bigger-best-build-zzz/inline-5.webp)


Playing Ben Bigger effectively requires a reactive mindset. He is not a character you "main" on the field; instead, you swap him in during specific windows to mitigate damage and buff the team.

### Step-by-step Combat Flowchart
1.  **Opening:** Start with your Stunner (e.g., Koleda or Anby) to build Daze.
2.  **Defensive Swap:** Watch for the "Yellow Flash" on the enemy. This indicates an incoming heavy attack. Switch to Ben to perform a **Defensive Assist**.
3.  **The Counter:** Immediately follow the Defensive Assist with Ben's **EX Special Attack**. This will block the hit and allow you to trigger the **Follow-up Counter**.
4.  **Buffing:** Triggering the Follow-up Counter generates a shield for the team. This activates Ben's Core Passive, giving the next Agent you swap to a Crit Rate boost.
5.  **Chain Attack:** When the enemy is Stunned, use Ben’s **Chain Attack** in the sequence. Ensure Ben is *not* the last person in the chain; you want to swap from Ben to your Main DPS (like Anton or Soldier 11) so they benefit from the Crit Rate buff during the stun window.

### Mastering the Parry Timing
The timing for Ben’s block is generous, but the "EX" version is what matters. Without Energy, Ben can still block, but he won't provide the team-wide shield or the massive Follow-up Counter. Always keep an eye on his Energy bar. If you are low on Energy, use Ben’s Basic Attacks or wait for him to gain Energy off-field before attempting a parry.

### Maximizing Shield Uptime
Ben’s shield is not permanent. It has a set duration and a maximum absorption value based on his DEF. To maximize uptime, only swap Ben in when a hit is guaranteed. Using his Special Attack preemptively without an incoming hit wastes Energy and reduces the overall efficiency of your rotation.

### How to Use Ben's Ultimate Effectively
Ben's Ultimate, "Bigger is Better," deals massive Fire damage in a large area and provides a very strong shield to all allies. In high-difficulty content like Shiyu Defense, save his Ultimate for moments when your team's health is low or when the enemy is about to unleash a screen-wide attack that is difficult to dodge. The invulnerability frames (i-frames) during the Ultimate animation can also be used to bypass "one-shot" mechanics.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/ben-bigger-best-build-zzz/inline-6.webp)


### Is Ben Bigger worth building?
Yes, Ben Bigger is highly worth building, especially for players who find the timing of "Perfect Dodges" difficult. He is essential for pushing through high-level Shiyu Defense stages where enemy damage is high enough to one-shot squishier DPS units. His Crit Rate buff also makes him a valuable long-term investment for any team.

### How do I get Ben Bigger for free?
Players can obtain one copy of Ben Bigger for free by completing the Chapter 2 Intermission story mission titled "A Helping Hand." This makes him one of the most accessible Defense Agents in the game for F2P players.

### Does Ben Bigger need Crit stats?
While Ben's primary stat is DEF, his counters actually have very high multipliers and can deal significant damage. His Core Passive also applies to himself. While you should prioritize DEF% first, having some Crit DMG in your sub-stats is not a waste, as it will make his counters hit significantly harder.

### Can Ben Bigger be a main DPS?
Ben is not designed to be a main DPS due to his slow attack speed and defensive kit. However, in a "Counter-focused" playstyle, he can contribute a surprising amount of total team damage. He is best used as a "Burst Support" who comes in, deals a massive hit, buffs the team, and leaves.

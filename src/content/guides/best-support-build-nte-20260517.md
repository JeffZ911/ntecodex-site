### The Role of Supports in the Esper Cycle

The Esper Cycle is the heart of NTE’s combat. As you use skills and basic attacks, you accumulate Esper Meter. When the meter reaches specific thresholds, the portraits of your off-field characters will glow. Switching to a character during this "Glow" window triggers a Resonance Attack. 

Supports are vital here because their Arcs (weapons) and Cartridges (artifacts) often feature "Charge Efficiency" stats. High Charge Efficiency allows your team to reach these Glow windows faster. A support's job is to enter the field, quickly dump their Resonance Skill and Ultimate to apply long-lasting buffs, and then swap out to the DPS to benefit from the accumulated energy.

### Top Support Characters in the Current Meta

Sakiri remains the undisputed queen of the support tier list. As an S-Rank Incantation unit, her ability to pull enemies into a central vortex while applying a significant ATK buff makes her indispensable for timed challenges like the Urban Anomaly sectors. 

Haniel and Adler are the premier A-Rank choices for players who need more survivability. Haniel provides a unique Psyche-based buff that scales with her own Max HP, making her both tanky and helpful for Crit-hungry DPS units. Adler, meanwhile, is the best "comfy" support, providing shields that prevent your DPS from being knocked back during heavy attack animations.

## Team Comp

![Team Comp](/img/best-support-build-nte-20260517/inline-4.webp)


Building a team in NTE requires understanding the "Elemental Lane." This refers to the order in which you place characters in your party UI, which influences how reactions are triggered during the Esper Cycle. A standard team consists of one Main DPS, one Sub-DPS/Reaction Enabler, and two Supports (or one Support and one Sustain).

### The Blossom Core (Anima + Cosmos)

The "Blossom" reaction is currently the most dominant meta for clearing high-end content like the "Neon Spire." This reaction occurs when Anima and Cosmos elements interact, causing an explosion of nature-based energy that deals AOE damage and roots smaller enemies.

*   **Main DPS:** Nanally (Anima)
*   **Sub-DPS/Enabler:** Jiuyuan (Anima)
*   **Support 1:** Sakiri (Incantation - for gathering and ATK buffs)
*   **Support 2:** Zero (Cosmos - to trigger Blossom)

In this team, Sakiri uses her vortex to group enemies. Jiuyuan applies off-field Anima pressure. Zero triggers the Blossom reaction with his Cosmos abilities, and Nanally enters the field to capitalize on the rooted, debuffed enemies with her high-speed physical/Anima strikes.

### The Scorch/Discord Core (Incantation + Chaos + Psyche)

For players who prefer high burst damage and "Discord" effects (which reduce enemy resistance to stagger), this composition is the preferred alternative.

*   **Main DPS:** Baicang (Chaos)
*   **Sub-DPS:** Daffodill (Psyche)
*   **Support 1:** Adler (Incantation - for shielding)
*   **Support 2:** Haniel (Psyche - for Crit buffs)

This team focuses on the Scorch reaction (Incantation + Chaos), which deals massive damage over time. Adler provides the protection Baicang needs to complete his long attack strings, while Haniel and Daffodill stack Psyche buffs to ensure every hit from Baicang is a Critical Hit.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/best-support-build-nte-20260517/inline-5.webp)


Maximizing a support build is useless if you do not follow a strict rotation to keep buffs active. The "Esper Cycle Glow" is your visual cue for when to swap, but the order of operations starts the moment you enter combat.

### Opening Buff Sequence

1.  **Start with your primary Buffer:** Switch to Sakiri or Haniel immediately. Use their Resonance Skill to generate initial energy and apply the first layer of buffs (ATK or Crit).
2.  **Gathering/Crowd Control:** If using Sakiri, use her Ultimate to pull enemies together. This ensures all subsequent damage from your team hits every target.
3.  **Deploy Sustain:** Switch to Adler or Fadia. Drop Adler’s shield or Fadia’s healing field. This step is crucial for preventing your DPS from being interrupted or killed during their burst window.
4.  **Enable Reactions:** Switch to your Reaction Enabler (Zero or Jiuyuan). Use their off-field skill (often called a "Coordinated Attack").

### Managing the Esper Cycle Glow

Once your supports have set the stage, look at your Main DPS icon. When the meter is full, the icon will glow and a prompt will appear.

*   **Step 4:** Swap to your Main DPS (Nanally or Baicang). They will perform an entrance Resonance Attack that benefits from all the buffs currently active.
*   **Step 5:** Unleash the Main DPS Ultimate. Only do this when the enemy's "Stagger Bar" (the white bar under their HP) is broken. Staggered enemies take significantly more damage, and this is when your support buffs provide the most value.
*   **Step 6:** Once the DPS window ends (usually 10-12 seconds), the buffs will expire. Immediately rotate back to Step 1 to restart the cycle.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/best-support-build-nte-20260517/inline-6.webp)


**Who is the best F2P support?**
Haniel is widely considered the best F2P support in NTE. As an A-Rank character, she is much easier to obtain and upgrade than Sakiri. Her ability to provide both healing and a massive Crit Rate buff makes her a staple for players who haven't pulled S-Rank supports yet.

**Should I pull for Sakiri or Hotori?**
Sakiri is a universal buffer who fits into almost any team due to her grouping and ATK boosts. Hotori is a high-skill "Support-DPS" hybrid who requires specific gear to shine. If you want a character that makes every team better, Sakiri is the safer investment.

**What is 'Charge Efficiency'?**
Charge Efficiency is the most important stat for supports. It determines how quickly your Resonance Ultimate and the team's Esper Meter fill up. A support with high Charge Efficiency can trigger the Esper Cycle Glow much more frequently, leading to higher overall team DPS.

**Do support buffs stack?**
Yes, buffs from different sources generally stack. For example, the ATK buff from Sakiri's Ultimate will stack with the ATK buff from the Speedy Hedgehog Cartridge set. However, buffs from the same source (e.g., two characters both wearing the Speedy Hedgehog set) usually do not stack; only the highest value or the most recent application will apply.

### Rode PSA1+ Professional Studio Boom Arm
*   **Price:** $129
*   **Rating:** 4.8/5
*   **Best For:** Professional streamers and heavy setups

The Rode PSA1+ stands as the gold standard for streamers who prioritize silence. When you are deep into a Neverness to Everness session, the last thing you want is a metallic "ping" or "creak" echoing through your stream every time you shift your weight. This arm utilizes a completely silent internal spring design, ensuring that even the most sensitive condenser microphones won't pick up mechanical noise during a high-stakes pull on The Ichi-daime banner.

With a massive 940mm horizontal reach and 820mm vertical reach, the PSA1+ can easily clear large 32-inch monitors often used to display the vibrant Unreal Engine 5 visuals of Hethereau. **Verdict:**
With a 940mm horizontal reach and a weight capacity of 1.2kg, the PSA1+ is the benchmark for stability. Its neoprene arm covers and internal damping make it the quietest option for streamers who move their mic frequently during live sessions.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/best-desk-mounted-boom-arms-for-streaming-gacha-grinders/inline-4.webp)


While a high-quality boom arm can improve your streaming setup, it isn't always a necessary purchase.

*   **Headset Users:** If you use a headset microphone, a boom arm provides zero benefit and only adds desk clutter. The built-in mic on high-end headsets is designed to stay at a fixed distance from your mouth, negating the need for an external arm.
*   **Hollow-Core Desks:** If your desk is made of hollow-core particle board (like many budget IKEA options), a heavy-duty boom arm clamp may crush the desk surface. Without a reinforcement plate, the pressure from the clamp can cause the wood to cave in over time.
*   **Occasional Speakers:** If you rarely speak during your farming sessions or mostly play solo without streaming, a simple desktop tripod is more cost-effective and less intrusive than a full boom arm.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can a boom arm support a heavy microphone like the Shure SM7B?
Most premium boom arms like the <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Elgato+Wave+LP&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Elgato Wave LP</a> and Gator Frameworks Deluxe are specifically designed to support heavy broadcast microphones. These arms have a weight capacity of 4.4lbs, which is more than double the weight of the SM7B. Always check the tension settings, as heavy mics may require you to tighten the hinges to prevent drifting.

### Why is a low-profile boom arm better for gacha gaming?
Gacha games like Neverness to Everness have complex UIs with important information located at the top and sides of the screen. A standard boom arm can block your view of your car's speed or your team's health bars. A low-profile arm sits beneath your monitor, keeping your entire screen visible while still positioning the microphone close to your mouth.

### How do I prevent my boom arm from damaging my desk?
Desk-mounted arms use C-clamps that exert significant pressure on a small area. To protect your desk, especially if it is made of soft wood or particle board, you can use a <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=mounting+reinforcement+plate&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">mounting reinforcement plate</a>. This spreads the pressure over a larger surface area, preventing the clamp from biting into the desk or causing it to warp.

### Do I need a shock mount with my boom arm?
While many boom arms like the Rode PSA1+ have internal damping to reduce noise, a shock mount is still recommended. It provides an extra layer of isolation from vibrations caused by typing or clicking your mouse during intense combat in Hethereau. Most boom arms use standard 3/8" or 5/8" threads that are compatible with universal shock mounts.

## What a multi-screen gacha setup actually needs

![What a multi-screen gacha setup actually needs](/img/best-adjustable-monitor-arms-multi-screen-gacha/inline-2.webp)


Playing an urban open-world RPG like Neverness to Everness is a multi-faceted experience. You aren't just controlling a character; you are managing a fleet of customizable vehicles and a roster of Espers like Nanally or Mint. This complexity often demands more than one screen.

### Independent Articulation
When running the main game on your primary OLED panel and a resource map on another, you need independent articulation. A fixed dual-monitor stand often forces both screens into the same tilt angle. High-quality arms allow you to tilt your primary screen slightly upward for a relaxed posture while keeping the secondary screen perfectly vertical for quick reference. This prevents the repetitive neck craning that leads to "gamer neck" during 6+ hour farming sessions.

### Portrait Orientation
Many menus and social features in modern gacha titles are optimized for vertical viewing. Having a mount that allows for 90-degree rotation without the bottom of the monitor hitting the desk is mandatory. This is particularly useful when tracking Anomalies across Hethereau, as vertical screens can display longer lists of locations or Discord walkthroughs without constant scrolling.

### Tension Adjustability
Your physical position shifts throughout a long session. You might start sitting upright and lean back as the night progresses. Monitor arms must have adjustable tension—usually via a hex bolt on the "elbow"—to stay in place when moved but remain easy to reposition with a single hand. If the tension is too low, the monitor will "droop"; if it is too high, you risk shaking your entire desk just to move the screen.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/best-adjustable-monitor-arms-multi-screen-gacha/inline-4.webp)


While a monitor arm can transform your gameplay experience in Neverness to Everness, it isn't always the right choice for every gamer.

First, check the back of your monitors. If they lack VESA mounting holes (usually in a 75x75mm or 100x100mm square pattern), you cannot use these arms without a specialized and often unstable adapter kit. Many budget or ultra-thin monitors omit these holes to save on production costs.

Second, consider your desk material. Avoid clamp-style arms if you have a glass-top desk. The concentrated pressure of the clamp, combined with the weight of two monitors acting as a lever, can cause the glass to shatter. If you must mount on glass, use a freestanding mount like the WALI or a reinforcement plate, though even then, the risk remains.

Finally, if you never move your monitors and they are already at the correct eye level, stock stands are often more stable than budget-tier arms. A cheap arm can introduce "monitor wobble" every time you type or move your mouse quickly during a high-speed chase in Hethereau.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can a single monitor arm hold an ultrawide monitor?
Yes, but you must check the weight of the monitor without its stand. For example, the Amazon Basics Premium Dual Monitor Stand can support up to 25 lbs per arm, which is sufficient for most 34-inch ultrawides. Always verify that the arm's weight range matches the [actual weight of the panel](https://www.lg.com/us/monitors/lg-34gp83a-b-gaming-monitor) to avoid damaging the internal springs.

### Is a gas spring or a mechanical spring better for gaming?
Gas springs, like those in the HUANUO, provide smoother, more fluid movement but can wear out over several years. Mechanical springs or "lift engine" technology, used in the Ergotron LX, are typically more durable and offer a longer lifespan, often reflected in their 10-year warranties. For a setup you plan to keep for a decade, mechanical is superior.

### How do I stop my monitors from wobbling when I type?
Wobble is usually caused by a desk that isn't heavy enough or a clamp that isn't tightened sufficiently. Ensure your monitor arm is clamped to a solid wood or metal portion of the desk. If the wobble persists, you may need to tighten the tension bolts on the arm's joints to reduce play in the mechanism.

### Do monitor arms save desk space?
Absolutely. By replacing bulky plastic stands with a single mounting point, you reclaim the area directly beneath your monitors. This is ideal for placing your keyboard, a high-fidelity audio interface, or even a small decorative item from your favorite gacha franchise.

Pela remains one of the most enduring and valuable units in Honkai: Star Rail since the game's launch on April 26, 2023. As a 4-star Ice character following the Path of Nihility, her primary contribution to a team is her ability to strip enemies of their defenses, effectively acting as a universal damage multiplier for your primary attackers.

# Pela Best Build HSR Guide: Best Light Cones, Relics, and Teams

Pela is the quintessential "low investment, high reward" support. Unlike many other characters who require specific 5-star Light Cones or complex relic setups to function, Pela performs exceptionally well with free-to-play equipment. Her kit is centered around her Ultimate, which applies a Zone Suppression debuff to all enemies on the field, reducing their DEF by a significant margin. Because DEF shred becomes more valuable the more you stack it (up to a 100% cap), Pela is a staple in high-end content like Memory of Chaos and Pure Fiction.

## Overview

![Overview](/img/pela-best-build-hsr-guide/inline-1.webp)


Pela serves as the Intelligence Officer of the Silvermane Guards, and her gameplay reflects her analytical nature. She identifies enemy weaknesses and removes their advantages. In the current meta, she is prized for her Skill Point (SP) efficiency; she can often go an entire battle using only her Basic ATK, generating SP for hungry damage dealers like Dan Heng • Imbibitor Lunae or Qingque, while still maintaining high uptime on her Ultimate debuffs.

### Trace Priority Chart

To maximize Pela's utility while conserving resources, follow this upgrade priority:

1.  **Ultimate (Zone Suppression):** This is her most important tool. Increasing its level directly increases the percentage of DEF shred applied to enemies.
2.  **Talent (Data Collecting):** This allows Pela to regenerate extra Energy when her attack hits a debuffed enemy. High levels are required to achieve consistent 2-turn or 3-turn Ultimate rotations.
3.  **Skill (Frostbite):** Useful for removing buffs from enemies (like the Mara-Struck's revive), but the [damage scaling](https://ntecodex.com/faq) is secondary.
4.  **Basic ATK (Frost Shot):** Used primarily for SP generation and Energy. Upgrade this last.

### Why Pela is a Top-Tier Support

Pela’s value stems from her AoE (Area of Effect) DEF reduction. While Silver Wolf offers stronger single-target debuffs and weakness implantation, Pela is often preferred in scenarios with multiple elites or waves of enemies. Furthermore, her E4 (Eidolon 4) adds an Ice RES reduction to her Skill, making her the undisputed best support for Ice-type DPS characters. Her ability to be completely SP-positive allows teams to run more aggressive rotations without running out of resources.

### Key Strengths and Weaknesses

**Strengths:**
*   **Universal Utility:** DEF shred benefits every damage dealer in the game, regardless of their element or path.
*   **SP Positive:** She generates more Skill Points than she consumes.
*   **Buff Removal:** Her Skill can dispel annoying enemy buffs, which is critical against certain bosses.
*   **Easy to Build:** She has low stat requirements compared to DPS units.

**Weaknesses:**
*   **Low Personal Damage:** Unless built specifically for sub-DPS (which is generally less efficient), her own damage output is negligible.
*   **Squishiness:** With relatively low base HP and DEF, she can be prone to being one-shot in high-difficulty content without proper defensive relics.

Choosing the right Light Cone for Pela depends largely on whether you have access to limited event equipment and what your team composition requires.

### Top Support Light Cones

**Before the Tutorial Mission Starts (S5):** This is Pela's absolute Best-in-Slot weapon. It was a reward from the Version 1.1 event "Starhunt Game." At Superimposition 5, it provides a 40% Effect Hit Rate (EHR) boost and restores 8 Energy whenever Pela attacks an enemy with reduced DEF. Since Pela’s Ultimate applies DEF reduction, she can trigger this effect constantly, enabling a highly coveted 2-turn Ultimate rotation.

**Resolution Shines As Pearls of Sweat:** If you do not have the event Light Cone, this is the best alternative. It allows Pela to apply an additional DEF-shredding debuff called "Ensnared." This debuff stacks additively with her Ultimate’s DEF shred. This is particularly powerful in teams featuring Dr. Ratio or Acheron, who require a high volume of debuffs to trigger their mechanics.

### F2P and Budget Alternatives

For players who missed the Version 1.1 event and don't have "Resolution," **Eyes of the Prey** is a viable choice purely for the Effect Hit Rate it provides. While the DoT (Damage over Time) boost is wasted on Pela, the EHR helps her reach the 67% threshold required to guarantee her debuffs land on high-level bosses. In the very early game, the 3-star Light Cone **Void** can help bridge the gap until you pull a 4-star Nihility option.

Pela’s relic choices focus on two things: Speed and Energy. The faster she moves, the more Skill Points she generates and the more often she can shred enemy defenses.

### Recommended Relic Sets

The **4-piece Eagle of Twilight Line** is widely considered the meta choice for Pela. While the 2-piece Wind DMG bonus is useless for her, the 4-piece bonus advances her action by 25% every time she uses her Ultimate. Because Pela can use her Ultimate very frequently, this set allows her to "lap" enemies and teammates, generating massive amounts of Skill Points and Energy.

For a more traditional support build, the **4-piece Messenger Traversing Hackerspace** is excellent. It provides Pela with extra Speed and gives the entire team a Speed boost whenever she uses her Ultimate. If you find your Pela dying too quickly in the Swarm Disaster or Gold and Gears, a **2-piece Guard of Wuthering Snow** and **2-piece Messenger** hybrid build provides a balance of speed and survivability.

### Planar Ornaments and Stat Targets

*   **Broken Keel:** The standard choice for boosting the team's Crit DMG. Pela needs 30% Effect RES to trigger this, which is easily obtainable through substats and her minor traces.
*   **Penacony: Land of the Dreams:** Excellent if Pela is supporting an Ice DPS like Jingliu. It provides 5% Energy Regeneration Rate (ERR) and boosts Ice DMG for all allies.
*   **Sprightly Vonwacq:** Useful if you are just a few percentage points short of a 2-turn Ultimate rotation, as it provides 5% ERR and an initial Action Advance.

**Main Stat Priority:**
*   **Body:** Effect Hit Rate (EHR) until you hit 67%, then HP% or DEF%.
*   **Boots:** Speed (SPD) is mandatory.
*   **Sphere:** HP% or DEF% (Pela needs the bulk to survive).
*   **Link Rope:** Energy Regeneration Rate (ERR) is mandatory.

**Substat Priority:**
1.  SPD (Target: 143 or 160+)
2.  Effect Hit Rate (Target: 57% - 67%)
3.  Effect RES (Target: 30% for Broken Keel)
4.  HP% / DEF%

### Premium Team Synergies

**Acheron Teams:** Pela is arguably Acheron’s best 4-star teammate. Acheron requires Nihility teammates to apply debuffs to charge her Ultimate. Pela can apply a debuff with her Ultimate, her Skill (at E4), and even her Basic ATK if she is wearing the "Resolution Shines As Pearls of Sweat" Light Cone. This makes her an incredibly efficient battery for Acheron.

**Jingliu Teams:** Jingliu is a powerhouse, and Pela is her perfect partner. At Eidolon 4, Pela’s Skill reduces enemy Ice RES by 12%. When combined with the universal DEF shred from Pela’s Ultimate, Jingliu’s damage reaches ceiling-shattering levels. Since Jingliu is relatively SP-efficient, this duo allows for a very flexible third slot, usually a Harmony unit like Bronya or [Ruan Mei](https://ntecodex.com/guides/hsr-memories-of-the-past-vs-signature-ruan-mei-gear-guide).

### Free-to-Play Friendly Teams

For players just starting out, Pela can carry the support role for any DPS. Pairing her with **Dan Heng** or **Serval** works well because Pela's DEF shred is universal. In these teams, Pela should focus entirely on Basic Attacks to ensure that the sustain (like **Natasha** or **Lynx**) and the DPS always have Skill Points available for emergencies or burst windows.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/pela-best-build-hsr-guide/inline-5.webp)


Understanding Pela's Energy math is the key to mastering her gameplay. Her goal is to have her Ultimate active as often as possible to keep the DEF shred debuff on the enemies at all times.

### Maximizing Ultimate Uptime

The "Golden Standard" for Pela is the **2-turn Ultimate rotation**. This means she uses two actions (usually Basic Attacks) and then has her Ultimate ready again.

**The 2-Turn Rotation Requirements:**
*   **Light Cone:** "Before the Tutorial Mission Starts" (S5).
*   **Link Rope:** Energy Regeneration Rate (5-star, +19.4%).
*   **Talent:** Level 7 or higher.
*   **Planar Set:** Vonwacq or Penacony (optional but helpful).

**The Calculation:**
1.  Ultimate use restores 5 Energy.
2.  Two Basic Attacks restore 40 Energy (20 each).
3.  Talent triggers twice (on the Basic Attacks) for ~20 Energy.
4.  Tutorial Light Cone triggers twice for 16 Energy.
5.  Total: (5 + 20 + 20 + 20 + 16) * 1.194 (ERR Rope) = ~96.7 Energy.
6.  *Note:* While this is slightly short of the 110 required, Pela's E1 (Energy on enemy defeat) or getting hit by an enemy typically bridges the gap, making a 2-turn rotation extremely consistent in actual combat.

### Skill Point Management

Pela should be played as an SP-positive unit. You should only use her Skill in three specific scenarios:
1.  **Buff Removal:** The enemy has a dangerous buff (e.g., Revive, ATK Up, SPD Up).
2.  **Ice RES Shred:** You are running a Jingliu team and need to apply the E4 debuff.
3.  **Emergency Energy:** You need just a few more Energy points to get your Ultimate before a boss takes their turn (Skills generate 30 base energy vs. 20 from Basic ATK).

Otherwise, stick to Basic Attacks. This ensures your main DPS never has to "Basic" because they ran out of points.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/pela-best-build-hsr-guide/inline-6.webp)


### Common Build Questions

**How much Effect Hit Rate (EHR) does Pela need?**
To guarantee that Pela's Ultimate lands on enemies with 40% Effect RES (the highest for most bosses), you need **66.7% EHR**. Since Pela gains 10% EHR from her traces, you only need to find about 57% from your gear. If you use the "Before the Tutorial Mission Starts" Light Cone, you only need an additional 17% from substats, which is very easy to achieve.

**Is Pela better than Silver Wolf?**
They serve different purposes. Silver Wolf is the queen of single-target debuffing and allows you to ignore elemental weaknesses. Pela is superior in AoE scenarios (Pure Fiction, multi-boss fights) and is significantly more SP-positive. In many meta teams, such as Acheron hypercarry, they are actually used together.

**Does Pela's DEF shred stack with the Genius of Brilliant Stars set?**
Yes. DEF shred (from Pela) and DEF ignore (from the Quantum relic set) are additive. If Pela provides 40% shred and the relic set provides 20% ignore, the enemy effectively has 60% less DEF. This stacking is extremely powerful because the damage increase grows exponentially as you approach 100% DEF reduction.

### Eidolon and Trace Priorities

| Eidolon | Value | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| E1 | High | Restores 5 Energy when an enemy is defeated. |
| E2 | Low | Increases SPD after using Skill to remove a buff. |
| E4 | Massive | Skill reduces enemy Ice RES by 12%. |
| E6 | Medium | Pela deals additional Ice DMG to debuffed enemies. |

Pela’s most important power spikes are **E1** and **E4**. E1 makes her 2-turn Ultimate rotation much more reliable in waves of enemies. E4 is a game-changer for Ice teams, making her a "hidden 5-star" support for characters like Jingliu or Yanqing. While her E6 adds some damage, it doesn't change her fundamental role as a support.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/best-gear-for-gacha-dps-testing-sessions/inline-4.webp)


If you already own a 144Hz IPS monitor with good color accuracy, the jump to OLED for DPS testing is a luxury, not a necessity. While the motion clarity is superior, a standard high-refresh IPS is still capable of providing 7ms to 10ms of total system latency, which is sufficient for most frame-counting needs.

Additionally, do not buy a standing desk if you do not have the floor space or a long enough cable management solution. A poorly set up standing desk—where cables are taut or monitors are unstable—causes more strain than it solves. Finally, avoid high-end ergonomic mice if your current gaming mouse is already comfortable and you aren't experiencing wrist tingling or pain. Ergonomics is highly personal, and a "vertical" mouse can feel awkward for those with specific grip styles.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why is 240Hz important for gacha DPS testing?
A 240Hz refresh rate allows for a new frame to be drawn every 4.17 milliseconds. This granularity is essential for identifying the exact frame an animation can be "canceled" by a dash or skill, which is the foundation of calculating maximum theoretical DPS.

### Can I use a vertical mouse for actual combat?
Yes, though it requires an adjustment period. While the polling rate of the MX Vertical is lower than a gaming mouse, it is perfectly adequate for the action-RPG combat found in ZZZ or NTE, where timing is more about rhythm than pixel-perfect aiming.

### Is OLED burn-in a risk when looking at static spreadsheets?
Modern OLEDs like the PG27AQDM include heatsinks and pixel-shifting technology to mitigate this. However, if you spend 90% of your time on static Excel sheets, it is recommended to hide your taskbar and use dark mode to further protect the panel.

### Does a footrest really help with back pain?
Yes, by elevating the feet, you ensure your hips are positioned correctly against the back of your chair. This prevents "slumping" and reduces the pressure on your lumbar discs, which is the primary cause of lower back pain during long gaming sessions.

## What high-APM players actually need

![What high-APM players actually need](/img/camellya-sanhua-vs-danjin-dps-comparison-gear/inline-2.webp)


In the world of competitive DPS testing—especially when comparing the frame-tight rotations of characters like Sanhua or Danjin—the "feel" of your gear is a functional requirement.

### Tactile Reset and Actuation
Characters that rely on specific timing windows, such as triggering a Critical Dodge at the moment of a "Red Flash," need switches with a clear reset point. If your keyboard or mouse buttons are too mushy, you may find yourself double-tapping or missing the window entirely. Mechanical switches with a tactile bump or rapid-trigger Hall Effect sensors allow for faster successive inputs, which is vital for the Esper Cycle where you must swap characters the moment they "glow" to trigger support skills.

### Lumbar and Posture Support
Grinding for Solid Dice to pull on limited boards like The Ichi-daime often involves hours of repetitive tasks. A chair with adjustable lumbar support prevents the "gamer neck" that occurs when players lean forward to focus on enemy telegraphs. Maintaining a neutral spine ensures that blood flow to the arms and hands remains optimal, preventing the "heavy hand" feeling that ruins high-APM rotations.

### High Refresh Rates for Visual Cues
NTE’s combat UI uses specific visual cues, such as the Red Flash for dodges or the white and red health bars that signal character status. On a 60Hz monitor, these frames can sometimes be skipped or blurred. A minimum of 144Hz—overclocked to 180Hz where possible—provides the fluid motion needed to track multiple enemies in Hethereau’s crowded alleys.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/camellya-sanhua-vs-danjin-dps-comparison-gear/inline-4.webp)


While premium gear provides a competitive edge, it isn't always necessary for every player.

*   **Incremental Refresh Rates:** If you already own a 144Hz IPS monitor, the jump to 180Hz is subtle. Unless you are performing professional-level frame-counting for a Camellya vs. Danjin DPS comparison, the $400 investment might be better spent elsewhere.
*   **Sensor Saturation:** If your current mouse features a high-end sensor like the [Focus+](https://www.razer.com/gaming-mice/razer-naga-v2-pro) or Hero 25K, you likely won't notice a difference in tracking accuracy. The main reason to buy the Naga V2 Pro is the button layout, not the raw sensor specs.
*   **Controller Players:** Neverness to Everness features full [cross-platform support](https://www.reddit.com/r/NevernessToEverness/comments/1f1v4m8/nte_neverness_to_everness_officially_releases_on/) including PlayStation 5. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the most important spec for NTE combat?
Input latency is the most critical factor. Because the game relies on Critical Dodges and timed Esper Cycles, any delay between your physical press and the on-screen action can result in missing a parry or failing to trigger a reaction.

### Do I really need 12 side buttons on a mouse?
It is not strictly required, but it is highly beneficial for managing a four-character team. Mapping each character's Redirect Skill and Ultimate to a specific thumb button allows you to keep your fingers on the WASD keys for movement at all times.

### Is a 1440p monitor better than 1080p for NTE?
Yes, because NTE is an urban open-world game with high detail density in its Hethereau cityscapes. The higher resolution of a 1440p panel like the <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=LG+27GP850-B&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">LG 27GP850-B</a> makes it easier to spot enemy telegraphs and UI indicators amidst the neon lighting and particle effects.

### Can I play NTE competitively on a wireless setup?
Modern wireless technology, such as <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Razer+HyperSpeed&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Razer HyperSpeed</a> or the Keychron Q1 Pro’s Bluetooth, is now fast enough that the latency is indistinguishable from a wired connection. This allows for a cleaner desk setup without sacrificing the frame-perfect inputs needed for high-APM rotations.

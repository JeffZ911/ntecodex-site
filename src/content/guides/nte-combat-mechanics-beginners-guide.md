## Overview

![Overview](/img/nte-combat-mechanics-beginners-guide/inline-1.webp)


Combat in Neverness to Everness (NTE) is a high-speed, tactical experience that revolves around the management of the Esper Cycle. Unlike traditional action RPGs where character swapping is merely a way to access different skill sets, NTE treats swapping as a primary offensive mechanic. To succeed in high-level Anomaly Commissions, you must master the synergy between the six core elements and the stability of your enemies.

### The Esper Cycle Meter
The Esper Cycle Meter is the circular gauge located in the bottom right of the UI, surrounding the character portraits. This meter fills as you perform basic attacks, land skills, and successfully execute defensive maneuvers. Once the meter is full, the portrait of your off-field characters will begin to glow. Swapping to a character during this "Glow-Swap" window triggers their Esper Cycle Ability—a powerful entry move that deals massive elemental damage and often applies specific debuffs or buffs depending on the character’s role.

### Break and Stagger Mechanics
Every elite enemy and boss in Hesperia possesses a white bar beneath their health pool known as the Stability Bar or Break Meter. Dealing damage reduces this bar, but certain elements and "Heavy" attacks (often found in Solid-type Arcs) deplete it significantly faster. 

When the Break Meter reaches zero, the enemy enters a "Staggered" state. During this window, the enemy is immobilized, and all damage taken is amplified by a base multiplier of 200% [exact value pending official confirmation]. This is the primary window for unleashing Ultimates and high-damage rotations.

### Perfect Dodging vs. Parrying
Defensive play in NTE is divided into two distinct systems:
1.  **The Red Flash (Critical Dodge):** When an enemy is about to land a heavy hit, a red light flashes on their weapon or limb. Pressing the dodge button at this exact moment triggers a slow-motion "Anomaly Time" effect, allowing you to reposition and counter-attack without consuming additional stamina for a brief period.
2.  **The Double Circle (Parry):** Certain telegraphed attacks display a "Double Circle" icon. If you timing a skill or a specific parry-enabled basic attack within these circles, you instantly fill a significant portion of your Esper Cycle Meter and deal massive Break damage to the enemy.

Matching the correct Arc type to your Esper’s native requirements is essential for activating their passive Resonance skills.

### How to Refine and Ascend Arcs
To maximize an Arc's potential, you must use duplicates for **Refinement (R1-R5)**. Each refinement level increases the percentage values of the Arc’s passive ability. **Ascension**, on the other hand, requires materials gathered from "City Exploration" and "Anomaly Rifts" to increase the Arc's level cap and Base ATK.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

Disks are the primary way to customize your Esper’s stats. Each Esper can equip six Disks, with set bonuses unlocking at 2 and 4 pieces.

### Substat Roll Priority
When farming Disks via Anomaly Commissions, prioritize the following stats based on your role:
*   **Main DPS:** Crit Rate > Crit DMG > Elemental DMG% > ATK%.
*   **Breakers (Shield Chippers):** Anomaly Intensity > Break Efficiency > ATK%.
*   **Supports:** Cycle Rate > Energy Regeneration > HP% (if healing scales with HP).

### Farming Disks via Anomaly Commissions
Disks are primarily obtained through Anomaly Commissions found throughout Hesperia. Higher difficulty tiers yield S-Rank Disks. It is recommended to spend your "Stamina" (Battery Power) on the "Industrial District" commissions early on, as they drop the versatile "Day Off" and "Anima Resonance" sets.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/nte-combat-mechanics-beginners-guide/inline-5.webp)


A proper rotation ensures that you are never caught with your skills on cooldown and that you maximize the damage during the enemy's Staggered state.

### Standard Opening Sequence
1.  **Start with your Support (e.g., Haniel or [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide)):** Cast their buffing skills or deploy shields immediately.
2.  **Swap to your Breaker (e.g., Daffodill or Adler):** Use their skills to aggressively target the enemy's white Break Meter.
3.  **Wait for the Glow:** Do not swap back to your DPS until their portrait glows.
4.  **The Glow-Swap:** Switch to your Main DPS (Nanally or Zero). This will trigger their entry skill, which usually deals a large burst of elemental damage.

### Managing the Esper Cycle Flow
If your Esper Cycle is filling slowly, focus on **Parrying**. A successful "Double Circle" parry provides a massive boost to the meter. Characters like Zero or Fadia also have "Redirect Skills" that can instantly fill a portion of the meter if they take damage while the skill is active.

### Maximizing Damage During Stagger
Once the enemy's Break Meter is empty:
1.  Ensure all Support buffs are active.
2.  Trigger your Main DPS's Ultimate.
3.  Spam basic attack chains to take advantage of the 200% damage multiplier.
4.  **Note:** Do not swap characters during the Staggered state unless the next character's entry skill is ready, as the swap animation can eat into your limited damage window.

### Combat Mechanics FAQ

**How do I fill the Esper Meter faster?**
The fastest way to fill the Esper Meter is by successfully performing "Double Circle" parries and "Red Flash" dodges. Additionally, equipping Disks with the "Cycle Rate" substat will naturally increase the generation speed.

**Does stamina affect combat?**
Yes. Stamina is consumed by sprinting, dodging, and certain charged attacks. For example, Mint's "Tornado Spin" consumes stamina continuously. If you run out of stamina, you cannot perform a Critical Dodge, leaving you vulnerable to heavy hits.

**What is Anomaly Intensity?**
Anomaly Intensity is a stat that determines how quickly you apply elemental status effects (like Scorch or Blossom) and how much Break damage you deal. High Anomaly Intensity is vital for Breaker units.

### Gear and Progression FAQ

**What is the difference between an Arc and a Disk?**
Arcs are the weapons your Espers wield; they provide Base ATK and unique passive abilities. Disks are the equivalent of artifacts or gear sets; they provide stat boosts and set bonuses. You can equip one Arc and six Disks per character.

**How do I get S-Rank Arcs?**

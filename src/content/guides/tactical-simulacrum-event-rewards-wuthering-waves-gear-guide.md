## What a long-session player actually needs

![What a long-session player actually needs](/img/tactical-simulacrum-event-rewards-wuthering-waves-gear-guide/inline-2.webp)


When engaging in a "grind" for rewards like Astrite or character upgrade materials, your needs differ from those of a casual player.

### Dynamic Lumbar Support
Static pillows included with many gaming chairs often flatten over time, losing their effectiveness. Integrated systems like the TITAN Evo's L-ADAPT are superior because they are built into the frame of the chair. This allows the support to adjust to your spine's natural curve even as you lean forward during intense combat or recline during cutscenes. Proper support prevents the lower back pain that typically starts around the 4-hour mark.

### Input Efficiency
Action-heavy events require rapid, repetitive inputs. An MMO mouse like the Razer Naga V2 Pro reduces the travel distance for your left hand. Instead of stretching your fingers to reach the numbers 1 through 5 on a keyboard while also trying to use WASD for movement, you can move those key binds to your thumb. This reduces the risk of repetitive strain and allows for more fluid character swapping.

### Eye Fatigue Mitigation
Repetitive farming tasks in high-contrast environments can lead to "computer vision syndrome." High-refresh monitors (144Hz+) provide smoother motion, which is easier for the brain to process than the choppiness of a 60Hz screen. Furthermore, proper task lighting—such as a monitor light bar—ensures that the contrast between your bright screen and a dark room doesn't cause your pupils to constantly dilate and constrict, which is a primary cause of gaming-induced headaches.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/tactical-simulacrum-event-rewards-wuthering-waves-gear-guide/inline-4.webp)


Not every player needs to spend nearly $2,000 on a full setup. You should skip these upgrades if:

*   **Existing High-Refresh Gear:** If you already own a 1440p monitor with at least a 144Hz refresh rate, the jump to 165Hz or 180Hz is negligible for non-competitive play. The difference in input lag is measured in single-digit milliseconds, which most players will not perceive.
*   **Functional Ergonomics:** If your current chair has a functional, adjustable lumbar mechanism and the foam hasn't bottomed out, a $500 upgrade won't necessarily improve your performance. You might find more value in a $20 footrest or a better desk height adjustment.
*   **Controller Preference:** Avoid upgrading to an MMO mouse if you primarily play with a controller. While these mice are excellent for keyboard and mouse players, the button density will be entirely wasted if you prefer the ergonomics of a gamepad.

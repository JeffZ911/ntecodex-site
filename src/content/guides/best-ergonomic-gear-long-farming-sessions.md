## What a long-session farmer actually needs

![What a long-session farmer actually needs](/img/best-ergonomic-gear-long-farming-sessions/inline-2.webp)


When engaging with the verticality of Neverness to Everness, your body faces different stresses than it would in a traditional RPG. A long-session farmer requires three core pillars of ergonomic support:

1.  **Dynamic Posture:** The ability to shift from sitting to standing is vital to prevent blood pooling and lower back stiffness. Using a standing desk allows you to maintain focus while engaging different muscle groups.
2.  **Neutral Wrist Positioning:** Standard gaming mice force the radius and ulna bones in the forearm to cross, which can lead to carpal tunnel over time. A vertical mouse keeps the hand in a "handshake" position, which is essential for the constant camera adjustments needed when navigating Hesperia's skyscrapers.
3.  **Circadian Lighting:** Extended sessions often run into the early morning hours. Using asymmetrical lighting reduces blue light exposure and screen glare, protecting sleep quality and reducing the "dry eye" effect common in dark-room gaming.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/best-ergonomic-gear-long-farming-sessions/inline-4.webp)


Not every player needs a full ergonomic overhaul. If you already own a high-end ergonomic chair like a Herman Miller Aeron or a Steelcase Gesture, a separate lumbar pillow or footrest may be redundant, as these chairs feature integrated, highly adjustable support systems.

Furthermore, players who prefer using controllers for their vehicle customization and exploration tasks may find the [MX Vertical](https://www.logitech.com/en-us/products/mice/mx-vertical-ergonomic-mouse.910-005447.html) unnecessary. For controller users, the ergonomic load shifts from the wrist to the thumbs and shoulders, making a high-quality chair or armrests a higher priority than a vertical mouse. Finally, if your current desk is already at the perfect height for your seated posture and you take frequent breaks to walk around, a standing desk is a luxury rather than a health necessity.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is a vertical mouse good for gaming in Neverness to Everness?
A vertical mouse like the [MX Vertical](https://www.logitech.com/en-us/products/mice/mx-vertical-ergonomic-mouse.910-005447.html) is excellent for the traversal and exploration elements of the game, as it keeps your wrist in a neutral position. However, if you are engaging in high-intensity combat that requires rapid "flick" movements, you may notice the higher click latency and weight compared to a dedicated gaming mouse. Many players find that the health benefits for long-term farming outweigh the slight performance trade-off.

### How often should I switch between sitting and standing?
Ergonomic experts generally recommend a 2:1 or 3:1 ratio of sitting to standing. For a 6-hour session in Hesperia, this means standing for about 20 minutes for every 40 to 60 minutes of sitting. The FlexiSpot E7 Pro makes this transition seamless with its programmable height presets, allowing you to change positions without interrupting your gameplay.

### Does a monitor light bar really help with eye strain?
Yes, a light bar like the BenQ ScreenBar Halo helps by reducing the contrast between your bright monitor and a dark room. By illuminating your workspace with 500 lux of light without creating glare on the screen, it allows your pupils to remain at a more consistent dilation, which significantly reduces the "heavy eye" feeling during late-night sessions.

### Why is a footrest important if I have a good chair?
Even with a high-end chair, many players find their feet don't rest flat on the floor if they have to raise their chair to reach their desk. An ErgoFoam footrest ensures your knees stay at a 90-degree angle, which takes the pressure off your lower back and promotes better blood circulation in your legs. This is especially important during the long, static periods of driving or flying through the city.

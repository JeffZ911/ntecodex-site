## What a long-session player actually needs

![What a long-session player actually needs](/img/ergonomic-footrest-combos-wfh-back-pain-mmo/inline-2.webp)

When playing an urban open-world RPG like NTE, your body is often in a state of "static loading." Here is what you should look for to survive 8+ hour sessions:
*   **Firmness over Softness:** While ultra-soft foam feels comfortable initially, it often fails to support the weight of your legs over several hours. This lack of support can lead to hip strain. High-density foam is preferred for long-term health.
*   **Height Customization:** Most standard desks are fixed at 29-30 inches. If you are under 5'10", you likely need at least 4 inches of elevation to keep your knees at the recommended 90-degree angle and your feet flat.
*   **Non-slip Stability:** A footrest that slides away forces you to slouch or "reach" with your legs. This movement causes lower back disc compression. Ensure your chosen model has a rubberized or beaded base.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/ergonomic-footrest-combos-wfh-back-pain-mmo/inline-4.webp)

Not every player requires a footrest. You might want to hold off on a purchase if:
*   **Your Chair is Highly Adjustable:** If your chair can lower enough for your feet to be flat on the floor while your elbows remain level with the desk, a footrest may actually cause hip impingement.
*   **You Use a Standing Desk:** If you spend [more than 70%](https://www.reddit.com/r/ergonomics/) of your day standing, a static footrest is less useful than a dedicated anti-fatigue mat.
*   **Weight Considerations:** Avoid cheap plastic models if you weigh over 200lbs. The structural integrity of budget plastic units often fails under the weight of active leg movement during gaming.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why does a footrest help with back pain during MMOs?
A footrest addresses "dangling feet," which pulls your pelvis forward and flattens the natural curve of your lower spine. By elevating your feet, you shift the weight back into the chair's lumbar support, reducing the pressure on your L4 and L5 spinal discs.

### Is foam or plastic better for a footrest?
Foam is generally better for those who play in socks or barefoot, as it provides a cushioned surface that conforms to the foot. Plastic or wood models like the Humanscale FM300 are superior for durability and for users who prefer to wear shoes while at their desk.

### How high should my footrest be?
Your footrest should be high enough so that your hips are slightly higher than your knees, or at least level. For most users at a standard 30-inch desk, a height of 4 to 5 inches is the "sweet spot" for maintaining a 90-degree knee angle.

### Can a footrest help with leg circulation?
Yes, especially models that allow for movement. The Humanscale FM300 encourages a rocking motion that engages the calf muscles, acting as a "second heart" to pump blood back up the legs and prevent swelling during long Neverness to Everness sessions.

# Aurelia Nova vs Lacrimosa NTE: Best Gaming Gear for Neverness to Everness Players

For Neverness to Everness (NTE) players, the Truthear Nova is the best thematic IEM for under $200, offering precise vocal clarity. The SteelSeries Arctis Nova Pro Wireless is our top premium pick for its dual-battery system and cinematic soundstage.

High-intensity urban exploration in Neverness to Everness (NTE), released on 2026-04-29, demands hardware that can keep up with both the fast-paced combat and the deep, atmospheric storytelling. Players spending six or more hours daily in the city of Hesperia need gear that prioritizes audio separation for complex soundtracks and ergonomic support to prevent fatigue during long Esper ability rotations.

### Truthear Nova In-Ear Monitor
The Truthear Nova has quickly become a favorite for players who want to hear every nuance of the game's Unreal Engine 5 powered soundscape.

*   **Price:** $149.99
*   **Best For:** Thematic Value & Vocal Clarity
*   **Pros:**
    *   Hybrid 1DD+4BA configuration provides exceptional detail for gacha soundtracks.
    *   Tuned to the Harman IE 2019 curve for a natural, non-fatiguing sound.
    *   DLP 3D-printed resin shells offer a premium, ergonomic fit for most users.
*   **Cons:**
    *   Large 6.2mm nozzle diameter can cause discomfort for those with small ear canals.
    *   Harman-target tuning lacks the mid-bass "thump" some prefer for cinematic boss themes.

**Verdict:** The Truthear Nova is a technical powerhouse at the $150 price point, utilizing a 3-way crossover to separate complex orchestral layers in NTE. Its focus on upper-midrange clarity makes it the ideal choice for players who prioritize hearing every line of character dialogue from Nanally or Mint.

### SteelSeries Arctis Nova Pro Wireless
For those who refuse to be tethered to a desk, this headset offers the most robust feature set for high-end gaming.

*   **Price:** $349.99
*   **Best For:** Premium Wireless Versatility
*   **Pros:**
    *   Hot-swappable dual-battery system ensures zero downtime during 12-hour grinds.
    *   Simultaneous 2.4GHz and Bluetooth allows for Discord chat while playing on mobile or PC.
    *   Active Noise Cancellation (ANC) effectively blocks out household distractions.
*   **Cons:**
    *   Internal ANC "nubs" can touch the ears of some users, causing physical irritation.
    *   Requires the SteelSeries GG Sonar software to unlock its full 10-band parametric EQ.

**Verdict:** The Arctis Nova Pro Wireless is the gold standard for NTE players who swap between PC and mobile. Its 40mm Neodymium drivers and 360° Spatial Audio provide a level of immersion that matches the game's high-fidelity urban environments and the intense sound design of [The Ichi-daime](https://www.reddit.com/r/NTE_NevernessToEverness/comments/1eb213u/nanally_character_card/) banner events.

### SteelSeries Arctis Nova 7 Wireless
A more accessible entry into the Nova ecosystem that doesn't sacrifice the essential features needed for modern RPGs.

*   **Price:** $179.99
*   **Best For:** Mid-Range Wireless Comfort
*   **Pros:**
    *   AirWeave memory foam ear cushions stay cool during long summer gaming sessions.
    *   38-hour battery life with fast charging (15 mins for 6 hours of use).
    *   Fully retractable ClearCast Gen 2 mic with bidirectional noise cancellation.
*   **Cons:**
    *   Earpads are shallower than the Pro model, which may lead to ear-to-driver contact.
    *   Lacks the dedicated base station of the Pro, relying on a USB-C dongle for connectivity.

**Verdict:** For players who want the "Nova" experience without the premium price tag, the Arctis Nova 7 offers the best balance of comfort and connectivity. It retains the dual-wireless capability essential for managing Discord-coordinated raids while grinding for anomaly materials.

### Secretlab TITAN Evo 2022 Regular
Physical endurance is just as important as in-game stats, and the TITAN Evo is designed for the long haul.

*   **Price:** $549.00
*   **Best For:** Long-Session Ergonomic Support
*   **Pros:**
    *   Proprietary [cold-cure foam](https://secretlab.co/products/titan-evo-2022-series) provides consistent support for 8+ hour sessions.
    *   4-way [L-ADAPT lumbar system](https://secretlab.co/products/titan-evo-2022-series) adjusts to the unique curvature of your spine.
    *   Magnetic memory foam head pillow allows for easy height adjustment without straps.
*   **Cons:**
    *   The seat foam is notably firm, requiring a "break-in" period of roughly 50 hours.
    *   Internal lumbar mechanism can occasionally produce a creaking sound when shifting weight.

**Verdict:** The TITAN Evo 2022 is a critical investment for NTE players prone to lower back pain. Its pebble seat base encourages natural movement, while the 165-degree recline is perfect for relaxing during the game's cinematic story beats or while managing your urban properties.

### Razer Naga V2 Pro Wireless MMO Mouse
NTE's combat system requires quick access to multiple skills and items, making a multi-button mouse a significant advantage.

*   **Price:** $179.99
*   **Best For:** Macro Management & Skill Rotations
*   **Pros:**
    *   Three [swappable side plates](https://www.razer.com/gaming-mice/razer-naga-v2-pro) (2, 6, and 12 buttons) for different game genres.
    *   <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Razer+HyperScroll+Pro+wheel&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Razer HyperScroll Pro wheel</a> offers adjustable tension and tactile feedback.
    *   Focus Pro 30K Optical Sensor ensures pixel-perfect tracking on any surface.
*   **Cons:**
    *   At 134g, it is significantly heavier than modern "superlight" competitive mice.
    *   The 12-button side plate has a steep learning curve for thumb muscle memory.

**Verdict:** The Naga V2 Pro is the ultimate tool for NTE players managing complex character rotations. The ability to map an entire skill bar to your thumb reduces keyboard hand fatigue, which is a common issue during high-intensity boss encounters in the Hesperia outskirts.

## What an NTE player actually needs

![What an NTE player actually needs](/img/aurelia-nova-vs-lacrimosa-nte-comparison-20260614-225f/inline-2.webp)


Neverness to Everness is not a typical mobile-first gacha; its Unreal Engine 5 foundation means it demands more from your hardware than its predecessors.

1.  **Vocal Clarity:** With a heavy emphasis on character-driven dialogue and story-rich missions involving characters like Sakiri, audio gear must have a clean midrange. Muffled audio can lead to missing crucial plot points during unvoiced or subtitled segments where environmental noise is high.
2.  **Multi-Device Connectivity:** The ability to swap between a high-end PC for story immersion and a mobile device for daily tasks is a staple of the NTE lifestyle. Headsets like the Arctis Nova series, which offer simultaneous Bluetooth and 2.4GHz wireless, allow players to stay on a Discord call with their guild while transitioning between devices.
3.  **Lumbar Support:** The "gamer slouch" is a real threat during the 50+ hour break-in periods of new patches. A chair with adjustable lumbar tension, such as the <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Secretlab+TITAN+Evo&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Secretlab TITAN Evo</a>, is non-negotiable for maintaining spinal health during extended sessions of car customization and city exploration.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/aurelia-nova-vs-lacrimosa-nte-comparison-20260614-225f/inline-4.webp)


Not every player needs to rush out and buy a $500 chair or a $350 headset. If you already own a high-end open-back headphone like the Sennheiser HD 600, the Truthear Nova will feel like a side-grade rather than a true upgrade, as it sacrifices soundstage for isolation.

Similarly, do not buy the TITAN Evo if you are under 5'2"; the "Regular" size will be too large, preventing your feet from resting flat on the floor and negating the ergonomic benefits. In such cases, you should look for the "Small" variant or a dedicated petite chair. Furthermore, if you only [play Neverness to Everness](/faq) casually for 30 minutes a day to complete daily commissions, your standard smartphone earbuds or a basic office chair are likely sufficient for the task. The gear listed here is specifically curated for those performing 6+ hour sessions where physical and auditory fatigue become significant factors.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Neverness to Everness better with IEMs or a full headset?
It depends on your environment. IEMs like the Truthear Nova offer superior detail and passive noise isolation, making them better for hearing subtle audio cues in the city. However, a full headset like the Arctis Nova Pro provides a wider soundstage and better long-term comfort for those who find in-ear tips irritating over several hours.

### Do I need an MMO mouse for NTE combat?
While not strictly necessary, a mouse like the Razer Naga V2 Pro allows you to map your Esper abilities and item shortcuts to your thumb. This significantly reduces the strain on your keyboard hand, which is beneficial during the complex skill rotations required for characters like Nanally.

### Can I play NTE on mobile with these headsets?
Yes, both the Arctis Nova Pro Wireless and the Arctis Nova 7 Wireless feature Bluetooth connectivity, allowing them to pair directly with smartphones or tablets. This makes them ideal for players who want a seamless transition from their PC setup to mobile play for daily tasks.

### Is the Secretlab TITAN Evo too firm for casual gaming?
The TITAN Evo uses high-density cold-cure foam designed for support rather than immediate plushness. While it may feel firm initially, it is designed to prevent the "bottoming out" that occurs with softer chairs, ensuring your spine remains aligned during the long hours required for car customization and open-world exploration.

### The Role of Sustain in the Esper Cycle

The Esper Cycle is the core combat loop of NTE, where switching characters and triggering elemental reactions fills a central gauge. Healers play a dual role here: they must provide enough health to survive "Stagger" windows while contributing enough elemental application to keep the cycle moving. 

Jiuyuan, for instance, does not heal with a simple button press. She applies marks that detonate for healing when the Esper Cycle resets or when she uses her Ultimate. This encourages a "swap-and-drop" playstyle where the healer facilitates the next character's entry rather than just fixing mistakes.

### Top Healer Rankings for May 2026

Jiuyuan currently holds the top spot for players who have reached her first Awakening (A1). Her ability to convert Anima DMG into team-wide healing makes her indispensable for "Blossom" teams. However, for players looking for a more consistent, low-investment option, Esper Zero is the premier choice. Zero provides "Charge" reactions that restore HP based on a percentage of Base ATK, allowing you to build them as a secondary damage dealer without sacrificing survivability.

Edgar remains the king of F2P progression. While he lacks the offensive utility of the S-Rank units, his raw HP-scaling heals are the most powerful in the game for pure recovery. If your team is under-leveled for a specific boss, Edgar is the safest "panic button" to include in your roster.

In Neverness to Everness, weapons are known as Arcs. Choosing the right Arc for your healer depends entirely on their scaling stat. Most modern healers in the May 2026 meta scale off ATK to maintain damage relevance, but traditional supports still rely on Max HP for their potency.

### S-Tier Arcs for Endgame Healing

The gold standard for HP-scaling healers like Edgar is **Eternal Waltz**. This S-Rank Synthesis Arc provides a flat 20% Max HP boost, which directly increases the healing ceiling. Additionally, it offers a unique passive that buffs the team's Mental DMG after the wearer uses a support skill, bridging the gap between pure healing and utility.

For ATK-scaling hybrids like Zero, **Marching Beyond Time** is the undisputed Best-in-Slot (BiS). It maximizes the "Appraiser" passive by boosting Base ATK and providing an energy recharge bonus for the Ultimate. Because Zero's healing is tied to reaction frequency, the faster they can cycle their Ultimate, the higher the team's effective HP becomes.

**Good Boy's Grand Adventure** is a specialized S-Rank Gas Arc that introduces a layer of RNG utility. Upon using an Ultimate, it triggers a random effect: either a targeted heal for the lowest HP ally or a team-wide shield. While less consistent than Eternal Waltz, it is highly valued in "Discord" team comps where damage redirection is the primary survival mechanic.

### Best F2P and Budget Arcs

If you haven't pulled S-Rank Arcs yet, **The Forgotten** (B-Rank, Solid) is a surprisingly potent alternative for Jiuyuan or Zero. It grants a 20% ATK boost as long as the wearer’s HP remains above 50%. Since healers generally stay off-field or sustain themselves, maintaining this buff is relatively easy.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/best-healer-build-nte-guide-20260516/inline-5.webp)


To get the most out of your healer, you cannot simply mash buttons. You must manage specific stacks and cooldowns to ensure healing is available when you actually need it.

### Jiuyuan: The Detonation Heal Loop

Jiuyuan's healing is back-loaded, meaning you need to set it up before the damage happens.
1.  **Step 1:** Use Jiuyuan’s Skill to apply **Lethal Rose Pact** stacks to all enemies.
2.  **Step 2:** Swap to your main DPS (like Nanally) and attack to build the Esper Cycle gauge.
3.  **Step 3:** Once the team’s HP dips or the gauge is full, swap back to Jiuyuan.
4.  **Step 4:** Use her Ultimate to detonate all marks. This triggers a massive team-wide heal based on the number of stacks consumed.

### Edgar: Emergency Burst Healing

Edgar should spend as little time on-field as possible to maximize your team's DPS uptime.
1.  **Step 1:** Maintain your rotation with your DPS and Sub-DPS.
2.  **Step 2:** Tag Edgar in only when an ally falls below 30% HP.
3.  **Step 3:** Use **Wild Current**. This skill automatically targets the ally with the lowest HP and restores a massive amount of health based on *their* Max HP.
4.  **Step 4:** Immediately swap back to your DPS. Edgar’s long cooldowns mean he is purely for emergencies.

### Zero: Passive Sustain via Reactions

Zero is the easiest healer to pilot because their sustain is mostly automatic.
1.  **Step 1:** Use Zero’s Skill to instantly fill a portion of the Cycle Gauge and apply the Cosmos status.
2.  **Step 2:** Swap to an Anima or Psyche character to trigger reactions.
3.  **Step 3:** Zero’s "Appraiser" passive will now trigger healing every time a reaction occurs, even while Zero is off-field.
4.  **Step 4:** Only use Zero’s Ultimate during "Stagger" windows to maximize the ATK buff provided to the team.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/best-healer-build-nte-guide-20260516/inline-6.webp)


**Does NTE have out-of-combat healing?**
Yes. You can restore your team's HP by visiting Wertheimer Towers or using ReroRero Phone Booths scattered around the city. Additionally, you can consume food items.

**Is Edgar worth building?**
Edgar is excellent for early to mid-game progression or for players who struggle with dodging boss mechanics. However, in the current May 2026 endgame meta, he is eventually outclassed by hybrid units like Jiuyuan who contribute more to overall team damage.

**How do I unlock Jiuyuan's healing?**
Jiuyuan does not start as a primary healer. You must reach Awakening 1 (A1) or A2 through the resonance system to unlock the sustain capabilities of her Lethal Rose Pact.

**What is the best food for healing?**
High-tier dishes crafted in the City Tycoon mode provide the best HP recovery. Specifically, meals purchased or cooked from the Cafe by Origen offer long-term regen buffs that are very helpful in open-world exploration.

**Can I play without a healer?**
It is possible but difficult. High-level players often use Adler's shields combined with Fadia's damage redirection to bypass the need for a dedicated healer, but this requires perfect timing and high-end gear.

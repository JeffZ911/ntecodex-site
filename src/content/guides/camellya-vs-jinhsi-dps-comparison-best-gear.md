## How we picked these peripherals

![How we picked these peripherals](/img/camellya-vs-jinhsi-dps-comparison-best-gear/inline-1.webp)


To ensure this list met the needs of the most dedicated players, we applied a rigorous filtering process based on both technical specifications and community feedback from hardware enthusiasts.

*   **Panel Technology:** We prioritized monitors with Nano IPS or OLED panels. These technologies are essential for maintaining color accuracy and motion clarity, which are vital when recording footage for frame-counting.
*   **Input Precision:** Mice were selected based on their button density and sensor precision. A minimum threshold of 30,000 DPI and a 1,000Hz+ polling rate was required to ensure that high-APM (actions per minute) rotations were captured without dropouts.
*   **Actuation Speed:** Keyboards were filtered for "Rapid Trigger" or Hall Effect capabilities. Standard mechanical switches often have a "debounce" delay that can add several milliseconds to an input; we only chose boards that minimize this latency.
*   **Ergonomic Data:** Ergonomic gear was vetted through resources like [RTINGS](https://www.rtings.com/) and specialized communities to ensure that the gear provides actual physiological benefits during 4+ hour sessions.

## What 'DPS Testers' actually need

![What 'DPS Testers' actually need](/img/camellya-vs-jinhsi-dps-comparison-best-gear/inline-2.webp)


Testing the damage potential of characters in a complex RPG like Neverness to Everness is different from casual play. It requires a level of consistency that standard office peripherals cannot provide.

### Low Input Latency
When performing a detailed character comparison, a difference of even 5ms in keyboard actuation can skew results. If one character requires rapid-fire skill presses to maintain a buff, a keyboard with a high actuation point will naturally result in lower recorded DPS compared to a board with 0.1mm sensitivity. Technical accuracy starts at the fingertip.

### Motion Clarity
High-refresh monitors (144Hz or higher) are not just for aesthetics. They are required to see the exact frame an animation starts or ends. In a game built on Unreal Engine 5, visual effects can be dense; a monitor with a fast response time helps distinguish between a character's hit-frame and the environmental particle effects.

### Sustained Comfort
Testing often requires repetitive motions—running the same boss fight 50 times to find an average damage value. Hardware must minimize wrist strain and provide lumbar support to prevent fatigue-related errors. A tired tester is a sloppy tester, and sloppy data is useless for the community.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does a higher monitor refresh rate actually improve DPS?
No, a monitor refresh rate does not change the game's internal math or damage numbers. However, it allows the player to see animation frames more clearly, making it easier to time "perfect dodges" or "animation cancels" that result in higher effective DPS.

### Is a 12-button mouse necessary for Neverness to Everness?
While not strictly necessary for casual play, a 12-button mouse like the Razer Naga V2 Pro allows you to map character swaps, skills, and item usage to a single hand. This is a significant advantage for testers who need to execute complex rotations with 100% consistency.

### What is "Rapid Trigger" and why does it matter for testing?
Rapid Trigger is a feature found in analog keyboards that allows a key to reset the instant it travels upward, rather than waiting to pass a fixed reset point. This allows for much faster repeated inputs, which is critical for characters that rely on "spamming" certain abilities or fast-paced animation canceling.

### How does lumbar support affect gaming performance?
Lumbar support maintains the natural curve of your spine, which prevents muscle fatigue in your lower back. When you are comfortable, you can maintain focus for longer periods, reducing the likelihood of making "input errors" during a long data-collection session.

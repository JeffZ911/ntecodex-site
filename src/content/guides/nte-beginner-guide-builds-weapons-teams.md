# Neverness to Everness Beginner Guide: Best Builds, Weapons, and Teams
> 📝 **Editorial Note:** This guide is AI-assisted and game data evolves rapidly. Please cross-reference with in-game information. _Updated: 2026-05-13._


Neverness to Everness (NTE) officially launched on April 29, 2026, introducing players to a sprawling urban open-world RPG where the line between reality and the supernatural is razor-thin. As an "unlicensed" Anomaly Hunter operating out of the Eibon antique shop hub, you are tasked with investigating urban legends and containing anomalies that threaten the city. Mastering the game requires a deep understanding of its unique combat systems, specifically the Esper Cycle, and knowing how to optimize your characters with the right Arcs and Console configurations.

## Overview

![Overview](/img/nte-beginner-guide-builds-weapons-teams/inline-1.webp)


In Neverness to Everness, combat revolves around a group of supernatural individuals known as Espers. Each character is attuned to one of six elements, and your success depends on how you cycle through these elements to trigger devastating reactions. The game follows the story of the protagonist, Esper Zero, a versatile Cosmos-attribute carry who serves as the foundation for most early-game rosters.

Progress in NTE is gated by two primary systems: Hunter Level and Appraisal Level. While Hunter Level tracks your overall experience, your Appraisal Level is the true measure of your progression. It determines the quality of rewards you receive from anomaly investigations and gates access to high-level endgame content like "Beyond the Rails."

### Understanding the Esper Cycle

The "Esper Cycle" is the core elemental system of NTE. Elements are arranged in two trios on the Esper Wheel: the Top Trio (Lakshana, Cosmos, Anima) and the Bottom Trio (Incantation, Chaos, Psyche). Reactions, known as Duo Cycles, occur only between neighboring elements on this wheel.

*   **Lakshana + Cosmos = Remora:** Marks enemies and slows their movement and attack speed.
*   **Cosmos + Anima = Blossom:** Spawns a "Vita Bud" turret that fires AOE projectiles.
*   **Anima + Incantation = Hexed:** Deals follow-up damage based on a percentage of damage taken by the enemy.
*   **Incantation + Chaos = Scorch:** Applies a 15-second Damage-over-Time (DoT) effect.
*   **Chaos + Psyche = Nova:** Attaches a mark to the enemy that explodes for burst damage after a delay.
*   **Psyche + Lakshana = Stain:** Increases the damage enemies take from Psyche and Lakshana attributes.

### Hunter Level vs. Appraisal Level

Your Hunter Level increases as you complete missions and explore Hethereau, but you must focus on your Appraisal Level to unlock higher-rarity gear. High Appraisal Levels increase the drop rate of Orange-rarity Modules and S-Rank Arcs from world bosses. You can raise this level by completing "Appraisal Exams" at the Bureau of Anomaly Control once you reach specific Hunter Level milestones.

## Best Weapons

![Best Weapons](/img/nte-beginner-guide-builds-weapons-teams/inline-2.webp)


In Neverness to Everness, weapons are known as "Arcs." They are categorized into five types: Plasma, Solid, Gas, Liquid, and Synthesis. A character can only equip an Arc that matches their compatibility type. Arcs provide not only base stats like ATK and CRIT but also unique passives that can be enhanced through "Mixing" (refining) using duplicate copies.

### Top DPS Arcs

For high-end damage, S-Rank Arcs are the gold standard. **Ready-Ready (Plasma)** is currently the premier choice for boss killing, specifically designed as the signature weapon for Nanally. Its passive, "Commander Tiger Talisman," increases ATK by 15% and grants a significant damage boost against bosses when you alternate between Redirect Skills and Ultimates.

Another top-tier option is the **Camellia Society (Synthesis)** Arc. This weapon is built for high-risk, high-reward CRIT builds. It grants a 12% CRIT Rate and triggers the "Silent Garden" effect, which consumes a small portion of the wearer's HP to unleash random high-damage Incantation attacks. It is the best-in-slot choice for characters like Baicang or Fadia who can manage their health pools effectively.

### Best Support and Utility Arcs

Utility Arcs focus on team-wide buffs or summoning assistance. **Hethereau's Keeper (Plasma)** is a standout for summoner-style play. It increases ATK and damage to bosses, but its unique feature is summoning "Officer Whisker," an anomaly that assists in combat by dealing damage equal to a percentage of the wearer's ATK. This is exceptionally strong on Sakiri to help manage crowd control while adding off-field pressure.

For F2P players, **Umbrella** is a reliable A-Rank choice for defensive utility, often used on Adler to bolster his shielding capabilities. Another recommended A-Rank Arc for consistent DPS is [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-13], which provides a steady damage increase without complex activation requirements.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

![Best Disks (Artifacts)](/img/nte-beginner-guide-builds-weapons-teams/inline-3.webp)


The "Console" system is NTE’s version of gear or artifacts. It consists of a grid where you place "Modules"—Tetris-like puzzle pieces that provide raw stats—and "Cartridges," which provide set bonuses.

### The Console and Module Puzzle System

To maximize a character's potential, you must fit Modules into their unique grid to activate 2-piece and 4-piece Cartridge set bonuses. The system is remarkably transparent; all sub-stats on a Module are visible before you spend resources to upgrade them, allowing you to plan your builds with precision.

Farming for high-rarity Orange Modules requires spending [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-13] (stamina) in the Rabbit Hole Anomaly Zone. These zones are combat challenges that drop specific Cartridge sets based on the day of the week.

### Best Early-Game Cartridge Sets

For beginners, the **Speedy Hedgehog** set is invaluable. Its 2-piece bonus increases Charge Efficiency by 12%, while the 4-piece bonus grants all allies a 15% ATK buff for 20 seconds after the wearer casts an Ultimate. Since this buff does not stack, you only need one character in your team (usually a support like Haniel or Adler) to run this set.

The **Lost Radiance** set is the go-to for Cosmos DPS characters like Esper Zero. It provides a 10% Cosmos DMG boost at 2 pieces and allows the wearer to ignore 25% of enemy DEF for 20 seconds after using an Ultimate at 4 pieces.

## Team Comp

![Team Comp](/img/nte-beginner-guide-builds-weapons-teams/inline-4.webp)


Effective team building in NTE relies on the "Elemental Lane" concept. You want to pair characters whose elements are adjacent on the Esper Wheel to ensure you can trigger Duo and Triple Cycle reactions constantly.

### Top-Tier Meta Teams

The current meta for single-target boss shredding is the **"Discord" Meta Team**, consisting of Nanally, Sakiri, Daffodil, and Fadia. While this team mixes elements, it focuses on the raw power of Nanally’s follow-up attacks and the massive Stagger damage provided by Daffodil’s Chaos attribute. Fadia ensures the team stays alive with her survival-focused Psyche kit, while Sakiri provides the necessary ATK buffs.

For clearing mob waves in the streets of Hethereau, the **"Blossom" AoE Team** is superior. This team uses Sakiri, Jiuyuan, and Esper Zero. By cycling between Zero (Cosmos) and Sakiri (Anima), you trigger the Blossom reaction, spawning multiple Vita Buds that clear the field. Jiuyuan can then be swapped in to trigger the "Charge" Triple Cycle (Lakshana + Cosmos + Anima) to rapidly refill the team's Ultimate energy.

### Best Free-to-Play (F2P) Teams

New players should focus on the **Standard Starter Team**: Esper Zero (Main DPS), Haniel (ATK Buffer), and Edgar (Healer). This team is exceptionally well-rounded. Edgar provides consistent healing through his Cosmos-attribute skills, while Haniel’s Psyche buffs ensure Zero deals enough damage to clear early story chapters. Because Zero and Edgar share the Cosmos attribute, they can easily set up reactions for a fourth flex pick like Adler or Jiuyuan.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/nte-beginner-guide-builds-weapons-teams/inline-5.webp)


Combat in NTE is a rhythmic dance of filling meters and swapping characters at the exact right moment. Every character has an Esper Meter next to their portrait. Once this meter is full, the portrait will glow—this is your **Switch Signal**.

### Executing the Esper Cycle

A standard meta rotation for the Blossom Reaction follows this flow

1.  **Start with Jiuyuan:** Use her "Lethal Rose Pact" to apply debuffs and build initial energy.
2.  **Swap to Sakiri:** Trigger her crowd control skill to gather enemies into a tight group.
3.  **Swap to Zero:** Use Zero's Skill or Basic Attacks to fill the Esper Meter. Zero’s Ultimate can instantly fill the meter of the next character in line.
4.  **Swap to Nanally:** Trigger the **Blossom Reaction** (Cosmos + Anima). This will spawn the Vita Bud turrets.
5.  **Repeat:** Continue cycling to maintain the turrets and trigger "Charge" for energy.

### Maximizing the Stun Window

Every elite enemy and boss has a white "Stun Meter" (Break bar) below their health. You should save your most powerful Ultimates for when this bar is depleted. While the enemy is stunned, they take significantly increased damage. Characters like Daffodil are specialized in "Breaking" these bars quickly using the Discord reaction (Incantation + Chaos + Psyche).

### Tips for Redirect and Dodge

Maintaining momentum is key. Use **Redirect Skills** to parry incoming attacks; a successful parry instantly fills a character’s entire Esper Meter. If you cannot parry, a "Critical Dodge" will still grant a significant burst of energy and often triggers character-specific passives, such as those found on the Camellia Society Arc.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/nte-beginner-guide-builds-weapons-teams/inline-6.webp)


**How does the NTE gacha work?**
The gacha system in Neverness to Everness, known as "Scarborough Fair," is one of the most player-friendly in the genre. There is **no 50/50 system**; if you pull an S-Rank character on a limited banner, it is guaranteed to be the featured character. Hard pity is at 90 pulls, but "Board Modification" at 70 pulls significantly increases your chances of an early drop to 19.59%.

**Where can I get free S-Rank Arcs?**
Several S-Rank Arcs can be obtained by defeating world bosses. For example, the **Raging Flames** Arc is a reward for defeating the Headless Biker in the Bridge Crossings area. You can also obtain the **Day Off** Arc by completing the hidden "Eclipse" questline involving the Bureau of Anomaly Control.

**Is there a reroll guide for NTE?**
Rerolling is possible but time-consuming. However, the game provides a "Standard Board Selector." After 50 pulls on the permanent "Strange Encounters" banner, you can choose any one of the standard S-Class characters (like Jiuyuan or Sakiri) for free.

**How do I earn Fons?**
Fons is the primary currency for purchasing vehicle parts, character outfits, and property in Hethereau. You earn Fons by completing city commissions, winning street races, or managing businesses in the "City Tycoon" simulation mode.

## What a long-session player actually needs

![What a long-session player actually needs](/img/nte-vs-genshin-impact-graphics-comparison-gear/inline-2.webp)


Long-session players face a unique set of challenges compared to casual users. Eye strain is the most immediate concern, making flicker-free technology and low blue light certifications mandatory. These features reduce the micro-fluctuations in backlighting that cause headaches over several hours of farming resources.

High contrast ratios, ideally 1000:1 for IPS or infinite for OLED, are necessary to distinguish details in dark environments or night cycles. In a game like Neverness to Everness, which features significant urban night-time exploration, a monitor with poor contrast will turn shadows into a gray, washed-out mess, making it difficult to spot Anomalies or navigate alleyways.

Ergonomic adjustability, including height, tilt, and swivel, is vital to maintain a neutral spine position. If a monitor is fixed too low, players tend to hunch forward, leading to "tech neck." A stand that allows the top third of the screen to sit at eye level is the ergonomic standard for preventing long-term strain.

## Comparison summary

![Comparison summary](/img/nte-vs-genshin-impact-graphics-comparison-gear/inline-3.webp)


The graphical divergence between Neverness to Everness (NTE) and Genshin Impact is primarily rooted in their respective engines. NTE is built on Unreal Engine 5, utilizing advanced features like Lumen global illumination and Nanite virtualized geometry. This results in a more realistic lighting model, especially in urban environments where neon signs and car headlights reflect off wet pavement.

In contrast, Genshin Impact utilizes a highly modified Unity engine to achieve its signature cel-shaded, painterly aesthetic. While it may not have the ray-traced reflections of a UE5 title, its art direction emphasizes color harmony and sweeping vistas. 

OLED panels offer the best visual experience for NTE because they can handle the high dynamic range (HDR) required for supernatural urban settings. The ability to have a bright neon sign next to a pitch-black shadow without "blooming" or "halo" effects is a massive advantage. However, players must be mindful of static UI elements like the mini-map or character icons, which are common in both games.

IPS panels remain the most reliable choice for consistent color and longevity. For players who spend hundreds of hours on a single title, the lack of burn-in risk provides peace of mind. Ultrawide monitors provide the most cinematic experience, expanding the field of view to see more of the landscape at once, though they require more powerful GPUs to maintain high frame rates at the increased resolution.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/nte-vs-genshin-impact-graphics-comparison-gear/inline-4.webp)


Not every player needs to rush out and buy a $900 OLED monitor. If you primarily play on mobile or a handheld device like the Steam Deck, a high-end desktop monitor upgrade will offer diminishing returns. The compressed textures and lower resolution of mobile versions cannot take full advantage of a 1440p or 4K high-refresh display.

Furthermore, if your current PC hardware cannot maintain at least 60 FPS at 1440p, upgrading to a high-refresh monitor will result in a choppy experience. A 240Hz monitor is wasted if your GPU is struggling to push 45 FPS; in fact, the stuttering may be more noticeable on a high-spec screen. It is often better to upgrade your GPU or CPU before investing in premium peripherals.

Finally, do not buy an OLED monitor if you leave your game running on a static screen for hours while AFK. Many gacha players leave their characters standing in a hub world while they do other tasks. This behavior significantly accelerates burn-in on OLED panels because the same pixels are firing at high brightness for extended periods. If you cannot commit to turning the screen off or using a screensaver, stick with IPS.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does Neverness to Everness support 21:9 ultrawide resolutions?
Most modern Unreal Engine 5 titles support ultrawide resolutions natively, allowing players to see more of the urban environment. However, certain UI elements or cutscenes may still revert to a 16:9 aspect ratio with black bars on the sides depending on how the developers have optimized the specific build.

### Is an OLED monitor worth it for Genshin Impact?
An OLED monitor significantly enhances Genshin Impact's visual depth, particularly in areas with high contrast like the Underground Mines or during night cycles in Liyue. The infinite contrast makes the elemental effects and "glow" of certain abilities pop much more than on a standard LCD, though the risk of HUD burn-in remains a factor for long-term players.

### What is the best monitor size for 1440p gaming?
27 inches is widely considered the "sweet spot" for 1440p resolution because it provides a high pixel density (PPI) that keeps images sharp without requiring Windows scaling. At 32 inches, the pixels are more spread out, which can lead to a slightly softer image if you sit close to the screen.

### How can I prevent eye strain during 4+ hour gaming sessions?
To prevent eye strain, use a monitor with a flicker-free backlight and enable a "low blue light" mode during evening sessions. Additionally, following the 20-20-20 rule—looking at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds every 20 minutes—helps relax the eye muscles that get fatigued from staring at a fixed distance.

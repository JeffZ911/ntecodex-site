### Who are the NTE Starter Characters?

The starter roster consists of Zero (the customizable protagonist), Mint, Adler, and Haniel. These characters are unlocked naturally as you navigate the opening chapters of the Hethereau storyline. Zero serves as the backbone of most early teams, offering consistent Cosmos damage. Mint acts as a high-mobility Anima attacker, while Adler provides the defensive utility necessary for surviving high-threat anomalies. Haniel rounds out the group by offering Psyche-based buffs that enhance the team's overall combat efficiency.

### The Role of the Main Character (Zero)

Zero is a unique unit in NTE because their power scales directly with world progression. As a Cosmos-element character with a "Solid" compatibility type, Zero is designed to stay on the field for extended periods. Their kit focuses on building "Cosmic Resonance" to unleash heavy-hitting area-of-effect (AoE) attacks. ### Understanding Esper Cycles for Beginners

The Esper system is the heart of NTE’s combat. Characters do not operate in isolation; instead, they contribute to an "Esper Cycle." When you use an ability, you apply an elemental status to the enemy. Switching to a character of a different element at the right moment triggers an "Entry Skill" and a reaction. For starters, the most important cycle to learn is Cosmos + Anima. This combination triggers "Blossom," which creates automated energy turrets on the battlefield that continue to deal damage even after your characters have swapped out.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

![Best Disks (Artifacts)](/img/best-build-starter-characters-nte/inline-3.webp)


The gear system in NTE is divided into "Disks," which consist of "Cartridges" (set bonuses) and "Modules" (individual stat pieces). Unlike traditional gear systems, NTE uses a Tetris-style "Console" where you must fit different-shaped Modules into a grid to activate set bonuses.

### Understanding Cartridges and Modules

A Cartridge bonus is activated when you place 2 or 4 Modules of the same set into your character’s Console. Modules come in various shapes (L-shape, T-shape, Square), and the challenge lies in maximizing the grid space to fit the best possible stats.

*   **Main Stats:** Modules provide primary stats like ATK%, Crit Rate, or DEF%.
*   **Sub-stats:** As you level up Modules, they unlock random sub-stats. For starters, focus on finding Modules with at least one "flat ATK" or "Crit" sub-stat.

### Best Disk Sets for DPS (Zero & Mint)

*   **Fireflies and the Forest (Set):** This is the mandatory choice for Anima characters like Mint. The 2-piece bonus increases Anima DMG by 10%, while the 4-piece bonus stacks Critical DMG every time the character deals damage, up to a maximum of 40% [exact value pending official confirmation].
*   **Cosmic Traveler (Set):** The best set for Zero. It focuses on increasing "Break" damage, allowing Zero to deplete enemy stagger bars significantly faster.

### Best Disk Sets for Supports (Haniel & Sakiri)

*   **Speedy Hedgehog (Set):** This set is essential for Sakiri and Haniel. The 4-piece bonus grants the entire team a 15% Energy Recharge boost for 10 seconds after the wearer uses their Ultimate.
*   **Guardian of Hethereau (Set):** Ideal for Adler. It scales off DEF% and increases the strength of any shields generated by the character.

### Module Placement: Maximizing the Console Grid

When arranging your Console, always place your 4-piece Cartridge Modules first. These are usually larger shapes. Fill the remaining gaps with "Off-set" Modules that have high-quality sub-stats. Early in the game, it is better to have a 2-piece set bonus with great stats than a forced 4-piece bonus with useless stats like "HP Recovery Bonus" on a DPS character.

## Rotation

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/best-build-starter-characters-nte/inline-6.webp)


**Is Zero (MC) viable for endgame content?**
Yes. Zero is designed to be a competitive 5-star unit. Their ability to switch between different "Solid" and "Liquid" styles in later chapters makes them one of the most versatile units in the game.

**Which S-Rank character should I choose from the Standard Selector?**
Sakiri is the top recommendation for most players. Her ability to group enemies (Crowd Control) and apply the "Hexed" debuff makes her an invaluable support that fits into every team archetype.

**How do I obtain more Arcs without spending Lunaria?**
You can obtain high-quality Arcs by completing "Urban Errand" side quests, reaching high levels in the "Kaleidoscope" challenge mode, and purchasing them from the "Beetle Coin Exchange" using currency earned from daily activities.

**What is the most important stat for early game progression?**
Flat ATK and ATK% are the most important stats early on. Critical Rate and Critical DMG only become superior once you have high-level Modules that allow you to reach at least a 50% Crit Rate threshold.

**Should I prioritize leveling characters or their Arcs first?**
Prioritize leveling your characters to the current world cap first, as this increases their base survivability. However, your Main DPS character's Arc should always be at the maximum possible level to ensure you don't hit a "damage check" wall against bosses.

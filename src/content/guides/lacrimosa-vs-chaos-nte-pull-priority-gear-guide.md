## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/lacrimosa-vs-chaos-nte-pull-priority-gear-guide/inline-4.webp)


While better gear can improve the gaming experience, it isn't always a necessary purchase. If your current chair already allows your feet to sit flat on the floor and your monitor is positioned at eye level, a $1,600 chair won't fix poor posture habits. Ergonomic benefits are only realized if you actively utilize the adjustments provided by the equipment.

Additionally, you should avoid buying a high-refresh-rate monitor like the LG UltraGear if your current PC hardware cannot consistently push 60 FPS in a demanding title like Neverness to Everness. Without a capable GPU, the 160Hz refresh rate will be wasted. Finally, skip high-end MMO mice if you primarily play with a controller; the complex macro buttons and side panels will go unused, making a standard ergonomic mouse a more cost-effective choice.

## What we didn't include and why

![What we didn't include and why](/img/lacrimosa-vs-chaos-nte-pull-priority-gear-guide/inline-5.webp)


We excluded several popular "gaming" categories that often fail to meet the needs of serious long-session players.

*   **DXRacer Formula Series and similar bucket seats:** The side bolsters on these chairs are designed for lateral G-forces in racing cars, which do not exist at a desk. In a gaming context, these bolsters simply push the shoulders forward, contributing to "rounded shoulder" posture and long-term discomfort.
*   **Generic "Gaming" Headsets under $50:** These budget options typically use poor-quality drivers that cause "listener fatigue" due to spiked treble and muddy bass. This makes long sessions painful for the ears and provides poor spatial awareness in-game.
*   **Full-sized 104-key Keyboards:** The inclusion of a numpad on the right side forces your mouse arm into an unnaturally wide angle. This increases the risk of shoulder impingement during long sessions. We opted for the 75% layout of the Keychron K2 to keep your arms in a more ergonomic, neutral position.

# Neverness to Everness Healer Build Guide: Best Weapons, Disks, and Teams

In the weeks following the April 29, 2026, global launch of Neverness to Everness (NTE), the community has quickly realized that "face-tanking" bosses is only viable with a dedicated sustain unit. While the game rewards precise dodging and parrying, the sheer density of projectiles in "Beyond the Rails" content makes healers an essential part of the meta. Whether you are using the F2P-friendly Edgar or the high-end hybrid sustain of Fadia, optimizing your gear is the difference between a successful anomaly clear and a total team wipe.

## Overview

![Overview](/img/neverness-to-everness-healer-build-guide/inline-1.webp)


In Neverness to Everness, healers are categorized under the "Sustain" or "Support" roles. Unlike traditional MMOs where a healer might stay on the field indefinitely, NTE healers are designed for high-impact, short-duration appearances. They are the backbone of the Esper Cycle, ensuring that your Main DPS can stay aggressive without fearing a sudden knockout.

Currently, the healer roster is specialized. Edgar serves as the primary pure healer accessible to all players, while S-Rank units like Fadia and Jiuyuan offer more complex forms of damage mitigation and lifesteal. Understanding how their healing scales is vital: most restorative skills in NTE scale with either the healer's Max HP or a percentage of the target's Max HP, making defensive stats the primary driver of your healing output.

### Healer Comparison Table

| Character | Element | Role | Healing Mechanic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Edgar** | Cosmos | Sustain / Pure Healer | Direct HP restoration via "Wild Current" and healing zones. |
| **Fadia** | Psyche | Sustain / Tank | Redirects 50% of ally damage to herself; self-heals in Lilith state. |
| **Jiuyuan** | Anima | Hybrid DPS / Support | Team-wide lifesteal triggered by "Pact Settlement" (requires A1). |

### The Role of Healers in the Esper Cycle
The Esper Cycle is the rhythmic heart of NTE combat. Healers contribute to this by providing "Safe Windows." When your team energy is high but health is low, tagging in a healer to trigger a Support Skill or an Ultimate allows you to reset the combat tempo. Healers often possess high "Break Intensity," meaning they can contribute to staggering an enemy while simultaneously topping off the team's HP.

### Edgar: The Primary F2P Healer
Edgar is an A-Rank Cosmos character who remains the most consistent dedicated healer as of May 15, 2026. His kit is straightforward: he enters the field, drops a healing zone, and departs. Because he is an A-Rank unit, players can easily acquire his Awakening levels, which significantly boost his utility. His A3 Awakening, for instance, increases healing effectiveness by 15% for any ally whose health drops below 80%.

### Sustain vs. Pure Healing
It is important to distinguish between "Pure Healing" (restoring lost HP) and "Sustain" (preventing HP loss). Fadia represents the latter; she doesn't just heal—she absorbs 50% of the damage intended for her teammates. This "Redirect" mechanic is often more valuable in endgame content where bosses can one-shot fragile DPS units like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte). A balanced team often pairs a Redirect tank with a Pure Healer for maximum survivability.

## Best Weapons

![Best Weapons](/img/neverness-to-everness-healer-build-guide/inline-2.webp)


In NTE, weapons are known as Arcs. For healers, the goal is to find Arcs that provide Max HP substats or passives that trigger "Healing Bonus" effects. Arcs are type-restricted (Liquid, Synthesis, Solid, etc.), so you must match the Arc type to the character’s compatibility.

### Top 3 Healer Arcs

| Arc Name | Rarity | Type | Key Passive |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Call of the Twisted City** | A-Rank | Liquid | +12%–20% Healing Bonus for 10s after using a Redirect Skill. |
| **Eternal Waltz** | S-Rank | Synthesis | +20% Max HP; +10% Mental DMG for 10s after Ultimate. |
| **Shiny Days** | B-Rank | Liquid | +48 Break Intensity; +10% DMG against Broken units. |

### Top Tier: Call of the Twisted City
Despite being an A-Rank Arc, "Call of the Twisted City" is widely considered the Best-in-Slot (BiS) for dedicated healers like Edgar. Its primary substat provides a massive 37.5% HP boost at Level 80. The passive, "Cardboard Castle," is specifically tuned for the quick-swap meta; casting a Redirect Skill (which Edgar’s "Wild Current" qualifies as) grants a 20% Healing Bonus at Max Refinement. This ensures that the few seconds the healer spends on-field are as efficient as possible.

### Best for Tanky Supports: Eternal Waltz
"Eternal Waltz" is the signature Arc for Fadia but works exceptionally well for any Synthesis-type support that scales with health. It provides a base Max HP increase of 41.25% through its substat. For Fadia, the added Mental DMG bonus after an Ultimate allows her to transition from a protector into a temporary burst dealer in her Lilith state, making it the premier choice for hybrid sustain builds.

### F2P and Budget Options
For players who haven't pulled high-rarity Arcs, "Shiny Days" (B-Rank, Liquid) is a functional placeholder. While it doesn't provide direct healing bonuses, its focus on Break Intensity allows your healer to contribute to the "Stagger" bar faster. Staggering an enemy is a form of indirect healing, as a staggered boss cannot deal damage. For energy-hungry supports, look for any B-Rank Arc that offers "Energy Recharge" to ensure Ultimates are available during emergencies.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

![Best Disks (Artifacts)](/img/neverness-to-everness-healer-build-guide/inline-3.webp)


The equipment system in Neverness to Everness consists of Cartridges (set bonuses) and Modules (individual stat blocks). Healers prioritize survivability and "Healing Bonus" main stats to maximize their efficiency.

### Recommended Cartridge Sets
As of the current 2026 meta, the most effective healer set is the **undisclosed** set, which is commonly referred to in community guides as the "Medic" or "Nurse" set.
*   **2-Piece Bonus:** Increases Max HP by 10%.
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** Increases the wearer's Healing Bonus by 20%.

For Edgar, players should focus on the "Cosmos" element-aligned modules. Using Type III Modules is particularly effective for Edgar, as they grant a 10% additional Max HP bonus when slotted into his unique Console configuration.

### Module Stat Priority
When farming for Modules, the main stat on Slot 4 should always be **Healing Bonus %**. If that is unavailable, **HP %** is a secondary alternative.

**Main Stat Checklist:**
*   **Slot 1 (Flat HP):** Fixed.
*   **Slot 2 (Flat ATK):** Fixed.
*   **Slot 3 (HP % or Energy Recharge):** Energy Recharge is preferred if your healer's Ultimate costs are high.
*   **Slot 4 (Healing Bonus %):** Essential for pure healers.

### Optimizing Substats for Sustain
The goal for a healer's substats is to reach specific "breakpoints" for Energy Recharge so they can cast their Ultimate every rotation.
1.  **HP %:** The primary scaling stat for almost all healing skills.
2.  **Energy Recharge:** Aim for 140%–160% to ensure Ultimate uptime.
3.  **DEF %:** Reduces the damage the healer takes while they are briefly on-field.
4.  **Flat HP:** A minor boost, but better than offensive stats for this role.

## Team Comp

![Team Comp](/img/neverness-to-everness-healer-build-guide/inline-4.webp)


A healer’s value is determined by the teammates they protect. In Neverness to Everness, team building revolves around "Elemental Lanes" and the Esper Cycle. A healer should ideally match or complement the elements of the DPS to trigger powerful reactions like "Blossom" or "Nova."

### Meta Team Compositions

| Team Name | Composition | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **F2P Starter Trio** | Esper Zero (DPS), Edgar (Healer), Haniel (Support) | A balanced, safe team that uses Cosmos and Psyche reactions. |
| **Face-Tank Duo** | Fadia (Tank), Edgar (Healer), Nanally (Main DPS) | Fadia absorbs damage; Edgar keeps Fadia's massive HP pool full. |
| **Blossom Meta** | Jiuyuan (Hybrid), [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide) (Sub-DPS), Nanally (Main DPS) | High-investment comp where Jiuyuan's A1 provides enough healing. |

### The F2P Reliable Trio
This composition is the standard for new players. Esper Zero provides consistent Cosmos damage, while Haniel offers buffs through her companion mechanics. Edgar slots in as the safety net. Because Zero and Edgar both share the Cosmos element, they can quickly build the Esper Gauge, allowing for frequent "Instant Cycles" that freeze enemies in place, giving Edgar time to channel his "Wild Current" without interruption.

### The Immortal Sustain Duo: Edgar + Fadia
For high-difficulty "Anomaly Raids," the combination of Edgar and Fadia is nearly unbreakable. Fadia's passive redirects 50% of the damage taken by the active character to herself. Since Fadia has an incredibly high HP pool (boosted by "Eternal Waltz"), she can survive hits that would kill a DPS. Edgar then tags in to heal Fadia. This loop allows the player to ignore many boss mechanics and maintain constant damage uptime.

### High-End Hybrid Comps
In the "Blossom Meta," players move away from pure healers to maximize damage. Jiuyuan, when unlocked at Awakening Level 1, restores HP to the entire team equal to 5% of the damage dealt by her "Pact Settlement." When paired with Sakiri’s grouping abilities and Nanally’s follow-up attacks, the team generates enough "Vita Buds" (Anima+Cosmos reaction) to keep everyone healthy through lifesteal alone, removing the need for a dedicated healer slot.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/neverness-to-everness-healer-build-guide/inline-5.webp)


Efficient healing in NTE is about timing and positioning. Because most healers are "Quick-Swap" units, you should never keep them on the field longer than necessary.

### Tag-In Healing Mechanics
The most common mistake is waiting until a character is at 5% HP to heal. In NTE, you should rotate your healer in when your Main DPS hits the 50%–60% threshold.
1.  **Tag-In:** Switch to Edgar when the Esper Gauge is at least half full.
2.  **Skill Cast:** Cast "Wild Current." (Note: Hold the button to increase the healing amount, but be wary of the increased cooldown).
3.  **Ultimate (Optional):** If the whole team is low, cast "Finnegan's Wake" to create a 10-second healing domain.
4.  **Tag-Out:** Immediately swap back to your Main DPS or a Support to continue the damage rotation.

### Managing the Esper Cycle Gauge
Healers often have Ultimates that provide team-wide buffs or massive sustained healing. However, these Ultimates consume the same gauge used for offensive "Charge" attacks. It is vital to manage this energy. If a boss is about to enter a "Stagger" window, save your energy for DPS. If the boss is about to perform a "World-Ending" anomaly attack, use the healer's Ultimate to provide the team with the necessary damage reduction and HP regen to survive the hit.

### Positioning and Zone Control
Edgar's "Finnegan's Wake" creates a static zone. In NTE's fast-paced combat, enemies often move out of these zones. Use Redirect Skills or "Grouping" supports like Sakiri to pull enemies into the healing zone, or use characters with high mobility to stay within the domain's boundaries while attacking.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/neverness-to-everness-healer-build-guide/inline-6.webp)


### Does healing scale with ATK in NTE?
For Edgar, the answer is no. His primary healing skill, "Wild Current," scales based on his own Max HP and the target's Max HP. While some hybrid units like Jiuyuan heal based on a percentage of damage dealt (which involves ATK), pure healers in the current meta are almost entirely HP-dependent.

### Is Mint a healer?
No. Mint is an A-Rank Anima DPS character. While her kit is versatile, she does not have any innate team-healing capabilities. Players often confuse her with Edgar because they are both A-Rank supports found early in the game.

### Who is the best healer in the game?
As of May 15, 2026, Edgar is the most consistent pure healer for general play. However, Fadia is considered the best "Sustain" unit for endgame content due to her damage redirection, and Jiuyuan is the best hybrid for veteran players who want to maintain maximum DPS.

### Can I play without a healer?
Yes, it is possible to play without a healer if you utilize high-investment shielders like Adler or if you have mastered the "Critical Dodge" mechanic. However, for most players tackling Level 60+ Anomalies, at least one sustain unit is recommended to account for human error.

### How do I unlock pseudo-healing for S-Rank units?
Certain S-Rank units gain healing through their Awakening levels. For example, Jiuyuan unlocks team-wide healing at Awakening Level 1 (A1). Other units may gain lifesteal or HP regeneration through specific S-Rank Arcs or Legendary Cartridge set bonuses.

## Overview

Xianyun is a 5-star Anemo Catalyst user who first appeared in Version 4.4. While she is known in Liyue lore as the haughty yet caring Cloud Retainer, her gameplay role is that of a highly specialized support and healer. Her primary contribution to a team is her ability to enable high-jumping for active characters, allowing anyone—from Diluc to Hu Tao—to perform plunging attacks that were previously restricted to specific characters like Xiao or niche mechanics like "Dragonstriking."

Beyond her unique jumping buffs, Xianyun provides substantial team-wide healing that scales with her total ATK. This makes her an ideal partner for characters who drain their own HP, most notably Furina. Her passive talent, "Consider, the Adeptus in Her Realm," grants a significant flat DMG bonus to plunging attacks based on her current ATK stat, further cementing her role as the cornerstone of plunge-based "Hypercarry" teams.

### Talent Priority
1.  **Stars Gather at Dusk (Elemental Burst):** This is the core of her kit. It provides the initial team heal, continuous healing, and the "Starwicker" mechanism that enables plunging. Maximize this first.
2.  **White Clouds at Dawn (Elemental Skill):** While it provides mobility and some energy generation, the damage is secondary for a support build. Level this second.
3.  **Word of Wind and Flower (Normal Attack):** As a support, Xianyun will rarely use her normal attacks. This can be left at level 1 unless you are building her for a Main DPS role.

### Xianyun's Role in the Meta
In the current meta, Xianyun occupies a unique niche as a "Plunge Enabler." Before her release, plunging was largely the domain of Xiao. Now, she has opened up new high-damage ceilings for older characters. Her synergy with Furina is also a major factor in her meta relevance; because Xianyun heals the entire party simultaneously, she can instantly generate massive amounts of Fanfare points for Furina’s damage buff.

### Pros and Cons of Building Xianyun
**Pros:**
*   **Unique Mechanics:** Enables plunging attacks for any character, creating entirely new team archetypes.
*   **Powerful Healing:** Provides both a large burst of team-wide healing and sustained active-character healing.
*   **Exploration God-Tier:** Her Elemental Skill allows for three consecutive mid-air leaps, making her one of the best characters for traversing vertical terrain.
*   **High Stat Efficiency:** She scales with ATK, meaning her healing and her damage buffs improve simultaneously as you build her.

**Cons:**
*   **High Energy Needs:** Her Burst costs 70 Energy, and as the solo Anemo unit, she often requires over 200% Energy Recharge.
*   **Niche Buffing:** If your team does not utilize plunging attacks, a large portion of her kit (and her A4 passive) is wasted.
*   **Single-Target Limitation:** The flat DMG buff from her A4 passive only applies to one enemy hit per plunging attack, making it less effective in heavy AoE scenarios.

## Best Weapons

Choosing the right weapon for Xianyun involves balancing her high Energy Recharge (ER) requirements with her need for high Base ATK. Because her A4 passive buff scales off her total ATK, weapons with high base stats and ATK% substats are generally preferred, provided you can meet her ER targets through artifacts.

### Top Tier: 5-Star Options
**Crane's Echoing Call (Signature)**
This is Xianyun's definitive Best-in-Slot weapon. It features a massive Base ATK of 741, the highest among all Catalysts. The passive, "Cloud-Traversing Willet," increases the Plunging Attack DMG of all nearby party members by 28% for 20 seconds after the wielder hits an opponent with a Plunging Attack. Additionally, it restores 2.5 Energy to the wielder when party members hit opponents with plunging attacks. This weapon significantly lowers her ER requirements while maximizing her ATK-based buffs.

**Skyward Atlas**
A powerful generalist 5-star option. It offers a high Base ATK of 674 and an ATK% substat. While it doesn't provide the energy utility of her signature, it is the best "stat stick" for maximizing the flat DMG buff provided by her passive.

### Best 4-Star and F2P Catalysts
**Oathsworn Eye**
This weapon was available during the "Three Realms Gateway Offering" event in Version 2.5. If you have it, it is arguably her best 4-star option. It provides a respectable 565 Base ATK and an ATK% substat. More importantly, its passive increases Energy Recharge by 48% (at R5) for 10 seconds after using an Elemental Skill. This perfectly aligns with Xianyun's rotation.

**Favonius Codex**
The standard choice for players struggling with Energy. While it has a lower Base ATK (510), it provides a high ER substat and generates Neutral Particles for the whole team. Using this weapon requires you to build some Crit Rate on Xianyun to trigger the passive consistently.

### Budget and Niche Alternatives
**Thrilling Tales of Dragon Slayers (TTDS)**
As a 3-star weapon, this is incredibly accessible. While its Base ATK is very low (which hurts Xianyun's personal buff), the 48% ATK buff it provides to the next character swapped in can result in a net damage gain for hypercarries like Xiao or Diluc.

**Flowing Purity**
The Fontaine craftable catalyst is a solid F2P option if you missed Oathsworn Eye. It offers 565 Base ATK and an ATK% substat. The passive provides an Elemental DMG Bonus and a "Bond of Life" mechanic that can be cleared by Xianyun’s own healing to grant further buffs.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

Xianyun's artifact choices are flexible depending on the team composition. Because she is an Anemo catalyst user, she can easily trigger the Viridescent Venerer set, but she also has access to newer sets designed for healers.

### Recommended Artifact Sets
**4-Piece Viridescent Venerer (VV)**
This remains the gold standard for any Anemo support in teams that rely on Pyro, Hydro, Electro, or Cryo damage. The 40% Elemental RES shred is often more valuable than any personal buff Xianyun can provide. Use this in Vaporize or Swirl-heavy teams.

**4-Piece Song of Days Past**
This set is specifically designed for healers who want to provide offensive buffs. It records the amount of healing provided (including overhealing) and converts it into a flat DMG increase for the active character. This is an excellent alternative in Xiao teams where VV's RES shred is useless (since Anemo RES cannot be shredded by VV).

**4-Piece Noblesse Oblige**
A reliable fallback. If no one else on the team (like Bennett or Faruzan) is wearing this set, Xianyun can use it to provide a 20% ATK buff to the entire team upon casting her Burst.

**2-Piece Combinations**
If you cannot complete a 4-piece set with good stats, mixing 2-piece ATK% (Gladiator’s Finale, Shimenawa’s Reminiscence) and 2-piece Energy Recharge (Emblem of Severed Fate) is a viable way to reach your stat targets.

### Main Stats and Substats Priority
*   **Sands:** Energy Recharge or ATK% (Use ER unless you have her signature weapon or Oathsworn Eye).
*   **Goblet:** ATK%.
*   **Circlet:** ATK% or Healing Bonus (ATK% is preferred to maximize the A4 damage buff).

**Substat Priority:**
1.  Energy Recharge (until target is met).
2.  ATK%.
3.  Flat ATK.
4.  Crit Rate (only if using Favonius Codex).

### Meeting High Energy Recharge Requirements
Xianyun’s Burst is expensive. Failing to have it ready every rotation severely hampers your team's DPS.
*   **Solo Anemo:** 200% – 240% ER.
*   **Double Anemo (with Xiao):** 160% – 180% ER.
*   **Triple Anemo (Xiao + Faruzan):** 130% – 150% ER.

## Rotation

To maximize Xianyun's buffs and ensure her Burst is active for the full duration of your DPS's field time, follow a strict rotation.

### Standard Support Rotation
1.  **Setup:** Cast off-field abilities first. (e.g., Furina Skill and Burst, Faruzan Skill and Burst).
2.  **Xianyun Skill (E):** Use one instance of "Sky Ladder" to leap, then immediately use the plunging attack "Driftcloud Wave." This generates 5 Anemo particles.
3.  **Xianyun Burst (Q):** Cast "Stars Gather at Dusk" immediately after the skill. This ensures she catches her own particles if she is on-field.
4.  **Main DPS:** Swap to your carry (Xiao, Gaming, Diluc) and begin the plunging sequence.

### Optimizing Skill Usage for Energy
Xianyun can use her Skill up to three times in the air. However, for support roles, using it three times is often a waste of time. One jump followed by a plunge is the fastest way to generate energy and trigger the "Galestalker" passive (which provides a Crit Rate buff to plunges based on enemies hit). Only use the triple jump for exploration or if you need to reposition in combat.

### Tips for Maximizing Buff Uptime
Xianyun's Burst lasts for 16 seconds, which is quite long. However, the "Adeptal Assistance" stacks (the jumps) are limited to 8. If you are playing a character with very fast plunges, be mindful of the stack count. In Xiao teams, ensure you are using his Burst after Xianyun's to cover his entire 15-second duration.

## FAQ

### Is Xianyun better than Jean?
In most scenarios involving Furina, Xianyun is superior. While Jean provides a massive "instant" heal, Xianyun provides continuous team-wide healing and the ability to shred RES with VV while also buffing plunging damage. Jean is only preferred if you specifically need her "Sunfire" interaction with Bennett or if you do not care about plunging attacks at all.

### How much ATK does she need?
Your goal should be between 3,000 and 3,500 ATK. Her A4 passive buff, "Consider, the Adeptus in Her Realm," caps out at 4,500 ATK (providing a 9,000 flat DMG bonus). Reaching 4,500 ATK is very difficult without her signature weapon and external buffs like Bennett or Noblesse Oblige.

### Is C1 necessary?
C1 (Purifying Breeze) gives her an extra charge of her Elemental Skill. This is not mandatory for combat but is a massive "Quality of Life" upgrade for exploration and significantly lowers her Energy Recharge requirements, as she can generate double the particles per rotation.

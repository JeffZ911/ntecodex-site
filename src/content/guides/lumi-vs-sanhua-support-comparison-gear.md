## What Neverness to Everness players actually need

![What Neverness to Everness players actually need](/img/lumi-vs-sanhua-support-comparison-gear/inline-2.webp)


Unlike traditional MMOs, urban RPGs like Neverness to Everness involve a mix of high-speed traversal and tactical combat. This variety in gameplay means your physical needs change throughout a single session.

**Dynamic Lumbar Support**
When you are exploring the city or managing your City Tycoon business, you might lean back. However, during a boss fight in the Uncharted Circle, most players naturally lean forward. A chair with dynamic lumbar support, like the Razer Iskur V2, moves with you to prevent "slumping," which can lead to chronic lower back pain.

**Breathable Materials**
The high graphical fidelity of NTE can lead to long, immersive sessions. If your chair uses low-quality synthetic leather, heat buildup can become a distraction. Mesh options or cooling-infused foams, such as the copper-infused foam in the Herman Miller Embody, are preferred for maintaining a neutral body temperature.

**Adjustable Armrests**
Repetitive inputs for Esper Cycles and parrying can cause wrist and shoulder strain. 4D armrests allow you to align your elbows with your desk height, ensuring your wrists remain in a neutral position while you execute Sakiri's Devour Whole or Hotori's replication skills.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/lumi-vs-sanhua-support-comparison-gear/inline-4.webp)


Not every player needs a high-end ergonomic chair to enjoy Neverness to Everness. You should consider skipping a new purchase if:

*   **Existing Support:** Your current chair already features a height-adjustable lumbar system and you do not experience back or neck pain after 3 hours of play.
*   **Mobile Gameplay:** If you primarily play NTE on a mobile device or tablet, a dedicated gaming chair offers less benefit than a high-quality tablet stand or a supportive couch setup.
*   **Size Mismatch:** If you are outside the standard height range (under 5'0" or over 6'6"), "Regular" sized chairs like the TITAN Evo may not provide support at the correct anatomical points. Always check the manufacturer's size guides before purchasing.

## What we didn't include and why

![What we didn't include and why](/img/lumi-vs-sanhua-support-comparison-gear/inline-5.webp)


Several popular chairs were omitted from this guide due to specific ergonomic drawbacks that clash with the needs of RPG players.

*   **DXRacer Formula Series:** While iconic, the "wings" on the backrest of racing-style seats are designed to keep a driver centered during high-G turns. In a gaming environment, these wings often push the shoulders forward, which is suboptimal for the long-term posture needed in open-world games.
*   **Generic $99 Gaming Chairs:** These chairs, common on mass-market retail sites, typically use low-density foam that "bottoms out" within months of daily use, leading to increased pressure on the tailbone.
*   **Herman Miller Aeron:** Despite being one of the best mesh chairs ever made, the hard plastic frame around the seat can be uncomfortable for players who prefer to sit cross-legged or shift their weight frequently during cutscenes.

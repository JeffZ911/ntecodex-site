*   **ASIN:** B093K8YGYK
*   **Price:** $399
*   **Rating:** 4.6/5
*   **Best for:** Vibrant Color Accuracy (Ruan Mei Aesthetic)

The LG 27GP850-B remains a staple for players who prioritize color saturation and motion clarity. Its [Nano IPS panel](https://www.rtings.com/monitor/reviews/lg/27gp850-b-27gp83b-b) covers 98% of the DCI-P3 color gamut, making the intricate traditional patterns on Ruan Mei’s character model look remarkably vivid. While its native refresh rate is 165Hz, it can be overclocked to 180Hz, ensuring that even the most complex Ultimate animations remain fluid.

**Pros:**
*   Nano IPS technology provides [98% DCI-P3 coverage](https://www.rtings.com/monitor/reviews/lg/27gp850-b-27gp83b-b) for superior color depth.
*   Fast 1ms (GtG) response time eliminates ghosting during fast transitions.
*   Certified G-Sync and FreeSync Premium support prevents screen tearing in open-world titles like Neverness to Everness.

**Cons:**
*   Contrast ratio is limited to approximately 800:1 to 1000:1, causing blacks to appear slightly gray in dark rooms.
*   Noticeable IPS glow can be distracting during dark cutscenes or night-time exploration in Hethereau.

**Verdict:**
The LG 27GP850-B is the gold standard for players who want the vibrant teals and plums of the Ruan Mei palette to pop. Its Nano IPS technology provides superior color saturation compared to standard IPS panels, though it struggles in pitch-black rooms due to limited contrast.

## What long-session players actually need

![What long-session players actually need](/img/hsr-dahlia-vs-ruan-mei-comparison-20260713/inline-2.webp)


Playing games for 4+ hours daily introduces physical and visual stresses that casual hardware often fails to address.

### Eye Strain Mitigation
Extended sessions in front of a monitor can lead to Digital Eye Strain (DES). To combat this, players should look for monitors with [flicker-free backlighting](https://www.rtings.com/monitor/reviews/lg/27gp850-b-27gp83b-b) and high refresh rates. A refresh rate of 144Hz or higher reduces the "stutter" perceived by the eye during camera movement, which is particularly important in the fast driving sequences of Neverness to Everness.

### Lumbar Support
Static turn-based gameplay in HSR often leads to "gamer slouch," where the player leans forward, putting immense pressure on the lower back. A chair with adjustable internal lumbar support is essential because it maintains the natural "S-curve" of the spine. Unlike cheap pillows that compress over time, internal mechanisms like the one in the TITAN Evo provide consistent resistance.

### Multi-Device Connectivity
The 2026 gaming ecosystem is cross-platform. Players often farm daily missions on their phones while using their PCs for endgame content like the Anomalies in Hethereau. Peripherals that allow you to take a call or switch audio sources with a single button press save minutes of frustration every day.

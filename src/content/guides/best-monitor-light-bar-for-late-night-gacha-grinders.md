## What 'late night gacha grinders' actually need

![What 'late night gacha grinders' actually need](/img/best-monitor-light-bar-for-late-night-gacha-grinders/inline-2.webp)


Playing Neverness to Everness involves tracking fast-moving Anomalies and navigating dense cityscapes. In a dark room, this creates a harsh contrast between the bright screen and the surrounding shadows. To combat this, a light bar must provide three specific things:

### Asymmetrical Lighting
Standard desk lamps throw light in all directions, which causes a "veil" of glare on the monitor surface. This forces your eyes to work harder to see the content behind the glare. A high-quality light bar uses a cutoff design to ensure light only hits your desk and keyboard, leaving the screen perfectly clear. This is vital when you are timing dodges or managing character skills in high-intensity combat.

### High CRI (Color Rendering Index)
Gacha and JRPG titles rely heavily on vibrant character art. A CRI of 90+ ensures that the light doesn't wash out or distort the colors on your desk or peripherals. When you are looking at your physical keyboard or notes, you want the colors to remain true to life. Lower CRI lights can make skin tones look gray or sickly, which ruins the aesthetic of a high-end setup.

### Bias Lighting
In a dark room, the contrast between a bright screen and a dark wall causes eye fatigue. This is known as "contrast-induced strain." Light bars with rear-facing LEDs, like the BenQ Halo, help bridge this gap by casting a soft glow on the wall behind the monitor. This raises the ambient light level of your field of view without adding glare, allowing your pupils to remain more stable rather than constantly dilating and contracting.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/best-monitor-light-bar-for-late-night-gacha-grinders/inline-4.webp)


While a light bar is a transformative upgrade for many, it is not a universal fix. 

*   **High Ambient Light:** If you already play in a well-lit room with high ambient light, the benefits of a light bar are significantly diminished. The primary goal is to reduce contrast in dark environments; if your room is already bright, a light bar may just add unnecessary clutter.
*   **1000R Curved Monitors:** If you use a monitor with an extremely aggressive curve (like the Samsung Odyssey G7/G9), many standard light bars will not fit correctly. The straight bar can cut off the corners of the screen or cause light to bleed onto the top edges of the panel. For these, you specifically need a curved-compatible mount like the one included with the BenQ Halo.
*   **Laptops:** Don't buy a light bar if you primarily use a laptop. The weight of the bar (often 1lb or more) can damage the screen hinges over time or cause the screen to flop backward. Laptops are better served by dedicated clip-on "reading" lights that are much lighter.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do monitor light bars actually reduce eye strain?
Yes, they reduce eye strain by providing a consistent light source on your desk while eliminating reflections on the screen. By using an asymmetrical lens, they ensure that the light only illuminates the workspace, which prevents the glare that causes your eyes to fatigue during long sessions.

### Can a light bar damage my monitor's webcam?
Most light bars are designed to sit on top of the bezel, which can block an integrated webcam. However, many models like the BenQ Halo include an accessory or a specific "lip" design to allow webcams to be mounted on top of the light bar itself.

### Will a light bar work on a curved monitor?
Standard light bars may cause glare on the corners of a curved screen or may not sit securely on the frame. If you have a curved monitor, you should look for a light bar with a specialized curved monitor accessory or a mount designed for thicker, non-flat panels.

### Do I need a high CRI for gaming?
While it doesn't affect your frame rate, a high CRI (Color Rendering Index) ensures that the colors of your physical desk setup—like your keyboard, mousepad, and any physical merch—look accurate. It also prevents the "washed out" look that low-quality lights can give to your room.

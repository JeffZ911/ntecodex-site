## Overview

![Overview](/img/nte-stamina-management-guide-beginners/inline-1.webp)


Unlike many contemporary urban RPGs that utilize a single energy system, NTE employs a tripartite structure: Character Pixels, City Stamina, and the Combat Stamina Gauge. Each serves a specific purpose in the gameplay loop, and neglecting one can lead to significant delays in character progression or financial stability within the game’s economy.

Character Pixels represent your primary gate for power-up materials. These are spent on Anomaly Hunts and Bosses to secure the items needed for leveling characters and Arcs. City Stamina, conversely, is the lifeblood of the Tycoon system. It limits how many deliveries you can make, how many races you can enter, and how effectively you can manage your Cafe. Finally, the Combat Stamina Gauge is a real-time resource used during encounters for tactical movements and specific character abilities.

### Understanding Character Pixels

Character Pixels are the most precious resource for players focused on combat efficiency. With a cap of 240 and a recovery rate of 10 per hour, you generate 240 Pixels every 24 hours. To avoid "capping" and wasting potential resources, it is recommended to log in at least twice a day to spend them.

In the early game, your priority for Pixels should be character and weapon (Arc) Ascension materials. While it is tempting to spend them on Console (artifact) farming early on, the drop rates for high-tier gear are significantly better after Hunter Level 40. Until then, focus on guaranteed power increases like level caps and talent upgrades.

### The Weekly City Stamina Cycle

City Stamina governs your interactions with the world of Hethereau outside of combat. This resource is used for "Tycoon" activities, which are the primary way to earn Fons, the game's main currency. Effective management of City Stamina allows you to upgrade your properties and eventually unlock S-Rank characters like Chiz through high-level Tycoon milestones.

Because City Stamina operates on a weekly cycle rather than a daily one, you have more flexibility in how you spend it. Some players prefer to burn their weekly allotment on high-stakes racing, while others find more consistent returns in Cafe management and deliveries. The key is ensuring that you do not leave City Stamina unused before the weekly reset, as it does not carry over.

### Combat Stamina: The Hidden DPS Variable

While Pixels and City Stamina are menu-based resources, Combat Stamina is a live gauge on your HUD. It is easy to overlook until you find yourself unable to dodge a boss's ultimate attack. In NTE, certain characters rely on this gauge for their primary damage output. For example, Mint’s "Minty Whirlwind" consumes combat stamina to maintain its rotation. Learning the rhythm of your gauge—knowing when to stop attacking to allow for a dodge—is the fundamental skill gap in NTE’s early game.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/nte-stamina-management-guide-beginners/inline-5.webp)


Mastering the rotation is about more than just pressing buttons in order; it is about managing the visual gauge of the Esper Cycle and the physical gauge of your combat stamina.

1.  **The Opening Setup**: Start the encounter with Sakiri. Use her Hold Skill (Devour Whole) to pull all enemies into a central point. This maximizes the efficiency of your subsequent attacks.
2.  **The Battery Phase**: Swap to Esper Zero. Use his Skill to instantly fill the Cycle Gauge. This prepares the field for an Anima reaction once Mint enters the fray.
3.  **The Repositioning**: Switch to Mint and immediately use her Skill (Overdrive: Super Claw). This provides a significant ATK boost and repositions her perfectly in the center of the grouped enemies.
4.  **The Whirlwind Window**: Hold the Basic Attack button to execute "Minty Whirlwind." Watch your Stamina Gauge closely. Once it hits approximately 20%, stop the attack. You need that remaining 20% to dodge away from any incoming counter-attacks.
5.  **Recovery and Re-cycling**: Cycle to Jiuyuan to trigger Blossom reactions. Her presence on the field allows the rest of the team to recover energy and allows Mint’s stamina to fully recharge for the next loop.

### Maximizing the Whirlwind Window

The biggest mistake beginners make is spinning until the gauge hits 0%. This leaves you "Stamina Broken," a state where your character moves slower and cannot dodge for several seconds. By stopping at 20%, you maintain mobility. During the recovery phase, focus on using Jiuyuan’s quick-tap skills to keep the damage flowing without consuming any significant amount of the gauge.

## FAQ

### General Stamina Questions

**How do I increase my Character Pixel cap?**
The base cap is 240, but you can effectively increase your "storage" and recovery by unlocking Anomaly Furniture for your properties. The "Dream Weaver's Knot" is one of the first pieces of furniture you should aim for, as it provides passive bonuses to your resource management.

**What happens if I run out of stamina while swimming?**
Swimming in the waters around Hethereau consumes combat stamina. If the gauge reaches zero, your character will begin to lose HP rapidly until they drown. You will then respawn at the nearest shoreline with a small HP penalty.

**Is it worth spending Annulith to refresh Pixels?**
For F2P players and low spenders, the answer is generally no. Annulith is the premium currency used for character banners like "The Ichi-daime." Using it for Pixels provides a temporary boost in progression but significantly slows down your ability to pull new characters.

### Tycoon vs. Combat Resource Management

**Can I convert City Stamina to Character Pixels?**
No. These are strictly separate systems. City Stamina is for the "Life Sim" and Tycoon aspects of the game, while Pixels are for character growth and combat. You cannot trade one for the other, so you must manage both independently.

**How do I recover City Stamina?**
City Stamina resets every Monday. However, you can find small recovery items through high-level Tycoon milestones or as rare rewards from city-based side quests. Use these sparingly, ideally during "Double Fons" events to maximize your return on investment.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/best-gear-for-long-gacha-sessions/inline-4.webp)


Not every player needs a $1,600 chair or a complex MMO mouse. If your current sessions are consistently under two hours, high-end ergonomic chairs like the Embody won't provide a noticeable return on investment over a standard, well-built office chair. The benefits of pixelated support and cooling foam are cumulative; they shine in the sixth hour, not the first.

Similarly, you should avoid upgrading your monitor to an ultrawide if your GPU cannot maintain at least 60 FPS at 1440p. The increased resolution will tank your performance, leading to a choppy experience that can cause eye strain. Finally, do not buy a 12-button MMO mouse if you primarily play with a controller; the utility of the side buttons is entirely lost on non-keyboard and mouse setups.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is an S-Class character selection box worth a gear upgrade?
While a selection box helps your in-game progress, physical gear ensures you can actually play the game comfortably. If you find yourself ending sessions early due to wrist or back pain, the ergonomic upgrade is far more valuable than a digital character.

### How do I use macros on the Razer Naga V2 Pro for NTE?
You can use the Razer Synapse software to bind character swaps or menu shortcuts to the 12-button side plate. This allows you to execute complex sequences, such as swapping from a support to a DPS character, with a single thumb press.

### Why is an ultrawide monitor better for open-world games?
The 21:9 aspect ratio provides a wider field of view, which is particularly useful in urban environments. It allows you to see more of the city streets and peripheral enemies, increasing immersion and situational awareness during combat.

### Can a standing desk really help with gacha grinding?
Yes, because standing increases blood flow and helps prevent the "slump" that occurs after hours of sitting. Transitioning to a standing position during repetitive tasks, like farming materials, can keep you alert and reduce the strain on your lower back.

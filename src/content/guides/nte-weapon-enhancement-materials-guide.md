## Overview

![Overview](/img/nte-weapon-enhancement-materials-guide/inline-1.webp)


In Neverness to Everness (NTE), weapons are referred to as Arcs. These devices are the primary source of raw stats and passive modifiers for your Espers, directly influencing the Esper Cycle—the core energy and reaction system that dictates the flow of combat. Unlike traditional RPGs where weapons are simple stat sticks, Arcs in NTE are categorized by their physical state—Plasma, Solid, Gas, Liquid, or Synthesis—which dictates how they interact with an Esper’s base attribute (Anima, Chaos, or Cosmos).

To keep your Arcs competitive against the escalating threats in the urban landscape, you must engage with three core upgrade paths. Enhancement is the most basic form of progression, involving the consumption of EXP Dyes to increase the Arc's base level. Once an Arc hits a level ceiling, you must perform Ascension (Cap Breaking) using specific Pigments and environmental breakthrough items. Finally, Mixing (Refinement) allows you to fuse duplicate Arcs together to enhance the potency of their unique passive abilities.

Managing these upgrades requires a steady flow of Beetle Coins, the primary currency of Hesperia, and Character Pixels, which function as the stamina system required to claim rewards from resource stages. Understanding the efficiency of your farming routes is essential for maintaining a high-tier roster.

### Types of Arcs: Plasma, Solid, Gas, Liquid, and Synthesis

Every Arc falls into one of five categories. While any Esper can technically equip any Arc of their rarity, matching the Arc type to the Esper's combat style is vital for optimizing the Esper Cycle.

*   **Plasma Arcs:** Typically focused on high-frequency energy generation and elemental application. These are favored by Main DPS characters who rely on rapid skill usage.
*   **Solid Arcs:** These prioritize Break Intensity and physical impact. They are the go-to choice for "Breaker" roles who need to deplete enemy Stagger bars quickly.
*   **Gas Arcs:** Often provide utility, such as movement speed buffs or area-of-effect (AoE) debuffs.
*   **Liquid Arcs:** Generally lean toward defensive utility, healing amplification, or damage reduction.
*   **Synthesis Arcs:** Rare hybrid Arcs that often scale with multiple stats or provide unique "Quickswap" bonuses.

## Best Weapons

Selecting the right Arc can be the difference between clearing a high-level rift or hitting a hard progression wall. As of Patch 1.0, S-Rank Arcs dominate the meta due to their transformative passive effects.

### Top S-Rank Arcs for DPS

The current gold standard for damage dealers is **Ready-Ready**, a Plasma-type Arc that serves as the signature weapon for [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte). Its unique passive, "Tiger Talisman," provides a significant damage multiplier against Elite and Boss-class enemies. When Nanally enters her "Ichi-daime" state, Ready-Ready accelerates her energy recovery, allowing for near-constant uptime on her high-damage rotations.

Another standout is **Camellia Society**, which excels in multi-target scenarios. It provides a stacking Critical Damage buff every time an Esper triggers an elemental reaction, making it ideal for Chaos-attribute characters who thrive on the "Discord" reaction.

For pure supports, look for Arcs that provide "Cycle Efficiency." These Arcs ensure that your off-field characters are constantly generating energy for your Main DPS's Ultimate.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

![Best Disks (Artifacts)](/img/nte-weapon-enhancement-materials-guide/inline-3.webp)


The Disk system in NTE is divided into two components: Cassettes and Driver Blocks. This is where you fine-tune your Esper's stats to match their equipped Arc.

### Cassette Set Bonuses Explained

Cassettes provide 2-piece and 4-piece set bonuses. To activate these, you must place the corresponding Driver Blocks into the character's "Driver Grid." This grid uses a "Tetris" placement mechanic where blocks of different shapes must be fitted together. If you overlap blocks or leave too many gaps, you won't be able to activate the full 4-piece bonus.

For a Main DPS, the priority is usually the **Elemental DMG** set (matching their Anima, Chaos, or Cosmos attribute). For example, Nanally benefits most from Anima-focused Cassettes that increase her Resonance damage.

### Cartridge Main Stat Priority

Each Disk has a specific slot (often called Cartridges). You should prioritize the following stats based on your role:
1.  **Main DPS:** Elemental DMG Bonus > Crit Rate > Crit DMG > ATK%.
2.  **Breaker:** Break Intensity > ATK% > Speed.
3.  **Support:** Energy Recovery Rate > HP% (for healers) > Elemental Mastery.

### Optimizing Driver Block Sub-stats

When farming Disks in the late game, the sub-stats on your Driver Blocks become the primary focus. You want to look for "Golden" tier sub-stats that offer flat increases to Crit Rate or specific elemental multipliers. Because the grid is limited, choosing blocks with efficient shapes (like 1x2 or 2x2 squares) is often more important than the individual stats of a poorly shaped block.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/nte-weapon-enhancement-materials-guide/inline-5.webp)


Mastering the Esper Cycle requires more than just mashing buttons; it requires precise timing and an understanding of the "Quickswap" mechanic.

### Mastering the Esper Cycle

Your Esper Cycle meter fills as you deal damage, use skills, and successfully perform Parries. Once the meter is full, your off-field characters' icons will begin to glow. This is your cue to swap. Swapping a character while they are glowing triggers an "Entry Skill," which deals massive damage and often applies a heavy elemental status.

### Nanally & Zero Burst Rotation

To maximize damage with the Nanally/Zero pairing:
1.  Start with **Adler** to apply an initial elemental status and drop his energy-generating field.
2.  Swap to **Daffodil** to use her Skill and Basic Attack string until the enemy’s Stagger bar is nearly depleted.
3.  Switch to **Fadia** to drop a shield and group the enemies.
4.  Once the enemy is "Staggered," swap to **Nanally**.
5.  Use Nanally’s Skill to enter the "Ichi-daime" state, followed by her Ultimate.
6.  If Nanally’s energy dips, quickswap to **Zero**, use his instant cycle skill, and immediately swap back to Nanally to maintain the burst window.

### Defensive Utility and Parry Timing

Parrying is not just for defense; it is a major offensive tool. A successful Parry instantly restores a portion of the Esper Cycle meter and slows down time (Bullet Time). During this window, your character deals increased Break damage. Timing your Parries against boss "Red Glint" attacks is the fastest way to trigger a Stagger state.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/nte-weapon-enhancement-materials-guide/inline-6.webp)


**How do I get more Beetle Coins quickly?**
The most efficient way to farm Beetle Coins is through the "Hesperia Logistics" stages. Additionally, completing your Daily Commissions and selling unwanted C-Rank Disks can provide a steady secondary income.

**Should I prioritize upgrading my Arc or my Cassettes first?**
Always prioritize your Arc (Weapon) first. Arcs provide a guaranteed increase to your base Attack and are much cheaper to upgrade than Disks. Disks (Cassettes) should be leveled once your Arc has hit the current level cap for your Appraisal Level.

**What is the maximum level for Arcs in Patch 1.0?**
As of the current patch, the maximum level for an Arc is 60, which requires an Appraisal Level of 41 or higher. Future updates are expected to raise this cap.

**Can I reset an Arc to get my enhancement materials back?**
Currently, there is no "Reset" function for Arcs. Once you spend Dyes and Beetle Coins on an Arc, those resources are permanently consumed. Be careful when investing heavily in B-Rank or A-Rank Arcs if you plan to replace them with S-Ranks soon.

**Where can I find the 'Golden Appleseed' ascension material?**
Golden Appleseeds are rare drops from high-level World Bosses and can be purchased in limited quantities from the Hunter Exchange using "Hunter Marks" earned from weekly bounties.

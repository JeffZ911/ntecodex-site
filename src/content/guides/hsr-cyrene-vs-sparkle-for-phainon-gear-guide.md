## What long-session players actually need

![What long-session players actually need](/img/hsr-cyrene-vs-sparkle-for-phainon-gear-guide/inline-2.webp)


Long-session players face unique challenges that standard "competitive" monitor guides often overlook. When you are spending hours daily managing Esper abilities or optimizing your character builds, hardware comfort becomes a gameplay factor.

### Eye Comfort
Flicker-free backlights and hardware-level blue light reduction are mandatory for sessions exceeding four hours. This prevents ocular fatigue, which can lead to missed telegraphs during difficult encounters. Many modern panels, such as those from LG and ASUS, include TUV Rheinland certification for low blue light emissions without significantly distorting the color balance of the game.

### Panel Type
IPS or OLED is preferred over VA or TN. While VA panels offer better contrast than IPS, they often suffer from "black smearing" in dark, fast-moving scenes—a common occurrence in the night-time urban environments of Hotta Studio's latest title. IPS panels ensure that character designs like Mint or Sakiri remain vivid from any sitting position. OLED represents the pinnacle of this, offering perfect contrast that makes neon signs and supernatural effects stand out against dark city backgrounds.

### Motion Clarity
A minimum 144Hz refresh rate is necessary to track fast-moving telegraphs and animations during intense combat sequences. Even if a game is capped at 60 FPS, a high-refresh monitor reduces perceived motion blur and input latency, providing a smoother experience when navigating menus or performing precise car customizations in the garage.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/hsr-cyrene-vs-sparkle-for-phainon-gear-guide/inline-4.webp)


While a 1440p monitor can significantly enhance the visual experience of Neverness to Everness, it is not always a necessary purchase.

If you already own a 1440p 144Hz IPS monitor from the last two to three years, the marginal gains in color accuracy or speed may not justify a $400 spend. The jump from 144Hz to 180Hz is far less noticeable than the jump from 60Hz to 144Hz.

Players primarily using mobile devices or tablets may find that a high-end portable monitor offers better utility than a fixed desktop display. If you enjoy the portability of gacha games, a stationary monitor might limit your playstyle. Furthermore, if your current PC hardware cannot maintain at least 60 FPS at 1440p in Unreal Engine 5 environments, upgrading your GPU should take priority over a high-refresh monitor. A high-refresh screen cannot fix the stuttering caused by an underpowered graphics card.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is 1440p better than 4K for Neverness to Everness?
For most players, 1440p is the superior choice because it allows for higher frame rates on mid-range hardware. In an open-world game with complex lighting like NTE, maintaining a stable 100+ FPS at 1440p feels much smoother than struggling to hit 60 FPS at 4K.

### Will playing gacha games cause burn-in on an OLED monitor?
Static UI elements like mini-maps and character portraits can increase the risk of burn-in over thousands of hours. However, modern OLED monitors like the Alienware AW2725DF include pixel-shifting technology and comprehensive warranties to mitigate these risks for daily players.

### What is a KVM switch and why do I need it?
A KVM switch allows you to control two different devices, such as your gaming PC and a docked mobile phone, using the same keyboard, mouse, and monitor. This is highly useful for players who switch between a PC for main story content and a mobile device for automated daily farming.

### Does Neverness to Everness support ultrawide 21:9 resolutions?
Official support for ultrawide resolutions varies by title, but most modern Hotta Studio games built on Unreal Engine 5 support 21:9 natively. This provides a wider field of view, which is particularly helpful for spotting anomalies in the urban environment of Hethereau.

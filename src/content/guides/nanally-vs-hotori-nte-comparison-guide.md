## Detailed Comparison

To truly understand which character fits your playstyle, we must look at how they function when the "Esper Cycle" begins and the anomalies start appearing.

### Combat Roles: Sustained DPS vs. Burst Utility
Nanally is designed to be the centerpiece of your team. Her gameplay revolves around the "Colucci Secret Skill" (Basic Attack) and her Skill, "Hehe, surprise time!". When she enters her enhanced state, she becomes a whirlwind of Anima damage. Because she specializes in follow-up attacks, her damage doesn't just come from your button presses; it comes from the "Underboss" she summons during her Ultimate, "Grand Entrance!". This summon mirrors her offensive rhythm for 6 seconds, effectively doubling her hit frequency and making her the best character for shredding enemy "Break" gauges in the current [NTE character tier list](#TODO:keyword:nte-tier-list).

Hotori, conversely, is the queen of the "Burst Window." She spends most of her time off-field or in a supportive capacity until her "Non-Closed Timepiece" energy is full. Her role is to "record" the most powerful abilities of her teammates using "Present Replay" and then unleash them all at once during "World's Tide." Because her Ultimate pauses the actual game timer for 5 seconds, she is indispensable for clearing high-level challenges where every second counts. She also provides team-wide healing and cleanses time-related debuffs, making her a much safer pick for high-difficulty boss encounters.

### Core Mechanics: Ichi-daime's Authority vs. Non-Closed Timepiece
The "Ichi-daime's Authority" state is what makes a [best Nanally build](#TODO:keyword:best-nanally-build-nte) so terrifying. Triggered by her Skill, this state lasts 12 seconds and grants a flat +30% Crit DMG bonus. More importantly, it enables her "Anti-Gravity Walking." In combat, this allows Nanally to ignore traditional battlefield constraints, running up walls or even onto the ceiling to avoid ground-based AoE attacks while continuing her assault.

Hotori’s "Non-Closed Timepiece" is a gauge that caps at 120 energy. It accumulates automatically while she is on the field or through specific combat actions. Her Skill, "Present Replay," consumes 60 energy to record the Support and Redirect skills used by her allies for the next 5 seconds. When she activates "World's Tide," she draws a katana from her umbrella, stops time, and replays every recorded attack simultaneously. This allows for massive "Blossom" elemental reactions that can delete boss health bars in a single rotation.

### Exploration Utility: Wall-Running vs. Time Manipulation
In the open world of Hethereau, both characters offer game-changing traversal. Nanally is the undisputed master of verticality. Her electromagnetic adhesion allows her to sprint directly up skyscrapers without the need for traditional climbing mechanics, making her the fastest character for reaching high-altitude caches and urban anomalies.

Hotori offers a different kind of utility. Outside of combat, she can use her accumulated Timepiece energy to slow down or even freeze time in the environment. This is essential for specific "Time-Stop" puzzles found throughout the city, such as freezing fast-moving platforms or stopping traffic to reach hidden areas. While she doesn't have Nanally’s raw speed, her ability to interact with the world’s physics makes her a staple for completionists.

### Team Synergy: How They Work Together
While this guide compares them, it is worth noting that Nanally and Hotori are actually designed to work perfectly together. In the "Time & Space" meta-team, Nanally acts as the Main DPS while Hotori provides the burst amplification. 

Nanally’s high hit frequency from her Vita Pistils and Underboss follow-ups helps build "Esper Cycle" energy rapidly. Hotori can then record Nanally’s "More Than Passionate" support skill (which fires 10 rapid shots) and replay it during the Time Stop window. This creates a loop where Nanally deals sustained damage, and Hotori provides the "finisher" burst that ensures no enemy survives the rotation. If you are lucky enough to pull both through the [Scarborough Fair gacha system](#TODO:keyword:nte-gacha-guide), you will have the strongest 1.0 duo currently available.

## Recommendation

Choosing who to pull depends heavily on your current resources (Solid Dice and Annulith) and what your account currently lacks.

### Who Should Pull Nanally?
You should pull for Nanally if:
*   **You are a F2P player:** Nanally has a very high "floor," meaning she performs exceptionally well even with F2P weapons like the "Song of the Whale."
*   **You enjoy fast-paced action:** Her "Street Fighter" style combos and wall-running mechanics make her one of the most mechanically fun characters to pilot.
*   **You need a Main DPS:** If your current team is struggling to kill enemies before the timer runs out, Nanally is the solution. Her follow-up attacks provide the consistent DPS needed for early-game progression.

### Who Should Pull Hotori?
You should pull for Hotori if:
*   **You plan for the long term:** Support and utility characters like Hotori typically stay in the meta much longer than pure damage dealers. Her Time Stop is a unique mechanic that is unlikely to be powercrept soon.
*   **You like technical rotations:** If you enjoy the "math" of gacha combat—timing your skills perfectly to maximize a 5-second window—you will find her extremely rewarding.
*   **You already have a Main DPS:** If you got lucky with a standard S-Rank pull and already have a solid damage dealer, Hotori is the best character to make that dealer hit even harder. Check out our [Hotori build guide](#TODO:keyword:best-hotori-build-nte) to see how to maximize her recording windows.

### The 'Both' Strategy: Managing Your Scarborough Fair Rolls
The NTE gacha system is notably player-friendly because it lacks the "50/50" mechanic found in other major titles. When you hit an S-Rank on a limited board, it is guaranteed to be the featured character. 

*   **Pity Mechanics:** Hard pity is at 90 rolls, but "Board Modification" typically triggers around 70 rolls, significantly increasing your chances. 
*   **Carryover:** If you pull for Nanally in Phase 1 but don't hit the S-Rank tile, your progress carries over to Hotori’s banner in Phase 2.
*   **The Strategy:** F2P players should aim to secure Nanally first to stabilize their combat power. If you manage to get her early (within 30-40 rolls), save everything else for Hotori. Having a "guaranteed" path to these characters makes it possible to get both if you diligently clear your daily urban anomalies and event tracks for Annulith.

**Final Verdict:** For immediate account progression and a smoother experience exploring Hethereau’s heights, **Nanally** is the recommended pick. However, for players looking to dominate the end-game "Deep Rift" challenges and maximize team synergy, **Hotori** is the superior long-term investment.

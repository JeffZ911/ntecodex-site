*   **Existing High-End Office Furniture:** If you already own a high-end task chair like a Herman Miller Aeron or Steelcase Gesture, "gaming" chairs will likely feel like a downgrade in breathability and long-term support. These chairs are designed for 12-hour workdays and offer superior ventilation compared to the Secretlab's synthetic leather.
*   **Controller Players:** If you primarily play with a controller, the investment in a $180 MMO mouse or $200 mechanical keyboard provides zero functional benefit. Your ergonomic focus should instead be on your chair and a [high-quality controller](https://www.razer.com/console-gaming/controllers) with back paddles.
*   **Hardware Bottlenecks:** If your PC cannot consistently output 100+ FPS in the detailed streets of Hesperia, a 160Hz ultrawide monitor will result in screen tearing or stuttering without significant hardware upgrades. Ensure your GPU can handle the 3440 x 1440 resolution before buying.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which character is better for Anomaly builds, Yanagi or Grace?
Yanagi typically offers higher burst potential through her stance-switching mechanics, making her ideal for quick-swap teams. Grace remains the queen of consistent Shock application, which is vital for Disorder-focused compositions that require high Anomaly Mastery.

### How does Neverness to Everness differ from Zenless Zone Zero?
While both are urban supernatural RPGs, *Neverness to Everness* (released April 2026) features a more expansive open-world city with drivable, customizable vehicles. ZZZ focuses more on instanced combat and rogue-lite elements, whereas NTE emphasizes exploration and "Esper" abilities within a seamless urban environment.

### Does a 160Hz monitor actually help with grinding?
Yes, a higher refresh rate reduces motion blur, which makes it easier to track small visual cues like Anomaly buildup bars or enemy telegraphs. This reduces the mental and ocular load during long sessions, allowing you to play longer without developing a headache.

### Why is a gasket-mount keyboard better for gaming?
A gasket-mount keyboard like the Keychron Q1 Pro places a piece of rubber or foam between the plate and the case. This creates a "cushion" that absorbs the force of your keystrokes, which significantly reduces the impact on your finger joints during high-APM combat rotations.

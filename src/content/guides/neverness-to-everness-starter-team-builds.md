## Overview

![Overview](/img/neverness-to-everness-starter-team-builds/inline-1.webp)


Success in Neverness to Everness hinges on the "Esper Cycle," a tag-team combat system that rewards players for swapping characters at precise intervals. Unlike other open-world RPGs where a single DPS might stay on the field for extended periods, NTE encourages a high-frequency rotation to maintain elemental pressure. 

Every team composition must adhere to a specific role distribution to handle the escalating difficulty of the New Herland District. A standard team consists of a Main DPS who occupies the field during burst windows, a Sub-DPS or "Battery" who generates energy and applies off-field elemental status, and a Support or Healer to provide sustain and stat buffs.

### Elemental Reactions: The Foundation of Damage

When two different elements interact on a target, they trigger a reaction that deals massive bonus damage or provides utility.

*   **Blossom (Cosmos + Anima):** Creates an ethereal flower that explodes for Area of Effect (AoE) damage based on the trigger character's ATK.
*   **Scorch (Incantation + Anima):** Deals damage over time and reduces the target's Physical Resistance.
*   **Nova (Cosmos + Incantation):** A high-impact blast that deals significant "Break" damage to enemy shields.
*   **Stain (Chaos + Cosmos):** Increases the damage taken by the enemy for 8 seconds.

### The Importance of the Esper Cycle

The Esper Cycle is the engine that drives NTE combat. As you use Skills and Ultimates, the Cycle Gauge fills. Once a character's portrait begins to glow, switching to them triggers a "Cycle Finisher"—a unique entrance animation that deals high damage and instantly applies their element. Mastering this timing is essential for triggering reactions like Blossom and Scorch without wasting field time.

### How to Use the Free S-Class Selector

New accounts receive an "Urban Recruitment Voucher" after completing the first major boss in the New Herland District. This allows you to pick one standard S-Class character. While [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide) is widely considered the best overall pick due to her universal ATK buffs and vacuum-style crowd control, players focused on pure damage often choose Chiz to pair with Zero for a dual-Cosmos resonance team.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

![Best Disks (Artifacts)](/img/neverness-to-everness-starter-team-builds/inline-3.webp)


NTE features a gear system called Cartridges (often referred to as Disks by the community). These are equipped in the "Module Console" and provide set bonuses that define a character's role. Each Esper has six slots for Cartridges, but you should prioritize completing 4-piece sets to unlock powerful passive effects.

### Premier Cartridge Sets

*   **Lost Radiance Set:** The gold standard for Cosmos DPS characters like Zero or Chiz. The 2-piece bonus increases Cosmos DMG by 15%, while the 4-piece bonus allows the wearer to ignore 25% of the enemy's DEF for 10 seconds after casting an Ultimate.
*   **Speedy Hedgehog Set:** This is essential for supports like Haniel or Aurelia. The 4-piece bonus grants a 15% ATK buff to the entire team whenever the wearer triggers an Esper Cycle swap.
*   **Fireflies and the Forest:** The best set for Anima-based damage dealers like Mint or [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte). It focuses on stacking Crit DMG, providing a [information unavailable] bonus that triggers when hitting enemies affected by the Scorch or Blossom status.

### Sub-stat Priority

When farming Cartridges in the "Rabbit Hole" dungeons, look for specific sub-stats. For Main DPS characters, the priority is:
1. Crit Rate (until 50%)
2. Crit DMG
3. Elemental DMG %
4. ATK %

For Supports, prioritize Energy Recharge % and HP % (if their healing scales with HP, like Edgar).

### Early Game Cartridge Farming (Rabbit Hole)

The "Rabbit Hole" is a series of combat challenges located in the New Herland District. Each floor rewards specific Cartridge sets. Beginners should focus on Floor 3 and Floor 4, which drop A-Rank versions of the Lost Radiance and Speedy Hedgehog sets. Do not spend your limited "Battery Packs" (stamina refills) on S-Rank Cartridge farming until you reach Account Level 40, as the drop rates are significantly better at that tier.

### Understanding the Module Console System

The Module Console allows you to "Tune" your Cartridges. Tuning uses a resource called "Anomalous Dust" to reroll a single sub-stat. This is a late-game mechanic, so early on, simply focus on leveling your 4-piece sets to +12 and ignoring the tuning system until your team is fully leveled.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/neverness-to-everness-starter-team-builds/inline-5.webp)


Mastering the rotation in Neverness to Everness is the difference between a 5-minute boss fight and a 60-second clear. The "Glowing" portrait prompt is your most important visual cue.

### Standard Burst Loop

To maximize damage, follow this sequence:
1.  **Start with Support:** Cast Haniel’s Skill to set up the ATK buff field.
2.  **Apply Primary Element:** Switch to Zero and use his Skill to fill the Cycle Gauge and apply Cosmos.
3.  **Trigger the Cycle:** Once Mint’s portrait glows, swap to her. This triggers her Cycle Finisher, applying Anima and triggering Blossom.
4.  **Ultimate Dump:** Cast Mint’s Ultimate while the enemy is affected by the Blossom debuff.
5.  **Reset:** Switch back to Zero using his "Instant Chain" ability to bypass the swap cooldown.

### Managing the Cycle Gauge

Zero possesses a unique mechanic called "Instant Chain." This allows him to force a swap even if the gauge isn't full, though it consumes a portion of his stamina. Use this to maintain elemental reactions if your rotation gets interrupted by enemy crowd control.

### How to Trigger Discord and Hexed Reactions

While Blossom and Scorch are the basics, advanced players look for Discord and Hexed.
*   **Discord:** Triggered when three elements (Cosmos, Anima, and Incantation) are applied within a 4-second window. It causes a massive elemental collapse that freezes the enemy's posture bar.
*   **Hexed:** [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-24]. It is theorized to involve Chaos and Incantation, but official frame data is still being analyzed by the community.

### Reaction Timing

Avoid "over-applying" elements. If an enemy is already under the "Stain" effect, wait for the duration to nearly expire before triggering it again. This ensures 100% uptime on the damage vulnerability window.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/neverness-to-everness-starter-team-builds/inline-6.webp)


### Should I reroll?
Rerolling in Neverness to Everness takes approximately 20 to 30 minutes per attempt. It is worth it if you are a competitive player aiming for Nanally or Sakiri. However, the free A-Class roster is robust enough to clear all current content, so rerolling is not mandatory for a casual experience.

### Who is the best free character?
Zero remains a top-tier unit even into the mid-game. Their "Instant Chain" mechanic is unique among the current roster, allowing for more flexible rotations than many S-Class characters.

### How do I get more Carrota Coins?
Carrota Coins (the premium currency) are primarily earned through "Rabbit Hole" first-time clears, district exploration chests, and daily commissions. Completing the "Urban Handbook" milestones is also a major source of coins for F2P players.

### Can I play without S-Class characters?
Yes. The synergy between Adler (Shield Break), Haniel (Buff), and Edgar (Heal) creates a very safe "turtle" comp that can slowly but surely defeat any boss in Version 1.0.

### Resource Management for F2P Players
Focus your resources on leveling only three characters at a time. Spreading your materials across six or seven characters will leave your main team underpowered for the Chapter 3 difficulty spike. Prioritize Character Level > Arc Level > Cartridge Level.

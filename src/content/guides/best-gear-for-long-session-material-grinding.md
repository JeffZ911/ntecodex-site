## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/best-gear-for-long-session-material-grinding/inline-4.webp)


Not every piece of hardware is a necessary investment for every player. Understanding the point of diminishing returns can save you hundreds of dollars that could be better spent elsewhere.

If you already own a 1440p IPS monitor with a refresh rate above 144Hz, the jump to 165Hz or 180Hz is negligible for material farming. The human eye struggles to perceive the difference in fluidity outside of professional-grade FPS titles. Unless your current panel suffers from significant ghosting or color inaccuracy that hinders your ability to see Sakiri or other characters in dark environments, your current screen is likely sufficient.

Don't buy a high-end MMO mouse if you primarily play with a controller. The ergonomic benefits of the Naga V2 Pro are lost if you aren't using the side buttons for keybinds. Neverness to Everness features robust controller support, and if that is your preferred input method, investing in a high-performance "pro" controller with back paddles would be a more ergonomic choice than a mouse you rarely touch.

If your current chair has a flat seat and you don't experience lower back pain, a $500 ergonomic chair upgrade may be unnecessary. While the TITAN Evo 2022 is excellent, many users find that a simple, high-quality lumbar roll or an adjustable footrest can provide 80% of the benefit for a fraction of the cost.

## What we didn't include and why

![What we didn't include and why](/img/best-gear-for-long-session-material-grinding/inline-5.webp)


To maintain a high standard of ergonomic health, several popular categories were intentionally omitted from our recommendations.

**DXRacer Racing Seats:** The aggressive 'bucket' wings found on many traditional gaming chairs push the shoulders forward. This "rounded shoulder" posture is detrimental during long material farming sessions, as it restricts lung capacity and leads to upper back tension. We favored chairs with a more open backrest design that allows for natural movement.

**Generic 'Amazon Choice' Gaming Mice:** These often use outdated sensors that spin out during fast movements. In a game that requires precise navigation of both 3D space and 2D menus, a sensor that "jitters" or fails to track on certain surfaces leads to frustration and unnecessary muscle tension. We only recommend sensors with proven track records in technical testing.

**TN Panel Monitors:** While TN panels are historically the fastest in terms of response time, their poor color shift and narrow viewing angles make material icons and UI elements look washed out compared to IPS or OLED. When you are spending hours looking at color-coded loot and supernatural effects, visual clarity is more important than a fractional millisecond of speed.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does a higher refresh rate help with material farming?
A higher refresh rate reduces motion blur, which makes it easier to spot small, glimmering materials or anomaly markers while moving quickly through the city. While it doesn't increase drop rates, it reduces the "visual noise" that causes eye fatigue during long sessions.

### Is a 12-button mouse necessary for Neverness to Everness?
It is not strictly necessary, but it significantly improves efficiency when managing the game's multiple UI layers, such as car tuning, character Arcs, and inventory. Mapping these to the side buttons allows you to keep your left hand on the movement keys at all times.

### How does a monitor light bar reduce eye strain?
A light bar like the BenQ ScreenBar Halo illuminates your workspace without reflecting off the screen. This reduces the contrast between your bright monitor and the dark room, preventing your pupils from constantly dilating and constricting, which is the primary cause of late-night headaches.

### Are firm gaming chairs better than soft ones for long sessions?
Yes, because firm cold-cure foam provides consistent support that doesn't "bottom out" over time. Soft, plush chairs may feel better for the first thirty minutes, but they often lead to poor posture and "tailbone ache" after several hours of continuous use.

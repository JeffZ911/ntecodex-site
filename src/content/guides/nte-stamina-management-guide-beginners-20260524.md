# Neverness to Everness Stamina Management Guide: Best Builds and Beginner Tips

Successfully navigating the urban landscape and supernatural anomalies of Neverness to Everness (NTE) depends heavily on how you manage three distinct resource pools. Unlike traditional open-world RPGs that rely on a single energy bar, NTE splits your activity potential into combat-focused energy, city-management resources, and physical traversal limits. Failing to optimize these can lead to stalled progression, especially when attempting to farm high-level Cartridges or participate in time-sensitive Tycoon events.

## Overview

![Overview](/img/nte-stamina-management-guide-beginners-20260524/inline-1.webp)


Neverness to Everness utilizes a multi-tiered stamina system designed to separate your exploration, combat, and economic activities. This prevents players from being "locked out" of the entire game once their combat energy is spent, allowing for continued play through city management and world traversal.

### Understanding Character Pixels

Character Pixels serve as the primary "gacha stamina" or resin equivalent in NTE. This resource is consumed whenever you claim rewards from urban anomalies, boss encounters, or the "Eclipse" domains used for leveling up Arcs and characters. 

The regeneration rate is fixed at 1 Pixel every 6 minutes, totaling 240 Pixels over a full 24-hour cycle. Because the cap is strictly set at 240, it is vital to spend them at least once a day to avoid "capping out" and wasting potential progress. Beginners should prioritize spending Pixels on "Arc Exp" materials and "Character Breakthrough" items during the first week to ensure their main DPS stays ahead of the world level scaling.

### The City Tycoon Stamina System

City Stamina is a separate, weekly resource pool used exclusively for the "City Tycoon" gameplay loop. This includes activities such as street racing, managing your personal café, and participating in high-stakes heists. Unlike Character Pixels, City Stamina does not refresh daily; instead, you receive a bulk refresh at the start of each week (Monday at 04:00 server time).

This system governs your ability to earn "Hethereau Credits" and specialized shop currency. If you exhaust your City Stamina early in the week, you can still visit your businesses, but you will not be able to initiate new profit-generating missions or enter competitive races.

### Movement Stamina and the Witch's House

Movement Stamina is the white bar that appears when you sprint, climb, or swim. While this bar refills automatically when you stand still or walk, its base capacity is quite low. To increase your maximum movement stamina, you must visit the Witch’s House, located in the secluded forest area southwest of Hankaku Street.

By trading "Oracle Stones"—which are obtained by finding and collecting the glowing blue birds hidden throughout the city—to the Witch, you can permanently increase your stamina cap. This is essential for characters like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte), who rely on stamina for specialized traversal mechanics.

## Best Weapons

In Neverness to Everness, weapons are known as Arcs. These Arcs are categorized by their elemental state: Plasma, Solid, Gas, Liquid, and Synthesis. Choosing the right Arc is not just about raw attack power; it is about how the weapon interacts with your energy regeneration and stamina consumption during the "Esper Cycle."

### Top Arcs for Energy Regeneration

For beginners, the most valuable Arcs are those that provide "Ultimate Energy" or reduce the cost of abilities, as this lessens the reliance on long, stamina-heavy basic attack chains.

1.  **"Day Off" (Solid):** This is widely considered the best F2P-friendly Arc for sub-DPS characters. Its passive effect restores a flat amount of Ultimate Energy whenever an enemy is defeated while an "Eclipse" field is active. This allows for nearly 100% uptime on buffs in multi-target scenarios.
2.  **"Good Boy's Grand Adventure" (Gas):** A premier support Arc. It provides a team-wide utility buff that increases the rate at which the "Esper Meter" fills. By filling the meter faster, you can trigger finishers earlier, saving you from having to dodge and burn movement stamina in prolonged fights.
3.  **"Raging Flames" (Anima):** A highly reliable choice for Anima-type damage dealers. It increases damage dealt based on the percentage of Movement Stamina currently remaining, rewarding players who manage their dodges effectively.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

![Best Disks (Artifacts)](/img/nte-stamina-management-guide-beginners-20260524/inline-3.webp)


The "Console" system in NTE is where you customize your characters using Cartridges (Disks) and Modules. Cartridges provide set bonuses, while Modules are Tetris-like pieces that fit into your Console grid to provide raw stat boosts like Crit Rate, ATK, and Elemental DMG.

### The Console System: Cartridges and Modules

To activate a Cartridge set bonus, you must equip at least two or four Disks of the same set. However, the effectiveness of these Disks is limited by your Console's "CPU Capacity." Higher-rarity Cartridges take up more "slots" on your grid. Beginners should focus on 2-piece bonuses early on, as attempting to cram a full 4-piece Legendary set into a low-level Console often results in having no room left for vital stat Modules.

### Top-Tier Cartridge Sets for Progression

1.  **Speedy Hedgehog:** This is the gold standard for utility. The 2-piece bonus provides +12% Charge Efficiency, while the 4-piece bonus grants a team-wide ATK buff for 12 seconds after the wearer uses an Ultimate. This set is perfect for characters like Sakiri or Adler.
2.  **Lost Radiance (Cosmos):** The premier choice for Cosmos-element dealers like Chiz. It focuses on increasing "Break" damage, allowing you to shatter enemy shields faster and end fights before your movement stamina runs out.
3.  **Fireflies and the Forest (Anima):** This set is designed for Nanally. It boosts Anima DMG and provides a stacking Crit Damage buff every time a "Wall-Walk" or "Air-Dash" is performed.
4.  **Tiny Big Adventure:** The best survival set. It significantly increases Max HP. This is the best-in-slot set for Fadia, as her damage scales directly with her total health pool.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/nte-stamina-management-guide-beginners-20260524/inline-5.webp)


Combat in NTE is defined by the "Esper Cycle." This is a rhythm-based combat flow where you build up energy through elemental interactions to unleash a devastating finisher.

### Mastering the Esper Cycle

The goal is to fill the circular meter at the bottom of the screen. You do this by applying "Anomalous Elements" to enemies. 

1.  **Setup:** Start with your supports (like Adler or Haniel). Cast their shields or off-field healing Arcs.
2.  **Application:** Swap to your sub-DPS to apply an initial element (e.g., Incantation or Psyche).
3.  **Reaction:** Swap to your main DPS (Nanally or Chiz) to apply a secondary element like Chaos. This triggers reactions:
    *   **Scorch (Incantation + Chaos):** High damage over time and reduces enemy defense.
    *   **Nova (Psyche + Chaos):** Massive AOE burst that deals heavy "Break" damage to shields.
4.  **Finisher:** Once the meter is full, a character icon in your UI will "glow." Immediately swap to that character to execute the Esper Cycle finisher. This move consumes no stamina and deals massive damage.

### Stamina-Saving Combat Maneuvers

To keep your movement stamina high for dodging lethal boss attacks, utilize Nanally’s follow-up attacks. After a successful dodge or a specific skill, Nanally can zip back to the enemy instantly. This "auto-repositioning" is much more stamina-efficient than manually sprinting back into melee range. Furthermore, using Sakiri’s ultimate to group enemies ensures you don't waste stamina running between targets.

### Standard Esper Cycle Rotation for Beginners

*   **Step 1:** Activate Support Skill (Haniel/Adler) for protection.
*   **Step 2:** Apply Primary Element (Sakiri/Zero) to build the meter.
*   **Step 3:** Trigger Elemental Reaction (Nanally/Chiz) to shred shields.
*   **Step 4:** Execute Glowing Finisher for maximum burst damage.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/nte-stamina-management-guide-beginners-20260524/inline-6.webp)


**How do I recover Character Pixels?**
Character Pixels recover naturally at a rate of 1 every 6 minutes. To recover them instantly, you can use a "De-noise Solution" item, which restores 60 Pixels. These are often given as rewards for increasing your Hunter Level or through limited-time event shops.

**Where do I get Oracle Stones?**
Oracle Stones are the reward for finding the hidden blue birds scattered throughout the Hethereau districts. Once you have collected a handful, take them to the Witch's House in the southwest forest to trade them for permanent movement stamina upgrades.

**What is the maximum Character Pixel cap?**
The base cap for all players is 240 Pixels. This cap does not currently increase with your Hunter Level, making it important to spend your Pixels daily.

**Does City Stamina affect combat?**
No. City Stamina is strictly used for the Tycoon management systems, racing, and heists. You can be at zero City Stamina and still participate in all combat missions and boss fights using Character Pixels.

**How do I heal without a healer?**
If you don't have Haniel or another healer in your party, you can heal by approaching any Wertheimer Tower or ReroRero Phone Booth. These serve as fast-travel points and will automatically restore your entire team's HP when you stand near them for a few seconds.

# Neverness to Everness Stamina Management Guide: Best Builds for Efficiency

In the urban sprawl of Hethereau, players must balance high-octane supernatural combat with the economic demands of city life. Unlike traditional open-world RPGs that utilize a single energy metric, Neverness to Everness (NTE) implements a sophisticated triple-stamina system. This guide breaks down how to optimize Combat Stamina, Character Pixels, and City Stamina to ensure your Esper team remains at peak performance while your Tycoon rank continues to climb.

## Overview

![Overview](/img/neverness-to-everness-stamina-management-guide/inline-1.webp)


Managing resources in NTE is a multifaceted endeavor. Because the game blends character progression with urban simulation, players must track three distinct pools of energy that govern movement, power growth, and economic activity. Failing to manage these effectively can result in stalled character levels or a lack of Fons (the primary city currency) needed for high-end upgrades.

The following table summarizes the primary stamina types available as of the 2026-04-29 release:

| **City Stamina** | 100–500 | Weekly Reset | Racing, Fishing, Tycoon XP, Fons Farming |

### The Combat Stamina System
Combat Stamina is the most immediate resource players interact with. It is represented by a bar near the character model that depletes during high-intensity actions. In Hethereau’s vertical environment, this stamina is consumed not just by dodging and sprinting, but also by wall-running and certain character-specific traversal skills. Managing this pool is critical during boss encounters, as running out of stamina leaves the player unable to trigger "Redirect Skills" or perform emergency evasions.

### Character Pixels: Daily Progression
Character Pixels serve as the "energy" or "resin" equivalent in NTE. With a cap of 240, this resource is the primary gatekeeper for character power. Spending Pixels in Anomaly Zones allows players to acquire leveling materials, Beetle Coins, and Modules. Because Pixels regenerate at a rate of 10 per hour, a full recharge takes 24 hours. Efficient players should ensure they never sit at the 240 cap to maximize their daily growth potential.

### City Stamina: Weekly Tycoon Growth
City Stamina is a unique resource dedicated to the "Hethereau Hobbies" and urban simulation aspects of the game. Unlike Character Pixels, which focus on combat power, City Stamina is used for activities like street racing and fishing. These activities are the primary source of Tycoon XP, which increases your rank in the city and unlocks higher-tier shops and passive income streams.

### Top S-Rank Arcs for Combat Efficiency
S-Rank Arcs provide the highest base statistics and often feature passives that drastically alter stamina or energy management. For boss encounters, Plasma-type Arcs are generally preferred for their ability to boost ATK and Crit Rate following a successful Redirect Skill. These weapons reward players who master the timing of parries and dodges, effectively turning defensive play into offensive momentum.

For players focusing on Ultimate-heavy rotations, Solid-type Arcs are essential. These Arcs often feature mechanics that restore Ultimate Energy upon defeating enemies during specific combat windows, such as the "Eclipse" state. This allows for faster cycling between Espers and reduces the downtime between powerful burst windows.

### Best F2P Weapon Alternatives
Free-to-play (F2P) players can find high-value Arcs through world exploration and the City Tycoon rewards. Liquid and Gas-type Arcs are frequently available as rewards for reaching specific Tycoon Ranks. While they may lack the raw scaling of S-Rank Plasma Arcs, they often provide utility such as increased Cosmos DMG or reduced Combat Stamina consumption for charged attacks, making them excellent for early-to-mid-game progression.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

![Best Disks (Artifacts)](/img/neverness-to-everness-stamina-management-guide/inline-3.webp)


The artifact system in NTE is known as Modules and Cartridges. These are slotted into a character's "Console," which features a grid-based interface. Unlike standard equipment slots, Modules must be fitted into the grid, requiring players to choose between different shapes and sizes (Types II, III, and IV) to maximize set bonuses and stat distributions.

### Understanding Module Types (II, III, and IV)
Modules are categorized by the number of squares they occupy on the Console grid. 
*   **Type II:** Smallest modules, usually providing minor stat boosts but easy to fit into tight corners.
*   **Type III:** Medium-sized modules that often serve as the core of a set.
*   **Type IV:** Large, powerful modules that provide the strongest individual stats but take up significant grid space.

Characters like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) receive specific resonance bonuses when using certain Module Types, such as a +6% Crit Rate increase when the grid is optimized with specific shapes.

### Best Module Sets for Every Element
To maximize damage and stamina efficiency, players should aim for 2-piece or 4-piece set bonuses.

*   **Crimson Twin Butterflies (Incantation Set):** This set is the premier choice for Incantation DPS characters like Baicang. It provides stacking ATK buffs whenever an enemy takes elemental damage, making it ideal for sustained combat.
*   **Lost Radiance (Cosmos Set):** Essential for Cosmos-element units, this set grants a 25% DEF ignore for a short duration after casting an Ultimate.
*   **Shadow Creed (ATK Set):** A highly versatile set that provides a 25% ATK boost for 20 seconds after a successful Redirect Skill, rewarding players who actively parry.
*   **Fireflies and the Forest (Anima Set):** The standard for Anima DPS like Nanally and Mint. This set boosts Crit DMG based on the frequency of Anima procs, significantly increasing burst potential.

## Team Comp

Combat in Neverness to Everness centers on the "Esper Cycle." This mechanic encourages players to swap characters when they begin to "glow," triggering powerful elemental reactions and chain attacks. A well-constructed team balances a Main DPS, a Sub-DPS, a Break specialist, and a Support.

### The Meta: Hyper Nanally Team
The Hyper Nanally composition is currently the most effective team for both high-level Anima burst damage and vertical exploration.
*   **Nanally (Main DPS):** Provides massive Anima damage and high mobility.
*   **Jiuyuan (Sub-DPS):** Offers off-field support and helps maintain the Esper Cycle.
*   **Zero (Break):** Specialized in shredding enemy shields and poise.
*   **Haniel (Support):** Provides essential buffs and debuffs to maximize Nanally's burst window.

### The Breaker: Discord Reaction Team
The Immortal Discord team focuses on high sustain and damage redirection.
*   **Fadia:** Acts as the anchor, using damage redirection to protect the team.
*   **Daffodil:** A Break specialist who excels at opening up enemies for "Discord" reactions.
*   **Adler:** Provides elemental utility and helps build the Esper Meter quickly.
*   **Nanally:** Included here for her consistent Anima application and cleanup potential.

### Best F2P Team for Beginners
For players just starting their journey in Hethereau, a reliable and stamina-efficient team can be built using early-game units:
*   **Zero:** Your primary shield breaker and poise damage dealer.
*   **Edgar:** A solid DPS who can carry the team through early Anomaly Zones.
*   **Haniel:** Provides the necessary utility to keep the team healthy and buffed.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/neverness-to-everness-stamina-management-guide/inline-5.webp)


Mastering the rotation in NTE is about more than just pressing buttons; it is about managing the Esper Meter and Combat Stamina simultaneously. A botched rotation can leave your Main DPS without enough stamina to dodge a lethal blow or without the energy needed to trigger a reaction.

### Mastering the Esper Cycle
The basic flow of an NTE rotation follows these steps:
1.  **Apply Debuffs:** Start with your Support (e.g., Haniel or [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide)) to apply elemental marks or defense shreds.
2.  **Build Meter:** Use Redirect Skills or parry enemy attacks to fill the Esper Meter. Parrying is the most efficient way to reach the "glow" state quickly.
3.  **Trigger Reactions:** Once the meter is full and the next character in the cycle is glowing, swap to your Sub-DPS or Break specialist.
4.  **Main DPS Burst:** Swap to your Main DPS (Nanally or Baicang) only when the Esper Cycle is fully primed to trigger reactions like Blossom or Scorch.
5.  **Finish with Ultimates:** Use Ultimates at the end of the rotation to capitalize on set bonuses like Lost Radiance.

### Stamina-Conscious Combat Tips
Stamina consumption for sprinting and dodging is significantly increased when enemies are nearby. To maintain efficiency:
*   **Avoid Dash-Spamming:** Only dodge when a "Redirect" prompt appears or to avoid a clear telegraph.
*   **Conserve with Mint:** If using Mint, her "Minty Whirlwind" (held basic attack) should only be used when your stamina is above 50% to avoid being left vulnerable.
*   **Use Verticality:** In the open world, use character abilities to gain height rather than relying solely on wall-climbing stamina.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/neverness-to-everness-stamina-management-guide/inline-6.webp)


**What is the maximum Character Pixel cap?**
The cap is 240 Character Pixels. They regenerate at a rate of 1 per 6 minutes, meaning a full recharge from zero takes 24 hours.

**When does City Stamina reset?**
City Stamina resets every Monday at 5:00 AM server time. It is important to spend your weekly allowance on hobbies before this reset to maximize Tycoon XP.

**How do I increase my City Stamina cap?**
You can increase your City Stamina cap by leveling up your City Tycoon rank. The cap starts at 100 and can reach a maximum of 500 at Tycoon Rank 16.

**Is there a way to recover Character Pixels?**
Yes, players can use De-noise Solutions to instantly restore Pixels. Additionally, you can spend Annulith to refresh Pixels up to 6 times daily, though the cost increases with each refresh.

**Does sprinting cost more stamina in combat?**
Yes. Neverness to Everness features a dynamic stamina cost system where sprinting and dodging consume more resources when the player is in an active combat state compared to exploration.

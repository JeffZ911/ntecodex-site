## Overview

![Overview](/img/nte-combat-tips-and-tricks-build-guide/inline-1.webp)


Combat in Neverness to Everness is a high-speed dance of swapping characters and managing elemental gauges. Unlike traditional action RPGs where characters act in isolation, NTE focuses on the **Esper Cycle**, a system that rewards players for constant rotation and reactive play. Every action you take—from landing a basic attack to executing a perfect parry—contributes to your team's tactical advantage.

### Understanding Esper Cycles

The core of NTE combat is the Esper Meter, located next to each character's portrait. As you deal damage or use skills, this meter fills. Once full, the character's portrait will glow, indicating they are ready to initiate an Esper Cycle swap. Swapping into a character with a full meter triggers a unique entry attack and, more importantly, facilitates elemental reactions based on the previous character's element.

To maximize your damage, you should never stay on one character for too long. The goal is to cycle through your four-person team, constantly triggering reactions. For example, applying an Anima mark with [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) and then immediately swapping to Zero (Cosmos) triggers the Blossom reaction, dealing massive area-of-effect (AoE) damage.

### Mastering the Stagger Meter

Elite enemies and bosses possess a white Stagger Meter located directly beneath their health bar. While this meter is active, the enemy has high resistance to hitstun and takes reduced damage. Breaking this meter is your primary objective in any boss encounter. 

Once the Stagger Meter is depleted, the enemy enters a "Break" state. During this window, the enemy is completely immobilized and takes [information unavailable] additional damage—often cited by the community as a 50% multiplier. Saving your Ultimates for this specific window is the difference between a five-minute slog and a thirty-second execution.

### Parry vs. Perfect Dodge

NTE provides two primary defensive tools: the Parry and the Critical Riposte (Perfect Dodge).

*   **Parrying:** This is the most efficient way to fill your Esper Meter. To parry, you must watch for two red circles closing in on an enemy. When the circles overlap, performing a Basic Attack or a character swap will trigger a Parry. A successful Parry completely negates incoming damage and instantly fills the active character's Esper Meter to 100%.
*   **Critical Riposte (Perfect Dodge):** If you dodge just before an attack lands, you trigger a slow-motion effect. This is safer than parrying but provides less offensive momentum. It is best used against multi-hit attacks where the parry window is too risky to time.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/nte-combat-tips-and-tricks-build-guide/inline-5.webp)


The goal of a rotation is to maximize the time the enemy spends in the "Break" state while keeping reaction buffs active.

### The 'Instant Chain' Swap Strategy

To execute a perfect rotation, follow this flow
1.  **Initiate with Support:** Start with Sakiri. Use her Skill to group enemies and her Ultimate to trigger the "Speedy Hedgehog" set bonus.
2.  **Apply Primary Element:** Swap to your Anima or Incantation unit (e.g., Nanally or Baicang). Use a Skill to apply an elemental mark.
3.  **Trigger Esper Cycle:** As soon as Zero’s portrait glows, swap to them. Zero’s entry attack will trigger a reaction (Blossom or Nova). Immediately use Zero's Skill to refill their meter and provide energy to the next character.
4.  **The Finisher:** Swap back to your Main DPS. If the enemy's Stagger Meter is low, use your Basic Attacks to break them.

### Maximizing the Break Window

Once the enemy is staggered, do not immediately spam every Ultimate. 
*   First, ensure a reaction like "Stain" (Psyche + Lakshana) is active to increase Crit Damage.
*   Then, unleash your Main DPS Ultimate.
*   Continue using Basic Attacks to generate energy for the next cycle before the enemy recovers.

### Energy Management Tips

If you find yourself waiting for cooldowns, you are likely not parrying enough. A single Parry provides more energy than an entire combo of Basic Attacks. If your rotation feels "stuck," swap to a character with a fast attack animation and fish for a Parry.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/nte-combat-tips-and-tricks-build-guide/inline-6.webp)


**How do I parry in NTE?**
Keep a close eye on the enemy's movements. When you see two red concentric circles closing in on the enemy's body, perform any attack or a character swap at the exact moment the circles overlap. This will trigger a Parry, negating damage and fully charging your Esper Meter.

**What is the best way to farm Cartridges?**
The most efficient method is using Character Pixels in the "Rewind" system. This is located within the Miguel District. You can target specific sets by selecting different "Anomaly Memories."

**Is the MC (Zero) worth building?**
Absolutely. Zero is an S-Rank Cosmos hybrid who is unique because of their ability to provide instant Esper Meter gain for the rest of the team. Even in the late game, Zero remains a staple in most meta teams because of their flexibility.

**Can I play NTE without pulling for Arcs?**
Yes. While S-Rank Arcs like "Ready-Ready" are powerful, you can obtain several high-quality S-Rank Arcs through gameplay. "Hethereau's Keeper" is farmable through City Tycoon ranks, and many A-Rank Arcs are viable for all story content.

**What does Cycle Intensity do?**
Cycle Intensity is a multiplier for your Esper Meter gain. The higher this stat, the fewer hits you need to land before you can swap to another character and trigger a reaction. It is the most important stat for maintaining a smooth rotation.

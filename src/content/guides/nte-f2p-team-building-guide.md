## Overview

The combat in NTE is built around the "Esper Cycle" system. Unlike traditional action RPGs where you might stay on one character for long periods, NTE encourages constant switching to trigger powerful effects. Every time you perform an action—whether it is a basic attack, a skill, or a parry—you build a meter that allows for an "Esper Swap." When a swap is triggered at the right time, the incoming character performs a unique entry attack that can initiate an elemental reaction.

## Team Comp

![Team Comp](/img/nte-f2p-team-building-guide/inline-4.webp)


Building an effective F2P team requires balancing elements to ensure you can always trigger reactions. The most common mistake is putting three characters of the same element together, which prevents the Esper Cycle from functioning correctly.

### The Ultimate F2P Starter Team

The most reliable team for new players consists of **Zero**, **Edgar**, and **Haniel**. 
*   **Zero (Cosmos DPS):** As the protagonist, Zero has a versatile kit that excels at both single-target and AoE damage.
*   **Edgar (Cosmos Healer):** Edgar provides consistent healing and can help maintain the Cosmos elemental lane.
*   **Haniel (Psyche Support):** Haniel offers crowd control and buffs that increase the team's movement speed and attack speed.

This trio provides a stable "lane" where you can safely learn the timing of parries and swaps without fear of dying.

### Transitional Mid-Game Comps

Once you pull more characters, consider the "Boss Shredder" team: **Adler**, **Mint**, and **Chiz**.
*   **Adler (Incantation):** Adler is arguably the best A-rank investment in the game. He provides DEF-scaling shields and applies a consistent fire-like DoT to enemies.
*   **Mint (Anima):** Mint’s primary strength is her "Tornado" charged attack, which groups enemies together, making it easy for your DPS to hit everyone at once.
*   **Chiz (Cosmos):** Chiz acts as a quick-swap DPS, coming in to deliver high-burst damage before rotating back to Adler to refresh shields.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/nte-f2p-team-building-guide/inline-5.webp)


Mastering the rotation is what separates a casual player from a top-tier F2P strategist. The goal is to maximize your "Cycle Value" so you can trigger as many reactions as possible during a single encounter.

### Mastering the Perfect Parry

Combat in NTE is highly reactive. When an enemy is about to attack, a yellow flash will appear. Performing a "Perfect Parry" at this moment does more than just negate damage; it instantly fills a significant portion of your Esper meter and can force an immediate reaction window. For F2P teams, parrying is the fastest way to generate energy for Ultimates.

### Maximizing the Break Window

The standard combat loop should follow this sequence:
1.  **Build Meter:** Start with your Support (like Haniel or Mint) and use basic attacks and skills to build Cycle Value.
2.  **Apply Element A:** Use a skill to apply the first element to the enemy.
3.  **Esper Swap:** Switch to your secondary character (like Adler) to trigger the first reaction and build the Break Meter.
4.  **The Stagger:** Once the enemy's white bar is gone, swap to your Main DPS (Zero or Chiz).
5.  **Unload:** Use your Ultimate and high-damage skills while the enemy is incapacitated.

Keep Zero on the field only during the Break window or when your other characters' skills are on cooldown. Cycling through Adler’s shields and Mint’s crowd control ensures that Zero is always protected when it is time to deal damage.

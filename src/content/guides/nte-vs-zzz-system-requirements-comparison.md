# NTE vs ZZZ System Requirements Comparison for Urban Open-World Players

ZZZ is highly optimized for mid-range hardware (GTX 1660), while NTE’s Unreal Engine 5 environment demands an RTX 3060+ for stable frames. We recommend the LG 27GP850-B for its color accuracy and the Secretlab TITAN Evo for long-session support.

The landscape of urban gacha games shifted significantly with the release of Neverness to Everness (NTE), introducing a level of graphical density that challenges even modern gaming rigs. While [Zenless Zone Zero](https://zenless.hoyoverse.com/) (ZZZ) focuses on stylized, high-speed combat within contained environments, NTE pushes the boundaries of the genre by utilizing Unreal Engine 5 to render a seamless supernatural city. This difference in engine philosophy creates a massive gap in system requirements. Players transitioning from the smooth, 120 FPS experience of New Eriadu to the ray-traced streets of Hesperia will find that their hardware needs to work much harder to maintain visual fluidity.

## What an urban open-world player actually needs

![What an urban open-world player actually needs](/img/nte-vs-zzz-system-requirements-comparison/inline-2.webp)


The shift from the semi-contained hubs of ZZZ to the full open world of NTE changes what hardware matters most. In a game like NTE, high color accuracy is paramount. The urban environments utilize complex lighting systems, including Lumen and Ray Tracing, to create realistic reflections on wet pavement and glass skyscrapers. A monitor with high DCI-P3 coverage allows you to see the subtle color shifts in these supernatural environments that budget screens would simply wash out.

Low latency remains a universal requirement. ZZZ's combat is heavily frame-dependent, requiring precise timing for perfect dodges. Mice with optical switches and high polling rates are essential to reduce input lag during these high-stakes encounters. Furthermore, because open-world games encourage long, stationary sessions, a chair must have adjustable internal lumbar support rather than static pillows to maintain healthy posture and prevent lower back fatigue over time.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/nte-vs-zzz-system-requirements-comparison/inline-4.webp)


If you are currently running an RTX 30-series card and a 1440p IPS monitor, the marginal gains for ZZZ will be minimal. The game is exceptionally well-optimized and hits its ceiling quickly on modern hardware. Similarly, there is no need to buy a high-end mechanical keyboard if you exclusively play with a controller; the ergonomic and tactile benefits of a custom deck won't apply to your specific use case.

Upgrading your chair is also unnecessary if your current seat already features functional, adjustable lumbar support. Many "gaming" chair upgrades are purely aesthetic and won't offer a tangible improvement to your posture if you already have a high-quality office chair. Focus your budget on the components that directly impact your ability to run NTE’s demanding Unreal Engine 5 features, such as your GPU or NVMe storage.

For these games, the visual trade-off is simply too high, making IPS or OLED panels the only viable choices for a premium experience.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I run Neverness to Everness on a GTX 1060?
While you may be able to launch the game, a GTX 1060 will struggle to maintain playable frame rates in NTE's open-world city due to the lack of DLSS support and high VRAM demands of Unreal Engine 5. For a stable 1080p experience, an RTX 2060 or higher is recommended to take advantage of AI upscaling.

### Does ZZZ support 120 FPS on PC?
Yes, Zenless Zone Zero supports 120 FPS on PC, provided your hardware can maintain it. This high frame rate is highly beneficial for the game's fast-paced parry and dodge mechanics, making a high-refresh-rate monitor like the LG UltraGear a worthwhile investment.

### How much storage do I need for both games?
As of mid-2026, you should set aside approximately 160 GB of space to house both titles comfortably. ZZZ requires about 57 GB, while NTE’s massive open world and high-resolution textures push its installation size closer to 100 GB. Using an NVMe SSD is highly recommended to minimize loading times between urban districts.

### Is a controller better than a mouse and keyboard for these games?
This depends on your playstyle. ZZZ feels very natural on a controller due to its action-heavy, combo-based combat. However, NTE’s open-world exploration and menu-heavy systems often benefit from the precision and hotkey availability of a mouse and keyboard setup.

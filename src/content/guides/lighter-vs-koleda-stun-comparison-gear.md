## What high-APM gacha players actually need

![What high-APM gacha players actually need](/img/lighter-vs-koleda-stun-comparison-gear/inline-2.webp)


High Actions Per Minute (APM) gameplay in modern gacha titles like Neverness to Everness or Zenless Zone Zero places unique demands on a display. Unlike turn-based games, these titles require precise timing for parries and dodges.

### Sub-5ms Input Lag
Input lag is the delay between pressing a button and seeing the action on screen. For players looking to maximize their Impact and Daze efficiency, sub-5ms lag is essential. A delay higher than this can cause you to miss the tight windows required for "Perfect Dodges," which are often the key to triggering powerful counter-attacks.

### Motion Clarity
When rotating the camera quickly in a 3D environment like Hesperia, slow pixel response times create a "smearing" effect. High refresh rates (144Hz and above) coupled with fast GtG (Gray-to-Gray) times reduce this blur. This allows you to maintain visual focus on Mint or other characters as they move rapidly across the battlefield.

### Eye Comfort
Flicker-free backlighting and low blue light modes are critical. During long sessions spent grinding for upgrade materials, a monitor with poor PWM (Pulse Width Modulation) dimming can cause significant eye fatigue and headaches. All our top picks feature TUV-certified eye comfort technologies.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/lighter-vs-koleda-stun-comparison-gear/inline-4.webp)


Not every player needs the latest 360Hz display. If you already own a 1440p 144Hz IPS monitor, the jump to 165Hz or 180Hz is visually negligible. In such cases, only a move to OLED technology will provide a truly transformative experience in terms of motion clarity and contrast.

You should also avoid upgrading to a 360Hz monitor like the Alienware AW2725DF unless your PC can consistently maintain frame rates above 300 FPS. In a demanding open-world game like Neverness to Everness, achieving these frame rates at 1440p may require a top-tier GPU. If your frame rate fluctuates wildly, the benefits of a high-refresh monitor are diminished.

Finally, skip these high-performance monitors if you primarily play turn-based JRPGs. In those titles, input latency is not a factor in gameplay success, and you would be better served by a 4K 60Hz monitor that prioritizes resolution and image density over raw speed.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does 1440p make a difference in gacha games?
Yes, especially for games with high-quality PC clients like Neverness to Everness. The increased pixel density over 1080p allows for much clearer text and finer detail on character models and environmental textures, which is essential for the "urban supernatural" aesthetic.

### Is OLED worth it for long gaming sessions?
OLED offers the best response times and contrast, but players should be aware of the risk of burn-in with static UI elements. Modern monitors like the <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=ASUS+PG27AQDM&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">ASUS PG27AQDM</a> include custom heatsinks and software features to mitigate this, making them viable for long-term use.

### Do I need G-Sync for Neverness to Everness?
G-Sync or FreeSync is highly recommended. Because open-world cities like Hesperia can be taxing on hardware, your frame rate may dip in crowded areas. Variable Refresh Rate (VRR) technology ensures the monitor stays synced with the GPU, preventing screen tearing without the lag of traditional V-Sync.

### What is the best refresh rate for character swapping?
A refresh rate of 144Hz or higher is ideal. This ensures that when you swap between characters like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) and [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide), the transition animation is fluid, allowing you to immediately identify your character's position and resume your attack rotation without visual disorientation.

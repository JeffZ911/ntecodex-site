## Overview

![Overview](/img/nte-f2p-friendly-character-builds/inline-1.webp)


Neverness to Everness (NTE) provides a robust roster of free characters including Esper Zero, Mint, Chiz, and Haniel. Unlike many contemporary urban fantasies, the "starter" characters in NTE remain viable well into the late game due to their unique elemental applications. F2P progression relies on mastering the Esper Cycle and elemental reactions like Blossom (Anima + Cosmos) and Hexed (Anima + Incantation). Understanding these interactions is the difference between struggling against basic street thugs and dominating high-level Hethereau Anomalies.

A successful F2P build focuses on the synergy between Arc types—Solid, Liquid, Plasma, and Gas—and specific character attributes. While premium banners offer powerful alternatives, the free units provided through the main story and the City Tycoon system are more than capable of handling current content. Character Pixels and Module upgrade materials are the primary bottlenecks for F2P players in the early game, making it vital to allocate resources toward a core team of three or four units rather than spreading investments too thin.

### The State of the F2P Meta in 2026

As of May 2026, the F2P meta is dominated by the "Blossom" reaction. By combining Anima and Cosmos elements, players can trigger a burst of area-of-effect (AoE) damage that scales with the character's Cycle Intensity. Esper Zero (the protagonist) serves as the primary Cosmos DPS, while Mint acts as the premier Anima applicator. Because Esper Zero receives free Awakening materials through story progression, they often outperform S-Rank pulls that lack duplicates for F2P players.

The City Tycoon system further supports non-spending players by gifting Chiz, a highly effective Cosmos sub-DPS, once you reach Level 18 in city management. This ensures every player has access to a dual-Cosmos or Blossom-focused composition without touching their gacha currency.

### Key Mechanics: Arcs and Cartridges Explained

Every character's power is defined by their Arc (weapon) and Cartridges (gear sets). Arcs are categorized by their physical state: Solid, Liquid, Plasma, and Gas. You cannot equip a Solid-type Arc on a character designed for Liquid-type Arcs. For F2P players, the goal is to find "farmable" Arcs—those obtained through side quests or world exploration—and refine them to Rank 5.

Cartridges, often referred to as Disks, provide the bulk of a character's statistics. These are equipped in sets of two or four. In the current patch, focusing on the "Lost Radiance" and "Fireflies and the Forest" sets is the most efficient path for progression, as these sets cater to the core free units.

## Best Weapons

![Best Weapons](/img/nte-f2p-friendly-character-builds/inline-2.webp)


The S-Rank Arc 'Day Off' is a top-tier F2P option for Solid-type characters like Esper Zero, obtainable via the 'Naked Ghost Tour' side quest. This weapon is unique because it provides a significant boost to CRIT DMG when the user hits an enemy affected by a Cosmos status. For F2P players, completing the 'Naked Ghost Tour' should be a priority as soon as the Hethereau map opens up.

A-Rank Arcs like 'Clear Skies' (Liquid) and 'Oraora!' (Plasma) serve as excellent placeholders for Mint and Nanally. While they lack the raw base stats of S-Rank weapons, their passive effects are often easier to trigger. For instance, 'Oraora!' increases Plasma damage by a flat percentage every time a character dodges, making it perfect for the high-mobility gameplay required in NTE.

Focus on Arcs with CRIT Rate or ATK% substats to ease the burden on Cartridge RNG. Refining A-Rank Arcs to Rank 5 often outperforms Rank 1 S-Rank Arcs for F2P players, as the passive multipliers at Rank 5 can bridge the gap in base attack power.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

![Best Disks (Artifacts)](/img/nte-f2p-friendly-character-builds/inline-3.webp)


Cartridges (Disks) are the primary gear system, featuring 2-piece Epic and 4-piece Legendary set bonuses. For F2P players, farming these becomes the primary endgame activity. The 'Lost Radiance' is the premier set for Cosmos DPS like Esper Zero, providing 25% DEF ignore after an Ultimate is used. This DEF ignore window is the most significant damage multiplier available for the protagonist.

'Fireflies and the Forest' is essential for Anima characters like Mint. This set stacks CRIT DMG up to 56% as the character applies Anima stacks to enemies. Since Mint’s kit is designed around rapid multi-hits, she can reach the maximum stack count in under four seconds. Support characters should prioritize 'Speedy Hedgehog' for Charge Efficiency and team-wide ATK buffs. This set ensures that your team's Support Skills are always ready when the Esper Cycle resets.

### Recommended Cartridge Sets for F2P Carries

Module specialization (Type I, II, III) provides hidden bonuses that are often overlooked by new players. Mint specifically gains 8% CRIT Rate per Type III module equipped if they are from the 'Fireflies and the Forest' set. This makes her remarkably easy to build for F2P players, as you can focus on CRIT DMG substats while the modules provide the necessary CRIT Rate.

**Cartridge Set Priority:**
1.  **DPS (Zero/Chiz):** 4-piece 'Lost Radiance' (Focus: CRIT Rate > ATK% > Cosmos DMG).
2.  **Sub-DPS (Mint):** 4-piece 'Fireflies and the Forest' (Focus: CRIT DMG > Cycle Intensity).
3.  **Support (Haniel/Adler):** 4-piece 'Speedy Hedgehog' (Focus: Charge Efficiency > HP% / DEF%).

### Module Substat Priority: CRIT vs. ATK vs. Cycle Intensity

For F2P players, the priority should always be attaining a 1:2 ratio of CRIT Rate to CRIT DMG. However, do not ignore Cycle Intensity. Cycle Intensity dictates how much damage your elemental reactions (Blossom, Hexed) do. If your Cycle Intensity is too low, your Blossom procs will feel weak, regardless of how high your ATK is. ## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/nte-f2p-friendly-character-builds/inline-5.webp)


Maximizing damage in NTE requires a strict adherence to the Esper Cycle. Start rotations with Haniel's Skill to apply the team-wide ATK buff before swapping to your main carry. This ensures that every subsequent hit benefits from the increased stats.

Mint's rotation focuses on 'Critical Riposte' (a perfect dodge counter) to reset her Skill cooldown for maximum Anima application. By timing your dodges correctly, Mint can stay on the field indefinitely, coating the battlefield in Anima. Once the enemy is fully debuffed, swap to Esper Zero. Esper Zero should use their Ultimate only after the enemy's Break bar is depleted to maximize the 'Lost Radiance' 25% DEF ignore window.

### Mint: The Skill-Reset Loop

To perform the Skill-Reset loop with Mint
1.  Enter the field and immediately use Mint's Elemental Skill.
2.  Wait for the enemy attack indicator and perform a Perfect Dodge.
3.  Trigger the 'Critical Riposte' (Normal Attack button after dodge).
4.  The Skill cooldown will reset; immediately use the Skill again.
5.  Repeat until the enemy's Anima stacks are maxed (usually 5 stacks).

### Esper Zero: AoE Burst and Break Management

Esper Zero excels at "Breaking" enemies. Use their heavy attacks to chip away at the white bar under the enemy's HP. Once that bar breaks, the enemy enters a vulnerable state. This is when you unleash Zero's Ultimate. During this period, the 'Lost Radiance' set bonus will ignore 25% of the enemy's DEF, resulting in massive damage numbers.

### Managing the Esper Meter for Support Skills

Utilize Support Skills (triggered at full Cycle Energy) to maintain elemental uptime. These skills do not require you to swap characters manually; instead, you press the character's portrait when the meter glows. For F2P teams, using Haniel’s Support Skill while Esper Zero is on the field is vital for maintaining the ATK buff without interrupting Zero’s combo string.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/nte-f2p-friendly-character-builds/inline-6.webp)


**Is Esper Zero (MC) worth building?**
Yes, they are a top-tier Cosmos DPS with free Awakening materials. Unlike other gacha games where the protagonist falls off, Esper Zero remains a staple of the meta due to their high Break efficiency and the availability of the 'Day Off' S-Rank weapon.

**How do I get Chiz?**
She is unlocked for free by reaching Level 18 in the City Tycoon system. This involves managing your Hethereau storefronts, completing deliveries, and increasing your city reputation. It takes approximately two weeks of casual play to reach this level.

**What is the best F2P weapon for Nanally?**
'Oraora!' is the best A-Rank Plasma option until you pull 'Ready-Ready' from her signature banner, "The Ichi-daime". 'Oraora!' provides a mobile playstyle that suits Nanally’s kit perfectly.

**Where can I farm S-Rank Cartridges?**
High-level Anomalies and the Hethereau Tower are the primary sources. Once you reach Account Level 35, the drop rate for Legendary (Gold) Cartridges increases significantly in the 'Anomalous Rift' farming stages.

**What are the best substats for F2P gear?**
Prioritize CRIT Rate and CRIT DMG. Because F2P players have fewer sources of these stats from weapon substats, getting them on your Cartridges is essential. After CRIT, look for Cycle Intensity to boost your Blossom and Hexed reaction damage.

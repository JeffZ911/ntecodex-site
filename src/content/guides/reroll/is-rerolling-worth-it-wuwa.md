# Is Rerolling Worth It in Wuthering Waves? 2026 Reroll Guide

Deciding whether to spend hours restarting your account in Wuthering Waves depends on your patience and your long-term goals. In the current 2026 meta, the gap between a standard start and an optimized reroll start has widened due to the introduction of high-tier supports and the natural powercreep of newer Resonators. This guide breaks down the efficiency, the targets, and the methods to determine if the time investment aligns with your playstyle.

## Why Reroll

![Why Reroll](/img/is-rerolling-worth-it-wuwa/inline-1.webp)


Rerolling is the process of creating multiple accounts to use the initial free "Convene" (gacha) pulls provided to new players. The goal is to secure a top-tier 5-star character immediately, allowing you to save your guaranteed selectors and future Astrite for upcoming banners.

### The Value of Early 5-Star Resonators
Securing a top-tier support like Verina or Shorekeeper early significantly eases endgame progression. Verina has maintained her status as a staple for almost two years because of her universal DMG amplification and off-field healing capabilities. Shorekeeper, often featured on the 2026 Beginner Limited banner, provides unmatched team-wide buffs that scale exceptionally well into the late game. By pulling one of these early, you free up your "Beginner's Choice Convene" to fill a specific elemental gap in your roster rather than using it on a basic necessity.

### Saving Resources for Limited Banners
Rerolling allows you to save Astrite and Radiant Tides for high-value 2026 limited banners like undisclosed or undisclosed. The initial influx of Lustrous Tides (standard pulls) is fixed. If you can "luck into" a 5-star within the first 10 to 20 pulls of the Novice Convene, you effectively save 30 to 40 pulls that would have otherwise been spent reaching the 50-pull pity. In a game where 160 Astrite equals one pull, this efficiency adds up to thousands of Astrite saved for limited-edition characters.

### Is it Necessary in 2026?
With the current powercreep in version 3.x, starting with a "perfect" roster helps bridge the gap for new players. While Wuthering Waves is skill-based and can be cleared with 4-star Resonators like Chixia or Yangyang, the comfort provided by a 5-star healer or a high-end DPS cannot be overstated. The Novice Convene (Utterance of Marvels) offers a 20% discount, making early pulls highly efficient for fresh accounts. For players looking to compete in the highest floors of the Tower of Adversity quickly, rerolling is highly recommended.

## How to Reroll

![How to Reroll](/img/is-rerolling-worth-it-wuwa/inline-2.webp)


The reroll process in Wuthering Waves is somewhat time-consuming because Kuro Games requires account registration and does not offer a "Reset Account" button. However, the path to unlocking the gacha system is linear and can be optimized.

### Account Creation Strategy
Use the "salted email" method (e.g., example+1@gmail.com) to create multiple Kuro Games accounts quickly. Most email providers treat addresses with a plus sign and a number as the same inbox, but the game server recognizes them as unique registrations. This allows you to manage all your verification codes from a single primary email address without needing to create dozens of new mailboxes.

### The Fastest Route to Union Level 8
To maximize your efficiency, follow this step-by-step checklist for a 40-minute reroll run:

1.  **Skip Dialogue:** Tap the screen rapidly during non-cutscene dialogue to speed up conversations.
2.  **First Resonance:** Complete the "First Resonance" main quest. This involves following the initial trail and defeating the first group of Tacet Discords.
3.  **Unlock Convene:** Reach the point in the story where you first arrive at Jinzhou and speak to the NPC who explains the Convene system. This typically occurs at Union Level 4.
4.  **Reach Union Level 8:** Do not stop at UL 4. Continue the main story until you hit Union Level 8. This unlocks the mail system, which is where the bulk of your pre-registration, anniversary, and compensation rewards are stored.
5.  **Claim Rewards:** Open the mail and claim all Lustrous Tides, Radiant Tides, and Astrite.
6.  **Pull:** Navigate to the Convene menu. Target the Novice Convene first, then use the Beginner's Choice Convene to guarantee your specific missing piece if you have enough Tides left.

### Managing Your Tides and Astrite
Always use your Lustrous Tides on the "Utterance of Marvels" (Novice) banner first. This banner guarantees a 5-star Resonator within 50 pulls and costs only 8 Tides for a 10-pull. Never spend Astrite on Lustrous Tides; Astrite should be strictly reserved for Radiant Tides (limited character banner) or Forging Tides (limited weapon banner).

## Best Starters

![Best Starters](/img/is-rerolling-worth-it-wuwa/inline-3.webp)


Knowing who to aim for is the most critical part of the reroll. In 2026, the value of standard Resonators has shifted as new mechanics have been introduced.

### S-Tier: The Must-Haves (Verina & Shorekeeper)
Verina remains the #1 standard pick due to her universal DMG amplification and healing. Her ability to provide a 15% All-Type DMG Deepen buff upon using her Outro Skill makes her viable in every single team composition. Shorekeeper is the priority for the 2026 Beginner Limited banner, offering unmatched team-wide buffs including Crit Rate and Crit Damage boosts. If you manage to pull Shorekeeper from her specific starter banner and Verina from the Novice banner, you have effectively "won" the reroll.

### A-Tier: Strong DPS Alternatives
Encore and Calcharo are solid DPS choices if you fail to pull a limited character like undisclosed. Encore provides consistent Fusion damage and is relatively easy to play. Calcharo, while having a higher skill ceiling due to his "Mercy" and "Death Messenger" mechanics, remains a top-tier Electro DPS when paired with the right supports.

### The 'Dream' Reroll: Snagging undisclosed
If you are extremely patient, the ultimate goal is to use your very limited Radiant Tides (usually 5-10 from the mail) to pull the current limited banner character, such as undisclosed, on the very first try. This has a base probability of 0.8% per pull. If you achieve this, you can then use your Beginner's Choice selector to get Verina, giving you the strongest possible start in the game.

## Time Estimate

![Time Estimate](/img/is-rerolling-worth-it-wuwa/inline-4.webp)


Rerolling in Wuthering Waves is a marathon, not a sprint. You should set aside a full afternoon if you are committed to getting a specific character.

### Average Run Duration
A standard reroll run to unlock the Convene menu (Union Level 4) takes approximately 35–45 minutes. However, most players will want to reach Union Level 8 to 10 to access the full suite of free rewards. Reaching Union Level 10 for maximum mail rewards can extend the run to 60 minutes.

### Probability and Luck Factors
The probability of hitting a 5-star on the limited banner with initial free pulls is roughly 0.8% per pull. Since you typically only get a handful of Radiant Tides at the start, the odds of getting a limited character are very low. Expect to spend 5–10 hours of total active time if you are aiming for a specific limited 5-star character. If you are only aiming for a specific standard character from the Novice banner, you can usually succeed within 2–3 runs (approximately 2 hours).

### Efficiency Tips: What to Skip
To maximize your speed, skip every optional chest and enemy encounter. Your only goal is the main quest "First Resonance." Do not explore or collect Echoes during a reroll run unless the quest requires it. PC is generally faster for menu navigation and combat than mobile, which can shave 5 minutes off every run.

**Time vs. Reward Comparison**

*   **UL 4 (35 mins):** Unlocks Convene. Minimum pulls (approx. 10-15). Best for "speed" rerolling.
*   **UL 8 (45 mins):** Unlocks Mail. Medium pulls (approx. 20-30). Best balance of time and reward.
*   **UL 10 (60 mins):** Unlocks Level Rewards. Maximum pulls (approx. 40+). Best for dedicated rerollers.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/is-rerolling-worth-it-wuwa/inline-5.webp)


### Common Rerolling Questions

**Can I get banned for rerolling?**
No, Kuro Games does not ban players for manually rerolling accounts. However, you must avoid using automated scripts or third-party software to play the game for you, as this violates the Terms of Service and will lead to an account ban.

**Is there a guest login?**
No, Wuthering Waves requires a registered account (Kuro Account, Google, Apple ID, etc.). This is why the "salted email" method is the most popular way to manage multiple attempts.

**Can I skip cutscenes?**
Only partially. While you can skip some minor dialogue and side-quest cinematics, most major story beats and cinematic action sequences remain unskippable. This is the primary reason why reroll runs take at least 35 minutes.

**Should I reroll on mobile or PC?**
PC is generally faster for menu navigation and allows for more precise movement during the tutorial combat. If you have the option, perform your rerolls on PC and then log into the successful account on your mobile device later.

### Account Security and Policies
Once you find an account you want to keep, make sure to bind it to a permanent email address and set up two-factor authentication if available. If you used a salted email (example+1@gmail.com), ensure you have full access to the primary email (example@gmail.com) so you can recover the account if needed. Kuro Games' policies are strict regarding account selling, so it is always safer to reroll your own account rather than purchasing one from an untrusted source.

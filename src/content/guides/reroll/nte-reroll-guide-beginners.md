# Neverness to Everness (NTE) Reroll Guide for Beginners

Rerolling is a common practice in gacha games where players restart their accounts multiple times to use the initial free summons to obtain the best possible characters. In Neverness to Everness, the process is particularly rewarding due to the high impact of S-rank supports and the generous "No 50/50" system on limited banners. By spending approximately 15 to 20 minutes per cycle, you can ensure your journey through the anomaly-filled streets of Hethereau begins with a meta-defining team.

## Why Reroll

![Why Reroll](/img/nte-reroll-guide-beginners/inline-1.webp)


The primary motivation for rerolling in NTE is to secure a "Double S-rank" start. The game provides a 50-pull standard selector banner, but players who reroll aim to pull a random S-rank character from the "Strange Encounters" banner using their initial free Fabricated Dice. This allows you to use the selector later to pick a complementary character, effectively starting the game with two high-tier units instead of one.

One of the most player-friendly aspects of NTE is the "No 50/50" system implemented on limited banners like "Misty Tipsy Style." In many other gacha titles, hitting an S-rank on a limited banner only gives you a 50% chance of getting the featured character. In NTE, if you trigger an S-rank on the limited banner, you are guaranteed the featured unit, such as [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte). This mechanic makes standard S-rank characters even more valuable; since you won't "accidentally" pull them while chasing limited units, obtaining them via rerolling or the selector is the most reliable way to diversify your elemental coverage and team roles.

For Free-to-Play (F2P) players, rerolling is a vital strategy for resource efficiency. By landing a top-tier support like Sakiri or a heavy hitter like Jiuyuan early, you can save your Annulith (premium currency) for upcoming high-value banners featuring characters like Lacrimosa or Chaos.

### Is Rerolling Necessary in NTE?

While not strictly mandatory to clear the main story, rerolling is highly recommended for players interested in endgame content or those who want to maximize their combat efficiency. The difference between an account starting with a B-tier character and one starting with Sakiri (the premier Incantation Buffer) is noticeable in both clear speeds and team flexibility. If you plan to play NTE long-term, the 20-minute investment per reroll is a small price for a significant power boost.

### The 50-Pull Standard Selector Explained

Every new account has access to a special beginner banner that allows you to select one specific S-rank character from the standard pool after 50 pulls. The optimal strategy is to **never** use this selector immediately. Instead, use your initial free Fabricated Dice on the "Strange Encounters" banner. If you get a random S-rank, you then use the selector to choose a character that synergizes with your pull. For example, if you pull Jiuyuan (a DPS), you should use your selector for Sakiri (a Buffer) to create a cohesive team.

## How to Reroll

![How to Reroll](/img/nte-reroll-guide-beginners/inline-2.webp)


The reroll process in NTE involves reaching the point in the story where the Scarborough Fair gacha system and the Mailbox are unlocked. This occurs shortly after arriving in the main city area of Hethereau.

### The Salted Email Method

Since NTE requires an account login, the "Salted Email" method is the most efficient way to create multiple accounts without needing dozens of unique email addresses. If your email is `username@gmail.com`, you can register using `username+1@gmail.com`, `username+2@gmail.com`, and so on. Most mail services recognize these as the same address, meaning all verification codes will go to your primary inbox, but the game treats them as entirely new accounts.

### Step-by-Step Reroll Checklist

1.  **Account Creation:** Sign up using a salted email and log into the game.
2.  **Tutorial Combat:** Complete the opening combat sequence. Focus on parrying and dodging to trigger slow-motion windows, which allows you to defeat enemies faster.
3.  **Bridge Crossings:** Follow the main quest markers through the "Bridge Crossings" objective. Skip all dialogue and cutscenes using the 'Skip' button in the top right corner where available.
4.  **Reach the City:** Once you enter the urban district of Hethereau, you will encounter the NPC named Mint.
5.  **Unlock Scarborough Fair:** Speaking with Mint and completing her initial dialogue unlocks the gacha menu (Scarborough Fair) and the Mailbox.
6.  **Claim Rewards:** Open your Mailbox and claim all pre-registration rewards, launch bonuses, and any active promo codes.
7.  **Perform Summons:** 
    *   Use your Fabricated Dice on the "Strange Encounters" (Standard) banner.
    *   Use any Solid Dice or Annulith on the "Misty Tipsy Style" (Hotori Limited) banner if you are targeting the current limited unit.
8.  **Evaluate:** If you didn't get an S-rank, log out and repeat from step 1.

### When to Stop: The 'God Account' Criteria

A "God Account" is defined as an account that hits an S-rank character within the first 10–20 pulls provided by the mail rewards. Ideally, you want to see Sakiri or Jiuyuan. If you manage to pull an S-rank and still have the 50-pull selector available, you have successfully "won" the reroll process. You can then comfortably progress through the game, knowing you can fill any role gap in your team later.

### S-Tier Targets: Sakiri & Jiuyuan

**Sakiri (Incantation Buffer):** Sakiri is widely considered the best long-term investment in the standard pool. As an Incantation Buffer, she enhances the skill damage of your entire team. In a game where ability rotations are key to high DPS, Sakiri’s value only grows as more powerful attackers are released. She is "future-proof" because her buffs scale with the stats of the characters she supports.

She utilizes the Anima element to deal massive burst damage. Her playstyle revolves around building up energy and unleashing a devastating ultimate that can clear entire waves of anomalies.

## Time Estimate

![Time Estimate](/img/nte-reroll-guide-beginners/inline-4.webp)


A standard reroll run in Neverness to Everness takes between 15 and 20 minutes. The variance depends on your familiarity with the map and your efficiency in the mandatory combat sections.

### Tutorial Speedrun Tips

To minimize your time per run, follow these optimization strategies:
*   **Combat Mechanics:** Use the dodge-counter mechanic. Successfully dodging an enemy attack at the last moment triggers a time-slow effect, allowing you to burn down enemy health bars much faster than standard attacking.
*   **Navigation:** Use the sprint function constantly. Hethereau is a large city, and while the "Bridge Crossings" objective is straightforward, walking instead of sprinting can add 3–4 minutes to your total time.
*   **Skip Dialogue:** NTE features a high volume of story dialogue. Aggressively using the skip button in the upper right corner is mandatory for efficient rerolling.

### Platform Differences: PC vs. Mobile

**PC:** The PC version is generally faster for rerolling due to shorter loading times and more precise controls during combat. Additionally, PC players can utilize "Multi-instance" managers if playing via an emulator, or simply open multiple windows of the native client (if their hardware allows) to run 2–3 reroll attempts simultaneously.

**Mobile:** Rerolling on mobile is slightly slower due to touch controls and potentially longer loading screens on older devices. However, the portability allows you to reroll while doing other tasks. Note that the "Salted Email" method works identically on both platforms.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/nte-reroll-guide-beginners/inline-5.webp)


### Can I get banned for rerolling?
Account bans for rerolling are highly unlikely in Neverness to Everness. Hotta Studio, like most modern gacha developers, generally tolerates rerolling as it inflates early player engagement numbers. As long as you are not using automated scripts or third-party software to "bot" the process, your account will remain safe.

### How do I transfer my account to PS5?
NTE supports full cross-save functionality. Once you have secured a reroll account you are happy with on PC or Mobile, go to the account settings and link it to a official Hotta/Level Infinite ID. When you launch the game on PS5, log in with those same credentials. Note that you should avoid starting your reroll on PS5, as PSN accounts are difficult to unbind and "salt."

### Should I pull for Arcs (Weapons) while rerolling?
In the early game, characters are significantly more important than Arcs. Arcs provide stat boosts and passive effects, but a top-tier character with a 4-star Arc will always outperform a mediocre character with a 5-star Arc. Only consider pulling for Arcs once you have a solid team of at least two S-rank characters.

### Should I use my Annulith for Fabricated Dice?
No. Annulith should be strictly reserved for Solid Dice (Limited Banners). Fabricated Dice are given out frequently through in-game events, progression rewards, and the monthly shop reset. Using premium currency on the standard banner is generally considered a poor use of resources for F2P and low-spending players.

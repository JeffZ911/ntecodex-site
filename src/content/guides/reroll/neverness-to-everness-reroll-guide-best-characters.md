### The Salted Email Method Explained

Since Neverness to Everness requires an account login and does not have a simple "Delete Account" button that bypasses the 30-day waiting period, the "Salted Email" method is the most effective workaround. This works primarily with Gmail accounts.

If your email is `username@gmail.com`, the game's registration system will recognize `username+1@gmail.com`, `username+2@gmail.com`, and so on, as unique accounts. However, all verification codes sent to these "salted" addresses will be delivered to your primary `username@gmail.com` inbox. This allows you to create dozens of accounts without needing dozens of actual email addresses.

### S-Tier: The Must-Haves

**Hotori (S-Rank, Cosmos)**
Hotori is currently the most sought-after character in Neverness to Everness. Her kit revolves around "Cosmic Displacement," allowing her to slow down or completely stop the movement of enemies within a specific radius. This is invaluable in the "Anomaly" challenges where bosses have fast, high-damage attack patterns. By stopping time, you can execute full combos without the risk of being interrupted.

**Sakiri (S-Rank, Incantation)**
While Hotori handles the damage, Sakiri handles the battlefield. Her Incantation abilities allow her to pull enemies together, making her the perfect partner for any AoE (Area of Effect) damage dealer. Her passive ATK buffs scale with her total Energy, making her a future-proof investment as you acquire better gear.

### A-Tier: Strong Alternatives

**Jiuyuan (S-Rank, Anima)**
Jiuyuan is a versatile pick for players who want a "comfy" start. She deals significant Anima damage in short bursts, which is great for the hit-and-run tactics often required in the early game. Once you reach her second Ascension, she gains the ability to heal the party based on a percentage of her burst damage, effectively consolidating two roles into one slot.

**Daffodill (S-Rank, Chaos)**
Daffodill is a specialized unit. The Chaos element is currently rare, meaning very few enemies have resistance to her attacks. While she may not have the raw utility of Sakiri, her sub-DPS capabilities allow her to deal damage even when she is not the active character on the field.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/neverness-to-everness-reroll-guide-best-characters/inline-5.webp)


### Is rerolling bannable in NTE?
No. Hotta Studio has historically allowed rerolling in their titles. As long as you are not using automated scripts or third-party hacks to bypass the tutorial, creating multiple accounts is within the Terms of Service.

### Does NTE have a 50/50 system?
No. One of the most player-friendly aspects of Neverness to Everness is the removal of the 50/50 mechanic. When you trigger an S-Rank on a limited banner, it is 100% guaranteed to be the featured character. This makes rerolling for the limited character much more rewarding than in other gacha games.

### How many pulls do I get at the start?
Depending on active events and pre-registration milestones, players typically receive between 20 and 30 pulls upon reaching Cronos City. This is usually split between Solid Dice (Limited) and Fabricated Dice (Standard).

### Can I change my name later?
Yes, you can change your name in the profile settings. However, the first change is usually free, while subsequent changes may require a "Name Change Card" or premium currency. It is recommended to use a generic name during rerolling to save your free change for your final account.

### Which banner should I pull on first?
Always prioritize the **Limited Board** with your Solid Dice. The characters on the limited banner are generally more powerful and will not be added to the standard pool for several months. Use your Fabricated Dice on the **Standard Board** only after you have exhausted your limited pulls.

### What are Arcs?
Arcs are the "weapons" or equipment cards in Neverness to Everness. While rerolling for an S-Rank Resonator is the priority, pulling a 5-star Arc that matches your character's element is a massive bonus. However, do not sacrifice a top-tier character just to get a good Arc.

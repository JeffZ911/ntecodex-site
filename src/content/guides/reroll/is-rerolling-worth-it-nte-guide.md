Since the global launch of Neverness to Everness (NTE) on April 29, 2026, the community has debated whether the initial time investment of rerolling outweighs the benefits. ## Why Reroll

![Why Reroll](/img/is-rerolling-worth-it-nte-guide/inline-1.webp)


Rerolling in NTE is more impactful than in many other contemporary gacha titles due to the specific structure of its summoning systems. Unlike games that utilize a 50/50 mechanic—where you only have a 50% chance to get the featured character upon hitting the highest rarity—NTE utilizes a "No 50/50" system. This means that when you trigger an S-Rank pull on a limited banner, you are guaranteed to receive the featured unit. 

### The 'No 50/50' Advantage

The absence of the 50/50 gamble makes every limited pull significantly more valuable. If you reroll until you see an S-Rank glow on the "Misty Tipsy Style" banner, you are 100% certain to walk away with Hotori. This eliminates the frustration of "losing" a reroll to a standard pool character. For F2P players, this is the most efficient way to use the 80 to 90 free pulls provided through launch rewards and pre-registration milestones. By securing the limited character early, you can save your future Annulith for upcoming Arcs or new character releases.

### Standard vs. Limited Banner Priority

The "Scarborough Fair" board is the permanent standard banner in NTE. It features a unique mechanic: an S-Rank selector becomes available after completing 50 pulls. Efficient rerolling involves a two-step process. First, you use your Solid Dice on the limited banner to try for Hotori. Second, you use your Fabricated Dice on the Scarborough Fair board. 

If you manage to pull a top-tier S-Rank like Jiuyuan or Daffodil from the standard board before hitting the 50-pull mark, you can then use your selector to pick a character that perfectly complements them. This "Double S-Rank" start is the primary goal for most hardcore players.

## How to Reroll

![How to Reroll](/img/is-rerolling-worth-it-nte-guide/inline-2.webp)


The process for rerolling in Neverness to Everness is relatively streamlined, though it requires a bit of patience during the initial narrative sequences. Hotta Studio has included an account deletion feature that simplifies the process for mobile and PC users, removing the need for hundreds of "salted" email addresses.

### Step-by-Step Reroll Route

1.  **Launch and Login:** Start the game on PC or Mobile and log in using a Guest Account. If you are on PC, using a throwaway email is also an option, but Guest is faster for the initial reset.
2.  **Complete the Prologue:** Follow the main story quest titled "A New Destination." You will need to play through the introductory dialogue and basic movement tutorials.
3.  **Bridge Crossing Combat:** You will eventually reach the "Bridge Crossing" combat tutorial. This is the first major scripted battle. Focus on using your character's abilities as soon as they off-cooldown to end the fight quickly.
4.  **Reach the Eibon Antique Shop:** The gacha system, known as the "Board" in NTE, unlocks once you arrive at the Eibon Antique Shop and complete the initial conversation with the shopkeeper. This typically takes 15 to 20 minutes.
5.  **Claim Rewards:** Open your in-game mail and the "Events" tab. Claim all pre-registration rewards, launch bonuses, and any active NTE codes. You should have enough Fabricated Dice and Solid Dice for several multi-pulls.
6.  **Execute the Pulls:** Spend your Solid Dice on the "Misty Tipsy Style" banner first. If you get Hotori, move to the "Scarborough Fair" board and use your Fabricated Dice.
7.  **The Reset:** If your pulls do not result in an S-Rank, go to **Settings > Account > Delete Account**. Confirm the deletion to clear your data. This allows you to restart the prologue immediately using the same device/client without needing to redownload the game assets.

### Fastest Tutorial Skip Tips

While many cutscenes in NTE are skippable, some narrative moments are mandatory. To maximize speed, always keep your mouse or finger near the top-right corner where the "Skip" button appears. During mandatory dialogue choices, spamming the spacebar (PC) or tapping the center of the screen (Mobile) will advance the text at the maximum possible rate. Avoid exploring side alleys or interacting with non-essential NPCs in Hesperia until you have finalized the account you intend to keep.

### How to Use the Scarborough Fair Board

The Scarborough Fair board is discounted for the first 50 pulls. It is vital that you do not spend Annulith (the premium currency) here. Only use the Fabricated Dice provided by the game. The goal is to reach the 50-pull milestone to trigger the S-Rank selector. If you happen to pull an S-Rank at pull 20 or 30, the counter does not reset; you still get your selector at 50, making it a "guaranteed" way to round out your team.

## Time Estimate

![Time Estimate](/img/is-rerolling-worth-it-nte-guide/inline-4.webp)


Rerolling is a marathon, not a sprint. Depending on your luck and your hardware, the time investment can vary wildly.

*   **Single Run:** 15–20 minutes.
*   **Average Success (1 S-Rank):** 45–60 minutes (3-4 runs).
*   **God Start (Limited + Standard S-Rank):** 2–5 hours.

### Efficiency Tips for Mobile vs. PC

PC players have a distinct advantage due to the ability to use emulators with multi-instance synchronization. By running four instances of NTE simultaneously, you can perform four reroll runs in the same 20-minute window, bringing your "effective" time per account down to roughly 5 minutes. 

Mobile players are limited by loading speeds and manual input. If you are rerolling on mobile, ensure you are on a stable Wi-Fi connection to minimize the transition times between Hesperia's different urban districts. The "Bridge Crossing" segment can be particularly taxing on older mobile devices, potentially adding 5 minutes to each run.

### When to Stop Rerolling

You should stop rerolling the moment you secure **Hotori** and at least one other S-Rank from the **Scarborough Fair** board (either through luck or the 50-pull selector). In NTE, character synergy is more important than having a full roster of S-Ranks. A team consisting of Hotori, a strong Breaker, and a dedicated Support will outperform an uncoordinated team of four S-Rank DPS units. 

If you have spent more than 4 hours without a "God Start," it is usually better to settle for a "Great Start" (Hotori + Selector) and begin playing. The progress you make in those 4 hours of gameplay—leveling up your Urban Level and unlocking daily commissions—will often provide more long-term value than continuing to chase a perfect 3-S-Rank account.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/is-rerolling-worth-it-nte-guide/inline-5.webp)


**Is rerolling bannable in NTE?**
No. Hotta Studio has traditionally been lenient with rerolling in their titles. The inclusion of an "Account Deletion" button within the settings menu is a clear indication that the developers acknowledge and allow the practice.

**Should I pull for Arcs (weapons) while rerolling?**
Generally, no. In the early game, characters provide a much larger power spike than Arcs. Most high-tier Arcs can be earned through late-game anomaly challenges or purchased with in-game currency over time. Focus your initial Solid Dice and Annulith on expanding your Esper roster.

**Does pity carry over if I delete my account?**
No. Deleting your account wipes all data from the server, including your pull history and pity counters. Only delete your account if you are fully committed to starting the process over from scratch.

**What is the best element to start with?**
Cosmos and Chaos are the most versatile elements at launch. Cosmos (Hotori) is effective against almost all enemy types, while Chaos (Daffodil) is essential for breaking the shields of high-level bosses. Starting with a mix of these two will ensure you don't hit an "elemental wall" in the early story chapters.

**Can I reroll on PS5?**
Rerolling on PlayStation 5 is significantly more difficult because the game is linked directly to your PlayStation Network (PSN) ID. To reroll on console, you would need to create multiple PSN accounts. It is highly recommended to reroll on a PC or Mobile device first, and then link that successful account to your PSN ID once you are satisfied with your starters.

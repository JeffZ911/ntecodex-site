# Neverness to Everness Reroll Guide: The Fastest Way to Get S-Rank Characters

Rerolling in Neverness to Everness is a common strategy for players who want to maximize their initial resources. Since the game features an urban open-world environment where character mobility and elemental synergy significantly impact your progression, starting with a high-tier DPS or a versatile support can change your entire experience. This guide covers the current fastest methods, character rankings, and technical tips to optimize your account-starting process.

## Why Reroll

![Why Reroll](/img/how-to-reroll-in-nte-fast/inline-1.webp)


Rerolling is particularly lucrative in NTE compared to other modern gacha games because of the "No 50/50" guarantee system. In many competing titles, when you pull an S-Rank on a limited banner, you only have a 50% chance of it being the featured character. NTE removes this frustration on its limited banners like "The Ichi-daime," ensuring that your first S-Rank pull is the featured character. This makes landing a character like Nanally much more predictable if you start with enough Solid Dice.

Furthermore, the game incentivizes early pulls through the "Strange Encounters" board. This is the beginner-focused Standard Banner that offers a permanent 20% discount. You can perform 50 pulls for the price of only 40 Fabricated Dice. Once you complete these 50 pulls, the game unlocks an S-Rank Selector, allowing you to choose a specific standard character to round out your team.

For Free-to-Play (F2P) players, rerolling is the most effective way to secure a Limited S-Rank (like Nanally or [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte)) while simultaneously working toward the Standard S-Rank Selector. This dual-threat approach ensures you have a top-tier DPS and a compatible support right from the start of your journey through Hethereau’s anomaly-filled streets.

### The Scarborough Fair Gacha System Explained

The gacha system in NTE is stylized as "Scarborough Fair," a board game-inspired interface. Instead of a simple animation, you watch a "Chuppa"—your digital game piece—move across tiles. Each tile represents a reward, ranging from upgrade materials to characters and weapons. 

There are two primary currencies used on these boards:
*   **Fabricated Dice:** Used for the Standard Board ("Strange Encounters").
*   **Solid Dice:** Used for Limited-Time Event Boards (such as "The Ichi-daime").

The pity thresholds are generous for the genre. The Standard Board guarantees an S-Rank within 80 pulls, but the initial 50-pull discount is where most rerollers find their success.

### Is Rerolling Worth It in NTE?

Whether rerolling is "worth it" depends on your patience. A single run takes approximately 17 minutes. Given the "No 50/50" rule, the value of a single S-Rank is higher here than in other games. If you plan to play NTE competitively or want to clear high-level anomalies without hitting a wall, spending an hour or two rerolling is highly recommended.

## How to Reroll

![How to Reroll](/img/how-to-reroll-in-nte-fast/inline-2.webp)


The goal of a fast reroll is to reach the "Bridge Crossings" objective as quickly as possible. This is the point in the narrative where the Mailbox and the Scarborough Fair menu unlock.

### Step-by-Step Fast Reroll Flow

1.  **Launch and Login:** Open the NTE client. Use the "Salting" method for your email (e.g., yourname+1@gmail.com) to create multiple accounts under one primary email address.
2.  **The Prologue:** Start the game and move through the initial narrative. Use the fast-forward button located in the top right corner of the screen to skip non-essential dialogue.
3.  **Combat Tutorials:** You will enter several scripted fights. To clear these faster, focus on the parry mechanic. Successful parries significantly reduce the "Stability" bar of enemies, allowing for instant finishers.
4.  **Reach Bridge Crossings:** Follow the main quest marker until you reach the "Bridge Crossings" objective. You will meet Mint here. Speaking with her unlocks the full main menu.
5.  **Claim Rewards:** Open your Mailbox to claim pre-registration rewards, which typically include at least 20 Fabricated Dice and 10 Solid Dice.
6.  **Redeem Codes:** Open the Settings menu, go to "Account," and enter the active May 2026 codes: **NTE0429** and **NTENANALLYGO**. These provide extra Annulith (premium currency).
7.  **The Pulls:** Spend your Fabricated Dice on the "Strange Encounters" board first. If you get an S-Rank early, move to the Limited Board to try for Nanally.
8.  **Reset if Needed:** If you do not get your desired characters, log out, go to the User Center, and repeat the process with a new salted email (e.g., yourname+2@gmail.com).

### The Salting Method for Unlimited Accounts

Salting is the most efficient way to manage multiple NTE accounts without creating dozens of unique email addresses. Most email providers (like Gmail or Outlook) ignore anything after a "plus" sign. If your email is `player@gmail.com`, the game sees `player+1@gmail.com` and `player+2@gmail.com` as entirely different accounts, but all verification emails are sent to your main inbox. This allows you to bypass the 7-day account deletion cooldown and keep your best rolls.

### Skipping the Tutorial Efficiently

To shave minutes off your time, avoid exploring the side alleys of Hethereau during the prologue. Stick strictly to the golden quest markers. During the driving segments, ignore the traffic laws; the faster you reach the destination, the sooner you unlock the gacha. Note that while most dialogue is skippable, certain "Anomaly" cutscenes are unskippable for engine loading purposes. Use these brief breaks to manage your next salted email address on a mobile device or second monitor.

### Top Target: Nanally (Limited)

Nanally is the current "must-have" for any serious starter account. As an Anima-element DPS, she uses her hammer and skateboard-inspired combat style to deal massive area-of-effect (AoE) damage. Her most significant advantage is her exploration utility; her kit allows for unique wall-running and vertical movement that makes navigating the skyscrapers of Hethereau significantly faster. If you pull Nanally, your reroll session is effectively over.

### Best Standard Banner Picks

If you are using your Fabricated Dice on the "Strange Encounters" board, Sakiri and Jiuyuan are the prizes. Sakiri is an Incantation-element buffer who provides "Resonance" stacks to your team, increasing the damage of your main DPS. Jiuyuan, on the other hand, is a pure Anima Burst DPS. Sakiri’s Incantation buffs perfectly complement Nanally’s high-frequency attacks, creating a powerful "Hyper-carry" team right at level 1.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/how-to-reroll-in-nte-fast/inline-5.webp)


**Can I reroll on PS5?**
Rerolling directly on a PlayStation 5 is difficult because the game links to your PSN ID immediately. The most efficient way for console players is to reroll on a PC or mobile device using a third-party login (like a salted email), then link that specific account to your PSN ID once you are satisfied with the results.

**Will I get banned for rerolling?**
As of May 2026, there have been no reports of players being banned for manual rerolling or using the salting method. However, using automated scripts or macros to play the tutorial for you is against the Terms of Service and can result in a permanent ban.

**When does the S-Rank Selector expire?**
The S-Rank Selector from the "Strange Encounters" board does not have an expiration date. You can keep it in your inventory for as long as you like. Many players hold onto it until they see which S-Rank they pull from a future 50/50 loss or a standard pity.

**What is the "Chuppa" in the gacha?**
The Chuppa is the mascot-like game piece that represents the player on the Scarborough Fair board. Its movement is purely cosmetic and does not affect the mathematical odds of your pulls, which are determined the moment you click the "Roll" button.

**Do pity counts carry over?**
Yes. Pity counts on limited banners carry over to the next limited banner. If you do 40 pulls for Nanally and don't get an S-Rank, those 40 pulls will count toward the 80-pull pity on the next featured character's board.

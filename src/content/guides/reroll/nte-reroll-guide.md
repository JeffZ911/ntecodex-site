## Why Reroll

Rerolling is the practice of resetting your account progress early on to use your initial free summons to pull for the highest-tier characters. In Neverness to Everness, this is particularly valuable due to the power gap between standard A-Rank units and the specialized S-Rank Espers available at launch.

Securing a top-tier DPS like Nanally or Hathor early significantly accelerates PvE progression and "Oddity" hunting. Oddities are supernatural anomalies scattered throughout the city that often require high damage output or specific elemental interactions to solve or defeat. Starting with a powerhouse character means you won't hit a progression wall in the first week of play.

NTE features a "No 50/50" system on limited banners, making a successful reroll for the featured character (like Nanally) extremely high value. Unlike other gacha games where you only have a 50% chance to get the featured unit on your first S-Rank pull, NTE guarantees the featured character. This makes every "Solid Dice" (limited pull currency) you earn during the reroll process significantly more impactful.

The standard banner offers a 20% discount for the first 50 pulls, allowing you to trigger the S-Rank selector faster. By rerolling, you can reach this 50-pull milestone within your first hour of gameplay, ensuring you start with at least two S-Rank units: one from your reroll luck and one from the selector.

Saving Annulith (premium currency) by rerolling for your first S-Rank allows you to stockpile for upcoming banners like Hotori. If you pull your target early without spending your saved Annulith, you are in a much stronger position for the version 1.1 and 1.2 updates.

Finally, early game synergy is vital. Starting with a specific element, such as Anima or Incantation, allows you to build around free characters like Mint and Adler immediately. ### The Advantage of the No 50/50 System

The most compelling reason to reroll in Neverness to Everness is the player-friendly banner logic. ## How to Reroll

The reroll process in Neverness to Everness is straightforward but requires a bit of patience during the initial tutorial sequence. To maximize your efficiency, you should use a PC if possible, as the loading times and menu navigation are significantly faster than on mobile devices.

### The Salted Email Method Explained

NTE allows for account registration via email. To avoid creating dozens of actual email addresses, use the "salted email" trick. If your email is `hero@gmail.com`, the game recognizes `hero+1@gmail.com`, `hero+2@gmail.com`, and so on, as unique accounts. However, all verification codes and communications will still be sent to your primary `hero@gmail.com` inbox. This allows you to cycle through accounts rapidly without leaving your browser.

### Step-by-Step Reroll Walkthrough

Follow this sequence to complete a single reroll cycle in approximately 15 minutes:

1.  **Launch and Login:** Open the game and create a new account using a salted email.
2.  **Tutorial Combat:** Complete the opening combat sequence. Focus on using Mint’s skills to clear the initial "Anomalies" quickly.
3.  **Bridge Crossings:** Follow the quest marker to the "Bridge Crossings" objective. This is the main story gate where you meet Mint and Adler in the city proper.
4.  **Dialogue Skips:** Use the fast-forward button located in the top right of the screen for all non-essential dialogue.
5.  **Unlock the Fair:** Continue the story until the "Fair" (the gacha board game menu) is unlocked. This usually happens after the first major interaction at the headquarters.
6.  **Claim Rewards:** Open your Mailbox to claim all pre-registration rewards. This includes Fabricated Dice for the standard banner and Annulith which can be converted to Solid Dice.
7.  **Redeem Codes:** Go to the settings menu and enter active launch codes to boost your pull count.
8.  **The Pulls:** Spend your Fabricated Dice on the discounted Standard Banner first. If you have enough Annulith, spend it on the Limited Banner for Nanally.
9.  **Reset:** If you didn't get an S-Rank, log out and repeat from step 1 with a new salted email.

### How to Skip the Tutorial Efficiently

To save roughly 5-7 minutes per run, you must be aggressive with the skip button. While some cinematic cutscenes are mandatory, most "talking head" dialogue segments can be bypassed. Additionally, ignore side NPCs and optional Oddity triggers during the tutorial walk. Stick strictly to the yellow quest markers. During combat, don't worry about perfect dodges; just spam your abilities to clear waves as fast as possible.

## Best Starters

Knowing who to aim for is just as important as the reroll itself. The current meta favors high-mobility DPS units and characters who can "Break" enemy defenses quickly.

### S+ Tier: The 'God Start' Targets

**Nanally (S-Rank, Anima DPS)**
Nanally is the gold standard for rerolling. As the first limited character, her power level is slightly higher than the standard pool units. Her primary advantage is her mobility; she can climb vertical surfaces and walls with her Anima-infused parkour skills, making urban exploration in Hethereau much smoother. In combat, she deals massive burst damage and can execute enemies with low health instantly.

**Hathor (S-Rank, Lakshana DPS)**
Hathor is the best target if you are pulling on the standard banner. She excels at dealing "Break" damage, which is essential for staggering bosses. Her "Blade Wing" attacks provide a wide AOE (Area of Effect), allowing her to clear groups of enemies in the city streets with ease. She is a long-term investment that remains relevant even in the late-game "Void Zones."

### S Tier: Strong Standard Alternatives

**Sakiri (S-Rank, Incantation Support)**
Sakiri is a top-tier buffer who provides team-wide ATK boosts. While she doesn't deal the same raw damage as Nanally, she makes every other character on your team significantly better. Her crowd control abilities can group enemies together, setting up perfect combos for your sub-DPS units.

**Jiuyuan (S-Rank, Anima DPS/Control)**
Jiuyuan focuses on controlling the battlefield. She can create Anima vortexes that pull enemies in. She has excellent synergy with the free character Mint, as they share the Anima element, triggering the "Resonant Wind" buff which increases movement speed and skill cooldown reduction.

### Characters to Avoid (Free Units)

Do not waste time rerolling for **Chiz** or **Aurelia**. While they are S-Rank units, they are obtainable through other means. Chiz is rewarded for reaching specific City Tycoon levels (the game’s business management sim), and Aurelia is part of the 7-day login rewards for the launch event. Getting duplicates is fine for their "Eidolons" (constellations), but they shouldn't be your primary reroll target.

## Time Estimate

Rerolling is a game of statistics. Depending on your luck, you might finish in one try or twenty. However, understanding the time investment helps you decide when to settle for a "good enough" account.

### Average Duration per Cycle

A standard reroll run takes approximately 15 to 20 minutes. This includes the time to create the account, run through the tutorial combat, and reach the point where the Fair becomes available. If you are extremely efficient with skips and have a fast internet connection for loading, you can push this down to 12 minutes.

### Total Pulls per Reroll Run

Each run yields roughly 30-40 pulls on the standard banner (due to the 20% discount and launch rewards) or ~20 pulls on the limited banner if you convert all your Annulith. 

Reaching the S-Rank selector requires 50 pulls on the standard banner. This can usually be achieved within 45 minutes of active play on a single account by completing the first few "Oddity" investigations and main story quests. If you get a lucky early S-Rank in your first 20 pulls, it is often better to keep that account and push for the 50-pull selector rather than starting over.

Expect to spend 2-3 hours if you are strictly aiming for a specific limited character like Nanally without spending premium currency. Given the "No 50/50" rule, your odds of success per hour are much higher in NTE than in other modern gacha games.

**Time vs. Pulls Efficiency:**
*   **15 Minutes:** ~25 Pulls (Standard) + 10 Pulls (Limited). Best for "quick fire" rerolling.
*   **45 Minutes:** 50 Pulls (Standard) + Selector. Best for players who want a guaranteed S-Rank and are willing to play a bit longer.

## FAQ

**Does NTE have a 50/50?**
No, limited banners in Neverness to Everness do not have a 50/50 mechanic. If you trigger an S-Rank pull on a featured banner, you are 100% guaranteed to get the featured character.

**Can I delete my account to reuse an email?**
Yes, you can request account deletion in the settings, but there is a 7-day processing period before the email becomes available again. Using the salted email method (`email+1@gmail.com`) is much faster and highly recommended.

**Does pity carry over?**
Yes, pity on limited character banners carries over to the next featured banner. For example, if you do 40 pulls on Nanally’s banner and don’t get an S-Rank, those 40 pulls will count toward the 90-pull pity for Hotori’s upcoming banner.

**Which banner should I use my Annulith on?**
Only use Annulith for Solid Dice to pull on Limited Banners. Never spend your premium Annulith on the Standard Banner, as the game provides plenty of free Fabricated Dice through progression and events to eventually hit your selector.

**Is rerolling necessary?**
Rerolling is not strictly necessary to enjoy the game. Neverness to Everness is quite generous, providing free S-Ranks like Chiz and Aurelia, plus the 50-pull selector. However, rerolling optimizes your "endgame" readiness and ensures you have a top-tier elemental carry like Nanally from day one.

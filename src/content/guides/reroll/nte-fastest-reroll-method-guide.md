### Step-by-Step Salting Instructions
Salting allows you to create dozens of game accounts using a single Gmail address. This keeps your login credentials organized and prevents you from needing to manage multiple email passwords.

1.  Go to the NTE registration page or the in-game login screen.
2.  Enter your email with a "+1" suffix (e.g., username+1@gmail.com).
3.  The game treats this as a unique account, but all verification codes and emails will be sent to your primary username@gmail.com inbox.
4.  Repeat the process with +2, +3, and so on, until you get the desired pull.

### Can I reroll on PlayStation 5?
Direct rerolling on PS5 is difficult because the game automatically links your PSN ID to a Hotta account upon startup. To reroll, create salted accounts on a PC or mobile device. Once you pull the character you want, go to the account settings on your PC/mobile, link a 3rd party login or a secondary email, and then use the "Link Account" feature on your PS5 to sign in with those credentials.

### Is salting bannable?
As of May 2026, there have been no reports of players being banned for using the Gmail salting method (+1, +2). This is a common practice in gacha games. However, avoid using automated "macro" scripts to play the game for you, as anti-cheat software may flag the account for "abnormal gameplay behavior."

### What happens to pre-registration rewards?
Pre-registration rewards, including the Fabricated Dice and the limited-edition vehicle skin, are tied to the specific account you create. They are sent to the in-game mailbox once you unlock the Eibon Antique Shop. If you switch to a new salted account, you will receive the rewards again on that new account.

### When does the S-Rank Selector expire?
The "Strange Encounters" beginner banner is permanent for new players. It does not expire until you have completed the 50 pulls. Once the 50th pull is made and the selector is used, the banner disappears from your Order menu.

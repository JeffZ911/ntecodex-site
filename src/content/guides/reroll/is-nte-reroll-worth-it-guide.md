Rerolling in Neverness to Everness is primarily about securing a competitive advantage without spending real-world currency. Securing a top-tier S-Rank Esper like [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte) or [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) early provides a massive power spike for Hethereau exploration. Hotori, in particular, dominates the current meta due to her ability to manipulate field time, making difficult combat encounters significantly more manageable. By obtaining these units through rerolling, you ensure that your initial team composition has the damage output and utility required to clear early content at a much faster pace.

The gacha system, known as "The Fair," is relatively generous compared to other urban open-world RPGs, but it still relies on luck. Starting with two S-Ranks—one from your successful reroll and one from the 50-pull selector board—optimizes long-term F2P resources. This strategy allows you to hold onto your Annulith for upcoming Version 1.1 characters like Lacrimosa or Chaos, rather than spending it all just to build a functional baseline team. High-tier supports like [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide) or burst DPS like Jiuyuan significantly speed up early-game boss clears and progress through the main story chapters.

## How to Reroll

![How to Reroll](/img/is-nte-reroll-worth-it-guide/inline-2.webp)


The reroll process in Neverness to Everness is straightforward but requires some patience due to the tutorial length. To begin, you will need a Hotta account or a third-party login that allows for easy iteration.

Use the "Email Salting" method to create multiple accounts without needing new email addresses. This is the most efficient way to manage your attempts. For example, if your primary email is `example@gmail.com`, you can register accounts using `example+1@gmail.com`, `example+2@gmail.com`, and so on. Most systems recognize these as unique accounts, but all verification emails will be sent to your primary inbox.

### The Email Salting Method Explained

Email salting works because of how mail servers handle the "+" symbol. By adding a plus sign and any string of characters after your username, you create an alias. Neverness to Everness treats `user+test1@gmail.com` and `user+test2@gmail.com` as two completely different players. This allows you to keep all your reroll attempts organized under one master email, making it much easier to recover the "winning" account later.

### Unlocking the Gacha: Episode 0 Walkthrough

The tutorial, "Episode 0: Unforeseen Yet Foretold," serves as an introduction to both combat and the urban exploration mechanics. You will play as the protagonist and meet Chiz early on. To speed this up, use the "Sprint" mechanic constantly and skip dialogue where the game permits. You cannot skip the major cinematic sequences, but you can fast-forward through the standard NPC interactions. The gacha menu, "The Fair," only becomes available after you have completed the initial investigation at the Eibon Antique Shop. Do not stop before this point, or you will not have access to your pre-registration pulls.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/is-nte-reroll-worth-it-guide/inline-5.webp)


### Can I get banned for rerolling?

It is highly unlikely that you will be banned for manual rerolling using the email salting method. Developers generally tolerate rerolling as it inflates launch-day player numbers. However, you should avoid using automated scripts or third-party software that plays the game for you, as these trigger anti-cheat systems and can lead to a permanent account ban.

### Which banner should I pull on first?

Always prioritize the Limited Banner (currently "Misty Tipsy Style" featuring Hotori). The resources for the Standard Board are easier to come by through gameplay rewards and leveling up. Using your precious Annulith on the Standard Board is generally considered a waste of resources for F2P players.

### Does pity carry over?

Yes, the pity on the limited character banner carries over to the next limited banner. If you perform 40 pulls on Hotori’s banner and don't get an S-Rank, those 40 pulls will count toward the 80-pull pity for the next character in Version 1.1. This makes rerolling even more valuable, as any "failed" reroll that you decide to keep still has progress toward a future guarantee.

### Is the S-Rank Selector permanent?

The S-Rank Selector on the Standard Board is a one-time bonus that triggers after you perform 50 pulls. It is not time-limited, so you do not need to rush to complete it on the first day. Most players reach this milestone naturally within the first week of active play.

### What should I do if I get an S-Rank weapon instead of a character?

In Neverness to Everness, weapons are called "Arcs." While an S-Rank Arc provides a significant stat boost, a new character is almost always better for a fresh account. If you pull an S-Rank Arc but no S-Rank character, it is usually recommended to continue rerolling.

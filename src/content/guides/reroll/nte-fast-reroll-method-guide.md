# Neverness to Everness Fast Reroll Method Guide

## Why Reroll

![Why Reroll](/img/nte-fast-reroll-method-guide/inline-1.webp)


Rerolling in Neverness to Everness is a strategic move designed to bypass the initial resource scarcity that every new player faces. By spending approximately 20 minutes per account reset, you can ensure your journey through Hethereau begins with a Tier 0 DPS like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or a high-utility support like [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide). These characters significantly accelerate the speed at which you can clear daily commissions and high-level Anomaly challenges, which in turn leads to faster account leveling and more "Solid Dice" income.

One of the most compelling reasons to reroll in NTE is the unique structure of "The Fair" gacha system. Unlike many other contemporary urban fantasy RPGs, NTE utilizes a "No 50/50" mechanic on its limited character banners. In "The Fair," when you trigger an S-Rank pull on a limited banner like "The Ichi-daime," you are guaranteed to receive the featured character. This mechanic removes the frustration of losing a coin flip to a standard pool unit, making every S-Rank pull during a reroll attempt incredibly high-value. By rerolling, you are essentially fishing for that early 0.99% drop to secure a powerhouse like Nanally before you even touch your main stash of Solid Dice.

Furthermore, the game provides a Standard Banner Selector at 50 pulls on the permanent banner. Smart players use rerolling to snag a specific S-Rank from the limited or standard pool first, then use the 50-pull selector to pick a character that creates perfect elemental resonance or team synergy with their initial pull. For example, if you reroll until you get Sakiri (a top-tier Support/CC unit), you can then use your selector to grab a DPS that benefits from her crowd control, creating a "god account" from day one.

Resource management is another factor. In the first month post-launch, "Solid Dice" (used for limited banners) and "Fabricated Dice" (used for standard banners) are provided in limited quantities through one-time rewards. Free-to-play players will find that after the initial launch honeymoon phase, the rate of acquiring these currencies slows down. Rerolling ensures that your limited "Solid Dice" are not wasted on a mediocre start, but instead supplement a roster that already contains at least one or two elite S-Rank Espers.

### The Importance of Early Team Synergy

Starting with a cohesive team is more important than simply having a high-rarity character. NTE relies heavily on elemental interactions and "Anima" or "Cosmos" resonance. A rerolled account featuring Nanally allows you to build around her high Anima DPS output immediately. When paired with the free "Esper Zero" provided at the start of the game, you can trigger early-game resonance buffs that increase your total damage output by [information unavailable] percent, making boss encounters in the city outskirts trivial.

### Maximizing the 50-Pull Standard Selector

The 50-pull selector on the standard banner is a one-time safety net. If you play normally, you might reach this selector after several days of gameplay. However, by rerolling for an early S-Rank within the first 30 pulls, you can effectively have two S-Rank characters within your first hour of play. This strategy allows you to fill specific gaps in your roster—such as grabbing Jiuyuan for DoT-focused teams if your initial pulls favored a defensive playstyle.

## How to Reroll

![How to Reroll](/img/nte-fast-reroll-method-guide/inline-2.webp)


The reroll process in Neverness to Everness is straightforward but requires a specific approach to save time. Since the game is available on both PC and mobile, the method varies slightly depending on your platform, but the "Salted Email" strategy remains the most effective for most users.

### The Salted Email Method

The most efficient way to reroll on PC or through a mobile browser is the salted email method. This allows you to create multiple game accounts using a single Gmail address. 

1. Go to the Hotta/Perfect World registration page.
2. Enter your email address with a plus sign and a number before the "@" symbol (e.g., yourname+1@gmail.com).
3. The game treats "yourname+1@gmail.com" and "yourname+2@gmail.com" as entirely different accounts, but all verification codes will be sent to your primary "yourname@gmail.com" inbox.
4. Once you land a "god account" with Nanally or [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte), you can stop and use that specific salted address as your main login.

### Mobile vs. PC Rerolling Efficiency

Mobile players have the option of using "Guest Accounts." This is faster for the initial setup as it doesn't require an email immediately. However, you must bind the guest account to a permanent Hotta ID once you are satisfied with your pulls. If you delete the app or clear your cache without binding, the account is lost forever.

PC players have the advantage of faster loading times and the ability to use multi-instance managers (if using an emulator) to run 3-4 accounts simultaneously. This cuts the effective reroll time per account down to about 5 minutes of active focus.

### Claiming Launch Rewards & Codes

To maximize your pulls per reroll, you must claim every available resource immediately after unlocking "The Fair" gacha menu.

**Mandatory Tutorial Milestones:**
*   Complete the "Hethereau Arrival" opening sequence.
*   Reach the first Urban Exploration hub and talk to the NPC [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-17].
*   Finish the "Anomaly Breach" combat tutorial.
*   Unlock the main menu (usually occurs after the first 15-20 minutes of gameplay).

Once the menu is open, check your in-game mail for pre-registration bonuses, which include a generous amount of Fabricated Dice and the free S-Rank character Haniel. Don't forget to enter any active "NTE Codes" in the settings menu to grab extra Solid Dice for the limited banner.

### Tips for Speeding Up the Tutorial

To hit the 15-minute mark, you must be aggressive with skipping. While the story in NTE is high-quality, rerolling is about efficiency.
*   **Dialogue:** Mash the skip button in the top right corner during all non-essential NPC interactions.
*   **Movement:** Use the dash mechanic constantly while navigating the city streets of Hethereau.
*   **Combat:** Don't worry about perfect rotations in the tutorial. Use Esper Zero's burst immediately to clear mobs and move to the next trigger point.

## Best Starters

![Best Starters](/img/nte-fast-reroll-method-guide/inline-3.webp)


Knowing who to aim for is vital because not all S-Ranks are created equal. The current meta heavily favors high-frequency DPS and units that provide "Cosmos" or "Anima" utility.

### Top Tier: The 'Must-Haves'

**Nanally (S+ Tier - Limited Anima DPS)**
Nanally is the current queen of the "The Ichi-daime" banner. As an Anima-element DPS, she excels at shredding shields and dealing massive AoE damage. Her kit is designed for "Hypercarry" setups, meaning if you get her, your entire team should be built to buff her damage. Starting with Nanally ensures you won't struggle with any DPS checks for the first several months of the game.

**Hotori (S+ Tier - Limited Cosmos Sub-DPS)**
Hotori offers a different playstyle, focusing on Cosmos-element off-field damage. She is incredibly versatile and fits into almost any team composition. If you prefer a more flexible account that can adapt to different elemental requirements, Hotori is the best target.

### Standard S-Rank Rankings

If you aren't pulling on the limited banner or if you're using your 50-pull selector, prioritize these characters:

1.  **Sakiri (S Tier - Support/CC):** Her ability to group enemies and apply [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-17] makes her the best long-term investment. Supports usually outlast DPS units in the meta.
2.  **Jiuyuan (S Tier - DoT DPS):** Excellent for sustained damage. She pairs exceptionally well with free units that apply elemental debuffs.
3.  **Baicang (S Tier - Tank/Utility):** While defensive units are less flashy for rerolling, Baicang provides a safety net that makes high-level Anomaly clearing much easier for casual players.

### Characters to Avoid Rerolling For

Do not waste time rerolling for **Chiz** or **Haniel**. Haniel is provided as a pre-registration reward to all players, and Chiz can be earned by reaching Tycoon Level 18. Getting duplicates of them early is fine for their "Resonance Nodes," but they shouldn't be your primary targets.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/nte-fast-reroll-method-guide/inline-5.webp)


### Account Security & Ban Risks

**Can I get banned for rerolling?**
The risk of being banned for manual rerolling using salted emails is extremely low. Developers like Hotta and Perfect World typically only target accounts associated with massive botting operations or those sold on third-party marketplaces. As long as you are manually playing the tutorial and using unique salted emails, your account should remain safe.

**How do I transfer a mobile guest account to PC?**
Once you are happy with your pulls on a mobile guest account, go to the "Account" section in the settings. Select "Bind Account" and link it to a Hotta/Perfect World ID or a social media profile. You can then use those credentials to log in on the PC client.

### Gacha Mechanics & Pity FAQ

**Does pity carry over between limited banners?**
Yes. Neverness to Everness features a pity carry-over system. If you perform 50 pulls on Nanally's banner ("The Ichi-daime") and do not get an S-Rank, those 50 pulls will count toward the 90-pull hard pity on the next limited character banner. This makes "failed" rerolls slightly less painful if you decide to keep an account that had decent A-Rank pulls.

**Should I pull for Arcs (weapons) while rerolling?**
Generally, no. Characters (Espers) provide a much more significant change to your gameplay experience and team power than Arcs do. The "Tri-Key" weapon pulls should be a secondary priority. Only consider an Arc-focused reroll if you have already secured a Tier 0 character and have leftover currency.

**What is the "No 50/50" rule exactly?**
In many games, when you hit the highest rarity, you only have a 50% chance of getting the featured character. In NTE's "The Fair" limited banners, if you see the S-Rank animation, it is guaranteed to be the featured character (e.g., Nanally). This makes rerolling for the featured unit much more consistent than in other games.

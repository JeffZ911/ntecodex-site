# Neverness to Everness Reroll Guide: How to Get the Best Start Fast

Securing a powerful lineup of Espers early in Neverness to Everness (NTE) is the most effective way to ensure long-term success without spending a fortune. Since the game’s release on April 29, 2026, players have discovered that the initial power gap between standard A-Rank units and top-tier S-Rank characters like [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide) or [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) is substantial. This guide provides the most efficient route to "salt" your accounts and secure a "God Start."

## Why Reroll

![Why Reroll](/img/nte-how-to-reroll-fast-guide/inline-1.webp)


Rerolling in Neverness to Everness is about more than just vanity; it is a strategic move to optimize your resource economy. A "God Start" typically involves pulling one S-Rank character from your initial free resources and then using the 50-pull beginner selector to grab a complementary unit. This creates a synergistic team that can clear content significantly faster than the default starter squad.

The launch rewards are particularly generous, offering over 30 Fabricated Dice (used for the standard banner) and more than 4000 Annulith (the premium currency). By rerolling, you effectively "save" these resources for future limited banners, as you won't need to spend Annulith to fix a weak roster. Furthermore, NTE features a unique gacha system where limited banners do not have a 50/50 mechanic—you are guaranteed the featured character. While this is great for limited units, it means that standard S-Rank Espers are actually harder to "stumble upon" later, making the initial reroll your best chance to target them.

### The Value of Early S-Rank Espers

S-Rank Espers possess unique mechanics and higher base scaling that A-Rank units cannot match. For instance, an S-Rank Support like Sakiri provides team-wide buffs and crowd control that remain relevant even in the late game, whereas A-Rank supports often fall off once enemy health pools increase in Hethereau’s harder districts.

| Feature | A-Rank Espers | S-Rank Espers |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Base Attack Scaling** | Moderate | High |
| **Ultimate Complexity** | Single-effect (Damage or Heal) | Multi-layered (CC, Buff, and Damage) |
| **Passive Synergy** | Limited to specific elements | Often universal or transformative |
| **Exploration Utility** | Standard movement | Enhanced mobility (e.g., Nanally’s dash) |
| **Long-term Viability** | Replaced by mid-game | Core pillars of endgame teams |

### F2P vs. P2W Rerolling Efficiency

For Free-to-Play (F2P) players, rerolling is the only way to "guarantee" a high-tier start. A Pay-to-Win (P2W) player might simply buy Annulith to force a character, but even then, starting with an S-Rank via rerolling saves approximately $50 to $100 in equivalent currency. The efficiency of rerolling in NTE is high because the prologue can be completed relatively quickly if you know which dialogue trees to skip and how to navigate the Bridge Crossing area efficiently.

## How to Reroll

![How to Reroll](/img/nte-how-to-reroll-fast-guide/inline-2.webp)


The reroll process in Neverness to Everness is straightforward but requires a specific workflow to avoid the 7-day account deletion wait period. Using the "Salted Email" method is the industry standard for fast cycling.

### The Salted Email Method Explained

You do not need to create dozens of separate Gmail or Outlook accounts. Instead, use the "plus" trick. If your email is `username@gmail.com`, the game will recognize `username+1@gmail.com`, `username+2@gmail.com`, and so on, as unique accounts. However, all verification codes and receipts will still be sent to your primary `username@gmail.com` inbox. This allows you to manage hundreds of reroll attempts from a single email address.

### Unlocking the Scarborough Fair Gacha

To reach the gacha system, known as the **Scarborough Fair**, you must progress through the opening story.

1.  **Step 1:** Launch the game and sign in using a salted email.
2.  **Step 2:** Complete the prologue mission titled "A New Destination." This involves basic combat tutorials and an introduction to the city of Hethereau.
3.  **Step 3:** Follow the navigation markers until the objective "Have a talk with Mint" appears at the Bridge Crossing. This is the final gate before the gacha menu is unlocked.
4.  **Step 4:** Once the dialogue with Mint concludes, the **Scarborough Fair** icon will appear in your main menu (top right on PC/Mobile).
5.  **Step 5:** Navigate to your Mailbox. Claim all pre-registration rewards, including the Fabricated Dice and Annulith milestones.
6.  **Step 6:** Go to the "Strange Encounters" banner. This is the beginner-friendly banner that offers a discounted rate of 8 Fabricated Dice per 10-pull.

## Time Estimate

![Time Estimate](/img/nte-how-to-reroll-fast-guide/inline-4.webp)


Rerolling is a test of patience. Depending on your platform and familiarity with the route, the time per attempt can vary significantly.

### PC vs. Mobile Reroll Speed

PC players generally have the fastest reroll times due to quicker load speeds and the ability to use keyboard shortcuts to skip dialogue. A seasoned reroller on PC can reach the Scarborough Fair in approximately 12 to 15 minutes.

Mobile players may face longer load times, especially when moving between the different districts of Hethereau. Expect a mobile reroll run to take between 20 and 25 minutes. PlayStation 5 players face a unique challenge: because PSN accounts are difficult to "salt," it is highly recommended to reroll on a PC or Mobile device first, then link that successful account to your PlayStation Network ID.

### Tips for Faster Tutorial Completion

To shave minutes off your run, follow this timeline:

*   **0:00 - 0:05:** Skip the opening cinematic and choose your protagonist. Run directly to the first combat encounter. Use AOE attacks to clear the tutorial bots quickly.
*   **0:05 - 0:10:** Sprint through the "A New Destination" corridors. Ignore any optional side-interacts or NPCs that do not have a main quest marker.
*   **0:10 - 0:13:** Reach Bridge Crossing. When talking to Mint, spam the "Skip" button in the top right corner.
*   **0:13 - 0:15:** Open the Scarborough Fair, claim mail, and pull.

**Efficiency Tip:** Never use your Annulith to buy Fabricated Dice (Standard). Only use the free Fabricated Dice provided by the game on the standard banner. Save all Annulith to purchase Solid Dice for the Limited Banner ("The Ichi-daime"). This ensures you are building pity toward a guaranteed limited character while using "free" pulls for your standard S-Ranks.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/nte-how-to-reroll-fast-guide/inline-5.webp)


### Can I reroll on PlayStation 5?
Rerolling directly on PS5 is not recommended because your progress is tied to your PSN account. The most efficient method is to create a HoYoverse-style account on PC or Mobile using the salted email method, find the character you want, and then use the "Link Account" feature in the NTE settings to bind it to your PS5.

### Is salting bannable?
As of May 2026, there have been no reports of players being banned for using the salted email method. It is a common practice in gacha games. However, using automated scripts or macros to reroll is against the Terms of Service and can result in a permanent ban.

### How do I unbind an account?
If you wish to get rid of a failed reroll account, you can go to the User Center in the settings. There is a "Delete Account" option, but be aware it usually has a 7-day "cooling off" period before the email is freed up. This is why salted emails are preferred over deleting accounts.

### Does pity carry over?
Yes, pity carries over between limited banners of the same type. If you pull 40 times on Nanally's banner and don't get an S-Rank, those 40 pulls will count toward the next limited character banner.

### Which banner should I pull on first?
Always use your free Fabricated Dice on the "Strange Encounters" standard banner first. If you get a lucky S-Rank early, it will influence which character you should choose later with your 50-pull selector. Once your standard pulls are done, spend your Annulith on the limited banner to try and snag Nanally.

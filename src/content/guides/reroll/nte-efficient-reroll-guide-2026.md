## Best Starters

![Best Starters](/img/nte-efficient-reroll-guide-2026/inline-3.webp)


Choosing the right character to keep is vital. While all S-Ranks are viable, some provide significantly more utility in the "City Tycoon" and "Anomaly" game modes that dominate the early-game experience.

### Tier S: The Must-Haves (Sakiri & Jiuyuan)
**Sakiri (S-Rank, Incantation)** is currently the most coveted reroll target. Her ability to manipulate the battlefield with elite crowd control and high-frequency AOE (Area of Effect) damage makes her invaluable for clearing waves of minor Anomalies. Her "Incantation" scaling allows her to bypass certain enemy defenses that stop physical-focused characters.

**Jiuyuan (S-Rank, Anima)** is the queen of single-target burst. If you prefer a playstyle focused on boss-killing and high-speed combat, Jiuyuan is the choice. Her Anima-based skills provide a mobility buff that is useful both in combat and while navigating the verticality of Hethereau.

### Tier A: Solid Alternatives (Hathor & Baicang)
**Hathor (S-Rank, Lakshana)** is a highly flexible unit. Her Blade Wings allow for unique aerial movement, making her one of the most fun characters to use in the open world. While her raw damage is slightly lower than Sakiri's, her mobility and consistent Lakshana-type damage make her a safe pick for any team composition.

**Baicang (S-Rank, Anima)** serves as a powerful sub-DPS and shield-breaker. While he might not carry a team as a solo DPS as effectively as Jiuyuan, his utility in higher-level Anomaly zones—where enemy shields become a major obstacle—makes him a great long-term investment.

### Free Characters: Why Your Reroll Needs Synergy
NTE provides several free units: **Chiz** (DPS), **Esper Zero** (Support/Utility), and **Haniel** (Healer/Support). Because Haniel is a highly effective free healer, you should avoid rerolling for **Fadia** or **Daffodill** early on. While Fadia and Daffodill are excellent in the late game, their support-heavy kits do not provide the "carry" potential you need to speed through the first 20 levels of progression. A team consisting of a rerolled Sakiri, the free Haniel, and Esper Zero is more than enough to handle all launch content.

## FAQ

### Gacha Mechanics & Pity FAQ
**Does NTE have a 50/50 system?**
No. One of the most player-friendly aspects of Neverness to Everness is that limited banners do not have a 50/50 mechanic. If you pull an S-Rank on a limited banner like Nanally's "The Ichi-daime," it is guaranteed to be the featured character.

**What is Soft Pity?**
Soft Pity, officially referred to as "Board Modification," begins at 70 pulls. At this point, your chances of hitting an S-Rank increase significantly with every subsequent pull until you hit the Hard Pity at 90.

### Account & Platform FAQ
**Can I reroll on PlayStation 5?**
While possible, it is not recommended. Deleting accounts on PSN is a cumbersome process. It is much faster to reroll on a mobile device or PC using the salting method and then link that successful account to your PlayStation 5.

**Is salting bannable?**
As of May 2026, Hotta Studio has not taken action against salted email accounts. It is a standard practice in the gacha community, though you should always ensure you eventually bind the account to a secure, permanent email address once you are satisfied with your pulls.

### Resource Optimization Tips
**Should I spend Annulith on the Standard Banner?**
Absolutely not. Annulith should be converted exclusively into Solid Dice for limited-time banners. The game provides enough Fabricated Dice through weekly missions, City Tycoon rewards, and world exploration to eventually clear the standard board and use your selector.

**When should I use the S-Rank Selector?**
Wait until you have pulled your first "natural" S-Rank from the standard board or a limited banner. This prevents you from using the selector on a character you might accidentally pull a duplicate of later that same day. Synergy is key; if you pull a powerful Anima DPS, use your selector to grab a Lakshana or Incantation unit to diversify your elemental coverage.

## Overview

![Overview](/img/dr-ratio-best-team-build-hsr/inline-1.webp)


Dr. Ratio is a 5-star Imaginary character following the Path of The Hunt, specializing in single-target follow-up attacks. Unlike traditional DPS characters who rely solely on their own stats, Dr. Ratio's effectiveness is directly tied to the number of debuffs present on his target. This unique mechanic makes him a "thinking man's" carry, requiring specific team compositions to unlock his full potential.

One of the most notable aspects of Dr. Ratio's history in Honkai: Star Rail is his distribution. To celebrate the game's success and his own character lore, Dr. Ratio was famously distributed for free to all players during the Version 1.6 to 2.1 period. This move made him a staple for many players, providing a high-tier Imaginary DPS option to the entire community. He excels in endgame content like Memory of Chaos (MoC) and Apocalyptic Shadow due to his massive single-target burst potential and the ability to frequently trigger attacks outside of his own turn.

### Dr. Ratio's Kit and Mechanics

Dr. Ratio’s kit revolves around his Talent, **Cogito, Ergo Sum**. When Dr. Ratio uses his Skill, he has a base 40% chance to launch a follow-up attack against the target. This chance increases by 20% for every debuff the enemy currently has. To guarantee a follow-up attack (100% chance), the enemy must have at least three debuffs.

His Skill, **Mind is Might**, deals Imaginary DMG to a single enemy. While the Skill damage itself is respectable, its primary purpose is to trigger the Talent's follow-up attack. His Ultimate, **Syllogistic Paradox**, deals significant Imaginary DMG and applies a special status called "Wiseman's Folly." When Dr. Ratio's allies attack an enemy with Wiseman's Folly, Dr. Ratio immediately launches a follow-up attack against that target. This effect can be triggered up to two times per Ultimate use.

### The Importance of Debuffs

Debuffs are the lifeblood of Dr. Ratio's damage profile. Beyond just triggering his follow-up attacks, his Traces further reward debuff stacking. The Trace **Summation** increases Dr. Ratio's Crit Rate by 2.5% and Crit DMG by 5% for every debuff on the target, stacking up to 6 times (maxing out at 15% Crit Rate and 30% Crit DMG). 

Furthermore, the Trace **Inference** gives his Skill a 100% base chance to reduce the target's Effect RES by 10% for 2 turns when he attacks. This counts as a debuff itself, helping him reach his own requirements. To maximize his damage bonus from the Trace **Target**, the enemy needs at least 5 debuffs to receive the full 50% DMG increase.

1.  **Baptism of Pure Thought (5-star Signature):** This is the undisputed Best-in-Slot (BiS) option. It provides a flat 20% Crit DMG increase. For every debuff on the enemy, the wearer's Crit DMG dealt to that target increases by an additional 8%, up to 3 stacks. After the wearer uses a follow-up attack, it applies the "Tame" state to the enemy (up to 2 stacks). 3.  **Cruising in the Stellar Sea (5-star Herta Shop):** The best F2P option. At Superimposition 5 (S5), it provides a 16% Crit Rate boost, with an additional 16% against enemies with HP less than or equal to 50%. It also grants a 40% ATK buff for 2 turns after defeating an enemy.

5.  **Only Silence Remains (4-star):** Increases ATK by 16% and, if there are 2 or fewer enemies on the field, increases Crit Rate by 12%. This is excellent for boss fights where adds are cleared quickly.

Dr. Ratio's Relic choices have evolved with the introduction of new sets. Because he relies heavily on debuffs and follow-up attacks, players should look for sets that capitalize on these specific mechanics.

### Top Relic Sets

1.  **4-Piece Pioneer Diver of Dead Waters:** This is Dr. Ratio’s best-in-slot Relic set. The 2-piece bonus increases DMG dealt to enemies with debuffs by 12%. The 4-piece bonus increases Crit Rate by 4%, and the wearer deals 8%/12% increased Crit DMG to enemies with at least 2/3 debuffs. These effects double for 1 turn after the wearer inflicts a debuff. ### Best Planar Ornaments

1.  **Duran, Dynasty of Running Wolves:** The premier Planar Ornament set for follow-up attack (FUA) focused teams. When an ally launches a follow-up attack, the wearer gains a stack. Each stack increases FUA DMG by 5% (up to 5 stacks). At 5 stacks, it also increases Crit DMG by 25%. In a team with Topaz or Aventurine, Dr. Ratio will max these stacks almost instantly.
2.  **Inert Salsotto:** A highly consistent choice that provides 8% Crit Rate. When Crit Rate reaches 50% or higher, Ultimate and follow-up attack DMG increases by 15%. This was his original BiS and remains excellent if you have better substats on these pieces.
3.  **Firmament Frontline: Glamoth:** Only recommended if you can reach the 135 or 160 SPD thresholds. It provides a flat ATK boost and a significant DMG% increase.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/dr-ratio-best-team-build-hsr/inline-5.webp)


The goal of a Dr. Ratio rotation is to maintain 100% follow-up trigger rates while maximizing the "Wiseman's Folly" stacks from his Ultimate.

### Skill and Ultimate Priority

Always use Dr. Ratio's Skill to trigger his Talent's follow-up attack. You should never use his Basic Attack unless Skill Points (SP) are absolutely critical and the enemy already has "Wiseman's Folly" stacks that can be triggered by other allies. 

His Ultimate, **Syllogistic Paradox**, should be used as soon as it is available, provided an ally is ready to attack immediately after. This applies "Wiseman's Folly," which allows allies to trigger two additional follow-ups from Dr. Ratio. Ideally, use the Ultimate on a boss or elite enemy that your team is currently focusing down.

### Managing Debuff Stacks

Before Dr. Ratio takes his turn, ensure at least 3 debuffs are active on the target to guarantee the 100% FUA chance. If you are running him with Silver Wolf or Pela, this is usually simple. However, in FUA teams, you must track Topaz's "Proof of Debt" and Aventurine's "Unnerved" status. If you are at 2 debuffs, using Dr. Ratio's Skill has an 80% chance to trigger the FUA. While high, the 20% failure rate can significantly disrupt your damage output in tight MoC cycles.

### Speed Tuning and Action Order

Speed tuning is vital for Dr. Ratio. He should ideally act after his debuffers. For example, Silver Wolf or Pela should have higher SPD than Dr. Ratio so they can apply DEF shred and other debuffs before he launches his first Skill. 

In a Robin team, Dr. Ratio should ideally be around 134-136 SPD. This allows him to act twice in the first cycle. When Robin uses her Ultimate, the entire team is advanced forward, so ensure Dr. Ratio has already used his Skill once in that cycle to maximize the number of actions he takes while Robin's buffs are active.

**Optimal Ability Priority:**
1.  **Debuffers:** Apply 3+ debuffs to the primary target.
2.  **Dr. Ratio Skill:** Trigger the guaranteed FUA.
3.  **Dr. Ratio Ultimate:** Apply Wiseman's Folly.
4.  **Allies:** Attack the target to trigger the 2 extra FUAs from Wiseman's Folly.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/dr-ratio-best-team-build-hsr/inline-6.webp)


**How many debuffs does Dr. Ratio need?**
Dr. Ratio needs 3 debuffs on a target to guarantee a 100% chance for his follow-up attack to trigger. However, he needs 5 debuffs to maximize the damage bonus from his "Target" Trace and 6 debuffs to fully stack his "Summation" Trace.

**Is Dr. Ratio still free?**
No, the free distribution of Dr. Ratio ended with the conclusion of Version 2.1. Players who did not claim him during that window must now wait for his limited banner to rerun to acquire him.

**Should I pull for his Signature Light Cone?**
Yes, if you plan on using Dr. Ratio as your primary Imaginary DPS. "Baptism of Pure Thought" is a significant 20-25% DPS increase over F2P options like "Cruising in the Stellar Sea" due to the massive DEF ignore and Crit DMG it provides.

**Can he work without Topaz?**
Absolutely. While Topaz is his best partner for a dual-DPS setup, Dr. Ratio excels in Hypercarry teams. Using Silver Wolf or Pela in place of Topaz allows Dr. Ratio to deal massive individual numbers by shredding the enemy's defenses to near zero.

**What is Dr. Ratio's best Relic set if I don't have Pioneer Diver?**
If you haven't farmed the Pioneer Diver set, a 4-piece Wastelander of Banditry Desert is your best bet, or a 2-piece/2-piece mix of Ashblazing Grand Duke and any ATK% set (like Musketeer of Wild Wheat).

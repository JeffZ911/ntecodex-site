## What an MMO player actually needs

![What an MMO player actually needs](/img/best-mechanical-keyboards-mmo-players-under-150/inline-2.webp)


MMO and urban RPG players have different requirements than competitive FPS players. While a CS2 pro might prioritize raw speed and a 8000Hz polling rate, a player exploring the supernatural city of Hethereau needs utility and comfort.

**Macro Support:** Whether through physical "G-keys" or software like VIA, the ability to map complex sequences to a single press reduces finger strain during 8-hour sessions. In Neverness to Everness, this might involve automating a specific ESP ability chain or managing vehicle shortcuts. The Keychron V6 is particularly powerful here because its VIA support saves your macros directly to the keyboard's onboard memory, meaning they work even if you plug the board into a different PC.

**N-Key Rollover (NKRO):** In high-intensity combat, you may need to press 5+ keys simultaneously; NKRO ensures every input is registered without "ghosting." This is critical when you are simultaneously maneuvering a vehicle and preparing an attack. Most modern mechanical boards under $150, including the SteelSeries Apex 7, offer full NKRO over a wired connection.

**Durability:** PBT keycaps are preferred over ABS because they do not develop a "greasy" shine after months of heavy use, maintaining a consistent grip for precise inputs. ABS keycaps, found on the Logitech G613 and SteelSeries Apex 7, tend to smooth out over time, which can feel slippery during intense boss fights. If you are a heavy-duty farmer, choosing a board with Double-shot PBT is a major quality-of-life upgrade.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/best-mechanical-keyboards-mmo-players-under-150/inline-4.webp)


While a new mechanical keyboard can improve your experience in Neverness to Everness, it is not always a necessary purchase.

If your current keyboard supports software remapping and you aren't experiencing "double-typing" or dead switches, a $150 upgrade may offer diminishing returns. Modern membrane keyboards from reputable brands often include basic macro support that is sufficient for casual farming. If your current setup isn't causing you physical pain or missing inputs during Nanally's skill rotations, your money might be better spent on a higher-quality mouse or an ergonomic chair.

Don't buy a new keyboard solely for "speed" switches (like Silver or Optical) if your primary goal is MMO farming; these switches are often too sensitive for casual typing and lead to accidental ability activations. Speed switches have a very short [actuation point](https://www.razer.com/gaming-keyboards/razer-blackwidow-v4-x), which is great for twitch reactions in shooters but can be frustrating when you accidentally trigger a long-cooldown ultimate in an MMO because you rested your finger too heavily on the key.

If you primarily play on a controller, a high-end mechanical keyboard will provide zero performance benefit for your actual gameplay. Neverness to Everness is designed with multi-platform support in mind, and many players will find the urban driving mechanics more intuitive on a gamepad. In this case, your keyboard is only used for chatting, where a basic $20 board performs just as well as a $150 enthusiast model.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What are the best mechanical switches for MMO players?
Tactile switches, such as Browns or Romer-G Tactile, are generally preferred for MMOs. They provide a physical bump when the keypress is registered, helping to prevent accidental ability activations while still being comfortable for long typing sessions.

### Can I use macros in Neverness to Everness without getting banned?
Most MMOs and gacha games allow simple 1-to-1 key remapping or hardware-based macros that do not automate gameplay (like botting). However, you should always check the official Hotta Studio terms of service before using complex scripts that play the game for you.

### Why is QMK/VIA support important for gaming?
QMK and VIA are open-source firmwares that allow you to remap any key and save that configuration directly to the keyboard's hardware. This means your custom macros will work on any computer without needing to install heavy software like Razer Synapse or Logitech G Hub.

### Do I need a full-size keyboard for MMOs?
While not strictly necessary, a full-size keyboard like the Keychron V6 provides a Numpad that can be used for secondary macros or camera controls. If you have limited desk space, a TKL (Tenkeyless) board like the Apex 7 is a better choice to allow more room for your mouse.

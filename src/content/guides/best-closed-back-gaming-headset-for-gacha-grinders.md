*   **Noise Isolation:** We specifically chose closed-back models for their ability to block out ambient noise. When you are listening for the subtle audio cues of an anomaly in Hethereau, you don't want to hear your roommate's TV or the hum of an air conditioner.
*   **Repairability and Durability:** Only models with high repairability or proven long-term durability were considered. The Beyerdynamic DT 770 PRO, for example, allows you to replace every single part, from the headband padding to the drivers themselves.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/best-closed-back-gaming-headset-for-gacha-grinders/inline-4.webp)


Not every player needs a new $200 headset. There are specific scenarios where your current setup might actually be superior to the ones listed above.

If you currently own a functional pair of high-end studio headphones and a standalone desktop microphone, most "gaming" headsets will actually offer lower audio fidelity. Gaming brands often tune their drivers with a "V-shape" EQ that boosts bass and treble, which can muddy the complex musical scores found in Neverness to Everness compared to a neutral studio pair.

Furthermore, if you play in a perfectly silent room alone, you might want to consider open-back headphones instead. Models like the Sennheiser HD 560S provide a much wider soundstage and better breathability than any closed-back model. The only reason to choose the headsets on this list is if you need to block out external noise or prevent your game audio from leaking out and bothering others.

Finally, don't upgrade specifically for "7.1 Surround Sound" marketing. Most modern urban RPGs use their own internal stereo engines that are already optimized for spatial awareness. Virtual surround processing in a headset often adds unnecessary reverb and can actually make it harder to pinpoint where an anomaly's sound is coming from.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why do I need a closed-back headset for gacha games?
Closed-back headsets are essential for gacha grinders because they provide superior noise isolation. When you are farming repetitive content for hours, blocking out household distractions helps maintain focus. Additionally, they prevent your game audio from leaking out, which is helpful if you are playing late at night while others are sleeping.

### Is wireless better than wired for long farming sessions?
Wireless is generally preferred for grinders because it allows you to get up, grab a drink, or stretch without removing the headset. However, if you choose wireless, you must ensure the battery life is sufficient (at least 30+ hours) or that it has a swappable battery system like the Arctis Nova Pro to avoid being tethered to a cable mid-session.

### Do I need a separate DAC or Amp for the Beyerdynamic DT 770 PRO?
The 80 Ohm version of the DT 770 PRO can be driven by most modern PC motherboards and gaming laptops without a dedicated amplifier. However, if you find the volume is too low, a budget DAC/Amp will help bring out the full dynamic range of the headset, especially for the deep bass found in the Neverness to Everness soundtrack.

### Can I use these headsets on my phone and PC at the same time?
Only the SteelSeries Arctis Nova 7 and Nova Pro Wireless on this list support simultaneous 2.4GHz and Bluetooth audio. This allows you to hear your PC game audio and your phone audio (like a Discord call or a YouTube guide) at the same time. The Razer BlackShark V2 Pro supports both but requires you to toggle between them.

# Neverness to Everness Stamina Guide: Optimal Management & Spending Build

## Overview

![Overview](/img/neverness-to-everness-stamina-guide/inline-1.webp)


Neverness to Everness (NTE) breaks away from traditional gacha energy constraints by implementing a dual stamina system. Instead of a single pool of energy for all activities, the game separates combat progression from urban simulation. This allows you to advance your character's power without sacrificing your ability to engage with the deep City Tycoon mechanics.

The two systems are **Character Pixels** and **City Stamina**. Character Pixels are your primary gate for power-leveling, while City Stamina dictates your economic growth and weekly rewards. Understanding the regeneration cycles and caps for both is vital for maximizing your account's potential in the early game.

### What are Character Pixels?

Character Pixels (often referred to simply as "Pixels") are the combat-focused stamina used to enter Anomaly Zones. These zones are the exclusive source for character experience materials, Arc (weapon) ascension items, and Cartridges (artifacts). 

Pixels regenerate at a rate of 1 every 6 minutes. This means you gain 10 Pixels per hour, and it takes exactly 24 hours to reach the maximum cap of 240. Because regeneration stops once you hit the cap, you must log in daily to spend your Pixels. A standard run in an Anomaly Zone costs 40 Pixels, though the game offers a "Double Reward" option for 80 Pixels to speed up your daily routine.

### Understanding City Stamina

City Stamina (Tycoon Stamina) is used for "Life" activities within Hethereau. This includes high-speed racing, professional fishing, delivery heists, and managing your various business properties. Unlike Character Pixels, City Stamina does not regenerate on a minute-to-minute basis. Instead, it follows a weekly refresh cycle, resetting every Monday at 04:00 (server time).

The primary purpose of City Stamina is to generate Fons (the main currency) and increase your Tycoon Level. As you spend City Stamina, you gain Tycoon EXP, which unlocks new city features, better housing, and even free S-rank characters like Chiz.

### How to Recover Stamina (De-noise Solution & Annulith)

If you run out of Pixels while farming, there are two primary recovery methods. The first is the **De-noise Solution**, a consumable item often rewarded through events, Hunter Level milestones, and the Battle Pass. Each De-noise Solution restores 60 Pixels.

The second method is using **Annulith**, the game's premium currency. You can refill 60 Pixels using Annulith up to 6 times per day. The cost is tiered:
*   **Refills 1-3:** 60 Annulith each.
*   **Refills 4-6:** 120 Annulith each.

For City Stamina, recovery is more limited. You can use **Refresh Recharge** items for a flat 100 stamina boost, but these are rare. Most players rely on the weekly reset.

## Best Weapons

![Best Weapons](/img/neverness-to-everness-stamina-guide/inline-2.webp)


Spending your Character Pixels on Arc (weapon) materials is a high-priority task, but you must focus on Arcs that provide the highest return on investment. In NTE, weapons are called Arcs, and they require specific materials from the **Bubble Can Factory** to ascend.

### Top Priority: Signature Weapons

You should always prioritize the signature weapon of your Main DPS. For example, if you are running **[Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)** (from "The Ichi-daime" limited banner), her signature Arc should receive your first 80 Pixels of the day until it hits the current level cap. 

For **Aurelia**, the best-in-slot weapon is **Stellar Veil**. This 5-star Arc provides a significant boost to Psyche DMG and increases CRIT DMG by 2% for 5 seconds when dealing Psyche DMG, stacking up to 10 times. Since Aurelia relies on high-frequency dodge attacks that consume stamina, the stacking mechanic of Stellar Veil is perfectly suited to her playstyle.

### Best F2P Weapons for Resource Efficiency

If you are a Free-to-Play (F2P) player, the A-rank Arc **Oraora!** is the most efficient use of your Pixels. It provides a stacking Basic Attack DMG buff (up to 10 independent stacks) and has a high ATK substat. It is significantly cheaper to ascend than 5-star Arcs, making it a great "bridge" weapon for your sub-DPS or early-game carries.

### When to Stop Investing in Beginner Weapons

Avoid spending Pixels on B-rank (Blue) or low-tier A-rank weapons past level 40. The resource cost for Arc Ascension spikes significantly at higher tiers, requiring materials like **Silver Appleseed** and **Golden Appleseed**. Save these high-tier materials for your end-game Arcs.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

![Best Disks (Artifacts)](/img/neverness-to-everness-stamina-guide/inline-3.webp)


In Neverness to Everness, "Disks" are officially known as **Cartridges** and **Modules**. These are equipped via the **Console** system, which acts as a grid where you slot in different shapes to activate set bonuses.

### Cartridge vs. Module: What to Farm First?

Cartridges provide the set bonus, while Modules provide the raw stats and fill the grid. You should spend your Pixels in the **Rabbit Hole** domain to farm these. However, the spending priority here is tricky. Early on, you should only farm enough to get a matching 2-piece or 4-piece set bonus, regardless of the stats.

### The Hunter Level 30 Milestone

It is widely considered a waste of Pixels to farm the Rabbit Hole extensively before **Hunter Level 30**. Before this milestone, the drop rates for Gold (S-rank) Cartridges are extremely low. Focus your stamina on guaranteed upgrades—Character Levels and Arc Ascension—until you reach Level 30. Once you hit this cap, the Rabbit Hole becomes your primary Pixel sink.

### Optimal Console Module Configurations

For a character like Aurelia, the **Devil's Blood: Curse (S)** set is the gold standard. 
*   **2-Piece Bonus:** Psyche DMG +10%
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** Increases wearer's DMG by 18% (increases to 36% against enemies affected by Nova or Stain).

To maximize this, aim for **Type III Modules**. The Console system provides a 10% ATK bonus for every Type III Module equipped. A perfect Aurelia build uses four Type III Modules to trigger a massive 40% ATK bonus on top of the set effects.

## Team Comp

![Team Comp](/img/neverness-to-everness-stamina-guide/inline-4.webp)


Building a team isn't just about combat synergy; it’s about stamina efficiency. Some characters possess "Life Skills" or movement passives that save you time and City Stamina during exploration.

### Best Exploration Team for Stamina Saving

The "Exploration Dream Team" consists of **Skia** and **Nanally**. 
*   **Skia:** Can transform into a shadow pool, allowing her to "climb" vertical surfaces without consuming the standard climbing stamina used in the open world.
*   **Nanally:** Her skill allows her to walk on walls, which is significantly faster than standard climbing and consumes less energy over long distances.

Using these two characters allows you to navigate Hethereau's verticality without waiting for stamina bars to refill, making world exploration much more efficient.

### Optimal Farming Team for Anomaly Zones

For clearing Anomaly Zones like **Houdini's Magic Stage** quickly, use **[Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide)**. Sakiri is an Incantation Esper who specializes in grouping enemies. By pulling mobs into a single point, your Main DPS (like Nanally or Aurelia) can clear the wave in one rotation. This doesn't save Pixels, but it saves significant real-world time during your daily farm.

### City Tycoon Character Assignments

When managing shops or properties, assign characters with high Life Skill ratings. For example, **Aurelia** has a Life Skill called **Perfect Fit**, which is specifically designed for shop management. Proper assignment increases the "Fons per City Stamina" ratio, ensuring you get the maximum 1000 Fons (or more with bonuses) for every point spent.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/neverness-to-everness-stamina-guide/inline-5.webp)


To keep your account progressing at a competitive rate, you must follow a strict daily and weekly rotation.

### The Daily Character Pixel Loop

Your daily login should follow this sequence to ensure no Pixels are wasted:
1.  **Check Anomaly Zone Daily Bonus:** Always look for the "Recommended Quest" in the Daily menu for extra Hunter EXP.
2.  **Spend 160 Pixels on Guaranteed Upgrades:** Use these on **Houdini's Magic Stage** (Character EXP) and **Bubble Can Factory** (Arc Materials). 
3.  **Spend 80 Pixels on Cartridges/Abilities:** If your levels are capped, spend the remaining amount on the **Rabbit Hole** or **Houdini's Schemes**.
4.  **Use the Double Reward Toggle:** If you are short on time, use the 80-Pixel "Double Reward" option in the Anomaly Zone to burn your stamina in just three runs.

### The Weekly City Stamina Strategy

Do not wait until Sunday night to spend your City Stamina. 
1.  **Monday-Wednesday:** Focus on high-value deliveries and racing to hit your **Tycoon Level** milestones.
2.  **Goal - Tycoon Level 16:** This is the most important milestone for stamina management. At Level 16, you unlock a toggle that doubles the City Stamina cost of activities but also doubles the rewards. This effectively halves the time you need to spend on "city chores" each week.
3.  **Thursday-Sunday:** Use leftover stamina for fishing or shop heists to maximize your Fons.

### Managing the 240 Pixel Cap

Since Pixels take 24 hours to refill, you should ideally spend them at the same time every day. If you spend your Pixels at 8:00 AM, they will be back at 240 by 8:00 AM the next day. If you miss this window, you lose potential regeneration.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/neverness-to-everness-stamina-guide/inline-6.webp)


**Does Character Pixel stamina overflow?**
No. Once you reach 240 Pixels, the regeneration timer stops. Any time spent at the cap is a loss of potential resources.

**What is the best use of Annulith for stamina?**
Most veteran players recommend doing 1 to 2 refills (60-120 Annulith) per day during the first two weeks of launch to reach Hunter Level 30 faster. After Level 30, refills are only recommended for hardcore Cartridge farming.

**How do I get more De-noise Solutions?**
These are primarily obtained through the "Hunter's Path" (milestone rewards), seasonal events, and the premium tier of the Battle Pass. They do not expire, so save them for when a new character drops and you need to farm their materials instantly.

**Can I recover City Stamina?**
City Stamina resets weekly on Mondays. While you can't "regrow" it daily, you can use **Refresh Recharge** items to add 100 stamina to your weekly pool.

**Is it worth farming Cartridges before Hunter Level 30?**
Generally, no. The "Rabbit Hole" is often called a "Pixel scam" in the early game because the drop rate for 5-star (Gold) Cartridges is non-existent. You are better off spending those Pixels on **Houdini's Magic Stage** to ensure your team is high-leveled enough to clear the harder difficulties later.

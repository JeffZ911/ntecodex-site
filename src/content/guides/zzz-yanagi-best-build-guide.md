## Overview

Tsukishiro Yanagi is an S-Rank Electric Anomaly Agent who functions as a primary on-field damage dealer or a quick-swap Disorder enabler. Unlike previous Anomaly characters who focus on a single elemental proc, Yanagi is designed to exploit the "Disorder" mechanic—the massive burst of damage that occurs when a second elemental anomaly is applied to an enemy already suffering from one. 

Her kit revolves around two distinct combat stances: Jougen and Kagen. Mastering the transition between these stances is the key to maximizing her damage output. Furthermore, Yanagi introduces the "Polarity Disorder" mechanic through her Core Passive, "Lunar Eclipse." This allows her to trigger a specialized version of Disorder damage even if the enemy is only afflicted by a single anomaly, or to trigger it without clearing the existing debuff, which was a major limitation for previous Disorder-focused teams.

### Stance Buff Comparison (Jougen vs. Kagen)

Yanagi switches stances by using her Special Attack (Ruten) or by completing certain Basic Attack strings.

*   **Jougen Stance (Blue):** Focuses on rapid attacks and Electric Anomaly buildup. While in this stance, Yanagi's Basic Attacks have increased range and higher hit counts, making it ideal for applying the Shocked state quickly.
*   **Kagen Stance (Orange):** Increases Yanagi's interruption resistance and provides a significant buff to her Anomaly Power. Attacks in this stance deal more raw damage and are better suited for triggering the final Polarity Disorder hit.

### Core Mechanics: Stances and Polarity Disorder

The "Shinrabanshou" state is the core of Yanagi's gameplay. Triggered by using her EX Special Attack, this state lasts for 15 seconds and grants her enhanced properties for both stances. While Shinrabanshou is active, Yanagi's Basic Attacks gain additional Electric DMG and increased Anomaly buildup.

Polarity Disorder is Yanagi's unique contribution to the ZZZ combat system. Normally, triggering Disorder removes the previous elemental status. Yanagi’s Polarity Disorder, triggered via her EX Special Attack or Ultimate, deals damage equal to [information unavailable] percentage of the original Anomaly damage while allowing the status (like Shock or Burn) to persist for its remaining duration. This allows for "chaining" Disorders in a way that was previously impossible.

### Skill Priority and Leveling

1.  **Core Passive (Lunar Eclipse):** This is the highest priority. It dictates the damage of Polarity Disorder and provides the team-wide Anomaly Proficiency buffs.
2.  **Special Attack (Ruten):** Since Yanagi spends most of her time using her Special Attack to swap stances and trigger Polarity Disorder, this should be leveled alongside her Core Passive.

## Rotation

Playing Yanagi effectively requires more than just button mashing. You must manage her "Shinrabanshou" state and time your stance switches to ensure you are always benefiting from the correct buffs.

### Step-by-Step Combat Flowchart

1.  **Initiation:** Start the fight by using Yanagi's EX Special Attack. This immediately puts her into the **Shinrabanshou** state for 15 seconds.
2.  **Basic Combo:** Perform her Basic Attack string. Focus on reaching the 3rd, 4th, and 5th hits, as these have the highest Anomaly buildup.
3.  **Stance Dance:** Every 8 seconds (or after a full N5 string), use her Special Attack (Ruten) to switch stances. Switching from Jougen to Kagen (or vice versa) refreshes her internal buffs and triggers a specialized strike.
4.  **Polarity Trigger:** When the enemy’s Anomaly meter is nearly full or an Anomaly is already active, use the EX Special Attack. This triggers Polarity Disorder, dealing massive burst damage.
5.  **Energy Management:** Do not spam EX Special blindly. Only use it to refresh Shinrabanshou or to trigger Polarity Disorder on an afflicted enemy.

### Mastering the Stance Switch

The most common mistake is dodging too frequently. Yanagi’s 3rd, 4th, and 5th Basic Attacks are her most valuable. If you dodge, the combo resets. Instead, use her Special Attack (Ruten) to parry incoming attacks. Ruten has invulnerability frames (i-frames) and allows her to continue her offensive pressure without resetting her combo count.

### Maximizing Polarity Disorder Windows

To get the most out of Polarity Disorder, ensure your teammate (like Burnice or Piper) has already applied their elemental status. Yanagi’s EX Special will then trigger the Disorder damage based on that element, but because of her "Lunar Eclipse" passive, the enemy will remain Burned or Assaulted, allowing Yanagi to immediately follow up with a Shock proc for a *second* traditional Disorder.

## FAQ

**Is Yanagi better than Grace Howard?**
Yanagi generally has a higher damage ceiling due to her Polarity Disorder mechanic and higher base stats. While Grace is excellent for consistent, long-distance Shock application, Yanagi excels at burst damage and multi-element team compositions.

**What is the minimum Anomaly Proficiency for Yanagi?**
You should aim for at least **375 Anomaly Proficiency**. This is the threshold required to fully activate the scaling bonuses on her signature W-Engine and to maximize the damage multiplier of her Core Passive.

**Can Yanagi work as a solo DPS?**
Yes. Thanks to Polarity Disorder, she can trigger Disorder-like damage even in a solo setting. However, her efficiency is nearly doubled when paired with another Anomaly agent like Burnice or Jane Doe.

**Should I pull for her M1 or her Signature W-Engine?**
The **Signature W-Engine (Timeweaver)** is a larger priority. It provides a unique Disorder DMG multiplier that cannot be found on any other engine. Her M1 (Mindscape Cinema 1) is strong as it provides Energy Regen and AP, but it doesn't change her damage profile as significantly as Timeweaver.

**Does Polarity Disorder clear the Shock status?**
No. This is what makes Yanagi unique. Her Polarity Disorder deals damage based on the current anomaly but does not consume the status bar, allowing the elemental debuff to continue ticking or to be used for a secondary traditional Disorder trigger.

2.  **M2 (Boundary Between Form and Spirit):** Significantly increases the damage of the Polarity Disorder multiplier. This is her biggest "power spike" Mindscape.

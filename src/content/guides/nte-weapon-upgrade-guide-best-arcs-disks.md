## Overview

![Overview](/img/nte-weapon-upgrade-guide-best-arcs-disks/inline-1.webp)


In Neverness to Everness (NTE), the power of your Espers is fundamentally tied to their Arcs—supernatural weapons that channel Anomaly energy. Unlike traditional RPGs where weapons are simple stat sticks, Arcs in NTE provide a complex layer of base ATK, secondary substats like CRIT Rate or ATK%, and unique passives that can completely change an Esper's playstyle.

The weapon upgrade system is built upon three primary pillars: Enhancement, Ascension, and Mixing. To reach the current endgame level cap of 80, players must balance their resources between raw experience gain and breakthrough materials found across the city.

### How to Enhance Arcs

Enhancement is the process of increasing an Arc's level to boost its base stats. This process requires a specific resource known as Dye, which comes in three distinct tiers: Rising Dye (Low), Senior Dye (Medium), and Elite Dye (High). Alongside Dye, you will consume Beetle Coins, the primary currency of Hethereau’s underground economy.

The most efficient way to farm these materials is through Houdinii's Magic Stage, a repeatable challenge located within the Anomaly Zone. Higher difficulty tiers of the Magic Stage yield significantly more Elite Dye per run.

| Senior Dye | A-Rank | 2,500 EXP | Magic Stage Tier 3-5 |

### Ascension and Breakthrough Mechanics

Every 20 levels, an Arc hits a level cap. To proceed, you must perform an Ascension (also referred to as a Breakthrough). Ascension does not just raise the level ceiling; it often provides a significant jump in the weapon's secondary stat.

Ascension materials are primarily sourced from the Bubble Can Factory. These items, such as the Golden Appleseed or the Collector's Drama Core, are gated by your current Appraisal Level. If your Appraisal Level is too low, you cannot enter the higher-difficulty factory floors required for S-Rank Ascension materials.

### Mixing: Maximizing Passive Potential

Mixing is the NTE equivalent of refinement or "limit breaking" weapon passives. By consuming a duplicate copy of the same Arc, you increase its Mixing Intensity from T1 to T5. Each level of Mixing Intensity enhances the numerical values of the Arc’s unique ability. For example, a T1 Arc might grant a 10% DMG boost, while a T5 version of the same Arc could grant 25% or more. Because S-Rank duplicates are rare, F2P players often find that a T5 A-Rank Arc outperforms a T1 S-Rank Arc in the mid-game.

## Best Weapons

![Best Weapons](/img/nte-weapon-upgrade-guide-best-arcs-disks/inline-2.webp)


Choosing the right Arc depends heavily on your Esper's elemental type and their role within the team. Arcs are categorized into types: Plasma, Solid, Gas, Liquid, and Synthesis. An Esper must match the Arc’s type to activate the weapon's passive effects; equipping a Plasma Arc on a Solid-type Esper will only provide base stats, rendering the weapon's unique power useless.

### Top Tier S-Rank Arcs

S-Rank Arcs are typically designed as "Signature Weapons" for specific 5-star characters, but they can be used by any Esper of the matching type to great effect.

*   **Ready-Ready (Plasma):** Currently the premier choice for boss encounters. Its passive grants "Tiger Talismans" every time the user lands a Critical Hit. These talismans stack to increase DMG against targets with more than 50% HP, making it the ultimate tool for the initial "shred" phase of a fight.
*   **Camellia Society (Synthesis):** This is a high-risk weapon favored by players who can manage their health effectively. It scales CRIT DMG based on the percentage of HP the wearer has lost. It is exceptionally powerful on Espers with self-healing or shielding capabilities that allow them to stay at low health safely.
*   **Marching Beyond Time (Solid):** The best-in-slot Arc for [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte). It focuses heavily on Cosmos DMG and energy regeneration. Its unique passive reduces the cooldown of the wearer's Ultimate whenever a "Recording" phase is successfully completed.

### Best F2P and A-Rank Alternatives

For players who haven't pulled S-Rank weapons, several A-Rank Arcs provide competitive utility.

*   **Dangerous Game:** An excellent general-purpose DPS Arc that increases ATK% for 10 seconds after a successful dodge.
*   **First Step to Success:** A support-oriented Arc that restores a small amount of Energy to the entire team when the wearer uses their Skill.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

![Best Disks (Artifacts)](/img/nte-weapon-upgrade-guide-best-arcs-disks/inline-3.webp)


In Neverness to Everness, the "Disk" system is split into two components: Cartridges and Modules. This dual-gear system requires players to not only find the right set bonuses but also solve a spatial puzzle to maximize their stats.

### Understanding Cartridges and Modules

A Cartridge acts as the "frame" for your gear. Each Cartridge belongs to a specific set and provides a 2-piece (Epic) and 4-piece (Legendary) bonus. However, a Cartridge has an internal grid—similar to a Tetris board. 

Modules are the individual stat blocks you drop into this grid. Modules come in different shapes (L-shapes, squares, lines) and types (Type 1, Type 2, Type 3). To maximize your build, you must fit Modules into the Cartridge grid without overlapping. Matching the Module type to the Cartridge's preferred "Socket Type" provides a 20% bonus to that Module's base stats.

### Top DPS Cartridge Sets

*   **Lost Radiance:** This is the undisputed king for Cosmos DPS characters like Hotori. The 2-piece bonus provides a flat Cosmos DMG increase, while the 4-piece bonus allows the wearer to ignore 25% of the enemy's DEF for 8 seconds after casting an Ultimate.
*   **Echoing Pulse:** Ideal for high-frequency attackers. The 4-piece bonus increases Normal Attack DMG by 5% every time a hit is landed, stacking up to 10 times.

### Support and Survival Cartridge Sets

*   **Speedy Hedgehog:** This set is mandatory for at least one support Esper in every team. The 4-piece bonus grants the entire team a 15% ATK buff for 12 seconds after the wearer uses their Ultimate.
*   **Iron Sentinel:** Best for "Breaker" characters who need to stay on the field and absorb hits while shredding the enemy's Stagger Bar. It provides increased Shield Strength and DMG Reduction.

## Team Comp

![Team Comp](/img/nte-weapon-upgrade-guide-best-arcs-disks/inline-4.webp)


Building a team in NTE is about more than just picking high-tier characters; it is about managing "Elemental Lanes." When different elements interact, they trigger powerful reactions that can shred enemy defenses or provide massive burst windows.

### Meta Team: The Blossom Cycle

The Blossom Cycle is currently the most popular team for clearing high-level Anomaly Zones. It relies on the Blossom (Anima) reaction to create a chain of exploding energy petals.

*   **Main DPS:** Hotori (Cosmos/Solid)
*   **Sub-DPS:** Nanally (Anima/Plasma)
*   **Support:** Zero (Chaos/Liquid)
*   **Breaker:** Jiuyuan (Incantation/Solid)

In this comp, Zero acts as the "insta-cycle" battery, using her abilities to rapidly fill the Esper Cycle meter. This allows Nanally and Hotori to swap in frequently, triggering the Blossom reaction and maintaining high uptime on Hotori's "Recording" phase.

### The Boss Execution Comp (Hyper Hathor)

For single-target bosses with massive health pools, the "Hyper Hathor" team is the gold standard. This team focuses on the Stain and Remora reactions, which apply stacking debuffs that increase the damage the boss takes over time.

*   **Main DPS:** Hathor
*   **Sub-DPS:** Haniel
*   **Support:** Zero
*   **Breaker:** Daffodill

Daffodill is the lynchpin of this team. She is currently considered the mandatory "Break Specialist" for endgame content. Her ability to shred the enemy's Stagger Bar is unmatched, allowing the team to reach the "Break" state—where the boss takes 200% damage—much faster than any other composition.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/nte-weapon-upgrade-guide-best-arcs-disks/inline-5.webp)


Understanding the "Esper Cycle" is the difference between struggling in the Anomaly Zone and clearing it with ease. The Esper Cycle is a circular meter that fills as you deal damage and use skills. Once full, the portraits of your off-field characters will glow. Swapping to a glowing character triggers a "Quick-Swap Attack" that deals massive damage and often applies elemental status effects.

### Mastering the Esper Cycle

To maximize DPS, you should never swap characters manually unless the Esper Cycle is ready. A standard high-level rotation follows this flow:

1.  **Buff Phase:** Start with your Support (Adler or Haniel). Cast shields and Ultimates to trigger the Speedy Hedgehog set bonus.
2.  **Shred Phase:** Swap to your Breaker (Daffodill) using the Esper Cycle trigger. Use her heavy attacks to deplete the enemy's Stagger Bar.
3.  **Burst Window:** Once the enemy enters the "Break" state, swap to your Main DPS (Hotori or Hathor). This is when you unload all Ultimates and high-damage skills.

### Hotori's Recording Rotation

Hotori has a unique mechanic called the "Non-Closed Time Piece." Her rotation is strictly defined by a 20-second recharge cycle.
*   Activate her "Recording" phase (5 seconds).
*   During these 5 seconds, deal as much damage as possible.
*   At the end of the phase, the "Time Piece" detonates, dealing a percentage of all damage dealt during the Recording window as a single Cosmos explosion.
*   If you time this detonation to occur during the enemy's "Break" state, the damage multiplier becomes astronomical.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/nte-weapon-upgrade-guide-best-arcs-disks/inline-6.webp)


**Where do I farm Arc upgrade materials?**
Dye (experience) is found in Houdinii's Magic Stage within the Anomaly Zone. Ascension items (breakthrough) are located in the Bubble Can Factory. Both require Stamina (Energy) to claim rewards.

**How do I unlock the Hunter Exchange?**
The Hunter Exchange is a special shop for high-tier Arcs and materials. You must reach Tycoon Rank 6 in the City Tycoon system by completing urban missions and managing your city reputation to unlock it.

**What is the maximum level for an Arc?**
As of May 2026, the maximum level for an Arc is 80. This requires five stages of Ascension.

**Can I dismantle Arcs?**
Yes. Dismantling unwanted B-Rank and A-Rank Arcs provides Arc EXP items (Dye) and Beetle Coins. It is generally recommended to keep at least one T5 version of every A-Rank Arc before dismantling duplicates.

**Do weapon types matter?**
Yes. An Esper must match the Arc's type (e.g., Plasma, Solid, Gas) to activate the weapon's passive ability. While you can equip a mismatched Arc for the base stats, you will lose the most powerful part of the weapon.

In the current meta, Nanally is highly valued for her elemental alignment. She utilizes the Anima element, which serves as the core component for the "Blossom" reaction. When Anima is combined with Cosmos energy, it triggers a powerful environmental effect that can clear waves of enemies with minimal effort. For beginners, prioritizing Nanally in your early pulls is a strategic move that simplifies both single-target boss encounters and complex AoE mob clearing.

### Why Nanally is the Best Beginner Choice

Nanally stands out because her power floor is remarkably high. Unlike some technical characters that require perfect timing or complex animation cancels, Nanally’s damage is consistent as long as her Underboss is active. This summon-based playstyle allows players to focus on dodging and positioning while the Underboss maintains a steady stream of follow-up attacks. Furthermore, her synergy with the protagonist, Zero, means that every player has access to her best elemental reaction partner from the very start of the game.

### Core Combat Mechanics: Follow-up Attacks

The heart of Nanally’s kit is her Ability to summon the Underboss. Once activated through her Skill, the Underboss stays on the field for a set duration, performing coordinated strikes every time Nanally uses a Normal Attack or an Enhanced Strike. These follow-up attacks are not just cosmetic; they inherit a significant portion of Nanally’s Crit Rate and Crit DMG. This makes her scaling exponential as you invest in her gear. Understanding the timing of these follow-ups is key to maximizing her "Break" potential, as the high hit frequency rapidly depletes the enemy's stagger gauge.

## Best Disks (Cartridges)

![Best Disks (Cartridges)](/img/neverness-to-everness-beginner-tips-nanally-build/inline-3.webp)


In NTE, the gear system for optimizing stats is known as "Cartridges." These function as your primary source of Crit, Elemental DMG, and set bonuses. For Nanally, the goal is to enhance her personal damage while ensuring her Underboss hits as hard as possible.

### Recommended Cartridge Sets

The "Fireflies and the Forest" set is the definitive best choice for Nanally. 
*   **2-Piece Bonus:** Provides a 10% Anima DMG boost.
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** Increases the team's Crit DMG whenever Anima DMG is dealt to an enemy. 

Because Nanally and her Underboss deal Anima DMG constantly, she can maintain 100% uptime on the 4-piece bonus, effectively buffing herself and her sub-DPS teammates throughout the entire fight.

If you are still below Hunter Level 35, do not spend your precious stamina over-farming gold Cartridges. During this transition phase, use the "Shadow Crit" set as a placeholder. It provides a straightforward Crit Rate boost that helps stabilize your damage until you can access the highest tier of the "Fireflies and the Forest" farm.

### Optimal Main Stats and Sub-stats

When rolling for Cartridges, you should prioritize the following stat distribution to ensure Nanally scales correctly into the late game

*   **Head (Fixed):** HP
*   **Drive (Variable):** Anima DMG %
*   **Power (Variable):** ATK %
*   **Logic (Variable):** Crit Rate or Crit DMG (depending on your weapon's substat)

**Sub-stat Priority Checklist:**
1.  **Crit Rate:** Aim for at least 60% to ensure consistent Underboss procs.
2.  **Crit DMG:** Once Crit Rate is stable, stack as much Crit DMG as possible.
3.  **ATK%:** Provides the baseline for all damage calculations.
4.  **Cycle Intensity:** Helps fill the Esper Cycle meter faster, allowing for more frequent Ultimates.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/neverness-to-everness-beginner-tips-nanally-build/inline-5.webp)


To get the most out of Nanally, you must follow a specific combat flow that prioritizes buff uptime and elemental reactions.

### Triggering the Esper Cycle

The "Esper Cycle" is the core mechanic that defines high-level play. Your goal is to fill the meter and trigger the "glow" signal, which indicates a reaction is ready to be maximized.

1.  **Step 1:** Start with Sakiri. Use her Skill or Ultimate to apply ATK buffs to the team and pull enemies into a tight cluster.
2.  **Step 2:** Swap to Zero (MC). Use their Skill to apply the Cosmos status to the grouped enemies. This will begin filling the Esper Cycle meter rapidly.
3.  **Step 3:** Swap to Nanally. Her entry will trigger the Blossom reaction immediately if the enemies are already marked with Cosmos.
4.  **Step 4:** Activate Nanally’s Skill to summon the Underboss. This is crucial—do not start attacking until the Underboss is present.
5.  **Step 5:** Spam Nanally’s Normal Attack string. The Underboss will perform coordinated strikes, dealing massive AoE damage through the Vita Buds generated by the Blossom reaction.

### Maximizing Damage During Break Windows

In NTE, enemies have a stagger bar. When this bar is depleted, the enemy enters a "Break" state where they take significantly increased damage. 

Save Nanally’s Ultimate for this specific window. Her Ultimate has a high damage multiplier that scales even further against staggered targets. If you use it while Sakiri’s buffs are active and the enemy is in a Break state, you can often bypass entire boss phases in a single rotation.

### Common Character Building Questions

**Is Nanally worth rerolling for?**
Yes. As the premier DPS of the 1.0 launch, Nanally provides the smootest progression experience. Her ability to handle both mobs and bosses makes her a "one-size-fits-all" solution for early-game hurdles.

**How do I get the 'Ready' weapon?**
The "Ready" Plasma Arc is a limited-time weapon available on the weapon resonance banner that runs concurrently with Nanally’s character banner, "The Ichi-daime."

**Can I use Mint instead of Nanally?**
Mint is a capable A-Rank Anima DPS and a great alternative if you fail to pull Nanally. However, Mint lacks the follow-up scaling of the Underboss mechanic, meaning her damage ceiling is significantly lower in end-game content.

### Resource Management Tips

**What is the most important beginner tip for NTE?**
Do not ignore the City Tycoon system. This urban simulation side of the game is not just a distraction; it is how you unlock "Chiz" and high-tier gear components for free. Increasing your City Tycoon Level provides passive bonuses that directly affect your Espers' combat stats.

**How does the Break system work?**
Every enemy has a white bar under their health. Certain characters are "Break Specialists" (like Daffodill) who deal increased damage to this bar. Use them to deplete the bar, then swap to Nanally to unleash her full damage during the stagger period.

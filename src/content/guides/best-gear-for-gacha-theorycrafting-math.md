## What a theorycrafter actually needs

![What a theorycrafter actually needs](/img/best-gear-for-gacha-theorycrafting-math/inline-2.webp)


Theorycrafting in modern urban open-world RPGs like [Neverness to Everness](https://www.ign.com/games/neverness-to-everness) involves more than just playing the game. Players must track Esper Resonance Skills, calculate Anomaly interactions, and optimize team compositions. This work creates unique physical and technical requirements for a desk setup.

### Screen Real Estate
Calculating burst windows versus sustained stack-based damage requires side-by-side viewing of game logs and spreadsheets. A dual-monitor mount is non-negotiable for efficiency. For example, a theorycrafter might use one screen to monitor the Ichi-daime banner pull rates or character skill animations, while the second screen holds a Google Sheet with thousands of data points. Portrait mode is particularly useful for viewing long vertical columns of data without excessive scrolling.

### Luminance Balance
Long sessions staring at white spreadsheet cells cause "eye-fry," a form of digital eye strain. High-CRI (Color Rendering Index) light bars, such as the BenQ ScreenBar Halo, are required to balance ambient light. By illuminating the desk surface without creating glare on the monitor, these tools help maintain focus during late-night math crunching when the sun has gone down but the calculations are far from finished.

### Neutral Wrist Position
Moving through thousands of spreadsheet cells and game menus leads to carpal tunnel syndrome if the wrist is kept in a traditional flat position. Vertical mice keep the radius and ulna bones from crossing, maintaining a "handshake" position that is significantly more sustainable for 6+ hour sessions. While these mice are less ideal for high-speed flick shots in competitive shooters, they are the gold standard for the data-heavy work of a theorycrafter.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/best-gear-for-gacha-theorycrafting-math/inline-4.webp)


Not every theorycrafter needs a $500 desk or a $200 monitor arm. There are specific scenarios where these upgrades might be unnecessary or even detrimental.

If you primarily play on a tablet or mobile device, a dual-monitor mount will offer zero benefit to your workflow. While Neverness to Everness is available on multiple platforms, mobile-first players often find that a high-quality tablet stand and a portable mechanical keyboard are more effective than a full desktop setup.

Do not buy a vertical mouse if you primarily play high-DPI competitive shooters on the same machine. The <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Logitech+MX+Vertical&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Logitech MX Vertical</a> has a lower polling rate than dedicated gaming mice like the G Pro Wireless. If you need one mouse for both high-level FPS play and theorycrafting, an ergonomic but traditional gaming mouse might be a better compromise.

Lastly, if your desk is made of thin glass or hollow-core particle board (common in budget furniture), avoid heavy-duty monitor arms. The concentrated pressure of a C-clamp holding 40 lbs of monitors can shatter glass or crush the internal honeycomb structure of a cheap desk. Always verify your desk material before installing a clamp-based mount.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why is a vertical mouse better for spreadsheet work?
A vertical mouse keeps the forearm in a "handshake" position, which prevents the radius and ulna bones from crossing. This reduces pressure on the carpal tunnel and allows for longer sessions of data entry without the wrist pain associated with traditional mice.

### Can a single monitor arm handle an ultrawide screen?
Yes, but only if it has a high enough weight capacity and tension control. The Ergotron LX is specifically recommended for ultrawide monitors because its Constant Force technology can handle the increased leverage and weight of a 34-inch or larger display.

### Does a light bar actually help with eye strain?
Yes, by providing asymmetric lighting that illuminates the desk without hitting the screen. This reduces the contrast between a bright monitor and a dark room, which is the primary cause of digital eye strain during late-night gaming or work sessions.

### Is a standing desk worth it for gaming?
A standing desk like the FlexiSpot E7 Pro Plus is valuable because it allows you to change your posture throughout the day. Switching between sitting and standing can improve circulation and focus, which is essential when performing complex calculations over several hours.

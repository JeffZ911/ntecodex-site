## What a long-session player actually needs

![What a long-session player actually needs](/img/best-nvme-ssd-gacha-gaming-sessions/inline-2.webp)


When you are deep into a four-hour session, your hardware is under constant stress. For a game like Neverness to Everness, which features complex Esper abilities and highly detailed character models, the storage drive does more than just load the game; it acts as a secondary pool of memory.

### High Random IOPS
Sequential speeds (like 7,450 MB/s) are great for moving large files, but games mostly perform "random" reads. This involves pulling thousands of small files—textures for a neon sign, sound effects for a car engine, or UI icons for your inventory—simultaneously. Drives with high random IOPS, such as the <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Samsung+990+Pro&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Samsung 990 Pro</a>, prevent the micro-stutter that occurs when the CPU is waiting for the SSD to find these small assets.

### DRAM Cache
A critical feature to look for is a dedicated DRAM cache. DRAM acts as a map for the SSD, telling it exactly where data is stored on the NAND flash. "DRAM-less" drives use a portion of your system's RAM (HMB) or a slower portion of the flash itself, which can lead to significant slowdowns when the drive is nearly full. For a primary game drive, a dedicated [LPDDR4 or LPDDR5 cache](https://semiconductor.samsung.com/us/consumer-storage/internal-ssd/990-pro/) is essential for rapid zone transitions.

### Thermal Headroom
Heat is the enemy of performance. If an SSD gets too hot, its controller will reduce speeds to 10% or less to protect the hardware. This is why many top-tier drives, like the WD_BLACK SN850X, offer versions with pre-installed heatsinks. If your motherboard already has built-in M.2 shields, you can save money by buying the "bare" version of the drive.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/best-nvme-ssd-gacha-gaming-sessions/inline-4.webp)


Not every player needs a top-of-the-line NVMe drive. You should consider sticking with your current setup if:

1.  **You already have a mid-range Gen4 drive:** If you are currently using a PCIe 4.0 drive with at least 5,000 MB/s read speeds, the jump to 7,400 MB/s will only shave fractions of a second off load times in current titles.
2.  **Your hardware is the bottleneck:** If your primary issue is a slow CPU or having less than 16GB of RAM, a faster SSD will not fix your frame rate issues or combat lag.
3.  **Incompatibility:** Don't buy a Gen5 drive like the Crucial T705 if your motherboard only supports Gen4; the drive will be capped at Gen4 speeds, wasting your investment. Always check your motherboard's manual to see which PCIe generation your M.2 slots support.

## What we didn't include and why

![What we didn't include and why](/img/best-nvme-ssd-gacha-gaming-sessions/inline-5.webp)


To ensure we only recommended the most reliable "drive discs" for long-term play, several categories of storage were intentionally excluded:

*   **SATA SSDs:** Capped at 600 MB/s, these are now too slow for modern asset-streaming technologies. Using a SATA drive in an Unreal Engine 5 game like Neverness to Everness will cause visible pop-in and significant delays when fast-traveling between urban zones.
*   **QLC-based drives:** While cheaper, QLC (Quad-Level Cell) NAND has significantly lower endurance (TBW) and slower write speeds once the temporary cache is full. For a game that requires frequent multi-gigabyte updates, QLC drives like the Sabrent Rocket Q may wear out much faster than the TLC (Triple-Level Cell) drives featured in our list.
*   **Generic/White-label SSDs:** These often swap controllers or NAND types mid-production without changing the model number. This leads to inconsistent performance that can cause crashes or data corruption during long, high-intensity sessions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does an SSD improve FPS in Neverness to Everness?
An SSD does not directly increase your maximum frames per second, but it significantly reduces "1% low" frame drops. By delivering assets to the GPU faster, a high-quality drive prevents the stuttering that occurs when the game engine has to wait for a texture or model to load from a slow disk.

### Is 1TB enough for a dedicated gacha game drive?
While 1TB is sufficient for a few titles, we recommend 2TB for long-term use. Modern urban RPGs and open-world gacha games can easily exceed 100GB each, and frequent updates require extra "buffer" space on the drive to process file installations efficiently.

### Do I need a heatsink for my NVMe SSD?
If you are using a Gen4 or Gen5 drive for long sessions, a heatsink is highly recommended. High-speed controllers generate significant heat, and without a heatsink, the drive will eventually thermal throttle, leading to a massive drop in performance just when you need it most during intense combat or exploration.

### Can I run Neverness to Everness from an external SSD?
Yes, you can run the game from an external SSD like the Samsung T7 Shield, provided you use a USB 3.2 Gen 2 port. While load times will be slightly longer than an internal NVMe drive, it is significantly faster than any mechanical hard drive and provides a stable experience for laptop and mobile players.

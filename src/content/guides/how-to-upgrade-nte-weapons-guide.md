## Overview

![Overview](/img/how-to-upgrade-nte-weapons-guide/inline-1.webp)


In Neverness to Everness (NTE), weapons are officially known as Arcs, which function as equippable disks that provide essential Base ATK and unique passive abilities. Unlike traditional gear, Arcs are categorized into five distinct types: Plasma, Solid, Gas, Liquid, and Synthesis. To activate an Arc's passive effect, you must equip it to an Esper of the matching type.

The upgrade system is split into three core mechanics: Enhancement (leveling), Ascension (uncapping the level), and Mixing (refining the passive). Progression is strictly gated by your Appraisal Level, which increases as you gain Hunter Level EXP from quests and city activities.

### Enhancing Arcs with Dye
Enhancement is the most basic way to increase an Arc's stats. By consuming Dye—which comes in three tiers providing 500, 2,500, or 10,000 EXP—you can raise the Arc's level to its current cap. This process also requires Beetle Coins, the primary currency for development. To farm Dye efficiently, players should spend their Character Pixels (stamina) in the Kaleidoscope stage of Houdinii's Magic Stage.

### Ascending and Appraisal Level Gates
Once an Arc reaches its level cap (initially level 20), you must Ascend it to continue upgrading. Ascension requires materials like Appleseeds and Bubble Cans, primarily found in the Bubble Can Factory. However, your ability to Ascend is hard-capped by your Appraisal Level. For example, reaching Appraisal Level 2 (unlocked at Hunter Level 20) is required to uncap Arcs to level 40. The current maximum level for any Arc is level 80.

### Mixing Duplicates for Passive Power
Mixing is the process of consuming duplicate copies of the same Arc to increase its Mixing Intensity, ranging from T1 to T5. This does not increase the weapon's base stats but significantly boosts the Arc Effect. For S-rank Arcs, this can mean a jump from a 15% ATK bonus to a staggering 25% bonus at max rank.

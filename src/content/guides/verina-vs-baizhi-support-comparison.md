# Verina vs Baizhi: Which Support Should You Build in Wuthering Waves?

In the current Wuthering Waves meta, team compositions almost always follow a "Main DPS / Sub-DPS / Support" structure. While damage dealers like Jiyan or Jinshi often take the spotlight, the effectiveness of these units is directly tied to the buffs and healing provided by their support. Currently, the two primary dedicated healers in the game are Verina, a 5-star Spectro Rectifier user, and Baizhi, a 4-star Glacio Rectifier user. While they share similar roles, their efficiency, buff durations, and resource requirements differ significantly.

## TL;DR Verdict

If you are looking for the absolute best support to maximize your damage and minimize field time, Verina is the winner. However, because the Tower of Adversity requires three separate teams for the final stages, Baizhi remains a mandatory investment for most players.

### The Quick Winner
Verina is the undisputed top-tier support. Her ability to provide a 15% All-Type DMG Deepen buff for 30 seconds via her Outro Skill "Blossom" is unparalleled. This long duration allows your entire team rotation to benefit from the damage increase without rushing. Furthermore, her passive "Gift of Nature" grants a 20% ATK boost to the whole team for 20 seconds whenever she performs a Starflower Bloom or uses her Resonance Liberation. Combined with her incredibly fast Concerto Energy generation, she is the most efficient unit for maintaining high buff uptime.

### Why You Actually Need Both
Despite Verina’s superiority, Baizhi is not obsolete. Wuthering Waves' endgame, the Tower of Adversity, utilizes a "Vigor" system that limits how many times a single character can be used in a specific zone. You cannot simply use Verina for every floor. Baizhi provides a similar 15% All-Type DMG Deepen buff, and while it only lasts for 6 seconds, it is often enough to boost a Main DPS’s initial burst or Resonance Liberation damage. If you are a Free-to-Play (F2P) player, Baizhi is your primary healer for your second team until you pull additional support units.

## Detailed Comparison

To understand why Verina sits at the top of every tier list while Baizhi remains a solid "A-Tier" alternative, we must examine their specific mechanics, energy generation, and how they interact with the game's "Deepen" damage multiplier.

### Healing Mechanics and Scaling
Verina’s healing is tied to her ATK stat. Her Resonance Skill, "Botany Tutorial," and her Resonance Liberation, "Arboreal Flourish," provide immediate and over-time healing to the active team member. Because she builds ATK, her heals are naturally high even with offensive sub-stats.

Baizhi, conversely, scales with Max HP. Her Resonance Skill, "Emergency Plan," calls her Remnant Entity, You'hu, to heal the team. Her Resonance Liberation, "Momentary Union," provides a large burst of healing followed by several "Remnant Entities" that follow the active character to provide sustained recovery. Because HP main stats are common on Echoes, Baizhi is often easier to build for pure survivability early in the game.

### Buffing Capabilities: Deepen vs ATK Buffs
In Wuthering Waves, "DMG Deepen" is a separate multiplier from "ATK%." This makes it one of the most valuable buffs in the game. 

*   **Verina:** Her Outro Skill, "Blossom," provides a 15% All-Type DMG Deepen buff for 30 seconds. This is the longest-lasting buff in the game as of the current patch. Additionally, her "Gift of Nature" passive provides a 20% ATK buff to all allies for 20 seconds when she uses her heavy attack (Starflower Bloom) or her Liberation.
*   **Baizhi:** Her Outro Skill, "Sequence: Cycle of Life," provides a 15% All-Type DMG Deepen buff for only 6 seconds. This requires the player to switch to their Main DPS immediately and execute their highest damage move within a very tight window. Her passive, "Harmonious Existence," drops a "Euphonia" on the field after her Resonance Skill. The character who picks it up gains a 15% ATK buff for 20 seconds.

The gap here is massive. Verina’s buffs are "set and forget," while Baizhi’s require precise timing and manual pickup of an item on the ground, which can be difficult in chaotic boss fights.

### Field Time and Concerto Efficiency
Concerto Energy is the resource required to trigger Outro and Intro skills. Verina generates this energy much faster than Baizhi. By using her Resonance Skill and then performing a jump into mid-air attacks (consuming Photosynthesis Energy), Verina can fill her Concerto bar in roughly 4 to 5 seconds.

Baizhi requires more field time. To maximize her Concerto, she needs to perform her full basic attack string or wait for the cooldown of her Resonance Skill. If Baizhi spends too much time on the field, your overall Team DPS (Damage Per Second) drops because your Main DPS is sitting on the sidelines. To mitigate this, Baizhi players almost always require the "Variation" weapon to boost Concerto generation.

### Resonance Chain (Sequence) Impact
As a 5-star, Verina's Sequences are harder to obtain but very powerful. Her S2 is a major milestone, as it grants 10 additional Concerto Energy when using her Resonance Skill, further reducing her field time. Her S0 already includes a "Cheat Death" mechanic (Grace of Nature) that prevents a team member from dying once every 10 minutes.

Baizhi’s Sequences are much easier to acquire as a 4-star. Her most critical milestone is S6 (Reminiscent of the Past). This Sequence allows Baizhi to revive a fallen teammate to 100% of their Max HP once every 10 minutes. For players struggling with the high damage of bosses in the Hazard Zone of the Tower of Adversity, an S6 Baizhi can be more valuable than an S0 Verina simply for the safety net of a full revival.

### Echo and Weapon Synergies
Both characters utilize the **Rejuvenating Glow** Echo set. The 5-piece set bonus increases the entire team's ATK by 15% for 30 seconds when the wearer heals an ally. This is the standard build for both.

For the Main Echo slot, the **Bell-Borne Geochelone** is the universal choice. It provides a shield that reduces incoming damage and increases the damage dealt by the current character.

**Weapon Options:**
1.  **Variation (4-Star):** The Best-in-Slot (BiS) for both. It provides Energy Regen and a significant amount of Concerto Energy when the Resonance Skill is used. 
2.  **Marcato (4-Star):** A solid alternative that also provides Concerto Energy, though slightly less efficiently than Variation.
3.  **Comet Flare (4-Star):** Good for Baizhi if you lack Energy Regen, as it boosts Healing Bonus based on Max HP, but it does nothing for Concerto speed.
4.  **Rectifier#25 (3-Star):** A decent F2P starter weapon for Baizhi due to the HP sub-stat.

## Recommendation

Choosing between Verina and Baizhi usually isn't a matter of "who is better," but rather "who goes on which team." 

### Best Teams for Verina
Verina should be paired with your strongest, most resource-intensive damage dealers. Because her buff lasts 30 seconds, she is perfect for "Hypercarry" setups where the DPS stays on the field for a long time.
*   **Jiyan / Mortefi / Verina:** The premier heavy-attack team. Verina’s long buffs cover Jiyan’s entire Resonance Liberation duration.
*   **Encore / Sanhua / Verina:** A high-speed swap team where Verina’s fast Concerto generation keeps the rotation fluid.
*   **Jinshi / Yuanwu / Verina:** Verina helps stack Jinshi’s "Incandescence" quickly through coordinated Spectro damage and heals.

### Best Teams for Baizhi
Baizhi excels in teams that have shorter burst windows or benefit from her Glacio element.
*   **Lingyang / Sanhua / Baizhi:** A Mono-Glacio team. While Baizhi doesn't provide specific Glacio buffs, she benefits from the elemental synergy in certain Tower of Adversity stages.
*   **Calcharo / Yinlin / Baizhi:** If Verina is taken by another team, Baizhi can support Calcharo. However, players must be careful to time Calcharo’s "Death Messenger" hits within Baizhi’s 6-second Deepen window.

### Tower of Adversity Strategy
In the Tower of Adversity, you should prioritize Verina for the "Central Tower" (Hazard Zone) or the final stages of the side towers (Experimental/Stable Zones). These floors have the tightest time limits, and Verina’s superior buff uptime is often the difference between a 2-star and a 3-star clear.

Save Baizhi for the earlier floors or the second side tower. If you have her at S6, she is particularly useful on floors with aggressive bosses like the Mephis twins or the Crownless, where the risk of a character dying is high. 

Investment-wise, you should aim for at least 200% Energy Regeneration on both characters. Verina can stop at Level 70/80 for a long time, as her utility comes from her kit rather than raw stats. Baizhi benefits more from Level 80 or 90 because it increases her base HP, which directly impacts her healing and survivability.

In summary, Verina is the peak of support efficiency in Wuthering Waves. Her universal buffs and rapid rotations make her the best unit in the game. Baizhi is a highly competent secondary healer who fills the gaps in your roster and provides a safety net that even Verina cannot match until high Resonance Chain levels. Build Verina first, but do not ignore Baizhi; you will need both to conquer the endgame.

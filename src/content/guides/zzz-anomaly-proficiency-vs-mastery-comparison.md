# ZZZ Anomaly Proficiency vs Mastery: The Ultimate Comparison Guide
> 📝 **Editorial Note:** This guide is AI-assisted and game data evolves rapidly. Please cross-reference with in-game information. _Updated: 2026-05-14._


In the high-intensity combat of Zenless Zone Zero, Anomaly Agents like [Jane Doe](https://ntecodex.com/guides/yanagi-vs-jane-doe-anomaly-comparison), Burnice, and Grace serve as the primary engines for elemental damage and team-wide synergy. However, players often face a difficult choice when equipping Disk Drives: should they prioritize Anomaly Proficiency or Anomaly Mastery? While both stats are essential for any Anomaly-focused build, they serve entirely different functions in the damage calculation formula.

Differentiating between these two stats is vital for maximizing your DPS, especially when tackling high-level content like Shiyu Defense or the Hollow Zero. This guide breaks down the mechanics of Anomaly Proficiency and Anomaly Mastery, explaining how they scale, where to find them, and which one your specific Agent needs most.

## TL;DR Verdict

![TL;DR Verdict](/img/zzz-anomaly-proficiency-vs-mastery-comparison/inline-1.webp)


If you are looking for a quick answer on how to build your Agents, the distinction comes down to "Damage vs. Speed."

*   **Anomaly Proficiency** increases the raw damage dealt by Attribute Anomalies (Burn, Shock, Corruption, etc.). Think of it as the "Attack" or "Crit" equivalent for elemental status effects.
*   **Anomaly Mastery** increases the speed at which the Anomaly gauge fills up. It functions similarly to "Impact" for Stun Agents, allowing you to trigger status effects more frequently.
*   **For Main DPS Anomaly Agents (like [Jane Doe](https://ntecodex.com/guides/yanagi-vs-jane-doe-anomaly-comparison)):** Prioritize Anomaly Proficiency. Jane Doe specifically scales her Attack and Crit Rate for Assault based on this stat, with a "Gold Standard" target of 420 Proficiency.
*   **For Support or Disorder-enabling Agents (like Grace or Burnice):** Prioritize Anomaly Mastery. These Agents often need to apply their status quickly before swapping to another character to trigger a Disorder burst.

## Detailed Comparison

![Detailed Comparison](/img/zzz-anomaly-proficiency-vs-mastery-comparison/inline-3.webp)


To truly optimize a build, you must look at the math behind the status effects. Zenless Zone Zero calculates Anomaly damage and buildup using two separate tracks.

### How Anomaly Proficiency Scales Damage

Anomaly Proficiency is a direct multiplier for the damage triggered when an enemy’s Anomaly gauge reaches 100%. Unlike standard physical attacks that rely on Crit Rate and Crit DMG, Anomaly effects like **Burn** (Fire) and **Shock** (Electric) cannot naturally crit unless a specific character passive allows it. Instead, they rely on Proficiency to reach high numbers.

The base formula for Anomaly Damage is:
`Anomaly DMG = Base DMG (Level-based) * (Anomaly Proficiency / 100) * ATK Multiplier * DMG % Bonuses * Enemy Resistance`

Every single point of Anomaly Proficiency adds 1% to your base damage multiplier. For example, if an Agent has 200 Anomaly Proficiency, their Anomaly effects will deal 200% of the base damage. If you push that to 400 Proficiency, the damage doubles again to 400%.

For characters like [Jane Doe](https://ntecodex.com/guides/yanagi-vs-jane-doe-anomaly-comparison), Proficiency is even more valuable. Her Core Passive, **Insight**, converts Anomaly Proficiency into flat Attack (up to a maximum of 600 ATK) and increases the Crit Rate of her **Assault** procs by up to 40%. This makes Proficiency the single most important stat for her total output.

### How Anomaly Mastery Accelerates Buildup

Anomaly Mastery does not make your hits harder; it makes them "heavier" in terms of gauge filling. Every attack in ZZZ has a hidden "Anomaly Buildup" value. Anomaly Mastery acts as a multiplier for that value.

If an attack has a base buildup of 10 and you have 130 Anomaly Mastery, that attack will contribute 13 points to the enemy's gauge. High-tier bosses often have "Anomaly Buildup Resistance," meaning their gauges are harder to fill. Without sufficient Mastery, you may find yourself attacking a boss for ten seconds or more before a status effect finally triggers, which significantly lowers your total team DPS.

Anomaly Mastery is exclusively found as a Main Stat on **Disk Drive Slot 6**. Because it is not available as a substat, choosing Mastery on Slot 6 usually means sacrificing ATK% or Energy Regeneration Rate. For most Anomaly Agents, this is a mandatory trade-off.

### The Synergy: Proficiency, Mastery, and Disorder

The "Disorder" mechanic occurs when you apply a new Attribute Anomaly to an enemy that is already under the effect of a different one. For example, if an enemy is currently **Shocked** and you apply **Burn**, the Shock status is consumed instantly to deal a massive burst of "Disorder" damage.

*   **Mastery's Role in Disorder:** To trigger Disorder frequently, you need to cycle between two different elements as fast as possible. High Mastery on both your primary and secondary Anomaly Agents is required to keep this cycle moving.
*   **Proficiency's Role in Disorder:** The damage of the Disorder burst is calculated based on the *remaining* damage of the previous status effect plus a multiplier from the Proficiency of the Agent who triggers the Disorder. If your first Agent has low Proficiency, the "remaining" damage will be low, resulting in a weak Disorder proc.

To maximize Disorder damage, you generally want your "Enabler" (the one who applies the first status) to have high Proficiency, and your "Trigger" (the one who applies the second status) to have high Mastery to close the gap quickly.

## Recommendation

Choosing between these stats often depends on the specific Agent you are playing and the role they fill in your squad.

### Best Stats for Jane Doe and Physical Agents

[Jane Doe](https://ntecodex.com/guides/yanagi-vs-jane-doe-anomaly-comparison) is currently the benchmark for on-field Anomaly DPS. Her kit is designed to stay on the field, dodge frequently to enter the **Passion** state, and trigger **Assault** repeatedly.

*   **Priority:** Anomaly Proficiency.
*   **Why:** Jane needs to hit the 420 Proficiency threshold to maximize her passive buffs. While she still needs Mastery to trigger Assault quickly, her kit provides natural buildup bonuses while in the Passion state, allowing her to focus more heavily on Proficiency for damage.
*   **Target Stat:** Aim for 420+ Anomaly Proficiency and 130+ Anomaly Mastery.

For other Physical Anomaly Agents like Piper, Proficiency remains the priority because **Assault** deals a massive chunk of damage at once. The higher the Proficiency, the larger that single burst becomes.

### Best Stats for Grace, Burnice, and Elemental Supports

Agents like Grace (Electric) and Burnice (Fire) are often used to set up the field for a Disorder trigger or to provide constant off-field pressure.

*   **Priority:** Anomaly Mastery.
*   **Why:** Burnice, for instance, applies Burn from off-field via her "Afterburn" mechanic. You want that Burn to trigger as fast as possible so you can switch back to your main DPS or trigger Disorder. Grace relies on her high-frequency multi-hits to Shock enemies; if her Mastery is too low, her "Shock" won't trigger in time to be useful in a fast rotation.
*   **Target Stat:** Prioritize an Anomaly Mastery Main Stat on Slot 6 before focusing on Proficiency substats.

### Best W-Engines for Anomaly Builds

Your choice of W-Engine can significantly alleviate the pressure of balancing these two stats.

1.  **Sharpened Stinger ([Jane Doe](https://ntecodex.com/guides/yanagi-vs-jane-doe-anomaly-comparison) Signature):** Provides high Physical DMG and Anomaly Proficiency. It also increases the wearer's Anomaly Buildup Rate, effectively giving you "free" Mastery.
2.  **Fusion Compiler (Grace Signature):** Provides a massive boost to ATK and up to 75 Anomaly Proficiency (at base rank) when using Special Attacks. It is the best general-purpose damage engine for Anomaly Agents.
3.  **Flamemaker Shaker (Burnice Signature):** Provides Fire DMG and Anomaly Mastery. This is the gold standard for Agents who need to trigger statuses quickly while off-field.
4.  **Rainweaver (Yanagi Signature):** undisclosed, but it is designed to enhance Electric Anomaly and Disorder efficiency.
5.  **Electro-Lip Gloss (A-Rank):** A fantastic F2P-friendly option that provides Anomaly Proficiency and increases DMG when an enemy is affected by an Anomaly.

### Final Verdict: Balancing Your Build

The most effective Anomaly builds do not ignore one stat for the other. Instead, they use the Disk Drive slots to cover both bases. 

*   **Use Slot 4 for Proficiency:** This is the most efficient way to get the +92 flat Proficiency needed for high damage.
*   **Use Slot 6 for Mastery:** This provides the 30% buildup boost necessary to handle bosses in the endgame.
*   **Substat Hunting:** Once your main stats are set, hunt for Anomaly Proficiency substats on every single piece of gear. Mastery cannot appear as a substat, so Proficiency is the only "variable" you can infinitely improve through farming.

If you find that your status effects are dealing massive damage but taking too long to trigger, swap your Slot 6 to Mastery. If you are triggering status effects every three seconds but the damage numbers look small, focus on increasing your Proficiency through Slot 4 and the Freedom Blues or Chaos Jazz set bonuses. For most high-end Proxies, the goal is to reach at least 350-400 Proficiency while maintaining the 30% Mastery bonus from Slot 6.

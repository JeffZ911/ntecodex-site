## Detailed Comparison

The gap between Jiyan and Calcharo isn't just about numbers; it's about how those numbers are delivered. Jiyan is a "sustained" DPS who stays on field and pummels everything in a wide radius. Calcharo is a "burst" DPS who relies on a very specific window of time to unleash his most powerful attacks, often requiring the player to manipulate character swap mechanics to bypass long animation frames.

### Playstyle and Ease of Use

Jiyan’s playstyle revolves around his Resonance Liberation, **Emerald Storm: Prelude**. When activated, he enters the Qingloong Mode. In this state, his basic attacks are converted into Heavy Attacks that deal massive Aero damage. The most significant advantage here is the "Windqueller" effect, which provides significant grouping and a massive hitbox. You can essentially hold down the attack button and watch Jiyan shred through entire rooms of enemies. His resistance to interruption during this mode is also [information unavailable], but generally high enough to allow for a very aggressive "unga bunga" approach.

Calcharo is the polar opposite. His kit is built around the **Mercy** and **Cruelty** stacks found in his Forte Circuit, **Hunting Mission**. To play Calcharo effectively, you must manage his Resonance Skill, **Extermination Order**, which can be cast three times in succession. During his Resonance Liberation, **Phantom Etching**, he enters a state where he can trigger "Death Messenger," a high-damage plunge attack. The difficulty lies in the fact that Calcharo is highly susceptible to being interrupted. To maximize his DPS, advanced players use "swap-canceling"—swapping to a sub-DPS like Yinlin during Calcharo’s heavy animation frames to keep the damage flowing without wasting time on the field.

### Damage Output: AoE vs. Single Target

In AoE scenarios, Jiyan is the undisputed champion. His Qingloong Mode attacks have a reach that covers nearly half the arena in the Tower of Adversity. Because his attacks pull enemies toward him, he naturally maximizes his damage by hitting every target simultaneously. His damage multipliers during his ultimate are specifically tuned to benefit from Heavy Attack DMG Deepen buffs, which are plentiful in the current meta.

Calcharo excels in single-target encounters, such as boss fights against the Mephis twins or the Mourning Aix. His "Death Messenger" hits have incredibly high scaling, but they are "back-loaded," meaning most of his damage comes at the very end of his combo. If a boss moves away or if you are forced to dodge mid-combo, Calcharo’s DPS takes a massive hit. However, if executed perfectly, his single-target burst can outpace Jiyan, particularly when his Electro damage is amplified by external buffs.

### Team Synergies and Support Requirements

The "Yinlin Factor" is the single most important consideration for Calcharo players. Yinlin provides a 20% Electro DMG Deepen and a 25% Resonance Liberation DMG Deepen through her Outro Skill. This is tailor-made for Calcharo, as almost all of his relevant damage is considered Resonance Liberation damage. Without Yinlin, Calcharo’s performance drops significantly, making him a "high-investment" unit in terms of team composition.

Jiyan has the "Mortefi Factor." Mortefi’s Outro Skill provides a staggering 38% Heavy Attack DMG Deepen. Since Jiyan’s entire Resonance Liberation kit is classified as Heavy Attack damage, this synergy is nearly perfect. Furthermore, Mortefi’s coordinated attacks trigger off Jiyan’s frequent hits, adding a layer of off-field damage that rivals the Yinlin/Calcharo duo. For those without Mortefi, Jiyan can still function well with Akebi or even Baizhi, but Mortefi remains his best-in-slot partner.

### Weapon and Echo Optimization

Both characters benefit immensely from their signature 5-star weapons, but the dependency varies.

*   **Jiyan's Verdant Summit:** This weapon provides a massive boost to Heavy Attack DMG and Crit Rate. For Jiyan, this weapon is a transformative power spike. Without it, he often relies on the 4-star **Autumntrace** (from the Insider Channel) or the standard 5-star **Lustrous Razor**.
*   **Calcharo's Lustrous Razor:** While Calcharo can use the Lustrous Razor effectively due to its Resonance Energy Regen and Liberation DMG buffs, he is often seen using **Autumntrace** for the consistent Crit Rate.

For Echoes, Jiyan utilizes the **Sierra Gale** set with the **Feilian Knight** as the primary Echo. The Feilian Knight provides an Aero DMG and Heavy Attack DMG boost upon using its skill. Calcharo uses the **Void Thunder** set with either **Thundering Mephis** or **Tempest Mephis**. The Thundering Mephis is generally preferred for its Electro and Liberation DMG buffs, though the animation is notoriously long, often requiring another swap-cancel to avoid DPS loss.

## Recommendation

The choice between Jiyan and Calcharo often depends on your current roster and your patience for learning complex rotations. As the meta has evolved toward the mid-2026 landscape, Jiyan remains the more "future-proof" unit due to the sheer utility of his grouping and the ease with which he can be slotted into various Aero teams.

### Who Should Choose Jiyan?

You should prioritize Jiyan if you value consistency and ease of use. He is the perfect unit for mobile players or those who do not want to spend hours practicing frame-perfect swaps. If you managed to pull Mortefi to at least Sequence 4 or Sequence 6, Jiyan becomes an unstoppable force. He is also the better investment for clearing the "trash mob" floors of the Tower of Adversity, where time is of the essence and grouping is mandatory.

### Who Should Choose Calcharo?

Calcharo is the choice for the "hardcore" player. If you enjoy the rhythm of a complex rotation and the satisfaction of a perfectly timed swap-cancel, Calcharo offers a more rewarding experience. He is also much easier to obtain for F2P players, as he is a permanent fixture on the standard banner and was part of the initial 5-star selector. If you pulled Yinlin during her debut or rerun, building Calcharo is almost a requirement to see the true potential of the Electro element.

### Final Meta Analysis

In the current Wuthering Waves meta as of May 12, 2026, Jiyan holds a slight edge in overall value. The ability to ignore enemy mechanics through sheer range and grouping is a utility that raw damage cannot always replace. However, Calcharo remains a top-tier contender for boss-killing.

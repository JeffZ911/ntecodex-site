## Overview

![Overview](/img/zzz-agent-level-up-guide/inline-1.webp)


In Zenless Zone Zero (ZZZ), Agent progression is the foundation of your combat effectiveness. Unlike many action RPGs where player skill can overcome a massive stat gap, ZZZ implements strict "Daze" and "Anomaly" mechanics that scale directly with your Agent’s level and stats. If your level is too low compared to the enemy, you will find it significantly harder to stun bosses or survive heavy hits.

Agents have a maximum level cap of 60. However, you cannot reach this cap immediately. Progression is gated by your Inter-Knot Level (IKL), which represents your account's overall rank. Every 10 levels, you must perform an "Agent Promotion" to unlock the next level bracket. This process consumes specific materials and Dennies (the primary in-game currency).

### Agent Promotion & Certification Seals

To promote an Agent, you need Certification Seals. These are categorized by the Agent’s Specialty. For example, an Attack Agent like Ellen Joe requires "Offensive Seals," while a Stun Agent like Anby Demara requires "Stun Seals." These materials are primarily farmed at the HIA Club through Combat Simulation.

The tier of seal required increases as you reach higher level brackets
*   **Basic Seals:** Used for Level 20 and 30 promotions.
*   **Advanced Seals:** Used for Level 40 and 50 promotions.
*   **Elite Seals:** Used for the final Level 60 promotion.

### Skill & Core Passive Progression

Leveling an Agent’s base stats is only half the battle. Each Agent has five active skills: Basic Attack, Dodge, Special Attack, Chain Attack (which includes the Ultimate), and Assist. Each of these can be leveled up to 12 to increase damage multipliers and utility.

Skill leveling requires Tactical Chips (e.g., [Freeze Chip] for Ice Agents) and specialized materials for higher levels.
*   **Tactical Chips:** Farmed in Combat Simulation.
*   **Advanced Materials:** Required for skills beyond level 7, obtained from Notorious Hunt (weekly bosses).
*   **Hamster Cage Pass:** An extremely rare resource required to max out a skill to level 12. Use these sparingly on your primary DPS characters.

In addition to active skills, Agents have "Core Passives" (labeled A through F). Upgrading these provides massive stat boosts, such as Crit Rate, Elemental DMG, or Impact. These upgrades require "Higher-Dimensional Data" from Expert Challenges.

### Inter-Knot Level Milestones

Your progression is tied to the following Inter-Knot Level milestones. Reaching these levels triggers a "Proxy Rank" ascension quest, which increases the world difficulty and the quality of rewards from farming.

| Agent Level Cap | Inter-Knot Level Requirement | Promotion Material Tier |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Level 20 | IKL 1-19 | Basic Certification Seal |
| Level 30 | IKL 20-29 | Basic Certification Seal |
| Level 40 | IKL 30-39 | Advanced Certification Seal |
| Level 50 | IKL 40-49 | Advanced Certification Seal |
| Level 60 | IKL 50+ | Elite Certification Seal |

### Investigation Log XP Values

To raise an Agent's level within a bracket, you consume Investigation Logs. These are the most common farming requirement in the early game.

*   **Trainee Investigation Log:** 1,000 XP
*   **Official Investigation Log:** 3,000 XP
*   **Senior Investigation Log:** 10,000 XP

## Best Weapons

![Best Weapons](/img/zzz-agent-level-up-guide/inline-2.webp)


In Zenless Zone Zero, weapons are known as W-Engines. These devices provide a flat Base ATK stat and a secondary stat (such as Crit Rate, ATK%, or Energy Regen). More importantly, every W-Engine has a unique passive effect.

### Top S-Rank W-Engines

S-Rank W-Engines are often "signature" weapons designed for specific characters. While they offer the highest stats, they are difficult to obtain.

*   **The Brimstone (Attack):** A powerhouse for sustained DPS. It increases ATK by up to 3.5% whenever a Basic Attack, Dash Attack, or Dodge Counter hits, stacking up to 8 times.
*   **Deep Sea Visitor (Attack):** The signature weapon for Ellen Joe. It provides a massive Crit Rate boost and increases Ice DMG when performing Dash Attacks.
*   **The Restrained (Stun):** Ideal for Lycaon. It increases DMG and Daze buildup when hitting enemies with Basic Attacks.
*   **Fusion Compiler (Anomaly):** Excellent for Grace, providing ATK and Anomaly Proficiency, which scales the damage of elemental procs like Shock.

### Best F2P W-Engines

For players not looking to spend on the weapon banner, several A-Rank W-Engines provide incredible value and can be purchased from the Gadget Store (Box Galaxy) using shards.

*   **Starlight Engine (Attack):** One of the best universal F2P weapons. It grants an ATK buff after using a Quick Assist or Dodge Counter.
*   **Steam Oven (Support):** A great choice for Nicole or Soukaku, as it boosts Energy Regen based on the amount of Energy currently held.
*   **Demara Battery Mark II (Stun):** Provides Electric DMG and Energy Regen, making it the perfect fit for Anby.

### Weapon Modification & Refinement

W-Engines can be "Modified" (leveled up) to 60 using W-Engine Power Supplies. Similar to Agents, they require promotion materials at every 10-level interval.

You can also "Phase" a W-Engine by consuming a duplicate copy of the same weapon. This increases the potency of the passive effect. For A-Rank and B-Rank weapons, reaching Phase 5 is highly recommended to narrow the gap between them and unrefined S-Rank options.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/zzz-agent-level-up-guide/inline-5.webp)


Combat in Zenless Zone Zero is not about button-mashing; it is about managing the "Daze" bar and maximizing the "Stun" window.

### Building Daze Efficiently

Your goal at the start of any encounter is to fill the enemy's yellow Daze bar.
1.  **Lead with your Stun Agent:** Agents like Anby, Lycaon, or Koleda have high "Impact" stats. Their moves generate significantly more Daze than Attackers.
2.  **Use Defensive Assists:** When an enemy attacks, switch characters just before the hit lands to perform a Parry (Defensive Assist). This deals massive Daze damage for a small cost of Assist Points.

### Executing Perfect Chain Attacks

Once the enemy's Daze bar reaches 100%, they enter a "Stunned" state, and your next heavy hit will trigger a Chain Attack.
1.  **The Chain Sequence:** You can select which Agent comes in next. Generally, you want to cycle through your Support first and end the chain with your Attacker.
2.  **The DMG Multiplier:** Stunned enemies take increased damage (usually 150% or more). This is the only time you should be using your Ultimate.

### Managing Energy & Ultimate Timing

Energy is a shared resource but built individually.
*   **Support Buffing:** Ensure your Support Agent (like Soukaku or Nicole) uses their EX Special Attack just before the boss is stunned.
*   **The Burst Window:** Once the boss is stunned and the Chain Attack is complete, switch to your Attacker. Dump all stored Energy into their EX Special Attacks.
*   **Ultimate Priority:** Never use your Ultimate on a non-stunned boss. The damage waste is significant. Always save it for the peak of the stun window when all buffs are active.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/zzz-agent-level-up-guide/inline-6.webp)


### Where can I farm Agent level-up materials?
All primary progression materials are found in the **HIA Club**.
*   **Investigation Logs & Certification Seals:** Combat Simulation.
*   **Skill Chips:** Combat Simulation (Elemental tabs).
*   **Core Passive Materials:** Expert Challenge (costs 60 Battery Charge).
*   **Weekly Boss Materials:** Notorious Hunt (Scott Outpost).

### Should I level my Support Agents to 60?
In the early game, priority should be: Attacker (Lv 60) > Stunner (Lv 50) > Support (Lv 45-50). Support Agents do not gain as much from the final ten levels compared to Attackers, who need the raw Base ATK and level-scaling damage. However, Stunners benefit from the level 60 cap because it allows them to equip higher-level Disk Drives with more Impact.

### How do I unlock higher-tier Certification Seals?
Higher-tier seals (Advanced and Elite) unlock automatically as your Inter-Knot Level increases. Once you hit IKL 40 and 50, the HIA Club will offer higher-difficulty stages that drop the superior seals.

### What is the best way to spend Battery Charge (Stamina)?
1.  **Character/Weapon Levels:** Always keep your main trio at the current level cap.
2.  **Core Passives:** These provide the largest "bang for your buck" in terms of stat increases.
3.  **Disk Drives:** Do not spend significant Battery Charge farming Disk Drives until you reach IKL 40, which guarantees S-Rank (Gold) drops. Before then, use whatever Disks you find from Hollow Zero or the Music Store.

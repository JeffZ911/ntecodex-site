# Kazuha vs. Sucrose: Which Anemo Support is Better in Genshin Impact?

Deciding whether to pull for Kaedehara Kazuha or invest in your C6 Sucrose depends entirely on your specific team composition and your tolerance for complex rotations. While both characters utilize the powerful Viridescent Venerer artifact set to shred enemy resistances, they provide buffs through fundamentally different mechanics.

## TL;DR Verdict

![TL;DR Verdict](/img/kazuha-vs-sucrose-support-comparison/inline-1.webp)


*   **Kazuha** is the superior choice for raw Elemental DMG teams (Mono-element, Freeze) and Hypercarries like Neuvillette or Arlecchino due to his consistent DMG% buffs.
*   **Sucrose** remains the queen of reaction-heavy teams (Vaporize, Melt, Aggravate) because her EM sharing often outweighs Kazuha's DMG bonus at C0.
*   **Kazuha** offers much more reliable and frequent crowd control (CC) with his Elemental Skill, while Sucrose's CC is tied to a high-cost Burst and auto-targeting.
*   At C2, **Kazuha** generally outclasses C6 Sucrose in almost every scenario, but Sucrose is significantly more F2P-friendly and can hold the powerful Thrilling Tales of Dragon Slayers (TTDS).

### The Quick Winner
For the majority of players, **Kaedehara Kazuha** is the "winner" due to his sheer versatility and ease of use. His Elemental Skill, Chihayaburu, provides immediate and reliable grouping that centers on the character, making it much easier to execute rotations in the Spiral Abyss. Furthermore, his ability to buff multiple elements simultaneously with a single plunge makes him the gold standard for modern multi-element teams.

### When to Choose Sucrose
You should prioritize **Sucrose** if you are running a Vaporize or Melt team where your primary damage dealer has low Elemental Mastery. Because Sucrose can pass over 200 EM to the entire party, she can often provide a higher damage ceiling for a single "nuke" hit than a C0 Kazuha. She is also the optimal choice if your team needs the 48% ATK buff from Thrilling Tales of Dragon Slayers, a catalyst-exclusive advantage.

### When to Choose Kazuha
Choose **Kazuha** for any team focusing on non-transformative damage, such as a Mono-Pyro team with Lyney or a Mono-Hydro team with Neuvillette. His DMG% bonus is a separate multiplier that does not suffer from the same diminishing returns as EM in these specific archetypes. Additionally, if you find yourself struggling with enemy positioning, Kazuha’s grouping is significantly more powerful and has a shorter effective cooldown than Sucrose’s.

### Passive Talent Breakdown
Kazuha's **Poetics of Fuubutsu** is his core identity. Upon triggering a Swirl reaction, he grants teammates a 0.04% Elemental DMG Bonus for the corresponding element for every point of Elemental Mastery he possesses. At 1,000 EM, this equates to a massive 40% DMG bonus.

Sucrose's **Mollis Favonius** and **Catalyst Conversion** work differently. When her Skill or Burst hits an opponent, she increases the EM of all party members (excluding herself) by 20% of her own EM. Her other passive adds a flat 50 EM to party members of the swirled element. If Sucrose has 1,000 EM, she shares 250 EM with the team.

### Energy and Burst Mechanics
Kazuha has a significantly easier time managing his Burst, Kazuha Slash. With a cost of only 60 and the ability to generate a high amount of particles via his Skill (especially with sacrificial or Favonius weapons), he can maintain 100% uptime with minimal Energy Recharge (ER) investment. Sucrose, however, struggles with an 80-cost Burst. While her C1 provides an extra skill charge to help with battery needs, she often requires 160-180% ER to burst consistently every rotation.

## Detailed Comparison

![Detailed Comparison](/img/kazuha-vs-sucrose-support-comparison/inline-3.webp)


### Buffing Math: EM vs. Elemental DMG%
The value of Kazuha vs. Sucrose often comes down to the "Damage Formula." Kazuha provides Elemental DMG%, which is added to your character's Goblet and other DMG% bonuses. Sucrose provides Elemental Mastery, which increases the multiplier of reactions like Vaporize, Melt, and Aggravate.

At 1,000 EM
*   **Kazuha** provides a 40% DMG bonus. For a character like Neuvillette, who already has high built-in DMG bonuses, this 40% is still a significant multiplicative increase to his total output.
*   **Sucrose** provides approximately 250 EM. If your Xiangling has 0 EM, that 250 EM increases her Vaporize damage by roughly 42%. However, if your Xiangling already has 300 EM, adding another 250 EM provides diminishing returns, increasing the reaction damage by a much smaller relative percentage.

Because of this, Kazuha is generally more "future-proof" as he scales with any character that deals Elemental damage, whereas Sucrose’s value is tied strictly to the reaction math of the specific team.

### The TTDS Factor
One of the biggest arguments for Sucrose is her status as a Catalyst user. She can equip **Thrilling Tales of Dragon Slayers (TTDS)**, a 3-star weapon that provides a 48% ATK buff to the next character swapped in. 

Kazuha cannot replicate this without his signature 5-star weapon, **Freedom-Sworn**, which provides a 20% ATK buff and 16% Normal/Charged/Plunge DMG bonus. Even then, a R5 TTDS on Sucrose provides more raw ATK than a R1 Freedom-Sworn. In teams where the main DPS scales heavily off ATK (like Navia or Diluc), Sucrose with TTDS can often result in higher screenshot damage than Kazuha.

### Crowd Control and Grouping Efficiency
The mechanical difference between their grouping is stark. Kazuha’s **Chihayaburu** pulls enemies toward his current position. This allows the player to dash into the center of a mob and instantly consolidate them for a follow-up attack. The vertical lift also provides a brief moment of safety from ground-based attacks.

Sucrose’s **Astable Anemohypostasis Creation - 6308** (Skill) and **Forbidden Creation - Isomer 75 / Type II** (Burst) launch enemies or pull them toward a target location. The issue is that Sucrose’s Burst often "juggles" enemies, bouncing them up and down. For melee characters like Hu Tao or Ayaka, this can actually be a hindrance, as it causes attacks to miss. Kazuha’s grouping is "flatter," keeping enemies tightly packed on the ground where they can be hit easily.

### Double Swirling
In high-level play, "Double Swirling" is the act of shredding the resistances of two different elements (e.g., Hydro and Pyro) and buffing both simultaneously. Kazuha is the undisputed king of this mechanic. Because his Elemental Burst prioritizes Pyro infusion, and his Skill can swirl an element already present on the enemy while his Plunge swirls the element he is currently infused with, he can buff a Childe/Xiangling "International" team with near-perfect efficiency.

Sucrose can also double swirl, but it is much more difficult to execute. It often requires specific timing with her Normal Attacks and a precise sequence of Skill uses that is easily disrupted by enemy movement or staggered hits.

### Constellation Value: C0 Kazuha vs. C6 Sucrose
A common question is whether a C6 Sucrose is better than a C0 Kazuha. 
*   **Sucrose C6** adds a 20% Elemental DMG bonus to the element infused in her Burst. This brings her closer to Kazuha’s buffing profile.
*   **Kazuha C2** is one of the strongest constellations in the game, providing 200 EM to himself and the active character within his Burst field. 

At C0 for both, Sucrose is often better for reactions. At C6 Sucrose vs. C0 Kazuha, the gap closes significantly, and Sucrose may win in ATK-scaling reaction teams. However, once Kazuha hits C2, he provides both the massive DMG% bonus and a flat 200 EM, effectively rendering Sucrose obsolete in almost every team except those requiring a dedicated Catalyst driver.

### 2026 Meta Shift
As of the mid-2026 meta, the introduction of Natlan characters like **Mavuika** and **Xilonen** has changed how we view Anemo supports. Mavuika, in particular, benefits immensely from Kazuha's ability to swirl Pyro reliably in high-pressure scenarios.

## Recommendation

![Recommendation](/img/kazuha-vs-sucrose-support-comparison/inline-4.webp)


### Best Teams for Kazuha
*   **Neuvillette Hypercarry:** Kazuha is essential here for the Hydro DMG buff and the ease of swirling Hydro while staying mobile.
*   **Ayaka/Ganyu Freeze:** His grouping is necessary to keep enemies inside Ayaka’s Burst or Ganyu’s icicles.
*   **Arlecchino / Lyney Mono-Pyro:** Kazuha provides the massive Pyro DMG% bonus that these characters lack in their base kits.
*   **Raiden Hypercarry:** The combination of Kazuha's DMG buff and Bennett's ATK buff maximizes Raiden's initial Slash.

### Best Teams for Sucrose
*   **National Variants (Xiangling/Bennett/Xingqiu):** Sucrose’s EM sharing is vital for Xiangling’s Vaporize hits.
*   **Taser (Beidou/Fischl/Xingqiu):** Sucrose acts as the "driver," using her Normal Attacks to trigger Swirls and off-field abilities constantly.
*   **Aggravate (Keqing/Fischl/Nahida):** EM is highly valuable in Quicken teams, and Sucrose provides it in spades.
*   **Budget Reaction Comps:** Featuring [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-14], where Sucrose’s F2P accessibility shines.

### Final Account Value Verdict
If you are a Free-to-Play player with a limited number of Primogems, a **C6 Sucrose** is more than "good enough" to clear the hardest content in the game, including Floor 12 of the Spiral Abyss. You do not *need* Kazuha if your Sucrose is well-built and you are comfortable with her clunkier targeting.

However, **Kaedehara Kazuha** remains the single best quality-of-life upgrade for any Genshin Impact account. His grouping makes daily commissions, domain farming, and elite boss fights significantly faster and more enjoyable. He is more versatile, easier to build, and fits into more top-tier "meta" teams than Sucrose.

**Verdict:** Kazuha wins for versatility, comfort, and high-floor performance. Sucrose wins for specific high-ceiling reaction damage and F2P accessibility.

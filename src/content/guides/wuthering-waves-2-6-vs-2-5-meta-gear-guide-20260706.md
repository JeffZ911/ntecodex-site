While these products offer top-tier performance, they aren't necessary for every Appraiser. You should consider skipping these upgrades in the following scenarios:

*   **Existing 1440p IPS Users:** If you currently own a 1440p IPS monitor with 144Hz, the jump to OLED is beautiful but won't fundamentally change your performance in the 2.6 meta. The visual upgrade is a luxury rather than a competitive necessity.
*   **Controller Players:** Avoid upgrading your mouse if you primarily play on a controller; the macro benefits of the Naga V2 Pro are lost in that ecosystem. NTE's controller support is robust enough that a standard high-quality gamepad often suffices.
*   **Portable Setups:** Do not buy the Keychron Q1 Pro if you need a portable setup or frequently play on your lap. Its 4-pound weight makes it a permanent desk fixture that is difficult to move.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How does the gacha pity system work in Neverness to Everness?
The NTE gacha system, known as "The Fair," features a 90-pull hard pity for a guaranteed S-rank character. A unique board modification mechanic triggers at 70 pulls, which significantly increases the S-rank acquisition rate to 19.59%.

### What are Arcs and how do they impact the meta?
Arcs are the primary weapon system in NTE, providing both stat boosts and unique passive effects. To maximize their power, players must engage in "Mixing," which involves using duplicates to increase an Arc's Mixing Intensity.

### Is Nanally still a top-tier character in the 2.6 meta?
While there has been community discussion regarding powercreep, Nanally remains a high-damage powerhouse. Her follow-up attack archetype continues to be a viable strategy for clearing endgame content in Hethereau.

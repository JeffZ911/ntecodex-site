## Best Weapons

![Best Weapons](/img/best-silas-build-neverness-to-everness/inline-2.webp)


In Neverness to Everness, weapons are known as Arcs. These are not merely stat sticks; they provide "Arc Abilities" that can summon assistants, trigger area-of-effect (AoE) explosions, or provide massive stat boosts during specific combat windows. If Silas were to be released as a main DPS, he would likely benefit from the top-tier Arcs currently available in the 1.0 meta.

### Best S-Rank Arcs

The following S-Rank Arcs are the most powerful options for damage-focused characters in the current game version.

**Ready-Ready (Plasma)**
This Arc is widely considered one of the best for Plasma-compatible damage dealers. It provides a significant boost to ATK and Crit Rate. Its unique effect enhances basic attacks for a duration after the wearer casts their Ultimate, making it ideal for characters who stay on the field to deal sustained damage.

**Camellia Society (Synthesis)**
A high-risk, high-reward weapon often obtained through the "Where Worlds Sprout, Fall Unfolds" side quest. It features a base ATK of 666 at level 1 and a Crit Rate substat. Its passive effect, "Silent Garden," grants stacks of CRIT DMG whenever the wearer's HP is reduced without taking direct damage (such as through life-drain mechanics). It is a niche but powerful choice for characters with self-depleting HP kits.

**Day Off (Solid)**
Day Off is a versatile Solid-type Arc that focuses on Charge Efficiency. Its primary draw is the "Eclipse" ability, which grants a flat 30% ATK boost and restores Ultimate Energy whenever an enemy is defeated. This makes it the premier choice for clearing waves of enemies in the "Beyond the Rails" endgame mode.

### Best A-Rank and F2P Arcs

For players who have not yet pulled S-Rank weapons from the gacha board game, several A-Rank Arcs provide excellent value.

*   **Failing You, Heavy in My Heart:** A reliable choice for burst-heavy characters, providing a significant DMG boost against enemies with high health.
*   **Youthful Fantasy:** Often rewarded from Anomaly Commissions, this Arc provides a steady increase in elemental damage that scales with the character's level.
*   **Reality Refuge:** A solid defensive option for characters who need a mix of survivability and utility.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

![Best Disks (Artifacts)](/img/best-silas-build-neverness-to-everness/inline-3.webp)


In NTE, the gear system consists of Cartridges and Modules. A character's Console can hold one Cartridge, which requires specific Modules to be slotted in to activate 2-piece and 4-piece set bonuses.

### Top Cartridge Sets

The current meta favors sets that provide unconditional ATK boosts or defense penetration.

**Shadow Creed (4-Piece)**
The Shadow Creed set is the gold standard for DPS units in Version 1.0. 
*   **2-Piece Bonus:** ATK +10%
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** Increases the wearer's ATK by 25% for 20 seconds after casting a Skill.
This set is highly favored because the 20-second window is long enough to cover most character rotations, ensuring near-permanent uptime on the damage buff.

**Lost Radiance / Legendary Set (4-Piece)**
Commonly referred to as the "Legendary Set" in community guides, Lost Radiance is the best choice for characters who rely on their Ultimate to deal damage.
*   **2-Piece Bonus:** Cosmos DMG +10%
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** Ignores 25% of enemy DEF for 20 seconds after casting an Ultimate.
Defense shred is a rare and valuable stat in NTE, especially when facing high-level bosses in Anomaly Commissions.

**Crimson: Twin Butterflies (4-Piece)**
This is the premier set for Incantation-based teams.
*   **2-Piece Bonus:** Incantation DMG +10%
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** Increases ATK by 6% per stack (up to 6 stacks) when nearby enemies take Incantation damage. This effect works even when the wearer is off-field, making it perfect for sub-DPS units.

### Recommended Main and Sub-Stats

While Silas's specific scaling is [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-17], most damage dealers in NTE follow a standard stat priority:

1.  **Main Stat (Cartridge):** Elemental DMG Bonus (matching the character's attribute) or ATK%.
2.  **Main Stat (Modules):** Flat ATK and Flat HP (these are fixed based on the Module type).
3.  **Sub-stats:** CRIT Rate > CRIT DMG > ATK% > Charge Efficiency.

In the current endgame, reaching a 1:2 ratio of CRIT Rate to CRIT DMG is essential for clearing the highest floors of the city’s challenge modes.

## Team Comp

![Team Comp](/img/best-silas-build-neverness-to-everness/inline-4.webp)


NTE combat revolves around the Esper Cycle, a system where different elemental attributes react to create powerful effects. Building a team around a main DPS requires supports that can trigger these reactions and provide buffs.

### Premium Team Composition

A high-end team in the current 1.0/1.1 meta typically consists of one Main DPS, two Supports/Sub-DPS, and one Survival unit.

*   **Main DPS:** [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-17] (Currently filled by Nanally or Jiuyuan).

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/best-silas-build-neverness-to-everness/inline-5.webp)


Maximizing damage in Neverness to Everness requires a strict rotation to ensure that all buffs are active when your Main DPS takes the field.

### Optimal Skill Sequence

While Silas's specific sequence is [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-17], the standard high-damage rotation in NTE follows this flow:

1.  **Initiate with Supports:** Start with a character like Sakiri or Haniel. Use their Skills to apply elemental status effects to the enemies.
2.  **Trigger the Esper Cycle:** Swap to your second support (e.g., Adler) to trigger a reaction. This will often drop the enemy's "Break" bar or apply a significant debuff.
3.  **Cast Ultimates:** Use your support Ultimates to activate team-wide buffs (such as the ATK boost from Sakiri).
4.  **Switch to Main DPS:** Bring in your primary damage dealer once the enemy is in a "Staggered" state or when all buffs are active. 
5.  **Unleash Burst:** Use the Main DPS's Skill and Ultimate while the Shadow Creed or Lost Radiance set bonuses are active.

### Maximizing the Esper Cycle

The Esper Cycle is the heart of NTE’s combat. By combining attributes like Incantation and Chaos, players can trigger "Scorch," which deals massive damage over time. Combining Cosmos and Anima can lead to "Stain," which slows enemies and increases the damage they take. Understanding these interactions is more important than raw stats when tackling the game's hardest challenges.

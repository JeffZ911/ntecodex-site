# Neverness to Everness Weapon Upgrade Guide: Best Arcs & Builds

Mastering the weapon systems in Neverness to Everness (NTE) is the primary hurdle for any Appraiser looking to tackle high-level Anomalies. Unlike traditional RPGs where weapons are simple stat sticks, NTE utilizes "Arcs"—sophisticated equippable devices that fundamentally alter how an Esper interacts with the Esper Cycle. This guide breaks down how to upgrade your Arcs, the best Disk sets to pair them with, and the meta builds currently dominating the 2026 landscape.

## Overview

![Overview](/img/neverness-to-everness-weapon-upgrade-guide/inline-1.webp)


Weapons in NTE are officially known as Arcs. Every Esper can equip one Arc, which provides three distinct layers of power: a Base ATK value, a secondary substat (such as CRIT Rate or Charge Efficiency), and a unique Arc Skill. These skills often provide transformative buffs that define a character's role within a team.

Arcs are categorized into five primary types that dictate their combat focus. Matching the correct Arc type to your character's combat style is essential for maximizing synergy.

### Understanding Arc Types

| Arc Type | Primary Combat Focus | Typical User Role |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Plasma** | Offensive Burst & ATK Stacking | Main DPS / Carry |
| **Solid** | Balanced Stats & Durability | Sub-DPS / Bruiser |
| **Gas** | Utility, Buffing, & Crowd Control | Support / Buffer |
| **Liquid** | Energy Regeneration & Skill Spam | Quick-swap / Battery |
| **Synthesis** | Hybrid Scaling & High-Risk/Reward | Specialized DPS |

### Enhancement vs. Ascension vs. Mixing

The upgrade system for Arcs is built upon three pillars. Neglecting any of these will leave your Esper underpowered in late-game zones like the Nautily Tunnel.

1.  **Enhancement (Leveling):** This is the basic process of increasing an Arc’s level to boost its Base ATK and substat. This requires **Dye** (available in rarities like Light, Colorless, and Chaotic) and **Beetle Coins**. The primary farming location for Dye is the **Kaleidoscope** stage within **Houdinii's Magic Stage** (located in the Bridge Crossings district).
2.  **Ascension (Increasing Level Caps):** Arcs hit a level ceiling every 10 to 20 levels. To break this cap, you must perform an Ascension. This currently allows Arcs to reach a maximum level of 80. Ascension requires specialized materials like **Golden Appleseed**, **Harmony**, and **Liquid Dream Cans**, which are primarily farmed at the **Bubble Can Factory**.
3.  **Mixing (Refinement):** Mixing involves consuming duplicate copies of the same Arc to increase its "Mixing Intensity" (ranked T1 through T5). Each rank significantly enhances the numerical values of the Arc’s passive skill.

## Best Weapons

![Best Weapons](/img/neverness-to-everness-weapon-upgrade-guide/inline-2.webp)


Choosing the right Arc depends on whether you are building a dedicated carry like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or a cornerstone support like [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide). While signature Arcs are generally best-in-slot (BiS), several farmable and F2P alternatives provide competitive performance.

### Top S-Rank Arcs for DPS

*   **Ready-Ready (Plasma):** This is the signature Arc for Nanally, obtainable through "The Ichi-daime" banner. It provides massive ATK% bonuses and a passive that triggers off-field follow-up damage. It is widely considered the gold standard for boss-killing due to its consistent damage scaling.
*   **Camellia Society (Synthesis):** The signature weapon for Baicang. It revolves around a high-risk mechanic called "Silent Garden," which grants stacking CRIT DMG when the user loses HP without taking direct damage. It is the premier choice for Scorch-based team compositions.
*   **Song of the Whale (Solid/Plasma):** An exceptional S-Rank Arc that can be obtained for free by defeating the **Sea Prisoner** anomaly in the Nautily Tunnel. It increases damage against broken enemies by 12% and provides HP restoration, making it a top-tier F2P choice for Nanally.

### Best Support and Utility Arcs

*   **Good Boy's Grand Adventure (Gas):** The undisputed best-in-slot for Sakiri. It offers an 18% increase to Charge Efficiency and grants the entire team a 16% ATK buff for 20 seconds after the wearer uses an Ultimate. Because Sakiri’s Ultimate naturally suppresses enemies, she triggers the secondary buff conditions of this Arc automatically.
*   **Blow Up the Crowd (Solid):** Haniel’s signature Arc. It focuses on providing team-wide ATK buffs and is highly versatile for quick-swap teams that rely on frequent character rotations.

### Best F2P Weapons for Beginners

*   **Day Off (Solid):** A powerful S-Rank Arc that is farmable through world exploration and Hunter Exchange. It is a reliable choice for the Protagonist (Zero) due to its balanced stat spread.
*   **Hethereau's Keeper (Solid):** Another farmable S-Rank option that excels in sustain-heavy teams. It provides defensive utility that helps beginners survive early encounters with field bosses like Morphix.
*   **Umbrella (B-Rank/A-Rank):** While Adler uses a signature S-Rank version, the lower-tier Umbrella Arcs are excellent early-game picks for survivability, providing flat HP boosts and shield strength.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

![Best Disks (Artifacts)](/img/neverness-to-everness-weapon-upgrade-guide/inline-3.webp)


In NTE, the "Disk" system refers to the combination of **Consoles** and **Cartridges**. These function as the game's artifact system, where Modules are slotted into a grid to activate set bonuses.

### Console and Cartridge Mechanics

Every character has a Console board. By equipping Cartridges of the same set, you activate 2-piece and 4-piece bonuses. The rarity of the Modules (Type I, II, or III) determines the strength of the individual stat rolls, but the set bonus is determined by the Cartridge type.

### Recommended Sets for Every Role

*   **Speedy Hedgehog Set:** The current meta-defining set for supports.
    *   **2-Piece Bonus:** +12% Charge Efficiency.
    *   **4-Piece Bonus:** Increases all allies' ATK by 15% for 20 seconds after the wearer casts their Ultimate. This is essential for Sakiri and Haniel.
*   **Fireflies and the Forest Set:** The premier choice for Anima DPS characters like Nanally.
    *   **Effect:** Increases Anima DMG and grants stacking CRIT DMG whenever the team deals Anima damage to a nearby enemy.
*   **Incantation DMG Set (Crimson Twin Butterflies):** Essential for Baicang or Adler to maximize the damage of the Scorch reaction.
*   **Shadow Crit:** A universal 2-piece filler set that provides flat CRIT Rate, useful for any DPS struggling to reach a 1:2 CRIT ratio.

## Team Comp

![Team Comp](/img/neverness-to-everness-weapon-upgrade-guide/inline-4.webp)


Combat in NTE is built around the **Esper Cycle**, a hexagonal elemental wheel. Reactions occur only between adjacent elements. Understanding these interactions is the difference between struggling against trash mobs and shredding elite bosses.

### The Esper Cycle Explained

There are six elements: **Lakshana (Order), Cosmos (Light), Anima (Life), Incantation (Force), Chaos (Dark), and Psyche (Mind).**

*   **Duo Reactions:** Triggered by swapping between two adjacent elements with full Esper Meters.
    *   **Scorch (Incantation + Chaos):** Deals massive damage-over-time (DoT).
    *   **Blossom (Cosmos + Anima):** Summons a turret that deals autonomous AoE damage.
    *   **Hexed (Anima + Incantation):** Records damage for 12 seconds and then detonates for a percentage of the total.
*   **Triple Cycle (Discord):** Triggered by combining the bottom trio (Incantation, Chaos, and Psyche). Discord deals massive Break/Stagger damage, instantly depleting boss shield bars.

### Meta Team Archetypes

1.  **Scorch/Discord Meta:**
    *   **Team:** Baicang (Main DPS), Sakiri (Support), Daffodil (Enabler/Break), Haniel (Support).
    *   **Focus:** This team focuses on the bottom half of the Esper Cycle. Baicang and Sakiri trigger Scorch, while Daffodil provides the Break shred necessary to trigger Discord.
2.  **Blossom/Hexed Team:**
    *   **Team:** Nanally (Main DPS), Zero (Sub-DPS/Enabler), Jiuyuan (Support), Sakiri (Buffer).
    *   **Focus:** This team leverages the top half of the wheel. Nanally and Zero trigger Blossom turrets, while Sakiri acts as a flex buffer to increase Nanally’s raw ATK.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/neverness-to-everness-weapon-upgrade-guide/inline-5.webp)


A proper rotation ensures that your buffs from Arcs (like Good Boy's Grand Adventure) and Disks (like Speedy Hedgehog) are active during your Main DPS's burst window.

### General Combat Flow

The standard flow follows this sequence: **Buffer -> Enabler -> Main DPS.**

1.  **Buffer:** Start with a character like Sakiri. Use her Skill to group enemies and her Ultimate to trigger the Speedy Hedgehog and Gas-type Arc buffs.
2.  **Enabler:** Swap to an enabler like Zero or Daffodil to fill the Esper Meter and prime the elemental reaction.
3.  **Main DPS:** Swap to Nanally or Baicang. Their "Esper Ability" will trigger automatically upon entry, detonating the primed reaction (Blossom or Scorch) while all ATK buffs are at their peak.

### Character-Specific Rotation Tips

*   **Sakiri Support Rotation:** Press E (Skill) to pull enemies -> Q (Ultimate) to lock them and activate team buffs -> Immediately swap to DPS. If you have her **Sensory Collapse** Awakening, launching an enemy with her skill will instantly reset her Ultimate, allowing for double buff windows.
*   **[Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte) "Temporal Nuke" Rotation:** Hotori relies on "Recording" damage. Use Zero to instant-charge the Esper Meter -> Hotori Skill (to begin recording) -> Swap through team supports to dump high-damage skills -> Swap back to Hotori for her Ultimate (Execution) to detonate the accumulated damage.
*   **Stagger Windows:** Always save your most powerful Plasma Arc skills for when the enemy’s Break Gauge is depleted. Enemies take significantly increased damage during the "Stagger" state.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/neverness-to-everness-weapon-upgrade-guide/inline-6.webp)


**What is the max level for Arcs?**
As of the current version, Arcs cap at level 80. This requires multiple Ascensions using materials from the Bubble Can Factory.

**Where do I farm Dye?**
The most efficient source is the Kaleidoscope stage within Houdinii's Magic Stage, located in the Bridge Crossings district. You can also obtain limited quantities from the Hunter Exchange at Tycoon Rank 6.

**How do I get free S-Rank Arcs?**
Several S-Rank Arcs are available through gameplay. **Song of the Whale** is a reward for the Sea Prisoner boss, and **Day Off** is often available through high-level Hunter Exchange milestones or field boss drops like Morphix.

**What are Character Pixels?**
Character Pixels are the stamina resource in Neverness to Everness. You must spend these to claim rewards from Anomaly Zones (Dungeons). They regenerate over time, so ensure you are spending them daily on Dye or Ascension materials.

**Does the Speedy Hedgehog 4-piece bonus stack?**
No. If two characters on the same team are wearing the 4-piece Speedy Hedgehog set, the 15% ATK buff will not double. It is better to put Speedy Hedgehog on one support and a set like **Lost Radiance** or **Crimson Twin Butterflies** on the other.

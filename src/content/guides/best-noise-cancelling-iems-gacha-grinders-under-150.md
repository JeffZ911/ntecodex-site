## What a competitive gacha grinder actually needs

![What a competitive gacha grinder actually needs](/img/best-noise-cancelling-iems-gacha-grinders-under-150/inline-2.webp)


Gacha gaming is a marathon, not a sprint. Whether you are farming the latest limited-time event or just doing your daily chores in Hethereau, your equipment needs to support long-term use.

### Passive Isolation vs. ANC
Wired IEMs, like the Etymotic ER2XR, rely on **Passive Isolation**. This is essentially a physical barrier that blocks sound. It is superior for high-frequency noises like people talking or the click of a mouse. **Active Noise Cancelling (ANC)**, found in the Soundcore Liberty 4 NC, uses electronics to cancel out low-frequency hums like an air conditioner or a plane engine. If you play mostly at home with a fan running, ANC is better. If you play in a public space with people talking, high passive isolation is more effective.

### Nozzle Diameter and Comfort
The "nozzle" is the part of the earbud where the silicone tip attaches. A wide nozzle (like the 6.7mm nozzle on the Truthear Zero) can cause "stretching" pain in the ear canal after two hours of play. If you have smaller ears, prioritizing a model like the Sennheiser IE 100 PRO will prevent the soreness that often ends a grinding session prematurely.

### Cable Microphonics
For players who move around while they play—perhaps pacing during a difficult boss fight—cable noise (microphonics) can be a major distraction. A cable that rubs against your shirt and sends a "thumping" sound into your ear ruins immersion. High-quality braided cables, like those found on the Moondrop Aria 2, help mitigate this issue.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need a DAC or Amp to use these IEMs for gaming?
Most of the IEMs on this list, such as the Moondrop Aria 2 and Truthear Zero: RED, have low impedance and high sensitivity, meaning they can be driven easily by a standard phone dongle or a motherboard's audio jack. However, a dedicated DAC like the Moondrop Dawn Pro can reduce background hiss and provide a cleaner signal for competitive play.

### Can I use these IEMs for voice chat in games like Neverness to Everness?
Most audiophile-grade IEMs do not come with a built-in microphone on the cable. If you plan to coordinate with friends in multiplayer modes, you will likely need to purchase a replacement cable with an inline mic or use a dedicated desktop microphone.

### Are wired IEMs better than wireless earbuds for gacha games?
Wired IEMs offer zero latency, which is crucial for rhythm-based mini-games or precise combat timing. Wireless earbuds like the Soundcore Liberty 4 NC are more convenient for commuting but may have a slight delay unless they support low-latency modes or high-end codecs like LDAC.

### How do I find the right ear tip size for long sessions?
Most IEMs include small, medium, and large silicone tips. For the best noise isolation, you need a complete seal. If the bass sounds thin or you can hear outside conversations clearly, try a larger tip. For long-term comfort, memory foam tips can be a great aftermarket upgrade as they mold to the shape of your ear canal.

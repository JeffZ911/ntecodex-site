## What an NTE player actually needs

![What an NTE player actually needs](/img/nte-mobile-vs-pc-graphics-comparison/inline-2.webp)


The urban environment of NTE is exceptionally dense, filled with reflective surfaces and intricate architectural details. To avoid aliasing artifacts and "shimmering" on screen, a monitor with a pixel density of at least 100 PPI (Pixels Per Inch) is necessary. This ensures that the fine lines of Hesperia's skyscrapers and the subtle particle effects of a character's Resonance Skill remain sharp.

Beyond visuals, physical health is a major concern for those spending 4+ hours daily in-game. Long-session grinding often leads to "gamer lean," where players hunch forward toward their screens. Active lumbar support is required to mitigate this, providing constant pressure to the lower back even as you shift positions. Furthermore, low-latency audio is vital for reacting to the fast-paced combat encounters against supernatural Anomalies. We selected gear that avoids the significant lag—often exceeding 200ms—found in many budget wireless audio solutions, ensuring your audio cues sync perfectly with the visual action.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/nte-mobile-vs-pc-graphics-comparison/inline-4.webp)


While high-end gear can significantly enhance your experience, it is not always a necessary purchase. If you primarily play NTE on a flagship tablet, such as an iPad Pro with an M4 chip, a portable monitor upgrade is likely redundant. The pixel density and peak brightness of high-end tablets already exceed those of most portable displays, providing a crisp image that is difficult to improve upon without moving to a full desktop setup.

Additionally, investing in a high-DPI mouse like the Razer Naga V2 Pro is unnecessary if you only play on mobile devices. The touch-to-mouse translation in most mobile emulators or casting software is often too jittery to take advantage of a 30,000 DPI sensor. Finally, check your PC's internal specs before buying a 1440p monitor. If your system lacks a dedicated GPU—at least a GTX 1660 or higher—running the game at 1440p will likely degrade your experience by forcing you to lower settings or deal with inconsistent frame rates.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I play Neverness to Everness on both PC and mobile with the same account?
Yes, Neverness to Everness supports full cross-save functionality, allowing you to switch between your PC and mobile devices without losing progress. This makes it easy to handle your daily business management tasks on your phone while saving major story chapters and high-stakes combat for your PC.

### What are the minimum PC requirements to run NTE with Ray Tracing?
To enable Ray-Traced Reflections in Hesperia, you generally need a GPU that supports hardware-level ray tracing, such as an NVIDIA RTX 20-series or higher. Using DLSS 3 can significantly help maintain a stable frame rate when these intensive lighting effects are active.

### Is a controller or a mouse and keyboard better for NTE combat?
This largely depends on your playstyle, but many players prefer a mouse and keyboard on PC to map the numerous skills of characters like Nanally. A multi-button mouse allows for quick access to menus and car customization options that might feel slower when using a traditional controller or touch screen gestures.

### Does the mobile version of NTE include the same map as the PC version?
The map of Hesperia is identical across both platforms, featuring the same urban districts and supernatural anomaly locations. However, the mobile version uses reduced draw distances and simplified geometry to ensure the game runs smoothly on portable hardware.

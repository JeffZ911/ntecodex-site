# Blade Best Build in Honkai: Star Rail: The Ultimate Guide

Blade is a powerhouse of the Destruction Path who thrives on the edge of death. Unlike traditional damage dealers who stack Attack (ATK), Blade scales almost entirely off his Maximum HP. This makes him naturally tanky while allowing him to output massive Wind damage to multiple targets simultaneously. Whether you are using him as a primary hypercarry or a sub-DPS alongside another heavy hitter, understanding his HP-consumption mechanics is vital for maximizing his potential.

## Overview

![Overview](/img/blade-best-build-guide-honkai-star-rail/inline-1.webp)


Blade’s kit is designed around a high-risk, high-reward playstyle. By consuming his own health, he enters an enhanced state that transforms his Basic Attack into a powerful AoE (Area of Effect) strike. Because his damage scales primarily with HP, building him requires a different mindset compared to characters like Seele or Jing Yuan.

### Blade's Role in the Meta
Blade occupies a flexible spot in the Honkai: Star Rail meta. He is one of the few DPS characters who does not heavily rely on Skill Points (SP), as his Skill only needs to be activated once every three turns. This SP efficiency allows him to be paired with SP-hungry supports or even other DPS characters. His ability to self-heal through his Talent and Ultimate makes him exceptionally durable in difficult content like Memory of Chaos and Pure Fiction.

### Key Mechanics: Hellscape and Shuhu's Gift
Blade's gameplay revolves around two major components: his Skill and his Talent.

1.  **Hellscape (Skill):** Activating his Skill consumes 30% of Blade's Max HP to enter the "Hellscape" state. This state lasts for three turns, during which his DMG is increased, and his Basic Attack is transformed into "Forest of Swords." Crucially, using this Skill does not end his turn, allowing for immediate aggression.
2.  **Shuhu's Gift (Talent):** Every time Blade takes damage or consumes his own HP, he gains one stack. Upon reaching five stacks, he immediately launches a follow-up attack on all enemies, dealing Wind damage based on his ATK and Max HP, while also restoring 25% of his Max HP.

Choosing the right Light Cone for Blade is specific because many Destruction weapons provide ATK% buffs, which are inefficient for him. You should prioritize Light Cones that offer HP, Crit Rate, Crit DMG, or general DMG% increases.

### Top 5-Star Light Cones

1.  **The Unreachable Side (Signature):** This is Blade's best-in-slot weapon by a wide margin. It provides an 18% Crit Rate boost and increases Max HP by 18%. Furthermore, whenever the wearer is attacked or consumes their own HP, their DMG is increased by 24%. This perfectly aligns with every aspect of Blade’s kit.
2.  **Something Irreplaceable (Clara's Signature):** While the ATK% buff is largely wasted on Blade, the 24% DMG increase and the HP restoration upon being hit or defeating an enemy provide decent utility and survivability.

2.  **Mutual Demise (3-Star):** For early-game players, this is a hidden gem. At S5, it increases Crit Rate by 24% whenever the wearer's HP is below 80%. Given Blade's mechanics, this condition is almost always met.

### Optimal Relic Sets

The **Longevous Disciple (4-pc)** is the definitive relic set for Blade.
*   **2-pc Bonus:** Increases Max HP by 12%.
*   **4-pc Bonus:** When the wearer is hit or has their HP consumed by themselves or an ally, their Crit Rate increases by 8% for 2 turns, up to 2 stacks (16% total).
Blade triggers the 4-pc bonus effortlessly, allowing you to focus your sub-stats more heavily on Crit DMG.

### Planar Ornaments Selection

1.  **Inert Salsotto:** This is generally considered the best choice for Blade. It provides an 8% Crit Rate boost and, once the wearer reaches 50% Crit Rate, increases Ultimate and follow-up attack DMG by 15%. Since a large portion of Blade's total damage comes from his Talent and Ultimate, this is highly effective.
2.  **Rutilant Arena:** This set is a strong alternative if you prefer to maximize his Enhanced Basic Attack damage. It provides 8% Crit Rate and increases Basic ATK and Skill DMG by 20% if Crit Rate reaches 70%.

## Team Comp

![Team Comp](/img/blade-best-build-guide-honkai-star-rail/inline-4.webp)


Blade requires specific teammates to shine. He needs healers rather than shielders, and he benefits from supports who can buff his DMG and Speed rather than his ATK.

### The Hypercarry Setup
In a hypercarry team, all resources are funneled into Blade.
*   **Support 1 (Bronya):** Bronya is Blade's best partner. Her Skill advances his turn, allowing him to attack twice in one cycle, and her Ultimate provides massive Crit DMG buffs. Since Blade uses very few Skill Points, Bronya can use her Skill every turn without draining the team's SP.
*   **Support 2 (Pela / Silver Wolf):** Since Blade doesn't scale well with ATK buffs (like those from Tingyun), Defense Shred is the most effective way to boost his damage. Pela provides AoE DEF reduction, while Silver Wolf is excellent for single-target encounters.
*   **Sustain (Luocha / Lynx):** Luocha is the gold standard for Blade. His passive healing field triggers when Blade attacks, keeping him healthy without using Skill Points. Lynx is the best F2P option because her Skill increases Blade's Max HP and his Taunt value, causing enemies to hit him more often and stack his Talent faster.

### The Blade-Jingliu Synergy
One of the most popular dual-DPS compositions involves pairing Blade with Jingliu. When Jingliu is in her "Spectral Transmigration" state, her attacks consume a portion of her allies' HP. For Blade, this counts as a stack for his "Shuhu's Gift" talent. This allows Blade to trigger his follow-up attacks much more frequently than in a standard team.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/blade-best-build-guide-honkai-star-rail/inline-5.webp)


Mastering Blade’s rotation ensures that you maintain his enhanced state while maximizing the damage of his Ultimate.

### Optimal Skill Usage
Blade’s rotation is relatively simple but requires attention to his buff timer.
1.  **Turn 1:** Use Blade's Skill (Hellscape). This consumes HP and enhances his Basic Attack for 3 turns. Immediately use his Enhanced Basic Attack (Forest of Swords).
2.  **Turns 2-3:** Use Enhanced Basic Attacks to generate energy and build Talent stacks.
3.  **Turn 4:** Re-apply the Skill once the Hellscape state expires.

### Maximizing Ultimate Damage
Blade’s Ultimate, **Death Sentence**, sets his current HP to 50% of his Max HP. The damage it deals scales with his ATK, Max HP, and the *total amount of HP he has lost during the battle* (capped at 90% of his Max HP).
*   **Tip:** Do not use Blade’s Ultimate immediately if he is at full health and hasn't taken much damage yet. Wait until he has consumed his HP several times or been hit by enemies to ensure you have reached the "HP lost" cap for maximum burst damage.
*   **Emergency Heal:** If Blade is dangerously low on HP (e.g., 5%), using his Ultimate will actually *increase* his health back to 50%, serving as a life-saving mechanic.

### Speed Tuning with Bronya
To achieve the highest possible damage floor, players often utilize "Speed Tuning."
*   **The 135/134 Strategy:** Aim for Blade to have 135 Speed and Bronya to have 134 Speed.
*   **Sequence:** Blade acts first -> Bronya acts immediately after and uses her Skill on Blade -> Blade acts again.
This allows Blade to attack twice per cycle, doubling his damage output and his Talent stack generation.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/blade-best-build-guide-honkai-star-rail/inline-6.webp)


### Common Build Questions

**Should I use HP% or Speed boots?**
If you are running Blade with a "Hyperspeed" Sparkle or Bronya (160+ Speed), you should use HP% boots to maximize his damage per hit. However, for most general builds and the 135/134 tuning strategy, Speed boots are mandatory to reach the necessary breakpoints.

**Can I use Tingyun with Blade?**
It is not recommended. Tingyun's primary value is her massive ATK buff, but because Blade’s ATK scaling is very low, he gains significantly less benefit from her than characters like Jing Yuan or Dan Heng - Imbibitor Lunae. Ruan Mei, Bronya, or Pela are much better choices.

**What is the 'HP Lost' cap for his Ultimate?**
The Ultimate tracks the total HP Blade has lost, up to 90% of his Max HP. Once you use the Ultimate, this counter resets. To maximize damage, you want to ensure Blade has lost a total amount of HP equal to 90% of his maximum health pool between each Ultimate cast.

### Eidolon Value Analysis
*   **E1 (Blade Cuts the Ground):** Significantly increases the damage dealt to the primary target of his Ultimate. This makes Blade much more effective against bosses.
*   **E2 (Ten Thousand Sorrows):** Increases Crit Rate by 15% while in the Hellscape state. This makes building his relics much easier.
*   **E6 (Labor of Love):** Reduces the stacks required for his follow-up attack from five down to four and increases the damage of the follow-up attack. This is a massive DPS increase and changes his playstyle to be even more aggressive.

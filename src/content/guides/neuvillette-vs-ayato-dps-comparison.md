Choosing between the two most prominent "Hydro Husbandos" in Genshin Impact often comes down to a choice between overwhelming raw power and versatile team utility. While both characters occupy the Main DPS slot, their mechanical execution and the way they interact with the game's elemental systems differ significantly.

# Neuvillette vs. Ayato: The Ultimate Hydro DPS Comparison

## TL;DR Verdict

![TL;DR Verdict](/img/neuvillette-vs-ayato-dps-comparison/inline-1.webp)


When comparing Neuvillette and Kamisato Ayato, the distinction is clear: Neuvillette is a specialized hypercarry designed to maximize personal damage, while Ayato is a flexible driver designed to facilitate team-wide elemental reactions. Neuvillette currently sits at the top of the meta due to his massive self-sufficiency and high damage floor, whereas Ayato remains a solid choice for players who enjoy variety in their team building.

*   **Winner: Neuvillette (Raw DPS)** – He offers the highest personal damage output of any Hydro character and can effectively solo high-level content like the Spiral Abyss.
*   **Winner: Ayato (Flexibility)** – His ability to function in Hyperbloom, Vaporize, Freeze, and Taser teams makes him a versatile asset for diverse rosters.

### Quick Comparison Summary

Neuvillette functions as a high-investment, high-reward carry. His kit revolves around a powerful Charged Attack beam that scales entirely with his Max HP. This makes him exceptionally easy to build, as he does not require ATK stats and can utilize the F2P-friendly Prototype Amber catalyst to great effect. His self-healing and damage-dealing mechanics are intertwined, allowing him to sustain himself without a dedicated healer in many scenarios.

Kamisato Ayato, conversely, relies on his Elemental Skill to enter a state where he performs rapid Hydro-infused slashes. His damage scales primarily with ATK, though he gains a secondary damage bonus based on his Max HP through his Namisen stacks. Ayato’s Elemental Burst provides a large, persistent AoE Hydro field that buffs Normal Attack damage, allowing him to contribute to the team even when he is not the primary focus of the rotation.

### Who Wins in the Current Meta?

In the current meta as of May 2024, Neuvillette is widely considered the superior choice for players looking to clear difficult content with minimal effort. His damage output is significantly higher than Ayato's in both single-target and multi-target scenarios. Furthermore, the introduction of Furina has pushed Neuvillette even further ahead, as their kits synergize perfectly to generate massive damage buffs through HP fluctuations.

Ayato has seen a slight decline in usage because his role as a Hydro enabler is often contested by characters like Yelan or Xingqiu, and his raw damage cannot compete with Neuvillette's "Equitable Judgment" beam. However, Ayato remains the "comfort" pick for players who prefer a simple, hold-to-attack playstyle and want a character who can fit into almost any new elemental reaction team that might be released.

## Detailed Comparison

![Detailed Comparison](/img/neuvillette-vs-ayato-dps-comparison/inline-3.webp)


### Damage Output and Scaling Mechanics

Neuvillette’s damage profile is unique because it is entirely decoupled from the ATK stat. His Charged Attack, "Equitable Judgment," fires a surging torrent that deals continuous AoE Hydro damage in a straight line. This beam's damage is calculated based on a percentage of his Max HP. Because he spends HP to fire the beam and recovers HP by absorbing Sourcewater Droplets (generated by his Skill and Burst), he constantly triggers the 4-piece effect of the Marechaussee Hunter artifact set, giving him a nearly permanent 36% Crit Rate bonus.

Ayato uses a more traditional scaling model. His Elemental Skill, "Kamisato Art: Kyouka," converts his Normal Attacks into Shunsuiken slashes. While these slashes gain a damage bonus based on his Max HP (the Namisen effect), the base damage still relies on his ATK. This makes Ayato harder to build because he requires a balance of ATK, Crit Rate, Crit DMG, and some HP, whereas Neuvillette can focus almost exclusively on HP and Crit stats. Neuvillette’s damage ceiling is higher because his multipliers are simply larger, and his ability to hit enemies at a significant distance gives him better effective DPS in spread-out encounters.

### Playstyle and Ease of Use

Ayato is often cited as one of the easiest characters to play in the game. To maximize his damage, you simply hold down the Normal Attack button during his 6-second Skill window. He automatically targets the nearest enemy and performs rapid slashes. His Elemental Burst has a massive radius and a long duration (18 seconds), making it a "set and forget" ability that provides consistent Hydro application.

Neuvillette requires slightly more management. You must use his Elemental Skill and Burst to generate Sourcewater Droplets, which are then consumed to instantly charge his beam. Without these droplets, his Charged Attack takes too long to wind up. While his beam allows him to hover and move slowly, he is vulnerable to being knocked back at C0. Players must learn to "spin" the camera or position themselves carefully to hit as many targets as possible. However, the sheer range of his beam—which can hit enemies across the entire length of the Abyss floor—gives him a tactical advantage over Ayato’s melee-range slashes.

### Team Compositions and Synergies

Neuvillette is a selfish carry who demands the team be built around him. At C0, his passive "Past Draconic Glories" encourages you to trigger three different types of Hydro-related reactions to maximize his beam damage. This usually results in "rainbow" teams featuring units like Kazuha (Swirl), Zhongli (Crystallize), and Fischl (Electro-Charged). His best partner is Furina, who provides a massive damage buff and Hydro Resonance, though this pairing often requires Neuvillette’s C1 to maintain his passive stacks.

Ayato is the king of team variety. He can be the center of a "Soup" team (Ayato, Bennett, Xiangling, Kazuha), a reliable driver for Hyperbloom (Ayato, Nahida, Kuki Shinobu), or a fast-attacking carry in a Freeze team. Because his Burst applies Hydro from off-field, he can even be used as a secondary DPS or a Burst support in certain rotations. If you enjoy changing your team members frequently to keep the gameplay fresh, Ayato offers significantly more options than Neuvillette.

### F2P Friendliness and Weapon Options

Neuvillette is arguably the most F2P-friendly 5-star DPS in Genshin Impact. The 4-star craftable catalyst, Prototype Amber, is his second-best weapon behind his signature, Tome of the Eternal Flow. Prototype Amber provides HP%, helps with energy regeneration, and adds a team-wide healing effect that synergizes with his kit. Because he scales with HP, players don't have to worry about low base ATK on 4-star weapons.

Ayato is more demanding. While the craftable Amenoma Kageuchi or Finale of the Deep are decent options, he sees a significant performance jump with 5-star swords like Haran Geppaku Futsu or Primordial Jade Cutter. His artifact options are also more varied but less efficient to farm. Neuvillette’s best set (Marechaussee Hunter) is found in the same domain as Golden Troupe, making it one of the most resin-efficient domains to farm. Ayato’s best set (Echoes of an Offering) is highly dependent on having a low internet ping, which can make his damage inconsistent for some players.

### Constellation Value (C0 vs. C1 vs. C6)

Neuvillette has one of the most impactful C1 constellations in the game. His C1, "Venerable Institution," provides two massive buffs: it gives him a free stack of his "Past Draconic Glories" passive and grants him total immunity to interruption while using his beam. This removes the need for a shielder like Zhongli and allows him to run double-Hydro teams (like with Furina) without losing damage.

Ayato’s constellations are generally linear damage increases. His C1 and C2 provide extra damage against low-HP enemies and increase his Namisen stack cap, respectively. His C6 is a significant burst of power, adding two extra slashes to his initial Skill attack, but it does not fundamentally change his playstyle the way Neuvillette’s C1 does. For low-spending players, Neuvillette at C1 offers a much higher power spike than Ayato at the same level of investment.

## Recommendation

![Recommendation](/img/neuvillette-vs-ayato-dps-comparison/inline-4.webp)


The choice between Neuvillette and Ayato depends on your current roster and your goals for your account. If you are struggling to clear the Spiral Abyss and need a powerhouse who can carry one side with minimal support, Neuvillette is the clear choice. If you already have a strong roster and want a character who can fill gaps in multiple team archetypes while being easy to play, Ayato is a fantastic addition.

### When to Choose Neuvillette

*   You want the highest possible Hydro DPS currently available.
*   You have Furina and want to maximize her potential.
*   You prefer a character who can sustain their own health and function independently.
*   You are looking for a character that is easy to build with F2P weapons like Prototype Amber.
*   You want a character capable of soloing high-level content.

### When to Choose Ayato

*   You value team flexibility and want to play Hyperbloom, Vaporize, and Taser.
*   You prefer the fast-paced, "hack and slash" sword gameplay over catalyst beams.
*   You already have well-built supports like Yun Jin or Bennett who benefit Normal Attackers.
*   You need a Hydro unit who can provide consistent off-field application via their Burst.
*   You want a character with a very low skill ceiling and high comfort level.

### Final Thoughts

The "Hydro Husbando" powercreep is a frequent topic of discussion, and it is true that Neuvillette has raised the ceiling for what a Main DPS can achieve. However, Ayato’s design focuses on a different philosophy. Neuvillette is the "tallest" character in terms of raw numbers, but Ayato is the "widest" in terms of how many different ways he can be played. 

If you are a new player, Neuvillette will likely provide more value to your account immediately. If you are a veteran player looking for a versatile tool to experiment with new compositions, Ayato remains a stylish and effective choice. Ultimately, both characters are more than capable of clearing all content in the game, so your preference for their aesthetics and playstyle should be the final deciding factor.

## Overview

![Overview](/img/nte-leveling-guide-fast-progression/inline-1.webp)


Progression in Hethereau is not a linear climb; it is a symbiotic relationship between your account’s reputation and your combat proficiency. By mastering the distinction between Hunter and Appraisal levels, you can avoid the common "level-locking" pitfalls that stall many new Appraisers.

### Hunter Level vs. Appraisal Level

Your **Hunter Level** represents your overall account progression. It is the primary gatekeeper for story Episodes, new gameplay modes like the Anomaly Hunt, and the expansion of the City Tycoon system. You gain Hunter EXP through almost every activity in the game, from completing main story missions to simply spending Character Pixels (stamina).

Conversely, your **Appraisal Level** functions as the "World Rank." It dictates the maximum level cap for your Espers and Arcs, as well as the difficulty and reward quality of enemies in the open world. While Hunter Level increases automatically as you gain EXP, Appraisal Level is gated.

| Hunter Level Milestone | Appraisal Level Unlock | Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Level 20 | Appraisal Level 2 | Complete Ascension Quest: "The First Appraisal" |
| Level 40 | Appraisal Level 4 | Complete Ascension Quest: "Urban Authority" |
| Level 50 | Appraisal Level 6 | Complete Ascension Quest: "Master of Hethereau" |

If you reach these Hunter Level milestones without completing the associated Ascension Quest, your Hunter EXP will overcap, and you will receive only 10% of the normal EXP rewards until the quest is cleared.

### City Tycoon and Economic Progression

The City Tycoon system is the backbone of your long-term progression. Beyond providing a steady stream of Fons (currency), it offers critical account milestones.
*   **Level 16 Milestone:** This is the single most important breakpoint for fast progression. At Tycoon Level 16, city stamina costs and rewards are increased by 100%. This effectively halves the real-world time required to complete weekly activities like "Heather Hobbies" while maintaining maximum resource gain.
*   **Level 18 & 21:** Reaching Tycoon Level 18 grants you the S-Rank Cosmos DPS, **Chiz**, for free. Level 21 unlocks her signature weapon, ensuring your secondary team is battle-ready without spending premium currency.

### Traversal Optimization: The Novis ST-X 950

Speeding through Hethereau is not just about having a fast car; it’s about deployment flexibility. While high-end supercars like the Pendragon offer higher top speeds, they are restricted to paved roads. For leveling, the **Novis ST-X 950 motorcycle** (250,000 Fons at the Regalia Dealership) is the superior choice. Its unique ability to be summoned off-road allows you to cut through parks and alleyways, significantly reducing travel time between objectives.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

![Best Disks (Artifacts)](/img/nte-leveling-guide-fast-progression/inline-3.webp)


Artifact management in NTE is handled through the **Console** system, where you slot **Modules** into **Cartridges**. This system becomes the primary focus of your grind once you hit Hunter Level 30.

### The Lost Radiance Set

The **Lost Radiance** Cartridge is the gold standard for early-to-mid game progression, specifically for Cosmos-element characters like Zero.
*   **2-Piece Bonus (Epic):** Cosmos DMG +10%.
*   **4-Piece Bonus (Legendary):** Ignores 25% of enemies' DEF for 20 seconds after the wearer casts an Ultimate.

This set is particularly powerful for fast progression because it allows you to "punch up" against higher-level anomalies in Anomaly Zones, bypassing their natural defense scaling.

### Module Stat Priority

When slotting Modules into your Lost Radiance Cartridge, prioritize the following stats to maximize Zero’s AoE (Area of Effect) clearing potential
1.  **Cosmos DMG Bonus:** The most valuable multiplier for Zero’s kit.
2.  **CRIT Rate / CRIT DMG:** Essential for consistent burst damage during Ultimate windows.
3.  **ATK%:** Increases the base scaling of all skills and the Officer Whisker summon (if using Hethereau's Keeper).
4.  **Charge Efficiency:** Helps cycle Ultimates faster, maintaining the 4-piece Lost Radiance DEF-shred bonus.

### Optimizing the Cassette Layout

To trigger the full Cartridge bonus, you must fit specific Module shapes into the Console grid. The "Ideal Cassette Layout" for Lost Radiance typically requires two Type III Modules (3-cell) and one Type IV Module (4-cell). Placing Type III Modules specifically grants an additional 10% Cosmos DMG per module for Zero, making the layout just as important as the stats themselves.

## Team Comp

![Team Comp](/img/nte-leveling-guide-fast-progression/inline-4.webp)


Efficiency in NTE is dictated by the **Esper Cycle**, a mechanic where different elements react to create devastating field effects. The most efficient F2P (Free-to-Play) core centers on Zero’s ability to act as a "driver" for off-field supports.

### The F2P Powerhouse: Zero + Adler + Edgar

This trio is widely considered the most stable team for rapid story progression.
*   **Zero (Cosmos):** Your primary DPS. Zero’s wide-swinging attacks and high-impact Ultimate make them the perfect unit for clearing waves of minor anomalies instantly.
*   **Adler (Incantation):** A defensive specialist who scales off DEF. Adler provides the "Sunyas Shield," which is vital for surviving the aggressive AI of high-Appraisal bosses. His Incantation DoT (Damage over Time) also sets up elemental reactions.
*   **Edgar (Cosmos):** Serving as the dedicated healer, Edgar ensures 100% uptime. Because he shares the Cosmos element with Zero, they generate Resonance particles more efficiently, allowing for faster Ultimate charging.

### The Flex Slot: Haniel vs. Mint

The fourth slot in your team should be chosen based on the content you are facing
*   **Haniel (Psyche):** Choose Haniel for bossing. Her ability to buff the team’s ATK power based on her own stats makes her the best support for maximizing Zero’s burst windows.
*   **Mint (Anima):** Choose Mint for general exploration. Her dual-blade "Tornado" ultimate is excellent for grouping enemies together, allowing Zero to finish them off with a single skill.

### Reaction Synergy: Triggering Blossom and Scorch

The interaction between Zero, Adler, and your flex support is key to the "Speed-Clearing" meta.
*   **Scorch:** Triggered by combining Incantation (Adler) with Cosmos (Zero). This creates an explosive reaction that deals massive AoE damage and reduces enemy stagger resistance.
*   **Blossom:** Triggered by combining Anima (Mint) with Cosmos (Zero). This creates a "pull" effect that keeps enemies grouped, ensuring Zero’s attacks hit the maximum number of targets.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/nte-leveling-guide-fast-progression/inline-5.webp)


Mastering the **Esper Cycle** meter is the difference between a 5-minute boss fight and a 60-second clear. Your rotation should focus on filling the meter to trigger swap-in bursts, which allow characters to enter the field with an immediate, high-damage attack.

### The Standard Clearing Loop

1.  **Initiate with Adler:** Use Adler’s Skill to deploy the Sunyas Shield. This protects your team and applies the Incantation status to nearby enemies.
2.  **Apply Flex Buffs:** Swap to Haniel to drop her ATK buff or Mint to use her grouping Skill.
3.  **The Scorch/Blossom Trigger:** Swap to Zero. The swap-in burst will immediately trigger an elemental reaction (Scorch or Blossom) based on the previous element applied.
4.  **Ultimate Window:** Once the Esper Cycle meter is full, wait for the enemy to enter a "Stagger Window" (indicated by a broken yellow bar under their HP). Activate Zero’s Ultimate to benefit from the 4-piece Lost Radiance DEF shred and the enemy’s increased damage intake.

### Maximizing the Esper Cycle Meter

The meter gains charge through three primary actions
*   **Perfect Parries:** The fastest way to gain meter. Timing your block just as an anomaly attacks grants a massive chunk of energy.
*   **Skill Usage:** Each character's Skill provides a set amount of meter gain.
*   **Basic Attack Chains:** While slower, completing a full basic attack combo provides a steady stream of energy.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/nte-leveling-guide-fast-progression/inline-6.webp)


### How do I unlock the motorcycle?
The **Novis ST-X 950** is unlocked by visiting the Regalia Dealership in the New Herland district. You must have at least 250,000 Fons. It is highly recommended to prioritize this over car upgrades during the first week of play due to its off-road summoning capabilities.

### Why is my Hunter Level stuck at 20/40/50?
You have hit an Appraisal Level gate. Check your mission log for "Ascension Quests." You must complete these specific combat trials to raise your Appraisal Level and allow your Hunter Level to continue progressing.

### What is the best way to spend Character Pixels?
For fast progression, spend your Pixels on **Anomaly Zones** that drop Lost Radiance Modules. Once you have a functional set, pivot to **Boss Hunts** to gather the materials needed to ascend your Arcs and Espers to their new level caps.

### Daily and Weekly Progression Checklist
*   [ ] **Daily:** Complete 4 Exploration Guide tasks (Dailies) for 2,000+ Hunter EXP.
*   [ ] **Daily:** Spend all 180 Character Pixels on Module farming or Bosses.
*   [ ] **Weekly:** Clear "Heather Hobbies" in City Tycoon to max out your weekly Fon income.
*   [ ] **Weekly:** Defeat the "Never-ending Arachne" weekly boss for high-tier Arc upgrade materials.
*   [ ] **Hidden Quest:** Complete the "Naked Ghost Tour of the City" to secure your free S-Rank "Day Off" Arc.

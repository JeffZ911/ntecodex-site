Success in the high-velocity urban battles of Hethereau requires more than just fast reflexes; it demands a deep understanding of how your team's elemental energies interact. This guide provides the essential Neverness to Everness combat tips, builds, and team compositions you need to master the Esper Cycle and shred enemy Break meters.

# Neverness to Everness Combat Tips: Best Builds and Team Compositions

## Overview

![Overview](/img/neverness-to-everness-combat-tips-build-guide/inline-1.webp)


Combat in Neverness to Everness (NTE) is built upon the momentum of the Esper Cycle and the strategic dismantling of enemy stability. Unlike traditional action RPGs where characters act in isolation, NTE rewards players who treat their four-person squad as a single, fluid engine of destruction. Mastery over defensive timings and elemental sequencing is the difference between a struggling Appraiser and a top-tier hunter.

### Mastering the Esper Cycle

The Esper Cycle is the heart of NTE’s combat system. Every character possesses an Esper Meter (100 units) located near their health bar. As you perform actions, this meter fills; once it reaches 100%, the character’s portrait glows, indicating they are ready to perform an Esper Attack upon swapping in.

The "Adjacency Rule" is the most critical aspect of this system. Elements are arranged in a specific wheel: **Cosmos → Anima → Incantation → Chaos → Psyche → Lakshana → Cosmos**. A reaction only occurs if you swap from a character of one element to a character of an adjacent element on this wheel. For example, swapping from a Cosmos character to an Anima character triggers the Blossom reaction. If you swap between non-adjacent elements, you lose the reaction bonus and the associated damage multipliers.

### The Break Meter and Stagger Windows

Every elite enemy and boss in Hethereau is protected by a white stability bar known as the Break Meter, located directly beneath their HP. While this bar is active, enemies take reduced damage and are resistant to hitstun. 

When you deplete the Break Meter entirely, the enemy enters a "Staggered" or "Broken" state. For approximately 4 to 5 seconds, the enemy is completely immobilized and susceptible to a massive damage multiplier. This is your primary window for unleashing Ultimates and high-damage skills. Efficient combat involves using "Breaker" units to reach this state as quickly as possible before swapping to your "Damage" units to capitalize on the vulnerability.

### Parrying and Defensive Mechanics

While dodging is a standard survival tool, parrying is the most powerful defensive action in the game. When an enemy prepares a heavy attack, a "Red Circle" indicator (specifically two overlapping circles) will appear. Executing an attack, skill, or character swap at the exact moment these circles overlap triggers a Parry.

A successful Parry provides three massive benefits:
1. It completely nullifies the incoming damage and stops the enemy's momentum.
2. It deals the highest possible amount of Break damage to the enemy.
3. It provides an instant 100% fill to the active character's Esper Cycle meter.

For characters with slow energy generation, baiting a parry is the most efficient way to get them back into the Esper Cycle rotation. Additionally, look for the "Red Flash" on standard attacks; dodging during this flash triggers a Critical Dodge, which allows for a Critical Riposte—a powerful counter-attack that serves as the secondary method for shredding enemy stability.

## Best Weapons

![Best Weapons](/img/neverness-to-everness-combat-tips-build-guide/inline-2.webp)


In Neverness to Everness, weapons are referred to as Arcs. These are categorized by their physical state: Synthesis, Gas, Plasma, Liquid, and Solid. A character can only equip an Arc that matches their specific type compatibility.

### Top-Tier S-Rank Arcs

S-Rank Arcs provide the highest base stats and unique passive effects that often define a character's role in the meta.

1.  **Good Boy's Grand Adventure (Gas):** This is the undisputed Best-in-Slot (BiS) for [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide). It provides an 18% boost to Charge Efficiency and increases the entire team's ATK by 10% for 20 seconds after the wearer uses an Ultimate. This bonus increases by an additional 6% if the Ultimate successfully controls or groups enemies, which perfectly aligns with Sakiri’s kit.
2.  **Cosmos Daze (Gas):** A premier choice for main DPS units that rely on consistent elemental application. While its exact scaling is undisclosed, it is highly valued for its synergy with high-uptime attackers.
3.  **Wild Reverie (Gas):** This Arc is designed for quick-swap sub-DPS units. It triggers an 18% to 30% Damage bonus for 10 seconds after a Support Skill is used, making it ideal for characters who enter the field just to trigger a reaction and then swap out.
4.  **Hethereau's Keeper (Solid):** A powerful choice for Solid-type damage dealers like Zero. It grants a flat ATK boost and summons a companion (Officer Whisker) to deal continuous automated damage, providing high passive pressure during boss fights.
5.  **Marching Beyond Time (Solid):** This Arc focuses on Critical Rate and ATK scaling. It is a top-tier choice for players looking to maximize their raw damage output during the Stagger window.

### Best F2P Weapon Alternatives

For players who haven't secured S-Rank Arcs from the [Scarborough Fair gacha](https://ntecodex.com/faq) system, several A-Rank options provide excellent utility.

*   **Shiny Days:** An excellent all-rounder that provides undisclosed. It is often used as a placeholder for Anima or Cosmos damage dealers.
*   **The Forgotten:** This Arc focuses on undisclosed, making it a viable choice for survival-focused units or early-game progression where gear is limited.

## Best Disks (Cartridges)

![Best Disks (Cartridges)](/img/neverness-to-everness-combat-tips-build-guide/inline-3.webp)


Cartridges, often referred to as Disks, function as the artifact system in NTE. They provide 2-piece "Epic" bonuses and 4-piece "Legendary" set bonuses.

### Offensive Cartridge Sets

*   **Lost Radiance:** This is the premier set for Cosmos-element damage dealers like Zero or Chiz. The 2-piece set grants +10% Cosmos DMG, while the 4-piece set allows the wearer to ignore 25% of the enemy's DEF for 20 seconds after using an Ultimate. This is currently the strongest damage-scaling effect in the game for burst windows.
*   **Devil's Blood: Curse:** Essential for reaction-heavy teams, particularly those utilizing Psyche units. The 4-piece bonus provides a flat 18% damage increase, which doubles to 36% when attacking enemies currently affected by the Nova or Stain reactions.
*   **Fireflies and the Forest:** The BiS set for Anima DPS units like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or Jiuyuan. It provides Anima DMG and increases Critical Damage by 8% whenever a nearby enemy takes Anima damage, stacking up to 7 times (56% total).

### Utility and Support Cartridges

*   **Speedy Hedgehog:** This is the mandatory set for your primary support or "buffer" unit. The 2-piece bonus provides +12% Charge Efficiency, helping the unit fill their Esper Meter faster. The 4-piece bonus increases the ATK of all team members by 15% for 20 seconds after the wearer casts an Ultimate, which stacks with other ATK buffs like those from Sakiri.
*   **Kingdom's Guard:** A defensive set often used on Survival-role characters like Fadia. It boosts DEF by 15% and increases the strength of all shields generated by the wearer by 20%.

## Team Comp

![Team Comp](/img/neverness-to-everness-combat-tips-build-guide/inline-4.webp)


Building a team in Neverness to Everness is about creating a loop where one character's actions perfectly set up the next character's reaction.

### The Blossom Meta (Anima Teams)

The "Anima Blossom" team is currently the most popular composition for clearing high-level Anomalies. It relies on the constant generation of Vita Buds to provide sustained AoE damage.
*   **Main DPS:** Nanally (Anima / Plasma)
*   **Sub-DPS:** Jiuyuan (Anima / Solid)
*   **Support/Buffer:** Sakiri (Incantation / Gas)
*   **Sustainer/Enabler:** [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte) (Cosmos / Solid)

In this setup, Hotori applies the Cosmos element, which Jiuyuan or Nanally then reacts with to trigger Blossom. Sakiri provides the necessary ATK buffs to ensure the Blossom projectiles deal maximum damage.

### The Scorch and Discord Archetype

This team is specifically designed for bossing and high-difficulty stability shredding.
*   **Main DPS:** Baicang (Incantation / Synthesis)
*   **Breaker:** Daffodill (Chaos / Liquid)
*   **Support:** Sakiri (Incantation / Gas)
*   **Survival:** Fadia (Psyche / Synthesis)

This team utilizes the **Discord** Trio reaction (Incantation + Chaos + Psyche). By cycling between Sakiri, Daffodill, and Fadia, you apply a constant Break meter deduction that allows Baicang to enter his burst window much earlier than other teams.

### Best F2P Starting Lineups

New players should focus on the core trio provided through progression.
*   **Zero (Esper):** A versatile Cosmos/Solid unit that can adapt to many roles.
*   **Edgar:** A Psyche/Liquid unit that provides reliable healing and survival.
*   **Haniel:** A Psyche/Solid buffer who can help bridge elemental gaps.
*   **Mint:** An Anima/Liquid damage dealer who is often given for free early on.

This "Soup" team allows for easy access to Remora and Blossom reactions, which are vital for controlling mobs during the early chapters of the Hethereau storyline.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/neverness-to-everness-combat-tips-build-guide/inline-5.webp)


A rotation in NTE is not a fixed sequence of buttons but a priority list based on your Esper Meters.

### Standard Opening Sequence

1.  **Buff:** Start with your Support unit (like Sakiri or Haniel). Use their Special Skill to group enemies and generate initial energy.
2.  **Apply Primary Element:** Swap to your Enabler (like Hotori or Zero). Use their skill to apply the first half of your desired reaction (e.g., Cosmos).
3.  **Trigger Reaction:** Once the next character's portrait glows, swap to your Sub-DPS (like Jiuyuan or Daffodill) to trigger the reaction (Blossom or Scorch).
4.  **Build Break:** Focus on using Critical Ripostes and Parries with your Breaker until the white bar is nearly gone.

### Maximizing the Stagger Window

1.  **The Stagger Trigger:** Just before the Break meter hits zero, ensure your Main DPS (Nanally or Baicang) has a full Ultimate meter.
2.  **The Burst:** As soon as the enemy is "Broken," swap to your Main DPS.
3.  **Ability Sequence:** Execute the Ultimate immediately to benefit from the Lost Radiance DEF-ignore bonus, followed by the Redirect Skill.
4.  **undisclosed:** Utilize specific character passives, such as Hotori's ability to reset skill cooldowns after a successful counter, to extend the damage output within this 5-second window.

### Quickswap Reaction Chaining

For advanced play, you can chain duo reactions into a trio reaction. In a Discord team, you would cycle:
*   **Incantation (Sakiri)** → **Chaos (Daffodill)** to trigger **Scorch**.
*   Immediately swap to **Psyche (Fadia)** to trigger **Nova**.
*   The simultaneous presence of Scorch and Nova triggers **Discord**, which will then begin melting the boss's Break bar automatically while you continue attacking.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/neverness-to-everness-combat-tips-build-guide/inline-6.webp)


### Combat Mechanics FAQ

**How do I parry?**
Watch for the double red circles on an enemy. You must time an attack, a skill, or a character swap to occur exactly when the outer circle overlaps the inner one. A successful parry fills your Esper Meter to 100% instantly.

**What is Cycle Rate and Cycle Intensity?**
Cycle Rate determines how much Esper Meter you gain from normal actions. Cycle Intensity is a stat that scales the damage of your reactions (Blossom, Scorch, etc.). The game typically uses the highest Cycle Intensity value among the characters involved in the reaction.

**What is the "Red Flash"?**
The Red Flash is a visual cue on enemy attacks. Dodging during this flash triggers a Critical Dodge, followed by a Critical Riposte counter-attack that deals significant Break damage.

### Gear and Progression FAQ

**Can I change the MC's element?**
Yes, Esper Zero is a versatile unit. While they start as Cosmos, you can adapt their elemental role and Arc compatibility as you unlock new "Paths" through the main story progression in Hethereau.

**How do I get more Arcs?**
Arcs are primarily obtained through the Scarborough [Fair gacha](https://ntecodex.com/faq) system or the Arc Collection mechanic. You can also farm specific S-Rank Arcs, such as "Good Boy's Grand Adventure," by defeating world bosses like Morphix or clearing high-level Anomaly hunts.

**What are Modules?**
Modules are sub-gear pieces that slot into your character's Console. They provide the base stats (ATK and HP) required to activate the set bonuses of your Cartridges.

# Neverness to Everness Leveling Guide: How to Progress Fast
> ⚠️ **Notice:** Auto-generated content. May contain approximations or minor inaccuracies in supporting details. Help us improve via the comment section below. _Last reviewed: 2026-05-23._


Reaching the level cap in Neverness to Everness (NTE) is a multi-layered journey that balances your account-wide progression with your influence over the city’s economy. Unlike traditional RPGs where you simply grind mobs for experience, NTE requires you to master the dual-leveling system of Hunter and Appraisal Levels while simultaneously scaling your City Tycoon rank to unlock the game's most powerful Espers and equipment.

## Overview

![Overview](/img/neverness-to-everness-leveling-guide-fast/inline-1.webp)


Progression in Neverness to Everness is defined by three distinct but interconnected leveling systems. To progress fast, you must advance all three in tandem, as they gate different aspects of your character's power.

### Hunter Level vs. Appraisal Level
Your **Hunter Level** represents your overall account experience. It is earned primarily through Main Story Episodes, side quests, and spending Character Pixels (stamina) in Anomalous Zones. As your Hunter Level increases, you reach milestones that trigger an **Appraisal Level** increase.

The Appraisal Level acts as the world rank. Increasing it raises the difficulty of enemies in the open world and Anomalous Zones, but it also improves the rarity of loot drops and, most importantly, raises the level cap for your Espers. If you find your characters hitting a "soft cap" where they can no longer gain XP, you likely need to clear an Appraisal Trial to advance.

### The City Tycoon Progression Loop
The **City Tycoon** system is unlocked via the "Good Morning, Hethereau" quest, which becomes available shortly after defeating the Viewfinder boss in the main story. This mode is a management simulation where you earn **Fons**—a specific currency used to upgrade your Tycoon Rank.

The Tycoon Rank is vital for leveling because it provides massive chunks of Hunter EXP (300–500 per rank) and is the only way to unlock the S-Class character **Chiz** and her signature Arc. At Tycoon Level 4, you unlock the Cafe by Origen, which provides passive Fons income, allowing you to level up even while offline.

### Daily Checklist for Max EXP
To ensure you are leveling at the maximum possible rate, follow this daily priority list
1.  **Spend Character Pixels:** Clear Anomaly Zones or World Bosses to prevent your stamina from capping.
2.  **City Tycoon Maintenance:** Claim your Cafe income and spend your weekly City Stamina on Hethereau Hobbies like racing or fishing.
3.  **Exploration Guide Tasks:** Complete the daily mission objectives found in your Hunter's Handbook.
4.  **Anomaly Cleanup:** Locate and resolve minor urban anomalies (marked with blue icons) for quick bursts of Hunter EXP.

## Best Weapons

![Best Weapons](/img/neverness-to-everness-leveling-guide-fast/inline-2.webp)


In Neverness to Everness, weapons are known as **Arcs**. These are high-tech disks that provide base stats and unique passive abilities. Arcs are categorized into five elemental types: **Plasma, Solid, Gas, Liquid, and Synthesis**. An Esper can only equip an Arc that matches their specific type.

### S-Tier Arcs for Main DPS
The most effective Arcs for fast leveling are those that provide either raw damage multipliers or energy restoration to keep your Ultimates cycling. 

*   **Day Off (Solid):** This is arguably the best leveling Arc for Anima or Solid-type DPS. It provides a significant ATK boost and creates an "Eclipse" window where defeating enemies restores Ultimate energy, allowing you to clear waves of trash mobs instantly.
*   **Ready-Ready (Plasma):** A specialized boss-slayer. It provides bonus damage against elite enemies and bosses, making it essential for clearing the Appraisal Trials that gate your world rank.
*   **Blow up the Crowd (Solid):** Excellent for quick-swap teams. It boosts the active character's ATK even when the wearer is off-field, making it a "best-in-slot" option for sub-DPS units like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte).

### Best Support & Utility Arcs
Support Arcs focus on team-wide buffs or utility. **Contemplative Cat (Gas)** is a unique option that scales its damage bonus based on how many Fons you are currently holding in your City Tycoon account, rewarding players who manage their economy well.

### How to Refine Arcs for Power Spikes
You can refine Arcs by consuming duplicate copies. Each refinement level increases the potency of the Arc's passive effect. For F2P players, focus on refining A-Rank Arcs like **Umbrella** (which provides high survivability) or **Hethereau’s Keeper**, as these are often easier to obtain than S-Rank signatures.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

![Best Disks (Artifacts)](/img/neverness-to-everness-leveling-guide-fast/inline-3.webp)


The gear system in NTE is divided into **Cartridges** and **Modules**. This "Console" system functions like a Tetris grid where you must fit geometric Modules into a Cartridge to activate set bonuses.

### The Cartridge Grid: How to Maximize Stats
Each Cartridge has a specific shape requirement. To activate the 4-piece set bonus, you must slot in Modules that occupy the required cells. 
*   **2-Piece Bonus:** Provides a flat stat increase (e.g., +10% Elemental DMG).
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** Provides a transformative combat effect.

### Best Universal Sets for All Characters
For early and mid-game leveling, the **Lost Radiance** set is the gold standard. Its 4-piece bonus grants 25% DEF-ignore for 20 seconds after casting an Ultimate. Since Defense is the primary stat that slows down kill times on high-level enemies, this set drastically accelerates your progression.

| Set Name | 2-Piece Bonus | 4-Piece Bonus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Lost Radiance | +10% Cosmos DMG | Ignores 25% Enemy DEF after Ultimate. |
| Shadow Creed | +10% ATK | +25% ATK for 20s after casting a Skill. |
| Speedy Hedgehog | +12% Charge Efficiency | +15% Team-wide ATK after Ultimate. |
| Devil’s Blood: Curse | +10% Psyche DMG | +36% DMG vs. Nova/Stain affected enemies. |

### Farming Cartridges via Hunter Exchange
While you can farm Cartridges in the **Rabbit Hole Anomaly Zone**, you can also purchase high-tier sets from the **Hunter Exchange** once you reach Tycoon Level 6. This is the most reliable way to get the **Lost Radiance** set early without relying on RNG drops.

## Team Comp

![Team Comp](/img/neverness-to-everness-leveling-guide-fast/inline-4.webp)


NTE uses the **Esper Cycle** system, where elements must be chained in a specific order to trigger powerful reactions. The elements follow a fixed wheel: **Cosmos → Anima → Incantation → Chaos → Psyche → Lakshana → Cosmos**.

### Top Meta Team: The Blossom Standard
The current meta for fast clearing is the **Blossom Team**, which focuses on the reaction between Cosmos and Anima. 
*   **Main DPS:** Nanally (Anima)
*   **Sub-DPS/Support:** [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte) (Cosmos)
*   **Support:** Jiuyuan (Anima)
*   **Flex/Healer:** Zero (Cosmos) or Edgar (Incantation)

This team triggers the **Blossom** reaction, which creates "Vita Buds" on the field. These buds fire homing projectiles at enemies, dealing massive AoE damage. Jiuyuan is essential here because her passive increases the maximum number of Vita Buds allowed on the field.

### Best F2P Progression Team
If you are just starting and haven't pulled S-Class characters, use this reliable starter comp
1.  **Zero (Cosmos):** Your primary damage dealer.
2.  **[Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide) (Incantation):** Provides crowd control by grouping enemies with her "Kito Maru" hold skill.
3.  **Edgar (Incantation):** The primary healer who also helps trigger **Hexed** reactions with Anima units.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/neverness-to-everness-leveling-guide-fast/inline-5.webp)


Mastering the "Quick-Swap Meta" is the difference between a 5-minute boss fight and a 30-second clear. NTE rewards players who switch characters the moment their **Redirect Skill** (Support Skill) is available.

### Standard Combat Rotation for the Blossom Team
To maximize damage with the Nanally/Hotori/Jiuyuan core, follow this sequence
1.  **Start with Sakiri/Jiuyuan:** Use their Hold Skill to pull all enemies into a tight cluster.
2.  **Switch to Hotori:** Use her "Enter Recording" skill. This records the damage dealt by the team to be unleashed during her Ultimate.
3.  **Switch to Nanally:** Activate her "Ichi-daime's Authority" to walk on walls or gain high-ground advantage, then spam her Basic Attacks to trigger Blossom reactions with Hotori's field.
4.  **Redirect Attacks:** Watch for the character icons on the right to glow. Tap them to perform a Redirect attack, which swaps the character in while maintaining the combo.
5.  **Finisher:** Once the enemy's Break bar is depleted, swap to Hotori and trigger her Ultimate to "freeze" time and detonate all recorded damage.

### Energy Management & Ultimate Timing
Do not use your Ultimates as soon as they are ready. Instead, wait for the **Stagger Window** (when the enemy's white bar under their HP is empty). Enemies take significantly increased damage while staggered, making this the ideal time to dump all your high-cooldown abilities.

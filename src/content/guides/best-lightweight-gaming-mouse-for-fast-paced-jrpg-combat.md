High-intensity combat in Neverness to Everness demands more than just strategy; it requires hardware that can keep up with rapid-fire anomaly suppression and high-speed urban traversal across Hesperia.

# Best lightweight gaming mouse for fast-paced JRPG combat

The Logitech G Pro X Superlight 2 is the top choice for its 60g weight and 95-hour battery. For those seeking the absolute lightest frame, the 54g Razer Viper V3 Pro is the best high-performance alternative.

Action JRPG and gacha players performing high-frequency clicking and rapid camera movements during 6+ hour sessions will benefit most from these ultralight designs. In titles like Neverness to Everness, where combat involves real-time character swapping and dodging Anomalies, reducing the physical weight of your mouse can significantly decrease wrist strain over long grinding sessions.

### Logitech G Pro X Superlight 2 Lightspeed Wireless Gaming Mouse
The <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Logitech+G+Pro+X+Superlight+2&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Logitech G Pro X Superlight 2</a> stands as the gold standard for players who prioritize consistency and build quality. Weighing in at exactly [60 grams](https://www.rtings.com/mouse/reviews/logitech/g-pro-x-superlight-2), this mouse removes almost all physical resistance when performing the wide, sweeping movements necessary to track fast-moving enemies in Hesperia.

The inclusion of the HERO 2 sensor is a significant upgrade, offering tracking up to 32,000 DPI. This level of precision is particularly useful when navigating the dense UI menus of gacha games or micro-managing inventory items. Furthermore, the 95-hour battery life ensures that even during the high-activity period following the April 29, 2026 release of Neverness to Everness, you won't be tethered to a charging cable.

**Verdict:** With a 60g chassis and the HERO 2 sensor, the Superlight 2 is the industry standard for reliability. Its 95-hour battery life and 2,000Hz polling rate make it the most consistent performer for players who cannot afford input lag during intense combat sequences.

### Razer Viper V3 Pro Wireless Esports Gaming Mouse
If your primary goal is to minimize the force required for every "flick" movement, the <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Razer+Viper+V3+Pro&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Razer Viper V3 Pro</a> is the premier choice. At only [54 grams](https://www.razer.com/gaming-mice/razer-viper-v3-pro), it is one of the lightest wireless mice ever produced by a major manufacturer. This weight reduction is achieved without a "honeycomb" shell, maintaining a solid feel that protects the internal [Focus Pro 35K Optical Sensor Gen-2](https://www.razer.com/gaming-mice/razer-viper-v3-pro).

This mouse is built for the high-speed action found in Hotta Studio's latest project. When controlling characters like Nanally, whose combat style relies on rapid positioning and heavy-hitting strikes, the 8000Hz wireless polling rate provides a level of responsiveness that feels almost instantaneous. However, users should be aware that utilizing the full 8000Hz polling will drain the battery much faster than the standard 1000Hz setting.

**Verdict:** The Viper V3 Pro is the lightest mainstream mouse on the market at 54g. While the 8000Hz polling is overkill for most JRPGs, the weight reduction significantly lowers the physical effort required for rapid camera resets in action-heavy titles.

### Razer DeathAdder V3 Pro Wireless Gaming Mouse
Not every player finds symmetrical mice comfortable for 10-hour sessions. The <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Razer+DeathAdder+V3+Pro&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Razer DeathAdder V3 Pro</a> caters to those who prefer an ergonomic, right-handed shape. Despite its larger size, Razer has managed to keep the weight down to a mere [63 grams](https://www.razer.com/gaming-mice/razer-deathadder-v3-pro), which is lighter than many smaller competitive mice.

The [Optical Mouse Switches Gen-3](https://www.razer.com/gaming-mice/razer-deathadder-v3-pro) are a standout feature here. In fast-paced JRPGs where you might be clicking hundreds of times per minute to trigger Mint's abilities or execute combos, these switches offer a [90-million click lifecycle](https://www.razer.com/gaming-mice/razer-deathadder-v3-pro) and eliminate the risk of double-clicking. The [Focus Pro 30K Optical Sensor](https://www.razer.com/gaming-mice/razer-deathadder-v3-pro) provides flawless tracking even on glass surfaces, which is a major plus for players with diverse desk setups.

**Verdict:** For JRPG players who prefer a palm grip, the DeathAdder V3 Pro provides the best ergonomic support while maintaining a competitive 63g weight. It utilizes the Focus Pro 30K sensor, ensuring high-speed tracking during chaotic encounters.

### SteelSeries Aerox 9 Wireless - Lightweight MMO Mouse
For players who find the two-side-button layout of most ultralights limiting, the SteelSeries Aerox 9 Wireless offers a unique middle ground. It features a 12-button side panel, allowing you to map every skill, inventory shortcut, and character swap directly to your thumb.

While its 89-gram weight is the highest on this list, it is significantly lighter than traditional MMO mice like the Razer Naga, which often exceed 120g. The Aerox 9 also features IP54-rated AquaBarrier protection, making it resistant to dust and accidental spills—a common hazard during long gaming sessions. This mouse is ideal for mapping complex Unreal Engine 5 action sequences that require more than just basic left and right clicks.

**Verdict:** The Aerox 9 is the only viable 'lightweight' option for players who need a full 12-button grid. While its 89g weight is higher than a standard ultralight, it is nearly 40% lighter than traditional MMO mice like the Razer Naga.

### Pulsar Gaming Gears - X2V2 Wireless Gaming Mouse
The Pulsar X2V2 is an enthusiast-grade mouse that punches well above its weight class in terms of value. At just 52 grams, it is the lightest mouse in this comparison. It utilizes the PixArt PAW3395 sensor, which is widely considered one of the most accurate sensors available today, supporting up to 26,000 DPI.

The X2V2 is particularly popular among the [r/MouseReview](https://www.reddit.com/r/MouseReview/) community for its crisp, tactile clicks and symmetrical shape that favors claw and fingertip grips. For players looking to maximize their performance in Neverness to Everness without spending over $150, the Pulsar offers a compelling alternative to the "big two" brands.

**Verdict:** The Pulsar X2V2 offers enthusiast-grade specs, including a 52g weight and PAW3395 sensor, for under $100. It is a top-tier choice for JRPG players who want the performance of a flagship mouse without the 'big brand' price premium.

Selecting the right hardware for a game as visually and mechanically complex as Neverness to Everness requires looking beyond simple marketing claims. Our selection process was guided by four primary criteria to ensure these mice can handle the rigors of modern action JRPGs.

*   **Weight Threshold:** We only considered mice under 90g, with a primary focus on the sub-70g 'ultralight' category. Reducing weight is the most effective way to minimize repetitive strain during the high-click-count combat typical of Hotta Studio games.
*   **Sensor Accuracy:** Every selected mouse features a top-tier optical sensor, such as the HERO 2 or [Focus Pro](https://www.razer.com/gaming-mice/razer-viper-v3-pro). This ensures that when you are driving through the streets of Hesperia or aiming a character's skill, there is zero jitter or pixel-skipping.
*   **Community Feedback:** Technical specs only tell half the story. We cross-referenced our picks with long-term durability reports from [r/MouseReview](https://www.reddit.com/r/MouseReview/) to identify potential issues like scroll wheel failure or side-wall flex.
*   **Battery Endurance:** Since JRPG sessions often involve significant grinding, we prioritized mice with at least 60 hours of real-world battery life. The Superlight 2's 95-hour rating set the benchmark for this category.

## What a JRPG player actually needs

![What a JRPG player actually needs](/img/best-lightweight-gaming-mouse-for-fast-paced-jrpg-combat/inline-2.webp)


While "esports" mice are often marketed toward FPS players, the mechanics of modern JRPGs like Neverness to Everness have evolved to require similar levels of precision and speed.

**Low Inertia for Rapid Movement**
In the urban open world of Hesperia, combat often involves managing multiple enemies at once. A lighter mouse (sub-70g) has less inertia, meaning it requires less physical force to start and stop a movement. This is critical when you need to quickly turn your camera to dodge an incoming Anomaly attack or swap between characters like Nanally and Mint to chain elemental reactions.

**The Importance of Optical Switches**
Traditional mechanical switches rely on metal-on-metal contact, which can wear down over time and cause "double-clicking" errors. Action JRPGs are notorious for high "Actions Per Minute" (APM). Mice like the [DeathAdder V3 Pro](https://www.razer.com/gaming-mice/razer-deathadder-v3-pro) use optical switches that trigger via a light beam, offering a much longer lifespan and faster response times.

**Coating and Comfort**
During a 6-hour session, the texture of your mouse matters. We looked for mice with high-quality matte finishes. A good coating prevents the mouse from becoming slippery as your hands warm up, which is vital for maintaining control during the most difficult boss encounters in Neverness to Everness.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/best-lightweight-gaming-mouse-for-fast-paced-jrpg-combat/inline-4.webp)


While a lightweight mouse can improve your experience in Neverness to Everness, it isn't a mandatory purchase for every player.

If you currently use a mouse in the 75-80g range and do not experience wrist fatigue or "heaviness" during combat, the jump to a 60g mouse may feel negligible. The benefits of weight reduction follow a curve of diminishing returns; the move from 120g to 60g is life-changing, but the move from 70g to 60g is much harder to perceive for non-competitive play.

Furthermore, if you exclusively play turn-based JRPGs, the extreme polling rates and ultra-low weights of these mice won't provide a tangible gameplay advantage. In turn-based combat, comfort and the number of programmable buttons are far more important than raw speed. Lastly, some players find ultralight mice to feel "floaty" or difficult to control. If you rely on a heavy mouse for stability and have a "shaky" hand, switching to a [54g Viper V3 Pro](https://www.razer.com/gaming-mice/razer-viper-v3-pro) might actually make your aim less consistent unless you also invest in a high-friction mousepad.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why is a lightweight mouse better for JRPGs like Neverness to Everness?
A lighter mouse reduces the physical effort required for every movement, which is essential for the real-time, action-heavy combat in Neverness to Everness. Lower weight minimizes wrist fatigue during long sessions of exploring Hesperia or grinding for character upgrade materials.

### Does polling rate matter for JRPG combat?
While ultra-high polling rates like 8000Hz are primarily designed for competitive FPS games, they do provide a smoother cursor feel on high-refresh-rate monitors. For most players, a standard 1000Hz or 2000Hz polling rate is more than sufficient to handle the anomaly-based combat in Hotta Studio's latest title.

### Can I use these mice for other gacha games?
Yes, these mice are excellent for any game requiring high-frequency clicking and menu navigation. The [optical switches](https://www.razer.com/gaming-mice/razer-deathadder-v3-pro) found in the Razer and Logitech models are specifically designed to handle the high click counts typical of gacha game daily tasks and combat.

### Is the Pulsar X2V2 reliable compared to Razer or Logitech?
The Pulsar X2V2 uses high-end components like the PAW3395 sensor, but as a smaller brand, its software and quality control can occasionally be less consistent than the "big two." However, it offers nearly identical performance at a much lower price point, making it a favorite for enthusiasts.

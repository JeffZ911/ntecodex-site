# NTE Disk Enhancement Guide for Beginners: How to Upgrade Arcs and Gear

In Neverness to Everness (NTE), the term "Disk" refers to the core weapon system known as Arcs. These devices are the primary source of power for your Espers, dictating their base stats and providing unique passives that can change the flow of combat. Beyond the Arcs themselves, the "Console" system introduces a layered gear mechanic involving Cartridges and Modules. This guide breaks down how to navigate these systems to maximize your team's efficiency from day one.

## Overview

![Overview](/img/nte-disk-enhancement-guide-beginners/inline-1.webp)


The progression system in NTE is divided into three distinct pillars: Leveling, Ascension, and Mixing. Unlike traditional RPGs where a weapon simply gains stats, Arcs require a combination of specialized liquid materials and duplicate items to reach their full potential.

### What are Arcs (Disks)?

Arcs are the "Disks" you equip to your Espers. Each Arc belongs to one of five elemental or physical types: Plasma, Solid, Gas, Liquid, or Synthesis. To achieve maximum stat synergy, you must match the Arc’s type to the Esper’s preferred combat style. An S-Rank Plasma Arc equipped to a Plasma-type Esper will yield significantly higher bonuses than if equipped to a Solid-type character.

Enhancement follows a strict path:
*   **Leveling:** Using "Dye" to increase the base level and stats (ATK, HP, DEF).
*   **Ascension:** Using "Pigments" and Beetle Coins to break level caps at specific intervals (e.g., Level 20, 40, 60).
*   **Mixing:** Consuming duplicate copies of the same Arc to upgrade the unique passive ability, which can be done up to five times.

### Understanding the Console: Cartridges vs. Modules

The Console is your secondary gear hub. It houses two types of equipment:
1.  **Cartridges:** These function as traditional artifacts. They provide one main stat and four sub-stats. A key feature in NTE is that all sub-stats are visible immediately upon acquisition; there is no hidden RNG that reveals stats as you level the item.
2.  **Modules:** These are Tetris-shaped stat blocks that you must manually fit into a grid within the Console. Fitting these pieces correctly allows you to activate "Sonata" effects—powerful set bonuses that trigger when specific shapes or colors are aligned.

### Farming Enhancement Materials: Character Pixels and Anomaly Zones

To keep your gear updated, you will need a steady supply of Beetle Coins and Dye. The primary source for these is the Anomaly Zones scattered across the city.
*   **Dye:** Primarily farmed through "Character Pixel" challenges.
*   **Beetle Coins:** The universal currency for all upgrades, obtained from nearly every activity, including the Hunter Exchange.
*   **Pigments:** These ascension materials are often gated by your Tycoon Rank, which increases as you complete city missions and world activities.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

![Best Disks (Artifacts)](/img/nte-disk-enhancement-guide-beginners/inline-3.webp)


In NTE, "Best Disks" usually refers to the optimal Cartridge sets. Unlike Arcs, which are individual weapons, Cartridges work in sets of two or four to provide massive stat swings.

### Top Cartridge Sets for DPS and Support

The current endgame revolves around maximizing the "Cosmos" and "Chaos" damage types.
*   **Lost Radiance Set:** This is the mandatory set for Cosmos DPS characters like [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte). The 2-piece bonus grants Cosmos DMG, while the 4-piece bonus allows the wearer to ignore a portion of the enemy's DEF when the Esper Cycle meter is full.
*   **Discordant Echo Set:** A premier support set that increases the rate at which the Esper Cycle meter charges, allowing for more frequent character swaps.

### The Module Tetris System: How to Fit Pieces for Max Stats

The Module system is a mini-game within your build. Each Module has a shape (L-shape, Square, I-shape) and must be placed on the Console grid. 
*   **Type II and Type III Modules:** These are higher-tier pieces that occupy more space but offer premium stats like Crit Rate and Crit DMG.
*   **Sonata Activation:** You activate a Sonata bonus by filling specific patterns on the grid. For beginners, focus on the "Offensive Sonata," which requires four red-colored Modules to grant a flat ATK bonus.

### Sub-stat Priority: What to Look for in S-Rank Gear

Since sub-stats are visible from level 1, you should be picky about which Cartridges you upgrade. For DPS Espers, the priority is always:
1.  Crit DMG / Crit Rate
2.  Elemental DMG (Matching the Esper)
3.  ATK %
4.  Energy Recharge

For Supports, prioritize HP % and Energy Recharge to ensure they can survive and cycle their skills effectively.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/nte-disk-enhancement-guide-beginners/inline-5.webp)


The flow of combat in Neverness to Everness is defined by the Esper Cycle. You should rarely stay on one character for more than 10-15 seconds.

### Mastering the Esper Cycle

Watch the character icons on the right side of your screen. When an icon begins to "glow," it indicates that swapping to that character will trigger a powerful Chain Hit. This swap does not just bring in a new character; it executes a unique attack that deals significantly more damage than a standard entry.

### Hotori's Recording Window: Timing Your Redirects

Hotori players must master the "Non-Closed Time Piece" cycle. This is a 20-second window where Hotori "records" damage taken and dealt.
1.  Activate the Recording Window.
2.  Cycle through your supports (Adler/Fadia) to apply buffs.
3.  Swap back to Hotori just before the 20 seconds end to unleash the accumulated damage in a single burst.

To trigger the "Discord" status, you must stack three different elemental reactions within a short window. A standard bossing rotation looks like this:

3.  **Fadia:** Use Skill to boost the Esper Cycle meter.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/nte-disk-enhancement-guide-beginners/inline-6.webp)


**Where do I farm Carrota Coins?**
Carrota Coins are a specialized currency used for cosmetic and high-end utility items. You can farm them by heading to the Rabbit Hole in the New Herland District and completing the daily challenges there.

**Can I reset enhanced Arcs?**
[Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-17]. Currently, players are advised to invest carefully in S-Rank Arcs, as a refund mechanic for Dye and Pigments has not been confirmed in the launch build.

**What is Tycoon Rank?**
Tycoon Rank is your world progression level. It gates the Hunter Exchange shop and determines the maximum level your Arcs and Espers can reach. You increase this by completing urban tasks, finding hidden collectibles, and defeating world bosses.

**Do sub-stats increase when leveling Cartridges?**
No. In NTE, leveling a Cartridge only boosts the Main Stat. However, you "unlock" the effectiveness of your existing sub-stats at levels +5, +10, +15, and +20. This makes high-rarity Cartridges with perfect initial rolls extremely valuable.

## Overview

Tingyun is a 4-star Lightning character following the Path of Harmony. While she was available at the game's launch on April 26, 2023, she has never fallen out of favor due to her unique ability to directly provide Energy to her teammates. Her kit revolves around the "Benediction" status, which she grants to a single ally to increase their Attack and provide additional Lightning damage on their hits.

Her versatility comes from being highly Skill Point (SP) positive. Because her Benediction buff lasts for three of the recipient's turns, Tingyun only needs to use her Skill once every three turns, allowing her to spend the remaining turns using Basic Attacks to generate Skill Points for the rest of the team.

### Trace Priority
To optimize Tingyun’s performance with limited resources, prioritize her Traces in the following order:

1.  **Ultimate (Amidst the Rejoicing Clouds):** This is her most important tool, restoring 50 Energy (60 at Eidolon 6) to a target ally and increasing their DMG by a significant percentage.
2.  **Skill (Soothed Breath):** Increases the target ally's ATK based on a percentage of Tingyun's own ATK. This is her primary buffing mechanic.
3.  **Talent (Violet Sparknado):** Allows Tingyun to deal extra Lightning DMG when she attacks an enemy that has been hit by the ally with Benediction.
4.  **Basic ATK (Dislodged):** Deals minor Lightning DMG. This is low priority as Tingyun is a support, though it helps with minor toughness breaking.

### Tingyun's Role in the Meta
Tingyun is often referred to as the "secret 5-star" of Honkai: Star Rail. Her ability to cut down the time between a DPS character's Ultimates is invaluable for characters like Jing Yuan, Argenti, and Dan Heng • Imbibitor Lunae. Unlike other Harmony units that focus purely on stat boosts, Tingyun’s Energy restoration can fundamentally change a character's rotation, making her a staple in Memory of Chaos and Pure Fiction clears.

### Key Traces and Ascension Passives
*   **Hidden Strategy (Ascension 2):** Tingyun's SPD increases by 20% for 1 turn after using her Skill. This helps her maintain a high turn frequency to generate SP.
*   **Jubilant Passage (Ascension 4):** Tingyun immediately regenerates 5 Energy at the start of her turn. This is crucial for hitting specific Energy thresholds for her 3-turn Ultimate rotation.
*   **Knotted Chopping (Ascension 6):** Increases Basic ATK DMG by 40%. While not essential for support, it adds a small amount of chip damage during her SP-generating turns.

### Top 5-Star Light Cones
**But the Battle Isn't Over** is Bronya's signature weapon but works exceptionally well on Tingyun. It increases the wearer's Energy Regeneration Rate by 10%–18% and recovers 1 Skill Point when the wearer uses their Ultimate on an ally. Additionally, when the wearer uses their Skill, the next ally taking action deals 30%–50% more DMG. This requires careful speed tuning to ensure the DPS acts immediately after Tingyun.

### Best F2P and 4-Star Options
**Dance! Dance! Dance!** is arguably Tingyun's best general-use 4-star Light Cone. Since Tingyun uses her Ultimate frequently, the team-wide Action Advance (16%–24%) can effectively "buy" extra turns for your entire squad, which is critical in time-sensitive modes like Memory of Chaos.

**Memories of the Past** is another strong contender, especially at Superimposition 5 (S5). It provides 8 Energy every time the wearer attacks. This allows Tingyun to reach her Ultimate faster while also contributing to Weakness Break efficiency.

### The Power of Meshing Cogs
It is rare for a 3-star weapon to be meta-relevant, but **Meshing Cogs** at S5 is a premier choice for Tingyun. It grants 8 Energy whenever the wearer attacks or is hit. When combined with an Energy Regeneration Rate Rope and Tingyun's "Jubilant Passage" passive, this Light Cone guarantees a 3-turn Ultimate rotation (Skill-Basic-Basic). For many players, the consistency of having Tingyun's Ultimate ready every three turns outweighs the higher base stats of 4-star or 5-star alternatives.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

Tingyun's relic requirements are unique because she needs a specific amount of ATK to maximize her Skill's buff, but she also requires high SPD and survivability.

### Recommended Relic Sets
*   **Sacerdos' Relived Ordeal (4-pc):** This is the premier set for Tingyun. The 2-piece set increases SPD, while the 4-piece set provides a stacking Crit DMG buff to the ally targeted by the wearer's Skill. Since Tingyun frequently targets her main DPS with her Skill, she can maintain high uptime on this powerful offensive buff.
*   **Messenger Traversing Hackerspace (4-pc):** A classic support choice. The 2-piece provides 6% SPD, and the 4-piece increases the entire team's SPD by 12% for 1 turn when the wearer uses their Ultimate. This is excellent for hitting team-wide SPD breakpoints.
*   **Musketeer of Wild Wheat (4-pc):** This set is the easiest to farm and provides 12% ATK and 6% SPD. It is highly effective for players struggling to reach Tingyun's ATK cap.

### Planar Ornament Options
*   **Broken Keel:** Increases the wearer's Effect RES by 10%. If Effect RES is 30% or higher, all allies receive a 10% Crit DMG boost. This is generally the best option for boosting team damage.
*   **Fleet of the Ageless:** Increases the wearer's HP by 12%. If the wearer's SPD is 120 or higher, all allies gain 8% ATK. This is great for survivability and helping the DPS reach higher ATK totals.
*   **Penacony, Land of the Dreams:** Increases Energy Regeneration Rate by 5% and boosts DMG for allies of the same Element (Lightning). This is mandatory for "Mono-Lightning" or dual Lightning setups involving Jing Yuan, Kafka, or Acheron (though Tingyun's synergy with Acheron is limited compared to Nihility units).

### Stat Goals: The ATK Cap Explained
Tingyun’s Skill, Soothed Breath, increases an ally's ATK by a percentage of their own base ATK, but this bonus cannot exceed a certain percentage of Tingyun's current ATK. 

To maximize this buff for most Level 80 DPS characters, Tingyun generally needs between **2,400 and 2,600 total ATK**. Once you hit this threshold, further ATK investment provides diminishing returns. At that point, you should pivot all remaining sub-stat priority into SPD and survivability (HP% and DEF%).

**Main Stat Priority:**
*   **Body:** ATK%
*   **Feet:** SPD
*   **Sphere:** ATK% or HP% (Use HP% if you have already hit the ATK cap through other pieces)
*   **Rope:** Energy Regeneration Rate (Non-negotiable)

**Sub-stat Priority:**
1. SPD (Target 160+)
2. ATK% (Until the cap is reached)
3. HP% / DEF% (To prevent her from being knocked out)
4. Effect RES (To trigger Broken Keel)

## Rotation

Understanding Tingyun's rotation is the difference between a mediocre support and a meta-defining one.

### The 3-Turn Ultimate Cycle
Tingyun's Ultimate costs 120 Energy. To achieve a 3-turn rotation (meaning she uses her Ultimate every three actions), you must use an Energy Regeneration Rate (ERR) Rope and specific Light Cones.

**The Math (with S5 Meshing Cogs and ERR Rope):**
*   **Ultimate usage:** 5 Energy
*   **Skill usage:** 30 Energy + 5 Energy (from Jubilant Passage)
*   **Basic Attack 1:** 20 Energy + 5 Energy (from Jubilant Passage)
*   **Basic Attack 2:** 20 Energy + 5 Energy (from Jubilant Passage)
*   **Light Cone (Cogs S5):** 8 Energy per action (8 * 3 = 24 Energy)
*   **Total Base Energy:** 5 + 35 + 25 + 25 + 24 = 114 Energy.
*   **With 19.4% ERR Rope:** 114 * 1.194 = **136.1 Energy.**

This setup comfortably exceeds the 120 Energy requirement, even without being hit by enemies. If you use a 4-star Light Cone like *Dance! Dance! Dance!*, you may need to be hit at least once or use the Penacony Planar set to guarantee the 3-turn cycle.

### Managing Benediction Uptime
Tingyun’s Skill lasts for 3 turns of the *buffed ally*. You can track this by looking at the colored dots under Tingyun's character icon in the turn order. When only one dot remains, it means the buff will expire after the ally's next turn. You should refresh the Skill on Tingyun's next turn to ensure 100% uptime.

### Step-by-Step Turn Sequence
1.  **Technique (Gentle Breeze):** Use this twice before entering battle to start with a full Energy bar.
2.  **Turn 1:** Use Skill on the Main DPS.
3.  **Ultimate:** Immediately use Ultimate on the Main DPS (ideally after they have used their own Ultimate to avoid wasting Energy overflow).
4.  **Turn 2:** Basic Attack (Generates SP).
5.  **Turn 3:** Basic Attack (Generates SP).
6.  **Turn 4:** Refresh Skill.

## FAQ

### How much ATK does Tingyun actually need?
As of version 2.6, most DPS characters have a base ATK (Character + Light Cone) of around 1,200 to 1,300. Tingyun’s Skill at Level 10 provides a buff equal to 25% of Tingyun's ATK, up to a maximum of 50% of the ally's base ATK. To hit this cap for a 1,250 base ATK ally, Tingyun needs 2,500 total ATK. If your DPS has a lower base ATK, Tingyun needs even less.

### Why does Tingyun get attacked so often?
There is a long-standing community meme that Tingyun has a "hidden taunt" because she seems to be targeted more than other units. Statistically, her base Taunt value is 100, which is standard for Harmony units. However, because she is naturally fragile (low base HP and DEF), when she does get hit, it is much more noticeable. Building some HP% or DEF% on her Sphere or through sub-stats is highly recommended to mitigate this.

### Is E6 Tingyun necessary?
While Tingyun is excellent at E0, her Eidolons are very powerful. E6 is her most significant milestone, increasing the Energy restored by her Ultimate from 50 to 60. This 10-energy difference is often the "break point" that allows certain DPS characters to achieve a 2-turn or 3-turn Ultimate rotation that was previously impossible.

### Eidolon Priority List
1.  **E6 (Peace Brings Wealth to All):** Increases Energy restoration from 50 to 60.
2.  **E1 (Windfall Coziness):** Increases the buffed ally's SPD by 20% for 1 turn after they use their Ultimate.
3.  **E4 (Jovial Versatility):** Increases the DMG multiplier of the Benediction buff by 20%.
4.  **E2 (Luminous Bloom):** Buffed ally regenerates 5 Energy when defeating an enemy.

### Can she be used in DoT teams?
Tingyun is viable but not optimal for Damage over Time (DoT) teams. While Kafka appreciates the ATK buff and Energy restoration, DoT teams generally prefer Ruan Mei or Robin, who provide team-wide buffs that benefit both Kafka and a second DoT unit like Black Swan or Guinaifen. Tingyun's buffs are strictly single-target, which limits her effectiveness in dual-carry setups.

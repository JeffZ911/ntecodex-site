# Neverness to Everness: Should You Pull Nanally or Wait for Hotori?

Deciding how to allocate your resources in the early days of a gacha game can define your experience for months to come. In *Neverness to Everness* (NTE), the stakes are slightly different than in other titles due to the unique "No 50/50" rule and the board-game-inspired Scarborough Fair system. Currently, players are faced with the debut of [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte/), a high-octane Plasma DPS, while the shadow of the time-manipulating Hotori looms just two weeks away.

## TL;DR Verdict

![TL;DR Verdict](/img/nte-should-i-pull-nanally-hotori-guide/inline-1.webp)


If you are looking for a quick answer, here is the breakdown of the launch meta decisions

*   **Nanally** is the premier Main DPS for the launch meta, offering high mobility and Plasma-element Anima damage. She is the best choice for players who want to dominate early-game combat and world exploration immediately.
*   **Hotori** (releasing May 13, 2026) provides superior crowd control and Cosmos-element utility, making her a more versatile long-term investment for complex boss mechanics.
*   The **"No 50/50" rule** in the Scarborough Fair gacha makes pulling on the limited banner significantly safer than in other gacha games; if you land an S-Rank on the limited board, it is guaranteed to be the featured character.
*   **F2P players** should pull for Nanally if they lack a strong carry, but those with a well-built Baicang or Jiuyuan from the standard pool may prefer waiting for Hotori's unique "Time Stop" utility.

### Quick Recommendation for F2P Players
For Free-to-Play (F2P) players, Nanally is an excellent "account starter." Because she is an Anima-type character with Plasma damage, she clears the initial story content and Anomaly Pilgrimages with ease. However, if you managed to snag **Jiuyuan** or **Baicang** from your discounted beginner pulls, your DPS needs are likely met for now. In that case, saving for Hotori—who acts as a force multiplier for your entire team—is the more strategic move.

### Quick Recommendation for Spenders
For those willing to invest, Nanally’s banner is high-value due to the launch celebration bonuses. Pulling for Nanally also puts you on the path to obtaining the **Phoenix Kick** outfit and the **Tiger Incoming! M1000** vehicle livery. Since pity carries over between limited boards, spenders can safely pull for Nanally and use any leftover "Board Modification" progress to secure Hotori later.

## Detailed Comparison

![Detailed Comparison](/img/nte-should-i-pull-nanally-hotori-guide/inline-3.webp)


To understand whether you should pull now or wait, we need to look at the mechanical "guts" of both characters. NTE combat relies heavily on "Esper Cycles" and "Break" efficiency, and both Nanally and Hotori interact with these systems in radically different ways.

### Nanally: The Queen of Early Game DPS
Nanally is the "Ichi-Daime" (Underboss) of the Colucci family, and her gameplay reflects that aggressive, "take-charge" attitude. She is a Plasma-element attacker who excels at high-speed strikes and battlefield control.

*   **The Ichi-Daime Playstyle:** Nanally’s core mechanic is her **Colucci Howling Technique** (Skill). When activated, she deals five instances of Anima damage and enters the **Ichi-daime's Authority** state for 12 seconds.
*   **Gravity Defiance:** While in her Authority state, Nanally gains electromagnetic adhesion. This allows her to run on walls and ceilings during combat, which isn't just a visual gimmick—it allows her to avoid ground-based AoE attacks from bosses like Morphix while maintaining constant damage uptime.
*   **The Underboss (Ultimate):** Her Ultimate summons a spectral "Underboss" that mirrors her attacks. For 6 seconds, every time Nanally strikes, the Underboss performs a follow-up attack, effectively doubling her damage output during her burst window.

Nanally is arguably the best character for clearing the "Tiger Special" Arc Research Program content, as her kit is perfectly tuned for the current boss rotations.

### Hotori: The Versatile Cosmos Contender
Releasing on May 13, 2026, Hotori represents the "Cosmos" faction. Where Nanally is about raw speed and aggression, Hotori is about tactical manipulation.

*   **Non-Closed Timepiece:** Hotori’s unique mechanic is a gauge that builds energy over time up to 120. She doesn't need to be on the field to charge this, making her an elite Sub-DPS.
*   **Present Replay:** Her skill, **Present Replay**, records the Support and Redirect skills used by your other teammates for 5 seconds. 
*   **World's Tide (Ultimate):** This is the game-changer. Hotori stops time for a period, preventing enemies (and even the mission timer) from moving. During this window, she replays every recorded skill from her teammates simultaneously. This creates a "burst window" that can delete boss health bars instantly.

Hotori is likely to have more "staying power" in the meta because time-stop mechanics are historically difficult to power-creep in ARPGs.

### The 'No 50/50' Factor: Why Pulling is Safer in NTE
One of the most significant reasons the community is asking "nte should i pull" is the generous nature of the **Scarborough Fair** system. 

Unlike other major gacha games where you have a 50% chance to lose your featured S-Rank to a standard character, NTE has removed this mechanic for limited boards. If you land on an S-Rank tile on the limited board, it **will** be Nanally (or Hotori, depending on the active banner). 

Furthermore, the **Board Modification** mechanic (soft pity) kicks in at 70 pulls. At this point, the board visually reconfigures, replacing standard "Hero Chests" with "Journey Together" tiles. This jumps your S-Rank rate from a base 1.87% to a staggering **19.59%**. Because this progress carries over between banners, you can safely pull for Nanally now; if you don't get her within 60 pulls, those 60 pulls will count toward an immediate "Board Modification" for Hotori on May 13.

### A-Rank Value: Are Edgar, Mint, and Adler Worth It?
The A-Rank characters featured on Nanally's banner are surprisingly high-tier
*   **Mint (Anima):** A whirlwind specialist who provides excellent grouping. She is the perfect partner for Nanally, as she can suck enemies into a tight pack for Nanally’s AoE Plasma kicks.
*   **Adler (Incantation):** The "Golden Edge Butler." He uses a Karma mechanic to provide shields to the team. In the early game where players are still learning boss patterns, Adler’s **Blessing Shield** is a literal lifesaver.
*   **Edgar (Cosmos):** Provides essential elemental coverage for Cosmos-weak anomalies found in the later stages of Hethereau.

### Weapon Banner (Arc Research) Considerations
Nanally’s signature Arc, **Ready-Ready**, is a Plasma-type weapon that provides a massive 24% CRIT Rate at max level. Its unique skill, **Commander Tiger Talisman**, increases damage to Bosses by 10%. 

However, F2P players should be aware of **Good Boy's Grand Adventure**. This S-Rank Gas Arc is available for **free** by completing the "Morphix" Anomaly Pilgrimage. While it doesn't offer the CRIT synergy of *Ready-Ready*, its team-wide ATK buff makes it a viable alternative if you want to save your Tri-Keys for a future weapon.

## Recommendation

![Recommendation](/img/nte-should-i-pull-nanally-hotori-guide/inline-4.webp)


The decision ultimately comes down to your current roster and your long-term goals in *Neverness to Everness*.

### Pull for Nanally if...
*   You do not have a dedicated S-Rank Main DPS (like Baicang or Jiuyuan).
*   You enjoy fast-paced, high-mobility combat and want to utilize the wall-running mechanics.
*   You want the limited-time **Phoenix Kick** outfit and other launch celebration cosmetics.
*   You are planning to build a mono-Anima team with Mint and Sakiri.

### Save for Hotori if...
*   You already have a strong DPS and need a "force multiplier" support.
*   You prefer a technical playstyle that revolves around "recording" and "replaying" team abilities.
*   You are struggling with timed challenges where her "Time Stop" Ultimate would provide a massive advantage.
*   You want to build a Solid-element team to counter specific end-game bosses.

### The Standard Selector Strategy
Don't forget that NTE offers a **Standard S-Rank Selector** after your first 50 pulls on the Standard Board (which only costs 40 Fabricated Dice due to the 20% launch discount). 

If you pull **Nanally**, you should use your selector on **Sakiri**. Sakiri’s Incantation-based buffs and crowd control pair perfectly with Nanally’s aggression. If you decide to wait for **Hotori**, using the selector on **Jiuyuan** provides a strong DPS that Hotori can support with her time-manipulation abilities.

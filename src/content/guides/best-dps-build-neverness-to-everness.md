## Best Disks (Artifacts)

Artifacts in NTE are called "Cartridges." These are equipped in sets of four, with significant bonuses unlocked at the 2-piece and 4-piece milestones. To further customize your build, you use "Modules," which provide fixed main stats like Flat ATK and HP.

### Top-Tier Cartridge Sets

For Nanally and any Anima-based DPS, the "Fireflies and the Forest" set is mandatory. The 2-piece bonus provides a flat 15% increase to Anima DMG. However, the 4-piece bonus is the real prize: it grants a "Luminescence" stack whenever an Anima reaction occurs, providing up to 56% CRIT DMG at maximum stacks. This synergizes perfectly with the Blossom reaction.

If you are running a Physical or Lakshana-based DPS like Jiuyuan, "Street Boxer" is the superior choice. It maximizes CRIT Chance by granting a 4% CRIT Rate boost (stacking up to 5 times) every time you land a critical hit. For a universal set that works on any DPS, "Lost Radiance" is highly recommended. Its 4-piece bonus provides 25% DEF ignore for 20 seconds immediately after casting an Ultimate, which is invaluable for clearing late-game "Anomaly" floors.

### Module and Substat Optimization

When farming Modules, you must prioritize your substat "rolls" to avoid diminishing returns on ATK%. Because NTE uses a flat defense calculation for enemies, CRIT stats and Elemental DMG bonuses are almost always superior to raw ATK in the endgame.

**Recommended Substat Priority:**
1. **CRIT Rate:** Aim for at least 60% to ensure consistent Underboss procs.
2. **CRIT DMG:** Aim for a 1:2 ratio with your CRIT Rate (e.g., 60% Rate / 120% DMG).
3. **Charge Efficiency:** Essential for Nanally to keep her Ultimate active. You need roughly 130% to maintain a "one-rotation" Ultimate cycle.
4. **ATK%:** A solid secondary stat, but lower priority than CRIT.
5. **Flat ATK:** Only desirable if the other three stats are already perfect.

## Rotation

To achieve the "Best DPS" status, you cannot simply mash buttons. You must follow a strict rotation to ensure that all buffs are active when Nanally begins her attack string. The goal is to maximize the uptime of the "Underboss" and ensure every hit is a critical strike within the Blossom window.

### Opening Setup

The rotation begins with **Zero**. You must cast Zero's Skill immediately to apply the Cosmos element and start the Esper Cycle meter. Zero’s "Rotation Accelerator" passive ensures that the rest of the team's cooldowns are slightly reduced upon this initial hit. 

Next, switch to **Sakiri**. Use her Skill to activate the "Covering Pattern" buff. If there are multiple enemies, use her "Devour Whole" ability to group them together. This grouping is vital because Nanally’s Underboss has a fixed attack radius; if enemies are scattered, your DPS will drop by over 50%.

### Maximizing the Underboss Window

Once the field is set, deploy **Nanally**. 

1. **Activate Skill:** Use "Colucci Howling Technique." This puts Nanally into the "Ichi-daime's Authority" state, enhancing her next few attacks.
2. **Trigger Ultimate:** Cast Nanally’s Ultimate immediately. This summons the "Underboss."
3. **Execute the Combo:** Perform 3 Basic Attacks followed by 3 Charged Attacks. 
4. **Repeat:** Continue this 3B-3C pattern until the Underboss disappears. 

The 3-Basic/3-Charged combo is the most efficient way to scale Anima damage because the third Charged Attack has a massive multiplier that "pops" any Genesis Buds created by the Blossom reaction. If timed correctly, the enemy's shield will break exactly as the Underboss performs its final finishing move.

## FAQ

**Who is the best DPS in Neverness to Everness?**  
Nanally is currently the strongest DPS in the game. Her ability to summon the Underboss for follow-up attacks allows her to deal double the damage of other characters in the same timeframe. Her Anima element also allows for the powerful Blossom reaction.

**How do I get more Arcs?**  
Arcs can be obtained through limited-time banners using "Resonance Strings," by defeating field bosses in the open world, or by crafting them in the Midas Arc Workshop using materials gathered from Anomaly rifts.

**What is the difference between Cartridges and Modules?**  
Cartridges are the "set" pieces of your gear. Equipping 2 or 4 of the same type grants powerful set bonuses. Modules are the individual components slotted into the Cartridges that provide fixed main stats (like HP or ATK) and random substats.

**Can I play the main character (Zero) as a DPS?**  
Yes. While Zero is often used as a "Rotation Accelerator" or Cosmos support, their burst damage is quite high. If you equip Zero with the "Lost Radiance" set and a high-ATK Arc, they can function as a primary Cosmos DPS, especially in the early to mid-game.

**What is the "Esper Cycle" in combat?**  
The Esper Cycle is a mechanic that tracks elemental interactions. As you trigger reactions like Blossom or Overload, the cycle meter fills. When full, it triggers a "Zone Effect" that buffs your team and weakens enemies, usually leading to a "Break" state where the enemy takes 200% more damage.

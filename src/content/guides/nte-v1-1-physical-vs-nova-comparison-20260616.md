To identify the best gear for the 2026 gaming landscape, we looked beyond simple frequency response graphs. We analyzed community feedback from r/HeadphoneAdvice and r/Gaming_Headsets to find shells that users can wear for a full workday without discomfort. 

Our selection process prioritized "non-fatiguing" sound signatures. In Neverness to Everness, repetitive sounds like the engine hum of your customizable vehicle or the high-pitched "ping" of Anomalies can become grating on headsets with sharp treble spikes. We specifically chose the Truthear Nova for its Harman 2019 tuning, which smooths out these harsh frequencies.

We verified that the Arctis Nova 7's 38-hour battery and the Nova Pro’s dual-battery system can actually sustain these sessions without requiring a tethered charging cable.

## What a long-session grinder actually needs

![What a long-session grinder actually needs](/img/nte-v1-1-physical-vs-nova-comparison-20260616/inline-2.webp)


For a dedicated player chasing the latest Nanally banner or completing daily commissions in the city of Hetero, weight is the most critical factor. Every gram matters after the four-hour mark. We look for IEMs under 6g per side or headsets under 300g to minimize neck strain and outer-ear pressure. The NiceHCK NTE v1.1 Physical excels here with its 4g resin build, making it feel almost weightless.

Breathability is the second pillar of long-term comfort. Standard closed-back gaming headsets often use protein leather earpads that trap heat, leading to "hot ears" and sweat build-up. For players in warmer climates or those prone to moisture issues, we recommend the [Sennheiser HD 560S](https://www.rtings.com/headphones/reviews/sennheiser/hd-560s) because its open-back design and velour pads allow for constant airflow to the ear canal.

Imaging takes precedence over heavy bass in an urban RPG context. While boomy explosions are fun, hearing the direction of a teammate's call during a complex Esper battle is vital. The Truthear Nova’s hybrid driver configuration provides the spatial accuracy needed to pinpoint sound sources within the 3D environment of NTE.

Finally, gacha players often juggle multiple devices. If you are grinding NTE on your PC while keeping an eye on mobile notifications, the ability to mix audio from two sources is a high-value feature. The SteelSeries Arctis Nova 7 allows for simultaneous 2.4GHz and Bluetooth connections, so you never miss a notification while immersed in a raid.

## Comparison summary

![Comparison summary](/img/nte-v1-1-physical-vs-nova-comparison-20260616/inline-3.webp)


The NiceHCK NTE v1.1 Physical is the undisputed value leader for those who want a "disappearing" feel on a budget. At $29, it offers a lightweight resin shell that avoids the physical fatigue common with heavier metal IEMs. It is the perfect choice for players who prioritize comfort over technical resolution during repetitive tasks like daily resource gathering.

The Truthear Nova is the technical powerhouse in this comparison. For $149, it provides the best audio fidelity for the orchestral and electronic soundtracks found in Neverness to Everness. However, this comes at a cost: its 6.2mm nozzle diameter is larger than many competitors, which can cause inner-ear soreness for users with smaller ear canals.

The SteelSeries Nova line (7 and Pro) dominates the convenience category. The Nova 7 offers exceptional wireless freedom for $179, making it the practical choice for most home setups. The Nova Pro Wireless, while expensive at $349, is the ultimate tool for hardcore MMO raid leaders who need Active Noise Cancellation to block out background distractions and a hot-swappable battery to ensure they never go silent mid-boss-fight.

The Sennheiser HD 560S remains the gold standard for players who prioritize ear health and a natural soundstage. If you play in a quiet room and don't need a microphone, the HD 560S offers a level of comfort and "airiness" that no IEM or closed-back headset can match.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/nte-v1-1-physical-vs-nova-comparison-20260616/inline-4.webp)


If you already own a high-quality pair of studio headphones, such as the [Beyerdynamic DT 770 Pro](https://www.rtings.com/headphones/reviews/beyerdynamic/dt-770-pro), switching to the NTE v1.1 Physical will feel like a significant downgrade in build quality and detail retrieval. While the NTE v1.1 is comfortable, it is built to a price point and cannot compete with the durability of professional studio gear.

You should avoid the Truthear Nova if you have experienced pain with standard IEMs in the past. The 6.2mm nozzle is physically larger than the industry average, and no amount of tip-swapping can change the diameter of the hard plastic stem. If you have small ears, the smaller shell of the NTE v1.1 or a full-sized headset like the HD 560S is a safer ergonomic bet.

Furthermore, the Arctis Nova Pro Wireless is an unnecessary expense if you do not plan to use its Active Noise Cancellation (ANC). The standard Arctis Nova 7 provides nearly identical comfort and the same dual-wireless capability for roughly half the price. Unless you are playing in a noisy environment or specifically need the infinite battery system, the Nova 7 is the more logical financial choice.

Finally, we did not include generic "gaming" headsets from chair brands like DXRacer. These products often focus on aesthetics over acoustic engineering, resulting in poor driver tuning and heavy, non-breathable materials. In the context of a high-fidelity game like Neverness to Everness, which utilizes Unreal Engine 5 for its environmental audio, these headsets fail to deliver the necessary clarity and comfort.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which audio gear is best for hearing footsteps in Neverness to Everness?
The Truthear Nova offers the best directional imaging due to its hybrid driver setup, allowing you to pinpoint the location of enemies or NPCs in the crowded streets of Hetero. However, if you prefer a wider, more natural sense of space, the Sennheiser HD 560S provides a superior soundstage that makes the open world feel much larger.

### Are IEMs like the NTE v1.1 Physical better than over-ear headsets for long sessions?
IEMs are generally better for preventing neck strain because they weigh significantly less than over-ear headsets. However, they can cause ear canal moisture build-up or irritation over 8+ hours. If you suffer from ear sensitivity, an open-back headset like the HD 560S is often the healthier long-term choice.

### Can I use the Arctis Nova 7 with both my PC and my phone at the same time?
Yes, the Arctis Nova 7 supports simultaneous audio, meaning you can listen to your NTE game audio via the 2.4GHz dongle while connected to your phone via Bluetooth. This is ideal for players who want to listen to podcasts or take calls while completing their daily gacha "sweeps."

### Is the Arctis Nova Pro Wireless worth the extra money over the Nova 7?
The Nova Pro Wireless is worth it if you play for 10+ hours a day and cannot afford to stop and charge your headset, thanks to its swappable battery system. It also features Active Noise Cancellation, which is essential if you play in a noisy household or want to block out the sound of loud PC fans.

The selection process for these storage upgrades focused on the unique demands of live-service open-world RPGs. Unlike single-player games that you might install once and finish, titles like Wuthering Waves and Genshin Impact are constantly evolving. This evolution brings frequent, massive data writes during multi-gigabyte updates. We prioritized drives that demonstrate high sustained write speeds rather than just short bursts of speed. This ensures that when a new version drops, your system doesn't crawl to a halt during the verification and installation phases.

Capacity was another non-negotiable factor. With Genshin Impact requiring [over 100GB of space on PC](https://genshin.hoyoverse.com/en/news/detail/123565) and Wuthering Waves following a similar trajectory, 512GB drives have become a bottleneck for players who enjoy multiple games. We set a 1TB minimum for our recommendations to provide enough "breathing room" for future patches. Reliability data was also a cornerstone of our research; we looked at Backblaze drive statistics to select brands and models with low failure rates over years of daily operation.

## What a dual-gacha player actually needs

![What a dual-gacha player actually needs](/img/wuthering-waves-vs-genshin-impact-storage-solutions/inline-2.webp)


When managing a library that includes Wuthering Waves, Genshin Impact, and the recently released Neverness to Everness, the technical requirements go beyond simple capacity. These games use sophisticated asset-streaming techniques to maintain a seamless open world. 

### High Random Read IOPS
Open-world games stream textures, character models, and audio files constantly as you move through the environment. A drive with high random read performance (measured in IOPS) prevents the micro-stuttering that occurs when the game engine has to wait for data. This is particularly noticeable in Neverness to Everness, where the Unreal Engine 5 architecture relies heavily on fast data throughput to render dense urban environments like Hesperia without loading screens.

### Thermal Management
Installing a 100GB+ update is a heavy workload that generates significant heat. If an SSD gets too hot, it will "throttle," or intentionally slow down to protect its hardware. We selected drives like the Samsung 990 Pro because they feature advanced thermal controllers. For players on the PlayStation 5, using a drive with a dedicated heatsink is essential to meet <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Sony%27s+internal+expansion+requirements&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Sony's internal expansion requirements</a>.

### Future-Proofing for Live Services
The storage footprint of a gacha game only grows over time. Genshin Impact launched at a fraction of its current size; years of new regions, voiced dialogue, and cinematic cutscenes have tripled its requirements. By choosing 2TB options for PC, players ensure they won't have to perform another hardware upgrade in two years when new regions are added to Wuthering Waves or when Neverness to Everness expands its supernatural city limits.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/wuthering-waves-vs-genshin-impact-storage-solutions/inline-4.webp)


Not every player needs to rush out and buy a new drive. If you are currently using a PCIe 3.0 NVMe drive and still have at least 20% of your total capacity free, the real-world loading difference when moving to a PCIe 4.0 drive is often less than two seconds. While the benchmark numbers look impressive, the actual "feel" of the game during play remains largely the same on older NVMe hardware.

You should also avoid upgrading your microSD card if your device—such as an older smartphone or a budget tablet—doesn't support the A2 bus speed. In these cases, the hardware becomes the bottleneck, and you won't see the performance benefits you're paying for with a premium card. Finally, if you only play on PC and have an empty secondary mechanical HDD (Hard Disk Drive), do not move these games there. The slow seek times of mechanical drives cause significant texture pop-in, audio desync, and can even lead to crashes during the high-speed urban traversal found in Neverness to Everness.

## What we didn't include and why

![What we didn't include and why](/img/wuthering-waves-vs-genshin-impact-storage-solutions/inline-5.webp)


We intentionally left out several popular storage categories that no longer meet the standards for high-end gacha gaming. SATA SSDs, while significantly cheaper than NVMe options, are limited to a maximum speed of about 560MB/s. This interface is increasingly becoming a bottleneck for modern asset streaming, especially as developers begin to optimize for the DirectStorage API.

Generic or "no-name" 2TB MicroSD cards found on various marketplaces for under $30 were also excluded. These are almost universally fake-capacity drives that use firmware hacks to report a larger size than they actually possess. Once you exceed the actual physical capacity—often as low as 32GB or 64GB—the card will begin overwriting your existing game files, leading to data corruption and the need for a full re-install.

Lastly, we avoided QLC-based (Quad-Level Cell) NVMe drives for this specific use case. While drives like the Crucial P3 are excellent for general file storage, their write speeds can drop to HDD-like levels during the massive, sustained writes required for 100GB game installations. For a "live service" player who downloads large patches every six weeks, the longevity and sustained performance of TLC (Triple-Level Cell) drives like the 990 Pro or SN850X are worth the extra investment.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much storage do I need for both Wuthering Waves and Genshin Impact?
On PC, you should reserve at least 250GB for both games combined to account for the initial installation and the temporary space required for unzipping future patches. On mobile devices, the combined footprint is smaller, typically requiring around 60GB to 80GB depending on how many localized voiceover packs you have installed.

### Can I play Wuthering Waves or Neverness to Everness from an external SSD?
Yes, you can play these titles from an external SSD like the Samsung T7 Shield, provided you use a USB 3.1 Gen 2 or faster port. While loading times might be slightly longer than an internal NVMe drive, the gameplay experience remains smooth as long as the drive maintains a consistent connection speed.

### Does the storage speed affect my gacha pull luck or combat performance?
Storage speed has no impact on the mathematical outcomes of gacha pulls or the damage numbers your characters produce. However, it significantly impacts combat performance by reducing stuttering during character swaps and ensuring that environmental assets load correctly before you enter a fight.

### Is a 1TB SSD enough for the next three years of updates?
A 1TB SSD is currently sufficient for three to four major gacha titles, but it may become tight as games like Genshin Impact and Neverness to Everness continue to add new regions. If you plan on keeping your PC for several years without another upgrade, a 2TB drive is the safer long-term investment for a dedicated gacha player.

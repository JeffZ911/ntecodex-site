## Overview

![Overview](/img/nte-for-beginners-build-guide/inline-1.webp)


Neverness to Everness (NTE) distinguishes itself from other urban RPGs through its high-speed, swap-heavy combat. Success in Hethereau is not determined by a single "hyper-carry" unit, but by how efficiently you can cycle through your four-person team to trigger elemental synergies. 

The core of the game’s progression is the "Esper Cycle," a mechanic that rewards players for switching characters at specific energy thresholds. When an Esper exits the field, they leave behind a residual elemental trace; the incoming Esper then consumes this trace to trigger a powerful reaction. To maximize this, you must align your characters into "Elemental Lanes," ensuring that your team composition covers the necessary reactions to break enemy shields and maximize stagger windows.

Building a character involves three primary components: Arcs (the weapons that define your combat style), the Console (a modular artifact system), and the Skill Tree. For beginners, focusing on the synergy between the protagonist, Zero, and easily obtainable A-Rank units like Mint and Haniel is the most resource-efficient path to the mid-game.

### Understanding the Esper Cycle

The Esper Cycle is the engine of NTE's combat. Every character has a "Resonance Meter" that fills as they deal damage or use skills. Once full, switching to another character triggers an "Entry Attack." This attack deals massive damage and applies a specific elemental status based on the incoming character's type. 

If the previous character applied a different element, an Elemental Reaction occurs. For example, applying Anima with Mint and then switching to Zero (Cosmos) triggers "Blossom." This reaction is the cornerstone of early-game DPS because it summons Vita Pistils—automated projectiles that track and strike enemies even while you are performing other actions.

### The Importance of Elemental Lanes

In NTE, enemies possess "Anomaly Shields" that are highly resistant to standard physical damage. To break these shields, you must use the correct Elemental Lane. There are five primary elements: Anima, Cosmos, Psyche, Incantation, and Chaos.

## Best Weapons

![Best Weapons](/img/nte-for-beginners-build-guide/inline-2.webp)


Weapons in NTE are known as Arcs. These are not merely stat sticks; they are categorized into five distinct types—Plasma, Solid, Gas, Liquid, and Synthesis—which dictate the Esper's attack speed, range, and energy generation.

*   **Plasma Arcs:** Focused on high attack speed and rapid Resonance Meter generation.
*   **Solid Arcs:** Slower, heavy-hitting weapons that excel at breaking enemy poise.
*   **Gas Arcs:** Typically used by support units for long-range application of elements.
*   **Liquid Arcs:** Specialized in enhancing elemental reaction damage and DoT effects.
*   **Synthesis Arcs:** Hybrid weapons that offer a balance of ATK and utility.

You do not need to spend real-world currency to obtain high-tier Arcs. The most reliable method is through "Anomaly Commissions." As you clear higher floors of these combat challenges, you earn "Anomaly Shards," which can be traded in the Commission Shop for specific S-Rank Arcs.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

![Best Disks (Artifacts)](/img/nte-for-beginners-build-guide/inline-3.webp)


The gear system in NTE is handled through the "Console." This is a highly customizable interface where you equip "Cartridges" and "Modules." Unlike traditional RPGs where you simply equip an item into a slot, the Console uses a spatial puzzle mechanic.

### Cartridges vs. Modules Explained

**Cartridges** act as the base frame for your gear. They determine the set bonuses you can activate. For instance, an "Eclipse" Cartridge provides a 2-piece bonus of +10% Cosmos Damage and a 4-piece bonus that increases Ultimate damage after an Entry Attack.

**Modules** are the individual stat components. These come in various Tetris-like shapes (L-shape, T-shape, square). Each Module has a main stat (such as ATK, Crit Rate, or HP) and several sub-stats. To activate the Cartridge's set bonuses, you must fit the required number of Modules into the Console's grid without overlapping them. 

This system requires you to balance high-stat Modules with their physical size. Sometimes, a smaller Module with slightly lower stats is better because it allows you to fit the final piece needed to trigger a 4-piece set bonus.

### Recommended Cartridge Sets for Beginners

1.  **Speedy Hedgehog (2-pc/4-pc):** This is the gold standard for beginners. The 2-piece bonus increases Move Speed and ATK, while the 4-piece bonus grants a massive boost to Resonance Meter generation. It is perfect for Zero and Mint to keep the Esper Cycle moving.
2.  **Eclipse (2-pc/4-pc):** Specifically designed for Cosmos units like Zero. It maximizes the damage of his Entry Attack and Ultimate, making it the best set for boss encounters.
3.  **Guardian Protocol (2-pc):** Excellent for Adler or any shielder. It provides a 15% Shield Strength bonus, which is crucial for surviving the high-damage attacks of world bosses.

### Farming the Rabbit Hole Anomaly Zone

To obtain high-quality Modules, you must farm the "Rabbit Hole" Anomaly Zone. This domain unlocks at Hunter Level 12. For beginners, focus on the "Level 1: Neon District" floors. These floors primarily drop Blue and Purple Modules from the Speedy Hedgehog set. Do not worry about farming Orange (Legendary) Modules until you reach Hunter Level 40, as the drop rates are significantly higher at that tier.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/nte-for-beginners-build-guide/inline-5.webp)


Understanding the rotation is the difference between struggling against a boss for ten minutes and ending the fight in two. The goal is to maximize the "Stagger Window"—the period when an enemy's Anomaly Shield is broken, and they take 50% increased damage.

### Standard Beginner Rotation Loop

1.  **Haniel (Buff):** Start the fight with Haniel. Use her Skill to deploy "Hootie." This ensures your entire team has an ATK buff for the next 15 seconds.
2.  **Adler (Defense):** Switch to Adler. Cast his shield immediately. This prevents your DPS units from being interrupted during their attack animations.
3.  **Mint (Setup):** Switch to Mint. Use her "Super Claw" to apply Anima to all enemies. Stay on Mint until her Resonance Meter is full.
4.  **Zero (Burst):** Switch to Zero. His Entry Attack will consume the Anima trace to trigger Blossom (Vita Pistils). Immediately use Zero's Ultimate if the enemy is staggered.

### The Blossom Reaction Loop

The Blossom reaction is unique because the Vita Pistils it creates remain on the field for 8 seconds. During this time, you should continue attacking with Zero. The Pistils will automatically target the enemy Zero is hitting, dealing supplementary Anima damage. If you time your swaps correctly, you can have a near-permanent uptime on Blossom, effectively doubling Zero's total damage output.

### Maximizing Stagger Windows

When an enemy's shield bar (the white bar under their HP) is nearly depleted, save your Ultimate abilities. Once the bar breaks, the enemy will enter a "Staggered" state. This is the only time you should unleash Zero’s full "Day Off" Arc combo. Using Ultimates while the shield is up is a waste of resources, as the shield absorbs a significant portion of the damage.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/nte-for-beginners-build-guide/inline-6.webp)


**How do I get more Fons?**
Fons are the primary currency in NTE. The most efficient way to earn them is through the "City Tycoon" system. Manage your businesses in Hethereau and complete daily commissions. High-level business management can yield over 50,000 Fons per day.

**Which character should I pull first?**
Nanally is highly recommended as your first limited S-Rank. Her banner, "The Ichi-daime," features a character with unparalleled Anima DPS and the ability to shred Anomaly Shields faster than any other unit currently in the game.

**Can I reset my Arcs?**
Yes, NTE allows you to reset an Arc to level 1 and recover 80% of the invested materials. However, "Arc Refinement" materials are not returned. Because of this, it is best to only fully invest in S-Rank Arcs or high-tier A-Ranks like "Mind Royale."

**What is Hunter Level?**
Hunter Level is your account-wide progression rank. It is increased by completing story missions, side quests, and spending "Stamina" in Anomaly Zones. Higher Hunter Levels unlock better rewards in the Rabbit Hole and allow you to level your Espers beyond the initial level caps.

**How do I unlock the City Tycoon system?**
The City Tycoon system unlocks automatically after completing the Chapter 1 story mission "Neon Business." Once unlocked, you can begin purchasing properties and generating passive income.

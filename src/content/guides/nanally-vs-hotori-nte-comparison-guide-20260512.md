## Detailed Comparison

To understand which character fits your roster, you must look at how they handle the "Anomalies" found in the open world and the high-pressure combat of the Scarborough Fair challenges.

### Combat Playstyle: Aggression vs. Tactics

Nanally is designed for players who prefer a "greedy" on-field playstyle. Her core mechanic, **Ichi-daime's Authority**, allows her to enter a heightened state where her basic attacks trigger "Underboss" follow-up strikes. These strikes deal Anima damage in a wide arc, making her exceptionally strong against groups of enemies. Her gameplay loop revolves around building "Respect" stacks to maintain this state as long as possible.

Hotori, conversely, operates on a "record and replay" system. Her **Non-Closed Timepiece** energy management system requires the player to time her skills carefully. Instead of staying on the field to deal sustained damage, Hotori enters the fray to set up "Time Anchors." She is a tactical character who rewards players for understanding enemy attack patterns, as her effectiveness scales with how well you can "record" and "replay" high-impact moments.

### Ultimate Comparison: Sustained Pressure vs. Time-Stop Burst

Nanally’s Ultimate focuses on extending her field time. It summons the full force of the Colucci family "Underbosses" to perform a coordinated strike, resetting the duration of her Ichi-daime's Authority. It is a tool for pure, sustained DPS.

Hotori’s Ultimate, **World's Tide**, is arguably the most visually impressive ability in NTE 1.0. It triggers a localized time-stop, freezing enemies in place while Hotori enters a specialized "Katana mode." During this window, she can deliver a series of rapid slashes that ignore a portion of the enemy's defense. Once the time-stop ends, all damage dealt during the window is applied instantly, often resulting in "one-shot" potential against elite monsters.

### Exploration: Verticality vs. Time Control

Hethereau is a city built on verticality. Nanally’s exploration perk is a game-changer for players who find traditional climbing tedious. Her anti-gravity walking allows her to run directly up the sides of skyscrapers and even across the underside of bridges without consuming stamina at the same rate as other characters.

Hotori’s utility is more niche but essential for completionists. Her time manipulation allows her to freeze moving platforms, reverse the state of broken objects, and solve "Chronos Puzzles" scattered throughout the city. While she doesn't move as fast as Nanally, she is the only character capable of accessing certain locked districts that require time-based interaction.

### The Blossom Cycle: How Anima and Cosmos Interact

Despite the "Nanally vs. Hotori" debate, the developers have clearly designed them to be used together. In Neverness to Everness, the interaction between Anima (Nanally) and Cosmos (Hotori) triggers the **Blossom Cycle** reaction.

When an enemy is afflicted with the Anima status and then hit by a Cosmos attack (or vice versa), the Blossom Cycle triggers a "Soul Bloom" effect. This effect deals a burst of True Damage and reduces the enemy's resistance to both elements for 12 seconds. Because Nanally provides constant Anima application through her follow-up attacks and Hotori can "replay" those attacks using her Cosmos-based Present Replay, they create a near-infinite loop of Blossom Cycle triggers.

## Recommendation

The choice between Nanally and Hotori depends largely on your current roster and how much you value ease of exploration versus long-term meta stability.

### Who Should Pull Nanally?

You should prioritize Nanally if you are a new player who hasn't secured a reliable 5-star (S-Rank) damage dealer yet. Her ability to carry through the early-game story missions is unparalleled due to her high base stats and simple execution. Furthermore, if you find the movement in Hethereau's urban environment frustrating, her wall-running will significantly improve your experience. If you have already pulled **Zero** from the standard banner, Nanally is a perfect partner, as Zero's grouping abilities allow Nanally's AoE follow-ups to hit every target simultaneously.

### Who Should Pull Hotori?

Hotori is the choice for players who are thinking about the "Endgame" (the Scarborough Fair higher floors). Support characters with unique mechanics like time-stop and skill-recording rarely fall out of the meta. If you already have a decent DPS like **Mint** or **Sakura**, Hotori will provide more value to your account than a second Main DPS would. She is also the superior choice for players who enjoy the puzzle-solving aspects of open-world games.

### Can You Get Both as F2P?

The launch rewards for Neverness to Everness have been exceptionally generous. By completing the "Urban Beginning" event and the initial story acts, players can accumulate enough currency for approximately 140 to 160 pulls. 

Because the **Scarborough Fair gacha system** features a 90-pull guarantee with no 50/50 (meaning the featured character is guaranteed on the 90th pull if not gotten earlier), it is entirely possible for a dedicated F2P player to secure both Nanally and Hotori. The "Golden Strategy" is to pull Nanally early, then use the remaining three weeks of the version update to farm the city's hidden chests and side quests to hit the guarantee for Hotori.

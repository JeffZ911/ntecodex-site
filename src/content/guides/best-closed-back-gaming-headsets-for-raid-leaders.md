## How we picked these closed back gaming headsets

![How we picked these closed back gaming headsets](/img/best-closed-back-gaming-headsets-for-raid-leaders/inline-1.webp)


Selecting the right gear for a raid leader involves looking beyond standard marketing specs. We prioritized several key factors to ensure these headsets meet the demands of high-level play in *Neverness to Everness* and other competitive titles.

This ensures that vocal callouts are distinct and don't get lost when the game's audio peaks during intense boss phases.
2.  **Ergonomics and Long-Term Comfort:** We analyzed data from [RTINGS](https://www.rtings.com/headphones/reviews/steelseries/arctis-nova-pro-wireless-pc-ps-xbox) and community feedback to identify headsets that maintain comfort over 6-8 hour continuous sessions. Features like the ComfortMAX system were highly weighted.
3.  **Noise Isolation:** We focused on closed-back designs specifically to prevent mic bleed. In a raid, the last thing your team needs is to hear their own voices echoing back through your microphone because your earcups are leaking sound.
4.  **Connectivity and Multi-Tasking:** For leaders, the ability to mix audio sources is a major efficiency boost. We favored headsets that allow simultaneous PC and mobile audio, allowing for real-time guide consultation without leaving the game.

## What a raid leader actually needs

Leading a raid is a cognitively demanding task that requires constant communication and environmental awareness. A headset for this role must excel in specific areas:

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/best-closed-back-gaming-headsets-for-raid-leaders/inline-4.webp)


While these headsets offer significant advantages, they aren't necessary for everyone.

If you currently use an open-back headset and play in a quiet room alone, the "closed-back" isolation might feel claustrophobic and increase ear heat. Open-back headsets generally offer a wider soundstage, which some players prefer for immersion, though they are unsuitable for noisy environments or sensitive microphones.

If you already own high-end studio headphones, adding a $50 ModMic or a Shure MV7+ is often a better investment than buying a dedicated gaming headset. Studio headphones often have better driver longevity than gaming-branded alternatives.

Finally, do not upgrade solely for "7.1 Surround Sound" marketing. Most competitive players find that high-quality stereo imaging provides better spatial awareness in MMO environments than the virtualized post-processing of surround sound software.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why is a closed-back headset better for raid leaders?
Closed-back headsets provide physical isolation that prevents your game audio from leaking into your microphone. This is crucial for raid leaders who often play at higher volumes to hear mechanical cues; without this isolation, your team would hear a distracting echo of the game through your mic.

### Does the 32kHz microphone on the BlackShark V2 Pro really matter?
Yes, especially during chaotic encounters. A higher sampling rate allows the microphone to capture more detail in your voice, making it sound less "compressed" or "nasal." This clarity ensures that even whispered commands are understood by your team over loud explosions and music.

### Can I use the DT 770 PRO with a PlayStation 5?
The 80 Ohm version of the DT 770 PRO can be driven by the PS5 DualSense controller, though you may find the volume slightly lower than when used with a dedicated PC sound card or DAC. For the best experience, a small USB DAC is recommended.

### Is simultaneous Bluetooth necessary for Neverness to Everness?
While not strictly necessary, it is highly beneficial. *Neverness to Everness* features complex urban management and anomaly tracking that often requires looking at community-made maps or spreadsheets. Having your phone's audio mixed directly into your headset allows you to listen to guide videos or Discord calls without taking your eyes off the screen.

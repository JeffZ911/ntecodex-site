The specific needs of a gacha player differ from those of a standard office worker. When you are deep into a session of Neverness to Everness, several technical factors become critical:

*   **Asymmetric Beam Pattern:** Standard desk lamps bounce light off your monitor and into your eyes. You need a lamp that cuts the beam off at the screen's edge, illuminating only your mouse, keyboard, and any physical notes or guides you have on your desk.
*   **Warm Color Temperature:** Late-night sessions require 2700K to 3000K settings to minimize blue light exposure. This helps prevent the suppression of melatonin, allowing you to actually fall asleep once you finally close the game.

*   **Physical/Wireless Controls:** Fumbling for a switch behind a monitor during a boss fight or an anomaly chase is a recipe for disaster. External controllers or easily accessible touch points are a massive quality-of-life upgrade.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do monitor light bars cause glare on the screen?
No, provided they use an asymmetric optical design. High-quality bars like the BenQ ScreenBar or the Quntis Pro are engineered to direct light downward at an angle that stops exactly at the edge of the screen, ensuring no light reflects off the glass and into your eyes.

### Is a high CRI important for gaming?
Yes, especially for games with high-fidelity graphics like Neverness to Everness. A high Color Rendering Index (CRI) ensures that the light hitting your desk doesn't distort the colors you see on your peripherals or your phone, and it helps maintain a natural color balance in your environment that reduces mental fatigue.

### Can I use a monitor light bar with a curved monitor?
Most modern light bars, including the BenQ ScreenBar Halo and the Quntis Pro, come with weighted clips or specialized adapters designed to fit the curvature of 1000R to 1800R monitors. However, you should always check the specific clip dimensions if you have an ultra-wide or heavily curved display.

### Does the auto-dimming feature actually work?
The auto-dimming feature on lamps like the Quntis Pro uses a small light sensor to detect the brightness of your room. It is quite effective at preventing your desk lamp from being too bright in a dark room, which is a common mistake that can lead to eye strain just as easily as having too little light.

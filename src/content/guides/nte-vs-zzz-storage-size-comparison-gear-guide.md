### High-Speed NVMe Storage
Modern urban RPGs use high-resolution assets that require fast streaming from the disk to prevent stuttering during world traversal. In Neverness to Everness, the seamless open world and vehicle driving mechanics mean the game is constantly pulling new data from your SSD. If your drive is too slow, you will experience "pop-in" or micro-stutters as you drive through the city. A drive like the Samsung 990 Pro ensures that your [loading times](https://semiconductor.samsung.com/consumer-storage/internal-ssd/990-pro/) remain under a few seconds, even as the game world expands with new patches.

### Ergonomic Lumbar Support
Long sessions require a chair that prevents slouching, as back fatigue is the primary reason players cut sessions short. Unlike traditional RPGs, gacha games often feature "daily" tasks that can take 30 minutes, but "event" days or new story chapters can lead to 6-hour marathons. The <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Secretlab+TITAN+Evo&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Secretlab TITAN Evo</a> provides the necessary firmness to keep your spine aligned, preventing the "gamer lean" that leads to long-term discomfort.

### Multi-Device Connectivity
Since ZZZ and NTE are cross-platform, headsets and monitors with versatile connectivity are highly valued. Many players use their PC for main story content but switch to a mobile device for quick energy spending. The SteelSeries Arctis Nova 7 excels here by allowing you to stay connected to your PC via 2.4GHz wireless while simultaneously connecting to your phone via Bluetooth. This is perfect for listening to a character build guide on YouTube while you farm for [Polychromes](https://zenless.hoyoverse.com/en-us/news/124441) or upgrade materials.

## When to skip the upgrade

![When to skip the upgrade](/img/nte-vs-zzz-storage-size-comparison-gear-guide/inline-4.webp)


Not every player needs to rush out and buy the latest hardware. If your current setup meets specific criteria, you can safely save your money for in-game pulls.

If you currently have a Gen4 NVMe SSD with at least 200GB of free space, you likely won't notice a functional difference in ZZZ or NTE by upgrading to a slightly faster drive. While the 990 Pro is faster on paper, the real-world difference between a 5,000 MB/s drive and a 7,450 MB/s drive in these specific games is often less than a second of loading time.

Don't buy a 4K monitor if your GPU has less than 10GB of VRAM. Both ZZZ and NTE are visually demanding, especially with NTE's use of Unreal Engine 5 features. A 1440p monitor like the LG UltraGear is a more balanced choice for most mid-range builds, as it allows for high frame rates without overwhelming your graphics card. Running these games at 4K on a mid-tier card will often result in frame rates below 60 FPS, which is detrimental to the fast-paced combat in [Zenless Zone Zero](https://zenless.hoyoverse.com/en-us/news/124441).

Avoid "racing style" chairs under $150. These chairs typically use cheap, open-cell foam that bottoms out within months of heavy use. If you cannot afford a premium ergonomic chair like the Secretlab, you are better off using a standard mesh office chair from a reputable brand, which will provide better breathability and support than a budget "gaming" chair.

## What we didn't include and why

![What we didn't include and why](/img/nte-vs-zzz-storage-size-comparison-gear-guide/inline-5.webp)


In our search for the best gear, several popular categories were intentionally omitted because they do not meet the specific technical needs of high-fidelity urban gacha games.

We excluded SATA-based drives like the Samsung 870 EVO. While they are affordable, their 560MB/s limit is becoming a significant bottleneck for modern open-world asset streaming. As NTE utilizes Unreal Engine 5, the lack of NVMe speeds can lead to significant texture streaming issues and hitching while driving vehicles through the city.

The DXRacer Formula Series was also left out. While iconic, the aggressive bucket-seat wings are designed to mimic a race car, which often pushes the user's shoulders forward. This posture causes discomfort during the 4+ hour sessions common in this genre. We prioritized chairs with a flatter "Pebble" seat base that allows for more freedom of movement.

Finally, we did not include the standard Samsung T7 (Non-Shield) portable SSD. While portable storage is convenient, the standard T7 lacks the thermal overhead required for massive 100GB game installs. During the prolonged write process of a large update, the drive can throttle its speed significantly, making a 50GB patch take much longer than it would on an internal drive or a more robust external model.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much storage do I need for both ZZZ and NTE on PC?
You should set aside at least 200GB of free space to accommodate both games. While [Zenless Zone Zero](https://zenless.hoyoverse.com/en-us/news/124441) takes up about 57GB, Neverness to Everness is expected to require over 100GB due to its Unreal Engine 5 assets. Always leave extra room for future patches and temporary files used during the installation process.

### Can I play Neverness to Everness on a standard HDD?
It is not recommended to run NTE on a mechanical Hard Disk Drive (HDD). The game's urban open world and high-speed driving mechanics rely on fast asset streaming that only an [NVMe SSD](https://semiconductor.samsung.com/consumer-storage/internal-ssd/990-pro/) can provide. Running it on an HDD will likely result in severe stuttering, long load times, and missing textures.

### Is an ultrawide monitor worth it for gacha games?
Yes, especially for games with high-fidelity environments like NTE and ZZZ. An ultrawide monitor like the <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=LG+34GP83A-B&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">LG 34GP83A-B</a> provides a 21:9 aspect ratio, which increases your peripheral vision. This makes the urban streets of Hesperia feel more immersive and helps you track multiple enemies during the fast-paced combat encounters in ZZZ.

### Do I need a special SSD for the PS5 version of these games?
To expand your PS5 storage for ZZZ and NTE, you need an M.2 NVMe SSD that meets Sony's requirements, including a read speed of at least 5,500 MB/s and a heatsink. The WD_BLACK SN850X is a top choice because it exceeds these speeds and comes with a pre-installed heatsink that fits perfectly in the console's expansion slot.

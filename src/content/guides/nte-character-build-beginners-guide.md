Since its release on April 29, 2026, Neverness to Everness (NTE) has introduced a complex layer of urban exploration combined with high-octane elemental combat. For new players, the sheer variety of Arcs, Disks, and elemental synergies can be overwhelming. Building a character in NTE is not just about stacking high-level gear; it is about understanding how each unit fits into the Esper Cycle and how their specific Arc Type interacts with the environment and enemy defenses. This guide provides a comprehensive breakdown of the best beginner builds to ensure you can clear early-game anomalies and boss encounters with ease.

## Overview

![Overview](/img/nte-character-build-beginners-guide/inline-1.webp)


The combat in Neverness to Everness revolves around the Esper Cycle, a system that rewards players for tactical character swapping rather than staying on a single "hyper-carry." Characters are categorized by one of six Elements: Anima, Chaos, Cosmos, Incantation, Lakshana, and Psyche. Furthermore, every character belongs to an Arc Type—Synthesis, Gas, Plasma, Liquid, or Solid—which dictates the type of weapons (Arcs) they can equip.

Beginners should prioritize the "F2P Core" team, which consists of Esper Zero, Haniel, and Chiz. This trio provides a balance of energy generation, offensive buffs, and consistent damage. The primary goal for any beginner build is to establish an "Elemental Lane." This means choosing characters whose elements react favorably with one another to ensure that your elemental reaction uptime remains near 100% during combat windows.

### Understanding the Esper Cycle

The Esper Cycle is the mechanic that tracks your team's synergy. As you use Skills and Ultimates, you fill a central meter. When this meter is maximized, your next character swap triggers an "Esper Outbreak," providing a temporary boost to elemental application and damage. For beginners, the goal is to rotate through your team quickly. Starting with a character like Zero allows you to generate energy rapidly, passing those benefits to your primary damage dealers.

### The Importance of Elemental Lanes

An "Elemental Lane" refers to the sequence of elemental applications you apply to a target. For example, applying Cosmos followed by Anima creates the Blossom reaction. By structuring your team to focus on two primary elements, you create a "lane" where every swap results in a powerful reaction. This is significantly more effective than a "rainbow" team of four different elements, which often leads to disorganized reactions and lower overall DPS.

### Top Free Characters to Build First

2.  **Chiz (Cosmos):** A high-tier DPS character available for free after reaching City Tycoon level 18. Chiz provides massive burst damage and scales well into the late game.

## Best Weapons

![Best Weapons](/img/nte-character-build-beginners-guide/inline-2.webp)


In Neverness to Everness, weapons are known as Arcs. These are not interchangeable across all characters; you must match the Arc Type to the character’s specific category (e.g., Synthesis, Gas, Plasma, Liquid, or Solid). Arcs provide the bulk of your base stats and often feature unique passives that can fundamentally change a character's playstyle.

### S-Rank Signature Arcs

Signature Arcs are designed for specific S-Rank characters and are usually featured on limited-time banners. For instance, [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)’s signature Arc, "Ready-Ready," is a Gas-type weapon that significantly enhances her Anima damage output and critical hit rate. Similarly, [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide)’s signature weapon, "Good Boy's Grand Adventure," is a Solid-type Arc that focuses on defensive scaling and counter-attack damage. While these provide the highest stat ceilings, they are often difficult for beginners to obtain without significant investment in the gacha system.

### Best A-Rank and F2P Alternatives

For players who are not pulling on limited banners, several A-Rank Arcs provide excellent value. "Clear Skies" is a top-tier A-Rank Arc for Anima characters like Mint, offering a flat percentage increase to Anima damage after using a Skill. "Oraora!" is another versatile A-Rank choice that scales basic attack damage, making it a solid pick for characters who spend a lot of time on the field.

One of the most important weapons for any beginner is the "Song of the Whale." This is a powerful overworld Arc that can be obtained for free by completing exploration objectives within the city of Hethereau. It provides a balanced mix of HP and ATK, making it an ideal "stat stick" for your primary support or secondary DPS.

### How to Upgrade and Refine Arcs

Upgrading an Arc requires "Arc EXP Materials" found in Hethereau Anomaly zones. Every 10 levels, you must "Ascend" the Arc using specific boss drops to unlock the next level cap. Refinement is a separate process where you consume duplicate copies of the same Arc to increase the power of its passive ability. For F2P players, a fully refined A-Rank Arc (Refinement Level 5) is often stronger than a base-level S-Rank Arc.

## Best Disks (Artifacts)

![Best Disks (Artifacts)](/img/nte-character-build-beginners-guide/inline-3.webp)


The Disk system is NTE’s version of gear customization. It consists of two parts: Cartridges (set bonuses) and Modules (individual stat pieces). These are slotted into a "Console," which is a Tetris-like grid. The challenge lies not just in finding the right stats, but in fitting the Modules into the grid to activate the desired Cartridge set bonuses.

### Cartridges vs. Modules: How the Console Works

A Console has a limited number of squares. Each Module has a specific shape (e.g., an L-shape, a square, or a line). To activate a 2-piece or 4-piece Cartridge bonus, you must slot Modules belonging to that set into the grid. Beginners should focus on filling the grid efficiently. It is often better to have a 4-piece set bonus with average stats than a collection of high-stat Modules that do not trigger any set bonuses.

### Top 3 Cartridge Sets for Beginners

1.  **Fireflies and the Forest:** This is the premier choice for Anima DPS characters like Nanally and Mint. The 2-piece bonus increases Anima DMG, while the 4-piece bonus offers a massive boost to Crit DMG after the character triggers a Blossom reaction.
2.  **Speedy Hedgehog:** This is the universal support set. The 2-piece bonus increases Ultimate charge efficiency, and the 4-piece bonus boosts the entire team's ATK for 12 seconds after the wearer uses their Ultimate. Use this on Haniel or Zero.
3.  **Lost Radiance:** A top-tier choice for general DPS characters. Its 4-piece bonus provides 25% DEF ignore for a short duration after casting an Ultimate, which is vital for taking down high-armor bosses in the late game.

### Module Stat Priority: What to Look For

When farming Modules in the Hethereau Anomaly zones, look for the following primary stats
*   **Main DPS:** Crit Rate, Crit DMG, and Elemental DMG % (matching their element).
*   **Supports:** Energy Recharge %, ATK % (for buffers like Haniel), or HP % (for healers like Edgar).
*   **Sub-DPS:** Elemental Mastery and Energy Recharge % to ensure they can frequently apply elements for reactions.

## Rotation

![Rotation](/img/nte-character-build-beginners-guide/inline-5.webp)


The key to high damage in NTE is not mashing buttons, but following a strict rotation to maximize the Esper Cycle and elemental reactions.

### The Cycle-Buffer-DPS Flow

1.  **Start with Esper Zero:** Use Zero’s Skill immediately. His instant-proc ability is the fastest way to fill the Esper Cycle meter.
2.  **Swap to your Buffer:** Once the meter is near full or your Ultimate is ready, swap to Haniel or Sakiri. Activate their buffs (ATK increase or shields).
3.  **Switch to Main DPS:** Immediately bring in Nanally, Mint, or Chiz. This swap will often trigger the Esper Outbreak if your timing is correct. Use their Skill and Ultimate while the buffs from step 2 are still active.
4.  **Trigger Reaction:** Ensure the enemy has the "primer" element (e.g., Cosmos from Zero) before your DPS hits them with the "trigger" element (e.g., Anima from Nanally) to proc Blossom.

### Managing Break and Stagger

Enemies have a white "Break" meter below their health bar. Reducing this meter to zero will Stagger the enemy, causing them to take significantly increased damage and stop attacking. The Blossom reaction is particularly effective at reducing this meter. Using "Critical Dodges" is also essential; a perfectly timed dodge triggers a Riposte attack, which deals high Break damage and can reset the cooldown of certain character skills.

### Maximizing Ultimate Windows

Do not use Ultimates as soon as they are available. Wait until you have successfully triggered a Stagger on a boss. Once the boss is immobilized, rotate through your buffers and then unleash all your Ultimates in a single window to take advantage of the increased damage multiplier during the Stagger state.

## FAQ

![FAQ](/img/nte-character-build-beginners-guide/inline-6.webp)


**Should I reroll in NTE?**
Rerolling is not strictly necessary. The game provides several high-tier characters like Chiz and Zero for free through progression. While getting an early S-Rank like Nanally is helpful, you can clear all early and mid-game content with the F2P Core team.

**How do I unlock Chiz?**
Chiz is a free reward for reaching City Tycoon level 18. You can increase your City Tycoon level by completing main story missions, side quests in Hethereau, and finishing Anomaly challenges.

**What is the best element in Version 1.0?**
Anima and Cosmos are currently considered the strongest elements in the meta. This is largely due to the strength of the Blossom reaction and the fact that high-tier characters like Nanally (Anima) and Zero (Cosmos) are easily accessible or highly impactful.

**Where do I farm Cartridges?**
Cartridges and Modules are farmed using Character Pixels in the Hethereau Anomaly zones. These zones function as repeatable dungeons where you fight waves of enemies or bosses to earn gear. Higher difficulty levels yield S-Rank Disks with better stat distributions.

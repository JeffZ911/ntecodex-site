## Stats

![Stats](/img/best-4-star-weapons-wuthering-waves-guide/inline-1.webp)


The effectiveness of a 4-star weapon is determined by two primary factors: its Base ATK scaling and its Secondary Stat. In Wuthering Waves, 4-star weapons are generally divided into two tiers of Base ATK. High-tier 4-stars typically reach 459 Base ATK at Level 90, while utility or free-to-play options may cap at 412 or 337.

### Base ATK vs. Secondary Stat Priority

Choosing a weapon is not just about the highest number; it is about finding the right balance for your Resonator's kit. Main DPS characters usually prioritize weapons with high Base ATK and offensive secondary stats like Crit Rate or ATK%. Because Base ATK acts as the foundation for all percentage-based ATK bonuses, a weapon with a higher base value will always yield better results from your Echoes.

For example, the "Pioneer Podcast" (Battle Pass) weapons are highly valued because they combine a high 459 Base ATK with a Crit Rate secondary stat. Crit Rate is notoriously difficult to stack through Echoes alone, making these weapons the gold standard for 4-star damage dealers. Conversely, weapons with lower Base ATK often compensate with a massive secondary stat, such as Energy Regen, which is vital for specific roles.

### The Value of Energy Regen on Support Weapons

Support Resonators like Verina or Baizhi do not rely on raw damage. For these characters, a weapon like **Variation** is superior despite its low 337 Base ATK. The reason lies in its 51.8% Energy Regen secondary stat at Level 90. This allows supports to cycle their Resonance Liberations more frequently, providing consistent healing and buffs to the team. When evaluating 4-star weapons, always consider the character's "Concerto" and "Liberation" needs before dismissing a low-ATK option.

## Effect

![Effect](/img/best-4-star-weapons-wuthering-waves-guide/inline-2.webp)


A weapon's passive effect can often be more impactful than its raw stats. In Wuthering Waves, these effects trigger based on specific actions, such as casting a Resonance Skill, performing Basic Attacks, or dodging.

### Top Offensive Passives for Main DPS

The most powerful 4-star passives for damage dealers are those that provide stacking buffs or direct damage percentage increases.
*   **Autumntrace (Broadblade):** This weapon provides an ATK increase whenever the user deals Basic or Heavy Attack damage. At Rank 1, this is a 4% increase per stack (up to 5 stacks), but at Rank 5, it jumps to 8% per stack. This makes it a formidable choice for characters who stay on the field for extended periods.
*   **Lumingloss (Sword):** This weapon focuses on Resonance Skill synergy. After casting a Skill, Basic and Heavy Attack DMG is increased significantly for a short duration. This is perfect for Resonators like Rover or Danjin, whose rotations revolve around weaving skills and attacks together.

### Support Passives: Buffing the Team

Utility weapons often focus on "Concerto Energy" generation. Concerto Energy is what allows you to trigger Intro and Outro Skills, which are the backbone of high-level combat rotations.
*   **Variation (Rectifier) & Cadenza (Pistols):** Both weapons share a passive that restores 8 to 16 Concerto Energy (depending on Rank) when a Resonance Skill is cast. This effect has a 20-second cooldown but is essential for speeding up team rotations, allowing you to swap characters faster and maintain 100% uptime on buffs.

### Refinement (Synthesis) Priority Guide

Synthesizing (refining) a 4-star weapon to Rank 5 (R5) can sometimes make it more powerful than a Rank 1 (R1) 5-star weapon. 
1.  **High Priority:** Weapons with percentage-based DMG increases (e.g., Lumingloss, Stonard). These scale linearly and provide massive boosts at R5.
2.  **Medium Priority:** Weapons with flat Energy or Concerto restoration (e.g., Variation). While the extra energy is nice, the weapon functions well enough at R1.
3.  **Low Priority:** Craftable weapons with niche triggers. Unless you have an abundance of materials, focus your resources on Gacha or Battle Pass refinements first.

## How to Get

![How to Get](/img/best-4-star-weapons-wuthering-waves-guide/inline-4.webp)


Acquiring 4-star weapons involves a mix of luck, persistence, and strategic resource management.

### Gacha: Weapon Permanent and Event Banners
Most 4-star weapons are obtained through the "Convene" system. Every 10 pulls on any banner (Character or Weapon) guarantees at least one 4-star item. On the Weapon Permanent Convene, you can actually set a "Target" for 5-star weapons, but 4-stars remain random. During limited Event Banners, specific 4-star weapons receive a "Rate-Up," making them much easier to pull and synthesize to Rank 5.

### Crafting Guide: Uncle Wei’s Workshop
If Gacha luck is not on your side, you can craft weapons at Uncle Wei's shop in Jinzhou. Crafting a 4-star weapon requires
*   **1 Standard Weapon Mold:** These are rare drops from Weekly Bosses (such as the Scar or Bell-Borne Geochelone challenges).
*   **150 Indigoite:** A blue-grade ore found throughout the world.
*   **Shell Credits:** The standard in-game currency.

The craftable "Series#18" or "Series#41" weapons are decent placeholders but generally lack the high-end power of Gacha or Battle Pass weapons.

### Pioneer Podcast: Is the Battle Pass Weapon Worth It?
The "Insider Channel" of the Pioneer Podcast allows you to choose one 4-star weapon upon reaching Level 30. These weapons (Autumntrace, Lumingloss, Augment, Stonard, and Thunderbolt) are unique because they are the only 4-star weapons that provide a Crit Rate secondary stat. For players looking to optimize their Main DPS, the Pioneer Podcast is the most reliable way to secure a top-tier 4-star weapon.

## Comparison

![Comparison](/img/best-4-star-weapons-wuthering-waves-guide/inline-5.webp)


Deciding whether to invest in a 4-star weapon or hold out for a 5-star depends on your account's current needs and your willingness to refine.

### Refinement 5 (R5) 4-Stars vs. Refinement 1 (R1) 5-Stars
A common question is whether an R5 Autumntrace is better than an R1 Lustrous Razor. 
*   **Base ATK Advantage:** The 5-star Lustrous Razor has a significantly higher Base ATK (587 vs 459). This means every ATK% roll on your Echoes is worth more with the 5-star.
*   **Passive Advantage:** At Rank 5, Autumntrace provides a 40% ATK bonus at max stacks. While this is impressive, the 5-star weapon usually provides a more consistent "Energy Regen" or "Resonance Liberation DMG" bonus alongside its superior base stats.
*   **Verdict:** Generally, an R1 5-star weapon will still outperform an R5 4-star weapon by about 5-10% in total damage output. However, the 4-star is much cheaper to obtain and allows you to save your pulls for new characters.

### F2P Craftable vs. Premium Battle Pass Weapons
The gap between craftable weapons (like Broadblade#41) and Battle Pass weapons (like Autumntrace) is noticeable. Craftable weapons focus on ATK% and DEF%, which are easier to find on Echoes. The Crit Rate provided by Battle Pass weapons is much harder to acquire, making them significantly better for the "Endgame" meta. If you are a purely F2P player, focus on the Gacha 4-stars like **Lunar Cutter** or **Variation**, as they generally outclass the craftable options in high-difficulty content like the Tower of Adversity.

Future-proofing your account means investing in weapons that have versatile passives. Weapons like **Variation** and **Cadenza** will likely remain meta-relevant for years because Energy Regen and Concerto Energy are universal needs for support characters, regardless of how high the power creep for DPS characters goes.

## Effect

Beyond raw numbers, the passive abilities and Arc Skills define how a weapon functions during the fast-paced combat of Hethereau. Each Arc belongs to one of the five primary Elemental Types: Solid, Liquid, Gas, Plasma, or Synthesis. Matching these types to an Esper’s natural affinity is the first step in unlocking their full potential.

### Passive Ability Breakdown

Passive abilities in NTE are often triggered by specific combat actions, such as dodging, using a Resonance Skill, or switching characters. Since the Mortenax Blade and Evanescia are not present in the game, players cannot find passive descriptions for them. However, the Raging Flames Arc provides a clear example of a top-tier passive. It often grants a stacking DMG bonus when the wielder deals Plasma damage, rewarding aggressive playstyles.

The Ready-Ready Arc focuses more on the Synthesis element, which often involves complex interactions between different elemental states. Its passive ability is designed to reward players who utilize the game's "Urban Tycoon" or "City Life" mechanics, sometimes providing buffs based on the player's progression in city-based activities or their current Resonance level.

### Arc Skill Mechanics

Arc Skills are active or semi-active effects that trigger during Hethereau city combat. These skills can range from massive AoE explosions to defensive barriers. Plasma-type skills, like those found on Raging Flames, typically focus on high-frequency hits that can shred through Anomaly shields. Synthesis-type skills, on the other hand, might focus on debuffing enemies or creating zones of influence that benefit the entire party.

Understanding the "state" of an enemy is vital. Enemies in NTE can exist in Solid, Liquid, or Gas states, and using an Arc with the opposing Elemental Type—such as using Plasma against a Solid-state foe—can lead to massive damage multipliers.

## How to Get

![How to Get](/img/mortenax-blade-vs-evanescia-dps-comparison-20260608/inline-4.webp)


Acquiring S-Rank Arcs is a primary goal for many players. The most common method is through the Arc Research Program, which serves as the game's gacha system. Unlike characters, Arcs have their own dedicated banners where players can use Fons or specialized currency to pull for specific weapons.

### Arc Research Program Banners

The Arc Research Program typically features a "pity" system, ensuring that players receive an S-Rank Arc after a certain number of pulls. Limited-time banners often feature a specific Arc, such as Raging Flames, with an increased drop rate. It is generally recommended to save your pull currency for Arcs that synergize with your primary DPS characters, rather than pulling on every banner that appears.

### Farming and Crafting Methods

For those who prefer not to rely solely on the gacha system, Neverness to Everness offers several in-game paths to earn powerful equipment:

*   **Hunter Exchange:** By participating in bounty hunts and clearing Anomalies, players earn materials that can be traded at the Hunter Exchange for A-Rank and occasionally S-Rank Arc components.
*   **City Tycoon Rewards:** Engaging in the city's economy—buying property, managing businesses, and completing urban tasks—can reward players with Annulith and other rare currencies used to upgrade Arcs.
*   **Anomalies and Bosses:** High-level world bosses have a small chance to drop powerful equipment. Farming these regularly is a staple of the daily gameplay loop for dedicated players.

## Comparison

When comparing the top-tier Arcs in the 1.1 meta, the choice often comes down to the specific combat scenario. ### Burst vs. Sustained Damage

Raging Flames is the undisputed king of burst damage. In short encounters where the goal is to delete an enemy's health bar as quickly as possible, its CRIT DMG scaling and Plasma-type bonuses are unmatched. This makes it the preferred choice for time-trial challenges and boss speedruns.

Ready-Ready, conversely, often provides better sustained damage and utility. In longer fights—such as those found in the deeper levels of Beyond the Rails—the ability to maintain consistent DPS while providing minor team buffs can be more valuable than a single burst of power. Synthesis Arcs are particularly good at handling multiple waves of enemies with varying elemental weaknesses.

### Meta Ranking and Verdict

In the current meta, Raging Flames remains the benchmark for most DPS Espers. Its stats are simply too high to ignore, and the Plasma element is currently very strong against the most common Anomaly types found in Hethereau. However, the importance of weapon resonance—achieved by obtaining multiple copies of the same Arc—should not be overlooked. A fully resonated A-Rank Arc can often outperform a base-level S-Rank Arc in specific niches.

For players starting their journey in Neverness to Everness, focusing on the Arc Research Program to secure at least one high-quality S-Rank weapon for their lead character is a top priority. Whether you choose the raw power of Plasma or the versatile utility of Synthesis, understanding the underlying stats and effects will ensure you stay ahead of the curve in Hethereau.

## Effect

Passive abilities in NTE are tied to "Mixing Intensity," a refinement system that allows you to upgrade an Arc’s unique skill from Rank 1 to Rank 5 using duplicates. A Max-Mixed A-Rank Arc often provides more consistent value than a Rank 1 S-Rank due to these enhanced multipliers.

### Mixing Intensity: How Refinement Changes the Meta

The Mixing system (often referred to as Refinement) consumes a duplicate copy of the Arc to raise its Intensity tier. Each rank increases the potency of the Arc's passive effect. For example, the ATK boost from "First Step to Success" scales from 12% at Rank 1 to 16% at Rank 5.

**Mixing Intensity Rank 1 vs Rank 5 Effects:**

*   **Dangerous Game (Synthesis):** Increases wearer's Break Intensity by 60 (Rank 1) to 84 (Rank 5) for 10 seconds when reducing an enemy's Break gauge. This effect has a 20-second cooldown.
*   **Be Happy (Gas):** Restores the wearer's HP by 12% (Rank 1) to 20% (Rank 5) upon defeating an enemy. This provides essential sustain during mob-heavy exploration.
*   **First Step to Success (Solid):** Grants a 12% (Rank 1) to 16% (Rank 5) ATK increase for 10 seconds after casting a Redirect Skill.
*   **Us (Plasma):** Increases the wearer's Basic Attack damage by 12% (Rank 1) to 16% (Rank 5).
*   **Real Music (Liquid):** Grants the wearer a 12% (Rank 1) to 16% (Rank 5) Redirect Skill DMG bonus.

## How to Get

Acquiring A-Rank Arcs involves a mix of gacha mechanics and dedicated farming. For F2P players, the game provides several avenues to obtain and refine these weapons without spending premium currency.

### Gacha Rates and Pity Mechanics

The primary source for A-Rank Arcs is the **Scarborough Fair** gacha system. Players use Dice or Annulith to pull on banners.
*   **Arc Research Program:** This specific banner allows players to target specific Arc types (e.g., Solid or Plasma) using Tri-Keys. 
*   **Pity:** There is a guaranteed A-Rank Arc or Esper every 8 to 10 pulls, making it relatively easy to accumulate the duplicates needed for Rank 5 Mixing Intensity.

### Farming Arcs via Anomaly Zones and Quests

For those who prefer farming over the gacha, NTE offers several reliable methods
*   **Arc Shop:** Players can purchase specific A-Rank Arcs using **U-00NE currency**, which is earned by completing Anomaly content and world challenges.
*   **Anomaly Zones:** Certain zones, such as the Bridge Crossings Kaleidoscope stage, drop Arc fragments and upgrade materials like **Dye**. 
*   **World Quests:** High-level world quests and "Anomaly Zone" clears can reward specific Arcs like **Day Off** and **Hethereau’s Keeper**.
*   **Hunter Exchange:** Unlocked at **Tycoon Rank 6**, this exchange offers rotating A-Rank Arcs and essential Ascension materials for veteran players.

## Comparison

Understanding how A-Rank Arcs stack up against the elusive S-Ranks is vital for resource management. While players often search for the best 4-star weapons in WuWa or NTE to save resources, the progression curve in NTE is unique.

### A-Rank vs. S-Rank: When to Switch?

While S-Rank Arcs (like **Ready-Ready** or **Blow up the Crowd**) offer higher ceilings with base ATK values reaching 666, a Max-Mixed (Rank 5) A-Rank Arc often outperforms a Rank 1 S-Rank in specific niches. For example, a Rank 5 **First Step to Success** provides a more reliable ATK buff for F2P players than a generic S-Rank that doesn't match the Esper's playstyle.

Furthermore, A-Rank Arcs are significantly easier to ascend due to lower material costs in the **Bubble Can Factory**. For most players, it is better to invest in a high-rank A-Arc early on rather than spreading resources thin on multiple S-Ranks.

### NTE Arcs vs. WuWa Weapons: Key Differences for New Players

For players transitioning from Wuthering Waves, the A-Rank Arcs in NTE provide a similar progression curve but require more attention to "Arc Type" matching. 
*   **Arc Skills:** In NTE, weapons act more like "companions" or "ability modifiers" than the pure stat-sticks found in some other gacha games. 
*   **Break vs. Raw DMG:** NTE places a heavy emphasis on Break Intensity (Stagger), making Arcs like **Dangerous Game** the "Broadblade" equivalent for high-risk, high-reward builds that focus on opening up boss windows.
*   **Type Locking:** You cannot equip a Gas Arc on a Solid Esper. This encourages building a diverse library of Arcs rather than relying on one "universal" 4-star weapon.

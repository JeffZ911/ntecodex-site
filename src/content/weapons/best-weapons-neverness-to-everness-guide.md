### Understanding Arc Types and Synergy

Matching an Arc type to an Esper isn't just about flavor; it's a mechanical necessity. When a Plasma-type Esper like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) equips a Plasma Arc, they unlock a "Resonance Link." This link typically provides a 10-15% bonus to their primary damage type. If you equip a Solid Arc on a Plasma character, you lose this bonus, often making a max-refined A-Rank of the correct type better than an unmatched S-Rank.

### Substat Priority for DPS vs. Support

For Main DPS characters, the priority is almost always CRIT Rate > CRIT DMG > ATK%. However, the May 2026 meta has seen a shift toward "Break Intensity" for specific encounters. Bosses in the late-game [Anomaly Commissions](#TODO:keyword:nte-anomaly-guide) have massive posture bars that mitigate 50% of incoming damage until broken. ## Effect

While stats provide the foundation, the "Arc Skill" defines the weapon's identity. These are unique passives or active triggers that can fundamentally change how an Esper plays. Refinement levels (Rank 1 through 5) allow players to enhance these effects by consuming duplicate Arcs. At Rank 5, most percentage-based bonuses are doubled, making some farmable A-Rank Arcs highly competitive with Rank 1 S-Ranks.

### Top Tier Offensive Effects

The most sought-after offensive Arcs currently revolve around Basic Attack amplification and HP manipulation. 

*   **Ready-Ready (Plasma):** This Arc features the "Double Tap" effect. At Rank 1, it increases Basic Attack DMG by 20%. After using a Resonance Skill, this bonus is doubled for 8 seconds. At Rank 5, the base increase starts at 40%, reaching a staggering 80% post-skill.
*   **Camellia Society (Solid):** This Arc rewards high-risk play. Its "Wither and Bloom" effect increases the wearer's DMG by 1% for every 2% of HP missing. Additionally, when the wearer's HP drops below 30%, they gain a temporary shield and a 30% Burst DMG boost.

### Utility and Support Arc Passives

Support Arcs focus on team-wide buffs or battlefield control. The "Day Off" Arc is the standout here, featuring the "Eclipse" zone mechanic. When the wearer uses their Ultimate, they deploy a field that restores 3 Energy per second to all allies within its radius for 10 seconds. This is vital for high-cost teams that rely on frequent Ultimate rotations.

## How to Get

Acquiring S-Rank Arcs in Neverness to Everness involves a mix of luck, persistence, and exploration. While the gacha banner is the fastest route, many of the game's best weapons are hidden behind world activities or challenging boss fights.

### Farming World Bosses for S-Rank Drops

Several S-Rank Arcs are part of the loot table for World Bosses found in Hethereau City.
*   **Headless Rider (Raging Flames):** Located in the industrial district, this boss has a low percentage chance to drop the **Raging Flames** Arc. It is a high-difficulty fight that requires dodging fire-based AoE attacks.
*   **Beat King:** Found in the club district, this boss drops materials that can be crafted into the **Blow up the Crowd** Arc at the Bureau workshop.

### Anomaly Commission Rewards

Completing high-tier Anomaly Commissions is the most reliable way for F2P players to earn S-Rank gear. As you increase your Commission Rank, you unlock "Targeted Acquisitions," allowing you to spend commission tokens on specific Arcs like **Silent Garden**.

### Secret Quests and Hidden Arcs

The **Day Off** Arc is a unique reward that cannot be pulled from the gacha. To obtain it, players must complete the "Eclipse" questline. This multi-stage investigation involves finding hidden Bureau logs scattered across the city and solving environmental puzzles in the "Void Zones." Once the questline is finished, the Arc is awarded as a symbol of the player's dedication to city safety.

### Gacha and Battle Pass Exclusives

*   **Limited Banners:** Arcs like **Ready-Ready** and **Youthful Fantasy** are typically featured alongside their respective Espers on limited-time banners.
*   **Nacupeda Coins:** These are earned by completing daily activities and can be exchanged at the Mall for **Your Happiness is Priceless**, a defensive Solid Arc.
*   **The "Fluff" Series:** Weapons like **Fluff of Fleetness** are exclusive to the premium tier of the Battle Pass. They are designed to be "comfy" options that provide high stats without requiring complex gameplay triggers.

## Comparison

With so many options available, players often struggle to decide between a farmable Arc and a gacha-exclusive one. The gap is often smaller than it appears, especially when refinement levels are factored in.

### Ready-Ready vs. Raging Flames

Both are top-tier Plasma Arcs, but they serve different purposes. **Ready-Ready** is the king of sustained DPS, favoring characters who stay on the field and use Basic Attacks. **Raging Flames**, however, provides a massive burst of ATK% upon entering the field, making it superior for "Quick-Swap" teams where Espers only stay out long enough to dump their skills.

### S-Rank vs. Max Refined A-Rank

A common question in the community is whether a Rank 5 A-Rank like **Mind Royale** is better than a Rank 1 S-Rank. 
*   **Stats:** The S-Rank will always have higher Base ATK.
*   **Utility:** The Rank 5 A-Rank often provides more consistent buffs.
For most players, a Rank 5 A-Rank is the better investment for secondary team members, while your Main DPS should always strive for an S-Rank Arc due to the way ATK% buffs scale off the higher base numbers.

### The Importance of Break Intensity

In the current May 2026 meta, "Break Intensity" has become a "must-have" stat for clearing the Level 90 Anomaly floors. Arcs like **Youthful Fantasy** have surged in popularity because they allow players to bypass the damage reduction of bosses much faster. If you are struggling with time limits in the endgame, switching to an Arc with higher Break Intensity is often more effective than simply stacking more ATK.

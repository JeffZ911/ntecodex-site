# Best Disks (Arcs) in Neverness to Everness: Complete Weapon Database
> 📝 **Editorial Note:** This guide is AI-assisted and game data evolves rapidly. Please cross-reference with in-game information. _Updated: 2026-05-13._


In Neverness to Everness (NTE), weapons are referred to as Arcs (or Disks). These high-tech devices act as the primary source of raw power for your Espers, dictating not only their base damage but also their playstyle through unique Arc Skills and Passive Perks. Choosing the right Arc requires a deep understanding of scaling, compatibility types, and the specific needs of your team composition.

## Stats

![Stats](/img/best-disks-nte-guide/inline-1.webp)


Arcs serve as the foundation of an Esper's combat capabilities. Every Arc in NTE features a primary Base ATK stat and a secondary substat. As of the 1.0 launch version, the level cap for Arcs is 90. S-Rank Arcs are the gold standard for damage dealers, reaching a maximum Base ATK of approximately 666 at level 90, whereas A-Rank Arcs typically cap out around [information unavailable].

Beyond raw power, the secondary substats determine which role an Arc is suited for. These include:
*   **CRIT Rate & CRIT DMG:** Essential for Main DPS characters like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) and Chiz.
*   **ATK%:** A versatile stat that benefits almost all offensive units.
*   **HP%:** Vital for sustain-based Espers or those who scale damage based on health.
*   **Charge Efficiency:** The most important stat for Support Espers, as it ensures high uptime for ultimates.

### Understanding Arc Types and Compatibility

Every Arc is categorized into one of five distinct Types. Matching an Esper's preferred combat style with their Arc Type is crucial for maximizing synergy.

1.  **Solid:** These are balanced Arcs that provide a mix of defensive and offensive utility. They are often favored by "bruiser" style characters.
2.  **Plasma:** High-energy Arcs focused on burst damage and offensive pressure. These often carry CRIT-related substats.
3.  **Gas:** Utility-focused Arcs that provide team-wide buffs or crowd control enhancements.
4.  **Liquid:** These Arcs focus on energy regeneration and skill cooldown reduction, making them ideal for "Quick-Swap" playstyles.
5.  **Synthesis:** Hybrid Arcs that offer conditional buffs. These require specific triggers, such as performing a dodge or hitting a specific number of enemies, to activate their full potential.

### Substat Priority for DPS vs. Support

When leveling your Arcs, you must prioritize the correct substat for the character's role. For a Main DPS, any Arc without CRIT Rate or CRIT DMG is generally considered a placeholder. Conversely, for a Support Esper like Haniel, an Arc with high Charge Efficiency will outperform an S-Rank offensive Arc because it allows for more frequent team-wide healing or buffing.

## Effect

![Effect](/img/best-disks-nte-guide/inline-2.webp)


The true power of an Arc lies in its effects, which are divided into two layers: Passive Perks and Arc Skills. Unlike the raw stats, these effects provide transformative gameplay changes that can alter how an Esper interacts with the environment and enemies.

### Passive Perks vs. Active Arc Skills

**Passive Perks** are always active as long as the Arc is equipped. For example, the S-Rank Arc "Blow up the Crowd" provides a 10-20% ATK boost while the character is off-field. This makes it an incredible choice for sub-DPS units who only enter the fray to drop their abilities before swapping out.

**Arc Skills** are more complex and often require a specific trigger. A prime example is the "Silent Garden" (Camellia Society) Arc. Its Arc Skill consumes a portion of the user's HP to unleash high-damage Incantation bursts. This high-risk, high-reward mechanic is a staple of endgame builds, requiring players to manage their health carefully in exchange for massive screen-clearing damage.

### The Impact of Refinement Levels

Refinement (R1 through R5) is the process of consuming duplicate Arcs to upgrade the potency of these effects. For many A-Rank Arcs, reaching R5 is more beneficial than using an R1 S-Rank Arc.
*   **R1 to R5 Scaling:** Most percentage-based buffs (like ATK% or Elemental DMG%) double in value from R1 to R5.
*   **Cooldown Reduction:** Some Arc Skills have their internal cooldowns reduced by 20-40% at max refinement, allowing for much smoother rotations.

**Top 5 S-Rank Arc Skills:**
1.  **Ready-Ready (Nanally Sig):** Increases Boss DMG by [information unavailable]% after using a Resonance Skill.
2.  **Contemplative Cat (Chiz Sig):** Converts every point of Fons held into a 0.5% Cosmos DMG bonus.
3.  **Camellia Society:** Grants massive CRIT DMG but drains HP over time.

## How to Get

![How to Get](/img/best-disks-nte-guide/inline-4.webp)


Acquiring Arcs in Neverness to Everness involves a mix of Gacha mechanics, world exploration, and urban management.

### Gacha Rates and Pity Mechanics

The primary method for obtaining high-tier Arcs is the Arc Gacha banner, which requires Tri-Keys.
*   **Soft Pity:** Starts at 60 pulls.
*   **Hard Pity:** Guaranteed S-Rank Arc at 80 pulls.
*   **Limited Weapon Banners:** These have a "75/25" rule. There is a 75% chance the S-Rank you pull is the featured signature Arc. If you miss, the next S-Rank is guaranteed to be the featured one within 20 additional pulls (effectively a 100-pull hard guarantee).

### Farming Arcs through Anomalies and Bosses

Not all powerful Arcs are locked behind the Gacha. Several S-Rank Arcs can be farmed through gameplay:
*   **Hethereau’s Keeper:** This Arc is a potential drop from high-level Anomalies in the industrial district.
*   **Day Off:** Obtainable by defeating specific Field Bosses located in the outskirts of Hethereau.
*   **City Tycoon System:** As you progress through the urban management side of NTE, you will unlock specific Arcs and refinement materials for Chiz. This makes Chiz one of the most F2P-friendly S-Rank characters in the game.

## Comparison

![Comparison](/img/best-disks-nte-guide/inline-5.webp)


Deciding whether to pull for a signature S-Rank Arc or stick with an A-Rank alternative is a common dilemma for players.

### S-Rank vs. A-Rank: Is the Gacha worth it?

Statistically, S-Rank Arcs offer roughly 20-30% higher total stat value compared to A-Rank Arcs at the same level. However, this gap narrows significantly when considering refinement. An **A-Rank Arc at R5** often outperforms an **S-Rank Arc at R1** for F2P players. For example, an R5 "Good Boy’s Grand Adventure" provides more consistent team utility than many R1 S-Rank Arcs that lack specific synergy with your support's kit.

### Signature Arcs vs. F2P Alternatives

Signature Arcs like Nanally's "Ready-Ready" provide unique mechanics that generic Arcs cannot replicate. These often include specific elemental reaction buffs or specialized resource generation.
*   **Hethereau’s Keeper (Farmable) vs. Blow up the Crowd (Gacha):** While "Blow up the Crowd" offers higher burst potential for quick-swap teams, "Hethereau’s Keeper" provides better sustained damage for characters with summons or pets. For Jiuyuan, the farmable option is often competitive enough that pulling for a Gacha alternative isn't strictly necessary.

In the current meta, the "Contemplative Cat" remains the most impactful signature Arc due to how perfectly it integrates with Chiz’s Fons-based kit, making it a high-priority target for players looking to main the Cosmos element.

# Best Closed Back Headphones for Gacha Grinders Under $200

Finding the right balance between noise isolation and long-term comfort is essential for those long sessions spent grinding for upgrade materials or participating in limited-time events like [The Ichi-daime](https://www.reddit.com/r/NevernessToEverness/) banner. The Audio-Technica ATH-M50x and Sony ULT WEAR are the top closed-back headphones under $200 for gacha grinders in 2026, offering the best balance of noise isolation and audio detail for Neverness to Everness.

## Stats

![Stats](/img/best-closed-back-headphones-gacha-grinders-under-200/inline-1.webp)


Technical specifications are the primary "base stats" of any audio equipment. For a gacha game like Neverness to Everness (NTE), which features a dense urban environment built in Unreal Engine 5, the size of the driver and the impedance rating determine how much detail you actually hear during combat.

Driver sizes typically range from 40mm to 45mm in this price bracket. A 45mm driver, such as the one found in the ATH-M50x, provides a wider surface area to move air, which translates to better clarity in the complex soundscape of Hethereau. This clarity is necessary when you are trying to listen for the specific audio "pings" that indicate a nearby collectible or a hidden side quest in the city's alleyways.

Impedance is another critical stat. For mobile and handheld gacha gaming, you want a low-impedance rating, usually between 32 and 48 ohms. High-impedance headphones require a dedicated amplifier to sound their best, but a low-impedance pair can be driven easily by a smartphone, tablet, or handheld console without losing volume or dynamic range.

### Audio-Technica ATH-M50x: The Gold Standard
The ATH-M50x remains a top choice due to its 45mm large-aperture drivers and 38-ohm impedance. It offers a professional-grade frequency response that covers 15 to 28,000 Hz, ensuring you hear the lowest rumbles of [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide)'s hammer and the highest frequencies of [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)'s lightning attacks. At 285 grams, it is light enough for extended sessions, though the clamping force can be tight initially.

### Sony ULT WEAR: The Bass-Heavy Contender
The <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Sony+ULT+WEAR&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Sony ULT WEAR</a> (WH-ULT900N) is designed for players who want to feel every explosion. It features 40mm drivers and a dedicated "ULT" button that boosts low-end frequencies. While it is a wireless-first headset, it supports a wired connection for low-latency gaming. Its frequency response in wired mode extends from 5 Hz to 20,000 Hz, making it excellent for the heavy urban beats of the NTE soundtrack.

### Beyerdynamic DT 770 Pro: The Comfort King
The DT 770 Pro is legendary for its velour ear pads, which offer superior breathability compared to the synthetic leather found on most closed-back models. The 32-ohm version is specifically optimized for mobile devices and laptops. With a weight of 270 grams and a frequency range of 5 to 35,000 Hz, it provides a spacious sound that helps in identifying the direction of enemy attacks in 3D combat.

### Sennheiser HD 569: The Soundstage Specialist
The Sennheiser HD 569 uses a proprietary transducer system to create a soundstage that feels wider than most closed-back headphones. It has an impedance of 23 ohms, making it the easiest to drive on this list. It is particularly effective for players who focus on the "Cosmos" abilities of characters like [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte), where mid-range clarity is essential for hearing ethereal sound effects.

## Effect

![Effect](/img/best-closed-back-headphones-gacha-grinders-under-200/inline-2.webp)


The "Effect" of a pair of headphones in Neverness to Everness is measured by how well it immerses you in the supernatural urban setting while filtering out real-world noise. Passive noise isolation is a hallmark of the closed-back design. By using thick ear pads and a solid outer shell, these headphones can block out [up to 20-30 decibels](https://www.rtings.com/headphones/reviews/audio-technica/ath-m50x) of ambient noise. This is vital when you are focusing on a difficult boss fight or listening for the specific audio cues of a Resonance Skill.

Sound signatures also play a role in how you perceive the game. A "V-shaped" signature, which boosts bass and treble while slightly receding the mids, makes ultimate abilities feel more impactful. The <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Sony+ULT+WEAR&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Sony ULT WEAR</a> excels here, giving a visceral "thump" to every heavy attack. Conversely, a neutral profile like the AKG K371 provides a more accurate representation of the game's audio engineering, allowing you to hear the subtle layers of the background music and environmental sounds.

Spatial imaging is another crucial effect. In the 3D environments of NTE, being able to tell if an enemy is behind you or on a rooftop above you can change the outcome of a fight. Headphones with good imaging allow you to track the movement of Esper abilities across the soundstage.

### Immersion Factor: Hearing the Streets of Hethereau
The sound design in Neverness to Everness includes a wide array of urban noises, from the screech of tires during car customization tests to the chatter of NPCs. High-quality closed-back headphones allow these sounds to exist in their own space without bleeding into each other. This creates a sense of "presence" that makes the city feel alive.

### The 'Gacha Pull' Euphoria: Audio Feedback and Dopamine
Gacha games are famous for their sensory feedback during the summoning process. The [clink of the animation](https://www.reddit.com/r/NevernessToEverness/) and the specific musical swell when a gold-rarity item appears are designed to be satisfying. A pair of headphones with good treble extension, like the ATH-M50x, highlights these shimmering sounds, making the "pull" experience more rewarding.

### Long-Term Ergonomics: Avoiding 'Grinder's Fatigue'
Comfort is effectively the "Stamina" stat for your ears. If a pair of headphones has too much clamping force or lacks breathability, you will likely have to stop playing after an hour. The Beyerdynamic DT 770 Pro is widely regarded as one of the most comfortable options because its circular ear cups distribute pressure evenly around the ear rather than on it.

## Comparison

![Comparison](/img/best-closed-back-headphones-gacha-grinders-under-200/inline-5.webp)


When choosing between the top contenders, the "Best Overall" title usually comes down to the ATH-M50x versus the <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Sony+ULT+WEAR&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Sony ULT WEAR</a>. The M50x is the choice for purists who want a wired, reliable connection with a balanced sound. The Sony ULT WEAR is for those who value modern features like Active Noise Cancellation (ANC) and the convenience of wireless play, though the added latency of Bluetooth can be an issue in fast-paced combat unless you use the included cable.

### Wired vs. Wireless: The Latency Factor
In a game like Neverness to Everness, where dodging is tied to precise timing, audio latency can be a dealbreaker. Standard Bluetooth often has a delay of 150-200ms, which can cause you to miss the audio cue for an enemy's ultimate attack. Wired headphones like the DT 770 Pro have zero latency, ensuring the sound is perfectly synced with the action on screen.

### Portability
For gacha grinders who play on the go, portability is a major stat. The ATH-M50x folds down into a compact shape and comes with a carrying pouch. The <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Sony+ULT+WEAR&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Sony ULT WEAR</a> also features a folding design and a hard-shell case, making it the better choice for commuting. The DT 770 Pro, however, does not fold and has a bulky frame, making it strictly a "home base" headphone.

### The Verdict: Best Overall for NTE Grinders
The **Audio-Technica ATH-M50x** is the best overall choice for most Neverness to Everness players. Its combination of high-detail 45mm drivers, low-impedance mobile compatibility, and durable folding design makes it the most versatile tool in your audio arsenal. It provides the clarity needed to track Nanally's lightning and the durability to survive years of daily grinding.

### Best Budget Pick (Under $120)
If you are saving your real-world currency for in-game pulls, the **Sennheiser HD 569** is the best budget pick. It often drops to around $100 and offers a level of comfort and soundstage that punches far above its price point. It is especially good for players who enjoy the narrative and environmental aspects of Hethereau.

### Best for Competitive Gacha (Low Latency)
For those who participate in high-level endgame content where every millisecond counts, the **Beyerdynamic DT 770 Pro (32 Ohm)** is the winner. Its wired-only design eliminates latency, and its superior comfort ensures you stay focused during the most intense boss encounters.

### Frequency Response and Soundstage

The frequency response range is critical for NTE because the game utilizes a wide spectrum of audio cues. Low-end frequencies (20Hz - 250Hz) carry the weight of the urban vehicles and heavy physical attacks, while high-end frequencies (5kHz - 20kHz) are where the supernatural "Anomaly" pings reside. A headphone with a flat, neutral response allows you to hear the game exactly as the sound designers intended, without artificial bass boost masking important tactical information.

### Passive vs. Active Noise Cancellation in 2026

While Active Noise Cancellation (ANC) is a staple of the <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Sony+WH-1000XM6&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Sony WH-1000XM6</a>, many hardcore NTE grinders prefer the passive isolation of a well-sealed closed-back like the Audeze Maxwell 2. Passive isolation doesn't require digital processing, which can sometimes introduce a "pressure" feeling during long sessions. However, for those playing in high-noise environments like subways, the AI-driven ANC of 2026 models can now filter out specific human speech patterns while keeping game audio crystal clear.

## Comparison

![Comparison](/img/best-closed-back-headphones-gacha-grinders-2026/inline-5.webp)


Choosing between the top-tier options often comes down to personal preference regarding build and sound signature.

### SteelSeries Arctis Nova Elite vs. Audeze Maxwell 2

The showdown between these two is the "Solid" vs. "Synthesis" of the audio world. The SteelSeries Arctis Nova Elite offers the most comprehensive feature set, including a base station with a second battery charger and multi-system switching. It is the better choice for players who jump between PC, PS5, and mobile.

The Audeze Maxwell 2, however, wins on pure audio quality. Its planar magnetic drivers provide a level of detail and bass extension that dynamic drivers simply cannot match. If your priority is hearing the "texture" of the music and the weight of the combat, the Maxwell 2 is the superior choice, despite being significantly heavier.

### Closed-Back vs. Open-Back for Gacha

While open-back headphones are praised for their wide soundstage, closed-back designs are the "Meta" for NTE. Because NTE is a cross-platform title played on Steam Deck, PlayStation Portal, and mobile devices, you are often playing in environments with ambient noise. Closed-back headphones prevent your game audio from leaking out to those around you and prevent outside noise from ruining your immersion. The "seal" also provides a more intimate and impactful bass response, which is essential for the game's modern, synth-heavy soundtrack.

### Wired vs. Wireless Latency

For frame-perfect dodges and "Critical Ripostes" in NTE, latency is the enemy. Traditional Bluetooth often has a delay of 100ms or more, which is unacceptable for high-level play. The 2026 flagships use proprietary 2.4GHz wireless tech to bring this down to negligible levels. However, for those who demand zero latency, the Beyerdynamic MMX 330 Pro offers a wired connection that bypasses all digital processing, providing the most direct link between the game's engine and your ears.

### In-Game Arcs vs. Real-World Gear

In Neverness to Everness, "Arcs" are items that provide stat boosts to your characters. High-end headphones function as a real-world Arc for the player. While the game features an A-Rank Arc called "Real Music" that boosts character stats, equipping a pair of Audeze Maxwell 2 headphones provides a tangible boost to your own performance. Being able to hear an enemy's telegraph sound 0.5 seconds earlier can be the difference between a successful Anomaly containment and a total party wipe.

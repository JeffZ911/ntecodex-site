# Best 4-Star Weapons for Healers in Neverness to Everness (NTE)

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/best-4-star-weapons-nte-healer/inline-1.webp)


Choosing the right weapon for a healer in Neverness to Everness (NTE) involves balancing raw healing output with team-wide utility and energy management. Players often ask:

*   **What are the best A-rank (4-star) Arcs for sustain and healing characters?**
*   **Which weapons provide the highest Healing Bonus and HP scaling to keep the team alive?**
*   **How do budget-friendly weapons compare to S-rank signature Arcs for healers like Fadia and Edgar?**

Finding the answers to these questions is essential for clearing high-level Anomalies and the challenging "Beyond the Rails" endgame mode, where a single mistimed heal can lead to a team wipe.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/best-4-star-weapons-nte-healer/inline-2.webp)


The absolute best 4-star (A-rank) weapon for pure healers in NTE is **Call of the Twisted City**. This Liquid-type Arc is specifically designed for sustain, providing a significant boost to the Healing Bonus stat after the wearer uses their Redirect Skill. For healers like Edgar, who rely on channeled healing, this provides nearly 100% uptime on increased restoration.

For healers who scale primarily with HP, such as the S-rank Psyche healer Fadia, **The Great Thief** (A-rank) is a strong alternative. While its substat focuses on Break Intensity, its compatibility with Synthesis-type characters makes it a vital slot-in for Fadia's kit. Additionally, for players looking for the best budget-friendly defensive options, the B-rank **Umbrella** provides a reliable HP boost and shield strengthening.

If your team struggles with Ultimate uptime, **Be Happy** is frequently used in utility-focused builds. Although officially classified as a B-rank Gas-type Arc, its HP substat and restorative passive make it a staple for early-to-mid-game sustain. For hybrid characters like Jiuyuan who need to balance damage with survivability, **Real Music** (B-rank) offers an ATK-based substat to keep their Redirect Skill damage relevant while maintaining team stability.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/best-4-star-weapons-nte-healer/inline-3.webp)


Understanding how to gear your healers requires a deep dive into the specific scaling of each character. In NTE, healing is not a universal stat; it is often tied to the character's unique Attribute (Element) and their specific Arc Type (Gas, Liquid, Plasma, Solid, or Synthesis).

### Understanding Healer Scaling and Stats

Unlike traditional RPGs where "Magic" or "Spirit" might govern healing, NTE healers scale their restorative powers based on their primary combat stats. 

*   **HP Scaling:** Characters like **Fadia** and **Edgar** primarily scale their healing based on their Max HP. This makes Arcs with HP% substats significantly more valuable than those with pure ATK.
*   **ATK Scaling:** Some hybrid supports or burst-healers may scale with ATK, though this is less common for pure sustain units.
*   **Healing Bonus:** This is a separate multiplier that applies to the final healing value. Call of the Twisted City is currently the only A-rank Arc that specifically targets this stat via its passive.
*   **Charge Efficiency:** This stat determines how quickly a character generates energy for their Ultimate. Healers like Edgar, whose Ultimate creates a healing domain, benefit immensely from high Charge Efficiency to ensure their "burst" heal is available during boss phases.

### Top A-Rank (4-Star) Arcs for Healers

The current meta for A-rank healer weapons is dominated by Arcs that provide either raw survivability or direct multipliers to restoration.

#### 1. Call of the Twisted City (A-Rank, Liquid)
This is the gold standard for 4-star healer weapons. With a base ATK of 395 and a massive **37.5% HP substat** at max level, it provides the raw bulk needed for HP-scaling healers. Its passive, "Cardboard Castle," increases the wearer's Healing Bonus by 12% (up to 20% at Mixing Level 5) for 10 seconds after casting a Redirect Skill. 

Since most healers in NTE use their Redirect Skill as their primary source of sustain, this buff is effectively permanent. It is the best-in-slot A-rank option for **Edgar** and a top-tier choice for any Liquid-compatible support.

#### 2. The Great Thief (A-Rank, Synthesis)
While often categorized as a utility weapon due to its **120 Break Intensity** substat, The Great Thief is the premier A-rank choice for **Fadia**. As a Synthesis-type character, Fadia can utilize this Arc to help her team shred the enemy's Break gauge while she focuses on damage redirection. 

Its passive, "Danzaburou," increases the Break Intensity of all characters of the same Esper Type by 70–126 when the team has three or more characters of that type. In a mono-Psyche or heavy Synthesis team, this Arc provides offensive utility that other sustain weapons lack.

### Best Budget B-Rank Alternatives

If you haven't pulled the specific A-rank Arcs mentioned above, several B-rank (3-star) options are surprisingly effective due to their ease of refinement.

*   **Be Happy (B-Rank, Gas):** This Arc is a favorite for F2P players because it offers a **30% HP substat**. Its passive, "Huggy Vines," restores 12% of the wearer's HP upon defeating an enemy. While this doesn't help with team-wide healing, it makes the healer exceptionally tanky, which is vital for characters like Fadia who redirect damage to themselves.
*   **Real Music (B-Rank, Liquid):** This weapon is better suited for hybrid characters like **Jiuyuan**. It provides a 20% ATK substat and increases Redirect Skill damage by up to 16%. If your Jiuyuan is built for burst damage but you still need her to provide some utility, this is a solid placeholder.
*   **Umbrella (B-Rank, Solid):** This is a purely defensive choice. It provides a flat HP boost and enhances the strength of shields. For characters who provide both shields and heals, Umbrella is a reliable budget pick.

### Character-Specific Weapon Recommendations

Each healer in NTE has a different "Redirect Skill" logic that dictates which weapon is best.

#### Fadia (S-Rank, Psyche, Healer)
Fadia's kit is unique because she redirects the damage her teammates take to her own HP bar. She then self-heals using her Ultimate.
*   **Best A-Rank:** *The Great Thief*. The Synthesis compatibility is key here, and the Break Intensity helps the team end fights faster, reducing the total damage Fadia has to soak.
*   **Budget Pick:** *Be Happy*. The 30% HP boost directly increases the amount of damage she can redirect before reaching the 1 HP threshold.

#### Edgar (A-Rank, Cosmos, Healer)
Edgar is a channeled healer. He holds his Redirect Skill to top up the ally with the lowest HP and drops an Ultimate domain for on-field healing.
*   **Best A-Rank:** *Call of the Twisted City*. This weapon was practically made for Edgar. The Healing Bonus triggers as soon as he starts his channel, ensuring his single-target sustain is unmatched.
*   **Budget Pick:** *Real Music*. Since Edgar's heal is constant, any boost to his Redirect Skill performance is beneficial.

#### Jiuyuan (S-Rank, Anima, Burst DPS/Utility)
While primarily a damage dealer, Jiuyuan is often used in sustain-heavy comps for her mob-gathering abilities.
*   **Best A-Rank:** *Call of the Twisted City*. If you are running Jiuyuan in a support-heavy role, the HP substat helps her stay on the field longer.
*   **Best Budget:** *Real Music*. This aligns with her Anima DPS role while providing the stat-stick benefits of a Liquid-type Arc.

### Refinement (Mixing) Priority for F2P Players

One of the biggest advantages of A-rank Arcs like **Call of the Twisted City** is the ease of reaching Mixing Level 5 (T5). In NTE, "Mixing" is the process of using duplicate Arcs to upgrade the weapon's passive effect.

A T5 **Call of the Twisted City** provides a 20% Healing Bonus, which often outperforms a T1 S-rank weapon that might offer higher base stats but less specific healing utility. F2P players should prioritize using their "Tri-Keys" on the Arc Research Program to secure duplicates of these key sustain weapons.

**Refinement Priority List:**
1.  **Call of the Twisted City:** The jump from 12% to 20% Healing Bonus is the single largest sustain increase available in the A-rank pool.
2.  **The Great Thief:** Upgrading this increases the team-wide Break Intensity bonus, which is crucial for high-level boss encounters.
3.  **Be Happy:** If using this as a budget HP stick, reaching T5 ensures the healer can self-sustain indefinitely against waves of smaller enemies.

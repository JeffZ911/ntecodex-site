# Neverness to Everness Elemental Reaction Guide: Every Esper Cycle Explained

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/nte-elemental-reaction-guide-esper-cycles/inline-1.webp)


Players navigating the urban supernatural landscape of Hethereau often ask: what are the elemental reactions in Neverness to Everness (NTE)? Unlike traditional elemental systems that allow any two elements to react, NTE utilizes a specific "Esper Cycle" wheel that dictates combat flow. Understanding which elements react with each other is essential for building a viable three-person team.

To master combat, you must know how the Esper Cycle system facilitates team rotations. This involves identifying the six primary elements—Cosmos, Anima, Incantation, Chaos, Psyche, and Lakshana—and learning the adjacency rules. You need to know how to trigger Duo reactions like Blossom or Scorch, and how to set up the more complex Trio reactions such as Charge and Discord. This guide covers the mechanics of the Esper Meter, the specific effects of every known reaction, and how to optimize your stats for faster cycles.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-elemental-reaction-guide-esper-cycles/inline-2.webp)


Elemental reactions in NTE are formally known as "Esper Cycles." These are triggered when you swap a character into the field while their Esper Meter is 100% full, provided the incoming character's element is adjacent to the outgoing character's element on the hexagonal Esper Cycle Wheel.

There are six elements in the game: Cosmos, Anima, Incantation, Chaos, Psyche, and Lakshana. Reactions do not occur between "opposite" or non-adjacent elements on the wheel. For example, Cosmos can react with Anima or Lakshana, but it cannot directly trigger a Duo reaction with Chaos. 

Combining two adjacent elements creates a Duo Reaction, such as Blossom (Cosmos + Anima) which spawns a supportive turret, or Nova (Chaos + Psyche) which deals delayed Cognito burst damage. Combining three specific elements in a sequence triggers a Trio Reaction. The two primary Trio Reactions are "Charge" (Lakshana, Cosmos, and Anima) for ultimate energy generation, and "Discord" (Incantation, Chaos, and Psyche) for destroying enemy Break Meters.

If your team consists of elements that are not adjacent on the wheel, you will lose out on the massive damage multipliers and utility buffs provided by Esper Cycles.

### Understanding the Esper Cycle Wheel & Adjacency

The Esper Cycle Wheel is a hexagon where each point represents an element. The connections are as follows:

*   **Lakshana** connects to **Cosmos** and **Psyche**.
*   **Cosmos** connects to **Lakshana** and **Anima**.
*   **Anima** connects to **Cosmos** and **Incantation**.
*   **Incantation** connects to **Anima** and **Chaos**.
*   **Chaos** connects to **Incantation** and **Psyche**.
*   **Psyche** connects to **Chaos** and **Lakshana**.

A Duo Reaction only occurs when you swap from an "Active" character to a "Waiting" character whose elements share a line on this wheel. If you swap from a Chaos character (like Nanally) to an Anima character (like Baicang), no reaction will occur because they are not adjacent. You would instead need to swap from Chaos to either Incantation or Psyche to trigger a cycle.

### How to Fill and Trigger the Esper Meter

The Esper Meter is the circular gauge located next to each character's portrait on the right side of the screen. You cannot trigger a reaction simply by swapping; the incoming character must have a full meter.

| Action | Esper Meter Generation Rate |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Normal Attacks** | Slow (requires full combo strings) |
| **Elemental Skills** | Moderate (varies by cooldown) |
| **Perfect Parry** | Instant 100% Charge |
| **Red Flash Dodge** | Significant Boost (approx. 50%) |

Parrying is the most efficient way to maintain a high "Cycle Rate." When an enemy attacks with a yellow flash, timing your block perfectly fills the Esper Meter of your standby characters immediately. Once the portrait glows, tapping that character triggers their "Intro Skill" and the corresponding Esper Cycle reaction.

### Trio Reactions: Unleashing Charge and Discord

Trio Reactions are the "Ultimate" form of the Esper Cycle. These are triggered by completing a full rotation of three specific adjacent elements in a short window.

*   **Charge (Lakshana → Cosmos → Anima):** This cycle focuses on energy economy. Completing this rotation grants the entire team a massive influx of Ultimate Energy. It is the preferred cycle for teams that rely on high-cost "Burst" abilities.
*   **Discord (Incantation → Chaos → Psyche):** This is the primary offensive cycle. It applies a "Fracture" debuff that significantly increases the damage dealt to an enemy's Break Meter. If you are facing a boss with a heavy shield or "Stance" bar, a Discord rotation is the fastest way to trigger a "Break" state.

### Advanced Stats: Cycle Rate, Cycle Value, and Arc Compatibility

As you progress into the mid-game and begin farming Arcs (the game's equipment system), you will encounter two critical stats:

1.  **Cycle Rate:** This stat functions like "Energy Recharge" in other titles. A higher Cycle Rate allows your characters to fill their Esper Meters faster through normal attacks and skills. High Cycle Rate is essential for "Support" characters who need to swap in and out frequently.
2.  **Cycle Value:** This determines the potency of the reaction itself. For example, a higher Cycle Value on a Chaos character will increase the DoT damage of the Scorch reaction or the explosion damage of Nova.

Arc Compatibility is also vital. Certain Arcs provide bonuses only when a specific reaction is triggered. If you are running Nanally (Chaos) on "The Ichi-daime" banner, you should look for Arcs that boost "Chaos-related Cycle Value" to maximize her Scorch and Nova output.

### Team Building Strategies: Creating Elemental 'Lanes'

To optimize your team, you should build "Lanes" on the Esper Cycle wheel. A popular lane is the **Chaos-Psyche-Incantation** lane.

*   **Slot 1 (Main DPS):** Nanally (Chaos) - Uses her high-speed attacks to build meter.
*   **Slot 2 (Sub-DPS):** Chizuru (Psyche) - Swaps in to [trigger Nova](https://ntecodex.com/faq) and provide crowd control.
*   **Slot 3 (Support/Utility):** Sakiri (Incantation) - Swaps in to trigger Scorch or finish the Discord Trio Reaction.

By staying within this three-element segment of the wheel, every single swap you perform will result in an Esper Cycle reaction, provided you are parrying or using skills effectively. If you were to swap one of these for Zero (Cosmos), you would break the lane, as Cosmos does not connect to Chaos or Psyche, leaving your rotations clunky and inefficient.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/neverness-to-everness-disk-sets-guide/inline-2.webp)


In Neverness to Everness, "Disk Sets" refer to the combination of a central Cartridge and various Modules installed within a character's 20-slot Console grid. Unlike traditional gear systems where you simply equip items in slots, NTE utilizes a spatial puzzle mechanic. A complete Disk Set build requires one Cartridge, which acts as the "brain" of the set and determines the available bonuses, and multiple Modules (geometric blocks) that must fit into the remaining grid space.

Set bonuses are divided into two tiers: the Epic (2-piece) bonus and the Legendary (4-piece) bonus. To activate these, you must equip the Cartridge and then slot the specific Module shapes required by that Cartridge. For Version 1.0, the "Lost Radiance" set is the gold standard for universal DPS, while "Speedy Hedgehog" is the primary choice for support units. Players farm these items in "Rabbit Hole" domains and utilize the "Rewind" system—a targeted gacha mechanic using Silver and Gold Cora—to obtain the specific Module shapes needed to complete their 20-slot grid.

### Understanding the Console and Tetris System

The Console is the heart of character progression in Neverness to Everness. Every character, from the anomaly-hunting Zero to the high-speed [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte), possesses a 20-slot grid. This grid is not just a menu; it is a physical space where gear must be arranged.

The system is built on two distinct components:

1.  **The Cartridge:** This is the primary component. It occupies a fixed portion of the grid and provides a random main stat, such as Elemental Damage Bonus or ATK%. Most importantly, the Cartridge defines the set bonus and lists the "Required Shapes" needed to unlock the 4-piece effect.
2.  **Modules:** These are the Tetris-like blocks that fill the rest of the grid. Modules provide fixed main stats based on their "Type" (size) and several random substats.

The most challenging aspect of the Console is the "No Rotation" rule. If you find a powerful Type IV Module that is a vertical 4x1 block, but your remaining grid space is only 2x2, you cannot rotate the Module to fit. This creates a high demand for specific shapes, making the spatial management of the 20 slots just as important as the stats themselves.

### Farming Strategy: Rabbit Holes and Rewind Optimization

High-tier Cartridges and Modules are primarily obtained through **Rabbit Holes**, which are supernatural pocket dimensions scattered throughout Hethereau. Each Rabbit Hole focuses on specific sets. For example, the "Neon Skyline" Rabbit Hole is the primary source for the Lost Radiance set.

Because the spatial puzzle system introduces an extra layer of RNG (you need the right set, the right stats, AND the right shape), the game provides the **Rewind System**.

*   **Silver Cora:** Obtained by recycling 3-star and 4-star Modules. Used to "Rewind" or reroll the shape of a Module while keeping its stats.
*   **Gold Cora:** Obtained by recycling 5-star Modules or completing weekly City Tycoon milestones. Used to "Rewind" a Module into a specific required shape needed for a Cartridge’s Legendary bonus.

Efficiency in the late game involves farming Rabbit Holes for Modules with perfect substats (CRIT Rate, CRIT DMG, Cycle Intensity) and then using Gold Cora to force those Modules into the shapes required to complete the 20-slot puzzle.

### Main Stat and Substat Priority Guide

When evaluating a Disk Set, the Cartridge's main stat is your first priority. For DPS characters, you generally want an **Elemental DMG Bonus** that matches their attribute (e.g., Physical for Nanally, Fire for Zero). For supports, **Energy Regeneration** or **Cycle Intensity** are the preferred Cartridge stats.

Substats on Modules are revealed immediately upon acquisition, removing the need to "level up" a piece of gear just to see if it is good. The priority for substats generally follows this hierarchy for damage dealers:

1.  **CRIT Rate / CRIT DMG:** Essential for scaling late-game damage.
2.  **Cycle Intensity:** Increases the damage and effects of elemental reactions within the Esper Cycle system.
3.  **ATK% / Flat ATK:** Provides the base for all damage calculations.
4.  **Energy Regeneration:** Ensures that Ultimate abilities are available on cooldown.

For defensive units, prioritize **DEF%** and **HP%**, especially if their abilities scale off these specific metrics.

Players often find themselves asking how to effectively scale their damage and survivability as they progress through the story. Specifically, the gear system in NTE can be divided into three primary components that contribute to your "Disk stats" and overall combat efficiency.

### Understanding the NTE Gear Terminology

Before diving into the upgrade process, it is essential to clarify the terminology used in the game's equipment menus:

*   **Arcs (Disks):** These function as your primary weapons. They provide the most significant boost to Base ATK and offer a secondary stat (such as Crit Rate or ATK%). Arcs also possess unique passive abilities that can be enhanced through refinement.
*   **Cartridges:** These are the primary gear pieces, similar to "Relics" or "Artifacts" in other RPGs. They provide a Main Stat and up to four sub-stats. Cartridges are organized into sets that provide powerful bonuses when multiple pieces from the same set are equipped.
*   **Modules (Sub-Gear):** These are Tetris-like components that are placed into a character's Console. They provide flat stat increases and are used to activate "Advanced Effects" within the Cartridge system.
*   **The Console:** This is the interface where Modules are arranged. It is a grid-based puzzle system where the placement and shape of Modules directly impact the activation of gear bonuses.

Improving these components requires specific materials, such as Bunny Coins for experience and various Ascension materials found throughout the world.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-how-to-increase-disk-stats-guide/inline-3.webp)


The gear progression in Neverness to Everness is a multi-layered process. To maximize your stats, you must address each system individually while ensuring they synergize with your character's specific role.

### Arc (Weapon Disk) Leveling & Ascension

Arcs are the foundation of your offensive stats. Every Arc provides a Base ATK value that scales significantly with every level. To increase an Arc's level, you navigate to the "Arc" menu and select "Enhance," where you consume Bunny Coins or lower-rarity Arcs to provide experience.

However, leveling is not infinite. Arcs hit a ceiling every 10 to 20 levels. To move past these ceilings, you must perform an Ascension. The materials required for Ascension are typically found in Anomaly Hunts located across the city. It is important to note that you cannot ascend an Arc beyond the limits set by your current Appraisal Level. If your Appraisal Level is too low, the "Ascend" button will remain locked even if you have the necessary materials.

Refinement is the final way to boost an Arc's power. By consuming a duplicate of the same Arc, you increase the rank of its passive effect. This does not change the raw stats like ATK, but it can drastically improve damage multipliers or utility effects.

### Cartridge Enhancement and Sub-stat Mechanics

Cartridges provide the bulk of your secondary stats, such as Elemental DMG bonuses, Health, and Cycle Intensity. Unlike Arcs, Cartridges only have a maximum level of 20. 

When you enhance a Cartridge, the Main Stat increases linearly. The sub-stats, however, follow a threshold system. If a Cartridge is acquired with fewer than four sub-stats, a new random sub-stat will be revealed at levels +5, +10, +15, or +20. If a Cartridge already has four sub-stats, one of those existing stats will receive a random numerical upgrade at these levels.

| Level Threshold | Effect on Sub-stats |
| :--- | :--- |
| +5 | Unlock 1st new sub-stat or upgrade random existing stat |
| +10 | Unlock 2nd new sub-stat or upgrade random existing stat |
| +15 | Unlock 3rd new sub-stat or upgrade random existing stat |
| +20 | Unlock 4th new sub-stat or upgrade random existing stat |

Because sub-stat rolls are randomized, high-level play involves "farming" for Cartridges that have desirable initial stats (like Crit Rate or Crit DMG) before investing materials into them.

### Mastering the Console: The Module Puzzle System

The Console is perhaps the most unique aspect of NTE's gear system. It functions as a grid where you must fit geometric shapes (Modules). Each Module carries its own flat stats, but the real power comes from how they interact with your Cartridges.

Cartridges often have "Advanced Effects" that remain dormant unless specific conditions are met in the Console. For example, a Cartridge might require you to place three "Square" Modules or two "L-shaped" Modules in the grid to activate a 15% damage boost. This requires players to treat their gear like a puzzle, balancing the raw stats of the Modules with the shapes needed to trigger their Cartridges' full potential.

### Character-Specific Module Type Bonuses

Beyond shapes, Modules are categorized into Types (Type II, Type III, and Type IV). Every character in Neverness to Everness has a "Preferred Type" listed in their profile. 

Using Nanally as an example, she is a Type II specialist. For every Type II Module you successfully fit into her Console, she receives a passive bonus, such as a 6% increase to her Crit Rate. If you fill her Console with matching Type II Modules, you can gain a massive amount of "free" stats that do not appear on the Modules themselves. Always prioritize matching the Module Type to the character before worrying about the individual flat stats of the Module piece.

### The Rewind System: Farming for S-Rank Gear

To obtain the highest rarity (S-Rank) Cartridges and Modules, players must use the "Rewind" system. This is essentially a gear-specific gacha or crafting system that uses Carrota Coins.

*   **Bronze Carrota Coins:** Used for basic Rewinds, providing B-Rank and occasionally A-Rank gear.
*   **Silver Carrota Coins:** Used for intermediate Rewinds, with a higher chance for A-Rank gear.
*   **Gold Carrota Coins:** The most valuable currency, used to target S-Rank Cartridges and Modules.

Carrota Coins are earned by completing daily activities, clearing the "Rabbit Hole" challenges, or participating in limited-time events.

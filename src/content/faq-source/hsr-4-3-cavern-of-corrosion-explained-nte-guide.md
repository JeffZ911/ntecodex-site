# HSR 4.3 Cavern of Corrosion Explained: Farming Gear in Neverness to Everness

The Neverness to Everness (NTE) equivalent to the Honkai: Star Rail (HSR) Cavern of Corrosion is the Rabbit Hole domain, which is the primary source for Cartridges. While HSR players farm Relics, NTE players utilize the Console System, which combines these Cartridges with Tetris-like Modules obtained through the Rewind System using Carrota Coins.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/hsr-4-3-cavern-of-corrosion-explained-nte-guide/inline-2.webp)


In Neverness to Everness, the gear system is known as the Console System, which uses Cartridges and Modules instead of traditional Relics. The direct equivalent to the Cavern of Corrosion is the Rabbit Hole domain. This domain is physically located in the Cape Square district of Hethereau, often found near the New Herland border.

Farming a single run of the Rabbit Hole costs 40 Character Pixels, which is the NTE version of Trailblaze Power. While the Cartridges themselves drop directly from these domain clears, the Modules—the sub-components that provide additional stats—are primarily obtained via the Rewind System. This system uses Carrota Coins earned as a secondary reward from your Rabbit Hole clears.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/hsr-4-3-cavern-of-corrosion-explained-nte-guide/inline-3.webp)


The transition from HSR to NTE involves moving away from linear gear slots into a more spatial, puzzle-oriented system. Understanding the relationship between the domain, the stamina, and the resulting gear is vital for optimizing characters like Nanally or Haniel.

### The Console System: NTE's Unique Gear Mechanic

The Console System is the heart of character progression in Neverness to Everness. Unlike the six slots in HSR (four Relics and two Ornaments), NTE uses a grid-based interface. Each Esper has a unique Console layout, which functions like a Tetris board. 

Players must first equip a Cartridge. This Cartridge determines the base stats and the available grid space for that character. Once the Cartridge is active, players insert Modules into the grid. These Modules come in various geometric shapes (L-blocks, squares, long bars) and provide the bulk of the character's sub-stats, such as Cycle Rate and Break DMG. The challenge lies in fitting the highest-quality Modules into the limited space provided by the Cartridge.

### Farming Cartridges: Accessing the Rabbit Hole

To obtain these Cartridges, you must head to Cape Square. The Rabbit Hole is an instanced domain that features various combat challenges against urban anomalies. 

The rewards from the Rabbit Hole scale based on your Appraisal Level. As you complete Appraisal quests (also known as Anomalous Realm challenges), you unlock higher difficulty tiers.

**Rabbit Hole Reward Tiers by Appraisal Level**

| Appraisal Level | Difficulty Tier | Primary Rewards (Cartridge Rarity) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1-10 | Tier 1 | Blue (Rare) |
| 11-20 | Tier 2 | Blue (Rare), Purple (Epic) |
| 21-30 | Tier 3 | Purple (Epic) |
| 31-40 | Tier 4 | Purple (Epic), Gold (Legendary) |
| 41+ | Tier 5 | Guaranteed Gold (Legendary) |

Reaching the higher tiers is the equivalent of farming Difficulty VI in HSR's Caverns. It is generally recommended to save your Character Pixel refills until you have reached the Appraisal Level required for Gold drops.

### Farming Modules: Carrota Coins and the Rewind System

While the Rabbit Hole drops Cartridges, it does not directly drop the Modules needed to fill them. Instead, each run rewards you with Carrota Coins. These coins are the currency for the Rewind System, which is essentially a targeted gacha for gear parts.

There are three types of Carrota Coins:
1.  **Bronze Carrota Coins**: Used for basic Module pulls, typically resulting in Blue or Purple rarity pieces.
2.  **Silver Carrota Coins**: Used for intermediate pulls with a higher chance for Gold Modules.
3.  **Gold Carrota Coins**: Used for high-end "Rewinding," allowing players to target specific shapes (like a 2x2 square) or specific stats (like Cycle Rate).

This system offers more control than HSR's Relic farming. If you have a Cartridge with a specific empty spot, you can use your Gold Carrota Coins to try and "Rewind" a Module that fits that exact geometry.

### Optimizing Your Build: Set Bonuses and Grid Placement

Activating set bonuses in NTE requires matching the Cartridge type with the character's Esper element or role. For example, an Incantation focused character like Nanally benefits from Cartridges that boost elemental damage or Cycle Rate.

The stats available on these items are divided into primary and secondary categories:

**Primary Stats (Cartridges)**
*   Attack (Flat or %)
*   HP (Flat or %)
*   Defense (Flat or %)
*   Cycle Intensity
*   Break Intensity

**Sub-Stats (Modules)**
*   Cycle Rate
*   Break DMG
*   Critical Rate / Critical DMG
*   Elemental DMG (Lakshana, Cosmos, Anima, Incantation, Chaos, Psyche)

Managing your Character Pixels is crucial. They regenerate at a rate of 1 point every 6 minutes, with a maximum cap of 240. This means you get 240 pixels per 24 hours, enough for six Rabbit Hole runs. Early on, it is often better to spend these on Hunter's Manuals for character leveling rather than gear domains.

Beyond the basic gear farm, the effectiveness of your Console System setup is heavily influenced by how it interacts with the broader game mechanics of Neverness to Everness.

### Esper Elements and Reaction Synergy

The stats you farm in the Rabbit Hole must align with the six Esper elements: Lakshana, Cosmos, Anima, Incantation, Chaos, and Psyche. 

High Cycle Rate on your Modules allows for faster elemental applications, leading to powerful reactions. For instance, combining Incantation and Anima can trigger the Blossom effect, while other combinations lead to Hexed states on enemies. Your gear choices directly dictate how often these high-damage windows occur.

### Progression Gates: Hunter and Appraisal Levels

Your ability to access the highest tiers of gear is gated by two separate leveling systems. Your Hunter Level represents your overall account progress and is increased by completing story missions and daily activities. 

However, the Appraisal Level is what specifically unlocks the higher difficulty levels of the Rabbit Hole. To raise this, you must complete periodic Appraisal quests. These are combat trials that test if your current team composition and gear are sufficient for the next tier of world difficulty.

### Urban Life: The Tycoon Management System

Farming gear is only one part of the NTE experience. As you stabilize your combat power, you will earn Fons, the primary urban currency. This currency is used in the Tycoon System, allowing you to buy property, customize businesses, and purchase vehicles. 

While Character Pixels are for combat growth, your Fons are for urban growth. Efficiently clearing domains like the Rabbit Hole ensures you can tackle harder missions that reward larger amounts of Fons, creating a loop between your combat prowess and your status in Hethereau.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best way to spend Character Pixels early in NTE?
Early in the game, it is most efficient to spend Character Pixels on character and ARC weapon ascension materials. Since high-rarity Cartridges only drop at higher Appraisal Levels, farming the Rabbit Hole before level 30 often yields gear that will be replaced quickly.

### How do I get more Carrota Coins for the Rewind System?
Carrota Coins are primarily earned by completing Rabbit Hole domain runs. You can also obtain them through certain urban side-quests in Hethereau or as rewards for increasing your Hunter Level.

### Can I change the shape of a Module I already own?
You cannot directly change the shape of an existing Module. However, using the Rewind System with Gold Carrota Coins allows you to pull for new Modules while selecting a preferred shape category to better fit your Console grid.

### Where is the Cape Square district located?
Cape Square is located in the southern portion of the Hethereau map, adjacent to the New Herland business district. It serves as a major hub for high-end shopping and contains the entrance to the Rabbit Hole gear domain.

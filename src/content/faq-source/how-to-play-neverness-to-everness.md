# How to Play Neverness to Everness: A Comprehensive Starter Guide

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/how-to-play-neverness-to-everness/inline-1.webp)


What is the core gameplay loop of Neverness to Everness (NTE)? How do beginners get started in the city of Hethereau? What are the primary mechanics for combat, driving, and city management?

Understanding the rhythm of Neverness to Everness is essential for any new "Appraiser." The game functions as a multi-layered simulation where your time is divided between three distinct activities. First, you engage in the core loop of anomaly investigation, which involves tracking supernatural disturbances across the city and neutralizing them through fast-paced combat. Second, you manage the "City Tycoon" system, an economic simulation where you purchase properties, run businesses, and generate the wealth needed to upgrade your gear. Finally, the driving and customization system serves as both your primary traversal method and a competitive endgame activity.

Success in Hethereau depends on balancing these pillars. You cannot ignore the economy, as high-end Arcs (weapons) and vehicle parts require significant amounts of Fons, the local currency. Similarly, neglecting your Esper team will make higher-level Appraisal missions nearly impossible. This guide breaks down the essential systems to ensure your career as an Appraiser starts on solid ground.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-play-neverness-to-everness/inline-2.webp)


Neverness to Everness is a supernatural urban open-world RPG where you play as an "Appraiser" investigating anomalies. The gameplay is split into three main pillars: high-octane combat using "Esper Cycles," deep vehicle customization and driving, and a "City Tycoon" life-sim system.

Players manage a team of four characters, known as Espers. These characters are defined by their unique elemental attributes, which include Cosmos, Anima, Incantation, Chaos, Psyche, and Lakshana. Progression in the game is tracked through two primary metrics: your Hunter Level (which governs account-wide unlocks and story access) and your Appraisal Level (which dictates world difficulty and the quality of loot dropped by anomalies).

The economic heart of the game is the City Tycoon system. By managing businesses like cafes and retail shops, you earn Fons. This currency is used for everything from buying car parts at the local tuning shop to upgrading your characters. Unlike many other RPGs, NTE places a heavy emphasis on passive income, rewarding players who invest in the city's infrastructure early on.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-play-neverness-to-everness/inline-3.webp)


### Mastering the Esper Cycle Combat

Combat in Neverness to Everness revolves around the "Esper Cycle" mechanic. Unlike traditional action games where you might focus on a single character, NTE requires constant swapping to maintain momentum. Every attack, dodge, and perfect parry contributes to a central Esper Cycle meter. Once this meter reaches specific thresholds, switching to a different Esper triggers a "Cycle Entry" attack, dealing massive damage and applying elemental stacks.

The interaction between different attributes is the key to maximizing your DPS. When two specific elements interact, they trigger powerful reactions.

**Elemental Reaction Table**

| Reaction Name | Attribute Combination | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blossom** | Cosmos + Anima | Deals AoE damage and reduces enemy defense. |
| **Discord** | — | — |
| **Resonance** | — | — |

To excel in combat, you must master the timing of perfect parries. A successful parry doesn't just negate damage; it instantly grants a large chunk of Esper Cycle energy. This allows you to cycle through your team's ultimate abilities much faster than through standard attacks alone.

### The City Tycoon Economy and Fons

The City Tycoon system is what sets NTE apart from other urban RPGs. You aren't just a visitor in Hethereau; you are a business mogul. Early in the game, you will be granted access to your first property—typically a small storefront or cafe. 

Managing these businesses involves hiring staff, setting inventory, and completing "Business Anomalies" to keep the customers happy. Your Tycoon Level is separate from your Hunter Level and offers some of the most tangible rewards in the game. For example, reaching Tycoon Level 18 grants you the S-Rank character Chiz for free.

Fons is the lifeblood of this system. While you can earn Fons through combat and mission rewards, the most efficient way to build wealth is through business optimization. Invest your early-game Fons back into your shops to increase their "Passive Yield" rate. This ensures that even when you are busy hunting anomalies or racing through the city, your bank account continues to grow.

### Vehicle Customization and Driving Mechanics

Driving is not just a way to get from point A to point B; it is a core gameplay system with its own progression. The physics engine in NTE favors a mix of arcade drifting and technical grip driving. Every car in the game can be stripped down and rebuilt at the tuning shops scattered across Hethereau.

For beginners, the most important vehicle to acquire is the Novis ST-X 950 motorcycle. While cars are better for high-speed highway travel, the Novis ST-X 950 can be summoned in narrow alleys and off-road areas where larger vehicles struggle. It is the gold standard for early-game exploration and anomaly hunting.

Customization affects more than just aesthetics. Upgrading your engine, tires, and suspension will change how the car handles during high-speed chases or "Anomaly Races." You can adjust gear ratios, downforce, and even the sensitivity of the drift trigger to suit your personal driving style.

### Anomaly Hunting and World Exploration

Hethereau is a dense, vertical city filled with secrets. Exploration is driven by the "Witch's House" system. Throughout the city, you will spot ravens perched on signs or buildings. Following these ravens often leads to Oracle Stones.

You must submit these Oracle Stones at the Witch's House to receive permanent account upgrades. These rewards include:
*   Increased maximum stamina for sprinting and climbing.
*   Expanded mini-map visibility.
*   The ability to detect high-level anomalies from a further distance.

Prioritize exploration in the early hours of the game. Unlocking the fast-travel nodes (bus stops and subway stations) will save you a significant amount of time when moving between your various business properties and mission objectives.

### Gacha Mechanics: Dice and Banners

Neverness to Everness uses a board-game-themed gacha system that differs significantly from other titles in the genre. Instead of traditional "pulls," you roll "Solid Dice" or "Fabricated Dice." 

The system is designed to be more player-friendly than many of its competitors. It features a 90-pull guarantee for S-Rank characters, and notably, there is no "50/50" mechanic. If you hit the S-Rank pity on a limited banner, you are guaranteed to receive the featured character.

Players arriving in Hethereau often find themselves balancing two distinct progression paths: combat prowess and urban influence. To maximize your efficiency, you must address several key questions regarding resource management.

**What are the primary methods for farming character and weapon materials in Neverness to Everness?**
Farming is categorized by the type of stamina consumed. Combat-related resources, such as character experience and weapon enhancement materials, are primarily obtained through instanced challenges like Houdinii’s Magic Stage and Kaleidoscope stages. These activities consume Character Pixels. Urban resources, including the primary currency Fons, are tied to life skills and business management, which utilize City Tycoon Stamina.

**Where can players find specific resources like Hunter Guides, Arc Dyes, and Ascension materials?**
Hunter Guides are the primary source of Esper EXP and are found within Houdinii’s Magic Stage. Arc Dyes, used to level up weapons (Arcs), are located in the Kaleidoscope stage within Bridge Crossings. More specialized Ascension materials require participating in Anomaly Hunts or visiting the Bubble Can Factory for items like the Golden Appleseed.

**How does the stamina system (Character Pixels and City Tycoon Stamina) affect farming efficiency?**
NTE utilizes a dual-stamina system. Character Pixels are a regenerating resource used for combat rewards, while City Tycoon Stamina is often distributed on a weekly basis for non-combat activities. Understanding when to spend your daily Pixels on Modules versus when to save your Tycoon Stamina for high-yield events like the Pink Paws Heist is critical for long-term growth.

### Farming Character EXP and Ascension Materials
Character progression is gated by two factors: experience points and Ascension milestones. Hunter Guides come in three primary tiers: Rising, Senior, and Elite. 

To farm these, you must enter Houdinii’s Magic Stage. This instance features waves of enemies that must be cleared within a time limit. The higher the difficulty tier you select, the better the ratio of Elite Hunter Guides you receive.

Ascension materials are more specialized. Once an Esper reaches a level cap (e.g., Level 20, 40, or 60), you must provide specific items to unlock the next bracket. These are found in Anomaly Hunts. For example, the "Golden Appleseed" is a common requirement for early-game Light-attribute Espers and is often sourced from the Bubble Can Factory challenges.

### Arc (Weapon) Enhancement and the Bubble Can Factory
Arcs require Dyes to level up. These are farmed in the Kaleidoscope stages. Similar to Hunter Guides, Dyes are tiered by rarity. However, Arcs also require "Refining" materials to increase their star rating. 

The Bubble Can Factory serves as a specialized farming node for these refinement items. Unlike the standard combat stages, the Factory often involves specific mechanical puzzles or objective-based combat. Farming here is essential for unlocking the passive traits of high-tier Arcs like those used by [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or Zero.

### Mastering the Rabbit Hole for Modules
The Rabbit Hole is the primary end-game farming location for Modules and Cartridges. Modules function as the equipment system in NTE, providing stat boosts and set bonuses.

Farming the Rabbit Hole effectively requires the use of Zanhorias. These items come in Bronze, Silver, and Gold tiers and are used to "rewind" or reroll specific sub-stats on your Modules. 
*   **Bronze Zanhorias:** Used for Basic-tier Module rerolls.
*   **Silver Zanhorias:** Required for Epic-tier Module optimization.
*   **Gold Zanhorias:** Reserved for Legendary Modules found in deep Rabbit Hole layers.

Because the Rabbit Hole consumes a significant amount of Character Pixels, it is recommended to only farm this once your Espers have reached their current maximum Ascension and level caps.

### Urban Resource Farming: Fons and Life Skills
Fons are the lifeblood of Hethereau. Every upgrade, from leveling an Esper to buying a new car, costs Fons. While combat provides a small amount of currency, the most efficient farming methods are found in the City Tycoon system.

1.  **Pink Paws Heist:** This is a bi-weekly event that provides a massive injection of 1 million Fons. It is a high-priority activity that should never be missed.
2.  **City Tycoon Stamina Conversion:** Activities like Fishing, Racing, and Deliveries convert City Tycoon Stamina into Fons at a general rate of 1 Stamina to 1,000 Fons.
3.  **Cafe Management:** Your Cafe provides passive income. Upgrading your Cafe furniture and staff increases the Fons generated per hour, reducing the need to active-farm life skills.

### Efficiency Tips: Using the Hunter Exchange and Mall
The Mall contains the Hunter Exchange, a shop where you can spend Beetle Coins and other specialized medals. This is the best place to acquire missing materials without spending Character Pixels.

Once you reach Tycoon Rank 6, the Mall unlocks "Bulk Material Crates." These allow you to trade excess urban resources for combat-related materials. This is particularly useful if you find yourself with a surplus of Fons but a shortage of Ascension drops from Anomaly Pilgrimages.

# Neverness to Everness Character Profile and Stats: The Ultimate Guide

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/nte-character-profile-and-stats-guide/inline-1.webp)


Players transitioning into the open-world metropolis of Neverness to Everness often find themselves overwhelmed by the sheer number of numerical values and categorization systems. The most common questions involve the distinction between primary stats like Attack (ATK) and advanced combat attributes such as Cycle Rate or Cycle Intensity.

To build an effective team, you must understand:
*   What are the primary and secondary stats for characters in Neverness to Everness (NTE)?
*   How do character profiles, including Elements and Archetypes, affect gameplay?
*   What are the base stats for popular characters like Zero, [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte), and Mint?

In NTE, characters are officially referred to as **Espers**. An Esper is an individual capable of wielding supernatural elemental energy to combat "Oddities" or anomalies within the city. Unlike traditional RPGs where stats are purely linear, NTE uses a complex "Esper Cycle" system where character attributes directly dictate the speed and power of team-wide elemental reactions.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-character-profile-and-stats-guide/inline-2.webp)


Characters in NTE (Espers) are defined by three core stats: HP, ATK, and DEF, alongside advanced combat attributes like Cycle Rate and Cycle Intensity. While HP and Defense determine survivability, the most critical stats for high-end play are those that govern the Esper Meter.

Every character belongs to one of six Elements (Cosmos, Anima, Incantation, Chaos, Psyche, Lakshana) and one of five Archetypes (Solid, Liquid, Gas, Plasma, Bose). These classifications are not merely cosmetic; they dictate which weapons (Arcs) an Esper can equip and which elemental reactions they can trigger on the battlefield.

Profiles include rarity (S-Class or A-Class), weapon compatibility, and specific combat roles such as Main DPS, Support, or Break Damage. Stats are increased through leveling, Ascension, Resonance (obtaining duplicates), and the unique Console/Module gear system.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-character-profile-and-stats-guide/inline-3.webp)


The "Info Tab" in the character menu is the primary source for all numerical data regarding your Espers. Understanding these values is the difference between a team that struggles against basic street-level anomalies and one that clears endgame "Rabbit Hole" challenges with ease.

### Core Attributes: HP, Attack, and Defense

The foundational stats in NTE follow a standard gacha-RPG progression but scale heavily with the Console system. 

*   **HP (Health Points):** Determines the amount of damage an Esper can take before being knocked out. High HP is vital for "Solid" Archetype characters who often serve as the team's frontline.
*   **Attack (ATK):** The base value used to calculate damage for Normal Attacks, Skills, and Ultimates. Most DPS characters like Nanally prioritize Flat ATK and ATK% through their Module sub-stats.
*   **Defense (DEF):** Reduces the amount of incoming damage. In NTE, Defense is particularly important during "Break" states, as certain enemies can bypass a portion of your damage reduction if your Defense stat is neglected.

### Advanced Stats: Cycle Rate, Intensity, and Break

Beyond the basics, NTE introduces three stats that govern the flow of combat:

1.  **Cycle Rate:** This is arguably the most important stat for "Quick-Swap" teams. It acts as a multiplier for Esper Meter generation. A character with a high Cycle Rate can fill their meter and trigger an Esper Ability significantly faster than one without it. For example, Zero (the protagonist) has a naturally high Cycle Rate on their Ultimate, allowing for near-constant reaction triggers.
2.  **Cycle Intensity:** This stat determines the potency of the reactions listed in the elemental table (e.g., the damage of a "Blossom" turret or the duration of the "Remora" slow). High Cycle Intensity is the primary goal for Support and Sub-DPS characters.
3.  **Break:** This stat dictates how quickly an Esper can deplete an enemy's "Stagger Bar." Once the bar is gone, the enemy enters a Break state, taking increased damage and becoming susceptible to certain Triple Cycle reactions like "Discord."

### The 6 Elements and Esper Cycle Reactions

The Esper Cycle is the heart of NTE’s combat. Elements are arranged in two Trios: the **Top Trio** (Lakshana, Cosmos, Anima) and the **Bottom Trio** (Incantation, Chaos, Psyche).

*   **Duo Reactions:** Triggered by swapping from a character with a full Esper Meter to one with a neighboring element. For example, swapping from a Cosmos character to an Anima character triggers **Blossom**, spawning a turret that deals autonomous AoE damage.
*   **Triple Reactions:** If you trigger two Duo reactions within the same Trio group in quick succession, you activate a Triple Cycle. 
    *   **Charge (Top Trio):** Generates massive amounts of Ultimate energy for the active character.
    *   **Discord (Bottom Trio):** Deals a percentage of the enemy's maximum Break bar, instantly stunning most elite foes.

### Archetypes: Solid, Liquid, Gas, Plasma, and Bose

In Neverness to Everness, "Archetypes" serve as the class system that restricts weapon usage. You cannot equip a "Liquid" type weapon (Arc) on a "Solid" type character.

*   **Solid:** Typically heavy hitters or tanks. Nanally is a prime example of a Solid Archetype character who utilizes high-impact weaponry.
*   **Liquid:** Often associated with agile, multi-hit combatants. Mint falls into this category, using her speed to trigger frequent reactions.
*   **Gas:** Generally ranged or utility-focused Espers.
*   **Plasma:** High-energy characters often focused on burst damage.
*   **Bose:** A specialized archetype usually reserved for rare S-Class Espers with unique mechanical properties.

### Stat Progression: Ascension, Awakening, and Resonance

Improving an Esper’s profile involves three distinct paths:

1.  **Ascension:** Every 20 levels, characters hit a "Level Cap." You must use specific materials farmed from Anomaly Zones to Ascend the character, which significantly boosts their base HP, ATK, and DEF while unlocking new passive talents.
2.  **Resonance:** When you pull a duplicate of a character from a banner (such as Nanally's "The Ichi-daime" banner), you receive a Resonance item. There are six levels of Resonance (A1 through A6). For Nanally, A1 provides a significant boost to her follow-up attack damage, while A6 extends the duration of her "Ichi-daime's Authority" state.
3.  **Awakening:** This is a separate progression track that enhances specific active skills. Prioritize awakening the "Esper Ability" first for most characters, as this directly impacts the damage of your elemental reactions.

### The Console System: Modules and Cartridges Explained

The Console is the most innovative part of the NTE stat system. Instead of standard equipment slots, each character has a "Console Board" where you place Tetris-shaped **Modules**.

*   **Modules:** These come in different shapes (Type II, Type III, and Type IV). Each Module provides two fixed main stats (Flat ATK and Flat HP) and up to four random sub-stats. 
*   **Tetris Mechanic:** You must physically fit these shapes onto the character's unique grid. A shape that fits Zero’s board might not fit Nanally’s.
*   **Cartridges:** This is the "brain" of the Console. Equipping a Cartridge provides a massive set bonus (e.g., the "Fireflies and the Forest" set boosts Anima damage). 
*   **Shape Synergy:** Cartridges often require specific Module types to unlock their full power. For example, Mint gains a 7.5% CRIT Rate bonus for every Type III Module placed on her board. If you ignore the shapes and just look at the stats, you lose out on these massive percentage-based multipliers.

### NTE Character Tier List

Character stats and elemental synergy heavily dictate the current meta. S-Class characters like Nanally and Sakiri currently sit at the top of the tier list due to their high base scaling and ability to trigger "Hexed" and "Blossom" reactions with high uptime. However, A-Class characters like Mint are essential for F2P players as they provide reliable Anima application for "Charge" teams.

### Best Team Compositions for Beyond the Rails

Effective team building in NTE is about "connecting the dots" on the Esper Wheel. 
*   **The Blossom-Charge Team:** Uses Zero (Cosmos), Nanally (Anima), and Hathor (Lakshana). This team focuses on the Top Trio to trigger the "Charge" Triple Cycle, ensuring your Ultimates are always available.
*   **The DoT Scorch Team:** Combines Sakiri (Incantation) with Chaos and Psyche characters to focus on the "Discord" Triple Cycle, which is currently the most effective way to take down high-HP bosses in the "Rabbit Hole" dungeons.

### Weapon and Arc Compatibility Guide

Remember that Archetype compatibility is absolute. Before pulling on a weapon banner, ensure the Arc matches your intended Esper's Archetype. Nanally’s signature weapon, "Ready-Ready," is a Solid Archetype Arc that provides a massive 15% ATK boost and specific buffs to her "Critical Riposte" mechanic. Equipping a lower-rarity Arc that matches the Archetype is always better than having a high-rarity Arc that the character cannot use.

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/how-to-increase-stamina-nte/inline-1.webp)


Players frequently ask: How do I increase the maximum stamina cap in Neverness to Everness? To answer this, we first have to identify which stamina system you are trying to improve. NTE utilizes three separate bars that do not share a pool:

*   **Character Pixels (Combat Stamina):** This is your primary "gacha" energy used for rewards in Anomaly Zones, boss fights, and talent material domains.
*   **City Stamina (Tycoon Stamina):** This is a weekly resource used for non-combat "urban" activities like fishing, professional racing, and business deliveries.
*   **Movement Stamina (Exploration Bar):** This is the blue bar that appears when you sprint, climb buildings, or use specialized character traversal skills.

Increasing these caps requires different gameplay focuses, ranging from interior decorating in your apartments to leveling up your reputation through Hethereau Hobbies.

### Understanding the Three Stamina Systems in NTE

The most important distinction for a new player is that Character Pixels regenerate over time, whereas City Stamina is a fixed weekly allotment. 

Character Pixels are the lifeblood of your character's power. Without them, you cannot claim the rewards from the Echoes of the Past or the various Anomaly instances scattered throughout the city. The base cap of 240 is often insufficient for players who want to clear their daily tasks in one sitting.

City Stamina, on the other hand, governs your economic and social standing. If you want to buy the fastest cars or the most expensive penthouses, you need to spend City Stamina to earn Fons (the primary currency). Unlike Character Pixels, this bar does not refill every few minutes; it resets once a week, making the maximum cap vital for long-term progression.

Movement Stamina is the only bar that is not "spent" in a way that requires a long-term refill. It regenerates almost instantly when you stop sprinting or climbing. While the total length of this bar is currently fixed for all players, you can effectively "increase" its utility by using characters with high-efficiency movement kits.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-increase-stamina-nte/inline-3.webp)


The systems in Neverness to Everness are designed to reward players who engage with the "Urban Simulator" aspects of the game, not just the combat. To truly master stamina management, you need to invest in the housing market and the hobby system.

### How to Upgrade the Dream Weaver’s Knot

The **Dream Weaver’s Knot** is a specialized piece of Anomaly Furniture that acts as a battery for your Character Pixels. You cannot simply find this item in the open world; it must be crafted or obtained through specific property milestones.

1.  **Purchase a Property:** You first need a place to put your furniture. The **Eden Apartment** is the most common entry point for this system, though the **Wiener Apartments** also offer the necessary space for high-tier furniture placement.
2.  **Acquire the Blueprint:** Blueprints for the Dream Weaver’s Knot are usually obtained through the Appraisal Level rewards or by purchasing them from the furniture vendor in the Central District for a significant amount of Fons.
3.  **Placement and Syncing:** Once placed in your apartment, you must "Sync" with the furniture. This provides the initial +10 to your Character Pixel cap.
4.  **Leveling Up Furniture:** By using materials found in high-level Anomaly Zones, you can upgrade the Knot. Each level increases the cap, eventually allowing players to hold up to 280 or more Pixels, which is essential for players who cannot log in every 24 hours to spend their energy.

### Raising Your Tycoon Level Fast

City Stamina is arguably more important for players who enjoy the "Everness" side of the game—car customization and property management. Your Tycoon Level represents your influence in Hethereau's economy.

The most efficient way to raise your Tycoon Level is through **Hethereau Hobbies**. Each hobby has its own progression track, but they all contribute to your overall Tycoon experience.

*   **Street Racing:** Participating in sanctioned races in the Industrial Zone provides high Tycoon XP.
*   **Fishing:** A more relaxed way to earn XP, though it yields less per hour than racing.
*   **Deliveries:** Completing timed delivery missions for local businesses is the most consistent way to grind Tycoon levels if you have a fast vehicle.

### Character-Specific Traversal Perks

While the blue movement bar length is static, the "cost" of movement is variable based on your active character. This is where the gacha elements of NTE intersect with open-world exploration.

*   **Nanally:** Her "Urban Vanguard" trait allows her to run horizontally along buildings. During this animation, the stamina consumption is significantly lower than traditional vertical climbing. If you are struggling with tall skyscrapers, Nanally is the optimal choice.
*   **Skia:** Skia can enter a shadow state to move across vertical surfaces. While this still consumes stamina, the speed at which she moves means she covers more distance per stamina point than any other character.

### Managing the Weekly City Stamina Reset

City Stamina resets every Monday at 5:00 AM server time. Unlike Character Pixels, any unused City Stamina is lost—it does not roll over. To maximize your account's growth, you should aim to spend every point of City Stamina before the reset.

If you find yourself with excess City Stamina on Sunday night, the fastest way to "dump" it is through the **Business Investment** terminal in your owned properties. This allows you to convert stamina into Fons and Tycoon XP instantly, though at a slightly lower efficiency than manual racing or fishing.

### Recovery Items and Annulith Refreshes

When you run out of Character Pixels but still need to farm, you have two primary options: consumption items or premium currency.

**De-noise Solution:** This is a rare consumable item that restores 60 Character Pixels. These are typically given out as event rewards, maintenance compensation, or as part of the monthly battle pass. It is highly recommended to save these for "Double Drop" events rather than using them for standard daily farming.

**Annulith Refreshes:** You can spend Annulith (premium currency) to refill 60 Character Pixels. However, the cost increases with each subsequent use per day.

### Best Characters for Stamina-Free Exploration

If you are out of both Character Pixels and City Stamina, you can still progress by farming Fons and exploration nodes that do not require energy. 

**Chiz** is widely considered the best character for this "zero-stamina" gameplay. Her passive abilities allow her to locate hidden Fons caches and breakable objects in the environment more easily. ### How to Farm Fons for Property Purchases

Properties are the primary gate for increasing your stamina caps. The **Eden Apartment** costs roughly 1.5 million Fons, while luxury penthouses can exceed 10 million. 

To farm Fons efficiently without spending stamina:
1.  **Daily Commissions:** These provide a steady stream of Fons and Hunter XP.
2.  **Open World Chests:** Exploring the nooks and crannies of the city districts.
3.  **Selling Materials:** You can sell excess materials gathered from the world to vendors, though this is generally less efficient than business deliveries.

### Hunter Level vs. Appraisal Level Progression

It is easy to confuse Hunter Level with Appraisal Level. 
*   **Hunter Level:** Increases through combat and questing. It determines the difficulty and reward tier of Anomaly Zones.
*   **Appraisal Level:** Increases through collecting items, finding anomalies, and filling out your "Hethereau Handbook."

Increasing your Appraisal Level is often the hidden requirement for unlocking the blueprints for the **Dream Weaver’s Knot** and other stamina-related furniture. If you find that you cannot buy the necessary upgrades despite having the Fons, check your Appraisal Level in the main menu.

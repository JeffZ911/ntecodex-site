Selecting the right weapon in Neverness to Everness (NTE) is the difference between struggling with anomalies and clearing high-level commissions with ease. As of May 21, 2026, the meta for "mage" characters—those who specialize in Anima, Incantation, and Mental damage—has solidified around high-rarity Arcs that prioritize elemental scaling and energy management.

# Best Weapons for Mage Characters in Neverness to Everness (NTE)

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/best-weapons-mage-nte/inline-1.webp)


What are the best weapons (Arcs) for magic-focused characters in Neverness to Everness? In the current May 2026 meta, "mage" characters are defined not by staves or wands, but by their reliance on high-frequency elemental skills and ultimate abilities. Players often ask which Arcs provide the highest damage for Incantation, Anima, and Mental damage types, and how the different weapon types—specifically Plasma, Liquid, and Synthesis—benefit these specific playstyles. Furthermore, identifying the top-tier options for popular characters like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte), Mint, and Fadia is crucial for efficient resource allocation.

### Defining the Mage Archetype in NTE
In Neverness to Everness, there is no formal "Mage" class. Instead, players use the term to describe Espers who deal non-physical damage. These characters typically fall into the following elemental categories:
*   **Anima:** Focused on high-speed multi-hits and "Blossom" reactions (e.g., Nanally, Mint).
*   **Incantation:** Often involving damage-over-time (DoT) and "Scorch" or "Hexed" effects (e.g., [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide), Baicang).
*   **Mental/Psyche:** Scaling heavily with Max HP or Mental stats to trigger "Nova" reactions (e.g., Fadia).

### The Role of Arcs in Elemental Combat
Arcs are equippable disks that serve as your primary weapon. Unlike traditional RPG gear, an Arc’s effectiveness is determined by its **Arc Type** (Plasma, Liquid, Gas, Solid, or Synthesis). A character can only equip an Arc that matches their internal compatibility. For a mage playstyle, the weapon's passive effect—such as boosting Elemental DMG or reducing Ultimate cooldowns—is far more important than its raw physical ATK.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/best-weapons-mage-nte/inline-2.webp)


The best "mage" weapons in NTE are S-Rank Arcs that scale with Elemental or Mental damage rather than physical stats. For primary damage dealers, you should prioritize Arcs with CRIT DMG or Elemental DMG substats.

*   **Top Tier (S-Rank):** **Raging Flames** (Best for Lakshana/Anima hybrids like Hathor or Nanally), **Eternal Waltz** (Best for Fadia and Mental-scaling users), and **Camellia Society** (Best for Incantation-focused DPS like Sakiri or Baicang).
*   **Best F2P Option:** **Day Off** (Solid Type) is the premier free S-Rank for the Main Character (Zero) and other Solid-compatible casters due to its massive ATK boost and energy restoration. **Clear Skies** is the best A-Rank alternative for Anima-focused characters like Mint.
*   **Key Stats to Prioritize:** When evaluating Arcs, prioritize **CRIT DMG**, **Elemental DMG** (Anima/Incantation), and **Charge Efficiency**. These stats ensure your Ultimate uptime remains high, allowing you to cycle through elemental reactions more frequently.

### Best-in-Slot (BiS) Recommendations
For **Nanally**, the current Best-in-Slot is **Ready-Ready**, which provides a massive 24% CRIT Rate and enhances her Basic Attacks after she uses her Redirect Skill. For **Fadia**, **Eternal Waltz** is mandatory for endgame content, as it provides the HP scaling necessary for her Mental damage to peak. For **Mint**, **Fluff of Fleetness** is the preferred choice for sustained on-field damage, though **Clear Skies** is a highly competitive free-to-play alternative.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/best-weapons-mage-nte/inline-3.webp)


The power of a mage in NTE comes from the synergy between their elemental kit and their Arc's passive "Tuned" abilities. Unlike physical Arcs that focus on Break Intensity, mage Arcs often manipulate the character's HP, energy, or specific elemental damage bonuses.

### Plasma Arcs: Burst and Ultimate Synergy
Plasma-type Arcs are designed for characters who rely on explosive burst windows. 
*   **Raging Flames:** This S-Rank Arc is the gold standard for Lakshana and Anima-hybrid builds. It provides a base 15% boost to Lakshana DMG. Its true power, however, lies in its post-Ultimate buff: for 10 seconds after casting an Ultimate, the wearer receives a 10% surge to Redirect Skill damage. This effect can stack up to two times if skills are cast in rapid succession, making it perfect for the high-frequency playstyle of characters like Hathor.
*   **Ready-Ready:** While technically Nanally's signature, it is a Plasma powerhouse for any Anima caster. It offers 24% CRIT Rate at max level, allowing you to focus your Cartridge substats entirely on CRIT DMG and ATK.

### Synthesis Arcs: Hybrid Scaling and Special Effects
Synthesis Arcs are often the most complex, offering high rewards for specific playstyles.
*   **Eternal Waltz:** This is the premier choice for Fadia and other tanky magic users. Fadia’s damage scales directly with her Max HP, and Eternal Waltz provides a massive 41.25% HP boost at max level. Furthermore, it grants an 10% Mental DMG bonus for 10 seconds following an Ultimate. This synergy is vital for triggering high-damage "Nova" reactions.
*   **Camellia Society:** This is a high-risk, high-reward Arc for Incantation DPS. Its unique passive, **Silent Garden**, triggers when the wearer loses HP (without taking direct damage). It grants a stacking CRIT DMG buff (up to 4 stacks) and can trigger random Incantation bursts like **Harsh Rebuke** (5 instances of 24% ATK damage) or **Incessant Nagging**. It is the best choice for players who can manage their HP effectively.

### Liquid Arcs: Sustained Elemental Damage
Liquid Arcs are generally suited for characters who stay on the field for long durations.
*   **Fluff of Fleetness:** This is the go-to S-Rank for Mint. It provides 44% CRIT DMG and a unique passive that increases ATK by 5% to 9% every second while the character is on the field. Because Mint’s kit relies on her staying active to perform her whirlwind attacks, she can easily reach the maximum 5 stacks (up to 45% ATK bonus) before rotating out.
*   **Clear Skies:** As an A-Rank Liquid Arc, this is the best accessible option for Mint. It provides a flat 20% Anima DMG boost to both Redirect Skills and Ultimates, making it a reliable "stat stick" for players who haven't pulled an S-Rank Liquid Arc yet.

### F2P Alternatives and Hidden Arcs
If you are playing F2P, the **Day Off** S-Rank Arc is your most valuable asset. It is a Solid-type weapon that can be obtained for free by completing a hidden questline involving the NPC "Eclipse." It provides a flat 30% ATK increase and unlocks the **Eclipse** field, which restores 6 Ultimate Energy for every enemy defeated. This makes it an incredible tool for the Main Character (Zero) or any Solid-compatible caster who needs to keep their Ultimate gauge full.

### Synergy with Cartridges
To maximize these weapons, you must pair them with the correct Cartridge sets.
*   **Fireflies and the Forest:** The best set for Anima mages (Nanally, Mint). It provides a 10% Anima DMG bonus and increases CRIT DMG by 8% every time an enemy takes Anima damage (up to 7 stacks).
*   **Tiny Big Adventure:** The mandatory set for Fadia when using Eternal Waltz. It boosts Max HP whenever the wearer takes damage, which directly increases the damage of Fadia’s Mental-scaling skills.

### Understanding Arc Mixing
"Mixing" is the NTE term for weapon refinement. Reaching **Mixing T5** is essential for S-Rank Arcs because it often doubles the potency of their signature passives. For example, Raging Flames goes from a 15% Lakshana DMG boost at T1 to a 25% boost at T5. If you are a F2P player, it is often better to have a T5 A-Rank weapon like **Clear Skies** than a T1 S-Rank weapon with poor synergy.

### How to Get Free S-Rank Arcs
While many S-Rank Arcs are found in the "Scarborough Fair" weapon banner, several can be farmed through gameplay:
*   **Raging Flames:** Obtained by defeating the **Headless Rider** world boss. You must encounter him at Fox Window Street around 20:00 in-game time.
*   **Eternal Waltz:** Unlocked by completing the "Arachne" story quest and revisiting the boss in the Anomaly Realm.
*   **Day Off:** Obtained through the "Solar Eclipse" hidden quest. You must find the NPC "Eclipse" in five different locations across Hethereau City during specific weather conditions.

### Recommended Team Comps for Mage DPS
To make the most of your mage weapons, you need to trigger the right elemental cycles:
1.  **Blossom Team:** Nanally (Main DPS) + Jiuyuan (Sub-DPS) + Zero (Support) + Sakiri (Buffer). This team focuses on Anima and Cosmos reactions to clear waves of enemies.
2.  **Nova Team:** Fadia (Sub-DPS/Tank) + Daffodill (Main DPS) + Adler (Shield) + Haniel (Buffer). This team uses Fadia’s HP-scaling Mental damage to trigger high-damage Nova explosions.

### NTE Weapon Banner (Scarborough Fair) Tips
When pulling on the weapon banner, always check the **Pity Counter**. NTE features a guaranteed S-Rank every 80 pulls, with a 50/50 chance of it being the featured signature weapon. If you are building Nanally, wait for the "The Ichi-daime" banner to ensure you get her signature **Ready-Ready** Arc.

## Direct Answer

The primary premium currency in Neverness to Everness is Annulith. This resource is the lifeblood of the gacha system, used to purchase Solid Dice for Limited Banners, Fabricated Dice for Standard Banners, and Tri-Keys for Arc (weapon) Banners. The standard conversion rate is 160 Annulith per single pull.

Players can earn Annulith through several active and passive channels. Active methods include completing the main story quests, finishing side missions (Anomalies), and fulfilling daily Circle Bounties. Passive income is primarily generated through the City Tycoon system, which rewards players for managing urban properties and businesses. For those looking for long-term guaranteed rewards, visiting Nacupeda’s Pool daily provides Mhm Coins, which eventually lead to a guaranteed S-rank Arc. Additionally, the Fair Exchange shop allows for the redemption of Warp Pieces and Lost Pieces—earned from gacha duplicates—to acquire additional monthly pulls.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-get-more-currency-nte/inline-3.webp)


The economic structure of Neverness to Everness is designed to reward both daily consistency and deep exploration of its open-world urban environments. By balancing your time between combat missions and city management, you can ensure a steady stream of Annulith and summoning materials.

### Primary Premium Currencies: Annulith and Riftcrystal

Riftcrystal is the strictly "paid" currency of NTE. When you purchase a monthly pass or a top-up pack, you receive Riftcrystals. These can be converted into Annulith at a 1:1 ratio. While Riftcrystals are occasionally used for exclusive cosmetic bundles in the shop, their primary purpose for most players is conversion into Annulith to facilitate pulls.

Annulith is the "free" equivalent that can be earned through gameplay. Whether you are a F2P player or a "whale," the 160:1 ratio remains the golden rule: 160 Annulith equals one Solid Die, one Fabricated Die, or one Tri-Key.

### Summoning Currencies: Solid Dice, Fabricated Dice, and Tri-Keys

Neverness to Everness separates its summoning items to prevent players from accidentally spending limited-pull resources on the standard pool:

1.  **Solid Dice:** These are reserved for Limited-Time Character Banners (such as [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)'s "The Ichi-daime" banner). These should always be your priority when spending Annulith.
2.  **Fabricated Dice:** Used for the "Permanent" or "Standard" banner. You will naturally accumulate these through leveling up your account and completing certain milestones. It is generally advised not to spend Annulith on these.
3.  **Tri-Keys:** These are specifically for Arc Banners. Arcs function as the weapons or equipment for your Espers.

### Daily and Weekly Tasks: Circle Bounties and Login Rewards

The most reliable way to build your Annulith reserves is through the Circle Bounty system. Each day, you are assigned four tasks that range from defeating specific Anomalies to interacting with city NPCs. Completing all four daily bounties grants a total of 60 Annulith.

Weekly rewards are tracked through the "Urban Report" progress bar. By completing a set number of daily tasks and participating in weekly challenges, you can earn an additional [information unavailable] Annulith per week, along with upgrade materials for your Arcs.

### World Exploration: Chests, Anomalies, and Achievements

Hethereau is filled with hidden rewards. Exploration is categorized into three main types:
*   **Supply Chests:** Found in alleys, on rooftops, and inside buildings. Common chests provide 5 Annulith, while Rare and Precious chests provide 10 to 20 Annulith respectively.
*   **Anomalies:** These are world puzzles or combat encounters. Clearing an Anomaly for the first time usually rewards a small amount of Annulith and specialized city tokens.
*   **Achievements:** The achievement menu tracks everything from your driving distance to the number of cats you’ve petted. Each achievement milestone grants 5, 10, or 20 Annulith.

### The City Tycoon System: Passive Income and Beetle Coins

The City Tycoon system is a management mini-game that becomes a significant source of secondary currency. By purchasing storefronts and assigning Espers to manage them, you generate Beetle Coins over time.

Beetle Coins are spent in the Hunter Exchange shop. While you cannot buy Solid Dice directly with Beetle Coins, you can purchase essential character ascension materials, Arc EXP, and occasionally limited-time Fabricated Dice. Maximizing your Beetle Coin production allows you to save your Annulith strictly for summoning, as you won't need to spend it on resource refreshes.

### Nacupeda’s Pool: The 100-Day Path to a Free S-Rank Arc

Located in the heart of Illusion Town, Nacupeda’s Pool is a daily interaction point that every player should visit. By "making a wish" at the pool once per day, you receive 1 Mhm Coin.

The Mhm Coin shop features a rotating selection of S-rank Arcs. A guaranteed S-rank Arc typically costs 100 Mhm Coins. While this is a slow process, it provides a completely free, non-gacha method to obtain top-tier equipment for your characters. Consistent daily visits are the only way to progress this timer.

### The Fair Exchange: Maximizing Warp and Lost Pieces

Whenever you pull on a banner and receive a duplicate character or an Arc you already own, you receive either Warp Pieces (from 4-star/5-star equivalents) or Lost Pieces (from 3-star equivalents).

*   **Lost Piece Exchange:** This shop resets on the first of every month. You should always prioritize buying the 5 Solid Dice and 5 Tri-Keys available here. This is essentially "cash back" for your pulls.
*   **Warp Piece Exchange:** This is a more premium shop. You can save these to buy specific S-rank Espers on rotation or spend them on an unlimited supply of Solid Dice if you are pushing for a specific banner pity.

### Limited-Time Events and Redeem Codes

Hotta Studio frequently releases time-limited events that are the largest sources of "burst" currency. These events often feature mini-games, boss rushes, or narrative side-stories. Completing an entire event cycle typically yields between 600 and 1,200 Annulith.

Redeem codes are also released during official livestreams and social media milestones. These codes are one-time use and often provide 100 Annulith or a bundle of Solid Dice.

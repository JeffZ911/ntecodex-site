### The Esper Wheel: Adjacency and Synergy

The core of NTE’s combat is the Esper Wheel, a hexagonal representation of the six elemental forces. Unlike many RPGs where any two elements can react, NTE limits reactions to "neighbors" on this wheel. For example, Cosmos (Silver) is adjacent to Lakshana (Gold) and Anima (Green). Therefore, a Cosmos character can trigger a reaction when swapping with a Lakshana or Anima character, but they cannot directly trigger a Duo Cycle with an Incantation (Red) character because they are on opposite sides of the wheel.

This adjacency requirement forces players to think carefully about team order. If your team consists of Anima, Incantation, and Chaos characters, you have a linear path of reactions (Anima ↔ Incantation ↔ Chaos). However, if you bring Anima, Incantation, and Lakshana, the Lakshana character will be "isolated" from Duo Cycles unless specific multi-element skills are used.

### Managing the Esper Meter: Cycle Rate and Parrying

You cannot trigger Resonance at will; you must first fill the Esper Meter. Each character has an individual meter that fills as they deal damage or take actions on the field.

1.  **Attacks and Skills:** Standard combat fills the meter at a baseline rate.
2.  **The Cycle Rate Stat:** This is a secondary stat found on Arcs (weapons) and equipment. A higher Cycle Rate reduces the time needed to fill the Esper Meter.
3.  **Perfect Parries:** Performing a perfect parry grants an "Instant Charge" to the current character's Esper Meter, allowing for an immediate swap-reaction.
4.  **The Protagonist (Zero):** Zero has a unique role in the meta. Their ultimate skill instantly fills the Esper Meters of all party members. This allows Zero to act as a "Resonance Enabler," jumping into the fight to force a Triple Cycle reaction even if your rotation was interrupted.

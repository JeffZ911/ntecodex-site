# Neverness to Everness Character Profile and Guide: Every Esper Explained

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-character-profile-and-guide-20260516/inline-2.webp)


Neverness to Everness features a launch roster of 16+ characters, known as Espers, categorized into S-Rank and A-Rank rarities. These characters are the core of the gameplay, each possessing unique supernatural abilities used to combat "Anomalies" throughout the city. 

Characters are defined by six Elemental Attributes: **Cosmos, Chaos, Anima, Psyche, Incantation, and Lakshana**. Combat is highly dynamic, relying on the **Esper Cycle** switching mechanic, which allows players to rotate through three active team members to trigger powerful elemental reactions. Furthermore, each Esper is restricted to a specific **Arc Compatibility** type (Solid, Gas, Liquid, Plasma, or Synthesis), which dictates which weapons (Arcs) they can equip.

At launch, the meta is dominated by S-Rank powerhouses like **Nanally** (Anima Main DPS), **Hotori** (Cosmos Burst Support), and **[Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide)** (Incantation Support). Most characters are obtained through the **Scarborough Fair**, a unique board-game-style gacha system. This system features a 90-pull hard pity and, notably, removes the traditional "50/50" mechanic, ensuring that any S-Rank pulled on a limited banner is the featured character.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-character-profile-and-guide-20260516/inline-3.webp)


The depth of Neverness to Everness lies in how its characters interact with the world and each other. Success in Hethereau requires more than just high stats; it requires a deep understanding of elemental synergy and weapon compatibility.

### Understanding Rarity: S-Rank vs. A-Rank Espers

Espers in NTE are divided into two primary tiers. **S-Rank Espers** are the rarest units, typically featuring complex mechanics like Nanally’s anti-gravity walking or Hotori’s time-stop ultimate. These characters usually serve as the "engine" of a team, providing the bulk of the damage or unique utility that cannot be replicated.

**A-Rank Espers** are more common and serve as the backbone of many team compositions. While they may have lower base statistics than S-Ranks, their "Mind Shard" upgrades (Awakenings) are much easier to obtain, often allowing a fully awakened A-Rank to outperform a base-level S-Rank in specific support roles.

### The Six Elemental Attributes and Combat Synergy

Every Esper is attuned to one of six elements. These elements are arranged on an "Esper Wheel," and reactions only occur between neighboring elements.

*   **Cosmos (Silver):** Focused on light and time manipulation.
*   **Anima (Green):** Focused on life force and physical enhancement.
*   **Incantation (Red):** Focused on force and gravity.
*   **Chaos (Purple):** Focused on dark energy and debuffs.
*   **Psyche (Azure):** Focused on mental energy and mind control.
*   **Lakshana (Gold):** Focused on order and defensive barriers.

**Elemental Reactions (Duo Reactions):**
*   **Blossom (Cosmos + Anima):** Spawns an automated turret that deals AoE damage.
*   **Hexed (Anima + Incantation):** Deals bonus damage based on a percentage of damage taken by the enemy in the last 12 seconds.
*   **Scorch (Incantation + Chaos):** Applies a 15-second Damage-over-Time (DoT) effect.
*   **Nova (Chaos + Psyche):** Attaches a bomb to the enemy that detonates for massive damage after 5 seconds.
*   **Stain (Psyche + Lakshana):** Increases the target's vulnerability to Psyche and Lakshana damage by 50%.
*   **Remora (Lakshana + Cosmos):** Marks and slows enemies, reducing their attack speed.

### Arc Compatibility: Matching Weapons to Espers

Weapons in NTE are known as **Arcs**. Unlike many RPGs where any character can use a sword or a gun, NTE uses a "Phase Compatibility" system. There are five types of Arcs: **Solid, Gas, Liquid, Plasma, and Synthesis**. 

If an Esper has **Solid Compatibility** (like Nanally), they can only equip Solid-type Arcs. Equipping the correct Arc is vital because S-Rank Arcs provide massive percentage-based stat boosts and unique passive abilities. For example, the **Song of the Whale** Arc is a top-tier Solid weapon that increases damage against "Broken" enemies and restores health upon their defeat.

### Featured Character Profile: Nanally (Anima DPS)

Nanally is the "Apex DPS" of the 2026 launch meta. As a member of the Eibon Antique Shop, she utilizes a "Street Fighter" combat style that emphasizes high-frequency follow-up attacks.

*   **Core Mechanic - Ichi-daime's Authority:** Activated via her Skill (**Colucci Howling Technique**), this state allows Nanally to ignore gravity. She can walk on walls and ceilings, providing a massive advantage in 3D combat environments. While in this state, she gains a 30% Crit DMG bonus.
*   **Ultimate - Colucci Ultimate Technique:** Nanally summons her **Underboss**, a spectral entity that fights alongside her for 6 seconds. The Underboss mirrors her attacks, dealing follow-up Anima damage and creating a small pull effect to group enemies.
*   **Best Build:** Focus on Anima Damage and Crit DMG. Her signature Arc is **Song of the Whale**, and her best Module set is **Fireflies and the Forest**, which provides stacking Crit DMG buffs.

### Featured Character Profile: Hotori (Cosmos Burst)

Hotori is a technical Sub-DPS and support who specializes in time manipulation. Her gameplay revolves around a unique resource called the **Non-Closed Timepiece**.

*   **Core Mechanic - Non-Closed Timepiece:** This gauge fills at a rate of 6 units per second while Hotori is active or off-field (up to 120 units).
*   **Skill - Present Replay:** Consumes 60 energy to enter a "Recording" state for 5 seconds. During this time, any Support or Redirect skills used by her teammates are "recorded."
*   **Ultimate - World's Tide:** Hotori stops time for a brief period, disabling character switching. During this window, she replays all recorded ally skills at once, delivering a massive burst of Cosmos damage.
*   **Role:** She is essential for "Burst Window" teams, allowing players to compress a whole team's damage into a single frozen moment.

### Featured Character Profile: Sakiri (Incantation Support)

Sakiri is an S-Rank Incantation specialist who uses her massive sentient weapon, **Kiroumaru**, to control the battlefield.

*   **Core Mechanic - Eating Mode:** By holding her Basic Attack, Sakiri mounts Kiroumaru. In this state, she can move freely while Kiroumaru pulls nearby enemies into its mouth. It can even "devour" weak enemies, removing them from the field for 180 seconds while they are "digested."
*   **Ultimate - Feast of Gluttony:** Sakiri releases **Gravity Slurry**, which pins enemies to the ground with intense gravity, preventing movement and reducing their Break gauge.
*   **Support Utility:** Her Support Skill provides a 30% ATK boost to the team based on her own stats, making her one of the most versatile buffers in the game.

### Free-to-Play Character Guide: Chiz and Esper Zero

Neverness to Everness is notably generous to free-to-play players, offering two powerful S-Rank characters through progression:

1.  **Esper Zero (The Protagonist):** Zero is a Cosmos-element Solid Esper. While often overlooked as a "starter" unit, Zero has a 100% Cycle Rate on their Skill, meaning they can fill their Esper Meter almost instantly. This makes Zero the perfect "battery" for triggering elemental reactions in any team.
2.  **Chiz:** This mice-themed Cosmos Esper is unlocked for free once you reach **City Tycoon Level 18**. Chiz is a unique DPS who uses "Grain" as a resource. By managing her "Surplus" and "Debt" states in the City Tycoon management system, players can eventually unlock all of her Awakenings (A6) without spending any premium currency.

### The Gacha Board Game: How to Recruit New Espers

Summoning in NTE takes place at the **Scarborough Fair**. Instead of a simple animation, players roll dice to move an avatar across a board.

*   **Tiles:**
    *   **Miracle Box:** Provides high-rarity Arcs.
    *   **Warp Piece:** Provides fragments used to buy more pulls.
    *   **Lost Piece:** Provides character and weapon upgrade materials.
    *   **Hero Chest:** Contains the actual Esper.
*   **Pity System:** The base rate for an S-Rank is approximately 0.99%. However, once you reach **70 pulls** without an S-Rank, the board enters a "Modified" state where S-Rank tiles appear every 5–6 spaces.
*   **Hard Pity:** At **90 pulls**, an S-Rank is 100% guaranteed. Because there is no 50/50 mechanic, if you are pulling on a limited banner (like "The Ichi-daime"), that S-Rank is guaranteed to be the featured character.

### Character Progression: Awakening and Modules

To keep your Espers competitive against high-level Anomalies, you must invest in three progression systems:

1.  **Leveling (Hunter Guides):** Use **Hunter Guides** and **Beetle Coins** to increase an Esper's base level. This is the most straightforward way to increase HP and ATK.
2.  **Awakening (Mind Shards):** Obtaining duplicate copies of a character grants **Mind Shards**. These are used to unlock six Awakening levels (A1–A6). These upgrades often provide transformative effects, such as Nanally's A2, which increases the duration of her Underboss summon.
3.  **Modules (Cartridges):** Modules are equippable items that provide set bonuses. A standard setup involves a 2-piece and 4-piece set. For DPS characters, look for Modules that provide **Crit Rate** or **Elemental Damage Bonus** main stats. Optimization involves farming these in Anomaly Zones to find "Legendary" grade Modules with perfect sub-stats.

# How to Increase Hunter Level in Neverness to Everness (NTE)
> 📝 **Editorial Note:** This guide is AI-assisted and game data evolves rapidly. Please cross-reference with in-game information. _Updated: 2026-05-21._


In Neverness to Everness (NTE), your Hunter Level serves as the primary indicator of your account's progression and dictates the strength of your roster. This account-wide rank determines which districts of Hesperia you can access, the quality of gear you can farm, and the maximum level your Espers can reach. Unlike individual character levels, which are increased using experience materials, the Hunter Level grows as you engage with the game's various systems, from main story "Episodes" to managing a business in the City Tycoon mode.

### What is the Hunter Level in Neverness to Everness?
The Hunter Level is a global experience system that tracks your total activity within the game. It is visible at the top of the main menu and serves as the primary "gate" for almost every other progression mechanic. While you might have a powerful S-Rank Esper, their potential is strictly limited by your Hunter Level; if your account rank is low, your characters cannot ascend past their current level caps.

### How does the account-wide Hunter Level differ from individual Esper levels?
It is essential to distinguish between your Hunter Level and your Esper levels. 
*   **Esper Level:** This applies to individual characters like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or Chiz. You increase this by using experience items earned from Anomaly Zones.
*   **Hunter Level:** This is your account rank. You increase this by completing quests, spending Character Pixels (stamina), and exploring the city.

The Hunter Level acts as the ceiling for Esper levels. For example, if your Hunter Level has not yet reached the milestone required to increase your Appraisal Level to Rank 2, your Espers will be hard-capped at Level 20.

### Why is increasing your Hunter Level the most important progression goal?
In the early game, your primary objective should be reaching Hunter Level 30. This specific level is the "tipping point" where the game’s economy shifts. At Level 30, Legendary (gold) Cartridges—the game's version of high-tier equipment—begin to drop from Anomaly Zones. Before this point, spending your Character Pixels on gear farming is generally considered inefficient because you will quickly replace blue and purple rarity items with the Legendary versions unlocked at Level 30.

### How does Hunter Level interact with the Appraisal Level (World Level) system?
The Appraisal Level is NTE’s world difficulty scaling system. As your Hunter Level increases, you will periodically hit "Appraisal Gates." These are special Ascension Quests that appear at Hunter Levels 20, 40, and 50. Completing these quests raises your Appraisal Level, which increases the level of enemies in the open world and Anomaly Zones but significantly improves the rarity and quantity of the loot you receive.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-increase-nte-hunter-level/inline-3.webp)


Increasing your Hunter Level is not a single-track grind but rather a "virtuous cycle" where city activities and combat encounters feed into each other. To reach the Level 30 milestone quickly, you should prioritize tasks that offer the highest "EXP-per-minute" ratio.

### Prioritizing Episodes and Spinoffs
Main Story Episodes are the backbone of your experience gain. Not only do they provide the highest individual EXP rewards, but they also unlock new regions like the Miguel District and the Unheard Shores. Many side systems, such as the City Tycoon management or specific Anomaly Zones, are locked behind story progress. 

Spinoffs are character-focused side stories that become available as you progress. These are excellent secondary sources of EXP and often reward you with character-specific upgrade materials. If you find yourself a few levels short of an Appraisal Gate, clearing your backlog of Spinoffs is the fastest way to bridge the gap.

### The Exploration Guide and Daily Quests Loop
Once you clear Prologue - Part 2, the Exploration Guide becomes your daily checklist. The Daily Tasks within this guide are the most time-efficient source of EXP in the game. Most players can finish these in under 15 minutes. 
*   **Activity Level:** Completing tasks like "Upgrade an Arc" or "Participate in a Race" fills an Activity Bar.
*   **Milestone Chests:** Reaching 100 Activity Level unlocks the final daily chest, which contains a large portion of your daily 1,500 EXP, along with Annuliths (premium currency).

### Maximizing Character Pixels (Stamina) Efficiency
Character Pixels are your primary combat resource. You gain 1 Pixel every 6 minutes, totaling 240 Pixels over a 24-hour period. 
*   **Never Let Pixels Cap:** Once you hit the 240 cap, you stop generating "free" Hunter EXP.
*   **Spending Strategy:** Before Hunter Level 30, avoid farming Cartridges. Instead, spend your Pixels on "Bubble Can Factory" nodes for Arc Ascension materials or "Rabbit Hole" stages for character elevation items. These provide the same 100 EXP per 20 Pixels but give you materials that remain useful throughout the entire game.
*   **De-noise Solutions:** Use these items to restore 60 Pixels immediately. These are best saved for when you are close to a Hunter Level milestone and need one or two more runs to rank up.

### Anomaly Commissions and Open-World Exploration
While exploring Hesperia, you will encounter Anomaly Commissions. These are short, localized combat or puzzle challenges marked on your map. They offer between 160 and 300 EXP and are repeatable to an extent. 

Exploration also rewards the diligent. Unlocking **Wertheimer Towers** reveals portions of the map and provides a fast-travel point along with 100 EXP. **ReroRero Phone Booths** serve a similar purpose, offering 50 EXP and additional fast-travel nodes. **Gifts from 21** are small cube-shaped anomalies scattered throughout the city; while they only grant 10 EXP each, there are hundreds of them, making them a significant source of "hidden" experience.

### City Tycoon Leveling and Passive EXP Sources
NTE features a robust management system called City Tycoon. By managing cafes, purchasing properties, and completing odd jobs, you increase your Tycoon Level. Each time this level increases, you receive a direct injection of 300 to 500 Hunter EXP. 
*   **Passive Income:** Your Cafe by Origen earns Fons (main currency) passively. Higher Tycoon Levels increase these earnings, which you can then reinvest into city activities that grant more Hunter EXP.
*   **Chiz Rewards:** Progression in City Tycoon is also the primary way to earn copies of the S-Rank character Chiz, making it a dual-purpose goal for both account EXP and roster strength.

### Understanding Appraisal Level Gates and World Scaling
Your progress will be halted at Hunter Levels 20, 40, and 50 until you complete an Appraisal Level Ascension Quest. It is a common mistake to ignore these quests while continuing to farm. If you are at Hunter Level 20 and have not cleared the Ascension Quest, any Hunter EXP you earn will be "stored" but will not increase your level until the gate is cleared.

## Related

![Related](/img/how-to-increase-nte-hunter-level/inline-4.webp)


Raising your Hunter Level does more than just increase numbers; it fundamentally changes the loot tables of the game. As your Appraisal Level rises, the drop rates for high-rarity Arcs and Cartridges improve. For instance, while S-Rank Arcs can technically drop at lower levels, the probability increases significantly once you surpass Hunter Level 40.

### Hunter Level Rewards and Milestones
Every time you level up, you can claim rewards from the Hunter Association menu. These rewards often include:
*   **Annuliths:** Used for gacha pulls and Pixel refreshes.
*   **Fabricated Dice:** A currency often used for City Tycoon progression or specific gacha banners.
*   **Elite Hunter Guides:** High-tier experience materials for your Espers.
*   **De-noise Solutions:** To help you maintain your Pixel spending streaks.

Special milestones, such as Level 18 and Level 30, often provide "Hunter Supply Packs" containing rare materials needed for Esper ability upgrades.

### Managing World Difficulty (Appraisal Level)
A unique feature of Neverness to Everness is the ability to lower your Appraisal Level. If you find that the world difficulty has scaled too quickly after a rank-up and your Espers are struggling against open-world enemies, you can manually lower your Appraisal Level by one stage through the main menu. 

Note that lowering your Appraisal Level will decrease the quality of the rewards you receive from world activities. However, it does not affect the level caps you have already unlocked for your characters. This is a useful tool for players who want to focus on city exploration or story content without the pressure of high-difficulty combat.

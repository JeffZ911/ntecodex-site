## Detailed Explanation

The depth of Neverness to Everness lies in its dual-layered progression: combat strength and urban management. Balancing these two requires a thorough understanding of how different resources interact.

### Mastering the Two Stamina Systems

NTE utilizes two distinct stamina pools that do not overlap, which can be confusing for new Hunters.

**Character Pixels** are your primary combat resource. These are required to claim rewards from Anomaly Zones (bosses), Material Instances, and Gear Domains. You have a maximum cap of 240 Character Pixels. They regenerate at a rate of 1 Pixel every 6 minutes, meaning a full recharge takes exactly 24 hours. Because this is a 1:1 ratio with the daily cycle, any delay in spending them results in "capped" stamina and lost progression.

**City Stamina**, on the other hand, is a weekly resource dedicated to "Hethereau Hobbies." This includes activities such as street racing, fishing, and certain high-stakes arcade games. Unlike Character Pixels, City Stamina does not reset daily; you receive a fixed amount every Monday. It is designed to allow players to engage with the city's sandbox elements at their own pace throughout the week.

### The Exploration Guide: Your Primary Source of Hunter EXP

The Exploration Guide serves as the "Daily Mission" hub for NTE. By completing Participation Tasks, you earn points that unlock tiered rewards. Reaching the 100-point milestone is mandatory for any player looking to save for upcoming banners.

The standard daily tasks usually include:
*   **Daily Login:** 10 Points.
*   **Earn 10,000 Fons:** 20 Points (Easily done via the Cafe or open-world combat).
*   **Spend 120 Character Pixels:** 40 Points (Half of your daily natural regen).
*   **Complete 1 City Commission:** 30 Points.

Accumulating 100 points grants a bundle of Annulith (the premium currency), Hunter EXP, and various upgrade materials like [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-21].

### Daily Interaction Points: Nacupeda’s Pool and The Witch’s House

Hethereau is filled with "Interaction Points" that offer unique long-term benefits. Two of these are non-negotiable for beginners.

**Nacupeda’s Pool** is located in the [Information unavailable] district. Every day, you can interact with the pool and select the "Sincere Wish" option. This grants you **Mhm! Coins**. While the daily amount seems small, these coins are the only currency used in the Nacupeda Exchange Shop. Saving 100 Mhm! Coins allows you to purchase a guaranteed S-Rank Arc, making this a vital task for F2P players.

**The Witch’s House** offers a daily "Fortune Telling" mechanic. Depending on your luck, you will receive one of three buffs:
1.  **Lost Tales:** Reveals the location of a hidden "Anomaly Side Quest" on your map.
2.  **Bless:** Provides a combat buff (e.g., +10% Elemental DMG) that lasts for 24 hours.
3.  **Treasure Hunt:** Marks a nearby location where you can find Oracle Stones, which are used for high-level gear refinement.

### City Tycoon: Passive Income and Fons Farming

The City Tycoon system is NTE’s business management layer. As a beginner, your focus is the **Cafe by Origen**. This establishment generates Fons (the standard gold currency) passively over time.

To maximize your daily intake:
*   **Check the Ledger:** Collect your accumulated Fons. The storage has a limit based on your City Tycoon level, so don't let it sit for more than 12-18 hours.
*   **Restock Ingredients:** Use Chiz to source ingredients from the open world or local vendors. Without ingredients, the Cafe stops producing Fons.
*   **Assign Staff:** Ensure your most efficient characters are assigned to the Cafe roles to boost the "Fons per Hour" metric.

### Social Systems: Bond Encounters and Gifting

Bond levels with characters like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or Mint are not just for flavor; they unlock powerful passive stat bonuses, exclusive outfits, and additional story chapters that reward Annulith.

You are limited to **10 gifts per day** across your entire roster, with a maximum of **3 gifts per individual character**. It is highly recommended to focus your gifts on your primary "Main DPS" first to unlock their Bond-exclusive combat modifiers. Gifts can be purchased at various convenience stores in Hethereau or found in chests during exploration.

### Open World Bounties: Trading Cookies for Annulith

While exploring, you will encounter "Illegal Activities"—essentially groups of rogue anomalies or street thugs. Defeating these enemies occasionally drops **Goodheart Crispy Mouse Cookies**. 

These cookies serve as a specialized currency. You can take them to the **Security Office** in central Hethereau and trade them with the NPC officers. The weekly trade limit allows you to exchange these cookies for a significant amount of Annulith and character EXP items. This is the most consistent way to "farm" premium currency outside of one-time mission rewards.

Once you have mastered the daily grind, you must look toward the broader horizon of weekly and monthly objectives to keep your account competitive.

### Don't Forget Your Weekly Checklist

While dailies provide the foundation, weeklies provide the massive spikes in power.
*   **Anomaly Pilgrimages:** These are high-difficulty combat trials that reset every Monday. They are the primary source of high-tier gear and "Arc" upgrade materials.
*   **Realm of Greed:** A weekly dungeon focused on Fons and EXP materials. It consumes a larger chunk of Character Pixels but offers significantly higher efficiency than standard farming.
*   **Circle Bounty (Battle Pass):** Ensure you hit the weekly 12,000 EXP cap. The Circle Bounty rewards include free pulls on the standard banner and rare materials required for "Trancension" (level cap breaking).
*   **Special City Commissions:** These are longer, multi-part quests that appear once a week and often involve driving or puzzle-solving mechanics in the city's outskirts.

### How Dailies Impact Your Hunter Level

Your **Hunter Level** (account level) is the ultimate gatekeeper in NTE. Most story chapters and high-level Anomaly Zones are locked behind specific Hunter Level milestones. 

Every Character Pixel spent and every Exploration Guide task completed provides Hunter EXP. By strictly following the daily routine, you can expect to reach the mid-game (Hunter Level 30-40) within the first two weeks of play. This opens up the **Appraisal Level** system, allowing you to identify and equip "Anomaly Gear" with randomized sub-stats, which is the core of the NTE endgame loop.

Finally, always keep an eye on **Ebisu’s Auction House** and the Mall exchanges. These shops reset monthly and often contain discounted "Limited Pull Tickets" that you should prioritize purchasing with the secondary currencies earned from your daily tasks.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-character-guide-beginners/inline-2.webp)


Espers are the playable characters in Neverness to Everness (NTE), each assigned one of six elemental attributes: Cosmos, Anima, Incantation, Chaos, Psyche, and Lakshana. Combat revolves around the "Esper Cycle," a mechanic where swapping between characters with adjacent elements on the Esper Wheel triggers powerful reactions. For example, transitioning from a Cosmos Esper to an Anima Esper triggers the Blossom reaction, providing both offensive and utility benefits.

To maximize efficiency, beginners should focus on the free S-Class characters available at launch. This includes Zero (your starting character), Aurelia (unlocked via the 3-day login event), and Chiz (earned through City Tycoon milestones). Building a team based on elemental adjacency is more effective than simply grouping high-rarity units. Furthermore, characters must be equipped with Arcs (weapons) that match their compatibility type—Plasma, Solid, Gas, Liquid, or Synthesis—to activate unique passive resonances. Consoles function as the primary stat-boosting gear, where players should prioritize "[Element] DMG Up" primary stats that match the Esper’s specific attribute.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-character-guide-beginners/inline-3.webp)


The depth of NTE combat lies in the interaction between character attributes and the timing of swaps. Unlike traditional action RPGs where you might stay on a "Main DPS" for long periods, NTE encourages constant rotation to keep the Esper Cycle active.

### Understanding the Six Esper Attributes

Every Esper in Hesperia is attuned to a specific frequency of supernatural energy. These are organized into a circular relationship known as the Esper Wheel. The position of an element on this wheel is vital because reactions only occur when you swap between "neighbors."

| Attribute | Adjacent To (Clockwise) | Adjacent To (Counter-Clockwise) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cosmos** | Anima | Lakshana |
| **Anima** | Incantation | Cosmos |
| **Incantation** | Chaos | Anima |
| **Chaos** | Psyche | Incantation |
| **Psyche** | Lakshana | Chaos |
| **Lakshana** | Cosmos | Psyche |

Focusing on these relationships allows you to plan a rotation. If your team consists of Zero (Cosmos), Aurelia (Anima), and [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) (Incantation), you can trigger reactions by swapping from Zero to Aurelia, and then from Aurelia to Nanally. Swapping directly from Zero to Nanally will not trigger a standard Duo Reaction because they are not adjacent on the wheel.

### Mastering the Esper Cycle and Reactions

The Esper Cycle is the engine of NTE combat. As you fight with your active character, you generate Esper Meter. Once the meter is full, the portraits of your off-field characters will glow, indicating that an Esper Cycle swap is available. 

#### Duo and Trio Reactions

When you swap to an adjacent element with a full meter, you trigger a Duo Reaction. These are high-impact effects that can turn the tide of a boss fight.

| Reaction Name | Elements Involved | Combat Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blossom** | Cosmos + Anima | Creates a floral field that deals continuous Anima DMG and heals the active Esper. |
| **Hexed** | Incantation + Chaos | Applies a curse that reduces enemy DEF and deals periodic Chaos DMG. |
| **Resonance** | — | — |
| **Shatter** | — | — |

#### Generating Esper Meter

Filling the meter quickly is the key to maintaining high DPS. Different actions generate meter at varying rates:

| Action Type | Meter Generation (Approximate) |
| :--- | :--- |
| Normal Attack String (Full) | 15 - 20 Points |
| Elemental Skill | 25 - 30 Points |
| Ultimate Ability | 40 Points |
| Perfect Dodge Counter | 15 Points |
| Successful Parry | 50 Points |

A successful Parry is the fastest way to trigger a swap. Mastering the timing of the Parry mechanic allows you to cycle through your entire team in seconds, layering multiple reactions on top of one another.

### Character Rarity and the Gacha System

Espers are divided into two primary rarities: S-Class (Gold) and A-Class (Purple). While S-Class units generally possess higher base stats and more complex ability kits, several A-Class units are essential for their specific elemental applications.

The gacha system in NTE uses a "pity" mechanic. You are guaranteed an A-Class unit every 10 pulls and an S-Class unit every 80 pulls. The "The Ichi-daime" banner, featuring Nanally, is the first limited-time rate-up banner available at launch. For beginners, the "Beginner's Choice" banner allows you to select a target S-Class from the standard pool (such as [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide) or [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte)) to receive after 50 pulls at a discounted rate.

### Best Beginner Characters and Free Units

You do not need to spend money to build a competitive team in NTE. The game provides several high-tier units through gameplay progression.

*   **Zero (S-Class, Cosmos):** Your protagonist. Zero is a versatile damage dealer whose kit scales well into the late game. Being Cosmos element makes Zero an easy starting point for the Esper Wheel.
*   **Aurelia (S-Class, Anima):** Available as a reward for logging in for three consecutive days. Aurelia excels at crowd control and triggering the Blossom reaction when paired with Zero.
*   **Chiz (S-Class, Lakshana):** Obtained by reaching Level 10 in the City Tycoon system. Chiz provides massive shields and defensive utility, making her perfect for difficult "Anomaly" boss fights.
*   **Mint (A-Class, undisclosed):** A reliable support unit given during the prologue.
*   **Adler (A-Class, undisclosed):** A ranged specialist provided during the first chapter of the main story.

For those pulling on banners, **Nanally (S-Class, Incantation)** is the premier DPS at launch. Her ability to stay airborne while dealing sustained Incantation damage makes her safer to play than many melee units. **Sakiri (S-Class, Chaos)** is another top-tier choice for players looking for high burst damage and "Hexed" reaction synergy.

### Equipping Your Espers: Arcs and Consoles

Once you have your team, you must optimize their gear. There are two main components: Arcs and Consoles.

#### Arcs (Weapons)

Arcs come in five compatibility types. An Esper can only equip an Arc that matches their internal compatibility. If you try to equip a Solid-type Arc on a Plasma-compatible Esper, you will lose out on the "Arc Resonance" bonus, which typically grants a 15% to 25% boost to the Esper's primary stat.

1.  **Plasma:** Often focused on Crit Rate and Attack Speed.
2.  **Solid:** Usually provides DEF and HP scaling for tankier Espers.
3.  **Gas:** Focused on Energy Regeneration and Esper Meter gain.
4.  **Liquid:** Provides Elemental Mastery and [reaction damage](https://ntecodex.com/guides/nte-chaos-vs-psyche-reaction-damage-gear-guide) bonuses.
5.  **Synthesis:** Versatile Arcs that offer balanced stat spreads.

#### Consoles (Equipment)

Consoles are the "software" you install into your Esper's neural link. You can equip up to six Consoles. They provide a primary stat and several sub-stats. For beginners, the most important rule is to match the Console's "DMG Bonus" type to the Esper's attribute. A Cosmos Esper like Zero gains zero benefit from an "Anima DMG Up" Console. 

Look for the following stat priorities for damage dealers:
1.  [Element] DMG Bonus % (Slot 4)
2.  Crit Rate / Crit DMG (Slot 6)
3.  ATK %
4.  Energy Recharge %

### The 'City Tycoon' System and Character Progression

Character growth is uniquely tied to the "City Tycoon" management sim. While you level up Espers using "Ether Shards" found in combat, their "Potential Breakthroughs" (level cap increases) often require materials produced by businesses you own in Hesperia.

To progress your characters efficiently:
*   **Assign Espers to Businesses:** Certain Espers have "Urban Talents." For example, Nanally provides a bonus to revenue when assigned to high-end retail shops.
*   **Upgrade Shops:** Higher-level shops produce rarer breakthrough materials. If you find your characters stuck at Level 40, check if your "City Tycoon" rank is high enough to upgrade the local convenience stores or tech hubs.
*   **Complete Urban Requests:** These side missions often reward "Console Tuning Kits," which are necessary for rerolling bad sub-stats on your endgame gear.

### Beginner Team Building: The Starter Core

A highly effective F2P (Free-to-Play) rotation for the early game is the **Zero-Aurelia-Chiz** core. 

1.  Start with **Zero** to apply Cosmos pressure and build meter.
2.  Swap to **Aurelia** once the meter is full to trigger **Blossom**, which deals AoE damage and keeps your team healthy.
3.  Swap to **Chiz** to trigger the **undisclosed** reaction, which provides a shield.
4.  Cycle back to **Zero** (Lakshana to Cosmos) to complete the wheel and reset your cooldowns.

This rotation ensures you always have a shield active and a healing field on the ground, making the early-game difficulty spikes much more manageable.

# ZZZ 3.0 How to Unlock Wind Attribute: Neverness to Everness Guide

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/zzz-3-0-how-to-unlock-wind-attribute-nte/inline-2.webp)


Neverness to Everness (NTE) does not have an attribute named "Wind"; the equivalent nature-based element is called Anima, which is represented by a green icon in the character menu and the Esper Wheel. To unlock the Anima attribute for your account, you must obtain an Anima-type Esper character through the Dice recruitment system, which serves as the game's primary gacha mechanic.

Currently, the available Anima characters include Nanally (S-Rank), Jiuyuan (S-Rank), and Mint (A-Rank). While Nanally is often featured on limited banners, Jiuyuan was made more accessible through the Version 1.2 update in June 2026, which introduced a Free 5-Star Selector for active players. Furthermore, players can unlock specific element-themed achievements such as [Wind Walker](https://www.reddit.com) and [Wind Barrier](https://www.reddit.com) by completing exploration feats in the Bridge Crossings district or by parrying high-level V-class Anomalies.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/zzz-3-0-how-to-unlock-wind-attribute-nte/inline-3.webp)


### Understanding the Esper System and Anima (Wind) in NTE

In the world of Hethereau, combat revolves around the Esper System. Unlike traditional RPGs that use standard elemental names, NTE uses supernatural classifications. Anima is the attribute that most closely aligns with "Wind" or "Nature" archetypes. It represents the life force and kinetic energy found in the natural world, often manifesting as emerald-colored swirls and high-speed strikes in combat.

The Anima attribute is vital for players who prefer a fast-paced, mobile playstyle. Characters aligned with this element often possess abilities that allow them to traverse the urban environment more efficiently or crowd-control enemies using vortex-like effects. When you first start the game, you may not have an Anima character immediately available, necessitating the use of the recruitment systems to "unlock" this part of the Esper Wheel.

### How to Unlock Anima Characters: Banners and Selectors

Unlocking the Anima attribute is synonymous with adding an Anima character to your roster. There are three primary methods to achieve this:

1.  **Solid Dice (Standard Recruitment):** You can use Solid Dice on the standard permanent banner. This pool includes Mint, an A-Rank Anima character who is the most common entry point for players looking to utilize this element.
2.  **Fabricated Dice (Limited Recruitment):** For high-tier characters like Nanally, players must use Fabricated Dice on limited-time banners. These banners have a pity system that guarantees an S-Rank character after a set number of pulls, though the specific character depends on the active event.
3.  **Version 1.2 S-Rank Selector:** As of the June 2026 update, a special selector was provided to the community. This allows you to choose one S-Rank character from a curated list, which includes Jiuyuan. This is currently the most direct way to unlock a top-tier Anima DPS without relying on gacha luck.

### Anima Character Profiles: Nanally, Jiuyuan, and Mint

Each Anima character fills a specific niche within a squad. Understanding their roles helps in determining which one to prioritize during the unlocking process.

*   **[Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) (S-Rank):** Known for her extreme mobility and rapid-fire Anima strikes. She is often considered the face of the Anima attribute and excels in clearing large groups of enemies in the neon-lit streets of Hethereau.
*   **Jiuyuan (S-Rank):** A powerful damage dealer who focuses on precision. Jiuyuan is frequently paired with characters who can trigger the Esper Cycle quickly.
*   **Mint (A-Rank):** A versatile support character. While she does not deal as much raw damage as the S-Rank options, Mint is essential for budget-friendly teams. Her effectiveness is greatly boosted when equipped with Arcs like Fluff of Fleetness or Clear Skies.

### Mastering the Anima Esper Cycle (Reactions)

Simply unlocking an Anima character is not enough; you must understand how they interact with other elements on the Esper Wheel. The Esper Cycle is the core mechanic for maximizing damage in NTE.

*   **Blossom (Anima + Cosmos):** When you apply Anima to a target already affected by Cosmos (or vice versa), you trigger Blossom. This reaction creates a nature-based explosion that deals area-of-effect damage and can stagger smaller enemies.
*   **Hexed (Anima + Incantation):** Combining Anima with Incantation results in the Hexed status. This reaction focuses on debuffing the enemy, often reducing their resistance to further Anima damage or slowing their movement speed.

### Unlocking Wind-Related Achievements: Wind Walker & Wind Barrier

NTE rewards players for mastering the movement and defensive capabilities of the Anima attribute through specific trophies.

The **[Wind Walker](https://www.reddit.com)** achievement is a hidden exploration trophy found in the Bridge Crossings district. To unlock it, you must maintain a continuous "airborne" state for a specific duration using Anima abilities or environmental hazards like ventilation fans. Using Nanally makes this significantly easier due to her mid-air dash capabilities.

The **[Wind Barrier](https://www.reddit.com)** achievement is combat-oriented. It requires you to successfully parry a V-class Anomaly's whirlwind attack. This requires precise timing and a character with a high Parry stat. Successfully performing this parry not only unlocks the achievement but also provides a massive opening to counter-attack the Anomaly.

### Enhancing Wind Damage: Arcs, Consoles, and Anomaly Furniture

Once you have unlocked and leveled your Anima Espers, you can further enhance their "Wind" damage through several endgame systems.

**Arcs** act as the primary gear system. For Anima characters, you should look for Arcs that provide flat Anima DMG bonuses. Mint benefits greatly from Fluff of Fleetness, which enhances her support capabilities, while Clear Skies is a solid general-purpose choice for any Anima user.

The **City Tycoon** system offers a unique way to gain account-wide power. By managing your properties and placing specific **Anomaly Furniture**, you can activate passive buffs. High-tier Anomaly Furniture can be tuned to boost Anima DMG for your entire squad, provided your City Tycoon Rank is high enough to support the energy requirements of the furniture.

### Other Esper Attributes in Neverness to Everness

While Anima is the focus for those seeking Wind powers, the Esper Wheel contains five other distinct attributes that are essential for team building:

*   **Lakshana (Physical):** Focuses on raw martial prowess and traditional weaponry.
*   **Cosmos (Light):** Deals with ethereal energy and is the primary trigger for the Blossom reaction.
*   **Incantation (Force):** Utilizes gravity and kinetic force, essential for triggering Hexed.
*   **Chaos (Dark):** Focuses on erosion and damage-over-time effects.
*   **Psyche (Mental):** Deals with crowd control and manipulating the battlefield.

### Best Team Compositions for Anima (Wind) Espers

The most effective way to utilize Anima is through a "Triple-Esper Cycle" team. This involves bringing one Anima character, one Cosmos character, and one Incantation character. By rotating through these three, you can constantly trigger Blossom and Hexed reactions, ensuring that the enemy is always under a debuff or taking burst damage. A popular July 2026 meta team consists of Nanally as the lead, paired with a strong Cosmos support to maximize Blossom uptime.

### NTE vs. ZZZ 3.0: Attribute Comparison

For players transitioning from Zenless Zone Zero to Neverness to Everness, the attribute systems may feel familiar but have distinct differences. In ZZZ, Wind is a straightforward elemental type used to exploit enemy weaknesses in Hollows. In NTE, Anima is part of a more complex supernatural ecosystem where the "Cycle" of switching characters is the primary driver of damage, rather than just matching an element to a weakness. The urban exploration in NTE also integrates Anima more deeply into movement, whereas ZZZ attributes are largely confined to the combat instances.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is there a free Wind character in Neverness to Everness?
While there isn't a character strictly named "Wind," the Anima character Jiuyuan was available for free via the Version 1.2 S-Rank Selector. Additionally, the A-Rank character Mint is frequently obtainable through standard gameplay rewards and early-game Dice pulls.

### How do I trigger the Blossom reaction?
The Blossom reaction is triggered by hitting an enemy with an Anima ability while they are already affected by the Cosmos attribute. This creates a powerful nature-based burst that is effective against groups of enemies.

### Where can I find the Wind Walker achievement?
The [Wind Walker](https://www.reddit.com) achievement is located in the Bridge Crossings district of Hethereau. You must use high-mobility characters or environmental tools to stay in the air for a prolonged period to trigger the unlock.

### What are the best Arcs for Anima characters?
For support-oriented Anima characters like Mint, the best Arcs are Fluff of Fleetness and Clear Skies. Damage-focused characters like Nanally typically perform best with Reality Refuge or other S-Rank Arcs that prioritize Anima DMG and Crit stats.

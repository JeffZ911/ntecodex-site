### Combat Fundamentals: The Esper Cycle and Elemental Synergy

Combat in Hethereau is built around the "Esper Cycle," a rhythmic rotation of your three-person squad. Characters are categorized into three primary roles: Front Runners, Helpers, and Protectors. 

1.  **Front Runners:** These are your primary on-field damage dealers who benefit most from the buffs provided by the rest of the team.
2.  **Helpers:** These characters typically provide off-field elemental application or damage, helping to prime enemies for reactions.
3.  **Protectors:** These Espers focus on survivability, providing shields or healing to keep the team in the fight during high-intensity Anomaly encounters.

The core loop involves using your Helper and Protector abilities to fill the Esper Cycle gauge located near the character icons. Once the gauge is flashing, switching to your Front Runner triggers a powerful "Switch Skill" that deals massive burst damage and applies elemental status effects. ### Daily and Weekly Routine: Optimizing Your Stamina

NTE utilizes a dual stamina system that can be confusing for beginners. 

*   **Character Pixels:** This is your "combat stamina." It regenerates daily and is used to claim rewards from Anomaly Zones, boss fights, and character ascension material domains. Never let this cap out; if you are short on time, use the "Quick Sweep" feature on previously cleared Anomaly Zones.
*   **City Stamina:** This is a "weekly stamina" pool used for urban activities like business management, high-stakes street racing, and certain property interactions. Because this pool is larger and resets weekly, you have more flexibility, but you should still ensure it is fully depleted before the weekly reset to maximize your City Tycoon experience.

Don't forget to visit Nakupa's Pool every day. Interacting with the environment there provides a small but permanent boost to your account's "Urban Resonance," which slightly improves the drop rates of rare materials in the open world.

### The Anomaly System: Hunting and Farming for Arcs

Arcs are the primary equipment for your Espers, functioning as both weapons and stat sticks. To get the best Arcs, you must engage with the Anomaly system.

1.  **Anomaly Commissions:** These are daily missions found on the city's bounty board. They provide a steady stream of experience and Fons.
2.  **Anomaly Zones:** These are instanced combat challenges found throughout Hethereau. They are the primary source for farming specific Arc sets. Each zone has a "Corruption Level"; ensure your team's average level matches or exceeds this to avoid a significant damage penalty.

When farming Arcs, focus on obtaining a full set for your Front Runner first. The set bonuses in NTE are powerful, often providing more utility than raw stats alone. If you find a particularly difficult Anomaly Zone, remember to visit the Witch in the back alley of the Central District; her daily potions can provide elemental resistance buffs that make these encounters much more manageable.

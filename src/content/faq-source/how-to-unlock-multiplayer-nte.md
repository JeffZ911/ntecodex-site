## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/how-to-unlock-multiplayer-nte/inline-1.webp)


Many new players are wondering exactly when and how the multiplayer features become available in Neverness to Everness (NTE). Since its release on April 29, 2026, the game has drawn millions of players to its supernatural urban setting, but the initial hours are a strictly solo affair. This often leads to confusion for those who downloaded the game specifically to engage in street racing or cooperative heists with their friends.

The game is an urban open-world RPG that blends solo narrative with cooperative social and combat elements. Hotta Studio designed the early game to ground players in the mechanics of Hethereau, the main city, and to introduce the complex relationship between humans and Anomalies. Because the story is so central to the progression of the world, certain systems are gated behind narrative milestones to ensure players understand the stakes of the setting before jumping into high-intensity group activities.

Multiplayer is not available immediately upon starting the game; it requires specific story progression and account leveling. You cannot simply open the menu and invite a friend the moment you step out of the initial cutscenes. This guide addresses the specific requirements for unlocking co-op, including the necessary Hunter Level and story milestones you must hit to start seeing other players in your world.

### Is Neverness to Everness a Multiplayer Game?

Neverness to Everness is primarily a single-player open-world RPG with robust multiplayer integrations. It follows a structure similar to other modern "live-service" RPGs where the main story is a personal journey, but the world itself can be shared. Once unlocked, the multiplayer component allows for seamless transition between solo exploration and group play. You can invite friends to your version of Hethereau to tackle world bosses, participate in mini-games, or simply show off your car collection and home decor.

### Why is Multiplayer Locked at the Start?

The decision to lock multiplayer behind the first few chapters of the story serves two purposes. First, it ensures that every player has a basic grasp of the combat system and the "Esper" abilities unique to their characters. Second, it prevents technical issues where players might enter a friend's world and encounter spoilers or "Anomalies" that they haven't yet learned how to counter in their own save file. By the time you reach the unlock criteria, you will have access to your first vehicle and a basic understanding of how to navigate the city's verticality.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-unlock-multiplayer-nte/inline-2.webp)


To unlock multiplayer in Neverness to Everness, you must reach Hunter Level 8 and complete the main story quest "Prologue II: Good Business, Better Treasure". This quest serves as the final tutorial phase for the game's social and economic systems.

This process typically takes between 25 to 35 minutes of active gameplay focusing on the main narrative. If you ignore side distractions and focus entirely on the yellow "Main" quest markers, you can reach this milestone very quickly. Once these conditions are met, the "Multiplayer" app will automatically appear on the second page of your in-game phone menu.

Multiplayer supports up to 4 players in a single session for world exploration, racing, and specific combat challenges. Below is a quick reference for the unlock requirements

Once the Multiplayer app is visible on your phone, you can use it to manage your friend list, search for other players using their unique 9-digit UID, and toggle your world permissions (Public, Friends Only, or Private).

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-unlock-multiplayer-nte/inline-3.webp)


Unlocking the ability to play with others is a straightforward process, but if you spend too much time exploring the back alleys of Hethereau early on, it may take you longer than necessary. Following the main path is the most efficient way to open up the social side of the game.

### Reaching Hunter Level 8 Fast

Hunter Level is your overall account progression rank, separate from the individual levels of your characters (Esper Level). To reach Hunter Level 8 quickly, you should prioritize the following activities

1.  **Main Story Quests:** These provide the largest chunks of Hunter XP. Following the narrative from the opening sequence through the first encounter with the "Antique Shop" staff will get you most of the way to Level 8.
2.  **City Navigation Tutorials:** Early tasks that teach you how to use the "Glider" or how to summon your first car provide small but frequent XP boosts.
3.  **Opening Basic Supply Crates:** As you run between quest objectives, keep an eye out for glowing crates in the environment. Opening these grants a small amount of Hunter XP and essential upgrade materials.
4.  **First-Time Combat Encounters:** Defeating groups of low-level Anomalies for the first time in specific city districts rewards "District Completion" XP, which contributes to your Hunter Level.

Avoid spending too much time on "Anomaly Commissions" (bounties) before unlocking multiplayer, as these are often more difficult to solo and are much more efficient to run once you have a team.

### Completing the Prologue II Milestone

The specific quest "Prologue II: Good Business, Better Treasure" acts as the formal introduction to the business management side of NTE. During this quest, you will interact with the manager of the Antique Shop and learn how to process supernatural artifacts. 

The quest concludes after a scripted combat encounter in a warehouse district. Once you return to the Antique Shop to report your success, a notification will pop up on your screen stating that "Multiplayer Functions are now Online." At this point, your in-game smartphone will receive a forced update, adding the Multiplayer app to your interface.

### How to Invite and Join Friends (UID System)

Neverness to Everness uses a UID (Unique Identifier) system to connect players across different platforms. Your UID is a 9-digit number located in the bottom-right corner of your screen at all times, and it is also visible at the top of your profile menu.

To invite a friend
1.  Open your in-game phone (default key 'Esc' on PC or tapping the phone icon on mobile).
2.  Swipe to the second page and tap the **Multiplayer** app.
3.  Select the **Search** tab.
4.  Enter your friend's 9-digit UID.
5.  Once their profile appears, click **Add Friend** or **Request to Join World**.

If you want to allow friends to join you without manual approval, you can set your "World Permissions" to "Allow Friends" within the same app settings.

### What You Can (and Can't) Do in Co-op

Multiplayer in NTE is quite flexible, but there are certain restrictions to keep the game balanced. 

**Available Activities:**
*   **Street Racing:** You can initiate races at designated starting lines or simply challenge a friend to a sprint across the city.
*   **Pink Paws Mode:** These are cooperative "heist" missions where players must infiltrate secured zones to retrieve Anomaly-tainted items.
*   **Mini-Games:** You can play Mahjong or Tetrominoes with friends at various parlors found throughout Hethereau.
*   **Home Visits:** You can invite players to your purchased apartments or houses to show off your furniture layouts.
*   **World Bosses:** High-level Anomalies that appear in the open world can be tackled by all 4 players simultaneously.

**Restrictions:**
*   **Story Missions:** All "Main" and "Character" story quests are strictly solo. If a friend is in your world and you start a story quest, they will be prompted to leave or wait in the open world while you enter a solo instance.
*   **Anomaly Commissions:** These specific bounty missions are disabled during co-op sessions to prevent "power-leveling" low-level players through high-tier rewards.
*   **NPC Shops:** Some specialized vendors may be unavailable for the guest players while in a host's world.

### The Appraisal Level Scaling System

NTE uses a system called "Appraisal Level" to manage world difficulty. The Appraisal Level is tied to the host's Hunter Level. When a player joins your world, the enemies will scale based on the host's level.

If a Hunter Level 50 player joins a Hunter Level 10 player, the enemies will remain at Level 10. However, the high-level player will have their damage output "normalized" so they don't one-shot every enemy, though they will still feel significantly more powerful. Conversely, if a low-level player joins a high-level world, they may find the enemies extremely difficult to damage. Loot drops are always instanced, meaning you only get rewards appropriate for your own progression level, regardless of whose world you are in.

Once you have unlocked the multiplayer app, there are several other social and technical features you should be aware of to make the most of the cooperative experience in Hethereau.

### Cross-Platform Play and Region Locking

Neverness to Everness supports full cross-platform play. A player on PlayStation 5 can seamlessly join a friend on an iPhone or a PC. Your progress is also cross-save compatible, provided you use the same official account login across all devices.

However, the game is strictly region-locked. If you are playing on the North America (NA) server, you cannot join a friend who is on the Europe (EU) or Asia servers. There is currently no way to transfer a character between regions, so ensure you and your friends all select the same server before starting your journey.

### Social Features: Housing and Antique Shop Visits

The "My House" system is a major component of the NTE endgame. Once you purchase a property in Hethereau, you can customize it with furniture earned from quests or purchased with in-game currency. In multiplayer, your house acts as a private social hub. Guests can interact with certain furniture items, use your crafting benches, or simply hang out between missions.

Similarly, the Antique Shop serves as a public-facing social space where you can often see other players (even those not in your immediate party) if you are in "Online Mode."

### Troubleshooting: Why Can't I Join My Friend?

If you meet the Hunter Level 8 requirement but still cannot join a friend, check the following common issues

1.  **Region Mismatch:** As mentioned, you must be on the same server.
2.  **Update Version:** Ensure both players have the latest game patch installed.
3.  **Wanted Level Freezing:** If the host has a high "Wanted Level" (indicated by the police sirens on the HUD), the multiplayer join function is temporarily frozen. The host must lose their Wanted Level by hiding or escaping the authorities before anyone can join their session.
4.  **Quest Locking:** If the host is currently in the middle of a cutscene or a solo-only story objective, the "Join" button will be greyed out.
5.  **Hunter Level Discrepancy:** While there is no hard cap on joining higher-level friends, some specific "Pink Paws Mode" heists require all participants to be at least Hunter Level 20.

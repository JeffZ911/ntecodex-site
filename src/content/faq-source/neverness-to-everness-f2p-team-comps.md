The combat system in Neverness to Everness revolves around the **Esper Cycle**, a hexagonal elemental wheel. To maximize damage, players must understand how these elements interact when applied to a target in sequence.

### Understanding the Esper Cycle for F2P Players

The Esper Cycle consists of six elements: **Cosmos, Anima, Incantation, Chaos, Psyche, and Lakshana**. Reactions occur when you combine two elements that are either adjacent or directly opposite on the wheel.

*   **Blossom (Cosmos + Anima):** Creates an energy field that pulls in light enemies and deals continuous damage.
*   **Scorch (Anima + Incantation):** Inflicts a burning debuff that reduces enemy defense by [information unavailable]%.
*   **Discord (Incantation + Chaos):** Increases Break efficiency, making it easier to stun elite enemies.
*   **Nova (Chaos + Psyche):** Triggers an explosion that deals damage based on the target’s maximum health.
*   **Resonance (Psyche + Lakshana):** Boosts the entire team's Energy Regeneration Rate.
*   **Void (Lakshana + Cosmos):** Slows enemy movement and attack speed.

For F2P players, focusing on **Blossom** and **Discord** is the most efficient path because the free character roster is heavily weighted toward these elements.

### Top 3 F2P Team Archetypes

1.  **The AOE Shredder (Zero / Mint / Aurelia):** This team is designed for clearing "Anomaly Containment" waves. Zero provides the Cosmos base, Mint triggers Blossom, and Aurelia uses her Lakshana abilities to trigger Void, keeping enemies grouped and slowed.

### F2P Resource Management

The most valuable resource for F2P players is **Fabricated Dice**, used for character and weapon pulls. It is recommended to save these exclusively for "Limited" banners like **The Ichi-daime** ([Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)'s banner), as the standard pool characters can eventually be obtained through the S-Class Selector or losing 50/50 pities.

Avoid spending "Stellar Shards" on anything other than **Arc Upgrade Materials**. Arcs are the NTE equivalent of gear, and an A-Rank Arc at level 60 will always outperform a level 1 S-Rank Arc.

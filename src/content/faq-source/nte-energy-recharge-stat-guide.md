# Neverness to Everness (NTE) Energy Recharge Stat Guide

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/nte-energy-recharge-stat-guide/inline-1.webp)


When players first enter the world of Neverness to Everness (NTE), one of the most common questions involves the "Energy Recharge" mechanic found in other urban RPGs. Specifically, players want to know:

*   What is the Energy Recharge stat called in Neverness to Everness (NTE)?
*   How does this stat function compared to other combat resources like the Esper Cycle Meter?
*   Which gear, characters, and elemental reactions help maximize Ultimate (Burst) uptime?
*   Is there a difference between combat energy and the game's daily stamina system?

Navigating these systems is essential because NTE utilizes a dual-resource system. While one resource dictates how often you can swap characters and trigger elemental chain reactions, the other determines when you can unleash your character's most powerful "Q" ability. This guide breaks down the "Charge Efficiency" stat to ensure your team remains combat-ready at all times.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-energy-recharge-stat-guide/inline-2.webp)


In Neverness to Everness, the equivalent of Energy Recharge is the **Charge Efficiency** stat. This stat acts as a percentage multiplier for the amount of Ultimate Energy (also referred to as Burst Energy) a character gains from various combat actions.

Charge Efficiency increases the energy generated from basic attacks, combat skills, successful parries, and specific elemental reactions. It is important to distinguish this from **Cycle Rate**, which is the stat governing the fill speed of the **Esper Cycle Meter**. While the Esper Cycle Meter allows for character swapping and "Esper Swap" skills, Charge Efficiency is dedicated solely to your Ultimate ability uptime.

The primary methods to boost Charge Efficiency include:
*   **Cartridge Sets:** Equipping the **Speedy Hedgehog** set.
*   **Arcs:** Equipping specific weapons (Arcs) that feature Charge Efficiency as a secondary attribute.
*   **Elemental Reactions:** Utilizing the **Charge** trio reaction, which consists of the Lakshana, Cosmos, and Anima elements.
*   **Character Awakenings:** Unlocking specific nodes in a character’s progression tree, such as Zero’s 2nd Awakening or Adler’s 5th Awakening.

Note that this guide focuses strictly on the combat stat used for Ultimate abilities. It does not pertain to "City Stamina" (used for world traversal/sprinting) or "Character Pixels" (the daily resin/stamina system used to claim rewards).

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-energy-recharge-stat-guide/inline-3.webp)


The combat loop in Neverness to Everness relies on a constant flow of energy. Unlike some games where energy is gained passively over time, NTE requires active engagement to fill the Ultimate gauge. Charge Efficiency serves as the engine that determines how quickly that gauge fills.

### Charge Efficiency vs. Cycle Rate: Knowing the Difference

It is easy to confuse Charge Efficiency with Cycle Rate, but they serve two distinct pillars of the NTE combat system. 

**Charge Efficiency** is a vertical resource. It builds up to a single, high-impact moment: the Ultimate. If your Charge Efficiency is too low, your rotation will "hang," leaving your DPS characters waiting for their cooldowns to align with their energy requirements.

**Cycle Rate**, on the other hand, is a horizontal resource. It dictates the "Esper Cycle," the circular meter in the center of the UI. When this meter is full, you can swap to another character to trigger an Esper Skill. While a successful Parry instantly fills the Esper Cycle Meter, it only provides a moderate, fixed amount of Ultimate Energy. Therefore, you cannot rely solely on Parrying to fund your Ultimates; you must build Charge Efficiency.

### How to Generate Ultimate Energy in Combat

Every offensive action in NTE generates a base amount of energy. Charge Efficiency then multiplies this base value. The following table outlines the estimated base energy generation for various actions before Charge Efficiency modifiers are applied.

**Table: Energy Generation Values by Combat Action**

| Action | Base Energy Generated (Estimated) | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Basic Attack (Full Combo) | 5 - 8 Energy | Varies by character attack speed. |
| Combat Skill (E) | 10 - 15 Energy | Usually the primary source of active energy. |
| Perfect Parry | 15 Energy | Also fully restores the Esper Cycle Meter. |
| Dodge Counter | 5 Energy | Minimal but useful for maintaining flow. |
| Charge Trio Reaction | 8 Energy per tick | Requires specific elemental setup. |

### The 'Charge' Trio Reaction: The Ultimate Energy Battery

The most efficient way to generate energy in NTE is not through individual attacks, but through the **Charge Trio Reaction**. This reaction is the "battery" of the meta, allowing teams to ignore high energy requirements on hungry DPS units.

The Charge reaction is a multi-step elemental process involving three elements: **Lakshana, Cosmos, and Anima**.

1.  **Step 1 (Remora):** Combine Lakshana and Cosmos to apply the **Remora** mark to an enemy. 
2.  **Step 2 (Blossom):** Combine Cosmos and Anima to create a **Blossom** turret or field effect.
3.  **Step 3 (The Charge):** When the Blossom turret hits an enemy marked by Remora, the **Charge** reaction triggers.

Every time this reaction occurs, the active character receives a flat amount of Ultimate Energy. Because Blossom turrets often have high hit frequencies, a single setup can provide a massive influx of energy to your active DPS, such as Chiz or [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte).

### Energy Sharing: How Off-Field Characters Recharge

In Neverness to Everness, energy is not "winner takes all." When the active character generates Ultimate Energy, the rest of the party benefits as well, though at a reduced rate.

*   **Active Character:** Receives 100% of the generated energy.
*   **Off-Field Characters:** Receive approximately 60% of the generated energy.

This sharing mechanic is why "Batteries" (characters designed to generate massive amounts of energy) are so valuable. A character like Mint, who has a very high attack frequency, can stay on the field to generate energy that helps the rest of the team's Ultimates get ready for the next rotation.

### Character-Specific Energy Mechanics and Requirements

Different characters have different "thirst" levels for energy. Understanding these requirements prevents you from over-investing in Charge Efficiency at the expense of damage stats.

*   **Zero (Main Character):** Zero is a versatile unit whose energy economy improves significantly with investment. Upon reaching their **2nd Awakening**, Zero gains a flat energy refund mechanic that makes maintaining Ultimate uptime much easier.
*   **Adler:** Adler is a high-cost Burst character. However, his **5th Awakening** provides a massive boost to his kit: he gains 20 Burst Energy immediately upon casting his Ultimate. This effectively reduces his "real" energy cost for the next rotation.
*   **Mint:** Mint acts as a natural battery. Her combat style involves a high frequency of hits, meaning she triggers the base energy generation more often than slower, heavy hitters.
*   **Chiz:** As a premier Burst-DPS, Chiz requires high uptime on her Ultimate to remain effective. For Chiz, Charge Efficiency is often prioritized as a substat on Cartridges until she hits a specific threshold (usually 140-160%) before focusing entirely on raw ATK and Crit.

### Soft Caps and Diminishing Returns

Is there such a thing as too much Charge Efficiency? Yes. In NTE, most characters find their "sweet spot" between **130% and 160% Charge Efficiency**.

If you go beyond 180%, you may find that your Ultimate is ready long before its cooldown has finished. This is "wasted" stat value that could have been spent on Crit Damage or Elemental DMG bonuses. The goal is to have your Ultimate ready exactly as the cooldown timer hits zero. 

For teams utilizing the **Charge Trio Reaction**, you can often run lower Charge Efficiency (around 120%) because the reaction provides so much flat energy that the percentage multiplier becomes less critical.

### Hunter Level and Appraisal Level Explained

In Neverness to Everness, your individual Espers cannot simply level up indefinitely. Their progress is tethered to your account's standing. The Hunter Level represents your overall account experience. You earn Hunter EXP by completing Main Story Episodes, finishing Side Quests (often found via the in-game phone interface), and spending Character Pixels on various activities.

The Appraisal Level acts as the "World Level" or "Rank." Once your Hunter Level reaches certain thresholds, you will be prompted to undergo an Appraisal Trial. Increasing your Appraisal Level raises the difficulty of enemies in the open world and within instances, but it also improves the quality and quantity of loot. Most importantly, each Appraisal Level increase raises the maximum level your Espers can reach.

### Breakthrough System: Materials and Milestones

When an Esper reaches their current maximum level (e.g., level 20), they enter a "Max" state. To continue leveling, you must perform a Breakthrough. This process requires specific materials dropped by Boss Anomalies found throughout the city or in specialized rifts.

Common Breakthrough materials include:
*   **Nestbound Bird mask fragments:** Required for several Anima-type Espers.
*   **Swallowtail ticket stubs:** Essential for specific high-mobility characters.
*   **undisclosed** for late-game Incantation types.

Each Breakthrough also requires elemental-specific shards, which can be farmed in Houdinii’s Magic Stage or purchased in limited quantities from the specialty shops in the business district.

### Upgrading Esper Abilities and Talents

Leveling the base stats of an Esper is only half the battle. To maximize damage and utility, you must upgrade their individual abilities. Each Esper has a Basic Attack, a Skill, and an Ultimate, along with several passive Talents.

These upgrades require specialized materials called Ability Pigments, which are found in "Houdinii's Schemes" challenges. Unlike character level, ability levels are often capped by the Esper's current Breakthrough phase. For example, you may need to reach Breakthrough Phase 3 before you can upgrade an Ultimate to level 4. Priority should be given to a character's primary damage-dealing skill or their most impactful utility passive.

### The Gear Factor: Arcs, Consoles, and Artifacts

An Esper's level determines their base HP, ATK, and DEF, but their gear provides the scaling necessary for end-game content.

1.  **Arcs:** These are the primary weapons. Like Espers, Arcs must be leveled using Enhancement Pigments and ascended using duplicate copies or specific ascension materials. An Arc at level 50 provides a massive flat ATK boost that scales with the Esper's base stats.
2.  **Consoles:** These act as secondary equipment slots that provide niche stat boosts like Crit Rate, Energy Regeneration, or Elemental Damage Bonus.
3.  **Artifacts:** This is a unique "Tetris-like" grid system. Each Esper has a grid where you must fit different shaped blocks (Artifacts). These blocks provide stat bonuses, and fitting blocks of the same "set" together unlocks powerful Resonance bonuses. Optimizing the grid is essential; sometimes a lower-stat block is better if its shape allows you to complete a set bonus.

### Best Farming Locations: Houdinii's Magic Stage

Efficiency in NTE is defined by how you spend your 240 daily Character Pixels. The most reliable source for leveling materials is Houdinii's Magic Stage. This instance allows you to select the specific resource you need:
*   **Manual Stage:** Drops Hunter's Manuals for character EXP.
*   **Coin Stage:** Drops large quantities of Beetle Coins.
*   **Pigment Stage:** Drops materials for Arcs and Ability upgrades.

As your Appraisal Level increases, the rewards from these stages improve. It is generally advised to only farm the minimum amount of materials needed to progress at lower Appraisal Levels, saving the bulk of your Pixel Potions for when you reach Appraisal Level 4 or higher to maximize the drop rates of high-tier manuals.

### NTE Best Beginner Team Compositions

For players just starting their journey in Hethereau, building a balanced team is more important than chasing high-rarity characters. A standard team consists of a Main DPS, a Sub-DPS/Support, and a Healer or Shielder.

*   **Zero (Main DPS):** A reliable damage dealer given to players early. Zero excels at clearing mobs and scales well with basic ATK artifacts.
*   **Mint (Support/Healer):** Essential for survivability. Mint provides consistent healing and can help trigger elemental reactions.
*   **Chizuru (Sub-DPS):** Offers excellent crowd control and elemental application, making her a staple for many early-game compositions.

Focusing your resources on these three initially will allow you to clear most Appraisal Trials without needing to pull on limited banners immediately.

### How to Increase City Tycoon Level Fast

The City Tycoon system is NTE's unique management and idle income mechanic. By leveling up your Tycoon rank, you earn Fons (a premium-adjacent currency) and can eventually unlock free S-Rank characters like Chiz. 

To level this fast:
1.  Complete all urban delivery quests.
2.  Invest Beetle Coins into high-yield businesses in the downtown area.
3.  Ensure your "Business Reputation" is maintained by clearing nearby Anomalies that threaten your properties.

A higher Tycoon level also provides passive buffs to your Esper's stamina recovery and undisclosed in the open world.

### NTE Element Reaction and Esper Cycle Guide

The combat in Neverness to Everness revolves around the "Esper Cycle." This involves rotating through your team members to trigger elemental reactions between Cosmos, Anima, and Incantation elements.

*   **Anima + Incantation:** Often results in high-area-of-effect (AoE) damage bursts.
*   **Cosmos Reactions:** Usually focused on shredding enemy shields or slowing down fast-moving Anomalies.

Understanding the "Cycle" is critical because triggering a successful reaction often restores a portion of the active Esper's energy, allowing for more frequent Ultimates. When leveling, ensure your team has at least two different elements to take advantage of these mechanics.

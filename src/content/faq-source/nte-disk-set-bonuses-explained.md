## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-disk-set-bonuses-explained/inline-2.webp)


Disk set bonuses, officially referred to as Cartridge Set Effects, are passive buffs that activate when a character equips a specific number of matching gear pieces within their Console. These effects are tiered based on the number of pieces from the same set currently active in the character's loadout. 

The system operates on two primary thresholds:
1.  **The 2-Piece "Epic" Effect:** Provides a foundational stat increase, such as a percentage boost to Attack, Elemental Mastery, or Critical Rate.
2.  **The 4-Piece "Legendary" Effect:** Unlocks a powerful, gameplay-altering passive. This might include cooldown reductions, damage multipliers for specific elemental reactions, or energy restoration for the entire party.

Unlike traditional gear systems in other gacha RPGs, NTE combines "Cartridges" (the core gear pieces) with "Modules" (geometric blocks that fit into a grid). While Cartridges provide the set bonuses, Modules provide supplementary stats and can trigger character-specific passives. A standout feature of the NTE gear system is sub-stat transparency: players can see all four sub-stats on a piece immediately upon acquisition, removing the "hidden RNG" frustration found in similar titles.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-disk-set-bonuses-explained/inline-3.webp)


The gear system in Neverness to Everness is housed entirely within the **Console**. This interface allows you to manage the two distinct types of equipment that define your character's power level.

### Understanding Cartridges vs. Modules

Cartridges are the "Disks" that provide the primary set bonuses. There are six available slots for Cartridges in a character's Console, labeled 1 through 6. Each slot has a dedicated main stat, while the sub-stats are randomized within a specific pool. 

Modules, on the other hand, do not contribute to the 2-piece or 4-piece set bonuses. Instead, they provide "flat" or "percent" stat increases and are used to fill the **Console Grid**. Modules are essential for fine-tuning a character's stats, such as reaching a specific Critical Rate threshold or increasing total Health.

### The Console Grid and Module Shapes

Each character in NTE has a unique Console Grid layout. This grid functions like a Tetris board. Modules come in various shapes and sizes, categorized into three main types:
*   **Type II Modules:** Usually smaller, 2-block shapes (horizontal or vertical).
*   **Type III Modules:** L-shaped or T-shaped 3-block configurations.
*   **Type IV Modules:** Large 4-block squares or long "I" shapes.

To maximize a character's stats, you must fit these Modules into the grid without overlapping. If a grid is not completely filled, you are leaving potential stats on the table. Conversely, certain high-rarity Modules offer such massive stat boosts that you might prioritize them even if they leave a few gaps in the grid.

### Character-Specific Module Type Bonuses

A unique layer of the NTE gear system is that certain characters receive "Resonance" bonuses based on the *type* of Modules equipped, regardless of the Module's individual stats.

### Farming Guide: Anomaly Zones and Rewind System

To obtain high-tier Cartridges, players must head to the **"Rabbit Hole" Anomaly Zone** located in the New Herland District. This zone features rotating daily rewards, meaning specific sets are only farmable on certain days of the week.

### Sub-stat Optimization and Leveling

NTE's gear leveling system is highly transparent. When you find a Cartridge, all four sub-stats are already visible. You do not need to "roll" to see what the stats are. However, you do need to level the Cartridge to enhance those stats.
*   **Level Caps:** Cartridges can be leveled up to +20.
*   **Stat Enhancements:** Every 5 levels (+5, +10, +15, +20), one of the four sub-stats is randomly selected for a significant increase.
*   **Resource:** Leveling requires "Data Fragments," which are obtained by dismantling unwanted Cartridges or clearing training simulations.

Weapons, known as Arcs, often have passives that trigger based on the same conditions as Cartridge sets. For example, the **"Silent Garden"** Arc increases damage against enemies affected by "Stain." Set bonuses are the primary way to scale the damage of elemental reactions:
*   **Nova (Ice + Fire):** Benefit most from the "Fireflies and the Forest" set due to the high Elemental Mastery requirements.

When managing your **Character Pixels** (stamina), prioritize your Main DPS first. A single character with a +20 "Lost Radiance" set can carry a team through mid-game content even if the supports are wearing unranked gear. 

Once your DPS is stabilized, move to your supports. The **"Speedy Hedgehog"** set is the universal "Best-in-Slot" for almost every support character in NTE. Its ability to buff the incoming DPS character's Attack by 15% creates a "burst window" that is essential for timed challenges.

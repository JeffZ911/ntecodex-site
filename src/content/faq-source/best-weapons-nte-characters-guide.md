### Understanding Arcs: Rarity and Type Synergies

In Neverness to Everness, weapons are referred to as Arcs. These are not traditional swords or guns but rather "Anomalous Arcs" that resonate with an Esper's abilities. They are divided into three rarity tiers: S-rank (Signature/Meta), A-rank (Versatile/F2P), and B-rank (Starter). 

The Arc Type system is the most important factor in character building. There are five distinct types:
*   **Plasma:** Focused on raw offensive power and burst damage.
*   **Solid:** Enhances durability, HP scaling, and Break Intensity.
*   **Gas:** Provides utility, elemental application, and support buffs.
*   **Liquid:** Focuses on energy regeneration and skill cooldown reduction.
*   **Synthesis:** Offers hybrid or conditional buffs that often require specific playstyles to trigger.

Equipping an Arc of the same type as the character’s natural affinity often unlocks hidden passive stats, though some hybrid builds allow for mixing types to fill gaps in a team's composition.

### Refinement and Leveling: How to Maximize Arc Potential

To maximize an Arc, you must focus on two systems: Leveling and Refinement.
*   **Leveling:** Uses "Arc EXP" materials. Every 20 levels, you will need "Ascension Materials" gathered from specific urban dungeons.
*   **Refinement:** If you obtain duplicate copies of an Arc, you can "Refine" them to increase the potency of the Arc Skill. For example, refining "Ready-Ready" increases the CRIT bonus from its base level to a much higher cap. For F2P Arcs like "Day Off," maxing out refinement is highly recommended as a Refinement 5 (R5) A-rank can often outperform a Refinement 1 (R1) S-rank.

### How to Farm Fons and Upgrade Materials

Fons is the primary currency used for upgrading Arcs. You can farm Fons by completing "Urban Commissions" found at the various kiosks around the city. For Arc-specific materials, you must use Tri-Keys to enter the "Arc Research Program" domains. These domains rotate daily, so it is important to check the schedule to see when the materials for Plasma or Solid types are available.

Understanding the "Esper Cycle" is also crucial for weapon selection. Some weapons provide buffs that only trigger when an elemental reaction occurs. If your team focuses on "Vapor" or "Electric" reactions, ensure your Arcs have passives that reward high Elemental Mastery or faster reaction internal cooldowns (ICD).

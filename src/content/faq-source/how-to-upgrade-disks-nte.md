## Question Restated

Players frequently ask how to maximize the potential of their equipment in Neverness to Everness (NTE). The most common points of confusion involve:

*   How do I increase the level and power of Disks (Arcs) in Neverness to Everness?

Understanding the distinction between Arcs (the weapons often referred to as Disks by the community) and the Console system (which houses Cartridges and Modules) is the first step toward building a powerful team.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-upgrade-disks-nte/inline-2.webp)


In Neverness to Everness, "Disks" primarily refers to Arcs, which serve as the weapon system for your characters. To upgrade them, navigate to the **Arc menu** within the character screen and use **Dye** (also known as Pigments) and **Beetle Coins**. 

To upgrade your gear (Cartridges and Modules), you must access the **Console tab** in the Character menu. Upgrading these components requires **Manhole** EXP materials or the consumption of lower-rarity gear. 

Key materials for character progression include:
*   **Dye / Pigments:** Used exclusively for leveling up Arcs.
*   **Manhole Fragments (Thug, Crook, Boss):** Used for leveling up Cartridges and Modules.
*   **Beetle Coins:** The universal currency required for almost every enhancement process.

Upgrading involves three main paths: **Enhancement** (increasing the base level), **Ascension** (breaking the level cap at specific intervals), and **Mixing** (consuming duplicate Arcs to refine passive abilities).

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-upgrade-disks-nte/inline-3.webp)


The equipment ecosystem in Neverness to Everness is split between the primary weapon (Arc) and the modular gear board (Console). While Arcs provide the bulk of your base ATK and a unique passive skill, the Console allows for fine-tuning stats like CRIT Rate, CRIT DMG, and elemental bonuses through a Tetris-like placement system.

### Upgrading Arcs (Disks): Enhancement and Ascension

Arcs are the most straightforward part of your build. Every character has a dedicated Arc slot, and upgrading it directly boosts their combat effectiveness. Enhancement is the process of feeding EXP materials (Dye) into the Arc to raise its level. As the level increases, the Arc's base ATK and secondary stat (such as CRIT Rate or ATK%) grow.

Once an Arc reaches its current level cap, you must perform an **Ascension**. This process requires specific materials typically farmed from the **Bubble Can Factory**. Ascension milestones occur at levels 20, 30, 40, 60, and 80. Note that your ability to ascend Arcs is tied to your **Hunter Level**; you cannot level a weapon past your account's current threshold.

### The Mixing System: Refining Passive Abilities

If you obtain duplicate copies of an Arc—either through the **Scarborough Fair** gacha or by farming specific world bosses—you can use the **Mixing** system. Mixing functions as the refinement or "awakening" system in NTE. 

By consuming a duplicate, you increase the **Mixing Intensity** (often denoted as T1 through T5, or A1 through A5). Each rank of Mixing significantly boosts the numerical values of the Arc's unique passive ability. For example, [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)'s signature Arc, **Ready-Ready**, sees a substantial jump in its Basic Attack and Critical Riposte damage bonuses at higher Mixing levels.

### Upgrading the Console: Cartridges and Modules

The Console is a handheld device unique to each character that features a grid. You place **Cartridges** (large base pieces) and **Modules** (smaller Tetris-like shapes) onto this grid to activate set bonuses and stat buffs.

Upgrading these pieces is essential for endgame viability. Both Cartridges and Modules can be leveled up to **+20**. Unlike Arcs, they use **Manhole** materials for EXP.

*   **Cartridges:** These determine your set bonuses (2-piece and 4-piece). Leveling a Cartridge primarily boosts its Main Stat.
*   **Modules:** These are filler pieces that provide Flat ATK and Flat HP as main stats, but their true value lies in their substats and their ability to trigger Cartridge set effects.

### Understanding Substat Activation and Fixed Values

One of the most player-friendly aspects of the NTE gear system is **Substat Transparency**. When you acquire a Cartridge or Module, all four substats are visible immediately. You do not need to level the item to see what stats you are working with.

However, these substats are not active at level 1. They "switch on" at specific level intervals:
*   **Level +5:** First substat activates.
*   **Level +10:** Second substat activates.
*   **Level +15:** Third substat activates.
*   **Level +20:** Fourth substat activates.

Because you can see the stats upfront, you can decide whether a piece is worth the Manhole material investment before spending any resources.

### Where to Farm Upgrade Materials

Efficient farming is the key to maintaining a strong roster. You spend **Character Pixels** (stamina) to claim rewards from various Anomaly Zones.

1.  **Houdinii's Magic Stage:** This is your primary source for Arc EXP (Dye) and character EXP (Hunter Guides).
2.  **Bubble Can Factory:** This zone drops Arc Ascension materials. Different days of the week or different stages provide specific items like **Silver Appleseed**, **Golden Appleseed**, **Harmony**, **Beaty**, and **Versey**.
3.  **Rabbit Hole (New Herland District):** This is the most important zone for gear. It drops **Cartridges**, **Manhole EXP materials**, and **Carrota Coins**.
4.  **Rewind System:** Located in the bottom-left of the Console menu, this is a gacha system for Modules. You spend the Carrota Coins earned in the Rabbit Hole to "pull" for new Module shapes and rarities.

### Best Early Game Disks (Arcs)

For players just starting their journey in Hethereau, certain Arcs provide much higher value per upgrade:

*   **Ready-Ready (S-Rank):** The best-in-slot choice for Nanally. It provides a permanent 15% ATK boost and increases Basic Attack damage after using an Ultimate.
*   **Fluff of Fleetness (A-Rank):** An exceptional choice for Mint. Even at Mixing Intensity T1, it provides a stacking ATK buff every second Mint is on the field.
*   **Aurora (A-Rank):** A solid free-to-play alternative for Anima-type DPS characters, focusing on Basic Attack damage.
*   **Clear Skies (B-Rank):** A reliable early-game "stat stick" for Mint that boosts Anima damage.

### Efficient Stamina Management for Upgrades

Your **Character Pixels** cap at 240 and regenerate over time. To avoid wasting resources:
*   Prioritize **Anomaly Pilgrimage** (weekly bosses) first, as these provide rare materials for Esper Ability levels.
*   Farm the **Bubble Can Factory** only when you are close to a level cap (e.g., reaching Level 20 or 40).
*   Spend the majority of your daily stamina in the **Rabbit Hole** once your Arcs are capped. This allows you to accumulate Carrota for the Rewind system while hunting for better Cartridges.

### Console Optimization Tips

The "Tetris" placement strategy is what separates average builds from elite ones. 
*   **Cover Every Cell:** Empty cells on the Console grid provide zero stats. Use Type II Modules to fill small gaps.
*   **Rotate for Fit:** You can rotate Modules and Cartridges. Always try to fit the highest-rarity pieces (S-Rank Orange) first, then fill the remaining gaps with lower-rarity pieces that have good substats.
*   **Match Shapes to Cartridges:** Each Cartridge set (like **Fireflies and the Forest**) requires specific Module shapes to be present on the board to activate its 4-piece bonus. If the required pattern isn't lit up, the set bonus is inactive.
*   **Check Character Passives:** Always check the text on the Console screen. If a character like Mint rewards you for using Type III Modules, try to prioritize those shapes even if it makes the "puzzle" slightly harder to solve.

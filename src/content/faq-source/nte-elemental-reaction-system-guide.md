What is the elemental reaction system in Neverness to Everness (NTE)? Unlike traditional action RPGs where elements simply apply status effects, NTE utilizes a momentum-based system known as the Esper Cycle. This mechanic dictates how different elements interact during combat in Hethereau, requiring players to build specific team compositions that follow the "Esper Wheel."

How do different elements interact during combat? In NTE, elements do not react randomly. Instead, they follow a hexagonal path. A reaction can only occur when you swap between characters whose elements are neighbors on this wheel. This creates a rhythmic "cycle" of combat where your rotation order is just as important as your character's individual power.

What are the specific requirements to trigger an Esper Cycle reaction? To initiate a reaction, a character must first fill their Esper Meter. Once the meter is full, the character's portrait on the right side of the screen will glow. Swapping into that character while the previous character's element is still "active" on the field or the enemy triggers the reaction, resulting in massive damage, buffs, or crowd control.

### Understanding the Core Combat Loop
The combat in Neverness to Everness is built around the concept of "Momentum and Release." You spend the first phase of an encounter using Normal Attacks, Skills, and Parries to generate Esper Energy. Once the Esper Meter is topped off, you "release" that energy by switching characters. If the incoming character's element is compatible with the outgoing character's element, an Esper Cycle is completed. This system discourages "staying" on a single DPS character for too long, as the highest damage numbers are locked behind these elemental transitions.

### The Role of Elements in NTE
Elements in NTE are not just flavor; they define the "frequency" of the Esper a character uses. These elements are categorized by their color and their position on the Esper Wheel. Because the game focuses on urban supernatural themes, these elements represent different manifestations of psychic and physical energy found within the city of Hethereau.

Reactions are categorized into Duo Cycles (two elements) and Trio Cycles (three elements from the same trio group). Duo Cycles provide immediate combat effects, while Trio Cycles like "Charge" and "Discord" offer powerful team-wide buffs or massive stagger damage.

To trigger these reactions, you must manage your Esper Meter efficiently. Your "Cycle Rate" is a secondary stat found on gear and Arcs that multiplies the amount of energy gained from all actions.

While less organized than a Blossom team, Soup teams can be incredibly versatile in the open world of Hethereau, as they aren't crippled if one element is resisted by an Anomaly.

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/nte-character-leveling-guide/inline-1.webp)


What is the process for increasing character levels in Neverness to Everness (NTE)?

The progression of Espers in NTE is a multi-layered system that involves direct experience point (XP) injection, currency management, and account-wide milestones. Unlike traditional RPGs where characters might gain significant experience from open-world combat alone, NTE focuses on the consumption of specific items to facilitate growth. To effectively raise an Esper's level, players must understand how to acquire and use Hunter Guides while managing their Beetle Coin reserves.

Which materials are required for Esper progression?

The primary materials required for leveling are Hunter Guides, which come in three distinct tiers of XP value. Beyond raw levels, the Ascension system—often referred to as the Breakthrough system—requires specialized materials dropped from world bosses and specific Anomaly Zones. These materials are essential for increasing the level ceiling of a character once they hit a temporary cap.

How do account-wide systems like Hunter Level and Appraisal Level affect character caps?

Character progression is not an isolated mechanic. It is strictly governed by the player's Hunter Level (the overall account level) and the resulting Appraisal Level. The Appraisal Level acts as a world tier system; as it increases, the maximum level to which an Esper can be raised also increases. Players cannot power-level a single character to the maximum level immediately upon starting the game, as they must first raise their Hunter Level to unlock higher Appraisal tiers.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-character-leveling-guide/inline-2.webp)


In Neverness to Everness, characters (Espers) are leveled up by consuming Hunter Guides and Beetle Coins within the character management menu. This process is the most efficient way to increase base stats such as HP, Attack, and Defense. However, leveling is not a continuous path; it is restricted by level caps that occur at regular intervals, typically every 10 to 20 levels. To bypass these caps, players must utilize the Ascension (Breakthrough) system, which requires specific boss materials and regional items.

The maximum level any Esper can reach at a given time is strictly gated by your Appraisal Level. This level is a reflection of your account-wide Hunter Level. For example, at Appraisal Level 1, your Espers are capped at Level 20. To raise them further, you must increase your Hunter Level through gameplay, which in turn allows you to perform an Appraisal Level trial to raise the world difficulty and the character level ceiling.

To sustain this progression, players must farm leveling materials from Houdinii's Magic Stage and various Anomaly Zones. These activities consume Character Pixels, the game's stamina resource. Without a steady supply of Hunter Guides from these stages and Beetle Coins from the economy, character growth will stall.

Character progression in NTE is a cycle of farming, spending, and account-leveling. Because resources are finite and tied to a regenerating stamina system, understanding the specifics of each material and gate is vital for efficient play.

### Hunter Guides and XP Materials

There are three primary tiers of Hunter Guides used to grant XP to Espers. Each guide requires a corresponding amount of Beetle Coins to activate.

| Item Name | XP Value | Rarity | Recommended Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Hunter Guide: Rising | +1,000 XP | Green (Common) | Early game (Levels 1-20) |
| Hunter Guide: Senior | +5,000 XP | Blue (Rare) | Mid game (Levels 20-50) |
| Hunter Guide: Elite | +20,000 XP | Purple (Epic) | Late game (Levels 60+) |

While you can use any guide at any level, the Beetle Coin cost scales with the amount of XP provided. It is generally more efficient to use higher-tier guides in the late game to reduce the number of clicks and menu management required. Beetle Coins are the universal currency for these transactions, and the cost of reaching Level 80 or 100 can run into the millions, making currency management just as important as XP material farming.

### The Ascension (Breakthrough) System

Once an Esper reaches their current maximum level (e.g., Level 20), they cannot gain further XP until they undergo Ascension. This process "breaks" the level cap and unlocks the next bracket of levels.

To perform an Ascension, you need
1. **Anomaly Boss Drops:** Each Esper requires a specific material dropped by bosses found in Anomaly Zones. For example, a Fire-type Esper might require [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-15] from a specific urban anomaly boss.
2. **Regional Specialty Items:** These are collectible items found in the open world of Hesperia, such as specific plants or mechanical parts found in districts like the Mistville area.
3. **Beetle Coins:** A significant amount of currency is required for each Ascension tier.

The first Ascension tier typically unlocks at Hunter Rank 10. Failing to ascend your main team will result in "wasted" XP from combat and missions, as the characters will remain stuck at their current cap.

The Appraisal Level is the most significant bottleneck for character progression. It ensures that players do not outpace the intended difficulty of the story content.

This trial tests your current team's strength and, upon completion, raises the level of all enemies in the world while improving the quality of loot dropped.

### Where to Farm: Houdinii's Magic Stage and Anomaly Zones

To get the materials listed above, you must spend Character Pixels (stamina).

*   **Houdinii's Magic Stage:** This is the primary source for Hunter Guides. It is a wave-based combat challenge where the rewards scale based on your Appraisal Level. At higher Appraisal Levels, you are guaranteed more Senior and Elite guides per run.
*   **Anomaly Zones:** These function as boss arenas. Each boss has a specific loot table containing the materials needed for Ascension. These zones are scattered across the city, and you can track specific materials via the "Source" button in the character upgrade menu.
*   **Coinicles Anomaly Zone:** If you find yourself low on Beetle Coins, this specific zone is dedicated to currency farming. It is highly recommended to run this during 2x drop events or when you are preparing to level up a new character from scratch.

### Early Game Leveling Strategy and Tips

In the early stages of Neverness to Everness, it is easy to run out of resources by trying to level every character you pull from the gacha. To avoid this, focus on a core team of four Espers. This team should ideally include one main DPS, one healer or shielder, and two support characters that provide elemental synergy.

Prioritize leveling your main DPS to the maximum possible cap allowed by your Appraisal Level first. Supports can generally lag 10 levels behind without significantly impacting your team's performance. This conservation of Hunter Guides and Beetle Coins allows you to quickly pivot if you pull a top-tier character like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) from the "The Ichi-daime" banner.

Additionally, pay attention to the City Tycoon system. By upgrading your Life Skills with Dreamless Seeds, you can unlock passive ways to acquire materials or discounts on shop items, which indirectly aids your leveling efficiency over the long term.

Leveling your characters is only one part of the power progression in NTE. To maximize your combat effectiveness, you must also consider your equipment and account-wide bonuses.

### NTE Arc Leveling Guide

Arcs are the primary weapons equipped by Espers. Like characters, Arcs have their own levels and caps. They are leveled using materials known as Pigments and Dyes.
*   **Pigments:** Provide the base XP for Arcs.
*   **Dyes:** Used for "Refining" or breaking the level caps of Arcs, similar to the Ascension system for Espers.
Increasing your Arc level is often more cost-effective in the early game than pushing character levels past Level 40, as Arcs provide massive flat stat boosts that benefit from the character's percentage-based scaling.

### How to Increase Hunter Level Fast

Since your Esper levels are gated by your Hunter Level, increasing your account rank is a high priority. The fastest ways to gain Hunter XP include
*   **Daily Anomalies:** These are quick tasks that reset every 24 hours and provide a substantial amount of Hunter XP.
*   **Story Missions:** The main campaign provides the largest chunks of XP but is often level-gated itself.
*   **Exploration Progress:** Finding data logs, opening chests, and activating fast-travel points in Hesperia provides small but consistent amounts of XP that add up over time.

### NTE Currency Farming: Beetle Coins and Fons

While Beetle Coins are used for character and Arc leveling, Fons are the premium currency used for pulls on banners.
*   **Beetle Coins:** Farmed in the Coinicles Anomaly Zone or earned through the City Tycoon business management mini-game.
*   **Fons:** Earned through one-time achievements, story completion, and real-money purchases.
Managing your Beetle Coins is crucial because the cost of leveling scales exponentially. At Level 1, a level-up might cost a few hundred coins, but at Level 70, a single level-up can cost tens of thousands.

By following this structured approach to leveling, you can ensure that your Espers are always prepared for the challenges of the Anomaly Zones and the evolving narrative of Neverness to Everness.

Since its global launch on April 29, 2026, Neverness to Everness (NTE) has captivated players with its blend of supernatural urban combat and deep city management. To stay ahead of the curve in Hethereau City, you must understand how to navigate the dual-leveling system efficiently.

# Neverness to Everness Beginner Leveling Guide: How to Level Up Fast

## How do you level up fast in Neverness to Everness?

![How do you level up fast in Neverness to Everness?](/img/nte-beginner-leveling-guide/inline-1.webp)


Progressing through the early stages of Neverness to Everness requires a strategic approach to two distinct systems: Hunter Level and Esper Level. While your individual characters (Espers) have their own power levels, your Hunter Level acts as the primary gatekeeper for the entire game. If your Hunter Level is too low, you cannot access high-tier Cartridges, advanced City Tycoon features, or the later chapters of the main story.

Most beginners hit a wall around Level 20 or 30 because they focus solely on combat and ignore the passive experience gains available through urban activities. To reach the Level 30 milestone—where efficient Cartridge farming begins—you must diversify your activities. The most effective way to gain Hunter EXP is a combination of completing "Episodes" (main story missions) and depleting your daily Character Pixels in high-yield Anomaly Zones.

### Understanding the Hunter Level System

Your Hunter Level is your overall account rank. It dictates the maximum level your Espers can reach and determines which areas of Hethereau City are open for exploration. Every time you rank up, you receive rewards such as Annulith, Hunter Guides, and Beetle Coins. More importantly, certain Hunter Level milestones trigger "Appraisal Level" increases, which raise the world difficulty and the quality of loot dropped by enemies.

| Hunter Level Milestone | Unlock or System Milestone |
| :--- | :--- |
| Level 10 | Ascension System Unlocked (Break character level caps) |
| Level 18 | Free S-Rank Character: Chiz (via City Tycoon) |
| Level 20 | First Breakthrough Quest; Appraisal Level 2 |
| Level 30 | Access to Mid-tier Cartridge Farming |
| Level 40 | Second Breakthrough Quest; Appraisal Level 4 |
| Level 50 | Third Breakthrough Quest; Appraisal Level 6; Max Tier Cartridges |
| Level 60 | Maximum Hunter Level Cap |

### Why Leveling Speed Matters in Hethereau City

Speeding through the early levels is not just about bragging rights; it is about economic efficiency. Many of the best rewards in NTE are time-gated or tied to weekly resets. For instance, the City Tycoon system provides a steady stream of Fons (the city's primary currency), but your earning potential is limited by your Tycoon Level, which scales with your Hunter Level. By reaching Level 30 quickly, you ensure that your weekly resource income is maximized as early as possible. Furthermore, high-level Hunter rewards include Solid Dice, which are necessary for pulling top-tier characters like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) from "The Ichi-daime" limited banner.

## The Quick Answer to Leveling in NTE

![The Quick Answer to Leveling in NTE](/img/nte-beginner-leveling-guide/inline-2.webp)


If you are looking for a streamlined path to power, follow this priority list every day. Consistency is the most important factor in bypassing level gates.

*   **Prioritize Main Story "Episode" Missions:** These are your largest sources of experience. A single Episode can award up to 2,000 Hunter EXP.
*   **Burn All 240 Character Pixels Daily:** Spend your stamina on Anomaly Zones. "Houdini’s Magic Stage" is the premier spot for early-game XP materials.
*   **Complete Daily Tasks and the Exploration Guide:** Finishing your daily checklist provides over 1,500 XP per day, which is essential for consistent growth.
*   **Reach Hunter Level 10 Immediately:** Do not stop until you hit Level 10, as this unlocks the Ascension system, allowing you to push your Espers beyond their initial level 20 cap.

### Top 3 Fastest XP Sources

1.  **Main Episodes and Spinoffs:** These provide the highest "XP per minute" ratio. If a story mission is available, do it immediately.
2.  **Anomaly Zones (Spending Character Pixels):** Every 40 Pixels spent translates into a significant chunk of Hunter EXP. Use the "double reward" option (80 Pixels) to save time while gaining the same amount of experience.
3.  **City Tycoon Progression:** Completing business objectives and managing your properties awards Hunter EXP that doesn't cost Character Pixels, making it a "free" way to level up while your stamina regenerates.

### Daily XP Priority Checklist
- [ ] **Daily Tasks:** Clear all 4-5 tasks for the bulk of your daily non-stamina XP.
- [ ] **Character Pixels:** Spend all 240 Pixels (prioritize Houdini’s Magic Stage or Bubble Can Factory).
- [ ] **City Tycoon:** Spend weekly City Stamina on heists, races, or deliveries.
- [ ] **Main Quests:** Progress the story until you hit a level requirement.
- [ ] **Exploration Guide:** Complete any pending phases for one-time XP boosts.

### The Hunter Level 10 Milestone

Reaching Hunter Level 10 is the first major turning point for a new account. Before this point, your Espers are stuck at level 20, making even basic open-world enemies feel like bullet sponges. Once you hit Level 10, the "Ascension" tab opens in the Esper menu. You will need specific materials—often found in the Bubble Can Factory or purchased with Beetle Coins—to ascend. Once ascended, your level cap increases, allowing you to use higher-tier Hunter Guides to boost your stats.

## Detailed Guide to Hunter and Appraisal Levels

![Detailed Guide to Hunter and Appraisal Levels](/img/nte-beginner-leveling-guide/inline-3.webp)


The progression system in Neverness to Everness is a dual-track experience. Understanding how Hunter Level and Appraisal Level interact is vital to avoiding "XP waste."

### Hunter Level vs. Appraisal Level Explained

While Hunter Level represents your time invested, Appraisal Level represents your world’s difficulty and your characters' potential. Think of Appraisal Level as a "World Level." When your Appraisal Level increases, the enemies in Hethereau City become stronger, but they also drop higher-quality upgrade materials and better Cartridges. 

A common mistake is thinking these two levels always move together. While reaching a Hunter Level milestone (like HL 20) makes an Appraisal increase available, it does not always happen automatically. You often have to prove your strength first.

### The Breakthrough Quest System (Levels 20, 40, 50)

At Hunter Levels 20, 40, and 50, your progress will stop until you complete a "Breakthrough Quest." For example, the Level 20 milestone triggers the quest "Hunter Level Limit Ascension: Part One." This usually involves heading to a specific Anomaly Zone—often near Houdini’s Magic Stage—and defeating several waves of level-appropriate enemies.

If you do not complete the Breakthrough Quest, any Hunter XP you earn will overcap. While the game tracks this excess XP, it is often applied at a reduced efficiency (sometimes as low as 10% of the original value) until the breakthrough is finished. Always prioritize these quests the moment they appear in your log to ensure you are gaining experience at 100% efficiency.

### Maximizing Character Pixels and Anomaly Zones

Character Pixels are your primary combat stamina. You have a maximum cap of 240 Pixels, and they regenerate at a rate of 1 Pixel every 6 minutes. This means a full recharge takes exactly 24 hours.

**Stamina Efficiency Table:**
| Activity | Pixel Cost | Primary Reward |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Houdini’s Magic Stage | 40 | Hunter Guides (EXP) & Beetle Coins |
| Bubble Can Factory | 40 | Arc Ascension Materials |
| Anomaly Pilgrimage | 40 | Esper Ability Materials |
| Rabbit Hole | 60 | High-tier Cartridges (Endgame) |

Never let your Pixels sit at the 240 cap. If you are short on time, use the double-spend feature to clear Anomaly Zones in half the runs. Early on, you should spend 90% of your Pixels on Houdini’s Magic Stage. You will need thousands of Hunter Guides to keep your main team of four Espers leveled up.

### Questing Strategy: Episodes, Spinoffs, and Side Quests

The quest log in NTE is divided into several categories. 
*   **Episodes:** These are the main story arcs. They offer the most XP and unlock new city districts.
*   **Spinoffs:** These are character-specific stories. While they offer slightly less XP than Episodes, they often provide "Minds of Shard" for specific characters, which are used for Awakenings.
*   **Side Jobs:** These are smaller tasks found by talking to NPCs in Hethereau. They are great for filling the gap when you are a few hundred XP away from the next Hunter Level.

### The City Tycoon Connection: Leveling Through Business

NTE is unique because it rewards you for being a business mogul. The City Tycoon system uses its own "City Stamina," which resets weekly rather than daily. Spending City Stamina on activities like car racing, the "Pink Paws" bank heists, or managing the "Cafe by Origen" provides Hunter XP.

A major goal for any beginner should be reaching Tycoon Level 18. At this rank, you unlock the S-Rank character Chiz for free. Chiz is a powerful Cosmos-element damage dealer who excels at generating Fons during combat. Reaching Tycoon Level 21 further grants you her signature weapon (Arc), the "Contemplative Cat." 

### Esper Ascension: Breaking the Level 20 Cap

Once you unlock Ascension at Hunter Level 10, you can start using materials to raise the level caps of your team.
*   **Level 20 to 40:** Requires basic materials from the Bubble Can Factory.
*   **Level 40 to 50:** Requires "Dyes" and more advanced drops from Appraisal Level 2+ enemies.
*   **Hunter Guides:** Use Rising Hunter Guides for levels 1-20, Senior Hunter Guides for 20-40, and Elite Hunter Guides for 40-60.

### Farming Spots: Houdini’s Magic Stage and Anomaly Pilgrimages

Houdini’s Magic Stage, located in the northern district of Hethereau, is the most important landmark for a new player. It is a repeatable arena where you fight waves of "Oddities." It is the most reliable source of Beetle Coins—the currency required for every single character and weapon upgrade. 

As you reach higher Appraisal Levels, Houdini’s Stage unlocks more difficult tiers (such as "Kaleidoscope") which drop significantly more materials for the same 40-Pixel cost. Always farm the highest tier you can comfortably clear.

Leveling your account is only the foundation. Once you reach Hunter Level 30 and higher, you must start optimizing your team to handle the increased difficulty of Appraisal Level 3 and 4.

### Advanced Character Building

In the early game, you can get by with raw levels. However, as enemies scale, you must master the "Esper Cycle" combat system. This involves swapping between characters to trigger elemental reactions. For example, using a Cosmos character like Zero to apply a status, then swapping to an Anima character like Mint, can trigger a "Blossom" reaction for massive area-of-effect damage.

**Recommended Early-Game Team:**
*   **Zero (Main DPS):** Your starting S-Rank. High burst damage and easy to play.
*   **Mint (Sub-DPS/Support):** Provides Anima synergy and excellent crowd control.
*   **Adler (Support/Shield):** Essential for survivability in high-level Anomaly Zones.
*   **Flex Slot:** Use Chiz (once unlocked) for extra damage or a healer if you are struggling with survival.

### City Tycoon and Life Skill Progression

Don't forget that your characters have "Life Skills" that are separate from their combat abilities. Some Espers are better at managing the Cafe, while others provide bonuses during car races or heists. Properly assigning your team to city tasks will increase your Tycoon Level faster, which in turn provides more Hunter XP.

Instead, it is converted into Beetle Coins at a ratio of [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-21]. This ensures that late-game players always have a steady supply of currency for upgrading new characters and Arcs.

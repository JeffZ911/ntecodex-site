### Understanding the Gacha Board and Dice Types

Neverness to Everness uses a Monopoly-style board for its summoning system. Instead of a simple animation, your "pulls" move a piece across a board filled with various rewards. Every roll of a Die provides materials, currency fragments, or even minor buffs for your next few rolls.

There are two primary types of Dice:
*   **Solid Dice:** Used for limited-time banners (such as "The Ichi-daime" for [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)). These are purchased with Annulith.
*   **Fabricated Dice:** Used for the standard "Scarborough Fair" banner and beginner banners.

The most critical feature for F2P players is the removal of the 50/50 system. On limited banners, the S-Rank pity is set at 90 pulls. When you land on an S-Rank tile or hit the 90-pull threshold, you are 100% guaranteed to receive the featured character. This removes the risk of "losing" a pity to a standard pool character, making every Solid Die significantly more valuable than limited pulls in competing titles.

When you roll the Dice, you also accumulate **Warp Pieces** and **Lost Pieces**. These function as a safety net; you can spend them in the Exchange Shop to buy specific 4-star Arcs (weapons) or additional Dice. Never spend Warp Pieces on materials that can be farmed with Character Pixels; save them exclusively for Arcs or Dice.

### The F2P Roster: Best Free Espers to Build

While the temptation to reroll for Nanally or [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte) is high, the free roster provided by the story is exceptionally capable of clearing end-game content.

**Zero (Cosmos):** As the protagonist, Zero is a staple for F2P teams. Her Cosmos element allows her to charge the "Esper Meter" faster than other elements. In the Esper Cycle, Zero should be used to initiate combat, quickly building energy before swapping to your main DPS.

**Mint (Nature):** Mint is essential for two reasons. In combat, she triggers the "Blossom" reaction when paired with Zero, which deals sustained area-of-effect damage. Out of combat, Mint is a top-tier staff member for your businesses in City Tycoon. Her "Price Buff" passive increases the Fons generated by "The Cafe by Origen" by [information unavailable]%.

**Chiz (Lightning):** As the free S-Rank character awarded after Chapter 3, Chiz should become the focal point of your resource investment. Her Lightning abilities provide high burst damage, and she scales effectively with F2P-friendly Arcs found in the Warp Piece shop.

### City Tycoon: Maximizing Fons and Passive Income

The City Tycoon system is not just a side activity; it is your primary source of Fons. Fons are required for leveling up Espers, upgrading Arcs, and purchasing vehicles.

Upon reaching Tycoon Level 4, you unlock **The Cafe by Origen**. This is your first major passive income source. To maximize its efficiency:
1.  **Decoration Score:** Use Fons to buy furniture. Higher decoration scores increase the "Comfort" level, which directly boosts the hourly Fon generation rate.
2.  **Staffing:** Assign Espers with business-related passives. Mint is the best early-game choice for the Cafe.
3.  **Weekly Stamina:** You have a 500-unit City Stamina cap that resets every Monday. Use this stamina to complete "Business Orders" or participate in street races. These activities provide a much higher return on investment than simple part-time jobs.

### Stamina Management: Pixels vs. City Stamina

NTE splits your progression into two distinct tracks, each with its own stamina pool.

**Character Pixels (240 Cap):** This stamina regenerates at a rate of 1 per 6 minutes. Use this exclusively for:
*   **Anomaly Suppression:** For Esper ascension materials.
*   **Arc Resonance:** For weapon upgrade materials.
*   **Hunter EXP:** To increase your account level and unlock higher world tiers.

**City Stamina (500 Cap):** This does not regenerate hourly; it is a weekly allowance. If you do not spend it by the weekly reset, it is lost. Prioritize using this on activities that grant "Tycoon EXP" to level up your businesses. High-level businesses eventually produce enough Fons to buy out the weekly material shops, reducing the pressure on your Character Pixels.

### Exploration Tips: The Witch's House and Vehicle Priority

The world of New Herland is vast, and traversing it efficiently is key to finding hidden Annulith.

Early in the game, locate the **Witch's House** in the outskirts of the New Herland District. Here, you can turn in "Anomalous Birds" you find throughout the city. Trading these birds for **Oracle Stones** is vital because these stones increase your maximum stamina for sprinting and climbing. More importantly, collecting enough birds will eventually reveal the locations of nearby collectibles on your mini-map, making it much easier to hunt for gacha currency.

Regarding vehicles, do not waste Fons on mid-tier cars early on. Your first major purchase should be the **Novis ST-X 950** motorcycle from the Regalia dealership, costing 250,000 Fons. Unlike the cars in NTE, which are restricted to paved roads, the Novis ST-X 950 can be summoned in off-road areas and parks. Its superior handling and ability to navigate tight alleys make it the most efficient tool for exploration and reaching "Warp Points" quickly.

### Combat Fundamentals: Mastering the Esper Cycle

F2P players cannot rely on raw stats to brute-force encounters. Instead, you must master the **Esper Cycle**. This combat system rewards you for swapping characters at the precise moment an "Esper Link" is formed.

When you use an Esper’s Skill, a circular gauge fills around their portrait. When full, switching to another Esper triggers a "Link Attack." For F2P teams, the most effective cycle is:
1.  **Zero (Cosmos):** Use her skill to pull enemies together and build energy.
2.  **Mint (Nature):** Swap in to trigger **Blossom**. This creates a field that deals continuous damage to all gathered enemies.
3.  **Chiz (Lightning):** Swap in as the finisher. Lightning interacting with a Blossom field triggers **Overcharge**, dealing massive burst damage and resetting the cooldowns for your support characters.

### Daily vs. Weekly F2P Priority Checklist

**Daily Tasks:**
*   Log in to collect passive Fons from the Cafe by Origen.
*   Spend 240 Character Pixels on Anomaly Suppression or Hunter EXP.
*   Complete "Daily Commissions" for 60 Annulith.
*   Check the "Wandering Merchant" in the New Herland District for discounted upgrade materials.

**Weekly Tasks:**
*   Spend all 500 City Stamina on Business Management or Racing.
*   Clear the "Deep Anomaly" (NTE’s version of a weekly tower) for Solid Dice fragments.
*   Purchase the discounted Fabricated Dice from the Warp Piece shop.
*   Refurnish your Cafe to maintain the maximum "Comfort" bonus for Fon generation.

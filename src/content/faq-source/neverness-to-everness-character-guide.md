### Defining Espers and Their Role in Hethereau

In Neverness to Everness, characters are known as Espers—individuals capable of manifesting supernatural abilities to combat anomalies. Unlike traditional RPGs where a single character might carry a team, NTE emphasizes a tag-team combat style. Each Esper is defined by their Element (such as Lakshana or Cosmos) and their Arc compatibility, which dictates the types of weapons or equipment they can utilize. Their roles are generally split into Main DPS, Sub-DPS, and Support, though the game’s fluid combat allows for significant overlap.

### The Importance of Character Synergy

Synergy is the core of NTE’s combat. A team of three S-Class damage dealers will often underperform compared to a well-balanced team that triggers elemental reactions. Character synergy is determined by how well their abilities interact—for example, a character who can gather enemies together (Crowd Control) paired with a character who deals massive Area of Effect (AoE) damage. Furthermore, the "Esper Cycle" requires players to swap characters frequently to maintain high "Cycle Rate" stats, making switch-in skills and lingering effects (off-field abilities) vital for endgame success.

### Understanding the Rarity and Arc System

Characters are primarily divided into S-Class (5-star equivalent) and A-Class (4-star equivalent). While S-Class characters generally have higher base stats and more complex kits, A-Class gems like Mint and Haniel are essential for free-to-play players due to their ease of acquisition and high utility. 

Every Esper must be equipped with an Arc, the game's weapon system. Arcs are categorized into five types: Plasma, Solid, Gas, Synthesis, and Liquid. Compatibility is strict; an Esper designed for "Solid" Arcs cannot equip a "Liquid" Arc. Matching the correct Arc to an Esper’s kit is the primary way to scale their damage and utility into the endgame.

The depth of Neverness to Everness lies in its elemental system and the way character progression interacts with Hethereau’s urban environment. To master the game, you must look beyond simple damage numbers and understand the underlying "Esper Cycle."

### The Esper Cycle: Mastering Elemental Reactions

Combat in NTE is built around six elements: Lakshana (Order), Cosmos (Light), Anima (Life), Incantation (Force), Chaos (Dark), and Psyche (Mind). When these elements interact, they create powerful reactions that can shred enemy defenses or provide massive buffs to your team.

**Duo Reactions (Two Elements):**
*   **Blossom (Cosmos + Anima):** Creates a field that restores health to the active Esper while dealing periodic damage to nearby enemies.
*   **Hexed (Chaos + Incantation):** Reduces enemy resistance to all elements for a set duration.
*   **Scorch (Incantation + Lakshana):** Triggers an explosion that deals massive burst damage and knocks back smaller enemies.
*   **Echo (Psyche + Cosmos):** undisclosed
*   **Wither (Chaos + Anima):** undisclosed

**Trio Reactions (Three Elements):**
*   **Charge:** Triggered by combining three offensive elements (e.g., Lakshana, Incantation, and Chaos). This drastically increases the active character's ATK and refills a portion of the Esper Meter.
*   **Discord:** Triggered by combining opposing elements. This causes a massive elemental collapse, dealing percentage-based health damage to bosses and elite anomalies.

### S-Class Character Profiles: Nanally, Sakiri, and Jiuyuan

**Nanally (Lakshana / Main DPS)**
Nanally is the face of the "The Ichi-daime" banner and remains the strongest physical-oriented DPS. Her unique mechanic involves her summon, "Underboss," which performs follow-up attacks whenever Nanally uses her Basic Attack or Skill. Her anti-gravity mobility allows her to fight in the air, making her immune to many ground-based anomaly attacks. When her Esper Meter is full, she enters a "High Gear" state, increasing her attack speed by [information unavailable] and converting all damage to Lakshana-type.

**Sakiri (Anima / Support)**
Sakiri is widely considered the best support in the game due to her "Urban Gardening" skill. She can deploy a large zone that pulls enemies toward the center, making them easy targets for AoE attacks. Additionally, Sakiri provides a team-wide ATK buff based on her total Cycle Rate stat. Her Resonance levels further enhance this by allowing her to shred enemy armor when they are trapped in her zone.

**Jiuyuan (Chaos / Main DPS)**
Jiuyuan excels in single-target damage and "Discord" reaction teams. Her kit revolves around stacking "Gloom" marks on enemies. Once an enemy reaches five marks, Jiuyuan can consume them with her Ultimate to deal massive Chaos damage that ignores a percentage of the target's defense. She is currently the go-to choice for endgame boss encounters.

### Character Progression: Resonance and Arc Compatibility

Upgrading a character involves four main pillars: Leveling, Resonance, Arcs, and Modules.

1.  **Leveling:** Uses Fons (the primary currency) and experience materials found during urban exploration.
2.  **Resonance (Awakenings):** This requires duplicate copies of the character. For S-Class characters, Resonance 1 (R1) and Resonance 2 (R2) are usually the biggest power spikes. For Nanally, her R1 increases her follow-up attack damage by [information unavailable].
3.  **Arc Compatibility:** As mentioned, matching the Arc type (Solid, Liquid, etc.) is mandatory. S-Class Arcs provide unique passive effects, such as "Increasing damage after a successful Parry."
4.  **Modules:** These are gear pieces with randomized stats. Players should focus on "Cycle Rate" and "Elemental Mastery" to maximize [reaction damage](https://ntecodex.com/guides/nte-chaos-vs-psyche-reaction-damage-gear-guide).

### Advanced Combat: Parrying and the Cycle Rate Stat

To play NTE at a high level, you must master the defensive mechanics. 
*   **Parrying:** Timing your attack to hit exactly when an enemy strikes will trigger a Parry. This instantly fills a significant portion of your Esper Meter and pauses the "Cycle Cooldown," allowing you to swap characters immediately.
*   **Dodge Counters:** A perfect dodge allows for a follow-up strike that deals increased elemental gauge pressure.
*   **Cycle Rate:** This is a hidden-gem stat. The higher your Cycle Rate, the faster your team can swap and the more frequently you can trigger Duo and Trio reactions. Endgame builds often prioritize Cycle Rate over raw ATK to ensure the "Esper Cycle" never stops.

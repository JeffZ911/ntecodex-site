To maximize your progression in the supernatural urban open world of Hethereau, establishing a consistent routine is mandatory. Unlocking dailies is the first major milestone for any player looking to accumulate Annulith for premium banners or bypass the strict Hunter Level gates that block main story progression.

# How to Unlock Dailies in Neverness to Everness (NTE)

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/how-to-unlock-nte-dailies/inline-1.webp)


Players arriving in Hethereau often find themselves overwhelmed by the sheer scale of the city and the variety of side activities available. However, many core progression systems remain locked behind the initial narrative. Understanding how to access the daily mission structure is the most common hurdle for new Hunters.

Specifically, players frequently ask
*   What are the specific requirements to unlock daily missions in Neverness to Everness?
*   At what point in the main story do dailies become available?
*   Where can players find the daily task menu once it is unlocked?
*   Are there different types of daily activities, such as combat missions versus life simulation tasks?

Unlocking these features is not just about rewards; it is about efficiency. Without dailies, your Hunter Level progression slows significantly, making it difficult to reach the requirements for Chapter 3 and beyond.

### When do you get dailies in NTE?

Dailies are not available immediately upon starting the game. You must first navigate the introductory sequences that establish the "Anomaly" investigation mechanics. Unlike some open-world titles that grant daily tasks after the first ten minutes, NTE requires roughly 90 to 120 minutes of gameplay, depending on how much time you spend exploring Hethereau City early on. Access is granted only after the narrative shifts from a linear tutorial to a more open structure where you are given the freedom to manage your own investigation schedule.

### Why unlocking dailies is a priority

The primary reason to rush the daily unlock is the "Annulith" income. Annulith is the premium currency used to pull on limited-time banners, such as [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)’s "The Ichi-daime" banner. Furthermore, dailies provide the most consistent source of Hunter Level EXP. As you progress, you will encounter "progression gates" where the game prevents you from starting the next story chapter until you reach a specific Hunter Level. For example, Chapter 3 requires Hunter Level 14, and Chapter 4 requires Hunter Level 24. Relying solely on main quests will often leave you several levels short, making the daily EXP boost essential.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-unlock-nte-dailies/inline-2.webp)


Dailies in Neverness to Everness (NTE) are officially unlocked by completing the **"Prologue - Part 2"** main story questline. The specific milestone that triggers the unlock is the completion of the **"Film Anomaly"** quest. This quest serves as the final tutorial for the game's core supernatural investigation mechanics, teaching you how to use your camera and sensors to identify and neutralize urban anomalies.

Once you finish "Film Anomaly," the **Exploration Guide** becomes available. This is your primary hub for all recurring tasks. You can access it via your in-game phone or the HUD. The icon for the Exploration Guide is a stylized "70" or a dial-like icon, representing your progress within the city’s various districts. Within this menu, daily tasks are officially categorized under the **"Participation Tasks"** tab.

### The 'Prologue - Part 2' Milestone

The Prologue of NTE is divided into segments that introduce the cast and the setting of Hethereau. Part 1 focuses on your arrival and basic combat, while Part 2 introduces the "Anomaly" aspect of the world. During "Film Anomaly," you are tasked with investigating a series of strange occurrences involving local media equipment. This quest is unmissable as it is part of the Golden Path (main story). Completing it signals to the game that you are ready to handle the daily responsibilities of a professional Hunter.

### Accessing the Exploration Guide

Once the unlock notification appears, you can open the Exploration Guide at any time. On PC, the default hotkey is typically "J" or accessed through the Escape menu. On mobile and console, you will find the dial icon on the quick-access wheel or the main phone interface. The Participation Tasks tab is the first screen you see, showing a progress bar that tracks your activity for the day. This bar resets every 24 hours, so checking it immediately after logging in is a best practice for any serious player.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-unlock-nte-dailies/inline-3.webp)


The daily loop in Neverness to Everness is more than just a simple checklist. It is integrated into the "Participation" system, which rewards you for engaging with different facets of the game, from high-octane combat to the "Life Sim" elements like spending Fons at local shops or managing your city energy.

### Participation Tasks and Activity Levels

The Participation system uses a 100-Point System. Every daily task you complete awards a specific number of points (usually between 10 and 30). As you accumulate these points, you unlock five reward chests at intervals of 20, 40, 60, 80, and 100 points.

Common Daily Tasks include
*   **Daily Login:** Grants 10 points automatically.
*   **Spend 10,000 Fons:** Spend the local currency at any shop or for gear upgrades.
*   **Consume Character Pixels:** Spend at least 30 Pixels (the game's stamina system) on resource domains or boss fights.
*   **Use Ultimates:** Use a specific number of Ultimate abilities in combat encounters.
*   **Complete a Bounty:** Finish one minor anomaly investigation in the open world.

Reaching 100 points is relatively fast and can usually be accomplished in 15 to 20 minutes of focused play.

*Note: The total daily Annulith yield from these chests is the primary source of free-to-play pulls.*

### Circle Bounty: The Battle Pass Daily Loop

Separate from the Participation Tasks found in the Exploration Guide is the **Circle Bounty** system. This is tied to the seasonal battle pass (the "Circle" system). These tasks are often more specific, such as "Defeat 10 enemies with Shadow-element characters" or "Collect 5 urban materials." Completing these provides a flat 1,000 EXP toward your battle pass level. While they do not provide Annulith directly like Participation Tasks, they are vital for unlocking the high-tier rewards in the seasonal pass, including 4-star weapons and specialized Arcs.

### The 'Hidden' Dailies: Nacupeda’s Pool and the Witch’s House

Beyond the menus, Hethereau City contains "hidden" daily interactions that provide unique benefits not listed in the Exploration Guide.

1.  **Nacupeda’s Pool:** Located in the central district, this fountain allows you to make a "Daily Wish" by tossing in a small amount of Fons. This interaction rewards you with **Mhm! Coins**, a special currency used for cosmetic items and rare furniture.
2.  **The Witch’s House:** Visiting this location once per day allows you to receive a daily reading or a "potion" that provides a 24-hour combat buff (e.g., +5% Physical Damage or +10% Stamina Regeneration). These buffs are particularly useful before attempting the Anomaly Pilgrimage weekly bosses.

### Managing Character Pixels and City Energy

Character Pixels serve as the stamina system in NTE. They regenerate at a rate of **1 Pixel every 6 minutes**, with a maximum cap of [information unavailable]. Spending these Pixels is the most efficient way to complete your Participation Tasks. If you are short on time, simply clearing a "Pixel Domain" for character ascension materials will fulfill both the "Consume Character Pixels" and "Complete Combat Encounter" daily requirements simultaneously.

### Daily Reset Schedule by Region

NTE operates on a global reset schedule. All daily tasks, Participation points, and "Hidden" daily interactions reset at **5:00 AM** based on the server's local time zone.

| Region | Reset Time |
| :--- | :--- |
| **US (North America)** | 5:00 AM EST |
| **EU (Europe)** | 5:00 AM BST / CEST |
| **Asia** | 5:00 AM CST |

### Optimal Daily Routine Checklist

To ensure you never miss a reward, follow this sequence
1.  **Log in** to claim the initial 10 Participation points.
2.  **Visit Nacupeda’s Pool** in Hethereau City for your daily wish and Mhm! Coins.
3.  **Check the Witch’s House** for your daily combat buff.
4.  **Spend Character Pixels** on necessary upgrade materials to hit the 30-Pixel spending task.
5.  **Complete Circle Bounties** to advance your seasonal battle pass.
6.  **Verify 100/100 points** in the Exploration Guide and claim all five chests.

## Related

![Related](/img/how-to-unlock-nte-dailies/inline-4.webp)


Once you have mastered the daily routine, you must look toward the weekly and long-term progression systems that open up as your Hunter Level increases.

### Weekly Reset Activities

While dailies provide the foundation, weekly activities provide the highest density of rare materials. The **Anomaly Pilgrimage** (weekly bosses) and the **Realm of Greed** (a rogue-like challenge mode) reset every Monday at 5:00 AM. These activities are the only way to obtain the materials required for high-level talent upgrades and "Arc" resonance.

### Unlocking Ebisu’s Auction (Chapter 3)

After you complete Chapter 3 ("Deal? Deal!"), you unlock **Ebisu’s Auction**. This is a weekly rotating shop where you can bid on rare items using Fons. Because some of the items are exclusive to the auction, managing your Fons income through dailies becomes even more critical once you reach this stage of the game.

### Hethereau Hobbies and City Stamina

As you increase your **City Tycoon Level**, you unlock "Hethereau Hobbies." These include mini-games like Street Racing, Fishing, and Delivery missions. These activities use "City Stamina," which is separate from the combat-focused "Character Pixels." Engaging in these hobbies daily is the only way to earn specialized currency for car upgrades and apartment decorations.

### Hunter Level Progression Gates

Dailies are the primary tool for overcoming the level gates that stop story progress. If you find yourself unable to start a new chapter, check your Hunter Level.

| Story Milestone | Hunter Level Required |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chapter 2 Conclusion** | Level 8 |
| **Chapter 3 Start** | Level 14 |
| **Chapter 4 Start** | Level 24 |
| **Endgame Domains Unlock** | [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-22] |

By maintaining your daily Participation Tasks, you ensure a steady stream of Annulith and Hunter EXP, allowing you to stay ahead of the level gates and keep your roster prepared for the challenges within Hethereau’s most dangerous Anomalies.

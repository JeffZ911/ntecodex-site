Players often find themselves hitting a progression wall where simple character levels are no longer enough to clear difficult Hollow Zero or Shiyu Defense stages. This leads to several common questions regarding the equipment system:

*   **What are the primary methods for obtaining Disk Drives in Zenless Zone Zero?** The two main avenues are Routine Cleanup missions, which use Battery Charge, and the Bardic Needle music store, which uses Master Copies obtained through dismantling or mission rewards.
*   **At what Inter-Knot Level (IKL) does Disk Drive farming become available?** While the system introduces itself early, efficient farming only begins once you reach specific milestones that unlock higher-tier rewards.
*   **Where can players find the specific stages for farming high-tier equipment?** Farming locations are split between the Scott Outpost and Sixth Street, each serving a different purpose in your gear progression.

### When Do You Unlock Disk Drive Farming?

The ability to equip and farm Disk Drives is not available from the very start of the game. You must progress through the main story until you reach the Chapter 2 Interlude. During this segment, you will unlock the Bardic Needle music store on Sixth Street. Shortly after, the Routine Cleanup feature becomes available at Scott Outpost. 

While you can technically start farming as soon as these unlock (roughly around Inter-Knot Level 26), it is widely considered inefficient to spend significant amounts of Battery Charge on Disk Drives before reaching Inter-Knot Level 35. At IKL 35, S-Rank (Gold) Disk Drives begin to drop. The efficiency increases further at IKL 45, where at least one S-Rank drop is guaranteed per 60 Battery Charge, and peaks at IKL 50, where you have a high chance of receiving two S-Rank disks per run.

### The Difference Between Routine Cleanup and Bardic Needle

Understanding the distinction between these two systems is vital for resource management. Routine Cleanup is your primary "active" farming method. It requires 60 Battery Charge per attempt and pits you against specific bosses or enemy waves. Each stage is tied to two specific Disk Drive sets, making it the best way to target a particular set bonus for a character like Ellen Joe or Zhu Yuan.

Bardic Needle, on the other hand, is a "passive" or "crafting" system. You do not spend Battery Charge here. Instead, you spend Master Copies—a currency gained by dismantling unwanted Disk Drives or completing specific tasks. Bardic Needle allows for high-precision farming, especially at higher store levels where you can eventually choose the specific slot (Partition) or even the main stat of the disk you are creating.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-farm-disk-drives-zzz/inline-3.webp)


Farming Disk Drives is a multi-layered process that evolves as your Inter-Knot Level increases. To build a "perfect" Agent, you must balance your daily stamina usage with the precision crafting tools provided by Elfy at the Bardic Needle.

### Routine Cleanup: Stage Selection and Stamina Costs

Routine Cleanup is accessed via the Compendium or by physically traveling to Scott Outpost. Each stage features a unique set of enemies and an "Environmental Effect" (buff) that usually favors a specific element or combat style. For example, some stages might provide a 50% DMG boost to Ice attacks, making Ice-based Agents like Ellen Joe or Lycaon ideal for clearing them quickly.

Each stage drops two specific sets. For example:
*   **Monster and Stranger:** Drops *Woodpecker Electro* (CRIT) and *Hormone Punk* (ATK).
*   **Hunter and Hound:** Drops *Soul Rock* (DEF) and *Shockstar Disco* (Impact).
*   **The Iron-Hearted:** Drops *Swing Jazz* (Energy Regen) and *Blues and Jazz* (Anomaly).

To maximize efficiency, you should use the Coffee Shop buff. Once per day, drinking a specific coffee at Tin Master’s shop on Sixth Street will grant you a bonus to Disk Drive drops for a specific set. Always check your coffee options before spending Battery Charge at Scott Outpost.

### Bardic Needle: Tuning Levels and Master Copies

As you increase your Inter-Knot Level, you can complete "Store Level-Up" missions for Elfy. These missions are critical because they upgrade the quality of the disks you can "Tune" (craft).

| Bardic Needle Level | Unlock Requirement | Tuning Capabilities |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Level 1-2 | Initial Unlock | Basic Tuning (B/A Rank disks) |
| Level 3 | IKL 35 | Intermediate Tuning (Guaranteed A-Rank, chance for S-Rank) |
| Level 4 | IKL 40 | Advanced Tuning (Guaranteed S-Rank every 5 tunings), Partition Selection |
| Level 5 | IKL 45-50 | Advanced Tuning (Higher S-Rank rates), Main Stat Orientation |

Once you reach Level 4, you can use "Partition Selection." This allows you to spend more Master Copies to ensure the disk you create fits into a specific slot (1 through 6). At Level 5, you can use a **Tuning Calibrator** to choose the exact main stat for a Slot 4, 5, or 6 disk. Because Tuning Calibrators are extremely rare (usually obtained through the Battle Pass or high-level Hollow Zero rewards), they should be saved for rare stats like Elemental DMG Bonus or Energy Regen.

### Understanding Disk Drive Partitions and Stats

Disk Drives are divided into six slots, known as Partitions. Each Partition has a different pool of possible main stats.

| Partition | Fixed Main Stat | Variable Main Stats |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Slot 1** | HP (Flat) | None |
| **Slot 2** | ATK (Flat) | None |
| **Slot 3** | DEF (Flat) | None |
| **Slot 4** | None | ATK%, DEF%, HP%, CRIT Rate, CRIT DMG, Anomaly Proficiency |
| **Slot 5** | None | ATK%, DEF%, HP%, PEN Ratio, Elemental DMG Bonus (Fire, Ice, etc.) |
| **Slot 6** | None | ATK%, DEF%, HP%, Impact, Energy Regen, Anomaly Mastery |

Slots 1, 2, and 3 are the easiest to farm because their main stats never change. Your farming focus should always be on Slots 4, 5, and 6, as these provide the most impactful combat modifiers.

### Farming Efficiency: When to Spend Your Battery Charge

The golden rule of Zenless Zone Zero progression is to avoid "hard farming" Disk Drives until IKL 45. Before this point, your Battery Charge is better spent on:
1.  **Agent Promotion Materials** (Combat Simulation)
2.  **W-Engine Components** (Combat Simulation)
3.  **Skill Chips** (Combat Simulation)

Once your main team of three Agents is fully promoted and their skills are leveled, you can transition into Routine Cleanup. If you farm too early, you will end up with a collection of A-Rank (Purple) disks that will eventually be replaced by S-Rank (Gold) versions, effectively wasting the stamina you could have used for guaranteed character power increases.

### Managing Resources: Plating Agents and Dennies

Upgrading Disk Drives requires **Plating Agents**, which come in three rarities (Basic, Intermediate, and Specialized). These are farmed primarily through Combat Simulation: Disk Development. While you get some Plating Agents from Routine Cleanup, it is rarely enough to max out a full set of S-Rank disks.

Be wary of your **Dennies** (currency). Leveling a single S-Rank Disk Drive from level 1 to 15 costs a significant amount of Dennies. If you are farming for multiple characters at once, you may find your currency reserves depleting faster than your Battery Charge.

### Salvaging Guide: What to Keep vs. What to Dismantle

As your inventory fills up, you must decide which disks to dismantle at the Bardic Needle.

1.  **B-Rank (Blue) Disks:** Dismantle immediately. These are only useful in the first few hours of the game.
2.  **A-Rank (Purple) Disks:** Dismantle once you have a functional S-Rank replacement. They provide Intermediate Master Copies.
3.  **S-Rank (Gold) Disks with "Flat" Stats:** If an S-Rank disk in Slot 4, 5, or 6 has flat HP, ATK, or DEF as its main stat, it is generally considered "trash" and can be dismantled for Advanced Master Copies.
4.  **The "Double CRIT" Rule:** Any disk that rolls both CRIT Rate and CRIT DMG in its sub-stats should be kept, regardless of its set, as it can serve as a powerful "off-set" piece or a placeholder for your DPS characters.

By following this structured approach to farming—prioritizing IKL milestones, using coffee buffs, and being selective with Tuning Calibrators—you will ensure your Agents are equipped to handle the toughest challenges New Eridu has to offer.

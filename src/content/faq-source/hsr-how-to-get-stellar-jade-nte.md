# How to Get Stellar Jade in Neverness to Everness: Annulite Farming Guide

## Question Restated

When players search for "hsr how to get stellar jade" in relation to Neverness to Everness (NTE), they are typically looking for the primary premium currency used to pull for new characters and equipment. Because NTE is an urban open-world RPG developed by Hotta Studio, it does not use the exact "Stellar Jade" terminology found in HoYoverse titles. However, the systems are functionally similar, leading many players to use familiar terms while they learn the ropes of Hethereau.

Clarifying these terms is essential for any "Anomaly Hunter" starting their journey. In the world of Hethereau, the resource you need for the gacha system is known as Annulite. Just as Stellar Jade is the lifeblood of your Warp count in Honkai: Star Rail, Annulite determines how many times you can roll on the Scarborough Fair boards.

### Is Stellar Jade Available in Neverness to Everness?

Technically, Stellar Jade does not exist within the game files of Neverness to Everness. If you see a guide or a player referring to "farming Jade" in NTE, they are using shorthand for farming Annulite. There is no cross-game currency transfer between Honkai: Star Rail and NTE, as they are developed by different companies (HoYoverse and Hotta Studio, respectively).

### Why Terminology Matters in Hethereau

Using the correct names for currencies helps you find the right shops and exchange menus. In NTE, the economy is split between premium gacha resources, city-building funds, and upgrade materials. Confusing Annulite with Fons or Beetle Coins could lead to accidental spending in the wrong department.

## Direct Answer

Stellar Jade is the premium currency of Honkai: Star Rail; in Neverness to Everness, the equivalent currency is called Annulite. This is the primary resource used to purchase Solid Dice (for Limited Banners like "The Ichi-daime") and Fabricated Dice (for the Standard Banner). 

To obtain Annulite, you must participate in the various activities offered within the city of Hethereau, including daily missions, world exploration, and management simulations. The standard exchange rate is 160 Annulite per single Dice roll on the Scarborough Fair gacha board. 

### Quick-Reference: Annulite Exchange Rates and Pull Costs

*   **Single Pull (1 Dice):** 160 Annulite
*   **Multi-Pull (10 Dice):** 1,600 Annulite
*   **Pity Threshold:** 90 Pulls (14,400 Annulite)
*   **Riftcrystal Conversion:** 1 Riftcrystal = 1 Annulite

Annulite is earned through a combination of recurring daily activities, permanent world puzzles, and one-time progression milestones. For players who have just started as of the April 29, 2026 launch, a significant amount of this currency is available through initial celebration events.

## Detailed Explanation

Farming Annulite requires a multi-pronged approach. Unlike some RPGs that limit you to a single source of income, NTE spreads its rewards across combat, social interaction, and business management.

### Daily Income: Circle Bounties and Login Rewards

The most consistent way to build your Annulite reserves is through Circle Bounties. These are daily tasks assigned to Anomaly Hunters that involve clearing minor supernatural disturbances or assisting citizens in Hethereau. Completing your full set of daily Circle Bounties provides a steady stream of Annulite.

In addition to these daily tasks, the game features weekly login milestones. By checking in every day, you can claim additional Annulite and occasionally Fabricated Dice. These rewards refresh every Monday, making consistency the key to a healthy gacha budget.

### World Exploration: Finding Anomalies and Chests

Hethereau is a dense urban environment filled with supernatural phenomena. Investigating these anomalies is the primary method of earning one-time Annulite rewards. While exploring, you will encounter puzzles that require specific character abilities to solve. Completing these world puzzles or opening hidden chests throughout the different districts grants small amounts of Annulite.

Specific areas, such as the back alleys and high-rise rooftops, often hide higher-value anomalies. While there are no "Luxurious" variants of these chests, the density of the city means that a thorough exploration of a single block can often yield enough Annulite for several pulls.

### Progression Rewards: Hunter Levels and City Tycoon

As you complete missions, you increase your Hunter Level. This progression system serves as a long-term roadmap for rewards. Reaching specific Hunter Level milestones grants substantial bonuses.

### Limited-Time Events and Launch Bonuses

For players joining during the launch window of April 29, 2026, the initial rewards are quite generous. Milestone rewards for the global release include over 1,900 Annulite and dozens of free Dice distributed through the in-game mail system and starter login events. These bonuses are designed to help new players reach their first 90-pull pity on the Scarborough Fair board.

### Premium Options: Riftcrystals and the Mining Pass

If you wish to supplement your F2P income, Riftcrystals are the paid-only currency. These are purchased through the in-game store and can be converted to Annulite at a 1:1 ratio. 

The most cost-effective premium option is the Riftcrystal Mining Pass. This monthly subscription provides an immediate injection of Riftcrystals upon purchase and a daily delivery of resources. Notably, the Mining Pass in NTE includes 30,000 Fons daily, which is essential for players focusing on the car customization and city management aspects of the game.

### F2P Strategy: Maximizing Monthly Annulite Yield

To get the most out of your time in Hethereau without spending money, focus on the following priority list:
1.  Complete all Circle Bounties every day.
2.  Clear the weekly login requirements.
3.  Focus on City Tycoon management to reach the Annulite payout thresholds.
4.  Clear every anomaly in a district before moving to the next one to ensure no chests are missed.
5.  Save your Solid Dice exclusively for high-tier characters on the Scarborough Fair limited banners.

## Related

Understanding how to spend your Annulite is just as important as knowing how to farm it. The gacha system in Neverness to Everness has several unique quirks that differentiate it from Honkai: Star Rail.

### Understanding Dice: Solid, Fabricated, and Tri-Keys

There are three primary items used for pulls in NTE. While Annulite can buy the first two, the third is often sourced from specific gameplay challenges.

*   **Solid Dice:** Used for Limited Character Banners (e.g., "The Ichi-daime"). These should be your primary focus for Annulite spending.
*   **Fabricated Dice:** Used for the Standard Banner. These are frequently given out as Hunter Level rewards and should rarely be purchased with Annulite.
*   **Tri-Keys:** These are the currency for the Arc Research Program, which is the NTE equivalent of a weapon or equipment banner. Arcs are essential for maximizing character potential.

### The Scarborough Fair Pity System Explained

The gacha system in NTE is called the Scarborough Fair. One of the most significant differences between NTE and other major gacha titles is the absence of the 50/50 mechanic. In many games, your first "pity" pull only has a 50% chance of being the featured character. In Neverness to Everness, the pity system is more straightforward.

The pity threshold is set at 90 pulls. Once you reach 90 pulls on a limited banner, you are guaranteed a high-tier character. Because the 50/50 mechanic is not present, players can plan their Annulite spending with much higher precision.

### Residual Currencies: Warp Pieces and Lost Pieces

When you pull duplicate characters or items from the Scarborough Fair, you receive residual currencies.
*   **Warp Pieces:** Obtained from high-rarity duplicates. These can be traded in the shop for Solid Dice or specific character upgrade materials.
*   **Lost Pieces:** Obtained from lower-rarity pulls. These are typically exchanged for Fabricated Dice or basic EXP materials.

This system ensures that even "unsuccessful" pulls contribute toward your next guaranteed character. Always check the exchange shop at the end of each month to see if you have enough Warp Pieces to pick up extra Dice before the shop refresh.

### Farming Fons for City Management

While Annulite is for characters, Fons are for the city. Fons are the standard currency used for almost everything else in Hethereau. You will need millions of Fons to engage with the deep car customization system and to upgrade your properties in the City Tycoon mode.

Fons are earned through combat, selling items, and business profits. If you find yourself short on Fons, focusing on your City Tycoon enterprises is the fastest way to recover. High-level management can generate 30,000 Fons or more in a very short period, especially if you have the Riftcrystal Mining Pass active.

### Achievement System and Social Interaction

Finally, do not overlook the social aspect of Hethereau. Interacting with NPCs and completing hidden social achievements provides small but frequent Annulite drops. These "Social Achievements" often involve using the in-game photo mode or participating in city activities like street racing or visiting specific cafes at certain times of the day. While each individual achievement might only grant a small amount of Annulite, the sheer number of them makes it a viable source for F2P players looking to squeeze out a few more pulls.

By combining daily bounties, city management, and thorough exploration, you can maintain a steady flow of Annulite to ensure your roster of Anomaly Hunters remains competitive in the ever-changing landscape of Hethereau.

Understanding how to efficiently increase character level in Neverness to Everness (NTE) is a fundamental skill for any player looking to progress through the main story and tackle high-level Anomaly Hunts. ### Understanding Character Leveling in NTE

In Neverness to Everness, your playable characters are known as Espers. Unlike traditional RPGs where characters might gain significant experience simply by defeating enemies in the open world, NTE relies primarily on a manual enhancement system. While minor EXP gains can occur through exploration and combat, the vast majority of your character's growth comes from consuming specific consumable items known as Hunter Guides. 

The leveling process is not a linear path from 1 to maximum. Instead, it is a stepped progression system where you must periodically "Breakthrough" a level cap to continue advancing. This ensures that character power remains somewhat balanced with your overall account progress, preventing players from funneling all resources into a single character to bypass the game's intended difficulty curve.

### The Importance of Leveling Your Espers

Leveling your Espers is the primary method for increasing their base statistics, specifically Health (HP), Attack (ATK), and Defense (DEF). These base stats serve as the foundation for all other power modifiers. For instance, many Arcs (weapons) and Consoles (gear) provide percentage-based bonuses. A 20% ATK bonus is significantly more impactful on a Level 60 Esper than a Level 20 Esper.

Beyond raw stats, reaching specific level milestones is often a prerequisite for upgrading other systems. You cannot fully upgrade an Esper's Abilities or unlock higher tiers of their Awakening tree if their base level is too low. In the urban supernatural setting of Hethereau, where enemies can quickly overwhelm an under-geared team, maintaining a consistent leveling schedule for your core party is the difference between a successful containment mission and a total party wipe.

### Hunter Level vs. Appraisal Level Gates

It is important to distinguish between your Esper's level and your account's level. In NTE, your account-wide progression is tracked via the **Appraisal Level**. As you complete story missions, side quests, and daily activities, your Appraisal Level increases.

The game uses the Appraisal Level to dictate the world difficulty (World Level) and the maximum level your Espers can reach. If you find that the "Breakthrough" button is locked despite having the materials, check your Appraisal Level. You may need to complete a "Rank Up" trial mission to unlock the next tier of progression for your entire account.

### Best Farming Locations for Leveling Materials

If you run out of Hunter Guides or Beetle Coins, you should head to **Houdinii's Magic Stage**. This is a repeatable combat challenge located within the city that acts as the primary source for growth resources.

*   **For EXP:** Select the "Hunter's Path" challenge within the Magic Stage. Higher difficulty tiers yield more Senior and Elite Hunter Guides.
*   **For Beetle Coins:** Select the "Wealth Accumulation" challenge.
*   **For Breakthrough Materials:** Use the **Exploration Guide** in your main menu. This tool allows you to select a specific material and "Track" it. The game will then place waypoints on your map leading to the specific enemies or Anomaly Hunts that drop that item.

### Leveling Priority: Which Espers to Focus On First

Resources in NTE are finite, especially Beetle Coins during the mid-game (Levels 40-60). You should avoid leveling every character you pull from banners like "The Ichi-daime." Instead, follow this priority:

1.  **Main Damage Dealer (DPS):** This character should always be at the maximum possible level allowed by your Appraisal Level. Their damage scales most heavily with base stats.
2.  **Sub-DPS:** Keep them within 5-10 levels of your main DPS. They provide necessary elemental reactions and off-field damage.
3.  **Support/Healer:** These characters are less dependent on raw stats. You can often leave them at Level 40 or 50 while your main attacker is Level 70, provided they have enough HP to survive a few hits.

### Common Leveling Mistakes to Avoid

*   **Over-leveling Supports Early:** Do not spend Elite Hunter Guides on a healer if your main attacker isn't at the cap. The marginal utility of a few extra HP on a support is lower than the ATK gain on a DPS.
*   **Ignoring the City Tycoon:** The City Tycoon (business management) mechanics provide a passive income of Beetle Coins. If you ignore this system, you will eventually run out of currency for Breakthroughs.
*   **Wasting Pixels on Low-Tier Stages:** Only farm enough materials to reach the next milestone. As soon as you increase your Appraisal Level and unlock a higher tier of Houdinii's Magic Stage, switch to the new tier for better drop efficiency.

Increasing an Esper's base level is only one part of the power equation in Neverness to Everness. To truly master the game's combat, you must also look at parallel progression systems.

### Increasing Your Hunter Level Fast

Your Hunter Level (or Appraisal Level) is the "master key" that unlocks everything else. To increase it quickly, focus on completing the "Daily Errands" and the "Hethereau Guidebook" tasks. These offer massive chunks of account EXP compared to standard mob grinding. Clearing the main story chapters is also mandatory, as certain level caps are hard-locked behind story completion milestones.

### Upgrading Arcs (Weapons) and Consoles

An Esper's level determines their base stats, but their **Arc** (weapon) and **Consoles** (gear/artifacts) provide the multipliers. 
*   **Arcs:** These must be leveled up using "Arc Refinement Materials." Similar to Espers, Arcs have Breakthrough levels every 20 levels.
*   **Consoles:** These function like gear sets. While they don't have "levels" in the same way, they can be enhanced by consuming other Consoles to boost their sub-stats.

### Esper Ability Progression

Once you increase an Esper's level, check their **Abilities** tab. Leveling a character unlocks the ability to upgrade their Active Skills, Ultimate, and Passive Talents. These upgrades often require "Ability Modules" found in specific weekly containment zones. A Level 60 Esper with Level 1 skills will perform significantly worse than a Level 50 Esper with Level 6 skills.

### The Awakening System and Mind Shards

The Awakening system is the NTE version of a "constellation" or "duplicate" system. By obtaining duplicates of a character (such as pulling multiple copies of [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) from "The Ichi-daime" banner), you receive **Mind Shards**. These shards are used to unlock permanent, powerful passive buffs. While this doesn't increase the character's numerical level, it represents a massive jump in combat effectiveness that complements their base level stats.

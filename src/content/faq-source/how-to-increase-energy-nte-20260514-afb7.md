**Character Pixels** serve as the standard stamina for farming Arcs and upgrade materials. The base regeneration rate is 1 Pixel every 6 minutes, totaling 240 Pixels per 24-hour period. You can increase the maximum cap for Character Pixels by purchasing high-end properties like the Eden Apartment for 1.8 million Fons and upgrading specific furniture items, such as the "Dream Weaver's Knot."

**City Stamina** is the resource used for the Tycoon system, including cafe management and business mini-games. This pool does not regenerate daily; instead, it resets fully every Monday at 5:00 AM. To increase your City Stamina cap, you must raise your City Tycoon rank. Key milestones at Level 5, 10, 16, and 23 provide significant expansions to your weekly pool.

**Combat Energy**, often referred to as the Esper Meter, is the resource required for Ultimates. ## Detailed Explanation

The energy systems in Neverness to Everness are designed to reward both long-term investment in the city's infrastructure and high-skill execution in combat. Mastering these systems involves a mix of financial management (Fons), social progression (Tycoon levels), and mechanical proficiency.

### Increasing Character Pixels via Housing

The Character Pixel system is your primary gateway to character power. Because high-level Arcs and breakthrough materials are gated behind Pixel costs, increasing your maximum cap allows for more flexibility in your farming schedule.

The most effective way to increase your Pixel cap is through the Hethereau housing system. Once you have accumulated 1.8 million Fons, you can purchase the Eden Apartment. This luxury residence acts as more than just a social hub; it provides the foundation for several furniture-based buffs.

Key furniture items that contribute to your Pixel cap include:
*   **The Bed:** Placing a high-tier Bed in your apartment provides a flat +20 increase to your maximum Character Pixels.
*   **Cloud Buddy:** This interactive furniture item offers a unique interaction point that, when upgraded, further expands your total stamina storage.
*   **Dream Weaver's Knot:** This is a specialized furniture piece that acts as a focal point for your apartment's "energy flow." Upgrading this item requires materials often found at Ebisu's Auction.

To find the materials needed for these upgrades, you must frequently check Ebisu's Auction. This location sells exclusive upgrade components like the "Blind Mammon" catalyst, which is required to push the Dream Weaver's Knot to higher tiers. Each tier of the Knot increases the efficiency of your housing buffs, ensuring that your Character Pixel cap remains high enough to store more than a full day's worth of regeneration.

### Boosting City Stamina through Tycoon Levels

City Stamina is a distinct resource that limits your weekly economic activities. Unlike Character Pixels, which are meant for combat growth, City Stamina is the lifeblood of the Tycoon system. If you want to maximize your Fon income through cafe management or city deliveries, you need a larger pool of this stamina.

The only way to increase this cap is by earning Tycoon EXP. You gain this experience by successfully completing business tasks, managing your storefronts, and fulfilling city-wide delivery contracts. As you level up your City Tycoon rank, you will hit specific milestones that grant permanent increases to your weekly City Stamina pool.

### Fastest Ways to Recharge Combat Energy

In the heat of battle, your "Esper Meter" determines your offensive pressure. While every character has a passive energy gain, relying on it will lead to slow rotations and lower damage output. To increase the speed at which you gain Combat Energy, you must utilize the game's reaction and defensive systems.

The most efficient method for energy generation is the **"Charge" Trio-Reaction**. This occurs when you cycle through three specific elements in quick succession:
1.  **Lakshana (Electric/Lightning):** Applies a conductive status to the enemy.
2.  **Cosmos (Space/Void):** Compresses the status, creating a gravitational anchor.
3.  **Anima (Spirit/Life):** Triggers the "Charge" effect, which grants a massive burst of Combat Energy to the entire party.

Using characters like [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte) or [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide), who can rapidly apply these elements, allows you to fuel Ultimates much faster than standard attacking. 

Furthermore, defensive play is rewarded with energy. A successful **Parry** does not just negate damage; it often provides an instant full recharge of the active character's energy meter, allowing for an immediate counter-ultimate. Similarly, the **"Red Flash" Critical Dodge** provides a window of slowed time and a significant energy boost. Mastering these timings is essential for high-level combat encounters where your Ultimate uptime defines your success.

### Exploration Stamina and Movement Recovery

Exploration stamina governs your ability to sprint, climb, and dodge in the open world of Hethereau. While this does not use the same pool as Character Pixels or City Stamina, managing it is vital for efficient world navigation.

Different characters have unique interactions with exploration stamina. For example:
*   **[Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte):** Known for her superior wall-climbing mechanics, she consumes less stamina while ascending vertical surfaces, making her ideal for reaching high-altitude Anomalies.
*   **Skia:** Offers horizontal movement advantages, allowing for faster sprinting with lower stamina consumption over long distances.

Currently, there are no permanent upgrades to the exploration stamina bar itself [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-14], but using food buffs or specific character passives can temporarily reduce the consumption rate or increase the recovery speed while standing still.

### Refill Items and Currency

If you run out of energy and cannot wait for the natural regeneration or the weekly reset, NTE provides a few "panic button" options.

*   **Stamina Refill Items:** These are often rewarded through event milestones or the Battle Pass. Each item typically restores 60 Character Pixels. It is generally advised to save these for double-drop events or when a new character banner drops and you need to farm materials immediately.
*   **Annulith Refreshes:** You can spend Annulith (the premium currency) to refill your Character Pixels. The first few refreshes of the day cost 60 Annulith for 60 Pixels. However, the cost increases with each subsequent refresh, making this an expensive option for F2P players.
*   **Butler Upgrades:** In your housing unit, certain Butler NPCs can be assigned to "stamina management" roles. While they do not increase the cap, they can provide small daily gifts that include minor energy restoration items.

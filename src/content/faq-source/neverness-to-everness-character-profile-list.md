# Neverness to Everness Character Profile List: All Playable Espers

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/neverness-to-everness-character-profile-list/inline-1.webp)


Since its global launch on April 29, 2026, Neverness to Everness (NTE) has introduced a complex combat system centered on "Esper Cycles" and "Arc Compatibility." Players often ask: What is the full list of playable characters in Neverness to Everness? Which Espers were available at the global launch? And what are the specific roles, elements, and rarities that define each character profile?

Navigating the roster requires understanding how each character fits into the elemental wheel and which "Arc" (weapon) type they can equip. As of May 15, 2026, the game features 16 playable Espers, ranging from high-damage S-Rank limited units to versatile A-Rank characters given for free through the main story and the City Tycoon management system.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/neverness-to-everness-character-profile-list/inline-2.webp)


Neverness to Everness launched with a roster of 16 playable characters, categorized into S-Rank and A-Rank rarities. Characters are classified by six distinct elements: Cosmos, Chaos, Anima, Psyche, Incantation, and Lakshana. These elements are not just cosmetic; they dictate which "Esper Cycle" reactions you can trigger during combat.

The current roster includes limited-time banner characters like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) and [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte), alongside a standard pool of six S-Rank Espers that players can acquire through the "Scarborough Fair" gacha system. To help new players, the game provides several characters for free through story progression (Zero, Mint, Adler) and launch-specific events or milestones (Chiz, Aurelia).

### Launch Rarity Distribution

The rarity distribution in NTE is designed to ensure players have a functional team regardless of their gacha luck. 
*   **S-Rank Espers (9):** These units possess unique mechanics, such as Nanally’s wall-running or Hotori’s time-stop. They have higher base stats and more complex skill trees (Blossom Reactions).
*   **A-Rank Espers (7):** While they have lower base stats, A-Rank units are essential for filling specific elemental slots in an Esper Cycle. Many are given for free, making them the backbone of free-to-play accounts.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/neverness-to-everness-character-profile-list/inline-3.webp)


Understanding a character profile in Neverness to Everness goes beyond just looking at their rarity. You must account for their "Arc Compatibility" and how their element interacts with the rest of your team.

### S-Rank Character Profiles & Abilities

The S-Rank roster contains the most powerful units in the game, each designed to anchor a specific team archetype.

*   **Nanally (Anima DPS / Plasma Arc):** As the first limited banner character, Nanally is the current gold standard for damage. Her kit centers on "Ichi-daime's Authority," a state triggered by her skill that increases Critical DMG by 30% and allows her to run on walls. Her Ultimate summons the "Underboss," a spectral entity that coordinates follow-up attacks with every strike Nanally makes.
*   **Hotori (Cosmos Support-DPS / Bose Arc):** Hotori is a technical character who uses a "Non-Closed Timepiece" energy system. She can enter a "Recording" phase where she stores the support skills used by her teammates. When she activates her Ultimate, she stops time for 5 seconds and unleashes all recorded attacks simultaneously.
*   **Sakiri (Incantation Support / Bose Arc):** Sakiri is the premier offensive support. Her skill, "Devour Whole," provides exceptional crowd control by pulling enemies into a central point. Her Ultimate provides a team-wide ATK buff equal to 30% of her own base attack for 20 seconds.
*   **Daffodill (Chaos Break / Liquid Arc):** Currently the only Chaos element S-Rank, Daffodill specializes in the "Break" mechanic. She is designed to shred enemy stagger bars quickly, allowing her teammates to deal massive damage during the "Downed" state.
*   **Chiz (Cosmos DPS / Gas Arc):** Chiz is unique because her damage scales with "Fons," the currency used in the City Tycoon mode. Her signature Arc, the "Contemplative Cat," grants a Cosmos DMG bonus based on the amount of Fons the player currently holds.

### A-Rank Character Profiles & Utility

A-Rank characters often provide utility that S-Ranks lack, particularly in terms of survivability and meter generation.

*   **Zero (Cosmos / Bose):** The protagonist of NTE. Zero is a versatile unit with the "Instant Cycle" mechanic, allowing them to fill the Esper Meter of teammates instantly. They also provide minor healing, making them a safe pick for any early-game team.
*   **Haniel (Psyche / Solid):** A dedicated buffer who provides ranged Psyche damage. Haniel is often paired with Daffodill to trigger the "Nova" reaction, which deals delayed burst damage.
*   **Adler (Incantation / Solid):** A defensive specialist who provides shields based on his Max HP. He is vital for high-difficulty Anomaly Zones where enemy damage can one-shot glass-cannon DPS units.

### Understanding Elements and Arc Types

The combat in NTE is built on the **Esper Cycle**. To deal maximum damage, you must swap between characters whose elements are adjacent to each other on the elemental wheel.

| Reaction | Elements Required | Combat Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blossom** | Cosmos + Anima | Summons an AoE turret that fires automatically. |
| **Hexed** | Anima + Incantation | Deals bonus damage based on 20% of damage taken in the last 12s. |
| **Scorch** | Incantation + Chaos | Applies a 15-second Damage-over-Time (DoT) effect. |
| **Nova** | Chaos + Psyche | Attaches a bomb to the enemy that explodes for burst damage. |
| **Stain** | Psyche + Lakshana | Increases damage taken from Psyche and Lakshana by 50%. |
| **Remora** | Lakshana + Cosmos | Marks and slows enemies, reducing their attack speed. |

In addition to elements, characters are restricted by **Arc Compatibility**. There are five types of Arcs: **Bose, Gas, Liquid, Plasma, and Solid**. A character can only equip an Arc that matches their compatibility type. For example, Nanally (Plasma) cannot equip the Contemplative Cat (Gas), even if you own it.

### How to Recruit New Espers

Acquiring new character profiles in NTE uses the "Scarborough Fair" system, which is a unique dice-based board game.

1.  **The Dice Board:** Instead of a standard "pull" animation, you roll dice to move a piece (a "Chuppa") across a board. You receive the reward of whatever tile you land on.
2.  **Pity System:** There is no 50/50 mechanic in NTE. You are guaranteed the limited S-Rank character within 90 rolls. At 70 rolls, the "Board Modification" triggers, turning multiple tiles into S-Rank character tiles to ensure you hit the pity.
3.  **Free Character Unlocks:**
    *   **Zero:** Available from the start of the game.
    *   **Mint & Adler:** Unlocked through Chapter 1 of the Main Story.
    *   **Chiz:** Unlocked for free by reaching Level 18 in the City Tycoon management mode. You can fully awaken her by reaching Level 45.
    *   **S-Rank Selector:** After 50 pulls on the Standard Board, players receive a "Standard Choice" item, allowing them to pick one of the six standard S-Rank Espers (Sakiri, Baicang, Hathor, Fadia, Daffodill, or Jiuyuan).

### Advanced Combat Mechanics

Mastering the **Triple Cycle** is the key to clearing high-level Anomalies. If you trigger two duo reactions from the same trio (e.g., triggering Remora then Blossom), you unlock a Triple Reaction.
*   **Charge (Top Trio):** Generates massive amounts of Ultimate Energy.
*   **Discord (Bottom Trio):** Deals 100% increased Break/Stagger damage, essential for boss fights.

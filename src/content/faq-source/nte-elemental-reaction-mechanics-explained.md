## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-elemental-reaction-mechanics-explained/inline-2.webp)


Elemental reactions in NTE are called "Esper Cycles." These are triggered by swapping between characters whose elements are adjacent to one another on the hexagonal Esper Wheel. Unlike other games where any two elements might react, NTE restricts reactions to "neighbors" on this wheel.

The system utilizes six primary elements: Cosmos, Anima, Incantation, Chaos, Psyche, and Lakshana. To trigger a reaction, you must first fill a character's Esper Meter through offensive actions like normal attacks, skills, or successful parries. Once the meter is full, swapping to an adjacent elemental character while the previous element's "Linger" effect is active triggers a Duo Cycle. If three characters from a specific "Trio" group are cycled in sequence, you trigger a powerful Triple Cycle.

These reactions provide essential combat utility, ranging from summoning automated turrets (Blossom) and applying damage-over-time (Scorch) to massive defense debuffs (Stain) and crowd control (Remora).

### The Core Rule: Adjacency

The most critical mechanic to memorize is the Esper Wheel's layout. Elements are arranged in a fixed hexagonal pattern. A reaction is only possible if the two elements are next to each other. For example, Cosmos is adjacent to Anima and Lakshana. Therefore, Cosmos can react with Anima to create Blossom or with Lakshana to create Remora. However, Cosmos cannot react with Chaos or Psyche directly, as they sit across the wheel from it. This adjacency rule forces players to think carefully about the order in which they swap their characters.

### The Six Duo Cycle Reactions

Duo Cycles are the bread and butter of NTE combat. They occur when you swap from an "Initiator" (the character currently on field with a full Esper Meter) to a "Reactor" (the incoming character with an adjacent element).

| Reaction Name | Elements Required | Primary Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blossom** | Cosmos + Anima | Summons a 'Vita Bud' turret that fires 'Vita Pistils.' |
| **Hexed** | Anima + Incantation | Records damage for 12s, then deals a 20% follow-up burst. |
| **Scorch** | Incantation + Chaos | Applies a 15-second Burn DoT effect. |
| **Nova** | Chaos + Psyche | Attaches a bomb that deals Mental DMG after 5 seconds. |
| **Stain** | Psyche + Lakshana | 50% vulnerability to Psyche/Lakshana for 12 seconds. |
| **Remora** | Lakshana + Cosmos | Marks enemies to significantly reduce movement/attack speed. |

#### Blossom (Cosmos + Anima)
When you trigger Blossom, a "Vita Bud" is summoned at the location of the reaction. This turret is immobile but has a wide range, firing "Vita Pistils" at the nearest target. This is the premier reaction for "lazy" DPS or clearing out multiple small enemies, as the turret continues to fire even if you swap characters again, provided the Anima/Cosmos synergy remains active in the background.

#### Hexed (Anima + Incantation)
Hexed is a technical reaction favored by high-skill players. Once triggered, a mark appears over the enemy. For the next 12 seconds, the game tracks the total damage you deal to that target. When the timer expires, the mark explodes for [exact value pending official confirmation] plus 20% of the recorded damage. This is best used immediately before unleashing a character's Ultimate.

#### Scorch (Incantation + Chaos)
Scorch is the most straightforward DPS reaction. It applies a 15-second damage-over-time effect. While the damage per tick is moderate, the long duration makes it highly efficient for boss encounters where you may need to spend time dodging or repositioning.

#### Nova (Chaos + Psyche)
Nova focuses on delayed burst. It attaches a "Mental Bomb" to the target. After a 5-second countdown, it detonates for massive Mental DMG. This reaction is particularly effective against enemies with low Mental Resistance, common among the "Hollow" type anomalies in Hethereau.

#### Stain (Psyche + Lakshana)
Stain is a powerful debuff. It increases the target's vulnerability to Psyche and Lakshana damage by 50% for 12 seconds. If your main DPS is a Psyche character, triggering Stain with a Lakshana support is the fastest way to see "big numbers" on your screen.

#### Remora (Lakshana + Cosmos)
Remora provides essential crowd control. It marks enemies, causing them to move and attack in slow motion. This is vital for high-difficulty content where enemy attack patterns are fast and punishing.

### Unlocking Triple Cycle Potential

Triple Cycles represent the pinnacle of NTE's elemental system. These occur when you complete a sequence of three adjacent elements within a short window. The wheel is divided into two Trios: the "Top Trio" and the "Bottom Trio."

| Trio Group | Elements Involved | Triple Cycle Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Top Trio** | Lakshana, Cosmos, Anima | **Charged:** Blossom hits on Remora-marked targets generate Ultimate energy. |
| **Bottom Trio** | Incantation, Chaos, Psyche | **Discord:** Simultaneous Scorch and Nova effects deal massive Break damage. |

The **Charged** cycle is the engine of "Burst" teams. By cycling through Lakshana, Cosmos, and then Anima, your Vita Bud turret will hit enemies slowed by Remora, which feeds massive amounts of energy back to your active character, allowing for nearly back-to-back Ultimates.

The **Discord** cycle is designed for shield-breaking. When you combine the DoT of Scorch with the burst of Nova in a rapid sequence involving Incantation, Chaos, and Psyche, the resulting Discord effect shreds the enemy's white Stagger bar (Break Meter) significantly faster than standard attacks.

### Mastering the Esper Meter and Parrying

You cannot trigger reactions without a full Esper Meter. While normal attacks generate a steady stream of energy, relying on them exclusively results in slow rotations.

*   **Normal Attacks:** Provide [information unavailable] energy per hit.
*   **Elemental Skills:** Provide a significant chunk of energy, usually around 30-40% of the meter.
*   **Perfect Parries:** This is the most important mechanic. A Perfect Parry (timing your dodge or block exactly as an enemy attack lands) instantly refills a large portion of the current character's Esper Meter.
*   **Cycle Rate Stat:** This is a secondary attribute found on Arcs (weapons) and gear. Increasing your Cycle Rate reduces the amount of raw energy needed to fill the Esper Meter, allowing for faster swaps.

### Optimizing Team Synergies

When building a team, the "Connected Arc" strategy is superior to the "Scattered Rainbow" approach. Because reactions require adjacency, a team of three characters should ideally sit next to each other on the Esper Wheel.

*   **Connected Arc (Strong):** Cosmos (Esper Zero), Anima ([Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)), and Incantation (Adler). This team can trigger Blossom and Hexed easily.
*   **Scattered Rainbow (Weak):** Cosmos, Chaos, and Psyche. In this setup, Cosmos cannot react with either of the other two, leaving you with only one possible reaction (Nova) between Chaos and Psyche. This effectively cuts your potential DPS and utility in half.

### Break Meter and Stagger Mechanics

Elemental reactions play a direct role in the "Break" system. Every elite enemy and boss has a white bar beneath their health. Once this bar is depleted, the enemy enters a "Staggered" state, taking increased damage and remaining immobile for several seconds. 

While all damage reduces this bar, specific reactions like **Discord** are specifically tuned to deplete it. Furthermore, triggering any Duo Cycle causes a "Stagger Flinch," which can interrupt smaller enemies' wind-up animations, providing a defensive layer to your offensive rotation.

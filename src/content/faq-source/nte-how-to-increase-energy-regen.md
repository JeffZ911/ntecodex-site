# Neverness to Everness: How to Increase Energy Regeneration

In Neverness to Everness (NTE), "Energy" is a multi-faceted term that refers to two distinct systems: Ultimate Energy, which fuels your character’s most powerful combat abilities, and Character Pixels, the stamina resource required to farm materials in Anomaly Zones. Mastering the regeneration of both is essential for clearing high-level content like Beyond the Rails and optimizing your daily progression.

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/nte-how-to-increase-energy-regen/inline-1.webp)


When players ask how to increase energy regeneration in NTE, they are typically referring to one of two bottlenecks:

*   **Ultimate Energy (Combat):** This is the gauge used to cast a character’s Ultimate. Slow regeneration leads to "dead time" in rotations where your Main DPS, such as [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or Chiz, cannot perform their burst window.
*   **Character Pixels (Stamina):** This is the daily resource used to claim rewards from Anomaly Zones and the Rabbit Hole. It regenerates over time, and running out prevents you from upgrading your Arcs, Consoles, or character levels.

Managing these resources requires a combination of specific gear stats, elemental reactions, and city-based progression.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-how-to-increase-energy-regen/inline-2.webp)


To increase combat energy (Ultimates), you should prioritize equipping Arcs with the **Charge Efficiency** secondary stat, such as the S-rank Arc **Day Off**, or support-focused Arcs like **Good Boy's Grand Adventure**. In combat, the most effective way to generate energy is by triggering the **Charge Trio Reaction** (Lakshana + Cosmos + Anima), which restores Ultimate Energy whenever your Blossom turrets hit enemies marked by Remora.

To increase your daily Character Pixel (stamina) capacity and management, you must upgrade your Penthouse or apartment using **Anomaly Furniture**. Specifically, the **Dream Weaver's Knot** (or Dreamweaver) furniture increases your maximum Pixel cap. For immediate recovery, use **De-noise Solutions** or spend Annulith (up to six times daily) to refill 60 Pixels per use.

Including "Battery" characters like **Hathor** is also vital. Hathor’s "Express Delivery Power" (Feather Stacks) restores 5 Ultimate Energy per stack gained, significantly speeding up the team's rotation.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-how-to-increase-energy-regen/inline-3.webp)


The depth of NTE's energy system lies in how combat mechanics and city life systems interact. Increasing your "Cycle Rate" and "Charge Efficiency" requires a nuanced understanding of the Esper Cycle and gear optimization.

### Mastering the 'Charge' Trio Reaction

The most powerful engine for Ultimate Energy in NTE is the **Charge Trio Reaction**. This is triggered by combining three specific elements: **Lakshana, Cosmos, and Anima**.

1.  **Remora (Lakshana + Cosmos):** Marks the enemy and slows their movement/attack speed.
2.  **Blossom (Cosmos + Anima):** Spawns a "Vita Bud" turret that fires "Vita Pistils" (projectiles) at nearby enemies.
3.  **Charge (The Trio):** When a Vita Pistil from a Blossom hits an enemy currently affected by the Remora mark, the active character gains **10 additional Ultimate Energy** per hit.

By maintaining this reaction loop, characters with multi-hit Blossom passives (like **Jiuyuan** or **Mint**) can fully recharge a teammate's Ultimate in seconds.

Arcs are the primary source of "Charge Efficiency," a stat that acts as a multiplier for all Ultimate Energy gained. While many Arcs focus on CRIT or ATK, a few are dedicated to rotation speed.

### Optimizing Consoles and Modules

Consoles and their attached Modules provide the granular stat boosts needed for endgame energy management. When farming the **Rabbit Hole Anomaly Zone**, look for the following stats:

*   **Cycle Rate:** This stat increases the speed at which your **Esper Meter** fills. A higher Cycle Rate allows you to swap characters more frequently, triggering more reactions and, by extension, more energy-generating Charge procs.
*   **Cycle Intensity:** While this primarily boosts reaction damage, some high-tier Cartridge sets provide energy-related bonuses based on your Cycle Intensity value.
*   **Recharge Speed:** Found as a substat on Modules, this directly impacts how quickly the Ultimate Energy gauge fills from standard combat actions.

In NTE, you can see all four substats of a Module before you spend resources to upgrade it. Always prioritize Modules with at least one "Cycle Rate" roll for your support and battery units.

### Top Energy Battery Characters

Certain characters are designed specifically to "feed" energy to the rest of the team.

*   **Hathor (Lakshana):** The gold standard for energy generation. Her "Express Delivery Power" generates Feather Stacks through her skill and dodge. Each stack restores **5 Ultimate Energy**. At Awakening 1 (A1), she gains 6 instant stacks upon using her hold skill, providing a massive 30 energy burst to the team.
*   **Zero (Cosmos):** The protagonist’s Redirect Skill ("Appraise and Engrave") fills their own Esper Meter to 100% instantly. At Awakening 2 (A2), Zero grants **8 Ultimate Energy** to the team after every skill cast.
*   **Sakiri (Incantation):** While primarily a crowd-control unit, her signature Arc and high base Cycle Rate make her an efficient engine for rotating through the team’s Ultimates.

### Advanced Combat Tactics: Perfect Parries

If your energy regeneration feels slow, you may not be utilizing the **Parry** mechanic effectively. Performing a **Perfect Parry** instantly refills the active character's **Esper Meter** to 100%. This allows for an immediate swap-in to trigger a reaction. 

Using a "Critical Riposte" (a follow-up attack after a parry) often generates a significant burst of Ultimate Energy compared to standard normal attack strings. For energy-hungry teams, switching to a character with a large parry window (like **Adler**) can reset your rotation mid-fight.

### Increasing the Character Pixel (Stamina) Cap

Character Pixels regenerate at a rate of **1 Pixel every 6 minutes**, totaling 240 Pixels every 24 hours. To increase your efficiency and prevent overcapping, you must engage with the housing system in Hethereau.

*   **Dream Weaver's Knot:** This Anomaly Furniture piece is the primary way to increase your Pixel cap. It can be obtained through specific Anomaly Commissions or purchased via the Hunter Exchange.
*   **Housing Upgrades:** Purchasing more expensive properties, such as the **Golden Capital** (2,000,000 Fons) or **Skyview Halls** (1,400,000 Fons), allows you to place higher-tier furniture that provides larger cap increases.
*   **The 330 Cap:** Through maxing out the Dreamweaver furniture and placing high-quality beds in your penthouse, players can increase their maximum capacity to 330 Pixels, allowing for more flexibility in daily login times.

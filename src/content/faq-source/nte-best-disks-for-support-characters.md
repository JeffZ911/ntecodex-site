# NTE Best Disks for Support Characters: Top Cartridge Sets Guide

Since the April 2026 launch of Neverness to Everness (NTE), the meta for support characters has solidified around maximizing the "Esper Cycle" and maintaining high uptime on team-wide buffs. While damage dealers focus on raw stats, supports rely on the Console system to provide utility through specialized gear sets.

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/nte-best-disks-for-support-characters/inline-1.webp)


When players search for the "best disks" for support characters in Neverness to Everness, they are typically referring to the **Cartridge and Module system**. In NTE, "Disks" is a common community shorthand for the hexagonal Cartridges that serve as the foundation of a character’s gear, while "Modules" are the geometric "Tetris" pieces that slot into the Console to activate powerful set bonuses.

### What are Disks in Neverness to Everness?

In-game, your gear is divided into two parts: the **Cartridge** (the frame) and **Modules** (the blocks). A Cartridge defines the potential set bonuses and provides a primary Main Stat, such as HP%, DEF%, or ATK%. To activate the 2-piece and 4-piece "Legendary" effects, you must fit matching Modules into the character's Console grid. For support characters, these set bonuses are more important than the individual stats of the pieces themselves, as they often provide unique team buffs that cannot be found elsewhere.

### Why Support Builds Matter for the Esper Cycle

The combat in NTE revolves around the **Esper Cycle**, a system of elemental reactions like Scorch, Nova, Blossom, and Remora. Support characters facilitate these reactions by staying off-field or swapping in briefly to trigger "Redirect Skills." A properly built support character using the correct Cartridge set ensures that your main DPS always has enough Ultimate Energy (Charge) and that the team's "Cycle Intensity" remains high enough to shred enemy Break bars. Without specialized support disks, your rotations will stall, leaving your team vulnerable in endgame content like "Beyond the Rails."

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-best-disks-for-support-characters/inline-2.webp)


The current meta identifies three primary Cartridge sets as the gold standard for support characters in Version 1.0.

*   **Best Universal Support Set:** **Speedy Hedgehog**. This set is the go-to for almost every buffer because it provides 12% Charge Efficiency (Energy Regen) and a massive 15% ATK buff to the entire team for 20 seconds after the wearer uses an Ultimate.
*   **Best Healing Set:** **Thea’s Night Tavern**. Essential for pure healers like Edgar, this set increases Max HP by 10% and grants a 20% Healing Bonus, ensuring the team can recover from heavy hits in Anomaly zones.
*   **Best Shielding Set:** **Kingdom’s Guard**. This is the specialized choice for Adler and other defense-scaling supports, offering a 15% DEF boost and increasing shield durability by 20%.

### Recommended Sub-Stats for Support Disks

While the set bonus is the priority, optimizing your Module sub-stats will make your supports more reliable. Unlike other gacha games, NTE allows the **Main Stat and Sub-Stats to overlap** on the same piece, meaning you can have a DEF% Main Stat Cartridge with a DEF% sub-stat for maximum scaling.

1.  **Charge Efficiency:** The most critical stat for supports. It ensures your Ultimate is ready as soon as the cooldown ends.
2.  **Cycle Intensity:** Increases the damage and effectiveness of Esper Cycle reactions (like the explosion damage of Nova).
3.  **HP% / DEF%:** Vital for survivability. Supports often have lower base stats, so these prevent them from being "one-shot" during a swap.
4.  **Break Effect:** Helps supports contribute to depleting the enemy's white Break bar, especially for characters like Sakiri who have high-impact Support Skills.

## Detailed Explanation

The gear system in Neverness to Everness is deeper than it initially appears. To truly optimize a support, you must understand how the Console grid interacts with Module shapes and character-specific "Type" bonuses.

### Understanding the Console: Cartridges vs. Modules

Every character has a **Console**, a grid where you arrange Modules. The **Cartridge** you equip acts as the "key" to the set bonus. For example, if you equip a **Speedy Hedgehog Cartridge**, the Console will only activate the 4-piece ATK buff if you successfully fit four "Speedy Hedgehog" Modules onto the grid. 

This creates a "Tetris" mini-game. Higher-rarity Modules (S-Rank) often come in complex shapes (Type III or Type IV) that take up more space but offer higher stat values. Balancing the shapes to fit the 4-piece set while also slotting in "filler" modules for raw stats is the core of endgame optimization.

### Speedy Hedgehog: The Buffing Meta

**Speedy Hedgehog** is currently the most used set in the game for a reason. In NTE, ATK buffs are calculated based on the receiver's base stats, making a 15% team-wide increase a massive boost for high-tier DPS characters like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or Hathor. 

The 2-piece bonus of 12% Charge Efficiency is also the highest energy-related buff available from gear. For characters like **Sakiri**, whose Ultimate provides both crowd control and a separate built-in ATK buff, stacking the Speedy Hedgehog effect allows her to nearly double the team's offensive output during her burst window.

### Thea’s Night Tavern: Maximizing Healing Output

While some veteran players argue that "damage is the best defense," the high-level Anomaly zones in Hethereau feature aggressive enemies that can deplete a health bar in seconds. **Thea’s Night Tavern** is the premier choice for **Edgar**. 

Edgar’s healing scales with his Max HP, so the 2-piece 10% HP bonus directly improves his base throughput. The 4-piece 20% Healing Bonus is a multiplicative modifier, meaning it applies after all other calculations. This makes it significantly more powerful than simply stacking more HP modules. In "Beyond the Rails" Stage 5 and above, this set is often the difference between a successful clear and a team wipe.

### Kingdom’s Guard: Shielding and DEF Scaling

**Adler** is the primary user of **Kingdom’s Guard**. His kit is unique because his damage, shield strength, and utility all scale with his **DEF** stat. 

*   **2-Piece Effect:** Increases DEF by 15%.
*   **4-Piece Effect:** Increases the durability of shields generated by the wearer by 20%.

When Adler uses his "Evil's Bane" Skill, he consumes Karma stacks to create a shield. Kingdom’s Guard ensures this shield can withstand several heavy blows from bosses, allowing your main attacker to stay on the field without dodging, which indirectly increases your team's total Damage Per Second (DPS).

### Tiny Big Adventure: The Tank-Support Choice for Fadia

For characters like **Fadia**, who function as a hybrid between a tank and a healer, **Tiny Big Adventure** offers a unique scaling mechanic. The 4-piece bonus grants a 4% Max HP increase every time the wearer takes damage, stacking up to 10 times. 

What makes this set meta for Fadia is the secondary effect: **Casting an Ultimate instantly grants all 10 stacks.** This provides an immediate 40% Max HP boost, which in turn increases the strength of Fadia’s healing and shields. It is the best set for "soaking" damage during difficult boss phases.

### How to Optimize Module Shapes (The Tetris Strategy)

The Console grid has limited space. To activate a 4-piece set bonus, you don't necessarily need the biggest pieces.
*   **Type II Modules** are small (2 cells) and easy to fit but have lower stats.
*   **Type III Modules** are the "sweet spot" (3 cells), often forming L-shapes or straight lines.
*   **Type IV Modules** are large (4 cells) and provide the highest sub-stat rolls but are difficult to arrange.

For a support, prioritize fitting the 4-piece set using Type II or Type III modules first. Only upgrade to Type IV pieces once you have unlocked enough grid expansion slots through character Leveling and Breakthroughs.

### Character-Specific Module Type Bonuses

A hidden layer of the NTE gear system is that each character has a preferred **Module Type** (II, III, or IV). If you fill the Console with their preferred type, they gain a massive passive bonus.

*   **Sakiri:** Gains a **9% Incantation DMG bonus** for every **Type III Module** equipped. With a full board of six Type III modules, she gains a 54% damage boost.
*   **Adler:** Gains **12% DEF** for every **Type III Module** equipped. This is why Kingdom’s Guard (which uses Type III modules) is so synergistic with him.
*   **Fadia:** Gains **6% HP** for every **Type II Module** equipped.

Always check the "Console Bonus" tab on the character screen to see which Type your support character prefers before you start farming.

### Farming S-Rank Disks: Rabbit Hole and Rewind Guide

To get the best support disks, you must participate in the **Rabbit Hole Anomaly Zone**, located in the Cape Square area of the New Herland District.

1.  **Rabbit Hole Farming:** Entering the zone costs **40 Character Pixels** (Energy). Different levels of the Rabbit Hole drop different Cartridge sets. You must reach Hunter Level 30 to unlock the "Hard" difficulty, which has a guaranteed drop rate for S-Rank Cartridges.
2.  **The Rewind System:** While Cartridges are farmed in the Rabbit Hole, **Modules** are primarily obtained through the **Rewind** feature on the character's gear screen. 
3.  **Carrota Coins:** Rewinding costs Carrota Coins (Bronze, Silver, or Gold). You earn these by completing Rabbit Hole runs and Anomaly Commissions. 
4.  **The S-Rank Strategy:** Use your Gold Carrota Coins to target specific Module shapes. For supports, you should aim for **Type III pieces** with Charge Efficiency sub-stats.

Optimizing your support disks is only half the battle. To reach the ceiling of Version 1.0 content, you must pair these sets with the right weapons and team compositions.

### Best Arcs to Pair with Support Disks

In NTE, weapons are called **Arcs**. Support Arcs often have passive abilities that trigger when the wearer is off-field or when an Ultimate is cast.

*   **Good Boy’s Grand Adventure (S-Rank):** The signature Arc for Sakiri. It provides 18% Charge Efficiency and grants the team an additional 10-16% ATK buff after an Ultimate. When paired with the **Speedy Hedgehog** set, the total team ATK buff reaches a staggering 31%.
*   **The Fools' Spring (A-Rank):** A top-tier choice for Adler. It provides a 52.5% DEF increase at Max Level, which perfectly complements the **Kingdom’s Guard** set.
*   **Umbrella (A-Rank):** Another defensive option that strengthens shields by 10% while the wearer is above 50% HP.

### Support Disk Synergies in Meta Team Comps

The choice of support disk often depends on your main attacker's element.
*   **Blossom Teams (Anima + Cosmos):** Use **Edgar** with **Thea’s Night Tavern** to keep the team healthy while Nanally triggers Blossom reactions.
*   **Nova Teams (Chaos + Psyche):** Use **Sakiri** with **Speedy Hedgehog** to group enemies together, allowing your Chaos DPS to hit multiple targets with Nova explosions.
*   **Discord Teams:** A high-end comp using **Adler** (Kingdom’s Guard) and **Fadia** (Tiny Big Adventure) to create an unbreakable defensive frontline, allowing a single carry to deal damage without interruption.

### Esper Cycle Guide: Reactions and Support Roles

Support disks are the engine of the **Esper Cycle**. By increasing **Charge Efficiency** and **Cycle Intensity**, your support characters allow the team to trigger reactions faster. 
*   **Remora (Anima + Incantation):** Slows enemies down. Kingdom’s Guard on Adler makes this even safer by providing a shield while the enemy is slowed.
*   **Scorch (Chaos + Incantation):** Deals massive DoT. Using the **Shadow Creed** set on a support can boost the ATK of the character dealing the Scorch damage, significantly increasing the tick damage.

As of May 2026, the support meta is stable, but keep an eye on upcoming characters like **Lacrimosa**, who may introduce new gear requirements for Chaos-based support archetypes.

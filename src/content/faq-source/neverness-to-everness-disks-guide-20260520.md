## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/neverness-to-everness-disks-guide-20260520/inline-2.webp)


In Neverness to Everness, "Disks" is a colloquial term often used by the community to describe **Cartridges**, which are the primary gear pieces equipped within an Esper's **Console**. The system functions as a hybrid equipment and puzzle mechanic. Each character possesses a Console Board—a 20-slot grid—where you must fit one Cartridge and several Modules.

The system is split into two distinct parts
1.  **Cartridges:** These provide a primary Set Bonus and a random Main Stat (such as Anima DMG%, Crit Rate, or ATK%). They also come with four visible sub-stats from the moment you acquire them.
2.  **Modules:** These are Tetris-like geometric shapes that provide flat stat increases (Flat ATK and Flat HP). 

To activate the powerful set bonuses on a Cartridge, you must slot specific Module shapes (Type II, III, or IV) into the Console grid as required by that Cartridge’s "Shape Requirement" list. A standout feature of NTE's gear system is transparency; you can see all four sub-stats on a piece before spending resources to upgrade it, significantly reducing the "RNG trap" found in other games.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/neverness-to-everness-disks-guide-20260520/inline-3.webp)


The Console system is the heart of character progression in Hellar City. To build a top-tier Esper, you need to master both the farming of high-rarity Disks and the spatial logic required to fit them into your board.

### Understanding Cartridges: The Core of Your Build

Cartridges serve as the foundation of your Esper's stats. They are categorized into three rarities: **B (Blue)**, **A (Pink)**, and **S (Orange)**. S-Rank Cartridges are the endgame goal, offering the highest stat ceilings and the most potent set effects.

Each Cartridge features
*   **One Main Stat:** This is randomized upon drop. For DPS characters, you generally look for Elemental DMG (Anima, Cosmos, or Chaos), Crit Rate, or Crit DMG.
*   **Four Sub-stats:** These are fixed at the moment of acquisition. You do not need to "roll" to reveal them; they are visible at Level 0.
*   **Set Bonus:** A unique passive effect that only activates if the "Shape Requirements" are met using Modules.

### The Module Puzzle: Solving the Tetris System

Modules are the secondary component of the Console. While they offer lower stat values than Cartridges, they are essential for activating the Cartridge's full power. Modules are categorized by their "Type," which corresponds to the number of blocks they occupy on the 20-slot grid

| Module Type | Grid Size | Primary Stats Provided |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Type II | 2 Blocks | Flat ATK / Flat HP |
| Type III | 3 Blocks | Flat ATK / Flat HP |
| Type IV | 4 Blocks | Flat ATK / Flat HP |

The challenge lies in the 20-slot limitation. A Cartridge typically occupies a 2x2 or 3x2 area, leaving limited room for Modules. You must choose Module shapes that not only fit the remaining empty slots but also satisfy the Cartridge's activation requirements.

### Activating Set Bonuses: Shape Matching Requirements

Every Cartridge has a specific list of shapes it needs to "see" on the board to trigger its set bonus. For example, a high-end Chaos DMG Cartridge might require
*   Two **Type II** Modules (Straight 1x2 pieces)
*   One **Type III** Module (L-shape)

If you place these exact shapes onto the grid, the Set Bonus lights up. If you use the wrong shapes—even if they provide better sub-stats—the Cartridge’s powerful passive remains inactive. This creates a strategic layer where "perfect" sub-stats on a Module might be less valuable than a "correctly shaped" Module that completes your set.

### Character Passives and Module Synergy

Some Espers have innate passives that interact directly with the Module system. A prime example is **[Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)**, whose kit is heavily tied to her gear configuration. 
*   **Nanally's Passive:** She gains a **6% Crit Rate bonus for every Type II module** equipped in her Console. 

For a character like Nanally, the goal isn't just to fit any modules; it's to maximize the number of Type II pieces on the board, even if it means passing up higher-stat Type IV pieces. This character-specific optimization makes the Disk system much deeper than a standard "stat-stick" gear set.

### Farming Guide: Rabbit Hole and Carrota Coins

Farming for the perfect Disk setup requires two different activities

1.  **The Rabbit Hole (Anomaly Zone):** Located in **Cape Square**, this is where you farm Cartridges. Each run costs **40 Character Pixels** (NTE's stamina system). Higher difficulty levels increase the drop rate of S-Rank Cartridges.
2.  **The Rewind System (Module Gacha):** Unlike Cartridges, Modules are primarily obtained through the "Rewind" menu. This is a dedicated gear gacha that uses **Carrota Coins**, a currency earned through daily activities, events, and City Tycoon management. 

Because Modules are obtained through a gacha-like system, players are encouraged to save their Carrota Coins until they have a specific Cartridge shape requirement they need to fill.

### Optimization Tips: Avoiding RNG Pitfalls

The most significant advantage in Neverness to Everness is the sub-stat visibility. Since you can see all four sub-stats at Level 0, you should never waste resources (Fons and upgrade materials) on a piece that doesn't have at least two desirable stats (e.g., Crit Rate and ATK%).

**Upgrading and Scaling:**
Gear in NTE caps at **Level +20**. Sub-stats do not "reveal" themselves at intervals; instead, the existing sub-stats receive a random enhancement at the following levels
*   **Level +5**
*   **Level +10**
*   **Level +15**
*   **Level +20**

If a sub-stat roll goes into a flat DEF stat at Level +5 and +10, it is often better to use that piece as experience fodder for a new Disk rather than taking it all the way to +20.

| Cartridge Rarity | Max Level | Sub-stat Enhancements |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| B (Blue) | +10 | 2 enhancements |
| A (Pink) | +15 | 3 enhancements |
| S (Orange) | +20 | 4 enhancements |

Mastering your Disks is only one part of the character-building process. To truly maximize your Espers, you should also look into the following systems

### Best Disks for Early Game
During the early levels (Account Level 1-20), do not stress about S-Rank Cartridges. Focus on completing any set bonus, as the percentage increases to Anima or Cosmos damage are more impactful than the raw stats of an un-activated A-Rank piece. Use the "Auto-Equip" function for Modules initially, but manually check if you can fit an extra piece to trigger the Cartridge's passive.

### Advanced Endgame Farming Strategies
Once you reach the endgame, your focus shifts to the **Anomaly Commission**. These world bosses and high-level challenges drop specialized upgrade materials and rare Carrota Coins. Additionally, engaging with the **City Tycoon Mode** is vital; the Fons (gold) generated by your businesses is the primary resource used to fund the expensive Level +15 to +20 upgrade process for S-Rank Disks.

For weapon synergy, ensure your Cartridge's main stat complements your equipped Arc. For instance, if you are using an Arc that provides high Base ATK, a Cartridge with ATK% Main Stat will yield better results than one with Flat ATK.

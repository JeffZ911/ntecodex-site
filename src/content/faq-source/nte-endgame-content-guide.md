## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/nte-endgame-content-guide/inline-1.webp)


What activities are available in Neverness to Everness after completing the main story?

Reaching the endgame in NTE means transitioning from narrative-driven missions to repeatable high-stakes challenges. Players primarily engage in **Beyond the Rails** for premium currency, the **Rabbit Hole** for gear optimization, and the **City Tycoon** system for passive wealth generation.

How do players progress their characters and urban status in the NTE post-game?

Progression in the post-game is measured by your **Hunter Level** and **Appraisal Level**. These stats gate the rarity of gear drops in Anomaly Zones and unlock higher-tier business opportunities in the city. Veteran players focus on min-maxing "Cartridges" and "Modules" on the character Console board to tackle the Fracture Circle difficulty.

What are the primary sources of Annulith and Beetle Coins for veteran players?

**Annulith**, the currency required for character and Arc pulls, is primarily sourced from bi-weekly resets of Beyond the Rails and daily Exploration Guide tasks. **Beetle Coins**, used to purchase Annulith and rare upgrade materials in the Hunter Exchange, are earned through City Tycoon level-ups, Hethereau Hobbies like racing and mahjong, and the bi-weekly Pink Paws Heist.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-endgame-content-guide/inline-3.webp)


The endgame of Neverness to Everness requires a balance between combat efficiency and economic management. You cannot simply ignore the city simulation, as your character's power is directly tied to the Beetle Coins and Fons generated through urban activities.

### Beyond the Rails: The Ultimate Combat Challenge

Beyond the Rails is the premier endgame mode located within the Anomaly Metro framework, often referred to as the "Ghost Train." It is split into 10 stages (stops) that scale in difficulty. 

*   **Prime Circle:** The standard tier, providing up to 700 Annulith per cycle. This is accessible at Hunter Level 12 and is the first goal for new players.
*   **Fracture Circle:** The high-difficulty tier, offering up to 1,600 Annulith per cycle. This mode features level 60+ enemies and requires a fully optimized roster.
*   **Platform Seals:** Each stop has three objectives (usually clear-time targets). Earning these seals unlocks tiers of Annulith and Train Logs.
*   **Two-Team Requirement:** Starting from the 6th stop, the mode introduces "Inbound" and "Outbound" routes. This forces you to field two separate teams with no overlapping characters, making roster depth essential.

Clearing the 10th floor of the permanent Rail content grants a free copy of the A-class character **Haniel**, a Psyche-element support vital for many endgame team compositions.

### The Rabbit Hole: Mastering Cartridges and Modules

The Rabbit Hole, located in the New Herland District, is the primary source of character gear. Unlike other RPGs, NTE uses a "Console" board where you slot **Cartridges** (base gear) and **Modules** (stat-boosting puzzle pieces).

*   **Carrota Coins:** You farm Bronze, Silver, and Gold Carrota from the Rabbit Hole. These are spent in the "Rewind" menu to pull for Modules.
*   **Rarity Gates:** Legendary (Orange) Modules and Cartridges only become common drops once you reach **Hunter Level 30**.
*   **Rewind Tiers:**
    *   **Easy:** Bronze Carrota, Blue Modules, no pool control.
    *   **Normal (HL 20):** Silver Carrota, Purple Modules, select a pool of 8 shapes.
    *   **Hard (HL 30):** Gold Carrota, Orange Modules, select a pool of 8 shapes.
*   **Cartridge Upgrades:** A Cartridge provides its full set bonus only if you match the required Module shapes (2-piece or 4-piece sets) on the Console board.

### City Tycoon: Real Estate and Passive Income

The City Tycoon system is your primary engine for Fons and Beetle Coins. The most important feature is **The Cafe by Origen**, unlocked at Tycoon Rank 4.

**The Cafe by Origen: Location & Costs**

| Shop Location | Management Level | Cost (Fons) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 182 Bluebeard Road | Level 1 | 5,000 |
| 106 Fiscus Avenue | Level 5 | 20,000 |
| 1122 North Davidia Avenue | Level 10 | 40,000 |
| 88 Moomin Street | Level 17 | 75,000 |
| 199 Hankaku Street Plaza | Level 25 | 100,000 |

To maximize income, you must assign Espers with specific "Life Skills" to each shop. For example, characters like **Adler** or **Haniel** provide flat price increases for dishes. You should restock every 24 hours to ensure you can swap in higher-priced recipes as they unlock.

At Tycoon Rank 10, you unlock the **Pink Paws Heist**. This activity allows you to earn up to 1,000,000 Fons every two weeks without consuming City Stamina, making it a mandatory bi-weekly task for maintaining your upgrade budget.

### Hesperia Hobbies: Racing and Auto Mahjong

While combat and management are the core, Hesperia Hobbies provide the "Beetle Coins" needed for the Hunter Exchange.

*   **Racing:** There are 6 stages of racing challenges. Completing these rewards you with Beetle Coins and car tuning parts. High-tier racing requires investing Fons into your vehicle's engine and handling at the Garage.
*   **Mahjong Inferno:** An auto-battle mahjong mode that offers a steady stream of Beetle Coins.
*   **Pink Paws Heist:** Mentioned above, this is the most lucrative hobby for raw Fons.
*   **Ebisu Auction House:** Unlocked via the quest "Deal? Deal!", the auction house allows you to bid on rare Arcs and furniture that provide permanent account buffs.

## Related

![Related](/img/nte-endgame-content-guide/inline-4.webp)


To succeed in the endgame, you must master the **Esper Cycle** system. This combat mechanic relies on switching between characters to trigger elemental reactions. The most potent endgame reaction is **Discord** (Incantation + Chaos + Psyche). To [trigger Discord](https://ntecodex.com/faq), you must apply **Scorch** (Incantation + Chaos) and **Nova** (Chaos + Psyche) to an enemy simultaneously. This causes massive Break/Stagger damage, allowing characters like **Baicang** to execute bosses once their health falls below a certain threshold.

For high-tier racing, vehicle customization is more than cosmetic. Upgrading your car's "Cycle Intensity" in the Garage allows you to maintain speed through Anomaly Zones during city races. Furthermore, certain character builds, such as **[Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)** (Ice DPS) and **Baicang** (Incantation DPS), are currently considered the meta for clearing the 10th stop of the Fracture Circle due to their high AoE damage and Break efficiency.

# How to Get More NTE Disks in Neverness to Everness

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/how-to-get-more-nte-disks/inline-1.webp)


When players search for "NTE Disks," they are typically looking for one of two things: Arc Disks or Dice. In Neverness to Everness, Arc Disks function as the primary equipment system for Espers, providing stat boosts and set bonuses. Conversely, "Dice" are the actual currency used to perform summons on the game's unique board-game-style gacha banners. Understanding the distinction between these items and the currencies used to acquire them is the first step toward efficient farming.

To unlock high-tier S-Rank Espers and top-tier Arcs, you must manage a variety of resources. The universal premium currency is Annulith, which can be converted into summoning Dice. While E-Crystals are the paid version of this currency, F2P players primarily focus on accumulating Annulith through gameplay.

### What are NTE Disks and Dice?

Arc Disks (NTE Disks) are slotted into your Espers to enhance their combat capabilities. These are farmed through specific endgame activities and the Arc Research Program. Dice, on the other hand, are your "pulls." 

*   **Solid Dice:** Used for the Standard Resonance banner.
*   **Fabricated Dice:** Used for Limited-Time Event banners (such as [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)'s "The Ichi-daime" banner).

### Why Farming Currency is Essential for F2P Players

Neverness to Everness features a generous but structured economy. Because the game utilizes a "No 50/50" rule for its limited character banners, every Fabricated Die you earn carries more weight than in traditional gacha games. However, S-Rank Arcs and specific weapon-equivalents still require significant investment. Without a consistent farming routine, players will find themselves unable to keep up with the power creep of new Anomalies and high-level Hunter commissions.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-get-more-nte-disks/inline-2.webp)


The fastest way to get more NTE Disks and Dice is by maximizing your Daily Participation Tasks and claiming all available Login Rewards during version updates. For a consistent long-term supply, you must focus on World Exploration—specifically finding Oracle Stones and completing Anomaly Commissions across the districts of Hethereau.

If you are looking for immediate bursts of currency, prioritize these five activities
1.  **Daily Participation:** Complete tasks to reach 100 points every day for a guaranteed Annulith income.
2.  **Hunter Level Progression:** Claim the milestone rewards for every 5 levels gained; version 1.0 provides over 169 standard pulls through this system.
3.  **City Tycoon Management:** Level up your business properties to generate passive rewards and one-time milestone bonuses.
4.  **The Mall Exchange:** Use your monthly Warp Pieces and Lost Pieces to buy discounted Solid and Fabricated Dice.
5.  **Anomaly Commissions:** Clear the first-time completion rewards for world bosses and urban anomalies in Miguel and New Herland.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-get-more-nte-disks/inline-3.webp)


Farming in Neverness to Everness is divided between active gameplay (combat and exploration) and passive management (City Tycoon). To maintain a steady flow of Dice and Arc Disks, you should follow a structured daily and weekly routine.

### Daily Participation Tasks and Circle Bounty

The Daily Participation system is the backbone of your Annulith income. By completing simple tasks—such as spending Energy, finishing a commission, or interacting with a city shop—you earn Participation Points. 

Reaching the 100-point threshold grants a total of 60 Annulith daily. Additionally, players should keep an eye on the Circle Bounty. This system has a daily EXP cap of 1,000. While it primarily levels up your Hunter rank, hitting specific daily milestones within the Bounty system provides secondary materials needed to upgrade Arc Disks.

### Exploring Hethereau: Oracle Stones and Anomalies

Hethereau is a massive urban playground divided into several districts, including the industrial Miguel area and the neon-lit New Herland. Exploration is rewarded through the discovery of Oracle Stones. These hidden collectibles function similarly to "oculi" in other open-world games. Turning them in at the district hub provides a significant amount of Annulith and Solid Dice.

Anomaly Commissions are combat-focused world events. Clearing an Anomaly for the first time usually yields a one-time bounty of Annulith. To achieve 100% completion in a district, you must also solve environmental puzzles and find hidden "Urban Legends," which often lead to high-tier Arc Disk drops.

### City Tycoon and Passive Rewards

One of the unique features of Neverness to Everness is the City Tycoon system. As you progress, you can purchase and manage various businesses. While the primary output of these businesses is City Credits (used for buying cars and furniture), reaching "Business Milestones" rewards the player with Annulith and Solid Dice.

By optimizing your business staff (Espers have specific management traits), you can increase the efficiency of your passive income. Higher Hunter levels unlock more lucrative business opportunities, creating a loop where exploration fuels your business empire, which in turn fuels your gacha pulls.

### Hunter Level and Appraisal Progression

Your Hunter Level is a direct indicator of your progress. Every 5 levels, you can visit the Hunter Association to claim milestone rewards. These rewards are heavily weighted toward Solid Dice, especially in the early game (Levels 5 through 30).

In addition to the Hunter Level, the "Appraisal" system tracks your collection of Arcs and Espers. Increasing your Appraisal rank by leveling up different characters grants one-time rewards of Tri-Keys, which are essential for the Arc Research Program.

### The Mall: Monthly Pull Resets

The Mall is the in-game shop where you can spend various currencies. The "Piece Exchange" section is vital for F2P players. Whenever you pull a duplicate character or item from the gacha, you receive Warp Pieces (for A-Rank/4-star) or Lost Pieces (for S-Rank/5-star).

Every month, the shop resets a limited stock of discounted Solid Dice and Fabricated Dice. You should always prioritize buying these before the monthly reset, as they represent the best "value" for your duplicate currency.

**Monthly Shop Priorities:**
*   **Fabricated Dice (Discounted):** Always buy first.
*   **Solid Dice (Discounted):** Buy second.
*   **Tri-Keys:** Buy if you have excess Warp Pieces.
*   **Arc Enhancement Materials:** Only buy if you have a massive surplus.

### Special Events and Pre-Registration Rewards

As Neverness to Everness is in its early launch window (released April 29, 2026), many players can still benefit from launch-week milestones. The "Urban Arrival" event and other pre-registration bonuses provided a significant head start. If you are a new player, check your in-game mail immediately to claim any lingering Fabricated Dice from these milestones.

Additionally, "Nacupeda's Pool" and "The Witch's House" are special daily interaction points. While they don't always provide Dice, they frequently reward "Mhm! Coins." These coins can be exchanged for S-Rank Arc Disks and unique exploration buffs that make finding Oracle Stones much easier.

Understanding how to get currency is only half the battle; knowing how to spend it effectively within the NTE gacha system is equally important.

### NTE Pity System and the No 50/50 Rule

Neverness to Everness uses a 90-pull hard pity system for its character banners. However, unlike many of its competitors, NTE features a "No 50/50" rule for limited-time banners. This means that when you trigger an S-Rank pull on a limited banner, you are guaranteed to receive the featured character (such as Aurelia or Haniel) rather than a standard pool character.

This system makes Fabricated Dice significantly more valuable than Solid Dice. Because you cannot "lose" the 50/50, you can plan your pulls with much higher precision.

### How the Board Game Summoning System Works

The summoning interface in NTE is styled after a board game. When you spend a Die, you aren't just opening a box; you are moving a piece across a board. Each tile you land on provides rewards. Even if you do not hit the S-Rank character tile immediately, you collect Warp Pieces, Miracle Boxes, and Arc upgrade materials along the way.

This "pity-on-the-way" mechanic ensures that even "unlucky" sessions provide progress toward upgrading your current roster of Espers.

### Spending Guide: Solid Dice vs. Fabricated Dice

You should never spend Annulith on Solid Dice. Solid Dice are given out frequently through Hunter Level rewards and exploration. Annulith should be strictly reserved for purchasing Fabricated Dice to use on limited banners. 

If you are looking for specific high-value targets, characters like Aurelia (for her high DPS potential) and Haniel (for her utility and support capabilities) are currently considered the best investments for your farmed currency.

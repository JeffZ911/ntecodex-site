When players discuss "Disk Sets" in Neverness to Everness (NTE), they are officially referring to the Console system. This is the primary equipment interface for every Esper in your roster. Unlike standard gear systems where you simply equip items into slots, NTE utilizes a grid-based equipment board where spatial management is just as important as the stats themselves.

The core questions for any player looking to optimize their characters are:
*   **What is the Console system?** This is the character equipment grid where you install Cartridges and Modules to gain stat boosts and set bonuses.
*   **How do Cartridges and Modules differ?** Cartridges act as the "brain" of the set, dictating the unique set bonuses, while Modules serve as the "body," providing raw stats and filling the grid to activate those bonuses.
*   **Why does the "Tetris" grid matter?** You cannot simply equip any item you find. Every Module has a specific geometric shape (Type-2, Type-3, or Type-4). ## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/neverness-to-everness-disk-sets-explained/inline-2.webp)


In Neverness to Everness, Disk Sets are managed through the Console system, a equipment grid that combines one central Cartridge with multiple geometric Modules. The Cartridge is the most critical component, as it provides a variable main stat (such as Elemental DMG or Crit Rate) and determines the set bonus. To activate these bonuses, you must successfully slot Modules into the grid. A 2-piece bonus typically requires a specific number of Module blocks to be filled, while a 4-piece bonus requires the grid to be fully optimized according to the Cartridge’s specific "logic" or layout requirements.

A defining feature of NTE is Sub-stat Transparency. When you obtain a Cartridge or Module, all four sub-stats are visible from the moment it drops. There are no hidden rolls or "new" stats that appear as you level the item. Leveling a piece of gear to its maximum of Level 20 only increases the value of the Main Stat, while the sub-stats remain at their initial values. This shifts the focus of the endgame from "rolling" gear to "finding" the perfect initial drop.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/neverness-to-everness-disk-sets-explained/inline-3.webp)


The Console system is the heart of character progression in NTE. Understanding the interplay between the central Cartridge and the supporting Modules is essential for tackling high-level content like the Rabbit Hole Anomaly Zones.

### The Console System: Cartridges vs. Modules

Every Esper has a Console grid. At the start of the game, this grid is relatively small, but it expands as you increase your Hunter Level and character rank.

**Cartridges** are the "Core" of your build. You can only equip one Cartridge per character. 
*   **Rarity:** Cartridges range from B-Rank (Blue) to S-Rank (Gold).
*   **Main Stats:** Cartridges feature randomized main stats. For a DPS character, you are typically looking for Crit Rate, Crit DMG, or specific Elemental DMG (e.g., Anima DMG for [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)).
*   **Set Bonuses:** The Cartridge defines the set. For example, the "Fireflies and the Forest" Cartridge provides the Anima-focused set bonus.

**Modules** are the "Components" that fill the rest of the grid. 
*   **Shapes:** Modules come in various "Tetris" shapes categorized by their block count: Type-2 (2 blocks), Type-3 (3 blocks), and Type-4 (4 blocks).
*   **Fixed Stats:** Unlike Cartridges, Modules have fixed main stats based on their type. Generally, Modules provide Flat ATK and Flat HP to bolster the Esper's base survival and damage.
*   **Sub-stats:** Both Cartridges and Modules come with four sub-stats. Because of the Sub-stat Transparency system, you will know immediately if a Module has the "God Roll" stats you need, such as ATK%, Crit DMG, or Cycle Intensity.

### How to Activate Set Bonuses (The Grid Puzzle)

Activating a set bonus is not as simple as clicking "Equip." Each Cartridge has a set of requirements displayed on the right side of the Console UI.

1.  **2-Piece Bonus:** This is usually activated by simply equipping the Cartridge and filling a minimum number of grid squares with any Modules.
2.  **4-Piece Bonus:** This requires "Grid Logic." The Cartridge will specify a certain configuration or a total number of Module blocks that must be connected to the Cartridge's influence zone within the grid. 

Because the grid has limited space, using Type-4 Modules might give you more raw stats, but their awkward shapes might prevent you from fitting enough Modules to trigger the 4-piece bonus. Conversely, Type-2 Modules are easy to fit but offer lower base stat values. Balancing these shapes is the "Tetris" mini-game of NTE gear.

### Understanding Stats: Main Stats and Sub-stats

When farming for your Disk Sets, you must prioritize the Cartridge's Main Stat first. Below is a breakdown of the potential Main Stats found on Cartridges:

| Stat Type | Description | Recommended For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Elemental DMG** | Increases damage of a specific element (Anima, Aether, etc.) | Main DPS |
| **Crit Rate** | Increases the frequency of critical hits | All Damage Dealers |
| **Crit DMG** | Increases the damage dealt by critical hits | Late-game DPS |
| **Cycle Intensity** | Increases Energy regeneration for Ultimates | Supports and Burst DPS |
| **Break Intensity** | Increases the speed at which enemy shields are broken | Breakers / Stunners |
| **Healing Bonus** | Increases the effectiveness of healing abilities | Healers |

**The Sub-stat Rule:** Since sub-stats do not increase when leveling gear to Level 20, a "Gold" (S-Rank) Cartridge with poor sub-stats may actually be weaker than a "Purple" (A-Rank) Cartridge with perfect sub-stats. Always check the initial four lines before investing Fons (currency) into leveling.

### Character-Specific Module Passives

NTE rewards players for paying attention to specific character synergies within the Console. Certain Espers have "Console Passives" that trigger based on the *type* of Modules you use.

For example, **Nanally** (the Anima DPS featured in "The Ichi-daime" banner) has a unique passive that grants a **+6% Crit Rate bonus** for every **Type-2 Module** equipped in her Console. While Type-2 Modules are smaller and provide less Flat ATK than Type-4 Modules, the Crit Rate bonus from her passive makes a full Type-2 grid much more powerful for her specific kit. Always read the "Abilities" tab of your Esper to see if they have specific Module preferences.

### Farming Guide: Rabbit Hole and the Rewind System

Acquiring the best Disk Sets requires engaging with two different systems:

1.  **Rabbit Hole Anomaly Zones:** This is where you farm **Cartridges**. These combat challenges cost Stamina (Fuel) and drop specific sets on different days of the week. 
    *   **Hunter Level 30:** S-Rank (Gold) Cartridges begin to drop at a low rate.
    *   **Hunter Level 40:** This is the peak efficiency threshold where S-Rank drops become guaranteed or significantly more common.
2.  **The Rewind System:** This is the primary source for **Modules**. The Rewind system is a dedicated gacha-like shop that uses **Carrotas**, a currency earned through daily activities and open-world exploration in Hethereau. You spend Carrotas to "roll" for Modules of specific shapes. This ensures that even if you are unlucky in the Rabbit Hole, you can always acquire the specific Type-2 or Type-4 shapes needed to complete your grid puzzle.

### Optimization Tip: Sub-stat Transparency

Remember that in NTE, the "grind" is front-loaded. Because you see all sub-stats immediately, you should be aggressive about recycling gear. If an S-Rank Cartridge drops with three flat defensive stats, it is usually better to use it as experience material for a piece that has at least two percentage-based offensive stats (ATK%, Crit Rate, etc.). Leveling gear to Level 20 is expensive in terms of Fons, so only invest in pieces that have a strong sub-stat foundation.

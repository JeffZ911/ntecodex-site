# Best 4-Star Disks in Neverness to Everness: Top A-Rank Arcs Guide

In Neverness to Everness (NTE), your character's power is dictated by "Arcs"—the equippable disks that serve as weapons. While the launch of the game on April 29, 2026, introduced several high-tier S-rank Arcs like "Ready-Ready" and "Day Off," most players will rely on A-rank (4-star) Arcs to carry them through the early and mid-game content. These disks are significantly easier to obtain and refine, often providing more value than a base-level 5-star weapon when properly "Mixed" to Rank 5.

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/best-4-star-disks-nte/inline-1.webp)


When building a team in Hethereau, players frequently ask: **What are the best 4-star disks (Arcs) currently available in Neverness to Everness?** Furthermore, F2P players need to know which A-rank weapons provide the highest value for progression and how these disks synergize with specific character types like Solid, Plasma, Gas, Liquid, and Synthesis.

### Defining 'Disks' in NTE: Arcs vs. Cartridges
It is important to distinguish between the two primary gear types in NTE. "Arcs" are the weapons of the game—disks that provide base ATK, a secondary stat (like HP% or ATK%), and a unique passive ability. "Cartridges," on the other hand, are the Tetris-like gear pieces that provide set bonuses and modular stat increases. This guide focuses specifically on Arcs, as they provide the fundamental combat mechanics for your Espers.

### Why 4-Star (A-Rank) Arcs are Essential for Early Game
A-rank Arcs are the backbone of any early-game build. Unlike S-rank Arcs, which typically require high investment in the "Arc Research Program" (the weapon gacha), A-rank Arcs can be reliably obtained through Anomaly Commissions, the Arc Shop, and standard pulls. Because NTE features a "Mixing" system—where duplicate Arcs are used to increase the power of the passive effect—a Rank 5 A-rank Arc will almost always outperform a Rank 1 S-rank Arc in terms of raw stat efficiency and passive utility.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/best-4-star-disks-nte/inline-2.webp)


The current meta for 4-star disks favors Arcs that provide unconditional stat boosts or high-uptime utility. For general DPS, **The Forgotten** (Solid Type) is widely considered the best A-rank disk due to its versatile ATK and DEF scaling. For support and healing roles, **Call of the Twisted City** (Liquid Type) is the premier choice for boosting recovery. While the community often discusses B-rank (3-star) options like **Be Happy** (Gas Type) and **Real Music** (Liquid Type) for their early accessibility, true A-rank power lies in disks that scale effectively into high-level urban anomalies.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/best-4-star-disks-nte/inline-3.webp)


The effectiveness of a disk in Neverness to Everness is not just about its rarity; it is about how well it integrates with an Esper's "Type." Every character in NTE is locked to a specific Arc Type. For example, **Esper Zero** and **Jiuyuan** can only equip Solid-type Arcs, while **[Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)** is restricted to Plasma-type Arcs. Attempting to equip an Arc that does not match the character's type is impossible, making it vital to farm a variety of A-rank disks for your roster.

### Top 4-Star Disks for DPS Characters

For damage dealers, the goal is to maximize ATK and Critical stats.

*   **The Forgotten (Solid Type):** This is the gold standard for Solid-type characters like Zero and [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte). Its passive, "Picture Frame," provides a massive 20% ATK boost at Rank 1 (scaling to 36% at Rank 5) as long as your health is above 50%. If you take a heavy hit and drop below that threshold, the Arc automatically swaps the bonus to DEF, providing a safety net that helps you stay alive until a healer can intervene.
*   **Drawn Blade (Plasma Type):** This is the best 4-star alternative for Nanally if you did not pull her signature S-rank Arc, "Ready-Ready." It provides a straightforward 37.5% ATK increase at maximum level, which is essential for Nanally’s follow-up attack-heavy playstyle.
*   **Watch Your Heads! (Gas Type):** Designed for Gas-type attackers, this disk focuses entirely on Critical Damage. With a 40% CRIT DMG secondary stat, it is the best-in-slot A-rank for characters who rely on high crit-rate builds to clear mobs quickly in the Hethereau streets.

### Top 4-Star Disks for Support and Utility

Support characters require Arcs that either boost their healing output or increase the team's "Break Intensity" to stagger bosses faster.

*   **Call of the Twisted City (Liquid Type):** This is the definitive healing Arc for A-rank users. It is perfectly suited for **Mint** or **Edgar**. By casting a Redirect Skill, the wearer gains a significant Healing Bonus for 10 seconds. In high-level anomalies where attrition is a factor, this 20% boost (at Rank 5) is the difference between a full team wipe and a successful clear.
*   **The Great Thief (Synthesis Type):** This Arc is unique because it rewards mono-type team compositions. It boosts the Break Intensity of all same-type Espers on the team by 70 to 126 points. If you are running a Synthesis-heavy team with characters like **Fadia**, this disk accelerates the "Break" phase of elite enemies significantly.

### Top 4-Star Disks for Sustain and Tanking

*   **The Good, The Bad, The Bitter (Synthesis Type):** This disk is the premier defensive choice for Synthesis characters. Its passive, "Bittercake," triggers a massive DEF boost whenever the character takes damage. While skilled players aim to dodge most attacks, this Arc is invaluable for sustain-focused builds that need to soak up damage while waiting for cooldowns.
*   **Be Happy (Gas Type):** Though technically a B-rank (3-star) Arc, "Be Happy" is often cited in 4-star guides because its Rank 5 performance is so reliable. It restores 12-20% of the wearer's HP upon defeating an enemy. For exploration and clearing "Pink Paw Heist" missions where multiple small enemies are present, this Arc provides infinite sustain.

### How to Obtain and Refine 4-Star Arcs

Acquiring these disks requires a mix of gacha participation and urban exploration.

1.  **Arc Research Program:** This is the dedicated weapon banner. It uses a currency called **Tri-Keys**. A-rank Arcs have a high drop rate here, and you are guaranteed an S-rank Arc every 60 pulls (with a featured "UP" Arc guaranteed every 8 pulls in specific limited programs).
2.  **Arc Shop (The Mall):** You can exchange **U-00NE** (a currency obtained from clearing Anomaly content) for specific A-rank Arcs. This is the most reliable way to get "The Forgotten" or "The Good, The Bad, The Bitter" without relying on luck.
3.  **Anomaly Commissions:** High-risk commissions in Wertheimer Towers often reward A-rank Arcs as first-time clear bonuses.
4.  **Refinement (Mixing):** To reach the maximum potential of a 4-star disk, you must use the "Mixing" system. By consuming a duplicate copy of the same Arc, you increase its Rank (up to Rank 5). This enhances the percentages in the passive effect (e.g., increasing "The Forgotten's" ATK boost from 20% to 36%).

### Transitioning from 4-Star to 5-Star Disks

While A-rank Arcs are powerful, you will eventually want to transition to S-rank (5-star) signature weapons. Signature Arcs like **Ready-Ready** (for Nanally) or **Reality Refuge** (for Jiuyuan) offer unique mechanics that A-ranks cannot replicate, such as summoning companions or creating "Eclipse" zones. However, do not rush to replace a Rank 5 A-rank Arc with a Rank 1 S-rank Arc unless the S-rank's passive provides a specific utility your team desperately needs. The raw 25% HP/ATK substat on a maxed A-rank Arc is often superior to the base stats of a low-level S-rank.

Choosing the right disk is only half the battle. To truly optimize your Espers, you must pair your Arcs with the correct Cartridge sets and team synergies.

### NTE Best 5-Star Arcs Guide
If you have been lucky with your Tri-Key pulls, you may want to look into S-rank options. The current meta-defining Arcs include **Ready-Ready**, which provides massive team-wide ATK buffs, and **Marching Beyond Time**, which is the premier choice for high-speed attackers. For those looking for a free upgrade, the hidden S-rank Arc **Day Off** can be found by completing specific exploration objectives in the "Bridge Crossings" area of Hethereau.

### NTE Cartridge Set Bonuses Explained
Arcs provide the stats, but Cartridges provide the playstyle. The **Shadow Creed** set is the best early-game option for DPS, providing a 10% ATK boost for the 2-piece set and a 25% ATK boost after casting a skill for the 4-piece set. Support characters should look for the **Speedy Hedgehog** set, which increases Charge Efficiency and grants team-wide ATK buffs.

### Character Build Guides: Zero, Nanally, and Sakiri
Every character has a "Signature" Arc that they were designed to use. While A-ranks are great placeholders, knowing the ultimate goal for your favorite Esper is key. For **Esper Zero**, the ultimate goal is **The Rain That Shook the World**, while **[Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide)** benefits most from **Good Boy’s Grand Adventure**.

### The Scarborough Fair: Navigating the Gacha Board

The gacha system in Neverness to Everness, known as Scarborough Fair, departs from traditional "pull" mechanics. It functions as a Monopoly-style board where players use dice to move their avatar across various reward tiles. There are two types of dice: Solid Dice, used for limited-time banners like Nanally’s "The Ichi-daime," and Fabricated Dice, used for the standard permanent banner.

The board contains tiles for Arcs (weapons), upgrade materials, and character shards. Landing on a "Resonance" tile triggers a character recruitment. The pity system is integrated into the board's mechanics:

| Pity Type | Pull Count | Guarantee |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Soft Pity | 70 | Significant increase in S-Class drop rate |
| Hard Pity | 90 | Guaranteed S-Class Character |
| 50/50 Mechanic | N/A | None (Featured S-Class is 100% guaranteed at Hard Pity) |

Because there is no 50/50 chance to lose the featured unit, players can plan their Solid Dice expenditure with 100% certainty. The "No 50/50" rule makes NTE one of the most player-friendly gacha systems currently on the market, as 90 pulls will always yield the specific character advertised on the limited banner.

### Combat Mastery: The Esper Cycle and Parrying

Combat in Hethereau revolves around the interaction of six Esper types. Each character belongs to one of these categories, and their placement on the Esper Wheel determines how they interact with others during a swap.

**The Six Esper Types:**
1.  Lakshana
2.  Cosmos
3.  Anima
4.  Incantation
5.  Chaos
6.  Psyche

Reactions occur when you swap from one element to an adjacent or opposing element on the wheel while the incoming character’s Esper Meter is full. For example, triggering a "Blossom" reaction provides a massive burst of Area of Effect (AoE) damage, while "Discord" can shred enemy defenses.

The most critical skill for any beginner is the parry. Enemies frequently telegraph heavy attacks with a distinct red circle. Timing a dodge or a specific defensive skill during this window results in a parry, which instantly fills the active character's Esper Meter to 100%. This allows for immediate character rotations and constant reaction uptime, which is far more effective than simply spamming basic attacks.

### City Tycoon: Building Your Empire in Hethereau

The City Tycoon system is not just a side activity; it is the primary engine for your account's wealth. Fons, the main currency, is required for everything from car repairs to weapon upgrades. 

Beginners should prioritize investing in cafes and small retail shops early. These businesses provide automated income that accumulates even while you are offline. As you increase your Tycoon Level, you unlock higher-tier rewards and essential character components.

**City Tycoon Level 1-20 Reward Roadmap:**
*   **Level 1-5:** Basic Fons generation and unlock "Rampage Players" shop access.
*   **Level 6-10:** Unlock the "Mistral" starter vehicle and initial customization parts.
*   **Level 11-15:** Access to mid-tier business investments and character duplicate materials (Mind-Links).
*   **Level 18:** **S-Class Character Unlock: Chiz.** This is the most important milestone for F2P players.
*   **Level 20:** Unlock high-tier vehicle performance mods and the "Hethereau Executive" title.

### Vehicle Mechanics: Customization, Summoning, and Maintenance

Vehicles are the primary mode of transportation in Hethereau's sprawling districts. Unlike many other open-world games, cars in NTE have realistic physics and a durability system. If you crash frequently or engage in high-speed street races, your vehicle's durability will deplete. Once it reaches zero, the car becomes unusable until repaired at a garage using Fons.

To mitigate these costs, look for characters with vehicle-related passives. For instance, Jiuyuan possesses a passive ability that reduces repair costs and slightly increases vehicle durability. 

Customization takes place at "Rampage Players" shops located throughout the city. Here, you can modify:
*   **Performance:** Engines, tires, and nitrous systems to improve speed and handling.
*   **Aesthetics:** Paint jobs, body kits, and neon underglow.
*   **Summoning:** You can summon your primary vehicle to your location at any time, provided you are near a road.

### Daily and Weekly Progression Loops (Pixels vs. City Stamina)

Understanding the difference between the two stamina systems is vital for long-term growth.

**Character Pixels (Daily):**
These are used for traditional RPG progression. You spend Pixels to enter anomaly domains that drop character EXP, Arc upgrade materials, and Skill chips. Pixels regenerate over time and have a cap of [information unavailable as of 2026-05-20]. You should aim to spend these every day to ensure no regeneration is wasted. Focus on guaranteed upgrades (Levels and Skills) first. Avoid farming Modules and Consoles (the game's "artifacts") until you reach a higher Hunter Level, as the drop rates for S-Class gear are low in the early game.

**City Stamina (Weekly):**
This stamina is dedicated to the City Tycoon and life-sim elements. It is used for managing businesses, completing urban delivery missions, and participating in street races. Because this pool is managed on a weekly basis, you have more flexibility in when to spend it, but failing to use it before the weekly reset results in a significant loss of Fons and Tycoon EXP.

### Free S-Class Characters and the Starter Selector

NTE is quite generous with its initial character roster. Aside from the aforementioned Chiz at Tycoon Level 18, players are given a "Starter Selector" after completing the first chapter of the main story. This selector allows you to choose one S-Class character from the standard pool.

While the "best" choice depends on your playstyle, most veteran players recommend selecting a character that complements the Esper type of your first 5-star pull from the Scarborough Fair. If you pulled Nanally (Lakshana), look for a character that can trigger a high-damage reaction with her element. 

Additionally, keep an eye on the "Hethereau Welcome" event, which typically provides enough Fabricated Dice to reach the first soft pity on the standard banner within the first week of play. By combining the Starter Selector, the Tycoon Level 18 Chiz, and your first gacha pity, you can have a full team of S-Class characters relatively quickly.

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/nte-how-to-level-up-fast/inline-1.webp)


Players arriving in the anomaly-filled streets of Hethereau often find themselves hitting progression walls where the main story pauses until a specific Hunter Level is reached. Understanding how to bypass these gates is essential for unlocking the full breadth of the game's supernatural and urban content.

### What is the fastest way to increase Hunter Level (account level) in Neverness to Everness?

The fastest progression involves a hybrid approach: clearing every available Main Story Episode while simultaneously exhausting daily Character Pixels and City Stamina. Unlike character-specific levels, Hunter Level represents your overall account progress and determines which world features, such as higher-tier shops and harder Anomaly Zones, are available to you.

### How do I unlock higher character level caps and harder world difficulty?

Character level caps are directly tied to your Appraisal Level. Even if you have enough Hunter EXP to reach level 40, your Espers (characters) will remain stuck at level 40 until you pass an Appraisal Level test. These tests act as "World Tier" promotions that increase the difficulty of enemies in the open world while significantly improving the quality of loot drops from bosses and chests.

### What daily and weekly activities provide the most experience points (EXP) for progression?

Daily Participation Tasks are the most consistent source of Hunter EXP, offering a fixed amount of progress for simple tasks like logging in, completing a delivery, or spending Character Pixels. Weekly activities, including the Anomaly Hunt and City Tycoon milestones, provide larger "lumps" of EXP that are vital for pushing through the later levels (35–50) where the requirement per level increases dramatically.

### Understanding the Hunter Level System

The Hunter Level system in Neverness to Everness functions as the primary gatekeeper for content. It is separate from individual Esper levels. Increasing your Hunter Level unlocks
*   **New Story Episodes:** The narrative is gated behind level milestones (e.g., Level 15, 24, 32).
*   **Anomaly Zones:** Higher-level zones provide better Ascension materials for Arcs (weapons).
*   **Console Tiers:** At Hunter Level 30, Gold (Legendary) Consoles begin to drop, which are the primary way to optimize your team's stats.
*   **City Tycoon Features:** Higher Hunter Levels allow for more complex business management and higher passive income.

### Why Leveling Up Fast Matters in NTE

Speeding through the early levels is not just about bragging rights; it is about resource efficiency. Low-level Anomaly Zones yield Blue or Purple rarity Consoles and Arcs that are eventually replaced. By reaching Hunter Level 30 and 40 quickly, you ensure that the Character Pixels you spend are going toward "Endgame" gear. Furthermore, the S-Rank character Chiz is locked behind the City Tycoon system, and reaching her requires a steady stream of account EXP to unlock the necessary business tiers.

## Direct Answer

The fastest way to level up in Neverness to Everness is to prioritize the completion of Main Story Episodes and the daily exhaustion of your Participation Tasks. By focusing on these two pillars, you can maximize your Hunter EXP gain within the first week of play.

Consistently spending Character Pixels (stamina) in Anomaly Zones provides a steady stream of approximately 1,200 EXP daily. Character Pixels regenerate at a rate of one every six minutes, totaling 240 Pixels per 24-hour period. Spending these on character elevation materials or Arc upgrade components is the most efficient use of your time.

Reaching Hunter Level 30 is the first major milestone for any player. At this point, the game unlocks Legendary (Gold) Console drops in Anomaly Zones. Before this level, players should avoid "farming" Consoles and instead focus on leveling materials. Additionally, progressing the City Tycoon system to Level 18 is essential, as this unlocks the free S-Rank character, Chiz, while providing substantial account EXP bonuses at each level-up milestone.

### The Core Leveling Strategy

The core strategy for rapid progression follows a strict hierarchy
1.  **Main Story:** Always complete "Episode" quests first. They offer the highest EXP-to-time ratio.
2.  **Participation Tasks:** Clear these as soon as you log in. They take less than 10 minutes and provide a massive chunk of your daily level progress.
3.  **Pixel Burn:** Never let your Character Pixels sit at the 240 cap. Even if you don't need specific materials, run an Anomaly Zone for Beetle Coins (gold) to ensure the EXP isn't wasted.
4.  **City Tycoon:** Spend your City Stamina on hobbies to push your Tycoon Level toward the Chiz unlock.

### Key Milestones: Level 30 and Beyond

Once you hit Hunter Level 30, the game's economy shifts. This is the "Console Threshold." You should transition from purely farming character levels to optimizing your gear. At Hunter Level 40, you will face the second Appraisal Test, which significantly increases world difficulty but also makes every chest in Hethereau more valuable. Plan your resource spending so that your main DPS, such as [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or Zero, is ready to hit the new level cap immediately upon passing the test.

### Optimizing Character Pixels and City Stamina

NTE uses two distinct energy systems: Character Pixels and City Stamina. 

**Character Pixels** are used for combat-related rewards. To level up fast, you must spend these every day. The most efficient use is in "Anomaly Hunt" zones. Early on, focus on "Elevation Materials" rather than Consoles. Elevation materials are guaranteed progress, whereas Consoles are subject to RNG (Random Number Generation) and are largely disposable until you can farm the Gold rarity at Hunter Level 30.

**City Stamina** is used for non-combat "Hethereau Hobbies." These include
*   **Racing:** High-speed street races through the neon districts.
*   **Deliveries:** Using your vehicle to transport goods across the city.
*   **Fishing:** A relaxing mini-game found near the city's waterfronts.

Each time you engage in these hobbies, you gain Tycoon EXP. Increasing your Tycoon Level provides a one-time Hunter EXP boost of 300 to 500 points per level.

### City Tycoon: Passive Income and Leveling

The City Tycoon system is more than just a side-hustle; it is a fundamental part of the account leveling loop. By managing businesses and properties in Hethereau, you generate passive income and account EXP.

Reaching Tycoon Level 18 should be your primary goal for the first two weeks of play. Chiz is a powerful Cryo-element DPS who can carry your team through the difficult Appraisal Level 2 and 3 tests.

### Exploration Secrets: Towers, Booths, and Ravens

When you have exhausted your daily stamina, exploration is the only way to continue gaining Hunter EXP. 
*   **Wertheimer Towers:** These act as fast-travel points. Unlocking one grants 100 Hunter EXP. There are dozens scattered across the map, making them a priority for new players.
*   **ReroRero Phone Booths:** These are interactive points that often trigger small side-quests or "Urban Myths." Each booth discovered provides 50 Hunter EXP.
*   **Ravens:** These are collectible supernatural entities hidden in hard-to-reach places (on rooftops, behind billboards). Finding a Raven grants 200 Hunter EXP. Collecting all Ravens in a district provides a massive completion bonus.

### The 'No-Farm' Rule

A common mistake is spending Character Pixels on "Console" (artifact) domains before Hunter Level 30. The drop rate for Gold Consoles is 0% before this milestone. Spending your Pixels on Purple Consoles is considered a waste of resources because the stat difference between Purple and Gold gear is roughly 40%. Instead, use your Pixels to stock up on "Hunter Guides" (EXP items) and "Beetle Coins" so that when you do hit level 30, you can instantly max out your best characters.

### Arc and Console Progression Guide

Arcs (weapons) are the primary source of base Attack and specialized passive effects. Arcs require "Ascension" materials to break past level caps (20, 40, 60). These materials are found in Anomaly Hunt zones. 

Consoles, on the other hand, provide percentage-based stat increases (e.g., % Critical Rate, % Anomaly Damage). You should equip any Purple Consoles you find during exploration, but save your serious "tuning" and Pixels for the Gold Consoles unlocked at Hunter Level 30.

### The Awakening and Resonance System

If you pull duplicate characters from a banner—such as getting a second copy of Nanally from "The Ichi-daime" banner—you receive a "Mind Shard." These shards are used in the Awakening system to unlock six distinct levels of power. 
*   **Awakening Levels 1 & 2:** Usually provide flat stat boosts.
*   **Awakening Levels 3 & 5:** Often enhance the Esper's "Resonance" effect (their ultimate or passive synergy).
*   **Awakening Level 6:** A transformative power spike that can change how a character is played.

Focus your early resources on a single main DPS.

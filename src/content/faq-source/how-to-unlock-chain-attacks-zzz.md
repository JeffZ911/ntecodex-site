# How to Unlock and Use Chain Attacks in Zenless Zone Zero (ZZZ)

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/how-to-unlock-chain-attacks-zzz/inline-1.webp)


Players arriving in New Eridu often wonder exactly when they can start performing the flashy, slow-motion character swaps seen in gameplay trailers. Specifically, many ask: What are the requirements to unlock Chain Attacks in ZZZ? Is there a specific character level or Inter-Knot Rank required, or is it tied to the main story?

The game introduces this combat mechanic very early, but the tutorial can be fast-paced, leading to confusion about the specific button prompts and conditions. You do not need specific S-Rank characters to perform a Chain Attack, but you must have at least two Agents in your active squad. This guide clarifies the timing of the unlock and the mechanical triggers necessary to execute these attacks consistently.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-unlock-chain-attacks-zzz/inline-2.webp)


Chain Attacks are unlocked automatically during the Prologue tutorial of Zenless Zone Zero, specifically during the first few combat encounters in the Hollow. You do not need to reach a specific Inter-Knot level to access this feature; it is a core component of the combat system provided to all players from the start of the game.

To trigger a Chain Attack, you must follow these specific steps:
1.  **Fill the Daze Meter:** Attack an enemy until the yellow bar beneath their health pool reaches 100%.
2.  **Enter the Stunned State:** Once the meter is full, the enemy will become immobilized and enter a "Stunned" state.
3.  **Land a Heavy Attack:** Hit the Stunned enemy with a move classified as a "Heavy Attack." This includes the final hit of most Basic Attack strings, EX Special Attacks, or Dodge Counters.
4.  **Select a Teammate:** A slow-motion UI will appear at the bottom of the screen. Click the icon of another Agent in your squad before the timer expires to switch them in for a powerful strike.

**Quick Reference: Chain Attack Trigger Conditions**

| Requirement | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Squad Size** | At least 2 Agents (or 1 Agent + 1 Bangboo) |
| **Enemy Condition** | Daze Meter must be at 100% (Stunned) |
| **Trigger Move** | Must land a "Heavy Attack" on the Stunned target |
| **Input Window** | Approximately 3-5 seconds (indicated by a shrinking circular timer) |

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-unlock-chain-attacks-zzz/inline-3.webp)


The Chain Attack system is the primary way players cycle through their team while maintaining offensive pressure. Unlike manual switching, Chain Attacks provide invincibility frames (i-frames) and deal significant burst damage, often applying elemental anomalies more rapidly than standard attacks.

### Understanding the Daze Meter and Stun State

Every enemy in Zenless Zone Zero, from basic Thugs to massive Ethereal bosses, possesses a Daze meter. This is the yellow bar located directly below the enemy's HP bar. As you land attacks, this bar fills up.

The rate at which this bar fills is governed by an Agent's **Impact** stat. Agents classified as "Stun" units, such as Anby Demara or Lycaon, have naturally high Impact stats and skill kits designed specifically to maximize Daze buildup. Once the meter hits 100%, the enemy is Stunned. In this state, the enemy takes increased damage (usually a 150% multiplier, which can be further increased by specific Agent passives). The Stun state lasts for a limited duration, during which the Daze meter gradually decays back to zero.

### Identifying Heavy Attacks to Trigger the Chain

Simply stunning an enemy is not enough to start a Chain Attack; you must "break" them further with a Heavy Attack. While the game does not always label these moves clearly in the heat of battle, they follow a predictable pattern across all character kits.

**List: Every Move Type that Counts as a Heavy Attack**
*   **Basic Attack Finishers:** The final hit in an Agent’s standard combo string (usually the 3rd, 4th, or 5th hit).
*   **EX Special Attacks:** The enhanced version of an Agent's Special Attack that consumes Energy.
*   **Dodge Counters:** The attack performed immediately after a Perfect Dodge.
*   **Chain Attacks:** Each hit within a Chain Attack sequence itself counts as a Heavy Attack, allowing the chain to continue to the next Agent.
*   **Ultimate Attacks:** Activating an Ultimate on a Stunned enemy will trigger the Chain Attack UI if the chain limit has not been reached.

### Chain Attack Limits: Normal, Elite, and Boss Ethereals

You cannot chain attacks indefinitely on a single Stunned enemy. The number of times you can trigger a Chain Attack during a single Stun window depends entirely on the "rank" or "weight" of the enemy you are fighting.

**Table: Chain Attack Limits by Enemy Category**

| Enemy Type | Max Chain Attacks | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Normal** | 1 | Small enemies (e.g., basic Ethereal strikers, scavengers). |
| **Elite** | 2 | Larger enemies with more complex move sets and higher HP. |
| **Boss** | 3 | Major story bosses or Notorious Hunt targets. |

If you are fighting a Boss, you can cycle through your entire three-person squad in a single Stun window. If you are fighting a Normal enemy, the first Chain Attack you trigger will be the only one available before the enemy recovers or is defeated.

### How to Include Bangboos in Your Chain Combo

Bangboos are not just cosmetic pets; they are active participants in combat. If you have a Bangboo equipped, they can participate in Chain Attacks under specific conditions. In a standard three-person squad, the Bangboo acts as an "extra" slot.

When fighting a Boss (which allows for 3 Chain Attacks), you can choose your Bangboo as one of the participants. The Bangboo’s attack does not count toward the "Agent" limit in the same way, effectively allowing you to squeeze in extra damage and utility (such as healing or elemental buildup) without sacrificing a character's turn. To include them, simply click the Bangboo icon when it appears in the selection UI during the slow-motion window.

### Manual vs. Auto Chain Attack Settings

With the release of Patch 1.1, HoYoverse introduced a Quality of Life (QoL) feature that allows players to choose how Chain Attacks are triggered. This is particularly useful for high-level players who want precise control over their rotations.

*   **Auto Mode (Default):** Landing a Heavy Attack on a Stunned enemy automatically brings up the Chain Attack UI. Any subsequent attack input (like clicking the left mouse button or tapping the screen) will automatically select the next Agent in the queue.
*   **Manual Mode:** Landing a Heavy Attack will still bring up the UI, but the game will not automatically pick the next character. You must specifically click the left or right character icon (or use the corresponding controller buttons) to trigger the swap. If you do not select a character, the Chain Attack will not occur, allowing you to stay on your current Agent to continue a specific combo.

To change this setting, navigate to the **Options** menu, select the **Other** tab, and look for the **Chain Attack Toggle**.

## Related

![Related](/img/how-to-unlock-chain-attacks-zzz/inline-4.webp)


Understanding how to unlock and trigger these attacks is only the first step. To maximize your efficiency in the Hollows, you must understand how Chain Attacks interact with other combat systems like the Decibel system and squad positioning.

### The Importance of the Impact Stat and Stun Agents

While every Agent can contribute to the Daze meter, Stun Agents are the engines that drive Chain Attacks. Characters like Anby, Koleda, and Lycaon have kits that multiply Daze generation. For example, Qingyi (an Electric Stun Agent) can increase the Stun DMG multiplier beyond the base 150%, making the subsequent Chain Attacks deal massive damage.

When building a team, you generally want one Stun Agent whose sole job is to fill that yellow bar as fast as possible. Once they trigger the Chain Attack, you switch to your Support or DPS (Attack) Agents to capitalize on the vulnerability window.

### Perfect Assists and Defensive Maneuvers

Chain Attacks are often confused with **Perfect Assists**. A Perfect Assist occurs when you switch characters just as an enemy attack is about to land (indicated by a gold flash). 

*   **Defensive Assist:** Your character parries the attack, consuming an Assist Point.
*   **Evasive Assist:** Your character dodges in slow-motion, consuming an Assist Point.

While Perfect Assists are a great way to build Daze and switch characters safely, they do not trigger the Chain Attack UI. Chain Attacks are strictly tied to the Stunned state of the enemy, whereas Assists are tied to the enemy's offensive actions.

### Optimizing Team Order for Maximum Damage

The order in which your Agents appear in the Chain Attack UI is determined by your squad setup (Slot 1, Slot 2, and Slot 3). During a Chain Attack, you are usually given a choice between the "Next" and "Previous" Agent in your lineup.

To maximize damage, you should aim to have your **Attack Agent** (your main DPS) be the final person to strike in the chain. This is because the final hit of a Chain Attack sequence usually has the highest damage multiplier and leaves that character on the field to continue attacking while the enemy is still Stunned.

**Comparison Table: Chain Attacks vs. Ultimate Attacks**

| Feature | Chain Attack | Ultimate Attack |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Resource Used** | Daze Meter (Enemy-based) | Decibel Rating (Squad-based) |
| **Trigger** | Heavy Attack on Stunned enemy | Pressing the Ultimate button at 3,000 Decibels |
| **Damage** | High Burst | Massive AoE/Single Target |
| **Invincibility** | Yes (during animation) | Yes (during animation) |
| **Frequency** | Every time an enemy is Stunned | Once per 3,000 Decibels earned |

By mastering the transition from building Daze with a Stun Agent to executing a full 3-person Chain Attack ending with an Attack Agent, you will be able to clear Shiyu Defense stages and high-intensity [Hollow Zero](https://ntecodex.com/guides/zzz-3-0-hollow-zero-tactical-buff-comparison) runs with much greater ease.

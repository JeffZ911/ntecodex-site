These Duo Reactions—such as Blossom, Scorch, and Nova—provide the primary source of burst damage in late-game content. Every character kit is standardized into four main components: Basic Attacks, a Special Skill, an Ultimate Ability, and a high-impact Intro Skill that activates immediately upon swapping through the Esper Cycle.

## Detailed Explanation

The depth of Neverness to Everness lies in how individual character kits interact with the global Esper System. Success depends on team composition, specifically how elements align on the Esper Wheel and how efficiently a player can cycle through their three-person squad.

### The Esper System and Elemental Reactions

The six Esper elements are not merely aesthetic; they dictate the flow of combat through Duo Reactions. When you swap characters using the Esper Cycle, the element of the outgoing character reacts with the element of the incoming character.

*   **Lakshana:** Often associated with physical stability and light-based effects.
*   **Cosmos:** Focuses on spatial manipulation and gravity-based crowd control.
*   **Anima:** Centered on biological growth and nature-based status effects.
*   **Incantation:** High-velocity magic and fire-based destruction.
*   **Chaos:** Debuff-heavy elements focusing on void energy and disruption.
*   **Psyche:** Mental energy used for buffs and tactical field manipulation.

The interaction between these elements produces six core Duo Reactions:

| Reaction Name | Element Combination | Primary Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blossom** | Anima + Lakshana | Creates a healing field that also deals periodic AoE damage. |

### Playable Character Database (Launch Roster)

Each character in NTE is designed to fit a specific niche within a team. While rarity often dictates raw base stats, the utility of A-Rank characters in specific elemental teams makes them viable even in high-tier Anomalous Zones.

#### Nanally (S-Rank, Incantation)
Nanally is the premier Damage dealer for Incantation-based teams. Her kit focuses on the "Underboss" mechanic, where her Special Skill triggers follow-up attacks from spectral associates. Her Ultimate Ability covers a massive area in Incantation flames, making her the top choice for clearing waves of smaller enemies. She is best paired with Anima or Chaos units to trigger Scorch or Hexed reactions.

#### Sakiri (S-Rank, Anima)
Sakiri functions as a hybrid between a Damage dealer and a Break Boost specialist. Her "Devour Whole" ability allows her to group enemies together before unleashing a high-impact strike that heavily depletes the enemy's Break Gauge. Sakiri is essential for boss fights where "Breaking" the opponent is required to bypass high-damage resistance phases.

#### Chiz (S-Rank, Psyche)
Chiz introduces the "Grain Market" economy mechanic. During combat, Chiz generates a unique resource that can be spent to enhance her Special Skill or saved to provide a passive damage buff to the entire team. This makes her a flexible Support/Damage hybrid who rewards players for managing her resources effectively.

#### Mint (A-Rank, Lakshana)
As the primary Survival unit available at launch, Mint focuses on keeping the team alive. Her Intro Skill provides an immediate shield to the active character, while her Ultimate Ability creates a Lakshana-infused zone that heals all allies within its radius.

### Skill Analysis: Active, Ultimate, and Intro Abilities

Every character in NTE follows a specific skill hierarchy. Understanding when to use each is key to maintaining a high damage-per-second (DPS) output.

1.  **Basic Attacks:** These are your primary tool for building the Esper Meter. Most characters have a 4-to-6 hit combo chain.
2.  **Special Skill (Active):** These have a short cooldown (usually 8 to 15 seconds) and provide character-specific utility, such as Nanally's follow-up strikes or Sakiri's crowd control.
3.  **Ultimate Ability:** These require energy generated through combat. Ultimates usually pause the game timer briefly and deliver a cinematic, high-damage attack or a team-wide buff.
4.  **Intro Skill:** This is the most important skill for high-level play. When the Esper Meter is full and you swap characters, the incoming character performs an Intro Skill. This skill usually deals high damage and is the trigger for the Duo Reactions mentioned earlier.

### Advanced Combat Mechanics: Parrying and Breaking

Beyond just pressing skills, NTE rewards mechanical skill through two specific systems: Parrying and Breaking.

**The Esper Cycle Meter**
The meter required to swap characters and trigger Intro Skills fills at different rates based on your actions:
*   **Parries:** Successfully timing a block against an incoming attack provides an instant, significant fill to the Esper Meter.
*   **Dodge Counters:** Dodging at the last second and following up with a Basic Attack provides a fast fill.
*   **Standard Skills:** Using your Special Skill provides a moderate fill.

**The Break System**
Elite enemies and bosses possess a secondary bar beneath their health known as the Break Gauge. By using characters with the "Break Boost" role (like Sakiri), you can deplete this bar. Once the bar reaches zero, the enemy enters a "Broken" state, where they take [exact value pending official confirmation] increased damage and remain incapacitated for several seconds. This is the optimal window to unleash all Ultimate Abilities and cycle through Duo Reactions.

While every character is viable, the current meta heavily favors S-Rank units like Nanally for her raw DPS and Chiz for her team-wide utility. ### Weapon and Module Guide
Espers are equipped with "Arcs" (the game's weapon equivalent) and "Modules" (the artifact/gear system). Arcs are element-specific, meaning an Incantation character like Nanally must equip an Incantation-type Arc to receive stat bonuses. Modules provide set bonuses, such as increasing Break Efficiency or boosting Duo Reaction damage.

### Urban Life Guide
Outside of combat, your characters possess "Life Skills." These are used in the city of Hethereau for various activities:
*   **Racing:** Certain characters have higher handling stats for the city's street racing circuits.
*   **Delivery:** Speed-based characters excel at the courier mini-games.
*   **City Tycoon:** Characters like Chiz provide bonuses to business management and passive income generation when assigned to urban properties.

### Gacha Guide
Neverness to Everness uses a standard 90-pull pity system for S-Rank characters. The debut banner for the launch period is "The Ichi-daime," featuring Nanally as the rate-up S-Rank unit. Players are guaranteed an S-Rank within 90 pulls, with a 50% chance of it being the featured banner character. If the first S-Rank pulled is not the featured unit, the next S-Rank is guaranteed to be the featured character.

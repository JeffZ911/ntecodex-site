If you are coming from other open-world gacha titles, you are likely looking for your "Union Level" or "Adventure Rank." In Neverness to Everness (NTE), this system is officially titled the **Hunter Level**. Your Hunter Level represents your overall account progress and determines which story chapters you can access, the maximum level your characters can reach, and the quality of loot dropped by enemies.

The Hunter Level works in tandem with the **Appraisal Level**, which functions as the world difficulty setting. As your Hunter Level increases, you will eventually hit "caps" that require you to complete specific Elevation Quests to raise your Appraisal Level. Raising the Appraisal Level makes enemies tougher but significantly improves the drop rates for high-tier Arcs and Consoles.

Many players use the term "Union Level" interchangeably with Hunter Level because NTE shares many structural similarities with urban supernatural RPGs. However, when navigating the in-game menus or the Exploration Guide, you should always look for the "Hunter" designation to track your experience points (EXP).

### What is Hunter Level in NTE?
Hunter Level is the numerical value that tracks your account-wide experience. Unlike individual character levels—which are increased using specialized EXP materials—the Hunter Level increases through almost every activity you perform in the city of Hethereau. It serves as the primary gatekeeper for the game's "Episode" (Main Story) progression. If you find yourself unable to start a new story mission, it is almost certainly because your Hunter Level has not yet met the requirement.

### Why You Need to Increase Your Level Fast
The primary reason to push your Hunter Level early is to unlock the "Legendary" (Orange-tier) gear. In NTE, gear is divided into Consoles and Arcs. High-level Anomaly Zones, which drop these items, are locked behind specific Hunter Level milestones. Furthermore, your characters are subject to level caps based on your Hunter Level. If you want to take your [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or Chiz to level 60 and beyond, you must first raise your account's Hunter Level to the corresponding tier.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-increase-nte-union-level-guide/inline-3.webp)


### Mastering the Daily Routine
The Participation Tasks system is your most reliable source of Hunter EXP. Every day at server reset, you are given a list of objectives. These are generally simple tasks such as "Daily Login," "Earn 10,000 Fons," or "Complete 1 Anomaly Zone." 

Each task grants "Participation Points." As you reach milestones on the 100-point progress bar, you unlock chests containing Hunter EXP, Fons, and premium currency. Completing the full bar typically yields around 1,200 EXP. Because this EXP is "free" (it does not require Character Pixels), it is the foundation of your leveling strategy.

### Spending Character Pixels: Priority Farming
Character Pixels are the stamina currency in Neverness to Everness. You regenerate 1 Pixel every 6 minutes, totaling 240 Pixels over a 24-hour period. Every activity that costs Pixels rewards Hunter EXP at a fixed ratio.

To level up efficiently, you should focus your Pixels on "Houdini's Schemes." These are combat challenges found within Anomaly Zones that provide character ascension materials and Arc enhancement chips. Early on, do not worry about "perfect" gear; simply spend your Pixels to ensure your Hunter Level keeps moving upward. If you are short on time, you can use the "Multi-Sweep" function (if unlocked) to spend Pixels instantly after clearing a zone once.

### The Tycoon Level Shortcut
NTE features a robust City Tycoon system where you manage businesses like cafes and delivery services across Hethereau. While this system is primarily for earning Fons (the main currency) and passive income, it is a hidden goldmine for Hunter EXP.

Every time you increase your Tycoon Rank by completing business objectives or decorating your properties, you receive a "Rank Up Reward." These rewards almost always include a substantial chunk of Hunter EXP. If you find yourself 500 EXP short of the next Hunter Level, checking your Tycoon tasks is often faster than hunting for chests in the overworld.

### Urban Exploration: Ravens and Towers
Hethereau is a dense urban environment filled with collectibles. The two most important for leveling are **Oracle Ravens** and **Towers**.
*   **Oracle Ravens:** These are supernatural crows perched on rooftops, balconies, and tucked-away alleys. They are marked with a blue diamond icon when you get close. Interacting with an Oracle Raven grants 200 Hunter EXP. There are dozens scattered across the districts, making them an excellent way to "grind" levels without spending Pixels.
*   **Towers and Phone Booths:** These serve as fast-travel points. Activating a new Tower or repairing a Phone Booth provides 100 EXP. Always prioritize unlocking these as soon as you enter a new district like the Central Business District or the Waterfront.

### Appraisal Level Elevation Quests
Upon reaching Hunter Levels 20, 40, and 50, your EXP bar will turn orange and stop filling. This indicates that you have hit a world cap. To continue leveling, you must navigate to the "Quests" menu and track the **Elevation Quest**.

These quests usually involve a timed combat trial against a boss or a wave of elite enemies. Completing the trial increases your Appraisal Level. While this makes the open world more dangerous, it is mandatory for progression. If you find the trial too difficult, ensure your main DPS character—such as Nanally from "The Ichi-daime" banner—has their Arcs and Consoles leveled to the current maximum.

### The Level 30 Gear Threshold
Many veteran players recommend "rushing" to Hunter Level 30. This is because the efficiency of your Character Pixels increases dramatically at this stage. Before Level 30, Anomaly Zones mostly drop Purple (Epic) or Blue (Rare) gear. Once you hit Level 30 and complete the necessary Elevation Quest, Legendary gear begins to appear in the loot tables. Saving some of your "Pixel Restoratives" (consumable stamina items) until you hit Level 30 is a common strategy to jump-start your endgame build.

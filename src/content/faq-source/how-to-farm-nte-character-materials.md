## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-farm-nte-character-materials/inline-2.webp)


Character materials in NTE are primarily farmed using **Character Pixels**, which serves as the combat-focused stamina system. Unlike some other open-world RPGs, NTE separates your combat energy from your city-life energy (City Stamina), allowing you to focus your Character Pixels entirely on Esper growth.

To level up your characters, you must acquire **Hunter Guides** (EXP items) found at **Houdinii's Magic Stage**. As you reach level caps (starting at Level 20), you will need to perform an Ascension. This requires a combination of the **Whisper** series common drops—found by defeating enemies in the open world or Anomaly Zones—and unique boss drops obtained from **Anomaly Hunts**. For example, ascending an Esper like Hotori requires the **Confessional Flower Seed**, which drops from the world boss **Serenetti**.

For skill progression, players must farm **Esper Ability Books** (such as *The Olive Branch* or *Dove's Flutter*) at **Houdinii's Schemes**. These stages operate on a daily rotation, so planning your week is essential. Finally, **Beetle Coins** are the mandatory currency for all combat-related upgrades, while **Fons** are used for city-based progression and purchasing rare materials from the Mall or Auction House.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-farm-nte-character-materials/inline-3.webp)


Efficiently farming in Neverness to Everness requires a deep understanding of how the game gates progression and how stamina regenerates. Because Character Pixels are a finite resource, wasting them on low-efficiency stages can stall your progress for days.

### Stamina Management: Character Pixels vs. City Stamina

NTE utilizes a dual-stamina system to prevent "progression burnout." 
1.  **Character Pixels:** These regenerate at a rate of **1 every 6 minutes**, totaling **240 Pixels per day**. This stamina is used for Houdinii's stages, Anomaly Hunts, and Weekly Bosses.
2.  **City Stamina:** This is used for "Hethereau Hobbies," such as car racing, managing businesses, or working part-time jobs to earn Fons.

You should aim to spend your Character Pixels every day to prevent them from hitting the 240 cap. If you are short on time, prioritize Houdinii’s Magic Stage, as it provides the most consistent value for your stamina.

### Currency Farming: Beetle Coins vs. Fons

In Neverness to Everness, you are essentially managing two different bank accounts.
*   **Beetle Coins:** Used for leveling characters, ascending Arcs, and upgrading talents. If you run out, you can farm the "Golden Vault" stage at Houdinii's Magic Stage.
*   **Fons:** Used for the "City Tycoon" side of the game. You need Fons to buy new cars, upgrade your apartment, and purchase certain rare materials from the **Mall**. Fons are earned through City Stamina activities and are not tied to your combat progression stamina.

### Weekly Bosses and Anomaly Pilgrimage

Once a week, you should clear the **Anomaly Pilgrimage** bosses. these require a higher amount of Character Pixels (usually 60) but provide rare "Advanced Talent" materials and a chance at S-Rank Consoles. These rewards are limited to three collections per week, so do not miss them.

**Consoles** and **Modules** are the "artifact" equivalent in NTE. Once you reach Hunter Level 30, you should begin farming the **Rabbit Hole**. This is where you can obtain S-Rank Consoles with randomized sub-stats. Farming the Rabbit Hole is the primary endgame "stamina sink" for veteran players.

### The Hunter Exchange and Mall Shops

If you find yourself short on a specific material and out of Character Pixels, check the **Hunter Exchange**. Here, you can spend **Lost Pieces** (obtained from the gacha system) to buy Hunter Guides, Whisper materials, and even Esper Ability Books.

The **Mall** in Hethereau also features a rotating stock of materials that can be purchased with **Fons**. This is a great way to use your "City Life" progress to speed up your "Combat Life" progress.

### Life Skills and Material Bonuses

Engaging in the **City Tycoon** mechanics can provide passive bonuses to your farming. Certain "Life Skills" unlocked through Hethereau Hobbies can increase the drop rate of common world materials or provide a small percentage chance to double the output when synthesizing materials at the **Hunter Exchange** crafting bench. For example, higher "Chemistry" life skill levels allow you to convert **Lost Whispers** into **Obscure Whispers** with higher efficiency.

### The Console and Cartridge System Explained

In Neverness to Everness, the gearing system for Espers is divided into Modules and Cartridges, collectively referred to as Disks. Unlike traditional gear systems where you simply equip an item in a slot, NTE uses a "Console" grid. This grid acts like a puzzle where you place different-shaped Modules to activate set bonuses.

Each Esper has a Console with a specific layout. Cartridges are the "software" that provides the set bonuses, while Modules are the "hardware" shapes you place on the grid. To activate a 4-piece set bonus like Thea’s Night Tavern, you must place four Modules that all belong to that specific Cartridge set. Because space on the Console is limited, players must balance the shape of the Modules (L-shapes, squares, or straight lines) to fit the maximum number of high-tier stats while maintaining the set synergy.

### Top Pick: Thea’s Night Tavern (Pure Healing)

For players struggling with the higher floors of the Hethereau containment zones, Thea’s Night Tavern is the definitive choice. Most healers in NTE, including the starter healer Jiuyuan and the premium support Edgar, have skills that scale directly with their Max HP. 

The 2-piece bonus of 10% HP provides a solid foundation for this scaling. The 4-piece bonus, a 20% Healing Bonus, is a multiplicative stat that applies after all other HP calculations. This makes it significantly more effective than stacking more HP% in the late game. In high-difficulty boss fights where "Anomaly Leaks" can drain your team's health rapidly, the raw output from this set is often the only way to keep the party standing.

### The Meta Choice: Speedy Hedgehog (Team Buffing)

As players reach the endgame and their characters become better invested, the need for raw healing often diminishes in favor of clear speed. This is where Speedy Hedgehog shines. 

The 2-piece bonus provides a 12% boost to Charge Efficiency. This is arguably the most important stat for a healer because it allows them to use their Ultimate (their primary source of massive healing) more frequently. The 4-piece bonus provides a 15% ATK buff to the entire team for 12 seconds following an Ultimate. Since top-tier healers like Edgar can maintain high Ultimate uptime, this buff can be active for a significant portion of the fight, directly contributing to the team’s total damage output.

### Character Build: Edgar (Dedicated Healer)

Edgar is currently the premier dedicated healer in NTE. His kit revolves around his "Wild Current" skill and his Ultimate, which provides sustained recovery. However, Edgar has a unique nuance: his "Wild Current" skill scales off the *ally's* Max HP rather than his own for a portion of the heal, while his Ultimate scales off his own Max HP.

To optimize Edgar:
*   **Disk Set:** Speedy Hedgehog (Meta) or Thea’s Night Tavern (Survival).
*   **Main Stat Priority:** Healing Bonus (Circuit 4), HP% (Circuit 2), Charge Efficiency (Circuit 3).
*   **Sub-stats:** Charge Efficiency > HP% > Flat HP.

By focusing on Charge Efficiency, Edgar ensures that his Ultimate is always ready to save a teammate from a lethal blow.

### Optimizing the Tetris Console for Supports

The Console system can be frustrating for new players. To optimize your healer's grid:
*   **Prioritize Type IV Modules:** These usually carry the most potent main stats like Healing Bonus.
*   **Shape Management:** Don't be afraid to use a lower-rarity Module if its shape allows you to complete a 4-piece set bonus. A Blue (B-rank) Module that completes the Speedy Hedgehog set is often better than an Orange (S-rank) Module that breaks the set.
*   **Symmetry:** Most healer Consoles have a central core. Place your highest-priority stats there to benefit from any "Core Resonance" bonuses your specific Esper might have.

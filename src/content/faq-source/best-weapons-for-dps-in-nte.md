Optimizing your damage output in the supernatural urban sprawl of Hethereau requires more than just high-level Espers; you need the right Arcs to scale your combat efficiency. This guide breaks down the best weapons for DPS in Neverness to Everness (NTE) to help you dominate the current May 2026 meta.

# What are the Best Weapons for DPS in Neverness to Everness?

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/best-weapons-for-dps-in-nte/inline-1.webp)


Choosing the right weapon, or Arc, is the most significant factor in determining your team's damage floor and ceiling. In Neverness to Everness, weapons are not merely stat sticks; they are complex mechanical components that define how an Esper interacts with the battlefield. As of May 21, 2026, the meta has solidified around a few key S-Rank Arcs that provide transformative power spikes.

Identifying the highest damage-dealing weapons involves looking beyond base Attack (ATK) values and focusing on how passives scale with specific character archetypes. The rarity of an Arc—S-Rank versus A-Rank—dictates the potential for stat growth, with S-Rank Arcs typically capping at significantly higher base stats at level 80. However, the true power lies in the Arc type (Plasma, Solid, Liquid, Gas, or Synthesis) and how it matches your Esper’s role.

Furthermore, weapon passives like "Silent Garden" or "Officer Whisker" are not just flavor text. These effects provide off-field damage, massive critical modifiers, or boss-specific execution mechanics that can shorten encounter times by half. Understanding the synergy between these passives and your character's kit is the difference between struggling against Anomaly bosses and clearing them with ease.

### What Makes a Weapon 'Best' for DPS?
A top-tier DPS weapon in NTE is defined by its substat and its passive multiplier. While ATK% is a solid foundation, late-game scaling favors Arcs that provide CRIT Rate or CRIT DMG substats. These stats allow you to focus your Cartridge and Module farming on other areas, making your build more flexible. Additionally, a "best" weapon must have a passive that triggers reliably within a character's rotation. If a passive requires staying on the field for 10 seconds but your DPS is a quick-swap burst dealer, that Arc is suboptimal regardless of its rarity.

### The Role of Arcs in the NTE Combat System
Arcs function as the primary equipment for Espers, categorized by elemental or physical archetypes. You cannot equip a Plasma Arc on a Liquid Esper, which makes the hunt for specific high-tier Arcs even more critical. Each Arc also features a "Mixing" level, which is the NTE equivalent of refinement. Increasing Mixing Intensity from T1 to T5 significantly boosts the percentages of the unique passive effects, often doubling the damage bonus provided by the Arc's signature skill.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/best-weapons-for-dps-in-nte/inline-2.webp)


The best DPS weapons in NTE are currently S-Rank Arcs, with **Ready-Ready** and **Camellia Society** leading the rankings for their respective types. **Ready-Ready** is the undisputed best-in-slot (BiS) for Plasma-type DPS characters like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte), offering a combination of raw ATK buffs, Critical Riposte multipliers, and a unique boss-slaying mechanic known as the Commander Tiger Talisman.

For Synthesis-type Espers, **Camellia Society** is the premier choice. It rewards high-risk gameplay by granting explosive CRIT DMG stacks when the user’s HP is manipulated, making it the perfect match for characters like Jiuyuan and Baicang. Meanwhile, **Fluff of Fleetness** remains the gold standard for Liquid-type carries who stay on the field for extended durations, thanks to its stacking ATK buff.

For players who are Free-to-Play (F2P) or haven't had luck with the Arc Research Program banners, A-Rank alternatives like **Dangerous Game** and **First Step to Success** offer the best damage-to-investment ratio. These Arcs are easier to reach T5 Mixing Intensity, often outperforming a T1 S-Rank Arc in the early-to-mid game.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/best-weapons-for-dps-in-nte/inline-3.webp)


The current power gap between S-Tier and A-Tier Arcs in NTE is largely due to the unique "Arc Skills" that only S-Rank weapons possess. These skills are not just stat adjustments; they are active or semi-active combat triggers that fundamentally change your damage output.

### S-Tier DPS Arcs: Mechanics and Passives

**Ready-Ready (Plasma)**
This Arc is designed for the current meta-queen, Nanally. Its passive, **Commander Tiger Talisman**, functions through a two-step trigger. Using a Redirect Skill grants a "Left Tiger Talisman," while an Ultimate grants a "Right Tiger Talisman." If both are obtained within 15 seconds, you unlock the full Commander effect, which increases damage dealt to boss units by 10% (at T1). Additionally, it provides a base 15% ATK increase and a 15% buff to Basic Attack and Critical Riposte damage. For a follow-up specialist like Nanally, these layers of multipliers are incomparable.

**Camellia Society (Synthesis)**
This is the highest-scaling Synthesis weapon in the game. Its **Silent Garden** passive increases CRIT DMG by 12% whenever the wearer’s HP is reduced without taking direct damage (e.g., through kit-based HP consumption), stacking up to 4 times. At max stacks, you are looking at a 48% CRIT DMG boost. Furthermore, it triggers random high-damage Incantation bursts:
*   **Harsh Rebuke:** 5 instances of 24% ATK damage.
*   **Incessant Nagging:** 6 instances of 18% ATK damage, with the final hit dealing double damage.

**Fluff of Fleetness (Liquid)**
Ideal for "selfish" carries like Mint, the **Motor Candy** effect rewards field time. It grants a 5% ATK boost every second the wearer is active, stacking up to 5 times (25% total ATK). At Mixing Intensity T5, this increases to 9% per second (45% total ATK). The only downside is that the buff resets immediately upon switching characters, making it strictly for Main DPS roles.

### Understanding Arc Types and Synergy
Matching Arc types is mandatory. An Esper like Nanally, who utilizes Plasma/Anima attributes, can only equip Plasma Arcs. This system prevents players from simply moving one "god-tier" weapon between every character. When building your team, you must consider the availability of Arcs. If you have two Synthesis characters but only one **Camellia Society**, you will need to invest in a **Dangerous Game** or another Synthesis alternative to ensure both characters can perform.

### How to Maximize DPS via Enhancement and Mixing
To reach the level 80 cap and maximize your weapon's potential, you must engage with two core systems: Enhancement and Mixing.

1.  **Enhancement (Leveling):** Arcs require **Dye** to level up. This material is primarily farmed in the **Kaleidoscope** stage of Anomaly Zones. Higher levels increase the Base ATK and the value of the substat (like CRIT Rate).
2.  **Ascension:** Every 20 levels, you must ascend the Arc using materials found in the **Bubble Can Factory** or dropped by specific world bosses like the Serenetti Anomaly.
3.  **Mixing (Refining):** This is the most potent way to increase DPS. By consuming a duplicate of the same Arc, you increase its **Mixing Intensity** from T1 to T5. This directly upgrades the percentages in the passive description. For example, the boss damage on **Ready-Ready** or the ATK per second on **Fluff of Fleetness** can nearly double at T5.

### Substat Priority for Main DPS Espers
When choosing between two Arcs of the same type, always prioritize the substat that your character lacks.
*   **CRIT Rate:** Essential for characters with high hit-counts like Nanally or Mint to ensure consistent damage.
*   **CRIT DMG:** Best for characters who already have high innate CRIT Rate or those who rely on massive single-hit Ultimates.
*   **ATK%:** A safe general-purpose stat, but it often yields diminishing returns compared to CRIT stats in the endgame.
*   **Break Intensity:** Crucial for "Breaker" sub-DPS roles, but usually secondary for your primary damage dealer.

## Related

![Related](/img/best-weapons-for-dps-in-nte/inline-4.webp)


Beyond the weapon itself, your damage in Neverness to Everness is influenced by the characters you choose and the secondary gear systems that support your Arcs.

### Top DPS Characters in the Current Meta
To make the most of the Arcs listed above, you should pair them with the following high-tier Espers:
*   **Nanally (S-Rank/Plasma):** The current apex DPS. Her follow-up attack frequency and "Ichi-daime's Authority" state make her the best user for **Ready-Ready**.
*   **Jiuyuan (S-Rank/Synthesis):** A powerful Synthesis carry who excels in high-intensity combat and benefits immensely from the CRIT DMG scaling of **Camellia Society**.
*   **Baicang (S-Rank/Synthesis):** Another top-tier Synthesis choice, often used for his high burst potential and synergy with HP-manipulation Arcs.
*   **Mint (A-Rank/Liquid):** While an A-Rank character, she is the primary beneficiary of **Fluff of Fleetness**, making her a formidable AOE damage dealer for F2P players.

### The Gear System: Cartridges and Modules
The **Console** and **Cartridge** system provides the final layer of stat customization. While Arcs provide the "Active" passive, Cartridges provide set bonuses. For example, a 4-piece Anima DMG set can increase your CRIT DMG by up to 56% when enemies take damage from the team. Always look for Cartridges that have "Main Stats" matching your Arc's focus—if you are using a CRIT Rate Arc, look for a CRIT DMG main stat on your Cartridge to balance your ratios.

### Farming Guide for Weapon Materials
*   **Dye:** Farm the **Kaleidoscope** stage. It is recommended to spend your daily stamina here once your main team reaches level 40.
*   **Ascension Items:** Visit the **Bubble Can Factory** regularly. Some high-tier materials only drop from the **Serenetti Anomaly** or the **Morphix** bosses in the Unheard Shores.
*   **New Arcs:** Keep an eye on the **Arc Research Program**. Version 1.1 is expected to introduce new signature Arcs for upcoming characters like [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte). If you are missing a specific type, check the **Tri-Key** shop, where you can exchange 25 Tri-Keys for an S-Rank Arc of your choice.

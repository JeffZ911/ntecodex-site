### The Esper Cycle and Swap Mechanics

The "Esper Cycle" is the defining mechanic of Neverness to Everness. As you deal damage with an active character, you accumulate elemental energy. When a character’s portrait on the right side of the screen begins to glow with a circular "Cycle" indicator, switching to them triggers a Swap-In Attack.

If the incoming character's element reacts with the element currently applied to the enemy, a **Duo Cycle** is triggered. The goal is to keep the "Cycle" moving. By rotating through all four members of your party in a specific order, you can maintain a constant flow of elemental reactions, preventing the enemy from recovering their poise.

### The Break Meter and Stagger System

Every elite enemy and boss in NTE has a "Break Meter" (a yellow bar located directly beneath their health bar). While this bar is active, the enemy has high damage resistance and cannot be interrupted.

To defeat these enemies, you must focus on "Break DMG." Once the meter reaches zero, the enemy is "Broken." During this state:
1.  The enemy is completely immobilized.
2.  All incoming damage is increased by a set multiplier (base is [information unavailable], though certain Arcs can increase this).
3.  The "Elemental Lane" is wide open, meaning reactions triggered during this window deal significantly more damage.

### Defensive Mastery: Parrying and Critical Dodges

NTE rewards aggressive, high-skill play through its defensive systems. 

*   **Parrying:** By timing a Normal Attack or certain Skills exactly as an enemy's hit lands, you perform a Parry. This negates all damage and deals massive "Poise Damage" to the enemy's Break Meter. Some characters, like those wielding heavy weapons, have wider parry windows.
*   **Critical Dodges:** If you dodge at the "perfect" moment (just before impact), the world slows down—a mechanic often called "Zero Time" by the community. This allows you to reposition and immediately follow up with a "Critical Counter," which is a unique, high-damage animation specific to each Esper.

### Team Composition and the 'Elemental Lane'

Building a team in Neverness to Everness isn't just about picking your favorite characters; it's about building an "Elemental Lane." This is the specific sequence of elements (e.g., Anima -> Chaos -> Incantation -> Lakshana) that your team uses to maximize Cycle triggers.

A standard team usually follows this structure:
1.  **The Opener:** Usually a Support or Sub-DPS who applies the first element and buffs the team.
2.  **The Breaker:** A character specifically designed to shred the yellow Break Meter.
3.  **The Catalyst:** A character who triggers the first Duo Cycle and sets up the Triple Cycle.
4.  **The Finisher (Main DPS):** The character who stays on field during the "Broken" window to deal the final burst of damage.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/neverness-to-everness-disk-system-explained/inline-2.webp)


The "Disk System" in Neverness to Everness is the foundational framework for character progression and combat power. It is divided into two main categories: Arcs (the weapons) and the Console (the gear/armor system). 

Arcs are equippable disks that serve as an Esper’s primary weapon. They provide a massive boost to base ATK, offer secondary substats like CRIT Rate or ATK%, and feature unique passive effects that can change how a character plays. Signature versions of these weapons, designed for specific S-rank characters, are known as Ethe-disks. Notable examples include Chiz’s signature disk, "Thinking Meow," and the powerful S-rank utility Arc, "Galactic Pause."

The Disk system overlaps with the Console gear system, where players must manage a grid-based interface. Instead of simply clicking "equip," you must fit "Cartridges" (primary gear) and "Modules" (secondary gear) into a Tetris-shaped grid. This system rewards spatial management, as matching specific shapes and filling the grid activates powerful set bonuses and attribute boosts essential for late-game Anomaly Zones.

### Understanding Arcs and Ethe-disks

In Neverness to Everness, every Esper requires an Arc to channel their abilities. These are not merely stat sticks; they are categorized by rarity and type. Arcs come in three primary rarities: B-rank (Blue), A-rank (Pink), and S-rank (Orange). While B-rank Arcs are suitable for the early hours of the story, S-rank Arcs—especially Ethe-disks—are required for high-level play in the Rabbit Hole.

Ethe-disks are specialized Arcs that provide "Signature Resonance" when equipped by their intended user. For example, "Thinking Meow" provides a significant boost to Psyche-based damage and cooldown reduction that is specifically tuned to Chiz’s kit.

### Arc Types and Character Compatibility

Every Esper and Arc is assigned one of five Types. You cannot equip an Arc if its Type does not match the Esper's requirements. This system ensures that weapons are balanced around specific combat roles.

### The Console: Cartridges, Modules, and the Tetris Grid

The Console is where the "Disk System" becomes a strategic puzzle. Each character has a Console grid (starting at 3x3 and expanding as you level up). 

1.  **Cartridges:** These are your "Main Gear" pieces. They typically take up a 2x2 or 2x1 space on the grid. Cartridges provide the largest stat boosts, such as flat HP or Elemental Damage bonuses.
2.  **Modules:** These are "Sub-Gear" pieces that come in various Tetris shapes (L-shape, T-shape, I-shape). They are categorized into Type I, Type II, and Type III. 
3.  **Grid Synergy:** To maximize an Esper's potential, you must fit these shapes into the Console without leaving empty spaces. Filling the grid or matching specific Module types activates "Grid Resonance," which provides percentage-based buffs to your total stats.

### Quality of Life: The No Hidden RNG System

A standout feature of the NTE Disk system is the "No Hidden RNG" transparency. In many contemporary games, you must level up a piece of gear to see its hidden substats. In Neverness to Everness, all four substats on a Cartridge or Module are visible the moment you acquire the item. This allows players to immediately identify "god-rolls" without wasting resources like Lost Pieces or enhancement ores.

### Refinement and Enhancement: Maximizing Disk Potential

Upgrading your Arcs involves two distinct processes: Leveling and Refinement.

*   **Leveling:** Use enhancement ores or low-level Arcs to increase the base ATK and secondary stat of your Disk. Every 10 levels, you will hit a "Breakthrough" point requiring specialized materials found in the city’s industrial districts.
*   **Refinement (M1 to M5):** If you obtain a duplicate of an Arc, you can "Refine" the original. This increases the power of the Disk’s unique passive ability. ### Unlocking Free S-Rank Disks via City Tycoon

New players should focus on the "City Tycoon" management mini-game. Upon reaching Tycoon Level 18, the game rewards you with a free copy of the S-rank Esper Chiz, along with her signature Ethe-disk "Thinking Meow." This is widely considered the best early-game investment for F2P (Free to Play) players, as it provides a top-tier damage dealer and a fully compatible weapon without spending gacha currency.

### Recommended Early-Game Arc-Disk Sets

For those just starting in Hecta City, focus on obtaining the "Urban Vanguard" set of Modules. While they are A-rank (Pink), their shapes are easy to fit into the early 3x3 Console grid and provide a universal ATK% boost that benefits all Esper Types, whether they are Plasma, Liquid, or Solid. 

Finally, do not neglect the "Rampage Players" shop. While the Disk system handles character combat, the Rampage Players shop is where you modify your vehicles. Vehicle stats are separate from the Disk system, but high-end car tuning is often required to reach the more distant Anomaly Zones where the best Arcs are hidden.

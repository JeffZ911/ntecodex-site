## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/best-disk-stats-nte-healers/inline-3.webp)


The gear system in Neverness to Everness, referred to as the "Console," is more complex than standard RPG equipment. It is divided into three distinct components: Arcs (the weapons themselves, often called Disks), Cartridges (the primary stat sticks), and Modules (the grid-based sub-stat system). To build an effective healer, you must synchronize all three components.

### Understanding the Console: Cartridges vs. Modules

Cartridges function as the primary gear slots. Each Esper can equip a set of Cartridges that provide significant percentage-based boosts and set bonuses. For healers, the "Main Stat" of the primary Cartridge slot should almost always be Healing Bonus. This is because Healing Bonus acts as a final multiplier in the game's restorative math, providing more value than simply stacking the base stat the healer scales from.

Modules, on the other hand, use a "Tetris" grid system. You place geometric shapes into a limited space to gain additional attributes. Unlike Cartridges, which are often RNG-dependent for their main stats, Modules allow for more granular control over your build. For healers, filling the grid with pieces that offer Charge Efficiency is the most effective way to ensure their healing Ultimates are available when needed.

### Character Breakdown: Edgar, Jiuyuan, and Zero

Not all healers in NTE use the same stats for their base calculations. Misallocating stats can lead to a healer that provides negligible recovery.

*   **Edgar:** As a pure HP-scaler, Edgar benefits most from "Thea’s Night Tavern." His heals are calculated as a percentage of his Max HP. Therefore, once you have a Healing Bonus main stat, every other slot should focus on HP% and Flat HP.
*   **Jiuyuan:** Unlike Edgar, Jiuyuan’s restorative abilities scale off his total Attack power. This makes him more fragile but allows him to contribute more to team DPS. For Jiuyuan, you must prioritize ATK% Cartridges.
*   **Zero:** Zero functions as a hybrid support. While she can heal, her primary value comes from her utility and ability to trigger elemental reactions. "Speedy Hedgehog" is her best set because it provides team-wide buffs upon using a skill, which fits her fast-paced rotation.

### Best Cartridge Sets for Healers

Choosing the right set bonus is the foundation of a healer build. As of May 23, 2026, two sets dominate the meta:

1.  **Thea’s Night Tavern (4-Piece):** This is the gold standard for pure sustain. The 2-piece bonus grants 10% Healing Bonus, while the 4-piece bonus increases the healing received by all allies by an additional 15% for 10 seconds after the wearer uses an Ultimate.
2.  **Speedy Hedgehog (4-Piece):** This set is designed for "Cycle" teams. The 2-piece bonus increases Charge Efficiency by 20%. The 4-piece bonus grants the entire team a 10% ATK boost whenever the wearer uses a support skill. This is ideal for Zero or Jiuyuan in high-speed compositions.

### Optimizing the Tetris Grid for Support

The Module system uses three types of pieces, each with different stat weights. For a healer, the goal is to maximize the surface area of the grid with stats that facilitate "Ultimate Spam."

*   **Type I Modules:** These are usually 1x1 or 1x2 blocks. They provide flat stats like Flat HP or Flat ATK. These are essential for increasing the "Base" number that your percentage modifiers multiply.
*   **Type II Modules:** These are larger (2x2 or L-shaped) and provide percentage-based stats like HP% or ATK%.
*   **Type III Modules:** These are the most valuable for healers. They provide "Utility" stats, specifically Charge Efficiency and Break Power.

For endgame content like "Beyond the Rails," a healer should aim for a **Charge Efficiency threshold of 130%–150%**. If your Charge Efficiency is below 130%, you will likely find yourself without a heal available during the boss's second phase.

### Healing Bonus vs. Scaling Stat Efficiency

A common mistake is stacking Max HP on Edgar or ATK on Jiuyuan while ignoring the Healing Bonus stat. In NTE, the healing formula is roughly:

`Total Heal = (Base Scaling Stat * Skill Multiplier) * (1 + Healing Bonus)`

Because the Healing Bonus is a separate multiplier at the end of the calculation, it is mathematically superior to stacking more of the base stat. For example, moving from 50% HP bonus to 70% HP bonus only increases the first half of the equation. Adding a 20% Healing Bonus multiplies the *entire* result, leading to significantly higher numbers in the mid-to-late game.

### Best Healer Characters in NTE

While Edgar and Jiuyuan are the primary dedicated healers, the current meta often favors shielders or hybrid supports. ### How to Get More Cartridges

Farming S-Rank Cartridges is a late-game loop. You primarily acquire them by spending "Character Pixels" at the Hethereau Urban District Exchange or by clearing "Anomaly" zones. The drop rate for S-Rank "Thea’s Night Tavern" pieces is notably low, so players often use the "Synthesis" feature to convert unwanted A-Rank Cartridges into specific set pieces.

### Advanced Combat: The Esper Cycle

In NTE, healers do more than just restore HP; they are vital for the "Charge" and "Hexed" elemental reactions. By equipping an Arc like "Mine Royale," a healer can generate enough energy to trigger their Ultimate, which in turn applies an elemental aura to the field. This allows your main DPS to trigger a "Resonance" effect, significantly increasing the team's total damage output.

To unlock the "Fountain" weapon for a Healer-Main Character (Zero) build, you must complete the "Echoes of the City" questline in the central plaza. This specific Arc provides a unique passive that converts 5% of the character's Max HP into a flat ATK buff for the active party member, making Zero one of the best offensive supports in the current version of the game.

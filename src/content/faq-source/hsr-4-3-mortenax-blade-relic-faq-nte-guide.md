For a DPS character with high-scaling damage similar to Mortenax Blade, the Diabolos Cartridge set is the premier choice for Chaos-element characters like Lacrimosa, while the Lost Radiance set serves Ultimate-heavy characters like Hotori.

### Mortenax Blade Relic & Stat Breakdown (HSR 4.3)

Mortenax Blade operates as a "0-SP" bruiser, meaning his primary combat loop does not consume Skill Points once he enters his enhanced state. His core mechanic revolves around his Ultimate, **Fornax Ex Corpore**, which places him into the **Infinite Fury** stance. During this stance, his basic attacks are replaced with enhanced Fire strikes that apply the **Balefire Bind** debuff. This debuff is the reason why the Divine-Querying Master Smith relic set is so effective; it shreds 30% of the enemy's DEF, allowing Mortenax to naturally trigger the set's CRIT DMG bonus.

When prioritizing stats, HP% is more valuable than ATK% because his Skill and Ultimate multipliers rely almost entirely on his health pool. Reaching the 5,000 HP threshold is mandatory to activate the full effects of the Bone Collection's Serene Demesne Planar set. Once this threshold is met, focusing on CRIT DMG becomes the priority, as Mortenax gains a natural CRIT Rate boost through his Ascension Traces.

### The Neverness to Everness Gear System: Cartridges and Modules

Neverness to Everness departs from the RNG-heavy gear systems of other titles by using a spatial "Console" grid. Instead of equipping a helmet or gloves, you equip a **Cartridge**, which defines your set bonus, and then fill the Console with **Modules**. Modules are geometric shapes that must be fitted into a limited grid, similar to a puzzle.

A major advantage of the NTE system is transparency. Unlike HSR relics, where substats are hidden until you level up the piece, NTE Modules show all four substats immediately upon acquisition. This allows players to know exactly how powerful a piece is before investing resources into it. The power of a build is determined by the "Type" of modules used—specifically Type II, III, and IV—and how many of them you can fit into the character's Console grid.

### NTE Equivalent Sets for Mortenax Blade's Playstyle

For players looking for a "Mortenax Blade-style" experience in NTE, the focus should be on characters who benefit from DEF-shred and elemental penetration.

1.  **The Diabolos Set**: This is the current meta for Chaos-element DPS characters like Lacrimosa. The Diabolos Cartridge provides a 10% Chaos DMG bonus and allows the wearer to ignore up to 24% of the enemy's Chaos RES. This mirrors Mortenax Blade’s ability to bypass enemy defenses to land heavy hits.
2.  **The Lost Radiance Set**: If your playstyle focuses on massive burst damage from Ultimates, the Lost Radiance set is the best choice. It is the BiS (Best-in-Slot) for Hotori, a Cosmos-element character. Upon using an Ultimate, this set ignores 25% of the enemy's DEF for 20 seconds, which is remarkably similar to the "Balefire Bind" mechanic in HSR.

### How to Optimize the NTE Console (The Tetris System)

Optimizing a character in NTE requires more than just finding good stats; it requires spatial management. Each character has a unique Console grid size. To maximize a build, you must use higher-tier modules:

*   **Type III Modules**: These are essential for endgame builds. For example, Lacrimosa gains a specific bonus to her Chaos DMG for every Type III module equipped in her Console.
*   **Type IV Modules**: These are the rarest and often have the most awkward shapes (such as long "I" blocks or large "Z" blocks). While they provide the highest stat values, they are difficult to fit alongside other high-tier modules.

The goal is to fill as many squares of the grid as possible without overlapping, as empty squares represent wasted potential stats. This "Tetris" style of gearing means that a "perfect" module with great substats might be unusable if its shape doesn't fit into your current optimized grid.

### Farming Guide: Rabbit Hole Anomaly Zone & Rewind Gacha

Farming for gear in NTE is split between two distinct activities. While HSR players spend Trailblaze Power in Caverns of Corrosion, NTE players head to the Rabbit Hole Anomaly Zone.

*   **Character Pixels**: These are farmed within the Anomaly Zone and are used to upgrade the base level of your Cartridges.
*   **Rewind Gacha**: This is how you obtain new Modules. You spend **Carrota coins**, which come in Bronze, Silver, and Gold varieties. Gold Carrota coins are the most coveted, as they guarantee at least a Type III module with a chance for a Type IV. These coins are earned through weekly challenges and high-tier Anomaly Zone clears.

### Elemental Reactions: The Esper Cycle

While Mortenax Blade relies on Fire damage and Nihility debuffs to win battles, NTE combat is built around the Esper Cycle. This system uses three primary elements—Incantation, Chaos, and Psyche—to create powerful reactions:

*   **Scorch**: Triggered by combining Incantation and Chaos. This deals massive damage over time, similar to a Fire DoT in HSR.
*   **Nova**: Triggered by Psyche and Chaos. This creates a large area-of-effect explosion that can stun smaller enemies.
*   **Discord**: A complex reaction involving all three elements (Chaos + Incantation + Psyche). This is the highest damage-producing reaction in the game and is essential for clearing the hardest floors of the Anomaly Zone.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Mortenax Blade available in Neverness to Everness?
No, Mortenax Blade is a character exclusive to Honkai: Star Rail Version 4.3. While NTE features characters with similar high-DPS and DEF-shred roles, such as Lacrimosa, there is no direct crossover between the two games.

### What is the most important stat for Lacrimosa in NTE?
For a Chaos-element DPS like Lacrimosa, you should prioritize Chaos DMG and CRIT DMG on your Modules. Additionally, ensuring you have enough Type III modules to trigger her passive damage bonuses is more important than simply chasing high substat numbers.

### How do I unlock the 6x6 Console grid in NTE?
The Console grid size is tied to a character's "Resonance Level." By using Character Pixels and specific boss drops from the Rabbit Hole Anomaly Zone, you can ascend your character, which eventually expands the grid from its starting size to the maximum 6x6 area.

### Can I change the shape of a Module in NTE?
No, the geometric shape of a Module is fixed once it is obtained from the Rewind gacha. If you find a Module with perfect stats but a difficult shape, you will need to rearrange your other Modules or wait until you find a more compatible piece to complete your Cartridge set.

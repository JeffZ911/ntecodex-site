# Best Disks for Neverness to Everness: Top Arcs & Gear Guide

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/best-disks-neverness-to-everness/inline-1.webp)


What are the best disks to equip in Neverness to Everness (NTE)? Since the game's launch on April 29, 2026, players have discovered that "disks" actually refer to two distinct equipment systems: Arcs (weapon disks) and Cartridges (gear disks). Arcs provide base stats and unique combat abilities, while Cartridges function as the primary armor system, utilizing a Tetris-style grid to activate powerful set bonuses.

Understanding which disks provide the highest DPS, Support, and Break utility is essential for clearing high-level content like the Rabbit Hole or the Scarborough Fair challenges. This guide identifies the top-tier options for current meta favorites like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte), [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide), and the protagonist, Esper Zero.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/best-disks-neverness-to-everness/inline-2.webp)


The current meta in Neverness to Everness favors Arcs that provide high universal scaling or character-specific mechanics. The "Day Off" Arc is widely considered the best overall S-Rank Disk due to its accessibility and powerful energy restoration. For offensive powerhouses, the "Ready-Ready" Arc (which activates the Tiger Talisman mechanic) and "Contemplative Cat" are the premier choices for Plasma and Gas types, respectively.

When it comes to gear "disks" or Cartridges, "Lost Radiance" is the gold standard for damage dealers due to its defense-shredding capabilities. Support units almost universally benefit from "Speedy Hedgehog," which turns every Ultimate into a team-wide offensive buff.

### Top Tier Arcs (Weapon Disks)

The best Arcs are those that synchronize with an Esper's specific "Type" (Plasma, Solid, Gas, Liquid, or Synthesis). While S-Rank Arcs like **Ready-Ready** and **Contemplative Cat** are often signature weapons obtained via the Arc Research Program, farmable options like **Day Off** and **Hethereau's Keeper** allow F2P players to compete at the highest levels.

### Top Tier Cartridges (Gear Disks)

Cartridges are the "gear" of NTE, and their power comes from matching 2-piece and 4-piece set bonuses. **Lost Radiance** is the most sought-after set for its 25% DEF ignore, while **Devil's Blood: Curse** is mandatory for teams relying on Psyche-based reactions like Nova or Stain. For survivability, **Tiny Big Adventure** provides massive HP scaling that helps tanks like Fadia soak up anomaly damage.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/best-disks-neverness-to-everness/inline-3.webp)


The equipment system in Neverness to Everness is deeper than it initially appears. To maximize your power, you must master the synergy between your Arc's type and your Cartridge's grid placement.

### Understanding the Disk System

Arcs function as the primary weapons for your Espers. Each Arc belongs to a specific category—Plasma, Solid, Gas, Liquid, or Synthesis—and can only be equipped by characters of the matching type. For example, Nanally is a Plasma-type Esper and requires a Plasma Arc like **Ready-Ready** to unlock her full potential.

Cartridges and Modules, on the other hand, are the "Tetris" gear system. Each Cartridge has a specific shape (e.g., a 4-cell L-shape or a 2-cell line). You must fit these shapes into your character's Console grid. Activating a 4-piece "Legendary" set bonus requires careful planning to ensure all modules fit without overlapping, making gear management a puzzle-solving mini-game.

### S-Rank Arc Breakdown

*   **Ready-Ready (The Tiger Talismans):** This Plasma-type Arc is Nanally's signature weapon. It provides a massive CRIT Rate substat and activates the **Tiger Talisman** mechanic. Casting a Redirect Skill grants a Left Tiger Talisman, while an Ultimate grants a Right Tiger Talisman. Holding both simultaneously unlocks the "Commander Tiger Talisman" effect, which increases damage against bosses by up to 20% at base refinement.
*   **Contemplative Cat:** This Gas-type Arc is the signature of Chiz. Its unique "Mammon" effect increases Cosmos DMG by 2.5% for every 100,000 Fons you currently hold (up to 10 stacks). This makes it the only weapon in the game that scales directly with your wealth in Hethereau City.

### Reaction Synergy: Devil's Blood and Nova/Stain

For players running reaction-heavy teams, **Devil's Blood: Curse** is the best cartridge choice. Its base effect increases the wearer's damage by 18%, but this bonus doubles to 36% against any enemy affected by the **Nova** or **Stain** reactions. This makes it a staple for Psyche-heavy compositions featuring characters like Fadia or Haniel.

### Best Disks for Main DPS (Nanally, Chiz, Jiuyuan)

Main DPS units require Arcs with high base ATK and offensive substats like CRIT Rate or DMG. Nanally reaches her peak with **Ready-Ready**, but F2P players can use **Oraora!** as a solid Plasma alternative. Chiz is almost entirely dependent on **Contemplative Cat** to reach her damage ceiling, as the Fons-scaling mechanic is unique to that Arc. For Jiuyuan, the **Rain That Shook the World** provides the necessary Cosmos DMG boost to capitalize on her Blossom reactions.

### Best Disks for Supports & Tanks (Sakiri, Adler, Haniel)

Support characters should prioritize Arcs that increase "Cycle Intensity" or provide team-wide buffs. **Blow up the Crowd** is an excellent S-Rank pick for Haniel, as it boosts the active character's ATK even while Haniel is off-field. For tanks like Adler, look for Arcs that provide HP% or Shield Strength, such as **Fool's Spring**.

### Best Disks for Break Specialists (Daffodill, Mint)

Break Specialists are responsible for depleting the enemy's white "Break" bar to stun them. **Daffodill** is the undisputed queen of this role in the 1.0 meta. While her signature Arc **Youthful Fantasy** is the best choice, the A-Rank **Time Bandit** is a phenomenal budget option. It provides a 90-point spike in Break Intensity for 10 seconds after using a Redirect Skill, allowing you to stagger bosses significantly faster.

### How to Unlock the Hidden 'Day Off' S-Rank Disk

The **Day Off** Arc is a "secret" S-Rank weapon designed for the protagonist, Esper Zero. You can obtain it for free by completing the "Naked Ghost Tour of the City" quest chain.

1.  **Trigger the Quest:** Go to BaC Headquarters (Area M10) at 08:00 AM. Speak to the NPC "Eclipse" standing in the right corner.
2.  **The Rooftop Tour:** You must find Eclipse at four specific locations across the city between 08:00 and 10:00 AM on Sunny days:
    *   The roof of **Film Orbit Studio**.
    *   The roof of the **Oops Chest Gift Shop** in Illusion Town.
    *   The coastal path near the **Coastal Highway**.
    *   The very top of the **Tikal Bridge** suspension cables (use Nanally's wall-running to make the climb easier).
3.  **Final Reward:** After speaking to her at the bridge, return to her original spot at BaC Headquarters. She will reward you with the **Day Off** Arc.

### Mastering the Cartridge Tetris System

The Console interface is where you slot your Cartridges and Modules. Modules are smaller pieces that provide flat stats (ATK, HP, DEF), while Cartridges are larger pieces that provide the set bonuses. 

To maximize efficiency:
*   **Prioritize Rarity:** Only level up Purple (A-Rank) or Gold (S-Rank) modules to level 20. Blue modules should be left at level 5.
*   **Shape Matching:** Look for "Type II" (2-cell) and "Type IV" (4-cell) modules that complement your Cartridge shapes. If a Cartridge is an L-shape, you'll need straight-line modules to fill the remaining gaps in the grid.
*   **Substat Grinding:** The best modules have "Break Intensity" or "Cycle Intensity" as secondary stats, which are vital for endgame anomaly hunts.

Upgrading and pulling for disks is a core part of the NTE gameplay loop. Here is how to keep your gear competitive.

### Farming and Upgrading Your Disks

Upgrading Arcs requires two types of materials: **EXP Dyes** and **Ascension Materials**.
*   **EXP Dyes:** Light Dye and Colorless Dye can be farmed at **Houdinii's Magic Stage**.
*   **Ascension Materials:** Items like **Golden Appleseed**, **Silver Appleseed**, and **Harmony** are primarily found at the **Bubble Can Factory** or purchased from the Hunter Exchange.
*   **Cartridge EXP:** To level up your cartridges and modules, farm the **Rabbit Hole** for "Manhole Crook" items, which provide 5,000 EXP each.

### Scarborough Fair: Pulling for S-Rank Arcs

The gacha system in Neverness to Everness is called **Scarborough Fair**. It uses a board-game mechanic where you roll **Solid Dice** (for limited banners) or **Fabricated Dice** (for standard banners).
*   **Arc Research Program:** This is the dedicated weapon banner. It requires **Tri-Keys** instead of dice.
*   **Pity Mechanics:** S-Rank Arcs are guaranteed within 80 pulls (8 multis). There is no "50/50" on the weapon banner; the featured Arc is the guaranteed S-Rank pull.
*   **Board Modification:** After 70 pulls without an S-Rank, the board "modifies," significantly increasing the number of S-Rank tiles until you land on one.

### Elemental Reactions and Disk Synergy

Pairing the right disks with elemental reactions is the key to high DPS.
*   **Nova (Psyche + ?):** Use **Devil's Blood: Curse** to capitalize on the massive damage window.
*   **Scorch (Chaos + Incantation):** Best triggered by pairing **Daffodill** (with **Youthful Fantasy**) and **Sakiri**.
*   **Blossom (Cosmos + ?):** The protagonist's specialty. Use the **Day Off** Arc to ensure you have enough energy to trigger Blossom repeatedly.

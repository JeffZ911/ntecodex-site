## Direct Answer

In Neverness to Everness, characters (known as Espers) are leveled up primarily through the consumption of **Hunter Guides** (EXP items) and **Beetle Coins**. You cannot rely on combat experience alone to reach high levels; instead, you must manually apply these resources via the Esper interface.

Character growth is not infinite from the start. It is gated by **Ascension** (also known as breakthroughs), which requires unique materials dropped by world bosses or "Anomalies." Furthermore, your characters' maximum level potential is strictly tied to your account's **Appraisal Level**. This Appraisal Level scales alongside your **Hunter Level**, creating a system where your overall account progress dictates the ceiling for your individual units.

To gather the necessary Hunter Guides and Beetle Coins efficiently, you must spend **Character Pixels** (the game's energy/stamina system) at **Houdinii's Magic Stage**. This specialized training area serves as the primary farming hub for all character-related experience materials.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-level-up-nte-characters/inline-3.webp)


The progression loop in Neverness to Everness is a cycle of exploration, resource management, and boss hunting. To keep your team competitive, you must balance your daily activities between earning account-wide experience and farming specific character materials.

### Using Hunter Guides and Beetle Coins

Leveling an Esper is a manual process. When you open the Esper menu, you can select a character and use Hunter Guides to grant them experience points. Every level gained requires a corresponding amount of Beetle Coins, the primary currency of Hethereau.

Hunter Guides come in three distinct rarities. Using higher-tier guides is more efficient as you reach higher levels, saving you time in the menu.

| Item Name | EXP Value | Rarity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Hunter Guide: Rising | 1,000 | Green (Common) |
| Hunter Guide: Senior | 5,000 | Blue (Rare) |
| Hunter Guide: Elite | 20,000 | Purple (Epic) |

Resource management is critical during the early game. While it may be tempting to level every new character you pull from the "The Ichi-daime" or other banners, doing so will quickly deplete your Beetle Coins. It is highly recommended to focus your resources on a core team of four characters—typically one main DPS, two supports, and a utility or elemental shield breaker—until you reach the mid-game.

### Understanding Ascension and Breakthroughs

Once an Esper reaches a specific level milestone (such as level 20, 40, or 50), they will hit a "Cap." At this point, further Hunter Guides cannot be used until the character undergoes **Ascension**.

Ascension requires a combination of common enemy drops and specialized "Anomaly Cores" obtained from boss encounters found throughout the city. These bosses are marked on your map and require Character Pixels to claim rewards after the fight. Each Ascension not only raises the level cap but also provides a significant boost to the character's base stats (HP, ATK, and DEF) and sometimes upgrades their passive talents.

### The Hunter Level and Appraisal Level Connection

Neverness to Everness uses a "World Tier" style system to regulate game difficulty and player power. Your **Hunter Level** represents your total account progress, earned through quests, exploration, and the City Tycoon system. As your Hunter Level increases, you will reach milestones that allow you to increase your **Appraisal Level**.

The Appraisal Level is the hard ceiling for all characters on your account. Even if you have thousands of Elite Hunter Guides, you cannot level a character to 60 if your Appraisal Level is only at Tier 2.

| Hunter Level (HL) | Appraisal Level (AL) | Max Character Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| HL 10 | AL 1 | Level 20 |
| HL 20 | AL 2 | Level 40 |
| HL 30 | AL 3 | Level 50 |
| HL 40 | AL 4 | Level 60 |
| HL 50 | AL 5 | Level 70 |
| HL 60 | AL 6 | Level 80 |

Raising your Appraisal Level also increases the difficulty of enemies in the open world but improves the quality of the loot dropped by bosses and chests.

### Where to Farm Leveling Materials

To keep a steady flow of materials, you must utilize your daily Character Pixels. These regenerate over time and are the lifeblood of character progression.

1.  **Houdinii's Magic Stage:** This is the most important location for leveling. You can choose different "stages" here to focus on Hunter Guides, Beetle Coins, or Skill upgrade materials.
2.  **Anomaly Hunts:** Once you hit a level cap, check the Ascension tab in the character menu. Clicking on a missing material will show you the exact location of the boss that drops it.
3.  **City Tycoon Rewards:** Managing your businesses and completing urban tasks provides a steady passive income of Beetle Coins and occasionally Hunter Guides.
4.  **Daily Commissions:** Completing your four daily tasks in Hethereau provides a significant amount of Hunter EXP, which is vital for raising your Appraisal Level ceiling.

**Daily Checklist for Maximizing Hunter EXP:**
*   Log in and spend Character Pixels at Houdinii's Magic Stage.
*   Complete all 4 Daily Commissions for Hunter Level progression.
*   Check the City Tycoon interface to collect accumulated Beetle Coins.
*   Clear any "Red Zone" anomalies that appeared on the map for high-tier drops.

### Additional Progression: Arcs, Consoles, and Abilities

Leveling the character's base stats is only one part of the equation. To truly maximize power, you must also invest in their equipment and skills.

*   **Arcs (Weapons):** Arcs must be leveled up using "Arc Refinement Chips." Like characters, they have level caps that require Ascension materials. Matching the right Arc to your Esper's specialty (e.g., Physical, Fire, or Ether) is essential.
*   **Console System:** This is the NTE "gear" system. It uses a Tetris-style grid where you place different components to boost stats. Leveling up your characters allows you to unlock more space in their Console grid, enabling more complex and powerful configurations.
*   **Abilities:** Character skills do not level up automatically. You must use specific "Ability Modules" found in Houdinii's Magic Stage to manually upgrade the potency of your Basic Attack, Resonance Skill, and Ultimate.
*   **Mind Shards (Awakening):** If you obtain duplicate copies of a character through the gacha system, you receive Mind Shards. These are used to unlock "Awakening" nodes, providing massive passive power spikes and sometimes changing how a character's abilities function.

Character leveling is deeply intertwined with other systems in Neverness to Everness. To be efficient, you cannot look at Esper levels in isolation; you must also master the city's economy and your account's overall rank.

### Hunter Leveling Guide

To level up characters faster, you must first level up your account. The fastest way to increase your Hunter Level is through the **City Tycoon** system and map exploration. Completing "Urban Stories" (side quests) and finding hidden collectibles in the back alleys of Hethereau grants substantial Hunter EXP. Unlike Character Pixels, which are limited, exploration is only limited by the time you spend in the game. Higher Hunter Levels unlock better farming stages in Houdinii's Magic Stage, making each point of stamina more valuable.

### NTE Console System Explained

The Console System is where the "theorycrafting" happens in NTE. Each character has a unique grid shape that expands as they reach higher Ascension tiers. You collect "Chips" of various shapes and sizes—some provide flat ATK, while others offer percentage-based Crit Rate or Elemental Damage. The goal is to fit the highest quality chips into the grid without leaving empty spaces. Some chips also have "Set Bonuses" if you place specific types adjacent to each other. Understanding this Tetris-like mechanic is often more important for damage output than the character's raw level.

### Best Team Compositions for Early Game

Because resources are scarce, your initial team should be versatile. A standard efficient team often includes a high-mobility character for exploration, a dedicated "Anomaly Breaker" to deplete enemy shields, and a sustained healer. For example, using [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) as a primary damage dealer alongside support units that can trigger "Elemental Resonance" will allow you to clear higher-level Anomaly Hunts even if your characters are slightly under-leveled. Focus your Hunter Guides on your Main DPS first, keeping your supports about 10 levels behind to save Beetle Coins.

### City Tycoon Progression Basics

City Tycoon is the management simulation side of NTE. By purchasing properties and assigning "Staff" (Espers you’ve recruited), you generate Beetle Coins and Hunter EXP passively. Some Espers have specific "Business Talents" that increase the efficiency of certain shops. For example, a character might have a +10% yield when assigned to a convenience store. Keeping your businesses running is the best way to ensure you never run out of Beetle Coins when trying to push a character from level 70 to 80, where costs become exponentially higher.

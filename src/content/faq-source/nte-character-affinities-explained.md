What are character affinities in Neverness to Everness (NTE)? In the context of this urban open-world RPG, affinities—officially known as Esper Types or Attributes—are the elemental classifications assigned to every playable character. These types dictate the elemental damage a character deals and, more importantly, how they interact with other party members during combat.

How do character elements affect combat and team building? Unlike traditional RPGs where elements simply counter one another (e.g., Water beats Fire), NTE utilizes an "Adjacency Rule" on a hexagonal wheel. Team building requires players to select characters whose affinities sit next to each other on this wheel to trigger specific elemental reactions.

What is the difference between an Attribute and an Esper Type? In the 2026 release of NTE, these terms are used interchangeably. "Esper Type" refers to the character's innate elemental nature (like Cosmos or Chaos), while "Attribute" is often used in the gear system (Arcs and Consoles) to describe the damage bonuses applied to those specific types.

How do you trigger elemental reactions in the 2026 release of NTE? Reactions are triggered through the Esper Cycle system. By filling a character’s Esper Meter and then swapping to a character with an adjacent affinity, you activate a "Duo Cycle" reaction. Swapping through three specific adjacent types in sequence activates even more powerful "Trio Cycle" reactions.

### The Esper Wheel: Understanding the Adjacency Rule

The Esper Wheel is a hexagonal diagram that shows the relationships between the six types. The order is: **Cosmos → Anima → Incantation → Chaos → Psyche → Lakshana → (back to Cosmos)**.

The "Adjacency Rule" states that a Duo Cycle reaction can only occur if you swap from one character to another that is immediately next to them on the wheel. For example, swapping from a Cosmos character to an Anima character triggers a reaction. However, swapping from Cosmos directly to Chaos triggers no reaction, as they are not neighbors on the wheel. This forces players to think carefully about their team composition order.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-elemental-reaction-guide-explained/inline-2.webp)


Elemental reactions in NTE are called "Esper Cycles" and occur when you swap between characters with neighboring elements on the Esper Wheel. Unlike traditional elemental systems where any two elements might interact, NTE uses a strict adjacency rule. If two elements are not next to each other on the wheel, swapping between them will result in a "Dry Swap" with no bonus reaction damage or effects.

There are six primary elements, known as Espers, in the game:

| Esper Element | Color/Theme | Combat Role Tendency |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Lakshana** | Gold / Order | Control and Buffing |
| **Cosmos** | Silver / Light | Hybrid and Energy Generation |
| **Anima** | Green / Life | Burst DPS and Follow-up Attacks |
| **Incantation** | Red / Force | Buffing and Sustained Damage |
| **Chaos** | Purple / Dark | Break Damage and Debuffs |
| **Psyche** | Azure / Mind | Survival and Vulnerability |

To trigger a reaction, you must fill a character's Esper Meter through combat actions. Once the portrait glows, swapping that character in while the previous character's element is still active on the target initiates the cycle. These are divided into **Duo Cycles** (two elements) and **Trio Cycles** (three elements), which provide the highest damage ceiling in the game.

## Detailed Explanation

### The Six Esper Elements and the Esper Wheel

The Esper Wheel is the fundamental rulebook of NTE combat. The elements are arranged in a specific order: Lakshana, Cosmos, Anima, Incantation, Chaos, and Psyche. This hexagonal arrangement means that each element has exactly two neighbors. For example, Anima can only react with Cosmos or Incantation. 

If you attempt to swap from an Anima character (like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)) to a Chaos character (like Daffodill), no reaction will occur because they are separated by Incantation on the wheel. This "Reaction Lane" concept is vital for team building; a "Rainbow Team" with non-adjacent elements will consistently deal lower damage than a team built around a specific segment of the wheel.

### Duo Cycle Reactions: Effects and Mechanics

Duo Cycles are the most common reactions you will trigger. They occur the moment a character enters the field via an Esper Ability swap.

| Reaction Name | Element Combination | Combat Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Remora** | Lakshana + Cosmos | Marks enemies and slows their movement and attack speed for 5 seconds. |
| **Blossom** | Cosmos + Anima | Spawns a "Vita Bud" turret that fires "Vita Pistils" (AoE damage) at the target. |
| **Hexed** | Anima + Incantation | Records damage dealt over a window (approx. 12s) and deals 20% of that total as bonus follow-up damage. |
| **Scorch** | Incantation + Chaos | Inflicts a Damage-over-Time (DoT) effect that lasts for 15 seconds. |
| **Nova** | Chaos + Psyche | Attaches a bomb to the enemy that detonates for massive burst damage after a 5-second delay. |
| **Stain** | Psyche + Lakshana | Debuffs enemies, increasing the Psyche and Lakshana damage they take by 50% for 12 seconds. |

### Trio Cycle Reactions: Unlocking Charge and Discord

Trio Cycles (also called Triple Cycles) are advanced reactions that require you to trigger two consecutive Duo reactions within a specific "Trio" group. The wheel is divided into the Top Trio (Lakshana, Cosmos, Anima) and the Bottom Trio (Incantation, Chaos, Psyche).

1.  **Charge (Top Trio):** Triggered when a Blossom turret (Cosmos + Anima) hits a target already affected by Remora (Lakshana + Cosmos). This reaction generates a massive amount of Ultimate Energy for the active character, acting as a "battery" for the team.
2.  **Discord (Bottom Trio):** Triggered when an enemy is simultaneously affected by Scorch (Incantation + Chaos) and Nova (Chaos + Psyche). This results in a massive reduction of the enemy's "Break" or Stagger meter, making it the premier choice for boss fights.

### Managing the Esper Meter and Cycle Rate

The Esper Meter is the circular gauge next to each character's portrait. You cannot trigger a reaction unless this meter is 100% full.

*   **Normal Attacks:** Provide slow but steady meter gain.
*   **Skills and Ultimates:** Provide moderate to high meter gain depending on the character.
*   **Dodge Counters:** Provide a significant burst of meter.
*   **Perfect Parries:** Instantly fill the character's Esper Meter to 100%.

The **Cycle Rate** stat is a multiplier that dictates how quickly these actions fill the meter. High-tier sub-DPS characters like Zero are valued because they can often fill their meter instantly using specific skills, allowing for rapid-fire reaction chains. ### Team Building Strategy: The 'Reaction Lane' Concept

Effective teams usually occupy a "Lane" of three or four adjacent elements on the wheel. 

*   **The Blossom Lane (Lakshana - Cosmos - Anima):** This is the current meta for general clearing. Using Hathor (Lakshana), Zero (Cosmos), and Nanally (Anima), you can rotate through Remora and Blossom to trigger the Charge Trio reaction, ensuring Nanally always has her Ultimate ready.
*   **The Discord Lane (Incantation - Chaos - Psyche):** This lane is specialized for boss shredding. By combining Adler (Incantation), Daffodill (Chaos), and Fadia (Psyche), you focus on the Discord reaction to break boss shields as quickly as possible.

### Advanced Combat: Using Parries to Force Reactions

While standard rotations rely on skill cooldowns, advanced players use the **Parry** mechanic to bypass the "build-up" phase of combat. Because a successful parry fills the meter instantly, you can force a reaction swap even if your character was at 0% meter a moment prior. This is essential for maintaining the uptime of short-lived debuffs like Stain or Remora. When the enemy's portrait glows gold during an incoming attack, timing your block correctly allows for an immediate "Counter-Reaction" swap.

### Beginner Combat Tips and Tricks

1.  **Watch the Glow:** Never swap characters manually unless their portrait is glowing. A manual swap without a full meter resets your reaction chain and loses significant DPS.
2.  **Stack Passives:** Many characters have passives that buff specific reactions. For example, both Nanally and Jiuyuan have passives that enhance Blossom. These effects stack even if one of the characters is off-field.
3.  **Prioritize Break:** In boss fights, don't worry about raw damage until the "Break" window. Use the Discord reaction (Scorch + Nova) to deplete the white bar under the boss's health. Once the boss is staggered, swap to your main DPS (like Nanally) to unload Ultimates.

This period ends once you have established your presence in the city and completed the initial narrative arc involving the local authorities and the first major anomaly investigation.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-how-to-unlock-multiplayer/inline-2.webp)


Multiplayer in Neverness to Everness (NTE) is officially unlocked upon reaching **Hunter Level 8**. Reaching this level signifies that you have gained enough experience through combat and exploration to handle the challenges of a shared world instance.

In addition to the level requirement, you must complete the main story chapter titled **"Prologue II: Good Business, Better Treasure"**. This mission involves a series of tasks that introduce you to the trading and anomaly containment systems of Hethereau. Once the quest is turned in, you will receive a notification that "Social Functions" have been enabled.

The feature is accessed via the in-game smartphone menu. To find it, open your phone, swipe to the second page of applications, and look for the "Multiplayer" icon. It is important to remember that all players in a party must meet these requirements and be on the same server region (NA, EU, Asia, etc.) to see each other's join requests.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-how-to-unlock-multiplayer/inline-3.webp)


The process of connecting with friends in NTE is straightforward once the initial barriers are cleared. The game uses a "World Host" system where one player acts as the anchor for the session, and up to three others can join their version of Hethereau.

### How to Find and Share Your UID

Every player in Neverness to Everness is assigned a Unique ID (UID). This number is the primary way to find friends across the server. You can find your UID in the top-left corner of the main screen at all times, or by opening the profile section of your in-game smartphone. To add a friend, you must enter their UID into the "Add Friend" search bar within the Multiplayer app. Once they accept your request, they will appear in your friend list, showing their current Hunter Level, Tycoon Level, and whether they are currently "In-World" or "In-Instance" (such as a story dungeon).

### Inviting Friends and Joining Worlds

There are two primary ways to start a co-op session:
1.  **Invite to Join:** From your friend list, you can select a friend and click "Invite." This sends a notification to their screen. If they accept, they will be teleported to your current location in Hethereau.
2.  **Request Access:** If you see a friend is already online, you can click "Request to Join." The host must manually approve this request.

Note that NTE uses a World Level system. You can generally join players who have a lower Hunter Level than you, but you may be restricted from joining players with a significantly higher World Level if you have not yet completed the necessary "Ascension" trials to reach that tier.

### What You Can and Cannot Do in Co-op

In NTE, the multiplayer experience is expansive but has specific rules regarding progression and rewards.

*   **Combat and Drops:** When fighting enemies in the open world or during Anomaly Investigations, all players receive individual loot drops. If a rare material drops from a world boss, every player in the party can pick up their own instance of that item.
*   **Chests and Collectibles:** This is a point of frequent discussion among the community. Standard world chests and "Gift Boxes" found in the environment are typically host-exclusive. If you are a guest in someone else's world, you cannot open their chests. However, certain "Co-op Challenges" found in the city are designed specifically for multiple players and provide rewards to everyone present.
*   **Story Progression:** You cannot progress your main story missions while in a multiplayer session. If you have an active story quest, it will be paused until you return to single-player mode.
*   **The Tycoon System:** Guests can visit the host's owned properties and businesses. While they cannot manage the business or collect profits, they can interact with certain facilities and participate in social mini-games within those locations.

### Pre-Multiplayer Connection Checklist

Before attempting to connect, ensure the following conditions are met to avoid "Connection Failed" errors:
*   [ ] All players have reached Hunter Level 8.
*   [ ] The host and guests have completed 'Good Business, Better Treasure'.
*   [ ] All players are on the same regional server (Server transfers are not available).
*   [ ] The host's world is not currently full (4-player maximum).
*   [ ] No player is currently inside a "Solo-Only" story instance.

### Hunter Leveling Tips for New Players

The fastest way to increase your Hunter Level to reach the multiplayer threshold is to focus on the "Daily Commissions." These are short tasks assigned by the Hethereau Hunter Association that provide a massive amount of experience compared to standard mob grinding. Additionally, completing "Anomaly Investigations"—the blue markers on your map—will provide the necessary materials to upgrade your Espers, such as [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte), while also boosting your level. Using a strong character early on, such as those featured in "The Ichi-daime" banner, can significantly speed up the combat encounters required for these missions.

### Understanding Tycoon Level Progression

While Hunter Level is about power, Tycoon Level is about influence. You increase this by purchasing cars, decorating your apartment, and investing in local businesses. As your Tycoon Level rises, you unlock the ability to host more complex multiplayer events. For example, you cannot host a 4-player street race until you reach Tycoon Level 3 and own at least one vehicle capable of competitive performance. This progression ensures that players have a "stake" in the city of Hethereau before they begin competing with others.

### Social and Housing Features in Multiplayer

A major component of the NTE social experience is the "My House" feature. Once you purchase your first apartment in the city, you can invite friends to visit. Unlike the open world, the housing system is purely social. You can display your rare collectibles, customize the layout, and even set up small interactive games for your guests. The communication tools in NTE include a robust chat system with various Esper-themed emotes, allowing for coordination during combat or simply hanging out in your customized space.

Increasing your Tycoon Level will eventually allow you to purchase larger properties, such as penthouses or even commercial storefronts, which provide more space for guests and more sophisticated interaction options. This "life sim" aspect of Neverness to Everness is what sets it apart from other urban RPGs, making the multiplayer unlock a gateway to both high-octane combat and relaxed social play.

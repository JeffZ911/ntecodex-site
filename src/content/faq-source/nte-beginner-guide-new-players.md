### Hesperia Progression: Hunter and Appraisal Levels

Progression in Neverness to Everness is split between your identity as a supernatural investigator (Hunter) and your status as an urban entrepreneur (City Tycoon). 

The Hunter Level is your account-wide experience. It determines the maximum level of your Espers and the difficulty of the Anomalies you encounter in the open world. You increase this by completing main story missions, side quests involving Hesperia’s citizens, and clearing Anomaly Zones. 

The City Tycoon Level (sometimes referred to as Appraisal Level in early tutorials) represents your economic influence. As you buy properties, manage businesses, and complete urban challenges, this level rises. Higher levels are required to purchase luxury vehicles from top-tier dealerships and to unlock advanced blueprints for car customization. Balancing these two is vital; if your Hunter Level is high but your Tycoon Level is low, you will have powerful characters but struggle to afford the Fons required for high-end gear and vehicle maintenance.

### Combat Mastery: The Esper Cycle and Team Building

Combat in NTE revolves around the "Esper Cycle," a system where four characters swap in and out to trigger elemental reactions. Each Esper belongs to one of six elemental types: Cosmos, Incantation, Anima, Chaos, Psyche, and Lakshana. 

Triggering reactions is the key to dealing massive damage to "Anomaly Bosses." For example, applying Cosmos to a target and following up with Chaos triggers a undisclosed effect that shreds enemy shields.

### The Scarborough Fair Gacha System Explained

The Scarborough Fair is unlike traditional gacha banners. It is presented as a supernatural board game where players use "Dice" to progress. 

*   **Solid Dice:** Used for limited-time character banners (e.g., Nanally’s "The Ichi-daime" banner).
*   **Fabricated Dice:** Used for the permanent standard banner and weapon (Arc) banners.

When you "pull," you roll a die and move across a board. Each tile contains rewards like upgrade materials, Fons, or lower-tier Arcs. Landing on or passing specific "Key Tiles" builds your pity counter. The system features a 100% guarantee at 90 pulls, meaning you will definitely receive the featured 5-star character on your 90th roll. Notably, NTE does not use a 50/50 mechanic for its limited character banners; if you hit the 5-star milestone on a limited banner, it is always the featured unit.

### Urban Life: Cars, Customization, and Businesses

Hesperia is too large to explore on foot. While the game provides a basic sedan early on, your first major goal should be purchasing or earning the Novis ST-X 950. This motorcycle is favored by beginners because it can be summoned in narrow alleys and possesses high suspension, making it ideal for the "Anomaly Parkour" challenges found in the Miguel District.

For car enthusiasts, the Rampage Players shops offer deep customization. You can modify:
1.  **Performance:** Engine blocks, turbos, and tires affect top speed and handling.
2.  **Aesthetics:** Paint, decals, and "Anomaly Underglow" (which reacts to your active Esper's element).
3.  **Utility:** Some cars can be equipped with "Echolocation" kits to find hidden collectibles in the city.

### Resource Management: Pixels, Fons, and Annulith

Managing your "stamina" is crucial for long-term growth. Neverness to Everness uses two distinct energy systems:

*   **Character Pixels:** This is your daily regenerating energy (capped at 240). Use these to enter "Memory Fractures" to farm Esper EXP materials, Arc ascension items, and talent books.
*   **City Stamina:** This is a weekly resource (refreshes every Monday). It is used for "Hethereau Hobbies"—mini-games like street racing, urban delivery, and supernatural photography. These are the best ways to earn large amounts of Fons.

**Annulith** is the premium currency. It is primarily used to purchase Solid Dice for the Scarborough Fair gacha. As a new player, avoid spending Annulith on Character Pixel refreshes or car parts. Save every bit of Annulith for limited character banners, as the power gap between 4-star and 5-star Espers is significant in the endgame "Everness Trials."

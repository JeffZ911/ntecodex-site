### Account Progression: Hunter Level vs. Appraisal Level

Progression in Hethereau is split into two distinct tracks. Your Hunter Level is your overall account experience. You earn Hunter EXP from almost every activity, including main story quests, side commissions, and spending "Pixels" (the game's version of stamina) on combat challenges. 

The Appraisal Level is the gatekeeper for your power. Every 10 Hunter Levels, you will face an Appraisal Trial. Successfully completing this trial raises your Appraisal Level, which increases the level cap for your characters, Arcs (weapons), and Consoles (artifacts). It also upgrades the rewards found in the open world and within the "Urban Mirage" endgame mode. If you find the world becoming too difficult, you can manually lower your Appraisal Level by one tier through the world settings menu, though this will reduce the rarity of item drops.

### The Tycoon System: Building Your Business Empire

The Tycoon system is not just a side activity; it is the primary source of Fons (the premium currency used for gacha) and passive income. After completing Chapter 1 of the Hesperia Incident, you gain access to your first property, a small cafe. 

You must manage inventory, hire staff from your roster of A-rank characters, and complete delivery missions. Unlike combat which uses Pixels, Tycoon activities use "City Stamina." This separate energy pool regenerates over time and is spent on "Uber-style" driving missions where you transport NPCs across Hethereau. High-tier Tycoon levels unlock the ability to customize your storefronts and, most importantly, provide the shards necessary to upgrade Chiz, the free S-rank Nature unit.

### Combat Fundamentals: Mastering the Esper Cycle

Combat in Neverness to Everness revolves around the Stagger meter and the Esper Cycle. Every enemy has a white bar beneath their health; when this bar is depleted through elemental attacks, the enemy enters a "Fractured" state, taking [information unavailable] massive increased damage.

The Esper Cycle refers to the synergy created by switching characters. When a character uses their "Entry Skill" (triggered by swapping when the icon glows), they apply an elemental status. Combining these statuses creates powerful reactions

**Common Esper Cycle Reactions:**

| Reaction Name | Elements Involved | Combat Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Blossom | Nature + Water | Creates spores that explode for AoE Nature damage. |
| Scorch | Fire + Wind | Increases the duration of burn and spreads it to nearby enemies. |

| Conductivity | Electric + Water | Chains damage between enemies and applies a brief stun. |

Pay close attention to the "Red Flash" mechanic. When an enemy's eyes or weapon flash red, an incoming heavy attack is imminent. Performing a perfect dodge during this window triggers a "Chrono Shift," slowing down time and instantly filling the swap gauge for your entire team.

### Gacha & Resource Management: The Scarborough Fair Board

The gacha system in NTE, known as "Scarborough Fair," uses a unique Monopoly-style board. Instead of a simple animation, you roll dice to move across a board filled with upgrade materials, Arcs, and character shards. 

**Scarborough Fair Gacha Rates & Pity:**

*   **S-Rank Base Rate:** 0.8%
*   **A-Rank Base Rate:** 6.0%
*   **Soft Pity:** Begins at 70 pulls, where the S-rank rate increases significantly per pull.
*   **Hard Pity:** 90 pulls.
*   **Limited Banner Rule:** There is no 50/50. If you hit an S-rank on a limited banner like Nanally’s "The Ichi-daime," it is guaranteed to be the featured character.
*   **Standard Selector:** After 50 pulls on the Standard Board, you can choose one S-rank character from the permanent pool (Haniel, Chiz, or [information unavailable]).

### Urban Life: Vehicles, Anomalies, and Movement

Hethereau is massive, and walking is inefficient. You receive your first vehicle, the E-Motor bike, during the prologue. Vehicles can be summoned almost anywhere in the city except inside buildings. The customization shop, "The Grease Pit," allows you to modify the engine for higher top speeds and change the neon underglow.

While traveling, keep an eye out for "Anomalies." These are supernatural disturbances indicated by visual distortions, static on your UI, or NPCs behaving strangely. Solving these world puzzles grants Hunter EXP and "Anomaly Shards," which are traded at the Hesperia Hub for high-tier Arcs.

### Gear Optimization: Arcs and Tetris-style Consoles

Characters equip one Arc (Weapon) and a set of Consoles (Artifacts). Arcs provide base stats and a passive ability, while Consoles are the primary source of build customization. 

The Console system uses a Tetris-like puzzle grid. Each Console has a specific shape (L-shape, T-shape, square, etc.) and must be fitted into a 5x5 or 6x6 grid. You cannot simply equip the "best" items; you must solve the spatial puzzle to fit as many high-stat Consoles as possible. Matching the "Brand" of the Consoles (e.g., the "Hethereau Heavy Industries" set) provides bonus effects like increased Fire damage or faster City Stamina regeneration.

*   **Weekly:** Clear the "Urban Mirage" floors for Scarborough Fair dice.
*   **Weekly:** Complete 5 "Uber-style" driving tasks to cap your weekly City Stamina rewards.

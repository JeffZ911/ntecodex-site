## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/neverness-to-everness-pity-system-guide/inline-2.webp)


Neverness to Everness uses a 90-pull hard pity system for S-Rank characters on limited banners. A significant departure from the industry standard is the lack of a 50/50 mechanic; when you hit an S-Rank on a limited character banner, the featured character is 100% guaranteed. Soft pity begins at 70 pulls, at which point the "Scarborough Fair" board transforms to significantly increase the frequency of S-Rank tiles.

The weapon system, known as Arc Research, operates on a separate pity scale. You are guaranteed a random S-Rank Arc at 60 pulls and the specific featured signature weapon at 80 pulls. While character and weapon pity carry over to future limited banners of the same type, progress toward cosmetic milestones (such as car skins or outfits) is typically tied to specific banner durations.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/neverness-to-everness-pity-system-guide/inline-3.webp)


The summoning system in Neverness to Everness is visually and mechanically distinct, styled after a Monopoly-inspired board game rather than a simple animation sequence. This system, known as the Scarborough Fair, integrates movement and RNG in a way that directly affects your pity progress.

### The Scarborough Fair Board Game Mechanic

When you spend your Dice (Solid or Fabricated) to summon, you are not just "pulling" from a pool; you are rolling to move an avatar across the Scarborough Fair board. Each tile on the board represents a potential reward, ranging from upgrade materials and A-Rank items to the coveted S-Rank characters.

Players can choose to roll one die at a time or perform a "10-roll" which fast-forwards the movement across the board. The position of your avatar does not negatively impact your pity; every roll counts as a single pull toward your 90-pull guarantee. However, the board aesthetic serves a functional purpose during soft pity, where the tiles physically change to represent the increased odds of landing on a high-tier reward.

### Limited Character Banners: The Death of 50/50

The most notable feature of the Neverness to Everness pity system is the 100% guarantee on limited banners. In most gacha games, hitting the highest rarity for the first time only gives you a 50% chance of getting the featured character. In NTE, if you see the S-Rank animation on a limited banner like "The Ichi-daime" ([Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)’s debut banner), you are guaranteed to receive the featured unit.

This removal of the 50/50 gamble makes the 90-pull hard pity much more valuable. You can plan your pulls with absolute certainty, knowing that 14,400 Annulith (the cost of 90 Solid Dice) is the maximum price for any featured character.

### Soft Pity and Board Modification

While the hard pity is set at 90, most players will acquire their S-Rank character between 70 and 80 pulls. This is due to the "Board Modification" mechanic that activates at 70 pulls without an S-Rank.

At the 70-pull mark, the Scarborough Fair board undergoes a visual transformation. The number of S-Rank tiles increases, and the base probability jumps from 0.99% to 19.59% per roll. This ramp-up continues until the 90th pull, where the entire board effectively becomes a single S-Rank tile, ensuring the guarantee.

### Arc Research: Weapon Gacha Rules

Weapon banners in NTE are called Arc Research Programs. These use a different currency called Tri-Keys and have a lower pity threshold than characters. 

1.  **Random S-Rank (60 Pulls):** You are guaranteed an S-Rank Arc within 60 pulls. However, this has a 25% chance of being the featured signature weapon and a 75% chance of being a standard S-Rank Arc.
2.  **Featured Guarantee (80 Pulls):** If you do not receive the featured Arc within your first 60 pulls, the 80th pull acts as a safety net, providing the featured signature weapon with 100% certainty.

This "safety net" system ensures that even with poor luck, you can fully equip your character within 80 pulls.

### Standard Board and the 50-Pull Selector

The Standard Board is the permanent summoning pool in Neverness to Everness. It uses Fabricated Dice and features a one-time beginner bonus. For the first 50 pulls on the Standard Board, players receive a 20% discount on Fabricated Dice costs (8 Dice for a 10-roll).

Upon completing 50 pulls on the Standard Board, players are granted an S-Rank Selector. This allows you to choose one permanent S-Rank character to add to your roster immediately.

### Pity Carry-Over Rules and Exceptions

Pity in Neverness to Everness is persistent across banners of the same type. If you perform 40 pulls on a limited character banner and the banner ends, those 40 pulls carry over to the next limited character banner. You will still only need 50 more pulls to hit the 90-pull hard pity.

**What Carries Over:**
*   **Limited Character Pity:** Carries to the next limited character banner.
*   **Arc Research Pity:** Carries to the next weapon banner.
*   **Standard Pity:** Permanent and never resets.

**What Does NOT Carry Over:**
*   **Cosmetic Milestones:** NTE features a milestone system for additional rewards based on total pulls on a specific banner. These rewards (car skins, gliders, outfits) are usually exclusive to that banner's duration or its specific rerun.

### Gacha Currencies: Annulith, Dice, and Tri-Keys

Managing your currency is vital to avoid accidental waste. The primary premium currency is Annulith, which can be converted into three different summoning items

1.  **Solid Dice:** Used for Limited Character Banners. These should be your primary focus for Annulith spending.
2.  **Fabricated Dice:** Used for the Standard Board. These are often given for free through exploration, missions, and the City Tycoon mode. It is generally advised not to spend Annulith on these.
3.  **Tri-Keys:** Used for Arc Research (Weapons). Only spend Annulith here if you already have the character associated with the featured Arc.

### The Warp Exchange

When you pull duplicate characters or weapons, you receive secondary currencies: Warp Pieces (from S-Rank duplicates) and Lost Pieces (from A-Rank and lower). These can be spent in the Warp Exchange shop.

The Warp Exchange is a reliable way to obtain standard S-Rank characters without relying on the gacha. After a certain number of rotations, standard characters like Sakiri or Hathor will appear in the shop for a set amount of Warp Pieces. Additionally, you can buy monthly discounted Solid Dice and Tri-Keys here, which should be a priority for every player.

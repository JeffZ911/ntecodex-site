## Direct Answer

Elemental Resonance in Neverness to Everness (NTE) is primarily driven by the "Esper Cycle" system, where swapping between characters with full Esper Meters triggers powerful reactions. These reactions occur when you swap between characters whose elements are adjacent on the hexagonal "Esper Wheel."

There are six Duo Cycle reactions, such as Blossom, Scorch, and Remora, which provide immediate tactical advantages or damage over time. Additionally, the game features two powerful Triple Cycle reactions: Charge and Discord. Charge (triggered by the "Top Trio") focuses on massive energy gain and ability uptime, while Discord (triggered by the "Bottom Trio") is designed for high stagger damage and breaking enemy shields.

Beyond these active cycles, "Resonance" also refers to character-specific passive buffs unlocked at Awakening levels A3 and A6. These "Awakening Resonances" significantly enhance elemental output or provide unique team-wide utility, such as [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)’s ability to boost the duration of Vita Buds in a Blossom-focused team.

### The Esper Wheel and Adjacency Rules

The foundation of all elemental interactions in NTE is the Esper Wheel. This hexagonal chart organizes the six primary elements into a specific order. Understanding this order is vital because reactions only trigger when you transition between elements that are physically next to each other on the wheel.

The six Esper Elements are

| Element | Color | Group Association | Primary Combat Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Lakshana | Orange | Top Trio | Physical Stability & Force |
| Cosmos | Yellow | Top Trio | Light & Aether Manipulation |
| Anima | Green | Top Trio | Nature & Life-force |
| Incantation | Blue | Bottom Trio | Ice & Crowd Control |
| Chaos | Red | Bottom Trio | Fire & Explosive Damage |
| Psyche | Purple | Bottom Trio | Gravity & Entropy |

The wheel is arranged such that Lakshana is at the top-center, followed clockwise by Cosmos, Anima, Incantation, Chaos, and Psyche. Adjacency is the strict requirement for a Duo Cycle. For example, swapping from a Chaos character to an Incantation character triggers a reaction because they are adjacent. Swapping from Chaos to Cosmos does not trigger a Duo Cycle reaction because they are on opposite sides of the wheel.

### Mastering the Esper Meter and Character Swapping

To trigger any Elemental Resonance effect, the active character must first fill their Esper Meter. This meter is visible as a circular gauge around the character's portrait or as a bar near the health display.

There are three primary ways to fill the Esper Meter
1.  **Normal and Heavy Attacks:** Consistent damage dealing builds the meter steadily.
2.  **Esper Skills:** Using a character's unique abilities provides a significant chunk of meter.
3.  **Perfect Parry:** This is the most efficient method. Executing a "Perfect Parry" against an incoming enemy attack triggers an "Instant Fill" mechanic, immediately capping the meter and allowing for an instant swap-reaction.

Once the meter is full, the portraits of your off-field characters will glow. Tapping a glowing portrait executes an "Esper Swap," pulling the new character onto the field with a high-impact entry move and triggering the corresponding Elemental Resonance reaction based on the element of the character leaving the field and the character entering.

Triple Cycles are advanced maneuvers that require you to cycle through three characters of a specific "Trio" group in succession. To trigger these, all three characters in your party must belong to either the Top Trio or the Bottom Trio.

Support gear in NTE is not just about raw stats; it is about maximizing "Charge Efficiency" to keep Ultimates active and ensuring that team-wide buffs have 100% uptime.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-best-disk-sets-support/inline-3.webp)


### The Console & Module System Explained

Unlike traditional gacha gear systems, Neverness to Everness uses a "Console" system that functions like a spatial puzzle. Each character has a unique grid (the Console) where you must fit "Modules"—the individual pieces that make up a Cartridge set. These Modules come in various geometric shapes, similar to Tetris blocks.

To activate a 4-piece Legendary set bonus, you must slot four Modules belonging to that specific Cartridge into the Console. However, the system allows for more than four Modules to be equipped simultaneously. Any extra space on the grid can be filled with "loose" Modules that provide raw stats or trigger a character's **Console Passive**.

Every character in NTE has a unique passive that grants bonuses based on the **Type** of Modules equipped. Modules are categorized into three main types
*   **Type II:** Small, 2-cell blocks.
*   **Type III:** Medium, 3-cell blocks.
*   **Type IV:** Large, 4-cell blocks.

For example, Nanally gains a significant CRIT Rate bonus for every Type II Module on her board, while Edgar gains 10% additional Max HP for every Type III Module. When building a support character, you must balance the 4-piece Cartridge bonus with the character's preferred Module Type to maximize their innate potential.

### Top-Tier Support Cartridges: Deep Dive

#### 1. Speedy Hedgehog (The Universal Buffer)
Speedy Hedgehog is arguably the most important set in the current meta. Its 2-piece bonus of 12% Charge Efficiency allows supports to generate Ultimate energy faster. The 4-piece bonus is the real prize: a 15% team-wide ATK buff. Since this effect does not stack, you should only run one character with this set per team. It is ideally placed on a character with a low-cost Ultimate or high energy generation, such as Zero or Hotori.

#### 2. Thea’s Night Tavern (The Healer’s Choice)
Dedicated healers like Edgar rely on Thea’s Night Tavern to keep the team topped off during difficult boss fights. The 10% HP bonus from the 2-piece set scales directly with Edgar’s healing area, "Finnegan’s Wake," while the 20% Healing Bonus ensures that even minor heals provide significant recovery. If you find your team dying too quickly in the Rabbit Hole's deeper levels, switching your healer to this set is the first step in troubleshooting your build.

#### 3. Tiny Big Adventure (Survival Scaling)
This set is tailor-made for Fadia. As a Psyche-type character, Fadia’s kit revolves around damage redirection—she takes a portion of the damage dealt to her allies. Tiny Big Adventure rewards this playstyle by granting a 4% Max HP stack every time the wearer is hit. At 10 stacks, Fadia gains 40% more HP, which increases both her survivability and the potency of her redirection mechanics. Casting her Ultimate instantly grants all 10 stacks, providing an immediate defensive boost.

#### 4. Kingdom’s Guard (The Shielder’s Bastion)
Adler is currently the primary user of Kingdom’s Guard. His kit creates shields based on his DEF stat. This Cartridge provides a 15% DEF boost at two pieces and a 20% Shield Strength bonus at four pieces. Because Adler’s Console prefers Type III Modules, you should look for Kingdom’s Guard pieces that specifically fit the Type III horizontal or "L-shape" patterns to maximize his DEF-scaling damage.

### Character-Specific Gear Recommendations

When gearing your supports, you must match the Cartridge to the character's elemental role and scaling needs.

*   **Fadia:** Priority is HP. Use **Tiny Big Adventure** with as many Type III Modules as possible to trigger her survival passives. Focus on HP% main stats on the Cartridge.
*   **Edgar:** Priority is Healing and HP. Use **Thea’s Night Tavern**. Edgar’s Console Bonus rewards Type III Modules, so prioritize those shapes even if you have higher-rarity Type II pieces available.
*   **Adler:** Priority is DEF. Use **Kingdom’s Guard**. Since his damage also scales with DEF, this set effectively serves as both a support and a DPS set for him.
*   **Zero (Main Protagonist):** Priority is Charge Efficiency. Zero acts as a "Battery" for the team. **Speedy Hedgehog** is the best fit, allowing Zero to trigger the team ATK buff frequently.

### Substat Priority for Support Units

NTE is more forgiving than other gacha games because you can see all four substats on a Module or Cartridge the moment you acquire it. There is no "hidden" stat that unlocks later. For support characters, you should ignore offensive stats like CRIT Rate or Elemental DMG in favor of utility.

1.  **Charge Efficiency:** This is the single most important stat for supports. Higher efficiency means more frequent Ultimates, which means higher uptime for buffs like the Speedy Hedgehog's ATK boost.
2.  **HP% / DEF%:** Most supports in NTE scale their utility (healing or shielding) off their total health or defense.
3.  **Break Intensity:** If your support character spends a lot of time on the field (like Adler or Sakiri), having high Break Intensity helps stagger enemies faster, opening them up for your DPS.

### How to Farm Support Cartridges Efficiently

To acquire high-rank Cartridges, you must venture into the **Rabbit Hole Anomaly Zones**, located on the south side of the **New Herland District**. 

*   **Character Pixels:** Each run costs 40 Character Pixels (the game's stamina system). 
*   **Carrota Coins:** Completing these zones also rewards you with Carrota Coins (Bronze, Silver, or Gold depending on difficulty). 
*   **The Rewind System:** Carrota Coins are used in the "Rewind" gacha, located on the Console screen. This is the primary way to obtain specific Module shapes. 

For early-game players, focus on farming A-Rank (Pink) sets. Once you reach Hunter Level 30, you should transition exclusively to S-Rank (Orange) farming. Always check the "Drop Info" on the Anomaly Zone entrance to ensure you are farming the specific set you need, as different zones drop different Cartridges.

### Best Support Arcs

Your Cartridge sets should be paired with Arcs (weapons) that complement their bonuses. 
*   **Day Off (S-Rank):** This is the premier support Arc, offering a massive 33% Charge Efficiency substat and a passive that restores Ultimate Energy when enemies are defeated. It pairs perfectly with the **Speedy Hedgehog** set.
*   **The Forgotten (A-Rank):** A solid F2P-friendly option for HP-scaling supports like Fadia or Edgar. It provides a 25% HP boost and grants ATK or DEF buffs based on the wearer's current health.
*   **Mind Royale (A-Rank):** Excellent for Edgar, as it provides 60 Break Intensity and grants 10 Ultimate Energy every time a Redirect Skill is used.

### Mastering the Esper Cycle

Support buffs are most effective when timed with the [Neverness to Everness Esper Cycle guide]. The goal is to use your support’s Ultimate to trigger the **Speedy Hedgehog** ATK buff right before swapping to your main DPS (like Nanally) to trigger a reaction like **Blossom** or **Remora**. 

Support characters often provide the "Setup" element. For example, Edgar can apply the Cosmos element to enemies, which your Anima-element DPS can then react with to trigger the Blossom effect, dealing massive area-of-effect damage.

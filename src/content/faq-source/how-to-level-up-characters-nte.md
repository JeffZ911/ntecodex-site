## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-level-up-characters-nte/inline-2.webp)


To level up characters (Espers) in NTE, you must consume Hunter Guides and Beetle Coins within the "Develop" menu of the character screen. Character progression is not a linear path; Espers are soft-capped every 10 to 20 levels. To bypass these caps, you must perform an Ascension, which requires specific boss drops from Anomaly Zones and regional specialty items found throughout Hethereau.

Your maximum character level is strictly gated by your Appraisal Level. This is an account-wide tier that increases as you raise your Hunter Level through missions and exploration. You cannot level an Esper to 80 until your account reaches the required Appraisal Level milestone. Beyond base levels, an Esper's power is further increased through Ability upgrades, the Awakening system using Mind Shards for Resonance bonuses, and Life Skill development which impacts their performance during the Esper Cycle.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-level-up-characters-nte/inline-3.webp)


The progression system in Neverness to Everness is designed to prevent players from over-leveling a single character and trivializing content. Instead, it encourages a balanced approach where your account rank dictates the strength of your roster. This multi-layered system ensures that as you explore deeper into the urban anomalies, your characters grow in tandem with the world's difficulty.

Character Pixels (stamina) act as the primary gatekeeper for all leveling activities. Whether you are farming Hunter Guides or hunting bosses for Ascension materials, you will need to manage your Pixels daily. Because Beetle Coin costs scale aggressively at higher levels, focusing on a core team of four characters—ideally covering different elements like Cosmos or Physical—is the most efficient strategy for the early to mid-game.

### Using Hunter Guides and Beetle Coins

The fundamental way to increase an Esper's level is by using Hunter Guides. These are digital manuals that provide flat Experience Points (EXP) to the character. You can access this by selecting an Esper in the menu and clicking the "Develop" tab.

Beetle Coins are consumed for every point of EXP gained. At lower levels, the cost is negligible, but moving from level 70 to 80 requires a significant reserve of coins. It is common to run out of Beetle Coins before running out of Hunter Guides, so participating in City Tycoon business activities is a necessary part of the leveling loop to maintain your gold flow.

### Ascension: Breaking Level Caps with Boss Drops

Once an Esper reaches their current maximum level (such as 20, 40, or 50), they hit a "Cap." To continue leveling, you must perform an Ascension. Each Ascension increases the base stats of the character significantly and unlocks new tiers for their Abilities.

Ascension requires three types of materials:
1.  **Boss Materials:** Dropped by Major Anomalies in the open world or within specific instances. For example, [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) requires drops from the "Phantom of the Opera" anomaly.
2.  **Regional Specialties:** Items gathered in the streets of Hethereau, such as "Neon Flora" or "Discarded Circuits."
3.  **Enemy Drops:** Common materials obtained by defeating standard urban thugs or minor anomalies.

The number of materials required increases with each Ascension tier. High-tier Ascensions also require higher-rarity versions of these materials, which can be crafted using the "Synthesis" station in the urban hub.

### The Hunter Level and Appraisal Level Connection

Your Hunter Level represents your overall account progress. As you complete story chapters, side urban requests, and daily tasks, you gain Hunter EXP. At specific intervals (usually every 10 Hunter Levels), you will be prompted to complete an Appraisal Trial.

Successfully completing the trial increases your Appraisal Level. This level governs the "World Tier," making enemies in the city tougher but also increasing the quality of the rewards they drop. Most importantly, your Appraisal Level dictates the maximum level your Espers can reach.

If you find that the "Level Up" button is greyed out despite having enough Hunter Guides, check your Appraisal Level in the Exploration Guide menu.

### Upgrading Esper Abilities and Talents

Leveling the character's base stats is only half the battle. Each Esper has a set of active and passive abilities that must be leveled independently. This is done through the "Abilities" tab in the development menu.

Upgrading these skills requires "Ability Scripts" which are categorized by the character's role (e.g., Attack, Support, or Tank). Like Ascension, these upgrades are gated by the character's current level. You cannot max out an Esper's ultimate ability if the Esper itself is only level 20. Focusing on the primary damage-dealing skill for your main DPS character should be your priority to ensure you can clear timed challenges like the Rabbit Hole Anomaly Zones.

### The Awakening System: Mind Shards and Resonances

Awakening is the process of using duplicate character data, known as Mind Shards, to unlock Resonance bonuses. This is the primary way to gain massive power spikes without increasing the character's numerical level.

Each Esper has six Resonance levels. While every level provides a stat boost, the 3-piece and 6-piece milestones usually offer transformative gameplay changes, such as reducing cooldowns or adding new elemental effects to attacks.

*   **Resonance 1-2:** Minor stat increases (ATK, HP, or DEF).
*   **Resonance 3:** Significant passive upgrade (e.g., "Increases Cosmos damage by 15% after using a Dodge Offset").
*   **Resonance 6:** Maximum potential (e.g., "Ultimate Ability now resets the cooldown of all Basic Skills").

Mind Shards are primarily obtained through the "Urban Resonance" gantry (the gacha system), though some can be earned through high-level City Tycoon rewards or event shops.

### Life Skills and the Esper Cycle

A unique mechanic in Neverness to Everness is the Life Skill system. Every Esper has a set of non-combat skills, such as "Street Racing," "Gourmet Cooking," or "Business Management." As you use these characters in the city for non-combat activities, their Life Skill levels increase.

Higher Life Skill levels improve the "Esper Cycle," which is a passive buff system. When an Esper is "in sync" with their city environment, they gain minor combat bonuses or increased resource drop rates. While this doesn't directly increase the character's level, it is a vital part of the end-game progression loop that separates a standard build from a specialized one.

### Best Farming Locations for Leveling Materials

To level up efficiently, you must spend your Character Pixels on the right activities. The following locations are the most efficient for gathering resources:

1.  **Houdinii’s Magic Stage:** This is the primary source for Hunter Guides and Beetle Coins. It features multiple difficulty tiers that unlock as your Appraisal Level rises.
2.  **Rabbit Hole Anomaly Zones:** These function as the "Artifact" or "Module" domains but also provide significant Esper EXP and Ability Scripts.
3.  **Urban Patrols:** For regional specialties and common enemy drops, use the map's tracking feature to locate specific mobs.
4.  **Major Anomaly Bosses:** These are marked on the Hethereau map with large purple icons. They require 40 Pixels to claim rewards and are the only source for high-level Ascension materials.

Character leveling is just one part of the power equation in NTE. To truly master the game's combat, you must also look into your equipment and team synergy.

### NTE Arc Upgrade Basics

Arcs are the weapons used by Espers. Like characters, they have their own levels and Ascension tiers.
*   **Dyes:** Used as EXP material for Arcs.
*   **Appleseeds:** Used to ascend Arcs and break level caps.
*   **Refinement:** Using duplicate Arcs to increase the weapon's passive effect.
It is generally recommended to keep your main DPS's Arc level equal to the character's level. If your character is level 60, their Arc should also be level 60.

### Understanding the Console and Module Tetris

The "Console" is NTE's version of an equipment system. You equip "Cartridges" and "Modules" to boost base stats like Critical Rate and Elemental Damage.
*   **Cartridges:** Provide the main stat and set bonuses.
*   **Modules:** These are smaller components that fit into a grid-based system (similar to Tetris). You must arrange them efficiently to fit the most powerful bonuses into the limited space of the Console.
Upgrading Modules requires "Module Data" and Beetle Coins. This system becomes the primary focus once your characters reach the level 80 cap.

### Daily Checklist for Efficient Progression

To ensure you never fall behind the Appraisal Level curve, follow this daily routine:
1.  **Spend Pixels:** Never let your Character Pixels cap at 180/180. If you are short on time, dump them into Houdinii’s Magic Stage for Hunter Guides.
2.  **City Commissions:** Complete the four daily urban requests for a large chunk of Hunter EXP.
3.  **Business Check-in:** Collect passive income from your City Tycoon properties to fund your Beetle Coin needs.
4.  **Anomaly Cleanup:** Defeat at least two world bosses per week to stock up on Ascension materials, even if you don't need them immediately.

By following this structured approach to leveling and resource management, you will be well-prepared for the high-level anomalies and competitive challenges found in the later stages of Neverness to Everness.

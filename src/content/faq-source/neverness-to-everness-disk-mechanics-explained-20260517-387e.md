*   How do Cartridges, Modules, and the Console system interact to provide stats?

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/neverness-to-everness-disk-mechanics-explained-20260517-387e/inline-2.webp)


The "Disk" mechanics in Neverness to Everness refer to the modular gear system housed within an Esper's Console. This system consists of a central Cartridge—a circular disc providing a major Main Stat and four Sub Stats—and various Modules, which are Tetris-shaped blocks that fit into the surrounding grid.

The Console acts as a 5x5 grid (though size varies by Esper level) where players must strategically place Modules to activate "Tetris Passives." These passives are character-specific bonuses triggered by equipping a set number of specific Module types (Type II, III, or IV). 

NTE distinguishes itself from other urban RPGs through two key features
1.  **Stat Overlap:** A single gear piece can have the same attribute as both its Main Stat and a Sub Stat, allowing for extreme specialization.
2.  **Stat Transparency:** All Sub Stat values are visible the moment an item is acquired, removing the need to level an item just to see if it is "good."

## Detailed Explanation

### The Console: Your Tetris Gear Board

The Console is the heart of an Esper's power. When you open the equipment menu for a character like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or Mint, you are presented with a grid. This is not just a visual flair; it is a functional puzzle. The center of the grid features a dedicated circular port for the Cartridge. The remaining tiles are where you must fit your Modules.

As you increase an Esper’s level and Appraisal Rank, more tiles on the Console grid unlock. Early game characters may only have a small 3x3 area available, while endgame Espers utilize a full grid that allows for the placement of multiple Type IV Modules. The challenge lies in the fact that Modules cannot overlap. If you have a powerful "L-shaped" Module with perfect stats, you must ensure your other "I-shaped" or "Square" Modules leave enough room for it to fit.

### Cartridges: The Core Disc Equipment

Cartridges function as the "Engine" of your build. They are categorized by element—Physical, Fire, Ice, Ether, etc. You generally cannot equip a Fire Cartridge on an Ice Esper, as the Console will reject the synchronization.

Each Cartridge comes with one Main Stat. For DPS characters, you are typically looking for Crit Rate or Elemental DMG Cartridges. For supports, Energy Regeneration or Max HP is often preferred. 

**Cartridge Enhancement Milestones:**
Leveling a Cartridge requires "Data Fragments" and Fons (the standard currency). As you reach specific level milestones, the Sub Stats are activated or enhanced

*   **Level +5:** First Sub Stat value increases.
*   **Level +10:** Second Sub Stat value increases.
*   **Level +15:** Third Sub Stat value increases.
*   **Level +20:** Fourth Sub Stat value increases and the Main Stat receives a final [exact value pending official confirmation] boost.

Unlike other games where leveling "unlocks" a hidden stat, in NTE, you see all four Sub Stats at Level 0. Leveling simply increases their potency.

### Modules: Tetris Blocks and Types

Modules provide the "Tetris Passives" that define a character's playstyle. They are divided into three main categories based on their complexity and the bonuses they provide.

**Module Types and Character Passive Examples:**

| Module Type | Common Shapes | Example Passive (Nanally) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |

The "Tetris Passive" is the most critical part of build-crafting. For example, Nanally’s signature build focuses on stacking as many Type II Modules as possible to maximize her Crit Rate. This requires finding Type II Modules with high-tier Sub Stats, which is often more difficult than fitting one large Type IV Module.

### Stat Overlap and Upgrading

One of the most player-friendly aspects of the Neverness to Everness disk mechanics is Stat Overlap. In most gacha games, if an item has "ATK %" as a Main Stat, it cannot have "ATK %" as a Sub Stat. NTE removes this restriction.

If you find an S-rank (Orange) Cartridge with a Crit Rate Main Stat, it is entirely possible for that same Cartridge to have a Crit Rate Sub Stat. This allows players to reach the "Crit Ceiling" much faster than in competing titles. 

Furthermore, the upgrade process is transparent. Because the values are visible from the moment of acquisition, you can immediately identify "god-tier" pieces. If a Module drops with low base rolls on its Sub Stats, you can safely use it as upgrade fodder without worrying that it might have "rolled well" later on.

### The Rewind System: How to Get Gear

Acquiring high-end gear in NTE involves two distinct loops: farming Anomaly Zones and utilizing the Rewind system.

**Farming Cartridges:**
Cartridges are obtained by clearing Anomaly Zones. These are combat challenges that consume "Character Pixels" (the game’s stamina system). The quality of the Cartridge depends on your Appraisal Level.
*   **Appraisal Level 1-10:** Primarily C-rank (Blue) and B-rank (Purple) drops.
*   **Appraisal Level 11-20:** A-rank (Gold) drops become common.
*   **Appraisal Level 21+:** S-rank (Orange) drops are unlocked.

**The Rewind System:**
Modules are primarily acquired through the Rewind system, a specialized gacha mechanic located in the New Herland District. To use Rewind, you need **Carrota Coins**. These coins are earned through city activities, such as managing your shops in the City Tycoon mode or completing urban requests.

In the Rewind interface, you can select a "Target Shape" or "Target Type." This significantly reduces the RNG involved in finding that one specific L-shaped Type III Module you need to complete your Nanally grid. You spend Carrota Coins to "Rewind" the data, generating a new set of Modules.

### Arcs (Weapons) vs. Disks

It is important to distinguish between Disks (Cartridges/Modules) and Arcs. In the NTE community, players sometimes use these terms interchangeably, but they are mechanically distinct. 
*   **Arcs:** These are the primary weapons obtained via the "Tri-Key" banner system. They provide a massive base ATK boost and a unique weapon skill.
*   **Disks:** These are the farmable gear pieces explained in this guide. You do not pull for Disks in the premium gacha; you farm them through gameplay and the Rewind system.

### Esper Cycles and Elemental Synergy

The stats you choose for your Disks should align with your team's "Esper Cycle." This refers to the rotation of elemental reactions, such as Nova (Ether + Ice) or Scorch (Fire + Physical). If your team relies on Scorch, your Cartridges should prioritize "Anomaly Proficiency" to increase the damage of the burn effect.

### Farming Carrota Coins in New Herland

Since Modules are essential for activating Tetris Passives, you will need a steady supply of Carrota Coins. The most efficient way to farm these is by engaging with the **City Tycoon** mechanics. By upgrading your boutique or cafe in the New Herland District, you generate passive income in the form of Fons and Carrota Coins. High-level players often spend more time optimizing their shop layouts than they do in combat, simply because the Module Rewind system is so vital for endgame power.

Choosing the right equipment in Neverness to Everness (NTE) is the difference between struggling against high-level Anomalies and effortlessly clearing the Hethereau endgame content. To maximize your damage output, you must understand how to optimize your S-Rank Arcs and Cartridge sets.

# Best 5-Star Disk for NTE DPS: Top S-Rank Arcs and Cartridges

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/best-5-star-disk-nte-dps/inline-1.webp)


What is the best 5-star disk for a DPS character in Neverness to Everness (NTE)? This is a common question among Assessors looking to optimize their damage dealers like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte), Mint, or the main character, Zero. In the context of NTE's gearing system, the term "Disk" is often used interchangeably by the community to refer to two distinct but connected systems: Arcs and Cartridges.

Arcs are the primary "weapons" in NTE. They provide a massive base Attack (ATK) stat, a critical secondary stat (such as CRIT Rate or ATK%), and a unique passive effect that can fundamentally change a character's playstyle. Cartridges, on the other hand, are the "equipment" or "gear sets" that you slot into your character's Console. While Arcs provide the raw power, Cartridges provide the elemental bonuses and set effects that allow your DPS to scale into the late-game meta of May 2026.

Choosing the right S-Rank (5-star) disk is critical because resources like upgrade materials and "Character Pixels" (the game's stamina system) are limited. Investing in a sub-optimal S-Rank Arc early on can stall your progress in the Bureau of Anomaly Control's high-tier challenges. Furthermore, NTE uses a strict "Type" system where Arcs must match the character's Type (Plasma, Liquid, Solid, Gas, or Synthesis) to fully unlock their passive potential.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/best-5-star-disk-nte-dps/inline-2.webp)


The overall best 5-star disk for a Plasma-type DPS like Nanally is the **Ready-Ready** Arc. It is currently the gold standard for burst damage, offering a high CRIT Rate substat and a unique boss-slaying passive. For Liquid-type DPS units such as Mint, the **Fluff of Fleetness** Arc is the undisputed best-in-slot (BiS) option due to its stacking ATK buff that rewards long on-field durations.

If you are looking for the best 5-star disk for the main character (Zero), the **Day Off** Arc is the premier choice. Unlike most S-Rank Arcs that require gacha pulls, Day Off can be obtained for free through a secret questline involving the NPC Eclipse, making it the most accessible high-tier weapon for F2P players.

Regarding equipment-based disks, the **Fireflies and the Forest** Cartridge set is the top choice for any Anima-element DPS. Its 4-piece bonus provides a massive boost to CRIT DMG that stacks whenever enemies take Anima damage, creating the highest damage ceiling currently available in the game.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/best-5-star-disk-nte-dps/inline-3.webp)


### Top S-Rank Arcs for Main DPS

The meta in Neverness to Everness is currently dominated by three primary S-Rank Arcs. Understanding the nuances of these weapons is essential for building a top-tier DPS.

**1. Ready-Ready (Plasma Type)**
This is the signature weapon for Nanally, featured on "The Ichi-daime" limited banner. At level 80, it provides a base ATK of 570 and a 24% CRIT Rate substat. Its passive effect, **Commander Tiger Talisman**, increases the wearer's ATK by 15% unconditionally. Furthermore, casting a Redirect Skill or Ultimate grants a 15% bonus to Basic Attack and Critical Riposte damage for 15 seconds. If you manage to trigger both bonuses within 15 seconds, you unlock the "Commander Tiger Talisman" effect, which grants a flat 10% damage increase against Bosses.

**2. Fluff of Fleetness (Liquid Type)**
Designed for sustained damage dealers like Mint, this Arc focuses on the **Motor Candy** passive. While the wearer is the active character, they gain a 5% ATK boost every second, stacking up to five times for a total of 25% extra ATK. The drawback is that the effect resets immediately upon switching characters, making it ideal for "hyper-carry" rotations where your main DPS stays on the field for extended periods.

**3. Day Off (Solid Type)**
While technically a "Solid" type Arc, Day Off is highly versatile. It provides a massive 30% ATK boost at base level. Its unique "Eclipse" effect creates a 40-second zone during which every enemy defeated restores 6 Ultimate Energy. This makes it incredible for wave-clearing in the open world and ensures that Zero can maintain high Ultimate uptime.

### Best 5-Star Cartridge Sets for Damage

While Arcs provide the base stats, Cartridges provide the multipliers. The most important set for the current DPS meta is **Fireflies and the Forest**.

*   **2-Piece Bonus:** Increases Anima DMG by 10%.
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** Whenever a nearby enemy takes Anima DMG from any team member, the wearer gains a stack of 8% CRIT DMG. This stacks up to 7 times for a total of 56% CRIT DMG. Since characters like Nanally and Mint hit multiple times per second, you can reach the maximum stack count almost instantly.

Another notable set is **Speedy Hedgehog**. While often used on supports, it can be a viable 5-star disk set for sub-DPS units who need to cycle their Ultimates frequently, as it provides a significant boost to Charge Efficiency and team-wide ATK buffs.

### Optimizing the Console and Module Synergy

The "Console" is where you slot your Cartridges and Modules (the tetris-like pieces that provide stats). Each character has a unique "Character-specific Module Bonus" that activates based on the shape and Type of modules you use:

*   **Nanally's Module Bonus:** She gains a **6% CRIT Rate bonus** for every **Type II Module** (2-cell pieces) equipped on her Console. To optimize her, you should prioritize filling the remaining space on her board with Type II modules after you have activated your 4-piece Cartridge set.
*   **Mint's Module Bonus:** Mint receives a **7.5% CRIT Rate bonus** for every **Type III Module** (3-cell pieces) used. This makes her one of the easiest characters to build for 100% CRIT Rate, as Type III modules are common and provide high base stats.

Modules come in three types: Type II (2 cells), Type III (3 cells), and Type IV (4 cells). While Type IV modules have higher individual stats, they are harder to fit and may prevent you from maximizing the character-specific bonuses mentioned above.

### How to Farm 5-Star Disks Efficiently

Farming S-Rank disks (Cartridges and Modules) requires "Character Pixels" (stamina). While you can start seeing S-Rank drops in domains at level 30, the community consensus is to **wait until Hunter Level 40** before spending your saved stamina refills. At Level 40, the drop rate for gold S-Rank Cartridges becomes guaranteed, ensuring you don't waste resources on purple A-Rank gear that will eventually be replaced.

To obtain Modules, you must use the **Rewind** system found within the Console menu. This uses "Carrota Coins" (Bronze, Silver, or Gold) which are farmed in the **Rabbit Hole [Anomaly Zone](https://ntecodex.com/faq)**. For the best DPS results, aim for Gold Carrota pulls to secure S-Rank modules with CRIT DMG substats.

### Secret Quest Guide: How to Get the 'Day Off' Arc for Free

You do not need to spend Annulith to get a top-tier S-Rank Arc for Zero. Follow these steps to obtain **Day Off** from the Bureau of Anomaly Control:

1.  Teleport to the Bureau of Anomaly Control (BAC) in Area M10.
2.  Locate the NPC **Eclipse** standing in the corner near the energy ball. Speak with her to start the "Unfinished Business" hidden mission.
3.  Set your in-game time to **8:00 AM (Sunny)**.
4.  You must find Eclipse at five more locations across Hethereau: the roof of the Film Orbit Studio, the Gift Shop, the beach near the studio, the Tikal Bridge, and finally back at the BAC.
5.  After exhausting all dialogue at the final location, she will reward you with the **S-Rank Day Off Arc**.

### Best A-Rank (4-Star) Alternatives

If you haven't pulled an S-Rank Arc yet, there are several powerful 4-star options that can carry you through the mid-game:

*   **Oraora!:** The best F2P option for Nanally. it provides a stacking Basic Attack DMG bonus that synergizes with her high-speed combo strings.
*   **Song of the Whale:** A free A-Rank Arc obtained by defeating world bosses. It provides a 12% ATK boost and increases damage against "Broken" (staggered) enemies, making it excellent for elite encounters.
*   **Clear Skies:** A solid choice for Mint or other Anima units, offering flat Anima DMG bonuses and Ultimate energy recharge.

In terms of stat scaling, an S-Rank Arc at level 80 typically has ~512-570 Base ATK, while an A-Rank Arc sits around ~395-424 Base ATK. This ~30% difference in base stats is why upgrading to a 5-star disk is a priority for late-game content.

### Sub-stat Priority for DPS Disks

In the late-game "Hethereau" challenges, flat ATK falls off significantly compared to crit-based scaling. When rolling your Cartridges and Modules, prioritize stats in this order:

1.  **CRIT DMG%:** Especially important if you are running the Fireflies set.
2.  **CRIT Rate%:** Aim for at least 60-70% total CRIT Rate (including character and module bonuses).
3.  **Elemental DMG% (Anima/Cosmos/etc.):** This acts as a separate multiplier to your total damage.
4.  **ATK%:** Good for general scaling, but less impactful than Crit in the endgame.

### Team Compositions for Maximum DPS

Your 5-star disks are only as good as the team supporting them. Support characters can provide buffs that multiply the effectiveness of your Arcs:

*   **[Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide):** Often used with the **Speedy Hedgehog** set to provide team-wide ATK buffs and grouping, allowing your main DPS to hit all enemies at once.
*   **Haniel:** Provides massive shields and damage buffs. When paired with a DPS wearing the **Ready-Ready** Arc, she ensures the character can stay on the field to maintain their "Commander Tiger" stacks without being interrupted.
*   **Jiuyuan:** A strong Anima sub-DPS who helps stack the **Fireflies and the Forest** 4-piece bonus quickly, as her off-field attacks count toward the Anima damage requirement.

### Future Outlook

As of May 2026, the current Arcs are highly specialized. However, leaks regarding the June 2026 update suggest the introduction of "Synthesis" type Arcs that may provide universal buffs, potentially powercreeping some current niche options. For now, investing in **Ready-Ready** and **Fireflies and the Forest** remains the safest bet for any serious DPS build.

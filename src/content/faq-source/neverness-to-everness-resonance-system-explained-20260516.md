### How the Awakening System Works

Every Esper starts at A0. To progress, you need Mind Shards specific to that character. For S-Rank Espers like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide), these shards are primarily found through the [Scarborough Fair gacha](https://ntecodex.com/faq) system. When you land on a character tile or pull a duplicate through the standard "Warp" mechanic, the duplicate is automatically processed into shards.

The Awakening grid consists of six nodes. In a significant departure from genre norms, these nodes are interconnected in a web rather than a straight line. This non-linear Awakening path means the "meta" for a character might involve skipping the A1 node to rush a specific energy regeneration node at A2 or A3. 

The "Toggle" feature is another critical component. This allows players to turn off specific Awakening nodes at any time outside of combat. This is particularly useful for theorycrafters who want to measure the exact impact of a Resonance Tier on their clear times in "Beyond the Rails" or for players who want to adjust their speed tuning for specific elemental reaction cycles.

### Awakening vs. Ascension: Key Differences

It is easy to confuse Awakening with Ascension, but they serve different purposes in the Esper progression loop.

*   **Ascension:** Increases the level cap of your Esper (e.g., from level 40 to 50). This requires regional materials found in Hella City and boss drops. Ascension primarily boosts base stats like HP, Attack, and Defense.
*   **Awakening (Resonance):** Unlocks new mechanical properties and massive percentage-based multipliers. This requires Mind Shards. Awakening is what defines an Esper's "kit" and their ceiling in endgame content.

You should view Ascension as the "floor" of a character's power and Awakening as the "ceiling." A level 90 Esper at A0 will often be outperformed by a level 70 Esper at A6 due to the sheer mechanical advantages provided by the Resonance Tiers.

Finding the strongest characters in Zenless Zone Zero is the top priority for any Proxy looking to conquer the difficult challenges of the Hollows. S-Rank Agents represent the peak of combat efficiency, offering superior stats, unique passive abilities, and flashy Ultimate attacks that can clear entire waves of Ethereals in seconds.

# How to Get S-Rank Agents in Zenless Zone Zero (ZZZ)

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/how-to-get-s-rank-agents-zzz/inline-1.webp)


Acquiring S-Rank Agents is a central goal for players, but the process can seem complex due to the various currencies and systems involved. New and veteran players often ask

*   How do players acquire S-Rank Agents in Zenless Zone Zero?
*   What are the different methods for obtaining the highest rarity characters?
*   How does the gacha system work for those just starting their journey in New Eridu?

Obtaining these elite units requires a mix of resource management, understanding the "Signal Search" mechanics, and leveraging the built-in "Pity" systems that ensure you eventually get the character you want. Whether you are aiming for limited-time event characters or the core standard roster, knowing how the math works behind the scenes is essential for maximizing your Polychrome budget.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-get-s-rank-agents-zzz/inline-2.webp)


S-Rank Agents are primarily obtained through the "Signal Search" gacha system by spending Master Tapes or Encrypted Master Tapes. Each pull has a small chance to yield an S-Rank character, but the game includes several "Pity" mechanics to guarantee rewards.

A "Hard Pity" system guarantees an S-Rank Agent every 90 pulls on both the Exclusive (Limited) and Stable (Standard) channels. For new players, Zenless Zone Zero offers a significant head start: you are guaranteed an S-Rank Agent within your first 50 pulls on the Stable Channel at a discounted rate of 8 Master Tapes for every 10-pull.

Furthermore, a one-time S-Rank Agent selector becomes available after you reach 300 total pulls on the Stable Channel, allowing you to choose any one of the six standard S-Rank Agents. Limited-time S-Rank Agents, which are often the most powerful in the current meta, are exclusive to the "Exclusive Channel" and require Encrypted Master Tapes, which are primarily purchased with Polychromes.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-get-s-rank-agents-zzz/inline-3.webp)


The Signal Search system is the heart of character acquisition in Zenless Zone Zero. To efficiently hunt for S-Rank Agents, you must understand how the different "Channels" interact and how the probability of success changes as you pull.

### Understanding Signal Search Channels

There are four primary types of Signal Search channels available in the menu. Each uses a specific currency and features a different pool of rewards

1.  **Exclusive Channel:** This is where limited-time S-Rank Agents appear. These characters are usually available for about three weeks before rotating out. You must use **Encrypted Master Tapes** (the colorful ones) here.
2.  **Stable Channel:** This is the permanent banner. It features a set pool of six standard S-Rank Agents and various S-Rank W-Engines. You use regular **Master Tapes** (the grey ones) here.
3.  **W-Engine Channel:** This banner is dedicated to high-rarity weapons. While it does not grant Agents, it is where you find the "Signature" W-Engines that make S-Rank Agents significantly stronger.
4.  **Bangboo Channel:** This unique banner uses **Boopons**. Unlike the other channels, you cannot buy Boopons with premium currency; you earn them through gameplay. This channel allows you to target specific S-Rank Bangboos to support your team.

### The Pity System: Soft Pity vs. Hard Pity

Zenless Zone Zero uses a "Pity" system to protect players from long streaks of bad luck. The base drop rate for an S-Rank Agent is 0.6%, which is quite low. However, the consolidated rate (including pity) is approximately 1.6%.

The "Hard Pity" is set at 90 pulls. This means if you have gone 89 pulls without seeing a gold signal, the 90th pull is a guaranteed S-Rank. Most players, however, will trigger "Soft Pity." Starting around pull 75, the percentage chance to find an S-Rank Agent increases significantly with every subsequent pull. Statistically, the vast majority of players will receive their S-Rank between pulls 76 and 81.

| Search Type | S-Rank Base Rate | S-Rank Hard Pity | A-Rank Hard Pity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Agent Banners | 0.6% | 90 Pulls | 10 Pulls |
| W-Engine Banners | 0.7% | 80 Pulls | 10 Pulls |

### The 50/50 Rule and Guarantee Status

On the Exclusive Channel, getting an S-Rank signal does not always mean you get the featured limited character. This is known as the "50/50" mechanic.

When you pull an S-Rank on a limited banner, there is a 50% chance it will be the featured Agent and a 50% chance it will be one of the six standard S-Rank Agents (Lycaon, Grace, Rina, Koleda, Nekomata, or Soldier 11). If you "lose" the 50/50 and receive a standard Agent, your very next S-Rank Agent on an Exclusive Channel is **guaranteed** to be the featured limited character. This guarantee carries over between different limited banners. For example, if you lose the 50/50 on a current banner and stop pulling, your first S-Rank on the next month's banner will be the featured character.

### Standard Banner Mechanics and the 300-Pull Selector

The Stable Channel is where you spend the Master Tapes earned from leveling up your Inter-Knot rank or completing the Battle Pass. While you should generally avoid spending Polychromes here, the Stable Channel has a major milestone reward.

After completing 300 searches on the Stable Channel, a "Recruitment" button appears. This allows you to pick one of the following standard S-Rank Agents to join your roster for free

*   **Von Lycaon:** A powerful Ice Stun Agent essential for Ice-element teams.
*   **Grace Howard:** An Electric Anomaly Agent who excels at applying Shock status.
*   **Alexandrina (Rina) Sebastiane:** An Electric Support who provides massive penetration buffs.
*   **Koleda Belobog:** A Fire Stun Agent with high impact and explosive burst damage.
*   **Nekomata (Nekomiya Mana):** A Physical Attack Agent known for high mobility and backstab damage.
*   **Soldier 11:** A Fire Attack Agent who rewards precise timing with massive fire damage.

### Signal Shop: Residual and Fading Signals

Every time you pull a duplicate character or a W-Engine, you earn secondary currencies: **Residual Signal** and **Fading Signal**. These can be spent in the Signal Shop (found within the Store menu) to get more S-Ranks.

*   **Residual Signal:** Earned from pulling 4-star (A-Rank) and 5-star (S-Rank) items. You can save these to buy specific S-Rank W-Engines or to purchase more Encrypted Master Tapes to reach your next pity faster.
*   **Fading Signal:** Earned from 3-star (B-Rank) pulls. This shop resets monthly and allows you to buy 5 Master Tapes and 5 Encrypted Master Tapes at a heavy discount. Always clear these out every month.

### Newbie Banner: Your First S-Rank Agent

When you first start Zenless Zone Zero, the Stable Channel features a "Newbie" promotion. For the first 50 pulls, you get a 20% discount on 10-pulls (meaning it only costs 8 Master Tapes to do a 10-pull). You are guaranteed to receive one of the six standard S-Rank Agents within these first 50 pulls. Once you receive your first S-Rank from this banner, the discount disappears, and the counter switches to the standard 90-pull pity and the 300-pull selector progress.

Securing S-Rank Agents is only half the battle. To truly master the game, you need to equip them properly and understand how to sustain your Polychrome income for future banners.

### S-Rank W-Engines and Bangboos

Agents require weapons, known as W-Engines, to reach their full potential. While S-Rank Agents have a pity of 90, the W-Engine Channel has a lower pity of 80. Furthermore, on the W-Engine banner, the "75/25" rule applies: you have a 75% chance to get the featured S-Rank W-Engine, making it much easier to gear your favorite character than it is to pull the character themselves.

Bangboos are also vital. S-Rank Bangboos like **Sharkboo** or **Amillion** provide massive elemental damage or utility. The best part of the Bangboo system is that it is entirely free; you cannot buy Boopons with money. You must choose which S-Rank Bangboo you want to "path" toward, and you are guaranteed to get that specific Bangboo when you hit an S-Rank signal.

| Feature | Agent Signal Search | W-Engine Signal Search |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Hard Pity | 90 Pulls | 80 Pulls |
| Soft Pity Start | ~75 Pulls | ~65 Pulls |
| Featured Rate-Up | 50% Chance | 75% Chance |
| Currency Type | Master Tapes | Master Tapes |

### Best Ways to Farm Polychromes

To get S-Rank Agents, you need Polychromes. Each pull costs 160 Polychromes. To hit a 90-pull pity, you need 14,400 Polychromes. Here are the most reliable ways to farm them

*   **Daily Errands:** Completing your daily tasks provides a steady 60 Polychromes per day.
*   **Hollow Zero:** This roguelike mode offers weekly rewards, including Polychromes and Boopons. Clearing the "License Level" rewards provides thousands of Polychromes over time.
*   **Shiyu Defense:** This is the endgame "spiral" content. The "Stable Node" provides one-time rewards, while the "Disputed Node" or "Critical Node" resets periodically, providing a recurring source of currency for high-level players.
*   **Trial Stages:** Every time a new Exclusive Channel launches, you can play a trial stage for the new S-Rank Agent to earn 20 Polychromes instantly.
*   **Inter-Knot Leveling:** Every few levels, you will receive Master Tapes or Polychromes as a reward from the Proxy association.

### Current S-Rank Agent Roster and Roles

As of mid-2026, the roster has expanded significantly since launch. However, the roles remain consistent. When pulling for an S-Rank, you should consider what your team lacks. A balanced team typically consists of

1.  **An Attack Agent:** Your primary damage dealer (e.g., Ellen, Zhu Yuan, or Soldier 11).
2.  **A Stun Agent:** Responsible for filling the Daze bar to immobilize enemies (e.g., Lycaon, Koleda, or Qingyi).
3.  **A Support or Anomaly Agent:** Provides buffs to the team or applies elemental debuffs (e.g., Rina, Caesar, or Grace).

By focusing your Signal Search efforts on filling these specific roles, you can create a team capable of tackling the highest difficulty "Withering Garden" runs in Hollow Zero. Always check the current "Exclusive Channel" to see if the featured Agent fits your current elemental needs, such as building a mono-Ice or mono-Ether team.

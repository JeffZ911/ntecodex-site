## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-character-affinity-system-guide/inline-2.webp)


The character affinity system in NTE is known as the Bond system, allowing players to build relationships with their Espers. It serves as a bridge between the urban life simulation and the tactical combat elements of the game. Affinity is raised primarily through daily gifting, city interactions (dates), and "Home Time" activities in your apartment.

There are 10 Bond levels total, though some characters like Aurelia are capped at lower levels in version 1.0. Progressing through these levels requires a combination of consistent daily interaction and reaching specific milestones in the protagonist's (Esper Zero) development.

Key rewards include Annulith (premium currency), permanent stat boosts for the specific character, and exclusive character outfits at Level 10. Additionally, the system unlocks unique "Home Time" interactions like hugging or holding hands, which provide unique animations and voiced lines.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-character-affinity-system-guide/inline-3.webp)


The Bond system is not a sprint; it is a marathon that requires daily attention. Because of the hard caps on how much affinity you can gain in a single 24-hour cycle, planning your interactions is necessary to avoid wasting resources or time.

### How to Increase Bond Levels Fast

To maximize your efficiency, you must understand the daily limits. You are limited to 10 total gifts per day across your entire account. Furthermore, there is a maximum of 3 gifts per individual character. This means you cannot simply dump an entire inventory of items into one character to max them out in a single afternoon.

The most effective way to gain points is to prioritize "Universal Gifts." Items like "Handwritten Letters" provide a massive 2000 affinity points and are accepted by all characters without penalty. These should be reserved for your primary combat Espers or those whose Level 10 outfits you want to unlock first.

Additionally, "Fluffy Clouds" are a free daily resource generated by apartment furniture. While they provide fewer points than premium gifts, they are essential for long-term progression as they do not cost Fons (the standard currency) to acquire. Consistent collection of Fluffy Clouds can save you millions of Fons over the course of a few months.

### Esper Zero's Life Skill: Five-Star Paragon

A common mistake new players make is ignoring the protagonist's life skills. Upgrading Esper Zero's "Five-Star Paragon" life skill is mandatory to unlock Bond level caps. 

*   **Five-Star Paragon Level 3:** Required to bypass the Level 7 cap and reach Bond Level 8.
*   **Five-Star Paragon Level 5:** Required to reach the final Bond Level 10.

Beyond just acting as a gatekeeper for level caps, upgrading this skill provides a passive 5% affinity bonus to all gifts given. Over the course of leveling a full team of Espers, this 5% bonus equates to several days' worth of saved daily gift limits. Investing in Five-Star Paragon early is the single most important step for players focused on the social aspects of NTE.

### Unlocking Home Time and Interactive Actions

At Bond Level 4, characters can move into your properties, such as the Wiener Apartments or the more upscale Eden Apartments. This unlocks the "Home Time" mechanic. Once a character is situated in your apartment, they provide passive affinity gain simply by being present while you are in the housing menu.

Apartment living also unlocks interactive actions. These are not just cosmetic; they often trigger "Mood" buffs that can slightly increase the rewards you get from daily missions.

*   **Level 2 (Car Rides):** Allows the character to sit in the passenger seat of your customized vehicles. This unlocks unique banter while driving through the city.
*   **Level 4 (Home Invitation):** The character becomes a permanent resident of your chosen apartment.
*   **Level 6 (Hug):** A social interaction that can be triggered once per day to provide a small "Inner Peace" buff.
*   **Level 10 (Casual Outfit):** The ultimate reward. This allows you to swap the character's tactical gear for a casual, urban-themed outfit that can be worn during both exploration and combat.

The generation of "Fluffy Clouds" is tied to your apartment's comfort level. By placing high-tier furniture, you increase the rate at which these affinity items are produced, creating a self-sustaining loop of relationship growth.

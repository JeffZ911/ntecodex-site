# Neverness to Everness Beginner Tips: The Ultimate Starter Guide

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/neverness-to-everness-beginner-tips-guide/inline-1.webp)


What are the most essential tips for players starting Neverness to Everness (NTE)? Success in Hethereau requires a balance between mastering the Esper Cycle combat system and managing the economic layers of the City Tycoon mode. New players often struggle with how to prioritize their time between the main story Episodes and the various lifestyle activities like car customization and business management.

How should new players prioritize their time between the main story and the City Tycoon system? The early game is heavily gated by account levels, meaning your first objective is to reach specific milestones that unlock essential features. Focusing on the narrative not only provides the bulk of your experience points but also introduces the core mechanics necessary to earn Fons, the city’s primary currency.

What are the common mistakes to avoid regarding character investment and gacha currency? Many beginners waste Solid Dice on standard banners or spread their upgrade materials across too many characters. Understanding the "no 50/50" guarantee and the dual stamina system is vital to ensuring your resources are spent efficiently as you progress toward the endgame.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/neverness-to-everness-beginner-tips-guide/inline-2.webp)


Focus on completing the main story "Episodes" to unlock the City Tycoon system and the free S-rank character Chiz. Chiz is awarded at Tycoon Level 18 and serves as a powerful Cosmos DPS, making her an essential addition to any free-to-play roster.

Master the "Esper Cycle" combat mechanic by swapping characters when their meters are full to trigger elemental reactions. Combat in NTE revolves around the Esper Wheel; switching between adjacent elements creates Duo and Triple reactions that significantly amplify your damage output.

Manage two separate stamina pools: Character Pixels for combat materials and City Stamina for lifestyle activities. Character Pixels regenerate daily and are used for farming gear and ascension items, while City Stamina is a weekly pool used for earning Fons through activities like racing and deliveries.

Save your Solid Dice for limited banners, as NTE features a 100% guarantee (no 50/50) for the featured S-rank character. Once you reach 90 pulls on a limited banner, you are guaranteed to receive the featured character, such as [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte), without the risk of pulling a standard unit instead.

Invest heavily in one main DPS early on rather than spreading resources across multiple teams. Characters like Zero or Nanally can carry you through most early-game content if you prioritize their levels, Arcs (weapons), and Console modules.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/neverness-to-everness-beginner-tips-guide/inline-3.webp)


### Mastering the Esper Cycle & Combat Mechanics

Combat in Neverness to Everness is defined by the Esper Cycle, a system where characters interact based on their elemental types. There are six primary elements: Anima, Chaos, Cosmos, Incantation, Lakshana, and Psyche. These are arranged on a hexagonal "Esper Wheel." Reactions only occur when you swap from a character of one element to a character of an adjacent element on the wheel.

To trigger these effects, you must fill a character's Esper Meter. This circular gauge next to the character's portrait fills through basic attacks, skills, and successful parries. Once the meter is full, the portrait glows, indicating that swapping to them will trigger an Esper Ability and a corresponding reaction.

| Reaction Type | Elements Involved | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blossom (Duo)** | Cosmos + Anima | Summons an AOE turret that fires at nearby enemies. |
| **Remora (Duo)** | Cosmos + Lakshana | Marks enemies and significantly slows their movement and attacks. |
| **Scorch (Duo)** | Incantation + Chaos | Applies a powerful Damage-over-Time (DoT) burn effect. |
| **Nova (Duo)** | Chaos + Psyche | Attaches a delayed energy burst that deals massive AOE damage. |
| **Hexed (Duo)** | Anima + Incantation | Deals follow-up damage based on a percentage of damage taken by the target. |
| **Stain (Duo)** | Psyche + Lakshana | Debuffs the enemy, increasing damage taken from Psyche and Lakshana. |
| **Charge (Triple)** | Lakshana + Cosmos + Anima | Rapidly generates Ultimate energy for the active character. |
| **Discord (Triple)** | Incantation + Chaos + Psyche | Deals massive Stagger damage to break enemy shields quickly. |

### The Three Levels of Progression: Hunter, Appraisal, and Tycoon

Progression in Hethereau is split into three distinct leveling systems that govern different aspects of the game.

1.  **Hunter Level (Account):** This is your overall account rank. It is increased by completing quests, spending stamina, and discovering city anomalies. Increasing your Hunter Level is the primary way to unlock new story chapters and game modes.
2.  **Appraisal Level (World):** This functions as the world difficulty. As your Hunter Level hits certain thresholds (20, 40, and 50), you must complete "Breakthrough" missions to raise your Appraisal Level. Raising this level increases enemy difficulty but also improves the quality of loot dropped by world bosses and Anomaly Zones.
3.  **Tycoon Level (Lifestyle):** This level tracks your economic success in the city. You increase it by earning and spending Fons. High Tycoon Levels unlock better properties, more advanced car parts, and the free S-rank character Chiz.

### Gacha Guide: Navigating the Board and Pity System

NTE uses a unique "Scarborough Fair" gacha system that looks like a monopoly board. Instead of a simple animation, your character moves across tiles to collect rewards.

*   **No 50/50 Mechanic:** On limited character banners (which use Solid Dice), the S-rank character you pull is guaranteed to be the featured unit.
*   **Soft Pity (Board Modification):** At 70 pulls without an S-rank, the board undergoes "Modification." Several tiles transform into S-rank tiles, increasing your success rate to approximately 19.59%.
*   **Hard Pity:** You are guaranteed an S-rank character at 90 pulls.
*   **Standard Selector:** After your first 50 pulls on the Standard Board (using Fabricated Dice), you can choose one S-rank character from the permanent pool for free.

### Urban Life: Earning Fons and Managing Your Cafe

Fons are the lifeblood of your progression in the City Tycoon system. While you earn some through combat, the most efficient way to accumulate wealth is through city activities.

The "Cafe by Origen" is your primary source of passive income. You can hire characters you have unlocked to work at the cafe, which boosts sales and generates Fons even when you are offline. Make sure to check the management menu daily to research new recipes and collect your earnings.

For active income, participate in "Bank Heists." These are supernatural challenges where you enter a distorted version of a bank to loot valuables. There is a cap of 1 million Fons per heist cycle, with a cooldown that typically lasts two weeks. Other activities like taxi driving, fishing, and delivery missions also provide steady Fons at the cost of City Stamina.

### Exploration Tips: Vehicles, Anomalies, and Wall-Running

Hethereau is a vertical city, and exploration is as much about height as it is about distance.

*   **Vehicles:** Use your garage to customize cars. High-tier engine parts are essential for winning city races, which provide unique rewards and Tycoon EXP.
*   **Anomalies:** Keep an eye out for distorted air or static on buildings. These indicate nearby anomalies. Solving these environmental puzzles often rewards Oracle Stones, which can be traded at the Witch's House for Annulith.
*   **Gravity Defiance:** Characters like Nanally have unique exploration passives. Nanally can wall-run and defy gravity, allowing you to reach rooftops without searching for stairs or elevators.

### The Warrant & Prison System Explained

Illegal activities in Hethereau, such as stealing vehicles or attacking civilians, will trigger the Warrant System. As your warrant level increases, the Bureau of Anomaly Control will send units to apprehend you.

If captured, you are sent to the Prison scenario. While this may seem like a setback, the prison contains unique "convict" NPCs who offer special missions and rewards not found anywhere else in the city. You can choose to serve your sentence or attempt a jailbreak, though the latter will keep your warrant level high. To avoid jail entirely, always "ask" for a vehicle from NPCs rather than hijacking them.

### Daily and Weekly Checklist for New Players

To maximize your efficiency, follow this routine to ensure no resources are wasted:

**Daily Tasks:**
*   **Spend Character Pixels:** Use your daily 240 stamina on Anomaly Zones to farm ascension materials and Consoles.
*   **Daily Commissions:** Complete the four tasks in the Exploration Guide for easy Hunter EXP and Annulith.
*   **Cafe Management:** Collect Fons from the Cafe by Origen and update your staff.
*   **Check the Shop:** Buy the discounted materials and Fabricated Dice from the daily exchange.

**Weekly Tasks:**
*   **Spend City Stamina:** Use your weekly pool on fishing, racing, or deliveries to max out your Fons for the week.
*   **Bank Heists:** Complete your bi-weekly heist to hit the 1 million Fons cap.
*   **Weekly Bosses:** Defeat world anomalies for high-tier Arc upgrade materials.

**Stamina Regeneration Rates:**
*   **Character Pixels:** 1 point every 6 minutes (240 per 24 hours).
*   **City Stamina:** Does not regenerate daily; resets to the cap every Monday at 5:00 AM server time.

# How to Trigger Nova Damage in Neverness to Everness (NTE)
> 📝 **Editorial Note:** This guide is AI-assisted and game data evolves rapidly. Please cross-reference with in-game information. _Updated: 2026-06-11._


To [trigger Nova damage](https://ntecodex.com/faq) in NTE, swap between a Chaos-type and a Psyche-type character when the Esper Meter is full. This applies a mark that explodes after 5 seconds for massive Mental damage.

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/how-to-trigger-nova-damage-nte/inline-1.webp)


Understanding [Nova damage](https://ntecodex.com/faq) is essential for players looking to optimize their damage rotations in Neverness to Everness. Many players find themselves asking what exactly Nova damage represents within the broader combat system. In the current meta, Nova is recognized as one of the strongest burst reactions because it bypasses several traditional defensive layers that enemies in Hethereau possess.

Unlike standard elemental types such as Cosmos or Anima, which often focus on sustained damage over time or immediate flat damage, Nova is a delayed reaction. This delay often leads to confusion among new players who may not see an immediate impact on the enemy's health bar. The reaction is specifically categorized as Mental damage (also known as Cognito damage), a unique damage type that scales differently than physical or elemental strikes.

The primary points of confusion usually involve:
*   The specific elemental requirements (Chaos and Psyche).
*   The 5-second delay before the damage "pops."
*   How to maximize the final explosion using the Cycle Intensity stat.
*   The difference between Nova and other reactions like Scorch or Blossom.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-trigger-nova-damage-nte/inline-2.webp)


Nova is an Esper Cycle reaction triggered exclusively by the combination of the Chaos and Psyche elements. To execute this, you must first fill the Esper Meter—also known as Cycle Energy—of your active character by dealing damage or performing successful defensive maneuvers. Once the meter is full, swapping to a teammate of the complementary element (swapping Chaos to Psyche, or Psyche to Chaos) triggers the reaction.

When the swap occurs, the incoming character performs a Support Skill that places a "Nova Mark" on the target. This mark remains active for 5 seconds. During this window, you can continue to attack, but the bulk of the reaction's power is contained in the final explosion. Upon expiration, the mark deals a massive burst of Mental damage.

The damage of this explosion does not scale with traditional Attack or Critical Hit stats in the same way standard skills do. Instead, it scales primarily with the Cycle Intensity stat. Specifically, the game calculates the damage based on the character in the pair who possesses the highest Cycle Intensity value, making it beneficial to have at least one dedicated "reaction trigger" character in your party.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-trigger-nova-damage-nte/inline-3.webp)


The combat system in Neverness to Everness is built around the Esper Wheel, a diagram that illustrates how different types of energy interact. Within this wheel, Chaos and Psyche are positioned as adjacent elements. This proximity allows them to resonate, resulting in the Nova effect.

### Understanding the Chaos and Psyche Synergy

The synergy between Chaos and Psyche is designed to reward tactical swapping. Chaos characters typically excel at destabilizing enemy defenses, while Psyche characters focus on mental manipulation and area-of-effect control. When these two forces meet, they create a "Cognito overload" represented by the Nova Mark. Because Mental damage often ignores a portion of the enemy's physical armor, Nova is particularly effective against heavily armored bosses or elite Anomalies that resist standard Anima or Cosmos attacks.

### Step-by-Step: Executing the Nova Trigger

1.  **Fill the Esper Meter:** Use your active character (e.g., Daffodil) to attack enemies. You will notice a circular gauge around the character portrait filling with white light.
2.  **Wait for the Visual Cue:** When the meter is full, the portraits of your off-field characters will begin to pulse.
3.  **Perform the Swap:** Switch to a character of the opposite element (e.g., Fadia).
4.  **The Support Skill:** The incoming character will automatically perform an Entry Skill upon arrival. If the previous character was Chaos and the new one is Psyche, the Nova Mark is applied to all nearby enemies.
5.  **The 5-Second Window:** A purple and gold icon will appear over the enemy's head. You now have five seconds to reposition or apply buffs before the Mental damage burst occurs.

### Optimizing Nova: Cycle Intensity and Stats

A common mistake is building Nova-focused characters with pure Critical Damage. However, Nova damage is a "fixed" reaction burst that does not crit by default. To increase the damage of the explosion, you must prioritize the Cycle Intensity stat. This stat acts as a multiplier for all Esper Cycle reactions.

In addition to Cycle Intensity, you should consider the character's level. Like many modern urban RPGs, the base damage of reactions in NTE is heavily influenced by the character's current level relative to the enemy's level.

### Advanced Combat: The Discord Trio Reaction

For advanced players, Nova is often just the first step in a more complex rotation. By introducing a third element—Incantation—you can trigger the Discord reaction. Discord is a "Trio" reaction that occurs when Psyche is introduced to a target already suffering from the Scorch effect (Incantation + Chaos).

The Discord reaction is widely considered the ultimate tool for breaking enemy shields. It combines the raw Mental damage of Nova with the shield-shredding properties of Incantation, effectively melting the "Break" gauge of even the toughest bosses in Hethereau.

The most popular team for triggering Nova frequently involves Lacrimosa and Fadia. ### Filling the Esper Meter Faster

Waiting for the Esper Meter to fill through basic attacks can be slow. To speed up your Nova rotations, utilize the following techniques:
*   **Perfect Dodges:** Successfully dodging an attack at the last moment grants a significant chunk of Cycle Energy.
*   **Parrying:** Characters capable of parrying can fill nearly half their meter with a single successful counter.
*   **Energy Recharge Arcs:** Equipping specific Arcs (the game's weapon/equipment system) can provide passive Cycle Rate bonuses, allowing for near-constant swapping.

By focusing on these mechanics, you can transform Nova from a simple elemental reaction into a reliable, high-damage engine that carries you through the most difficult challenges Hethereau has to offer.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does Nova damage scale with my character's Attack stat?
Nova damage primarily scales with Cycle Intensity and the character's level rather than raw Attack. While a higher Attack stat improves your basic skills, the specific explosion at the end of the Nova Mark relies on the elemental mastery provided by the Cycle Intensity stat.

### Can Nova damage land a Critical Hit?
By default, Nova damage cannot land a Critical Hit. It is a fixed-damage reaction that bypasses traditional crit checks. To increase its output, you should focus on increasing your Cycle Intensity and shredding the enemy's Mental resistance rather than building for Critical Rate or Critical Damage.

### What is the difference between Mental damage and Cognito damage?
In Neverness to Everness, Mental damage and Cognito damage refer to the same damage category. This damage type is unique because it targets the enemy's psychological stability rather than their physical armor, making it highly effective against enemies with high physical defense or shields.

### How do I know if the Nova Mark is active?
Once you trigger the reaction by swapping characters, a purple and gold icon will appear above the enemy. Additionally, the enemy will often have a shimmering aura. After 5 seconds, this icon will shatter, dealing the accumulated Mental damage burst to the target and nearby foes.

### Understanding the Leveling Hierarchy

Progression in NTE is divided into three distinct layers: Hunter Level, Appraisal Level, and Esper Level. 

1.  **Hunter Level (HL):** This is your account-wide level. It determines which story Episodes you can access and serves as the primary gate for all other systems.
2.  **Appraisal Level (AL):** Think of this as your World Level. Increasing your AL makes enemies in the overworld and Anomaly Zones tougher, but it also significantly improves the quality of loot drops and is the only way to raise the level cap of your Espers.
3.  **Esper Level:** This is the individual level of your characters (Zero, Mint, etc.). You cannot raise an Esper's level beyond the cap set by your current Appraisal Level.

### Common Leveling Roadblocks for Beginners

The most frequent hurdle is the "Breakthrough Quest." At Hunter Levels 20, 40, and 50, your Hunter EXP will stop accumulating until you complete a specific Appraisal Breakthrough mission. Many players ignore these missions to keep the world difficulty low, but doing so freezes your account progression and prevents you from accessing higher-tier Arcs and Cartridges.

Another common mistake is neglecting the City Tycoon system. While it seems like a side-management sim, it is actually the source of massive Hunter EXP chunks and the only way to obtain the S-rank character Chiz and her signature gear for free.

### Mastering the Hunter Level and Appraisal System

Hunter Level is the engine of your account. Every activity you perform—from finishing a main story Episode to opening a "Gift from 21" chest—contributes to this level. However, the Appraisal Level is what actually translates that progress into power.

When you hit Hunter Level 20, your EXP bar will turn gold. You must go to the Quest menu and track the Appraisal Breakthrough mission. These missions usually involve a combat challenge against an Anomaly boss. Once cleared, your Appraisal Level rises, and your Espers can be ascended to Level 30. If you find the world too difficult after an upgrade, the game allows you to manually lower your Appraisal Level by one tier starting at AL 3, though this will reduce the quality of your rewards.

### Stamina Management: Pixels vs. City Stamina

NTE uses a dual-stamina system that beginners often find confusing.

**Character Pixels (Combat Stamina):**
Used for Anomaly Zones, boss fights, and material domains. 
*   **Regeneration:** 1 Pixel every 6 minutes.
*   **Daily Total:** 240 Pixels.
*   **Efficiency Tip:** Use these on "Anomaly Pilgrimages" three times a week for massive bonus rewards. For daily farming, prioritize "Arc Ascension" materials over Cartridges until you can reliably clear Level 40+ domains.

**City Stamina (Management Stamina):**
Used for City Tycoon activities like racing, fishing, and delivery jobs.
*   **Regeneration:** This stamina does not regenerate daily; it refreshes at the start of each week.
*   **Efficiency Tip:** Focus your City Stamina on activities that grant the most Fons (the city currency). Reaching Rank 4 unlocks the **Cafe by Origen**, which provides passive Fons income even when you aren't playing.

### City Tycoon: The Secret to Free S-Rank Power

The City Tycoon system is not optional for players who want a strong roster. Reaching **Tycoon Rank 18** is a major milestone because it unlocks **Chiz**, an S-rank Cosmos DPS. 

To level up your Tycoon Rank quickly
1.  **Property Management:** Purchase and furnish apartments like the Wii Apartments. Increasing "House Comfort" to Level 20 is a requirement for Rank 8.
2.  **Hethereau Hobbies:** Engage in side jobs like package delivery or taxi driving. These cost City Stamina but return large amounts of Fons and Tycoon EXP.
3.  **The Garage:** Buy and customize vehicles. Owning at least four vehicles is a requirement for higher Tycoon ranks.

### Exploration and the Oracle Stone Loop

Hethereau is filled with exploration objectives that grant small amounts of Hunter EXP that add up quickly. 
*   **Oracle Stones:** These are floating blue shards. At Oracle Level 3 (upgraded at the Witch's House), these appear on your minimap. Collecting them is the fastest way to bridge the gap between story chapters.
*   **ReroRero Phone Booths & Wertheimer Towers:** Activating these serves as your fast-travel network and provides a one-time Hunter EXP boost.
*   **Gifts from 21:** These are the primary treasure chests of NTE. Higher Appraisal Levels improve the rewards inside these chests, so try to save large-scale chest hunting for after you hit AL 3.

### Gearing Up: Arcs, Cartridges, and the Tetris Module System

Leveling your characters is only half the battle. You must also manage their Arcs (weapons) and Cartridges (equipment).

**The Tetris Module System:** 
Unlike traditional gear systems, NTE uses a "Console" grid. You place "Modules" of different shapes (Type II, III, and IV) onto this grid to activate Cartridge set bonuses. 
*   **Lost Radiance Set:** This is the most important early-game set for DPS characters like Zero or Chiz. It grants a 10% Cosmos DMG bonus and ignores 25% of enemy DEF for 20 seconds after using an Ultimate. 
*   **Acquisition:** You can get a full Legendary "Lost Radiance" set by reaching **Phase 8** in the Exploration Guide.

**Traversal Efficiency:**
To speed up your exploration (and thus your leveling), visit the **Regalia dealership** on Camelot Avenue in the New Herland District. Purchase the **Novis ST-X 950 motorcycle** for 250,000 Fons. Unlike cars, the Novis can navigate tight alleys and off-road anomalies, making it much faster for collecting Oracle Stones and reaching distant quest markers.

### Optimizing Your Daily Routine

To maximize your growth, follow this checklist every day
1.  **Log in and check the Exploration Guide:** Claim any "Phase" rewards you've earned.
2.  **Spend all 240 Character Pixels:** If you are short on time, use the "Sweep" function on previously cleared Anomaly Zones.
3.  **Check the Cafe by Origen:** Collect your accumulated Fons.
4.  **Complete Daily Tasks:** These usually involve simple actions like "Modify a vehicle" or "Purchase a drink" and provide easy Hunter EXP.
5.  **Progress the Episode:** If your Hunter Level is high enough, always do the main story first.

### Advanced Combat Tips for Faster Clears

Faster combat means faster farming. Master the **Esper Cycle** to shred enemy shields
*   **Blossom (Cosmos + Anima):** Combining the MC (Zero) with Mint creates a Blossom turret that deals automatic AOE damage. This is perfect for clearing groups of mobs.
*   **Hexed (Anima + Incantation):** Use Mint and Adler to trigger Hexed. This reaction records damage dealt over 12 seconds and then unleashes it in a massive burst—essential for world bosses.
*   **Charge (Cosmos + Anima + Lakshana):** If you have a three-element team, triggering Charge provides massive Ultimate Energy regeneration, allowing you to spam high-damage abilities more frequently.

Focusing your resources on a core team of four—typically **Zero, Mint, Adler, and Haniel**—will ensure you don't run out of upgrade materials before reaching the level 60 cap.

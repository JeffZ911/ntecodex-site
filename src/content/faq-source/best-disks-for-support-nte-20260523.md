In Neverness to Everness (NTE), players often refer to the character's gear pieces as "disks" due to their circular design and the urban, music-themed aesthetic of the game’s UI. However, the official in-game term for these equipment items is Cartridges. These items are slotted into a character's Console to provide stat boosts and powerful set bonuses that can alter the flow of combat.

For support characters, the priority shifts away from raw Critical Rate or Elemental Damage. Instead, the focus is on identifying which gear sets provide the best buffs for the rest of the team, how these sets interact with the Esper Cycle, and which stats allow for the highest uptime on Ultimate abilities.

### Understanding NTE's Gear: Disks vs. Cartridges

While the community uses "disks" interchangeably with "Cartridges," the distinction is important when navigating the "Rabbit Hole" farming instances. Cartridges are divided into four distinct Types (I, II, III, and IV). To activate the most powerful 4-piece set bonuses, you must equip one of each Type within the same set. Support Cartridges specifically target "Charge Efficiency" and "Cycle Intensity," which are the primary engines behind the game’s Esper Reaction system.

## Detailed Explanation

The Cartridge system in Neverness to Everness is the backbone of character customization. Unlike DPS characters who look for "Crit Value," support characters are the "batteries" and "amplifiers" of the squad. Their primary goal is to accelerate the Esper Cycle—the mechanic where different elemental anomalies interact to produce massive burst damage.

### Top-Tier Support Cartridges (S-Tier)

**Speedy Hedgehog**
This is the gold standard for support gear as of the May 2026 meta. The 2-piece bonus provides a 12% boost to Charge Efficiency, which directly reduces the time it takes to build up an Ultimate. The 4-piece bonus is what makes it meta-defining: a 15% team-wide ATK buff that triggers upon Ultimate activation. In high-level content like the "Deep Rewind" challenges, this ATK buff is essential for meeting damage checks, especially when paired with a hypercarry like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte).

**Shadow Creed**
While often viewed as a Sub-DPS set, Shadow Creed is the premier choice for "Skill-looping" supports. If your character has short cooldowns and generates a high amount of particles through their basic Skill, the 25% ATK buff ensures that even your support contributes meaningful chip damage while setting up the field for your main attacker.

### Niche & Survival Support Sets

**Kingdom’s Guard**
Characters like Adler rely heavily on DEF scaling to provide shields. Kingdom’s Guard is currently the only set that offers both a flat DEF increase and a team-wide Damage Reduction (DR) buff. This is vital for players who struggle with the aggressive attack patterns of Boss-class Anomalies in the open world.

**Thea’s Night Tavern**
Fadia remains the most popular healer in the game, and Thea’s Night Tavern is her signature set. The energy restoration mechanic (2 energy per second) is often overlooked but allows for "infinite looping" of Ultimates in well-constructed teams.

**Tiny Big Adventure**
This set is specifically designed for characters whose utility scales with their health pool. By converting HP into Cycle Intensity, it allows a tanky support to increase the team's reaction damage without sacrificing their own survivability.

### Main Stat and Sub-stat Priority for Supports

To optimize your support Cartridges, you must look for specific Main Stats on your Type II and Type IV Modules. The following priority list ensures your support functions efficiently:

1.  **Charge Efficiency (Type II Main Stat):** This is non-negotiable. If your support cannot use their Ultimate every rotation, your team damage will plummet.
2.  **Cycle Intensity (Type IV Main Stat):** This stat determines how much damage your Esper Reactions (like "Shatter" or "Overload") deal. High Cycle Intensity on a support makes the entire team's elemental combos hit harder.
3.  **Flat HP / DEF (Type III Main Stat):** Depending on whether the character scales with HP (Fadia) or DEF (Adler).
4.  **Sub-stat Priority:** Charge Efficiency > Cycle Intensity > HP% > Flat DEF.

### Understanding the Module System (Type II, III, IV)

In the NTE Console UI, you will notice that you cannot simply equip four copies of the same "disk." Cartridges are categorized into Types:
*   **Type I:** Usually provides base flat stats (ATK/HP).
*   **Type II:** The "Utility" slot where you find Charge Efficiency.
*   **Type III:** The "Defensive" slot where you find DEF and HP percentages.
*   **Type IV:** The "Specialty" slot where you find Cycle Intensity and Elemental DMG.

To unlock the 4-piece Legendary effect (the golden text on the item description), you must have a matching set name across all four Types. Mixing sets is generally discouraged for supports unless you are in the very early game, as the 4-piece team buffs are significantly stronger than any individual stat gain from a "broken" set.

### Optimizing the Esper Cycle

The Esper Cycle is the circular gauge located near your character's health bar. It fills as you deal elemental damage. Support characters using the Speedy Hedgehog or Tiny Big Adventure sets accelerate this gauge significantly. When the gauge is full, switching to your main DPS triggers an "Esper Burst," which consumes all current buffs to deal a massive finishing blow. 

To optimize this, always rotate through your supports first to trigger the 4-piece Cartridge buffs (like the 15% ATK from Speedy Hedgehog) before switching to your Nanally or other hypercarry for the final burst. This ensures the Esper Reaction damage inherits the ATK buffs from your gear.

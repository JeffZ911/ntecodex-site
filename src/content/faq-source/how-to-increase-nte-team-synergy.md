### Mastering the Elemental Wheel: Adjacent vs. Non-Adjacent Swaps
The Elemental Wheel is the most critical UI element to understand for team synergy. The wheel is arranged in a circle: Lakshana connects to Cosmos and Incantation; Cosmos connects to Lakshana and Anima; Anima connects to Cosmos and Chaos; and so on. 

When you swap from an active character to one whose element is directly next to them on this wheel, you trigger a Duo Reaction. If you swap to a non-adjacent element—for example, swapping from a Lakshana character directly to a Chaos character—you lose the reaction bonus and reset your Cycle multiplier. To increase synergy, your team rotation should always follow the path of the wheel, either clockwise or counter-clockwise.

### Duo Reactions: The Foundation of Combat Flow
Duo Reactions are the bread and butter of NTE combat. To increase synergy, you should choose a "Lead Reaction" that fits your playstyle. If you prefer a defensive, sustainable style, building around **Blossom** (Anima + Lakshana) is ideal. If you want to speedrun bosses, focusing on **Hexed** (Lakshana + Incantation) to shred enemy armor is the superior strategy. 

The effectiveness of these reactions scales with the "Cycle Mastery" stat found on your gear and Consoles. High synergy teams prioritize this stat on their Support and Sub-DPS characters to ensure that every swap-in hits as hard as possible.

### Trio Reactions: Unleashing Charge and Discord
While Duo Reactions are powerful, Trio Reactions represent the pinnacle of NTE synergy. These occur when you complete a sequence of three adjacent elements.
*   **Charge (The Upper Trio):** By rotating through Cosmos, Anima, and Lakshana, you enter a "Charged" state. This state is essential for characters who rely on Basic Attack strings, as it significantly increases animation speed.
*   **Discord (The Lower Trio):** By rotating through Incantation, Chaos, and Psyche, you trigger "Discord." This is currently the meta-dominant reaction for clearing mob waves, as the resulting explosion scales with the number of enemies hit.

### The Role of the Esper Cycle Meter and Tactical Parrying
Synergy isn't just about the order of characters; it's about how fast you can swap them. The Esper Cycle Meter fills as you deal damage and take actions. However, the fastest way to fill the meter and trigger a synergy swap is through **Tactical Parrying**. 

When an enemy flashes with a gold or red indicator, performing a perfectly timed dodge or skill-based parry will instantly fill a significant portion of the Cycle Meter. This allows for a "Forced QTE," where the next character in your synergy chain can enter the field with an empowered attack immediately, skipping the usual cooldowns.

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Does team order matter in the character select screen?**
Yes. While you can manually click any portrait to swap, the default "Quick Swap" button cycles through your team in the order they are placed (1 through 4). To increase synergy, you should arrange your team in the order of their Elemental Wheel adjacency (e.g., Slot 1: Cosmos, Slot 2: Anima, Slot 3: Lakshana, Slot 4: Incantation).

**What happens if I swap to the same element twice?**
Swapping from an Anima character to another Anima character does not trigger a Duo Reaction. It provides a small amount of elemental energy but effectively "stalls" your Esper Cycle, leading to lower overall DPS.

**How do I know when a synergy swap is ready?**
When the Esper Cycle Meter for a benched character is full, their portrait on the right side of the screen will glow with their elemental color. If that character is an "adjacent" element to your current active character, the portrait will also have a pulsing "Reaction Ready" border.

**Is it better to focus on Duo or Trio Reactions?**
For general exploration and mob clearing, Duo Reactions are sufficient and easier to maintain. For boss fights and timed challenges like the "Hethereau Deep Dive," mastering the three-step rotation for Trio Reactions (Charge/Discord) is mandatory to meet the damage requirements.

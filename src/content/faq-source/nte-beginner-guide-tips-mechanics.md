Neverness to Everness (NTE) is a supernatural urban open-world RPG where players balance high-octane combat with a deep "City Tycoon" lifestyle simulation. You play as an "Appraiser" or "Esper" tasked with investigating supernatural anomalies throughout the city of Hethereau.

The core combat revolves around the "Esper Cycle," a reaction-based elemental system featuring six distinct types: Lakshana, Cosmos, Anima, Incantation, Chaos, and Psyche. Success in battle depends on triggering Duo and Trio reactions by switching characters in a specific order on the elemental wheel.

Progression is tied to both character leveling and City Tycoon rank, which unlocks vehicle customization, property ownership, and high-stakes street racing. Unlike many of its contemporaries, NTE places a heavy emphasis on the "lifestyle" aspect, where your car’s performance and your apartment’s location are just as important as your weapon’s stats.

The gacha system, "Scarborough Fair," uses a board-game mechanic with Solid and Fabricated Dice. It features a guaranteed hard pity at 90 pulls. Notably, the limited character banners in the 1.0 launch do not feature a 50/50 chance; if you hit the 90-pull pity on the limited banner, you are guaranteed the featured character.

## Detailed Explanation

### Mastering the Esper Cycle and Elemental Reactions

Combat in NTE is fast-paced and rewards aggressive play. Every character has an **Esper Meter** that fills as you deal damage or perform successful parries. Parrying is a core mechanic; a "Perfect Parry" not only negates damage but also provides an instant 25% refill to your Esper Ability cooldown.

The **Esper Cycle** is the heart of your team's damage output. Elements are arranged in a wheel, and switching characters in clockwise or counter-clockwise order triggers different effects.

| Element | Role/Vibe | Common Synergy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Lakshana** | Light/Burst | Pairs with Cosmos for "Radiant Burst" |
| **Cosmos** | Gravity/Control | Pairs with Anima for "Vortex" |
| **Anima** | Wind/Speed | Pairs with Incantation for "Gale Burst" |
| **Incantation** | Fire/DoT | Pairs with Chaos for "Abyssal Burn" |
| **Chaos** | Dark/Debuff | Pairs with Psyche for "Mental Fracture" |
| **Psyche** | Mind/Support | Pairs with Lakshana for "Soul Link" |

**Duo Reactions** occur when you apply two elements. For example, hitting a target with Anima and then switching to an Incantation character triggers **Gale Burst**, which deals massive AOE fire damage based on the wind speed. **Trio Reactions** are more complex, requiring three specific elements in sequence (e.g., Chaos + Psyche + Lakshana = **Void Collapse**) to execute a screen-clearing finisher.

### Navigating the Scarborough Fair Gacha System

The gacha system in NTE, known as **Scarborough Fair**, is presented as a digital board game. When you pull, your avatar moves across a board filled with rewards.

*   **Solid Dice:** Used for Limited Character and Weapon banners. These are primarily purchased with Beetle Coins.
*   **Fabricated Dice:** Used for the Standard "Hethereau Commons" banner.
*   **The Board Mechanic:** As you move, you land on "Lucky Tiles" (materials), "Anomaly Tiles" (minor buffs), or "Grand Prize Tiles" (4-star and 5-star characters/weapons).
*   **Arcs (Weapons):** Arcs are NTE's version of weapons. They require a **Tri-Key** to fully unlock their potential, which is usually obtained by pulling duplicates or through high-level City Tycoon rewards.

### City Tycoon: Vehicles, Properties, and Passive Income

The "Everness" part of the title refers to the lifestyle simulation. Your **City Tycoon Rank** is just as important as your combat level.

**Vehicle Mechanics**
Cars are not just for travel; they are essential for certain "Rampage" missions.
*   **Key Fob System:** You can summon your car to any road by pressing the 'V' key (PC) or the car icon (Mobile).
*   **Road Restrictions:** Cars cannot be summoned in "Pedestrian Only" zones or inside Anomaly rifts.
*   **Durability:** Crashing or taking damage during combat reduces vehicle durability. If it hits zero, the car explodes, and you must pay a significant **Fons** penalty at a repair shop to get it back.

### Early Game Progression: Quests and Rerolling

The most efficient way to progress is to follow the **Main Story (Chapter 1: The Appraiser's Debt)**. This will naturally introduce you to the different districts of Hethereau and unlock the various City Tycoon features.

**Exploration Skills**
Characters have unique ways of moving through the city.
*   **Nanally:** Can run up vertical walls for a limited time, making her essential for finding hidden Beetle Coins on rooftops.
*   **Skia:** Can transform into a liquid form to pass through fences and grates.
*   **Hotori:** Uses her umbrella to glide across large gaps between skyscrapers.

**Rerolling Strategy**
Rerolling in NTE takes approximately 20 minutes. You need to play until you unlock the Scarborough Fair tutorial.
*   **Target Jiuyuan:** As the first limited 5-star, Jiuyuan is currently the meta-defining Lakshana DPS. Her ability to trigger "Radiant Burst" solo makes her incredibly valuable.
*   **Target Daffodill:** If you are pulling on the standard banner, Daffodill is the premier Chaos support. Her debuffs increase the damage of all elemental reactions by 15%.

### Building Your First Team: Free Characters and Synergy

You don't need to spend money to have a viable team. The free characters provided in the first hour are surprisingly strong.

1.  **The Protagonist (Adaptive):** Can switch elements later in the story, but starts as a solid physical/all-rounder.
2.  **Nanally (Anima):** Your primary mobility and crowd control character. Use her to group enemies before switching.
3.  **Hotori (Incantation):** A strong DoT (Damage over Time) dealer. Apply her fire stacks, then switch to an Anima character to trigger Gale Burst.

A common early-game rotation is: **Nanally (Group) -> Hotori (Burn) -> Protagonist (Finish).** This simple cycle handles almost all open-world content in Chapter 1.

# Neverness to Everness: Charge Reaction Mechanics Explained

The Charge reaction in NTE is a Triple Esper Cycle triggered by combining Lakshana, Cosmos, and Anima elements. It restores 10 Ultimate Energy when Vita Pistils hit a target affected by the Remora slow effect. Unlike damage-focused reactions, Charge serves as a utility engine that allows players to sustain high-cost Ultimate abilities through rapid energy generation.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-charge-reaction-mechanics-explained/inline-2.webp)


Charge is one of two advanced Triple Cycle reactions in the NTE Esper system. It is specifically designed to facilitate "Infinite Ultimate" builds by providing a flat energy refund to the active character.

To trigger Charge, you must follow a three-element rotation involving Lakshana (Gold), Cosmos (Silver), and Anima (Green). The reaction occurs when the unique projectiles created by the Bloom status (Vita Pistils) strike an enemy who is already suffering from the Remora status.

The primary benefit of Charge is utility: it restores 10 Ultimate Energy to the active character upon every successful trigger. This makes it the preferred reaction for teams featuring "energy-hungry" characters whose primary power comes from their high-cost Ultimate skills.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-charge-reaction-mechanics-explained/inline-3.webp)


The depth of NTE combat lies in the Esper system, where characters are categorized into six distinct elements. These elements are arranged on a circular wheel, and reactions can only occur between adjacent elements or through specific three-part cycles.

### The Esper Wheel and Element Adjacency

The Esper Wheel is divided into two halves. One half contains the elements associated with the Charge reaction: Lakshana, Cosmos, and Anima. For a reaction to occur, you must apply one element and then quickly swap to a character of an adjacent element to trigger the effect.

In the case of Charge, the adjacency works as follows:
1.  **Lakshana + Cosmos:** Triggers the Remora reaction, which slows enemies.
2.  **Cosmos + Anima:** Triggers the Bloom reaction, which generates Vita Pistils.
3.  **Anima + Lakshana:** Triggers the Hexed reaction.

The Charge "Triple Cycle" effectively bridges these interactions. By maintaining both Remora and Bloom effects on a single target, the game registers a Charge reaction.

### How to Fill the Esper Meter Fast

To trigger any reaction, you must first fill the Esper Meter for your characters. Once the meter is full, a character's portrait will glow, indicating that their "Esper Ability" (an entry attack) is ready. Swapping to a character with a full meter triggers this ability, applying their element and initiating the reaction.

| Action Type | Meter Gain Efficiency |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Normal Attacks** | Low (sustained build-up) |
| **Resonance Skills** | Medium (burst build-up) |
| **Instant Parry** | High (massive gain upon successful parry) |
| **Perfect Dodge** | Medium-High |

The Instant Parry mechanic is the most efficient way to cycle through reactions. By timing your defense against enemy attacks, you can instantly fill a large portion of the Esper Meter, allowing for faster character swaps and more frequent Charge triggers.

### The Anatomy of a Triple Cycle: From Remora to Charge

Executing a Charge reaction requires a disciplined rotation. A standard execution looks like this:

1.  **Step 1:** Start with a Lakshana character (like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)) and use attacks to fill the meter.
2.  **Step 2:** Swap to a Cosmos character (like Zero). This triggers the Remora reaction, slowing the enemy.
3.  **Step 3:** While the enemy is slowed, fill the Cosmos character's meter and swap to an Anima character (like Skia). This triggers Bloom, creating Vita Pistils.
4.  **Final Result:** As the Vita Pistils strike the slowed enemy, the Charge reaction activates, granting 10 Ultimate Energy.

### Key Stats: Cycle Rate and Cycle Intensity Explained

Two primary statistics govern the effectiveness of your Charge team: Cycle Rate and Cycle Intensity. These are often found on "Cartridges" (the game's gear system) or as passive bonuses on Arcs.

*   **Cycle Rate:** This stat increases the speed at which the Esper Meter fills. High Cycle Rate is vital for the characters responsible for setting up the reaction (the Lakshana and Cosmos units) so they can swap out quickly.
*   **Cycle Intensity:** This stat scales the magnitude of the reaction's effect. While the base energy gain for Charge is 10 Ultimate Energy, a high Cycle Intensity stat can further enhance the utility or duration of the associated status effects (like the Remora slow).

### Best Characters for Charge Teams (Nanally, Zero, Skia)

The current "meta" for Charge reactions focuses on three specific characters who excel at their respective elemental roles:

*   **Nanally (Lakshana):** Often used as the starter or "driver" of the team. Her quick attacks allow her to build meter rapidly to initiate the Remora setup.
*   **Zero (Cosmos):** A versatile unit whose Esper Ability has a wide area of effect, making it easy to apply the Cosmos element to multiple targets for large-scale energy gains.
*   **Skia (Anima):** Typically the "finisher" in a Charge rotation. Her abilities generate numerous projectiles, ensuring that multiple Vita Pistils hit the target to maximize the Charge triggers.

Beyond Charge, the Neverness to Everness combat system offers an offensive counterpart and several foundational reactions that players must understand to master endgame content.

### Discord vs. Charge: Which Trio is Better?

While Charge focuses on energy and utility, the Discord reaction is the go-to choice for pure damage. Discord is the second Triple Cycle in NTE and uses the other side of the Esper Wheel: Incantation, Chaos, and Psyche.

| Feature | Charge Reaction | Discord Reaction |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Elements** | Lakshana, Cosmos, Anima | Incantation, Chaos, Psyche |
| **Focus** | Energy Regeneration / Utility | Damage Amplification / Burst |
| **Key Mechanic** | 10 Ultimate Energy Gain | Massive Elemental Damage Proc |
| **Best Use Case** | Sustaining Ultimates | Speedrunning Boss Fights |

Choosing between Charge and Discord depends on your team's needs. If your main damage dealer relies on an expensive Ultimate (over 100 energy cost), Charge is often more effective than Discord because it allows for higher "up-time" on your most powerful skills.

### Essential Duo Reactions for Charge Setups

Before you can master the Triple Cycle, you must be comfortable with the Duo reactions that form its foundation.

*   **Remora (Lakshana + Cosmos):** This reaction is vital for crowd control. It applies a significant slow effect to enemies, making them easier to hit with the subsequent Vita Pistils required for Charge.
*   **Bloom (Cosmos + Anima):** Bloom creates Vita Pistils, which are homing projectiles. These are the "active ingredient" in the Charge reaction; without Bloom, the energy restoration cannot trigger.
*   **Hexed (Anima + Lakshana):** While not directly part of the Charge sequence, Hexed is the third duo in this elemental group and provides additional debuffs that can help soften up targets.

### Weapon Synergy: How Arcs Influence Your Esper Cycle

In NTE, weapons are known as Arcs. These items do more than just provide raw attack power; they often come with passives that specifically target the Esper system.

For a Charge-focused team, look for Arcs that provide:
*   **Energy Recharge:** Complements the 10 energy gain from Charge.
*   **Esper Meter Gain:** Helps characters reach their swap-in state faster.
*   **Elemental Mastery:** Increases the damage of the Bloom and Remora procs that occur during the rotation.

### Advanced Combat: Animation Canceling

High-level players often use animation canceling during the "Esper Ability" entry. When you swap characters with a full meter, the incoming character performs a unique attack. By inputting a dash or a Resonance Skill immediately after the character appears, you can cut the recovery frames of the entry animation, allowing you to start building the next meter immediately. This is crucial for maintaining the fast-paced rotation required for consistent Charge procs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much energy does the Charge reaction actually give?
The base effect of the Charge reaction restores 10 Ultimate Energy to the active character. This occurs when a Vita Pistil (created by the Bloom reaction) strikes an enemy affected by the Remora (slow) status. This gain can be further optimized by equipping gear with the Cycle Intensity stat.

### Can I trigger Charge with just two characters?
No, Charge is a Triple Cycle reaction that requires three distinct elements: Lakshana, Cosmos, and Anima. Because each character in NTE is locked to a single element, you must have a full team of three characters—one for each element—and rotate through them to successfully trigger the reaction.

### What is the difference between Cycle Rate and Cycle Intensity?
Cycle Rate determines how quickly your characters fill their Esper Meter through combat actions like attacking or parrying. Cycle Intensity, on the other hand, determines the strength of the reaction's effect once it is triggered. For a Charge team, Cycle Rate is generally prioritized to ensure the energy-gain loop remains constant.

### Which characters are best for a Charge-based team?
The most popular team for triggering Charge consists of Nanally (Lakshana), Zero (Cosmos), and Skia (Anima). Nanally provides rapid meter build-up, Zero applies the necessary Remora slow effect over a wide area, and Skia’s multi-hit abilities ensure that plenty of Vita Pistils trigger the Charge energy refund.

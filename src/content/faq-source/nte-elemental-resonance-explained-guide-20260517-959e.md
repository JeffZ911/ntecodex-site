## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-elemental-resonance-explained-guide-20260517-959e/inline-2.webp)


Elemental Resonance in NTE is a specialized combat system where neighboring elements on the "Esper Wheel" trigger powerful reactions when a character is swapped into the field while the Esper Meter is full. There are six primary elements: Lakshana (Gold), Cosmos (Silver), Anima (Green), Incantation (Red), Chaos (Purple), and Psyche (Azure). 

Reactions are strictly categorized into Duo Cycles (combining two adjacent elements) and Trio Cycles (combining three specific elements in sequence). These reactions provide essential combat utility, such as the **Blossom** reaction which creates AoE turrets, the **Scorch** reaction for damage-over-time, or the **Discord** Trio reaction for massive Break damage against boss shields. To master resonance, you must build teams that follow the wheel's path and stack character passives that enhance these specific elemental interactions.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-elemental-resonance-explained-guide-20260517-959e/inline-3.webp)


The combat in Neverness to Everness is built around the "Esper Cycle," a flow state achieved by rotating characters in a specific elemental order. If you swap from an element to one that is not its immediate neighbor on the Esper Wheel, you lose the opportunity for a reaction, effectively wasting your Esper Meter.

### The Six Elements and the Esper Wheel

The Esper Wheel is a circular diagram that defines the relationship between all six elements. In NTE, the order is fixed. To trigger a resonance, the character you swap *in* must possess an element that is adjacent to the character currently *on* the field.

The official order of the Esper Wheel is:
1.  **Lakshana (Gold)**
2.  **Cosmos (Silver)**
3.  **Anima (Green)**
4.  **Incantation (Red)**
5.  **Chaos (Purple)**
6.  **Psyche (Azure)**
*(The wheel then loops back from Psyche to Lakshana)*

If you are currently playing as Baicang (Lakshana) and swap to a Cosmos character, you trigger a Duo Cycle. However, if you swap from Baicang directly to [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) (Incantation), no elemental resonance occurs because Incantation is not adjacent to Lakshana on the wheel.

### Unlocking Trio Cycle Power: Charge and Discord

While Duo Cycles are frequent, Trio Cycles represent the peak of NTE’s combat depth. These require you to complete a three-step rotation across adjacent elements without breaking the chain. Successfully completing a Trio Cycle grants a massive "Esper Burst" effect that can turn the tide of a boss fight.

**1. The Charge Cycle (Lakshana + Cosmos + Anima)**
The Charge reaction is the ultimate utility combo. When you rotate through these three elements in order, your entire team receives a massive surge in energy regeneration. This allows you to use Ultimate Arcs much more frequently. It is often used at the start of a fight to set up your team's buffs.

**2. The Discord Cycle (Incantation + Chaos + Psyche)**
Discord is the primary tool for dealing with "Anomalous Shields" found on world bosses. This Trio reaction focuses on "Break" damage. When Discord is triggered, it deals a heavy blow to the enemy's stagger bar (Break Gauge). In the 1.0 meta, teams featuring Nanally (Incantation), Daffodill (Chaos), and [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte) (Psyche) are highly valued specifically for their ability to trigger Discord and shred boss defenses.

### Esper Meter Management: The Key to Constant Rotations

You cannot trigger a resonance reaction simply by swapping characters. You must first fill the circular **Esper Meter** located around the character's portrait on the HUD. Once the meter is full, the portrait will glow, indicating that a "Cycle Swap" is ready.

The speed at which this meter fills is determined by several factors:
*   **Normal Attacks:** Provide a steady, slow build-up of the meter.
*   **Elemental Skills:** Grant a significant chunk of meter on hit.
*   **Swap Parries:** If you swap characters the moment an enemy attack lands (indicated by a white or red flash), the incoming character gains an instant 50% to 100% meter charge depending on their "Cycle Rate" stat.
*   **Cycle Rate Stat:** Characters like **Esper Zero** and **Fadia** have naturally high Cycle Rate scaling, making them excellent "drivers" for teams that rely on rapid-fire reactions.

**Esper Meter Generation Rates**
*   **Normal Attack String:** ~10-15% per full combo.
*   **Elemental Skill:** ~30-45% per cast.
*   **Perfect Swap Parry:** 100% (Instant Charge).
*   **Dodge Counter:** ~20% per successful evasion.

### Stacking Resonance Passives for Meta Teams

In the current 1.0 version of Neverness to Everness, a popular strategy is to run two characters of the same element alongside a third "Reaction Driver." While having two characters of the same element doesn't trigger a reaction between them, it allows you to stack **Resonance Passives**.

For example, running **Nanally** and **Jiuyuan** (both Incantation) provides a team-wide buff to Incantation damage. When you swap from this duo into a Chaos character like **Daffodill**, the resulting **Nova** reaction inherits the buffed stats of the Incantation resonance. This "Mono-Core" strategy is currently dominant in the Urban Shadow challenge modes.

Daffodill is a unique case in the 1.0 roster because she possesses a specialized "Resonance Stack" system. As a Chaos-user, she is designed to be the "finisher" in many rotations. 

When teammates use their Support Skills or trigger a Duo Cycle involving Chaos, Daffodill gains a "Resonance Stack" undisclosed.

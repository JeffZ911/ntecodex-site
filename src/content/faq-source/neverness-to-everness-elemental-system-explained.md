## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/neverness-to-everness-elemental-system-explained/inline-2.webp)


The elemental system in Neverness to Everness is known as the "Esper Cycle." It features six unique elements: Lakshana, Cosmos, Anima, Incantation, Chaos, and Psyche. Each character in the game is attuned to one of these six types, which determines their role in a team rotation and the types of reactions they can trigger.

Unlike traditional RPG systems based on fixed elemental weaknesses (such as Water beating Fire), NTE uses an "Adjacency Rule." In this system, reactions only occur between neighboring elements on a hexagonal Esper Wheel. You cannot trigger a reaction between two elements that are on opposite sides of the wheel; you must follow the circular path to create "Cycles."

Combat revolves around filling a character's Esper Meter. Once this meter is full, swapping that character onto the field triggers a powerful Esper Ability. If the incoming character's element is adjacent to the previous character's element on the Esper Wheel, a Duo Reaction occurs. If three adjacent elements are used in a specific sequence, a Trio Reaction is triggered. These reactions provide massive buffs, crowd control, or burst damage, such as "Blossom" (which creates autonomous turrets) or "Discord" (which deals massive [Break damage](https://ntecodex.com/guides/hsr-3-0-super-break-damage-calculation-gear-guide) to enemy shields).

### The Core Adjacency Rule

The Esper Wheel is a hexagon. To trigger a reaction, you must move from one element to an immediately adjacent one. For example, moving from Lakshana to Cosmos triggers a reaction because they are neighbors. However, moving from Lakshana to Incantation will not trigger a Duo Reaction because they are separated by Cosmos and Anima on the wheel. This rule forces players to build teams with "connecting" elements to ensure every character swap contributes to a Cycle.

### Duo Reactions: The Six Basic Cycles

Duo Reactions are the bread and butter of NTE combat. They occur when two adjacent elements are applied in sequence. The order does not matter for the type of reaction, but it does matter for the rotation flow.

### Trio Reactions: Unleashing Charged and Discord

The highest level of elemental play involves the Triple Cycle, or Trio Reaction. This occurs when you successfully trigger two Duo Reactions in a row using three adjacent elements. The Esper Wheel is divided into two halves for these powerful effects.

**Trio Reaction (Triple Cycle) Mechanics**

*   **The Top Trio (Charged):** Triggered by cycling through Lakshana, Cosmos, and Anima. Completing this cycle grants the "Charged" status to the entire team. Charged significantly increases the Cycle Rate (meter gain) for 15 seconds, allowing for much faster rotations.
*   **The Bottom Trio (Discord):** Triggered by cycling through Incantation, Chaos, and Psyche. Completing this cycle triggers "Discord." This is a massive burst of Mental and [Break damage](https://ntecodex.com/guides/hsr-3-0-super-break-damage-calculation-gear-guide) that can instantly shatter the shields of elite enemies and bosses in Hethereau.

### Key Stats: Cycle Rate vs. Cycle Intensity

When equipping your characters with Arcs and Modules, you will notice two primary stats related to the elemental system:

1.  **Cycle Rate:** This stat determines how quickly a character generates Esper Meter. A high Cycle Rate is essential for "Battery" characters who need to swap in and out frequently to keep the rotation moving.
2.  **Cycle Intensity:** This stat scales the actual damage and utility of the reactions themselves. For example, a character with high Cycle Intensity will create "Blossom" turrets that deal significantly more damage or a "Scorch" explosion with a larger radius.

Balancing these two stats is critical. If your Cycle Rate is too low, your rotations will feel sluggish. If your Cycle Intensity is too low, your reactions will feel weak even if you are triggering them constantly.

### Strategic Team Compositions for Maximum DPS

A standard team in Neverness to Everness consists of three characters. To maximize the Esper Cycle, your team should ideally consist of three adjacent elements.

**The "Blossom" Core (Lakshana - Cosmos - Anima)**
This is a popular defensive and utility-focused team. By rotating from Mint (Lakshana) to Nanally (Cosmos) and then to Jiuyuan (Anima), you trigger Remora (grouping) followed by Blossom (turrets). Completing the rotation triggers the "Charged" Trio Reaction, allowing you to start the cycle again almost immediately.

**The "Break" Core (Incantation - Chaos - Psyche)**
This team is designed for boss killing. By focusing on the bottom half of the Esper Wheel, you consistently trigger Scorch and Nova, culminating in the Discord Trio Reaction. This is the most efficient way to deplete a boss's Break gauge.

**The "Soup" Rotation**
Advanced players often use characters that have high off-field elemental application. By using an Anima character's skill that stays on the field and then swapping through Cosmos and Lakshana, you can technically trigger multiple "Cycles" simultaneously if the timing is precise.

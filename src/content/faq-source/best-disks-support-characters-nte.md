### Understanding the 'Disk' Terminology in NTE

In Neverness to Everness, the "Disk" system is technically the Console. The Console consists of a grid where you place Modules (Tetris-like shapes) and slots for Cartridges (the items that provide set bonuses). 

*   **Cartridges:** These function as the primary gear pieces. You can equip up to four. Having two or four of the same set activates powerful passive abilities.
*   **Modules:** These are the geometric pieces you slot into the Console grid. They provide raw stats and are essential for activating the "Tetris Passive" unique to every character.

### The Tetris Puzzle: How to Activate 4-Piece Bonuses

Every character has a Console grid with a specific shape. To activate a 4-piece Cartridge bonus, you don't just "click and equip." You must place Modules into the grid that connect the Cartridge slots. 

Each character has a "Tetris Passive" visible in the top-left of the Console screen. This passive rewards you for using specific Module types (Type II, Type III, or Type IV). For example, Sakiri’s passive increases the team's Nova damage if you fill her grid using at least four Type III Modules. This adds a layer of complexity; you aren't just looking for the best stats, but the right shapes to fit the grid while satisfying the passive requirements.

### Advanced Stat Min-Maxing: Cycle Intensity vs. Break Intensity

A common mistake for new players is over-investing in ATK for support characters. In NTE, the "Esper Cycle" mechanic is the primary driver of DPS. An Esper Cycle occurs when you switch characters after filling a specific gauge. 

Supports are the primary "chargers" for this gauge. If you have 150% Cycle Intensity, your support can fill the gauge in half the time it takes a support with 100%. This allows you to switch back to your main DPS (like Nanally) much faster. 

Break Intensity is a separate calculation. While Cycle Intensity helps you use abilities, Break Intensity ensures those abilities actually stop the enemy from attacking. In the 1.0 meta, it is generally recommended to reach a "soft cap" of 140% Cycle Intensity before putting points into Break Intensity or Elemental Mastery.

### How to Farm High-Tier Modules and Carrota Coins

Farming gear in NTE requires managing two different resources: **Character Pixels** and **Carrota Coins**.

1.  **Cartridges (Rabbit Hole):** You farm Cartridges in Anomaly Zones, specifically the "Rabbit Hole" instances located in the New Herland District. Entering these costs Character Pixels (the game's stamina system). Higher difficulty levels guarantee 5-star (Gold) Cartridges.
2.  **Modules (Rewind Gacha):** Unlike Cartridges, Modules are primarily obtained through the "Rewind" system. You spend Carrota Coins—earned through daily missions, world exploration, and side quests—to pull for Modules. 
3.  **Sub-stat Visibility:** One of the most player-friendly features of NTE is that you can see all four sub-stats on a Module or Cartridge before you spend resources to level it. This allows you to "trash" gear that doesn't have Cycle Intensity or HP/DEF rolls immediately, saving your upgrade materials for perfect pieces.

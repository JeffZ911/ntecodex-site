## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/neverness-to-everness-elemental-resonance-explained/inline-2.webp)


Elemental Resonance in NTE refers to the synergy between characters' Esper types, primarily manifested through the "Esper Cycle" reaction system and passive "Awakening Resonance" buffs. Unlike traditional elemental systems where any two elements might interact, NTE utilizes a directional "Esper Wheel" where reactions only occur between specific, adjacent elements.

The system relies on six distinct elements, each associated with a specific theme and color

| Element | Theme | Primary Color |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Lakshana | Order | Gold / Yellow |
| Cosmos | Light | Blue / Cyan |
| Anima | Life | Green |
| Incantation | Force | Red / Orange |
| Chaos | Dark | Purple |
| Psyche | Mind | Pink / Magenta |

Active resonance (reactions) occurs when you swap to a character with a full Esper Meter, provided their element is adjacent to the previous character's on the Esper Wheel. This is the "Esper Cycle." Passive resonance, on the other hand, is achieved by stacking characters with similar reaction-buffing passives or reaching specific Awakening levels (A1-A6) that provide team-wide elemental bonuses. For example, specific Awakening tiers can shred enemy resistance to a specific element or increase the Cycle Rate for the entire party.

### The Esper Wheel: Understanding Elemental Adjacency

The foundation of Neverness to Everness combat is the Esper Wheel. This circular diagram dictates which elements can "Cycle" with one another. In NTE, elements do not react with every other element in the game. Instead, an element only reacts with its two immediate neighbors on the wheel.

The standard order of the Esper Wheel is
**Lakshana → Cosmos → Anima → Incantation → Chaos → Psyche → (Back to Lakshana)**

If you are currently controlling a Cosmos character (like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or Chiz), you can only trigger a Duo Cycle reaction by swapping into a character that is either Lakshana or Anima. Attempting to swap into an Incantation or Chaos character will still switch the character, but it will not trigger a Cycle reaction, resulting in a significant loss of potential damage and utility.

### Managing the Esper Meter and Cycle Rate

The Esper Meter is the circular gauge around a character's portrait. You cannot trigger a resonance reaction unless this meter is 100% full. While the meter fills naturally as you deal damage, certain actions provide much higher efficiency.

| Action | Esper Meter Generation |
| :--- | :--- |
| Normal Attack String | Low |
| Elemental Skill | Moderate |
| Ultimate / Burst | High |
| Perfect Parry | 100% (Instant Charge) |

**Cycle Rate** is a critical character stat that acts as a multiplier for all energy generation. Characters with a high base Cycle Rate are often used as "Enablers" or "Batteries." By stacking Cycle Rate on your support characters, you ensure that they are always ready to swap in and trigger a resonance reaction the moment your main DPS has finished their combo.

### Awakening Resonance and Artifact Synergy

Beyond the active swapping of the Esper Cycle, "Awakening Resonance" provides vertical investment for your team. As you obtain duplicates of characters and unlock their Awakening levels (A1 through A6), they gain passive traits that often benefit their specific element or the entire team's resonance efficiency.

For example, Chiz at A6 provides a significant reduction to enemy Cosmos Resistance, which indirectly boosts the damage of any Cosmos-based resonance reactions, such as Blossom (when paired with an Anima unit). 

Artifact sets (known as Arcs) further enhance this. Certain high-tier Arcs provide set bonuses that trigger specifically upon an Esper Cycle swap. A common endgame strategy involves equipping your support characters with Arcs that shred elemental resistance for 10-15 seconds after they enter the field via a Cycle reaction.

### Meta Team Building: Stacking Passive Resonance

Building a meta team in Neverness to Everness is not just about picking the highest-rated characters on a tier list; it is about ensuring your elemental types can actually talk to each other on the Esper Wheel.

A standard "Resonance Core" usually consists of
1.  **Main DPS:** The character who stays on field longest (e.g., Nanally).
2.  **Primary Reactor:** A character whose element is adjacent to the Main DPS to trigger Duo Cycles (e.g., Jiuyuan for Blossom).
3.  **Utility/Flex:** A character who provides buffs, healing, or a third element for Triple Cycle reactions (e.g., [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte) for Lakshana support).

By aligning your team according to the Esper Wheel, you ensure that every swap contributes to a reaction, rather than just resetting your attack string.

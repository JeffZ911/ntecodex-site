# How to Farm Currency in Neverness to Everness (NTE)

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/how-to-farm-nte-currency-guide/inline-1.webp)


Players arriving in the supernatural metropolis of Hethereau often find themselves overwhelmed by the various denominations of money required to progress. Whether you are looking to pull for the latest limited character on "The Ichi-daime" banner or simply want to purchase a new high-end sports car, understanding where to focus your time is essential.

Effective farming requires answering three main questions:
*   What are the most efficient ways to farm currency in Neverness to Everness?
*   How do I distinguish between premium Annulith and lifestyle Fons?
*   Where can I find daily and weekly tasks that reward gacha pulls?

### Understanding the NTE Economy

The economy of Neverness to Everness is split into three distinct categories: premium gacha resources, lifestyle/world currency, and character development materials. Unlike traditional mobile RPGs where one "Gold" currency covers everything, NTE separates your ability to buy a house from your ability to upgrade a weapon. This separation ensures that players can enjoy the "urban life" simulation aspects of the game without depleting the resources needed for combat power.

### The Difference Between Annulith, Fons, and Beetle Coins

Before starting your farm, you must know what you are digging for:

1.  **Annulith:** This is the premium currency used primarily for purchasing "Warp" items to summon new characters and Arcs (weapons). It is the most valuable and scarcest resource.
2.  **Fons:** The "lifestyle" currency. You use Fons to buy cars, furniture for your apartment, clothing, and items at the various shops throughout Hethereau. While it is more abundant than Annulith, high-end luxury items require millions of Fons.
3.  **Beetle Coins:** These are strictly for character and Arc progression. If you need to level up your team’s combat effectiveness or breakthrough level caps, Beetle Coins are the required medium of exchange.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-farm-nte-currency-guide/inline-2.webp)


The primary way to farm premium currency (Annulith) is through Daily Participation Tasks, Spinoff side quests, and [world exploration](https://ntecodex.com/guides/nte-vs-zzz-open-world-exploration-gear-comparison) (Anomalies). Fons, the lifestyle currency, is best farmed via the Cafe by Origen management system and the bi-weekly Pink Paws Heist. Beetle Coins for character upgrades are obtained through Circle Bounty dailies and spending City Stamina on activities like racing or fishing.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-farm-nte-currency-guide/inline-3.webp)


### The Daily Farming Loop: Participation Tasks & Circle Bounties

The foundation of your NTE income is the Daily Participation system. By completing standard gameplay activities—such as finishing a bounty or spending City Stamina—you earn points toward five daily reward chests. 

Reaching 100 points daily is mandatory for any player looking to maximize their Annulith income. Each chest provides a combination of Fons and Annulith, totaling undisclosed Annulith per day.

Circle Bounties serve as your main combat-focused daily task. You can complete up to three bounties per day to earn Beetle Coins and character EXP materials. These are located at designated "Circle" kiosks around the city and usually involve clearing a specific group of Anomalies or supernatural threats in a timed encounter.

### Daily Routine Checklist & Rewards

| Task Name | Requirement | Primary Reward |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Login Reward** | Open the game | Small Fons bonus |
| **3 Circle Bounties** | Combat encounters | Beetle Coins & EXP |
| **Spend City Stamina** | Racing/Fishing/Delivery | 1,000 Fons per 1 Stamina |
| **Cafe Check-in** | Collect earnings | Passive Fons |
| **100 Participation Points**| Various activities | Annulith & Fons |

### Passive Income: Maximizing the Cafe by Origen

The Cafe by Origen is the most significant source of Fons in the game. Once unlocked, you can manage recipes, staff, and shop upgrades to generate income while you are offline. 

To maximize this, you should prioritize unlocking new recipe slots and hiring high-efficiency staff members. Your "Tycoon Level" dictates your maximum storage capacity for Fons and the rate at which you generate income. Investing Beetle Coins into your Cafe early pays off significantly in the long run, as it allows you to purchase expensive vehicles without grinding manual tasks.

### High-Yield Weekly Activities: Pink Paws Heist & World Bosses

Once you reach Tycoon Level 11, you unlock the Pink Paws Heist. This is a bi-weekly event located at the Pink Paws Bank in Hethereau. Unlike daily tasks, the Heist is a multi-stage mission involving stealth, puzzle-solving, and a final combat escape.

The rewards are tiered based on your performance and the "Heat" level you choose. At maximum difficulty, a successful heist can net you up to 1,000,000 Fons every two weeks.

### Urban Exploration: Hunting Anomalies and Ravens

For one-time injections of Annulith, you must explore the city of Hethereau. 
*   **Anomalies:** These are blue markers on your map representing environmental puzzles or small combat challenges. Completing these usually grants a small amount of Annulith and Beetle Coins.
*   **Ravens:** These are collectible entities hidden throughout the city (often on rooftops or inside narrow alleys). Finding a certain number of Ravens allows you to claim milestone rewards from the Raven Collector NPC, which include significant Annulith sums and exclusive furniture.
*   **Spinoffs:** These are light-blue side quests. While they take longer than Anomalies, they provide some of the largest one-time Annulith rewards outside of the main story chapters.

### Endgame Farming: Beyond the Rails and Prime Circle

"Beyond the Rails" is the primary endgame mode for Neverness to Everness. It functions as a monthly reset tower where you fight through increasingly difficult floors of enemies. 
*   **Anuweth:** This is a high-tier reward material found in the deeper levels of Beyond the Rails, used for final-stage character enhancements.
*   **Summon Currency:** Every few floors, you are rewarded with Annulith or direct Warp Pieces. Clearing the entire mode each month is the most important task for F2P (Free to Play) players to keep up with the gacha cycle.

The Prime Circle is a weekly boss challenge. While it doesn't provide much Annulith, it is the best place to farm high-rarity Arcs and the Beetle Coins needed to upgrade them.

### Resource Conversion: Using Warp Pieces and Lost Pieces

When you perform summons (Warps), you will occasionally receive "Lost Pieces" (for duplicate lower-rarity pulls) or "Warp Pieces" (for higher-rarity duplicates). These can be traded in the Exchange Shop for:
*   **Direct Annulith:** undisclosed.
*   **Limited Warp Tickets:** Highly recommended to save your pieces for these.
*   **Rare Upgrade Materials:** Only purchase these if you are in immediate need of a breakthrough and have an excess of pieces.

### City Stamina Management

City Stamina is a separate resource from the combat stamina used for bosses. It regenerates over time and can be spent on "Lifestyle" activities.
*   **Delivery Missions:** Low effort, consistent Fons.
*   **Racing:** High Fons yield if you place in the top 3, but requires a tuned vehicle.
*   **Fishing:** A relaxing way to earn Fons and unique materials for Cafe recipes.

The conversion rate is generally 1 City Stamina to 1,000 Fons. Never let your City Stamina sit at its cap, as this is essentially wasting "passive" Fons generation.

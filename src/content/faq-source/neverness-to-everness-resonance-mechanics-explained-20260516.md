The depth of the Resonance system lies in how these six elements interact on the "Esper Wheel" and how the player manages the "Esper Meter" during high-speed combat.

### Mastering the Esper Wheel

The Esper Wheel is the UI element that dictates which elemental reactions are possible. The six elements—Lakshana, Cosmos, Anima, Incantation, Chaos, and Psyche—are arranged in a circular formation. Reactions generally occur between neighboring elements or specific "Trios."

To trigger a Resonance reaction, you must fill a character's **Esper Meter**. This gauge fills as the active character performs actions:
*   **Normal Attacks:** Provide a steady, low-level gain.
*   **Skills:** Offer a moderate burst of energy.
*   **Ultimates:** Often refund a portion of the meter upon completion.
*   **Parry Mechanic:** Successfully parrying an enemy attack provides an "Instant Fill," allowing for an immediate swap-in reaction.

When an off-field character's portrait glows, their Esper Meter is full. Swapping to them at this moment triggers a "Resonance Entrance," applying their element to the current field state and initiating a Duo or Trio reaction.

### Duo and Trio Reaction Mechanics

Reactions are the primary way to deal massive "Break" damage to bosses. Understanding the specific effects of each combination allows you to tailor your team for specific encounters.

#### Duo Reactions
Duo reactions occur when you swap between two compatible elements. Key reactions include:

*   **Blossom (Cosmos + Anima):** Creates a localized field that pulls enemies toward the center while dealing rapid multi-hit Anima damage. This is excellent for clearing waves of smaller enemies.
*   **Scorch (Incantation + Chaos):** Triggers a volatile explosion that applies a "Corrupted Burn" status. This deals high initial damage and continues to eat away at the enemy's Stability bar over time.
*   **Stain (Psyche + Lakshana):** Weakens enemy resistances and creates a "Resonance Link" between nearby foes, causing damage dealt to one to be shared among all "Stained" targets.

Trio reactions are more complex and require a specific sequence of three characters. These are categorized into "Charged" and "Discord" reactions based on the positioning of the elements on the Esper Wheel.

### The Awakening System: Unlocking A3 and A6 Resonance

While the Esper Cycle handles the "how" of combat, the Awakening system handles the "how hard." Every character can be Awakened from A1 to A6 using Mind Shards (obtained by pulling duplicates on banners like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)’s "The Ichi-daime").

While every level provides a stat increase, the A3 and A6 milestones are known as **Resonance Effects**. These are not mere numerical buffs; they often change how a character’s kit functions.

*   **A3 Resonance:** Usually enhances the character’s Esper Skill or their contribution to Duo reactions. For example, a character might gain the ability to trigger a reaction twice or ignore a portion of enemy defense during a Resonance Entrance.
*   **A6 Resonance:** The ultimate upgrade. This often introduces a new mechanic entirely, such as resetting cooldowns upon triggering a Trio reaction or allowing the character to remain on field during their Resonance Entrance for a "Dual-Attack" window.

For top-tier Espers like **Nanally**, **Baicang**, and **Lacrimosa**, reaching A3 is often considered the "meta" baseline for high-level endgame content, while A6 is reserved for those looking to maximize speed-clear potential.

### Cycle Rate and Rotation Strategy

A hidden but crucial stat in Neverness to Everness is **Cycle Rate**. This stat determines the "cooldown" of the Resonance system itself. Even if you have a full Esper Meter, you cannot swap into a Resonance Entrance if your Cycle Rate is currently on cooldown.

Optimizing your gear (Arcs) to include Cycle Rate bonuses allows for faster "Quick-Swapping." A high Cycle Rate team can chain four or five reactions in the time a standard team chains two.

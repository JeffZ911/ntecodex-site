Support Agents are the backbone of high-tier team compositions in Zenless Zone Zero, providing the essential buffs and energy utility required to clear high-difficulty Shiyu Defense stages. Selecting the right gear for these characters ensures your primary damage dealers can maintain maximum uptime on their burst windows.

# What is the Best W-Engine for Support in Zenless Zone Zero?

Choosing the best W-Engine for a Support Agent involves balancing team-wide stat increases with the individual Agent's energy requirements. Unlike Attack or Stun types, Support W-Engines focus on "Energy Generation Rate" (ERR) and "Passive Buffs" that trigger when the equipper is off-field or performing specific assist actions.

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/zzz-best-w-engine-for-support/inline-1.webp)


When looking for the top-performing W-Engines for Support Agents in ZZZ, players must evaluate how these specialized tools differ from other gear types. Support W-Engines are unique because their primary value lies in their secondary stats and passive effects rather than raw Base ATK.

### Defining the Support Role in ZZZ

Support Agents in Zenless Zone Zero are designed to enhance the performance of the rest of the squad. Their kits typically include abilities that provide Attribute DMG bonuses, ATK buffs, or Energy restoration. In combat rotations, a Support Agent usually enters the field to apply a specific buff via an EX Special Move or a Chain Attack before immediately swapping back to the primary DPS. Because they spend very little time on the field, their W-Engines must provide "off-field" utility or buffs with long durations that persist after the Agent is swapped out.

### Key Stats for Support W-Engines

The most critical stats for a Support W-Engine are:

*   **Energy Regen / Energy Generation Rate (ERR):** This allows the Support Agent to use their EX Special Move more frequently. Since most Support buffs are tied to EX Special Moves or Ultimates, high ERR is mandatory for consistent rotations.
*   **PEN Ratio:** This stat allows the team to ignore a portion of the enemy's DEF. While often seen on Attack units, some Support W-Engines (like Weeping Cradle) provide PEN Ratio to help the Support contribute meaningful chip damage and amplify the team's total output.
*   **Team-Wide ATK or DMG Buffs:** The passive effect is the heart of a Support W-Engine. Look for passives that trigger on "Chain Attacks," "Ultimates," or "EX Special Moves," as these are the most common ways Supports interact with the battlefield.

### Top Pick: Weeping Cradle (S-Rank)

Weeping Cradle remains the gold standard for high-end Support utility. It provides 24% PEN Ratio at Level 60, but its true power lies in the "Punishment" passive. This effect grants the equipper 0.6 Energy per second while off-field. Furthermore, when the equipper hits an enemy, the entire squad deals 10% more DMG to that target for 3 seconds, a value that ramps up the longer the effect lasts. This makes it the absolute best choice for Rina, whose dolls stay on the field to trigger the effect even after she swaps out.

### Best A-Rank Alternatives

For players who haven't pulled an S-Rank Support weapon, **Kaboom the Cannon** and **The Vault** are exceptional substitutes. Kaboom the Cannon is particularly versatile because it provides a massive 50% Energy Regen boost, making it easier to maintain buffs on Agents like Lucy. The Vault is the definitive choice for Nicole, as it triggers its team-wide 15% DMG buff specifically when dealing Ether DMG, which Nicole does with every ability in her kit.

### Best F2P Support W-Engine

The **[Reverb] Mark III** is the most accessible and effective budget option. While it is a B-Rank weapon, its passive "Booming Sound" provides a flat 8% to 12% ATK buff to the whole team after the equipper uses a Chain Attack or Ultimate. Because it is easy to obtain from Signal Search, most players can reach Refinement Phase 5 (P5) quickly, maximizing the buff potential without spending premium resources.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/zzz-best-w-engine-for-support/inline-3.webp)


Understanding why certain W-Engines outperform others requires a deep dive into the mechanics of energy management and buff uptime. In Zenless Zone Zero, the "Support Specialty" is unique because the W-Engine's passive effect only activates if the Agent's specialty matches the engine's type.

### S-Rank Analysis: Weeping Cradle

Weeping Cradle is Alexandrina Sebastiane's (Rina) signature weapon, and it is tailored to her specific playstyle. The PEN Ratio advanced stat is highly valuable in the endgame, where enemies have massive DEF pools.

*   **Ramping DMG:** The passive effect increases team DMG by 10% initially. However, every 0.5 seconds, this increases by another 1.7%, reaching a maximum additional bonus of 10.2% (totaling 20.2% DMG increase at Phase 1).
*   **Off-Field Energy:** Support Agents often struggle to gain energy because they aren't the ones hitting the enemy most of the time. The 0.6/s energy gain (at P1) ensures that Rina or Nicole always has their EX Special Move ready when the rotation comes back to them.

### A-Rank Tier List: The Vault, Kaboom the Cannon, and Bashful Demon

A-Rank W-Engines often provide more specialized bonuses than their S-Rank counterparts.

1.  **The Vault (Nicole's BiS):** This W-Engine is built for Ether teams. When Nicole hits an enemy with Ether DMG (which her EX Special black hole does multiple times per second), it increases team DMG by 15% and her own Energy Regen by 0.5/s. This allows Nicole to spam her DEF-shredding black holes almost indefinitely.
2.  **Kaboom the Cannon (Lucy's BiS):** Lucy relies on her Guard Boars to deal damage. When any friendly unit (including the boars) hits an enemy, Kaboom the Cannon provides a 2.5% ATK stack to the whole team, up to 4 stacks (10% total ATK). This is incredibly easy to maintain.
3.  **Bashful Demon (Soukaku's BiS):** This W-Engine increases Ice DMG by 15%. When Soukaku uses her EX Special, the team gains a 2% ATK buff per stack, up to 4 stacks. For an Ice-heavy team featuring Ellen Joe, this engine is mandatory for maximizing the "Freeze" and "Shatter" damage windows.

### Utility Options: Slice of Time and Unfettered Game Ball

These two A-Rank options provide utility that doesn't fit into a specific elemental niche:

*   **Slice of Time:** This engine is focused on Decibel generation. It allows the team to reach their Ultimate faster. It is a niche pick for teams that rely on heavy Ultimate usage rather than sustained Basic Attack damage.
*   **Unfettered Game Ball:** This Battle Pass (New Eridu City Fund) exclusive weapon provides a team-wide CRIT Rate buff of 12% to 20% whenever the equipper triggers an Attribute Counter. It is highly effective in teams that frequently exploit elemental weaknesses.

### B-Rank and F2P: The [Reverb] Series

The [Reverb] series consists of three marks, but **Mark III** is the standout for Support.

*   **[Reverb] Mark I:** Focuses on increasing team Impact. This is generally less useful for Support Agents and is better suited for Stun-adjacent builds if you lack a proper Stun engine.
*   **[Reverb] Mark II:** Increases team Anomaly Mastery and Proficiency. This is a solid niche pick for Anomaly-heavy teams (e.g., Grace and Piper compositions).
*   **[Reverb] Mark III:** The ATK buff provided on Chain Attacks is universally useful. At P5, the 12% ATK increase is comparable to some A-Rank weapons at P1.

### How to Choose: Energy Regen vs. Team Buffs

When deciding between a W-Engine with high Energy Regen (like Kaboom) and one with a stronger team buff (like Bashful Demon), consider your rotation. If your Support Agent is always waiting for energy to use their EX Special, prioritize **Energy Regen**. If your Support Agent already has their energy ready but your Main DPS is struggling to kill enemies within the stun window, prioritize **Team DMG or ATK buffs**.

### How to Obtain and Upgrade Support W-Engines

Support W-Engines can be obtained through several methods:

1.  **Signal Search:** Standard and Limited banners are the primary sources for S-Rank and A-Rank engines.
2.  **Box Galaxy (Gadget Store):** You can craft specific A-Rank W-Engines using W-Engine Chips and specialized components.
3.  **New Eridu City Fund:** The Battle Pass offers the Unfettered Game Ball at Level 30.
4.  **Leveling:** To upgrade these engines to Level 60, you will need **Support Components**, **Reinforced Support Components**, and **Specialized Support Components**, which are farmed in the Combat Simulation center.

### Top Support Agents in ZZZ

As of Version 2.7, the Support meta is dominated by **Sunna** and **Ukinami Yuzuha**, who offer unprecedented utility for Physical and undisclosed teams respectively. However, the 1.0 roster remains essential for F2P players:

*   **Nicole:** Essential for Ether teams and grouping enemies.
*   **Soukaku:** The premier buffer for Ice teams.
*   **Lucy:** A flexible ATK buffer who fits into almost any team.

### W-Engine Leveling Guide

Upgrading a W-Engine from Level 1 to 60 requires a significant amount of Denny and materials. For Support W-Engines, the most important milestones are Level 40 and Level 50. At Level 50, most A-Rank engines provide enough of their advanced stat (like 40%+ ERR) to function perfectly in endgame content. Pushing to Level 60 is recommended only for S-Rank engines like Weeping Cradle to maximize the Base ATK scaling.

### Recommended Team Compositions

Support W-Engines shine best when paired with the correct Drive Discs. For most Support Agents, pairing their W-Engine with a 4-piece set of **Swing Jazz** is optimal. This set provides 20% Energy Regen and increases the team's DMG by 15% for 12 seconds after the equipper launches a Chain Attack or Ultimate, stacking effectively with the buffs provided by weapons like The Vault or Kaboom the Cannon.

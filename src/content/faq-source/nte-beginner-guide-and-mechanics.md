## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-beginner-guide-and-mechanics/inline-2.webp)


Neverness to Everness is a supernatural urban open-world RPG where players act as "Appraisers" tasked with containing anomalies—supernatural phenomena that manifest in the city of Hesperia. Unlike traditional fantasy RPGs, NTE splits your progression into two distinct paths: **Hunter Level**, which tracks your combat prowess and story progress, and **Tycoon Level**, which tracks your status in the city’s economy.

The core combat revolves around the **Esper Cycle**. You field a team of four characters, each belonging to one of six elemental types: Cosmos, Anima, Psyche, Incantation, Lakshana, or Chaos. By attacking and parrying, you build an Esper Meter that allows for powerful character swaps and reactions.

The gacha system, titled **"The Fair,"** uses a unique board-game visualizer. Most notably, NTE does not feature a "50/50" mechanic on its limited banners. Players are guaranteed the featured S-Rank character at 90 pulls, with a "Board Modification" system that increases luck starting at 70 pulls. During your first week, focus on completing the "Appraiser’s Handbook" tasks to unlock free characters like **Mint** and **Aurelia**, and begin investing in your first business to generate passive income (Fons).

### Combat Fundamentals: Esper Cycle and Parrying

Combat in NTE is fast-paced and emphasizes momentum. Each character has a unique kit including a Basic Attack, an E-Skill (Anomaly Talent), and a Q-Ultimate (Burst). However, the most important element is the **Esper Meter** located next to each character's portrait.

As you deal damage or perform **Critical Ripostes** (perfect dodges or parries against red-flashing attacks), the Esper Meter fills. When full, switching to that character triggers an **Esper Entry**. If the previous character applied a specific element, the entry triggers a reaction.

The **Break System** is the second pillar of combat. Elite enemies and bosses have a white bar beneath their health. Once depleted, the enemy enters a "Fractured" state, taking significantly increased damage and becoming immobilized. To shred this bar quickly, focus on "Critical Ripostes." When an enemy flashes red, timing your dodge perfectly will trigger a slow-motion window where your next few hits deal massive [Break damage](https://ntecodex.com/guides/hsr-3-0-super-break-damage-calculation-gear-guide).

### The Tycoon System: Making Fons and Managing Businesses

Progression in Hesperia isn't just about killing monsters; it's about owning the city. Your **Tycoon Level** determines which districts you can buy property in and which high-end vehicles you can purchase. 

*   **Fons:** This is the primary currency used for everything from upgrading Arcs to buying furniture for your apartments.
*   **Business Management:** Early in the story, you will unlock the ability to manage a cafe. By assigning characters with high "Charisma" or "Management" stats to these businesses, you generate Fons passively.
*   **Pink Paws Heist:** This is a weekly activity that consumes **City Stamina**. It involves a tactical stealth mission where you recover stolen goods. Completing this is the fastest way to earn Tycoon XP and rare vehicle customization parts.

### Character Building: Arcs, Artifacts, and the Tetris Grid

Character power is derived from two main sources: **Arcs** (weapons) and **Artifacts** (gear).

**Arcs** provide a base attack stat and a unique passive. For example, [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)’s signature Arc, which is featured in "The Ichi-daime" banner, provides significant vertical mobility and aerial damage bonuses. Arcs can be leveled up to 90 using materials farmed with **Pixels**.

**Artifacts** use a highly unique "Tetris-like" grid system. Instead of simply equipping five items, you are given a 5x5 or 6x6 grid (depending on character level). Each Artifact is a geometric shape (blocks, L-shapes, or crescents). 
*   **Sonata Effects:** To activate set bonuses, you must fit specific shapes into the grid without overlapping.
*   **Optimization:** Later in the game, you will find "Golden Crescents" which act as wildcards, helping you complete complex Sonata sets that provide massive boosts to elemental damage or Esper Meter gain.

### The Fair: Understanding the Dice-Based Gacha System

The gacha system in NTE, called **The Fair**, is presented as a holographic board game. When you spend "Anomaly Tickets," a die is rolled, and your piece moves across the board, picking up small rewards (upgrade materials) before landing on a pull result.

*   **No 50/50:** If you pull an S-Rank on a limited-time character banner, it is guaranteed to be the featured character.
*   **Pity Milestones:**
    *   **70 Pulls:** "Board Modification" activates. The board changes visually, and the probability of landing on an S-Rank space increases significantly.
    *   **90 Pulls:** "Hard Pity." You are guaranteed to land on the featured S-Rank character space.
*   **Standard Selector:** After 40 pulls on the beginner banner, players can choose one S-Rank from a select pool, which includes **[Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide)** and **Daffodil**.

### Exploration Tips: Anomalies, Vehicles, and Hesperia Districts

Hesperia is divided into several districts, ranging from the neon-soaked Central Plaza to the industrial outskirts of the North Dock. 

1.  **Vehicle Customization:** Driving is a core mechanic, not just for travel but for racing. You can customize engines, tires, and paint at the "Hesperia Customs" shop. High-tier cars are required to win the "Midnight Circuit" races, which reward exclusive Artifacts.
2.  **The Prison System:** Hesperia has a functional law enforcement system. Committing crimes—such as attacking NPCs, stealing cars in view of drones, or causing excessive property damage—will raise your "Anomaly Heat." If caught, you may be sent to the "Bureau Detention Center," resulting in a temporary loss of Tycoon buffs.
3.  **Verticality with Nanally:** If you are lucky enough to pull Nanally, use her for exploration. Her ability to grapple and wall-run makes finding hidden "Anomaly Shards" on rooftops much easier than using standard parkour.

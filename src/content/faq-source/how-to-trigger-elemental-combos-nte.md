### Defining Elemental Combos in NTE

Elemental combos in Neverness to Everness are not triggered by simply applying two elements to an enemy simultaneously, as seen in other urban open-world RPGs. Instead, combos are part of a structured sequence called a "Cycle." When a character uses their abilities, they accumulate energy in a specific meter. Once full, swapping to a compatible teammate triggers a burst of elemental energy and a unique status effect or damage instance. These interactions are categorized by the number of elements involved in the sequence, ranging from simple Duo reactions to high-impact Triple reactions.

### The Role of the Esper Cycle

The Esper Cycle is the engine that drives NTE’s combat. It serves as a cooldown and resource management system that prevents players from spamming swaps mindlessly. Every character belongs to one of six elemental affinities, and these affinities are arranged on a circular "Esper Wheel." The Cycle dictates that combos can only occur between elements that are physically adjacent on this wheel. Activating the Cycle requires filling the Esper Meter of the active character and then executing a "Switch Entry" to a character whose element follows or precedes the current one in the wheel's logic.

Elemental combos are triggered through the Esper Cycle system by filling a character's Esper Meter to 100% and swapping to a teammate when their portrait glows on the right side of the HUD. This mechanic relies entirely on the "Adjacency Rule," meaning combos only occur between elements that sit next to each other on the Esper Wheel: Lakshana, Cosmos, Anima, Incantation, Chaos, and Psyche.

To trigger a Duo Cycle reaction, you must swap from an active character with a full meter to a teammate of an adjacent element. To trigger a Triple Cycle reaction (either Charge or Discord), you must perform two consecutive Duo reactions within the same elemental Trio (either the "Top Trio" or the "Bottom Trio") without breaking the chain or swapping to a non-adjacent element.

### The Quick-Start Guide to Combos

1.  **Build Energy:** Use the active character's Normal Attacks, Skills, or Ultimates to fill the circular Esper Meter located next to their portrait.
2.  **Observe the Glow:** When the meter is full, the portraits of your off-field characters will begin to glow and pulse if they are eligible for a Cycle reaction.
3.  **Execute the Swap:** Press the corresponding character key (or tap the portrait) to bring the new character onto the field.
4.  **Confirm the Reaction:** A text prompt (e.g., "Scorch" or "Blossom") will appear on the screen, indicating the Duo reaction was successful.

The Esper Meter is the circular gauge that surrounds the character's element icon in the HUD. Unlike Ultimate energy, which is used to cast a character's most powerful skill, Esper energy is used exclusively for triggering Cycles. The speed at which this meter fills is governed by the "Cycle Rate" stat.

A character with a high Cycle Rate can trigger reactions more frequently, making them excellent "Enablers" or "Drivers" in a team composition. You can increase this stat through specific Arcs (weapons) and gear sets.

### The Power of Triple Cycles: Charge vs. Discord

Triple Cycles are the pinnacle of the Esper system. These are triggered by completing a three-part chain within a specific "Trio" on the wheel. You must swap from Element A to Element B, and then from Element B to Element C.

1.  **The Top Trio (Lakshana, Cosmos, Anima):** Completing a chain within these three elements triggers the **Charge** reaction. Charge provides a massive boost to the entire team's Ultimate energy and increases the Cycle Rate of all members for a short duration.
2.  **The Bottom Trio (Incantation, Chaos, Psyche):** Completing a chain within these three elements triggers the **Discord** reaction. Discord is specifically designed for boss encounters, as it deals immense "Break" damage to the enemy's Stagger meter, forcing them into a vulnerable state much faster than standard attacks.

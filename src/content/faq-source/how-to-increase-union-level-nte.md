# How to Increase Union Level in Neverness to Everness (NTE)
> 📝 **Editorial Note:** This guide is AI-assisted and game data evolves rapidly. Please cross-reference with in-game information. _Updated: 2026-05-21._


In Neverness to Everness, your progression is measured by your Hunter Level, a metric that many players initially refer to as the Union Level. This account-wide rank serves as the primary gatekeeper for story progression, character power, and world difficulty. Increasing your Hunter Level is mandatory if you want to access high-level Anomaly Zones, upgrade your Arcs, or reach the current level cap of 60.

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/how-to-increase-union-level-nte/inline-1.webp)


While the community often uses the term "Union Level" due to its prevalence in other gacha RPGs, the official terminology in Neverness to Everness (NTE) is the Hunter Level. This level represents your overall account progress and is the most important number on your profile.

The Hunter Level is the primary engine for account progression because it dictates when you can unlock new Episodes (main story chapters), Spinoffs (side quests), and essential gameplay features like the City Tycoon system. Without a high enough Hunter Level, you will find your characters—known as Espers—hitting "soft caps" where they can no longer be leveled up despite having the necessary materials.

A common point of confusion for new players is the difference between Hunter Level and Appraisal Level.
*   **Hunter Level:** Your total account experience (EXP) accumulated through all activities.
*   **Appraisal Level:** Your "World Level." This determines the strength of enemies in Hethereau City and the quality of loot dropped by bosses and Anomaly Zones.

Your Appraisal Level is directly tied to your Hunter Level. Once you reach certain Hunter Level milestones, your Appraisal Level will increase, either automatically or after completing a specific trial.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-increase-union-level-nte/inline-2.webp)


The most efficient way to increase your Hunter Level in Neverness to Everness is by completing the daily Participation Tasks and spending your Character Pixels (stamina) every day. While the Main Story Episodes provide the largest one-time infusions of EXP, the daily routine provides the consistent growth needed to push through the mid-game plateaus.

To maximize your leveling speed, you should prioritize the following:
1.  **Main Story Quests:** Complete every available Episode as soon as you meet the level requirement.
2.  **Participation Tasks:** Earn 1,000 Hunter EXP daily by reaching 100 Participation Points.
3.  **Character Pixels:** Spend all 240 daily Pixels in Anomaly Zones (1 Pixel = 5 Hunter EXP).
4.  **City Tycoon:** Level up your businesses to gain substantial one-time EXP rewards.
5.  **Exploration:** Unlock Wertheimer Towers and ReroRero Phone Booths across the city.

You must also clear Appraisal Level Ascension Quests at specific milestones to continue your progression. If you reach these levels and do not complete the quest, your Hunter Level will stop increasing, and any additional EXP earned will be significantly reduced.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-increase-union-level-nte/inline-3.webp)


The leveling process in Hethereau City is a marathon that rewards daily consistency over short bursts of grinding. Because the game uses a stamina-gated system for combat rewards, you cannot simply farm enemies in the open world to level up your account quickly.

### Understanding Hunter Level vs. Appraisal Level

The relationship between these two systems is the most critical mechanic to master. Your Hunter Level is the "requirement," while the Appraisal Level is the "result." As your Hunter Level climbs, you unlock the ability to raise your Appraisal Level. 

Higher Appraisal Levels make the game more difficult by increasing enemy levels, but they are necessary for character growth. For example, you cannot level [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or any other Esper past Level 40 until you reach Appraisal Level 3. If you find the world difficulty too high after an increase, the game allows you to manually lower your Appraisal Level by one stage (once you reach Appraisal Level 3) via the main menu, though this has a 24-hour cooldown.

### The Daily Routine: Circle Bounty and Participation Tasks

The Circle Bounty is the seasonal Battle Pass in NTE, but it is closely linked to your daily Participation Tasks. Every day, you can earn up to 1,000 Hunter EXP by completing simple objectives found in the Exploration Guide. These tasks refresh daily and should be your first priority upon logging in.

**Daily Participation Task EXP Breakdown**

| Task Category | Activity | Hunter EXP Reward |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Daily Login | Simply log into the game | 200 EXP |
| Fons Collection | Earn 10,000 Fons (currency) | 200 EXP |
| Pixel Consumption | Spend 120 Character Pixels | 300 EXP |
| Activity Points | Reach 100 Participation Points | 300 EXP |
| **Total Daily Potential** | — | **1,000 EXP** |

Reaching 100 Participation Points also rewards you with Annulith (the premium currency used for banners like "The Ichi-daime") and Beetle Coins, which are essential for Esper development.

### Maximizing Character Pixels (Combat Stamina)

Character Pixels are the primary stamina resource for combat activities. You have a maximum capacity of 240 Pixels, and they regenerate at a rate of 1 Pixel every 6 minutes. This means a full recovery takes exactly 24 hours.

Every time you spend Pixels in an Anomaly Zone, you receive Hunter EXP at a fixed ratio of 1:5. If you spend 40 Pixels on a standard run, you gain 200 Hunter EXP. By spending your full daily allotment of 240 Pixels, you contribute 1,200 EXP toward your Hunter Level.

**Character Pixel Recovery Rates and Daily Caps**

*   **Maximum Capacity:** 240 Pixels
*   **Regeneration Rate:** 1 Pixel per 6 minutes (10 per hour)
*   **Daily Regeneration:** 240 Pixels per 24 hours
*   **Manual Refill:** De-noise Solutions (restores 60 Pixels)
*   **Annulith Refill:** 60 Annulith for 60 Pixels (up to 6 times daily)

To be efficient, never let your Pixels sit at the 240 cap. If you are short on time, use the "Double Reward" option in Anomaly Zones to spend 80 Pixels in a single run for twice the loot and 400 Hunter EXP.

### City Tycoon and Weekly City Stamina Efficiency

Unlike Character Pixels, City Stamina is used for urban activities like racing, shop management, and heists. This system is tied to your Tycoon Level. While City Stamina resets weekly rather than daily, increasing your Tycoon Level provides massive one-time Hunter EXP boosts.

Progression in the City Tycoon system is vital because it eventually unlocks the S-Rank character Chiz for free. Reaching Tycoon Level 18 rewards you with Chiz, while higher levels provide her best-in-slot weapon and duplicates. The EXP gained from reaching these milestones is often enough to jump multiple Hunter Levels in the early game.

### Main Story and Side Quest Prioritization

Episodes (Main Quests) are the fastest way to gain large chunks of EXP. A single Episode mission can reward between 500 and 2,000 Hunter EXP. These should be completed immediately as they unlock.

Once you hit a level gate for the main story, turn your attention to:
*   **Spinoffs:** These are major side stories with voice acting that offer high EXP.
*   **Side Quests:** Marked with blue exclamation points on the map, these are shorter but provide supplemental EXP, Fons, and Annulith.
*   **Anomaly Commissions:** These are combat-focused quests found on the map that reward Hunter EXP and help unlock Anomaly Hunts.

### Exploration, Anomalies, and World Discovery

Hethereau City is filled with hidden EXP sources that do not require stamina. Exploring the city and interacting with its infrastructure is a valid way to push through the final few points needed for a level up.

*   **Wertheimer Towers:** Activating these towers reveals the map and provides a permanent EXP boost.
*   **ReroRero Phone Booths:** These serve as fast travel points. Each one unlocked grants a small amount of Hunter EXP.
*   **Gifts from 21:** There are various collectibles scattered by the character "21" throughout the city. Finding these rewards you with Annulith and account EXP.
*   **Anomalies:** Small open-world puzzles or combat encounters (often called Hethereau Anomalies) provide one-time EXP rewards.

### Appraisal Level Ascension Quests and Level Caps

When you reach Hunter Levels 20, 40, and 50, your EXP bar will stop moving. This indicates that you have reached a "Limit Milestone." You must navigate to your Quest menu and track the Appraisal Level Ascension Quest.

These quests usually involve a combat trial against a specific boss or a series of waves in a specialized arena. Once cleared, your Appraisal Level will rise, your Hunter Level cap will increase by 10 or 20 levels, and your Espers' maximum level caps will expand. Failing to do these quests promptly is the biggest mistake a new player can make, as it wastes potential EXP from daily tasks.

**Key Hunter Level Rewards**

As you level up, visit the Hunter Rewards menu to claim:
*   **Annulith:** The primary currency for summoning new characters.
*   **Hunter Guides:** Consumables used to level up your Espers.
*   **Beetle Coins:** The standard currency for character and item upgrades.
*   **Solid Dice:** Pull currency for limited banners like "The Ichi-daime."
*   **Fabricated Dice:** Pull currency for the standard banner.

## Related

![Related](/img/how-to-increase-union-level-nte/inline-4.webp)


Increasing your Hunter Level is just the beginning of account optimization. As your level rises, you will need to manage the increased power of your team to keep up with the scaling world difficulty.

### How to Level Up Espers and Arcs

Your Hunter Level dictates the maximum level of your Espers and their Arcs (weapons). To keep your team competitive:
1.  **Ascension:** Once an Esper hits a level cap (e.g., Level 20), use materials gathered from Anomaly Zones to "Ascend" them. This requires a specific Appraisal Level.
2.  **Arc Upgrades:** Use materials from the Bubble Can Factory to upgrade your Arcs. Arcs also have ascension milestones that mirror the Esper system.
3.  **Ability Enhancement:** Spend Beetle Coins and specific materials to level up Esper skills.

### NTE City Tycoon Progression Guide

The City Tycoon system is a secondary progression path that focuses on the life-sim aspects of NTE. By managing the cafe, completing deliveries, and participating in high-speed races, you earn City Tycoon EXP. This system is the only way to obtain Chiz, an S-Rank DPS character who is essential for F2P (Free-to-Play) players. Efficiently spending your weekly City Stamina ensures you reach Tycoon Level 18 as quickly as possible.

### Best Characters to Invest in Early

In the early Hunter Levels, resources are scarce. It is better to focus on a core team of four rather than spreading your materials thin.
*   **Nanally:** If you pulled on "The Ichi-daime" banner, she is your primary DPS and should receive the most investment.
*   **Chiz:** Once unlocked via City Tycoon, she becomes a powerhouse for combat content.
*   **Esper Zero:** Your main character is surprisingly versatile and cheaper to ascend than some S-Rank pulls.
*   **Jiuyuan:** An excellent support character who pairs well with Nanally for high-difficulty Anomaly Hunts.

By focusing on these characters and maintaining a strict daily routine of Participation Tasks and Pixel spending, you will reach Hunter Level 30 and unlock the full cartridge farming loop in no time.

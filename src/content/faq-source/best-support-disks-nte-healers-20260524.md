In Neverness to Everness (NTE), the difference between a successful clear and a team wipe often comes down to the Support Disks (Arcs) and Cartridge sets equipped to your survival-class Espers.

# What Are the Best Support Disks for Healers in Neverness to Everness?

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/best-support-disks-nte-healers-20260524/inline-1.webp)


As of the Version 1.0 launch on April 29, 2026, players are focused on identifying the top-tier "Support Disks"—officially known in-game as Arcs—and the corresponding gear sets to maximize the efficiency of survival-class Espers. Unlike traditional RPGs where a single "weapon" defines a character's power, NTE utilizes a multi-layered equipment system that demands a synergy between your chosen Arc, your Cartridge set, and the Tetris-like Module layout on your character’s Console.

To build an effective healer, you must first distinguish between the two primary gear types. Arcs serve as your "weapon disks," providing massive base stat blocks and a unique passive ability that often triggers during specific skill rotations. Cartridges, on the other hand, are "gear cassettes" that provide 2-piece and 4-piece set bonuses. These are further augmented by Modules, which are the physical blocks you slot into the Console grid to activate those Cartridge bonuses and provide additional substat scaling.

Determining which equipment maximizes healing output involves balancing three core metrics: raw HP recovery, energy regeneration (to ensure Ultimate uptime), and team-wide offensive buffs. In the current 1.0 meta, healers like Edgar and Fadia are the cornerstones of survival, but their gear requirements differ significantly based on their elemental compatibility and skill mechanics.

### Understanding NTE Gear: Arcs vs. Cartridges

The NTE gearing system is divided into two distinct categories that must work in tandem:

*   **Arcs (Support Disks):** These are your primary stat sticks. Each Esper is compatible with one of five Arc types: Gas, Liquid, Plasma, Solid, or Synthesis. For example, Edgar is a Liquid-type Esper, meaning he can only equip Liquid Arcs. These provide a "Main Stat" (usually ATK or HP) and an "Arc Effect" passive.
*   **Cartridges (Gear Sets):** These provide the "Set Bonuses" (Epic at 2 pieces and Legendary at 4 pieces). Unlike Arcs, Cartridges are not restricted by element, but their efficiency is dictated by the Console's Module layout. You must fit specific Module shapes (Type II, III, or IV) into the Console grid to "power" the Cartridge.

### Who are the Primary Healers in Version 1.0?

As of May 24, 2026, the healer roster is specialized:

1.  **Edgar (Cosmos / Liquid):** The definitive "pure healer." His kit revolves around *Wild Current* (a Redirect Skill) and his Ultimate, *Finnegan's Wake*, which creates a massive healing domain. He scales primarily with Max HP.
2.  **Fadia (Psyche / Synthesis):** A survival-class support who focuses on damage redirection. While she can heal via her Ultimate, her primary role is absorbing 50% of the damage intended for her teammates. She is a "tank-healer" hybrid who requires massive HP pools to function.
3.  **Jiuyuan (Anima / Solid):** Primarily a sub-DPS, but becomes a potent off-healer once you unlock her A1 Awakening. Her healing is tied to her *Final Reckoning* Ultimate, which restores team HP based on the damage accumulated by her *Lethal Rose Pact*.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/best-support-disks-nte-healers-20260524/inline-2.webp)


The best Support Disks (Arcs) for pure healers in NTE are **"Call of the Twisted City"** for maximizing Healing Bonus and **"Mind Royale"** for Energy Regeneration. Both are A-Rank Liquid Arcs, making them the best-in-slot options for Edgar. For Fadia, who uses Synthesis Arcs, the S-Rank **"Eternal Waltz"** is the undisputed top choice due to its massive +41.25% HP substat.

For gear sets, the definitive Cartridge for maximizing recovery is **"Thea’s Night Tavern."** Its 4-piece Legendary bonus provides a flat +20% Healing Bonus, which is superior to raw HP stacking for pure recovery throughput. However, if your team is already surviving well, the meta choice shifts to the **"Speedy Hedgehog"** set, which grants a +15% team-wide ATK buff for 20 seconds after the wearer uses an Ultimate.

Healer-specific stat priority should focus on **Healing Bonus %** for the Cartridge Main Stat and **Max HP %** for all Module substats. Because most healers in Version 1.0 scale their recovery based on their total health pool, increasing your Max HP not only makes your healer unkillable but also directly increases the amount of health restored to the team.

### The 'Best in Slot' Loadout for Edgar
For Edgar, the goal is 100% uptime on his Ultimate domain. The optimal build uses **Call of the Twisted City (A5)** paired with a 4-piece **Thea’s Night Tavern** set. This combination ensures that every time Edgar uses his *Wild Current* skill to channel healing, he triggers the Arc’s +20% healing bonus, which then stacks with the Cartridge’s +20% bonus for a massive 40% increase in recovery efficiency.

### The 'Best in Slot' Loadout for Fadia
Fadia requires a different approach since her "healing" is often a byproduct of her survival. Her best-in-slot loadout is the S-Rank **Eternal Waltz** Arc with a 4-piece **Tiny Big Adventure** Cartridge set. This build focuses entirely on Max HP stacking. By reaching 10 stacks of the Cartridge bonus, Fadia can increase her total health pool by an additional 40%, allowing her to absorb significantly more damage for the team before her HP redirection shuts off at 1 HP.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/best-support-disks-nte-healers-20260524/inline-3.webp)


The depth of the NTE equipment system lies in the interaction between skill types and gear triggers. A "Redirect Skill" is a specific classification of ability used by healers like Edgar (*Wild Current*) and Fadia (*Existence*). Understanding how these skills interact with your Support Disks is the key to high-level play.

### Arc Breakdown: Call of the Twisted City vs. Mind Royale
While both are top-tier Liquid Arcs, they serve different roles in the "Esper Cycle." 

**Call of the Twisted City** is the "Panic Button" Arc. It scales from a 12% Healing Bonus at Rank 1 to a 20% bonus at Rank 5. Because Edgar’s *Wild Current* is a Redirect Skill that can be held or toggled, he can maintain almost 100% uptime on this buff. This is the preferred choice for high-pressure Anomaly Hunts where the boss deals constant AoE damage.

**Mind Royale**, conversely, is a "Battery" Arc. It provides up to 18 Ultimate Energy every time a Redirect Skill is used. In NTE, Ultimate Energy is the most restricted resource. By using Mind Royale, Edgar can bypass the need for high "Charge Efficiency" stats on his Cartridges, allowing him to focus entirely on HP and Healing Bonus. If your team rotation feels sluggish and you are struggling to get your Ultimate back before the boss’s next big phase, Mind Royale is the superior choice.

### Cartridge Deep Dive: Thea’s Night Tavern Mechanics
Many players wonder why **Thea’s Night Tavern** is often preferred over **Tiny Big Adventure** for pure healers. The answer lies in the math of "Healing Output Scaling." 

In Version 1.0, healing is calculated as:  
`[Base Healing value (scales with HP)] x [1 + Healing Bonus %]`

Because healers like Edgar already have high base HP from their levels and Console bonuses, adding more HP (via Tiny Big Adventure) results in diminishing returns. However, "Healing Bonus %" is a separate multiplier that is much rarer to find. A 20% Healing Bonus from Thea’s Night Tavern provides a significantly larger boost to the final HP restored than a comparable 20% increase in Max HP would.

### The Console System: Maximizing Module Type Bonuses
The Console is where the "Tetris" mechanic comes into play. Every Esper has a preferred Module Type (II, III, or IV). Using the correct type unlocks a hidden "Console Bonus" that can drastically change a build.

*   **Edgar’s Console Bonus:** For every **Type III Module** (3-block pieces) slotted into his grid, Edgar receives a **+10% Max HP bonus**. This is why the meta for Edgar involves using Type III Modules almost exclusively. If you fill his Console with five Type III pieces, you gain a massive 50% Max HP boost before even considering the Cartridge stats.
*   **Fadia’s Console Bonus:** Fadia benefits from **Type II Modules** (2-block pieces), receiving a **+6% Max HP bonus** per piece. Because Type II modules are smaller, she can fit more of them on her grid, often reaching up to 12 pieces for a 72% HP increase.

### Fadia: Building the Ultimate Damage-Redirection Support
Fadia's role is unique because she doesn't just heal; she prevents damage. Her passive, *All Eyes on Me*, redirects 50% of the damage taken by the active ally to her. To optimize this, you must build her as a "Sponge."

The **Tiny Big Adventure** set is designed specifically for this. The 4-piece bonus increases Max HP by 4% every time the wearer takes damage. Since Fadia is *always* taking damage via redirection, she hits the 10-stack cap (+40% Max HP) within seconds of starting a fight. When combined with her S-Rank signature Arc, **Eternal Waltz**, Fadia can easily surpass 100,000 Max HP, making the entire team virtually indestructible.

### Jiuyuan: Balancing Damage and Healing in Hybrid Builds
Jiuyuan is an Anima-element Solid Esper who is often played as a sub-DPS. However, her healing potential is unlocked through her A1 Awakening, which restores 5% of the damage dealt by her *Lethal Rose Pact* to the whole team. 

Because her healing scales with her damage, you should **not** use healer sets like Thea’s Night Tavern on her. Instead, use **Fireflies and the Forest**. This set provides:
*   **2-pc:** Anima DMG +10%.
*   **4-pc:** +8% CRIT DMG every time an enemy takes Anima DMG (up to 56% total).

By maximizing Jiuyuan’s CRIT DMG, you increase her Pact damage, which in turn increases the amount of HP she restores to the team. She is the premier choice for "No-Healer" speedrun compositions where damage is the best form of survival.

### Best Healer Team Compositions
Healers in NTE are most effective when they contribute to the "Esper Cycle" (the elemental reaction system).

*   **The Blossom Nuke Team:** **[Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) (Main DPS) + Zero (Support) + Edgar (Healer) + Jiuyuan (Sub-DPS)**. This team focuses on the Anima + Cosmos reaction (Blossom), which generates Genesis Buds that explode for massive AoE damage. Edgar provides the necessary sustain while Jiuyuan groups enemies.
*   **The Charge Cycle Team:** **Daffodill (Main DPS) + Mint (Sub-DPS) + Edgar (Healer) + Adler (Shield)**. This team uses the Liquid and Cosmos elements to accelerate Ultimate energy gain, ensuring that Edgar’s domain and Daffodill’s burst are always available.

### Where to Farm Healer Gear: The Rabbit Hole Guide
To obtain S-Rank Cartridges like Thea’s Night Tavern or Speedy Hedgehog, you must clear the **Rabbit Hole** Anomaly Zones.
*   **Location:** Cape Square, New Herland District (south of the Regalia Dealership).
*   **Cost:** 40 Character Pixels per run.
*   **Strategy:** The Rabbit Hole uses a wave-based combat system. Bringing a character with high "Break Intensity" (like [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide) or [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte)) is recommended to shatter enemy shields quickly, as Cartridge drops are improved by the speed of the clear.

### Survival Alternatives: Shields and Food
If you haven't yet farmed a perfect set of Support Disks, you can supplement your team's survival with external methods:
*   **Shields:** Using **Adler** with the **Kingdom’s Guard** Cartridge provides a 20% shield strength bonus, which can often replace the need for a dedicated healer in shorter fights.
*   **Out-of-Combat Recovery:** NTE features several free healing locations. Interacting with **Wertheimer Towers** or **ReroRero Phone Booths** scattered around Hethereau will instantly restore your party to full HP. For a more immersive recovery, you can use the bed in the **Eibon Antique Shop** to rest and heal.
*   **Food:** Stocking up on "Anomaly Jerky" from the convenience stores in the North District provides a steady HP-over-time buff that can help during the early-game grind.

### Defining Energy in Hethereau

In the world of Hethereau, "Energy" is not a monolithic resource. Instead, it is a localized power source derived from a character's Esper type. While some games use a single bar for all powerful moves, NTE splits this into individual character Ultimates and a shared tactical resource used for swapping and triggering reactions. Ultimate Energy is what fuels your most powerful cinematic attacks (often mapped to the Q key or a dedicated button on the UI), while the Esper Cycle Meter dictates the tempo of your rotations.

### The Two-Meter System

The NTE combat UI features two primary indicators for energy. The first is the Ultimate icon, located in the bottom right of the screen. This icon fills with a glowing color corresponding to the character's element (Lakshana, Cosmos, Anima, Incantation, Chaos, or Psyche) as they gain energy. When it reaches 100%, the icon pulses, indicating the Ultimate is ready for activation.

The second indicator is the circular Esper Meter found next to each character's portrait in the party list. This meter is divided into 100 units and is the primary gateway for switching characters. Unlike the Ultimate Energy, which is character-specific, the Esper Meter is a shared tactical resource that determines when you can execute a "Switch Skill." Understanding that these two bars fill via different actions—though they often overlap—is the first step toward optimizing your combat efficiency.

The depth of NTE's energy system becomes apparent when you look at the interaction between the Esper Cycle Wheel and individual character kits. Energy isn't just a byproduct of fighting; it is a resource you actively "mine" from the enemy through precise timing and elemental synergy.

### Mastering the Esper Cycle Wheel

The Esper Cycle Meter is the engine of your team. Every character swap requires a certain amount of meter, and "Switch Skills" (the attacks performed when a character enters the field) are the primary way to initiate duo and trio reactions. 

The **Cycle Rate Stat** is the most important modifier here. This stat acts as a multiplier for all meter-building actions. For example, the protagonist, Zero (MC), has a base Cycle Rate of 100 on their Redirect Skill. This high value is designed to ensure that Zero can almost always trigger a swap immediately after using their skill, making them an ideal "bridge" character in any rotation. If you equip Zero with Arcs that further boost Cycle Rate, you can effectively ignore the cooldowns that usually limit character switching.

### The 'Charge' Reaction: Your Team's Battery

While individual actions generate energy, the "Charge" Trio Reaction is the gold standard for team-wide battery builds. This reaction is a two-step process
1.  **Blossom:** Formed by combining Cosmos and Anima. This creates a "Blossom turret" on the field that fires automated projectiles at nearby enemies.
2.  **Remora:** Formed by combining Lakshana and Cosmos. This adds a "tracking" effect and multi-hit properties to attacks.

When you combine all three—Lakshana, Cosmos, and Anima—you trigger **Charge**. During this reaction, every hit landed by the Blossom turret or the character's enhanced attacks grants the active character a flat amount of Ultimate energy. This is significantly faster than relying on Basic Attack strings. For players running a "Hyper-Carry" style team where one character does 90% of the damage, having two off-field supports of the Cosmos and Anima elements is essential to keep that carry's Ultimate available on every cooldown.

Parrying is the highest-impact energy mechanic in Neverness to Everness. While dodging is safer, parrying provides the "Momentum" needed to end fights quickly. When an enemy prepares a heavy attack, a red circle will appear.

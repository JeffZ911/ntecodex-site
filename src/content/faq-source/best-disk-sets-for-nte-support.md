When optimizing a team in Neverness to Everness, players frequently ask: what are the best disk sets (Cartridges) for support characters? We also need to identify which specific sets should be prioritized for healers, shielders, and buffers to maximize their utility. ## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/best-disk-sets-for-nte-support/inline-3.webp)


The gear system in Neverness to Everness, centered around the "Console" and "Cartridges" (Disks), is more complex than standard gear systems in other urban RPGs. To maximize a support character, you must understand how the 4-piece bonuses interact with the Console grid and which module types (II, III, and IV) are necessary to trigger Legendary-tier effects.

### Speedy Hedgehog: The Meta-Defining Buffer Set

Speedy Hedgehog is currently the most used disk set for supports in high-level content like the "Anomaly Singularity" floors. Its strength lies in its dual-purpose design. The 2-piece bonus grants a +12% increase to Cycle Intensity. In NTE, Cycle Intensity determines how quickly your Esper accumulates the energy required to cast their Ultimate. For supports like Sakiri, who provide buffs primarily through their Ultimate, reaching a 160% or 180% Cycle Intensity threshold is mandatory for "one-rotation" loops.

The 4-piece bonus is what truly sets it apart. Upon casting an Ultimate, all team members receive a 15% ATK increase for 15 seconds. Since most support Ultimates have a cooldown between 15 and 20 seconds, a well-built support can maintain nearly 100% uptime on this buff. This effectively turns your support into a permanent stat stick for your main DPS.

### Kingdom's Guard: Maximizing Team Survivability

While offensive buffs are popular, the difficulty spikes in Version 1.1 have made defensive supports like Adler indispensable. Kingdom's Guard is designed for characters whose utility scales with Defense. The 2-piece bonus provides a flat +15% DEF increase. For Adler, whose shield strength is a direct percentage of his total DEF, this is a massive boost to the team's "Effective HP."

The 4-piece bonus increases Shield Effect by 20%. This doesn't just make the shield "thicker"; it also improves the shield's resistance to poise break, allowing your DPS to attack through enemy hits without being staggered. When running characters that are susceptible to interruption, such as [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte), a Kingdom's Guard set on your tank is a significant DPS gain because it prevents lost uptime from dodging or being knocked back.

### Thea's Night Tavern: The Healer's Choice

Healers in NTE, such as Edgar, often struggle with the balance between building enough Cycle Intensity to heal often and building enough raw stats to heal for a meaningful amount. Thea's Night Tavern solves this by providing a massive 20% Healing Bonus as its 4-piece effect.

This set is particularly effective because the Healing Bonus is a multiplicative stat. It is applied after the flat scaling from Edgar's ATK or HP (depending on the skill). If you find your team dying to "Scorch" or "Hexed" status effects in late-game zones, switching your healer to Thea's Night Tavern is often more effective than simply leveling up their weapon (Arc).

### Sub-stat Priority: What to Look for in Modules

Even with the correct 4-piece set, your support will underperform if the modules have poor sub-stats. The Console grid allows for various module shapes, but the priority for supports remains consistent:

1.  **Cycle Intensity (Charge Efficiency):** This is the non-negotiable priority. A support who cannot use their Ultimate every rotation is a wasted slot. Aim for at least 150% on most units.
2.  **Flat and Percentage Scaling:** For Adler, look for DEF%. For Edgar, look for ATK% or HP% depending on his current build.
3.  **Speed/Action Advance:** undisclosed regarding exact speed breakpoints, but generally, you want your support to move before your DPS to apply buffs.
4.  **Anomaly Mastery:** For supports like Sakiri who help trigger reactions like "Blossom," having some Anomaly Mastery helps increase the [reaction damage](https://ntecodex.com/guides/nte-chaos-vs-psyche-reaction-damage-gear-guide) without sacrificing utility.

### Character Pairings: Who Wears What?

To help you gear your roster, here are the best-in-slot recommendations for the current top-tier supports:

*   **Sakiri:** Speedy Hedgehog. Her kit focuses on rapid-fire Ultimate usage to keep [enemy resistances](https://ntecodex.com/guides/zzz-3-0-disputed-node-enemy-resistances-gear-guide) shredded. The Cycle Intensity from this set is vital.
*   **Haniel:** Speedy Hedgehog. As a pure buffer, Haniel needs to stack as much team-wide ATK as possible.
*   **Adler:** Kingdom's Guard. His role as a Physical Tank requires the DEF scaling provided by this set to keep his shields from breaking.
*   **Edgar:** Thea's Night Tavern. As the premier healer, he needs the outgoing healing bonus to keep up with high-damage bosses.
*   **Hotori:** Tiny Big Adventure or Speedy Hedgehog. Hotori is versatile, but if you find her too squishy, the Max HP from Tiny Big Adventure provides a safety net.

### Farming Guide: Character Pixels and Carrota Coins

Farming Legendary (Gold) disks requires a significant investment of "Stamina" (Action Points). To efficiently farm these sets:
1.  Focus on the "Cartridge Salvage" domains during the weekends when [drop rates](https://ntecodex.com/faq) for Type IV modules are slightly higher.
2.  Use **Character Pixels** to craft specific slots for your Speedy Hedgehog set if you are missing a specific shape for the Console grid.
3.  Keep an eye on the **Carrota Coin** shop; it resets monthly and often contains Epic or Legendary modules with pre-determined favorable sub-stats.

### Team Compositions and Reactions

Support disk sets are the engine behind high-tier elemental reactions. For a **Blossom** team (Nature + Water), your supports need high Cycle Intensity to keep the elemental application constant. In **Scorch** (Fire + Wind) or **Hexed** (Dark + Lightning) compositions, the ATK buff from Speedy Hedgehog is often more valuable than raw elemental damage because it scales the base hit before the reaction multiplier is applied.

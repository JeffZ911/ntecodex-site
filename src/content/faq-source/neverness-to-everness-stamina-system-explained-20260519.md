### The Dual-System Approach in NTE

Neverness to Everness distinguishes between your life as an Esper fighting supernatural anomalies and your life as a citizen of Hethereau. This is reflected in the dual stamina system. Character Pixels govern your combat growth, including character leveling and weapon upgrading, while City Stamina governs your economic growth and "Fons" (the primary currency) generation through urban activities. This separation ensures that players can engage with the city's sandbox elements without sacrificing their ability to farm for character power.

## Direct Answer

Neverness to Everness features a dual stamina system: Character Pixels for combat/progression and City Stamina for urban activities. Character Pixels regenerate at a rate of 1 every 6 minutes, totaling 240 Pixels per day. These are primarily used for claiming rewards from Anomaly Zones and World Bosses. 

City Stamina operates on a weekly cycle rather than a daily one. It resets every Monday at 4:00 AM (UTC+8) and is consumed during Hethereau Hobbies to earn Fons and Tycoon experience. Additionally, a separate, non-replenishable Combat Stamina bar (often called the "Exploration Bar") is used for physical actions like sprinting, dodging, and climbing.

### Character Pixels: The Progression Engine

Character Pixels serve as the traditional "energy" found in most gacha RPGs. Every account starts with a hard cap of 240 Pixels. Because they regenerate at a rate of 1 every 6 minutes, it takes exactly 24 hours to go from zero to a full tank. Managing this resource is vital for account power because almost every material required to upgrade your Espers is gated behind Pixel consumption.

While the base cap is 240, players can effectively manage "overflow" or increase the utility of their Pixels through specific housing mechanics. By interacting with the Eden apartment system and placing "Dream Weaver's Knot" furniture, you can access minor bonuses to resource efficiency, though the [exact value pending official confirmation] for the maximum possible cap increase remains a point of discussion among the community.

#### Anomaly Zones and Reward Scaling
Most combat activities, known as Anomaly Zones, require 40 Character Pixels to claim rewards upon completion. A key feature in NTE is the "Double Stamina" mechanic. When entering a cleared Anomaly Zone, you can choose to spend 80 Pixels in a single run to receive double the rewards, significantly cutting down the time required for daily "grinding."

### City Stamina: The Urban Economy

City Stamina is a unique system that separates NTE from its competitors. Instead of recharging every few minutes, City Stamina is a bulk weekly allowance. This design allows players to binge-play minigames on the weekend or spread them out through the week without feeling the pressure of a "capping" timer.

The amount of City Stamina you have access to is tied directly to your Tycoon Level. As you engage in the city's economy—buying cars, managing businesses, and winning minigames—your Tycoon Level increases, which in turn expands your City Stamina pool.

**Tycoon Level City Stamina Cap Scaling:**
*   **Tycoon Level 5:** 200 City Stamina
*   **Tycoon Level 10:** 350 City Stamina
*   **Tycoon Level 15:** 500 City Stamina
*   **Tycoon Level 23:** 700 City Stamina (Current Soft Cap)

### Exploration & Combat Stamina Mechanics

Separate from the "currency" stamina (Pixels and City Energy) is the physical stamina bar visible on your UI during movement. This bar governs your character's physical exertion and does not require time or items to recharge; it refills automatically after a few seconds of inactivity.

*   **Sprinting:** Normal sprinting in the open world consumes stamina slowly.
*   **Combat-Sprinting:** Sprinting while in a combat stance or inside Anomaly Zones consumes stamina at a 25% faster rate.
*   **Dodging:** Each dodge consumes a fixed segment of the bar.

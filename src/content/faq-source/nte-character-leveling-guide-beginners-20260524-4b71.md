As a new player in Hethereau, understanding the multi-layered progression system can be overwhelming. Beginners often find themselves asking

*   How do you level up characters in Neverness to Everness (NTE)?
*   What materials are required to increase Esper levels?

The leveling process in NTE is not restricted to a single experience bar. It involves managing your account rank, world tier, and individual character breakthroughs simultaneously. If you fail to keep up with any of these systems, your Espers will quickly fall behind the power curve of the city's increasingly dangerous anomalies.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-character-leveling-guide-beginners-20260524-4b71/inline-2.webp)


Character leveling in NTE requires consuming Hunter Guides and Beetle Coins via the Character menu. You cannot simply gain enough experience through open-world combat to keep pace with the game's difficulty; manual investment of items is mandatory.

Leveling is gated by the Appraisal Level system, which is directly tied to your overall Hunter Level (Account Rank). You cannot raise an Esper's level beyond the current cap set by your Appraisal Level. To bypass these caps at specific milestones (Level 20, 40, 60, etc.), you must perform a "Breakthrough" using elemental materials farmed from Anomaly Hunts.

For the most efficient start, prioritize leveling your Main DPS character first. Characters like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or Chiz should receive resources before your supports to ensure you can clear farming stages quickly.

### The Core Leveling Loop

The progression loop in NTE follows a predictable pattern
1.  Earn Hunter Level EXP by completing missions and spending Character Pixels.
2.  Reach a Hunter Level milestone (e.g., Level 20).
3.  Complete the Appraisal Level promotion quest to raise the world tier and character level cap.
4.  Farm Anomaly Hunts for Breakthrough materials.
5.  Spend Hunter Guides and Beetle Coins to reach the new level cap.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-character-leveling-guide-beginners-20260524-4b71/inline-3.webp)


Navigating the streets of Hethereau requires a deep understanding of the various systems that contribute to an Esper's total combat power. While the character level is the most visible stat, it is only one piece of the puzzle.

### Understanding Hunter Level vs. Appraisal Level

Your Hunter Level is your account-wide progression. You increase this by completing main story chapters, side quests (Urban Stories), and spending Character Pixels (stamina) on any resource stage. 

The Appraisal Level is the "World Tier" of NTE. Every 10 levels (and later every 5 levels), your Hunter Level will hit a soft cap. At this point, you must talk to the Hunter Association NPCs or access the menu to complete a "Promotion Trial." Successfully completing this trial increases your Appraisal Level, which
*   Increases the maximum level cap for all Espers and Arcs.
*   Increases the difficulty of enemies in the open world.
*   Improves the quality and quantity of drops from Anomaly Hunts and Magic Stages.

### Character Ascension and Breakthrough Mechanics

Once an Esper reaches their current level cap (e.g., Level 20/20), they cannot gain more stats until you perform a Breakthrough. This process consumes Beetle Coins and elemental-specific materials. 

There are six elements in Neverness to Everness
*   **Cosmos**
*   **Chaos**
*   **Anima**
*   **Psyche**
*   **Incantation**
*   **Lakshana**

Breakthrough materials are primarily obtained from Anomaly Hunts—boss encounters found throughout the city that cost Character Pixels to claim rewards. Each character requires a specific boss drop. For example, Nanally requires materials from the "Void-Stained" boss types, while Chiz utilizes materials found in the industrial sectors of Hethereau.

### Upgrading Esper Abilities and Talents

Leveling your character's base stats is only half the battle. You must also manually upgrade their Esper Abilities (Basic Attack, Skill, Ultimate, and Passive). These upgrades require a different set of materials, most notably "The Olive Branch" series of items.

Lower-level ability upgrades use common drops from city thugs and minor anomalies. However, higher-level talents require rare drops from weekly Anomaly Pilgrimages. These are high-difficulty boss resets that can only be claimed three times per week. Because these resources are strictly limited, you should only upgrade the abilities of characters you use in your primary team.

### The Console System: Cartridges and Modules

Unique to Neverness to Everness is the Console System, a gear mechanic that functions like a spatial puzzle. Each character has a "Console" where you insert "Cartridges." Within these Cartridges, you must fit "Modules" of various shapes (L-shapes, squares, and lines) to activate stat bonuses.

*   **Modules:** These provide the actual stats (ATK%, Crit Rate, HP). They come in different rarities and shapes.
*   **Cartridges:** These provide the grid and "Set Bonuses." If you fill a Cartridge with Modules of a specific type, you unlock powerful passive effects.

Early in the game, focus on simply filling the grid to get the base stats. As you reach Hunter Level 40, you should start optimizing the shapes to maximize the number of Modules you can fit into a single Cartridge.

### Arc Leveling and Anomaly Links

"Arcs" are the weapons of NTE. Like characters, they must be leveled up using a material called Pigment. Arcs also have their own Breakthrough system, which requires materials found at the Bubble Can Factory or purchased with Fons in the commercial districts.

A critical mechanic for beginners is the Anomaly Link. If you have multiple Arcs of the same name, you can "Link" them together (Refinement) to increase the potency of the weapon's passive ability. For F2P players, a fully Linked 4-star Arc is often stronger than a base 5-star Arc.

### Efficient Farming: Spending Character Pixels Wisely

Character Pixels are your most valuable resource. They regenerate over time, and you should never let them sit at the maximum cap. For efficient leveling, follow this priority list for spending your Pixels

1.  **Weekly Anomaly Pilgrimages:** Always do these first after the weekly reset to get high-level talent materials.
2.  **Breakthrough Materials:** Farm only what you need to reach the next level milestone for your main team.
3.  **Hunter Guides (Houdinii's Magic Stages):** Spend the majority of your daily Pixels here during the early game (Hunter Level 1–30).
4.  **Console Modules:** Avoid heavy farming for Modules until you reach Appraisal Level 3 or 4, as the drop rates for gold-rarity Modules are significantly higher at those tiers.

**Daily and Weekly Leveling Priorities Checklist:**
*   [ ] Complete all 4 Daily Commissions for Hunter Level EXP.
*   [ ] Spend all Character Pixels to avoid capping.
*   [ ] Check the City Tycoon interface for passive Beetle Coin income.
*   [ ] Clear the weekly Anomaly Pilgrimage (3/3 attempts).
*   [ ] Purchase the discounted Hunter Guides from the convenience store vendors in Hethereau.

Beyond the raw numbers of leveling, your progression is heavily influenced by how you manage your resources and who you choose to invest in during the first 20 hours of gameplay.

### Team Building for Beginners

For a smooth leveling experience, you need a balanced team that can handle different anomaly types. A standard "Starter" team that is highly effective for early-game progression includes
*   **Main DPS:** Nanally (Physical/Fire) or Chiz (Electric). These should be 10 levels higher than the rest of your team.
*   **Support/Shield:** Zero. Her ability to provide shields significantly reduces the need for healing items during boss fights.
*   **Healer/Buffer:** Mint. She provides consistent sustain and allows your DPS to stay on the field longer.
*   **Sub-DPS/Crowd Control:** Haniel. Useful for grouping smaller enemies together for Nanally’s AoE attacks.

### City Tycoon and Economic Progression

NTE features a robust urban simulation system called City Tycoon. While it may seem separate from combat, it is vital for leveling. By managing businesses and real estate, you earn **Fons**. 

Fons are used for "Urban" upgrades, such as buying faster cars or upgrading your apartment, but they can also be exchanged for **Beetle Coins** or used to buy leveling materials directly from high-end boutiques. Chiz is particularly useful in this mode, as her character passives provide bonuses to business efficiency. If you ignore the City Tycoon system, you will eventually run out of Beetle Coins when trying to level multiple characters to 80 or 90.

### Weapon (Arc) Tier List

When deciding which Arcs to level alongside your characters, look for those that complement the Esper's scaling. For example
*   **Nanally:** Look for Arcs that boost "Basic Attack DMG" or "CRIT DMG."
*   **Zero:** Prioritize Arcs that scale with "DEF" or "Max HP" to strengthen her shields.
*   **Chiz:** Focus on "Energy Regeneration" or "Elemental Mastery" to keep her ultimate active.

One of the best early-game investments is the Novis ST-X 950 motorcycle. While not a weapon, upgrading your vehicle's speed and handling allows you to reach Anomaly Hunts and gathering nodes much faster, indirectly speeding up your character's leveling process by reducing travel time across the massive map of Hethereau.

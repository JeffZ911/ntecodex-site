## Question Restated

Players looking to progress through the story and tackle high-level Anomaly Hunts often ask: how do you level up Espers in Neverness to Everness? Beyond the basic application of experience points, there are several layers to character progression that players must understand. This includes identifying the specific materials required to increase character stats and level caps, as well as understanding how Appraisal Levels and Hunter Levels restrict character progression. 

The leveling system in NTE is not a simple linear path; it is gated by your overall account progress. Without increasing your Appraisal Level, your strongest characters will remain stuck at early-game caps, regardless of how many resources you have. This guide breaks down the mechanics of the Esper upgrade system, the resources required for Ascension, and the most efficient ways to farm the necessary materials.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-increase-character-level-nte-20260520/inline-2.webp)


To increase character level in NTE, navigate to the Esper menu, select your character, and consume Hunter Guides (EXP items) along with Beetle Coins. This process provides immediate stat growth but is subject to hard caps. Characters hit level caps at specific intervals (e.g., Level 20, 30, 40) which require a 'Breakthrough' or 'Ascension' to bypass. 

Your maximum character level is strictly gated by your Appraisal Level, which increases as you gain Hunter Level experience through quests and exploration. For example, you cannot push an Esper past Level 20 until you have reached the requisite Hunter Level to perform the first Appraisal Level upgrade.

Primary farming locations for leveling materials include Houdinii’s Magic Stage for EXP items and Anomaly Hunts for breakthrough materials. Using these stages effectively requires the consumption of Character Pixels (stamina).

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-increase-character-level-nte-20260520/inline-3.webp)


Character leveling serves as the foundation for your team's combat effectiveness. While gear and weapons provide significant modifiers, the base stat growth from leveling—specifically HP, ATK, and DEF—determines whether an Esper can survive a single hit from a world boss or clear a timed challenge. 

The progression loop involves four main steps: gaining account experience to raise your Hunter Level, performing an Appraisal Level breakthrough to raise the global level cap, using Hunter Guides to reach the current cap, and performing individual Esper Ascension to unlock the next ten levels.

### The Core Leveling Process: Using Hunter Guides
The most direct method of leveling is the manual application of Hunter Guides. Unlike some traditional RPGs where characters gain significant experience from defeating overworld enemies, NTE relies on a "bottled EXP" system. This ensures that you can instantly bring a newly pulled Esper, such as [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or Zero, up to the level of your current team without grinding low-level mobs.

To level up:
1. Open the main menu and select the 'Esper' tab.
2. Choose the character you wish to upgrade.
3. Click on the 'Level Up' or 'Upgrade' button.
4. Select the quantity of Rising, Senior, or Elite Hunter Guides you wish to use.
5. Confirm the Beetle Coin cost to apply the experience.

### Appraisal Levels: The Character Level Cap System
The 'Appraisal Level' acts as a global cap that prevents players from over-leveling their characters relative to the game's story content. This system ensures that the challenge remains consistent as you explore Hesperia. Your Appraisal Level is tied directly to your Hunter Level (your account level).

| Hunter Level | Appraisal Level | Character Level Cap |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1-19 | 1 | 20 |
| 20-29 | 2 | 40 |
| 30-39 | 3 | 50 |
| 40-44 | 4 | 60 |
| 45-49 | 5 | 70 |
| 50+ | 6 | 80 |

Once you reach the Hunter Level milestone (such as Level 20), you must complete an Appraisal Trial quest to unlock the next level. This often involves a combat challenge against specific anomalies.

### Character Ascension: Breaking Through the Level Wall
Even if your Appraisal Level allows for Level 40, an individual Esper will still stop gaining experience at Level 20, 30, and 40 until they undergo Ascension (also known as Breakthrough). This process requires specific boss drops and regional materials.

Common materials include:
*   **Anomaly Drops:** Obtained by defeating specific world bosses or completing Anomaly Pilgrimages.
*   **Lost Pieces:** Regional specialty items found while exploring Hesperia's various districts.
*   **Beetle Coins:** The standard currency required for every upgrade transaction.

### Farming Locations: Houdinii’s Magic Stage and Anomaly Hunts
To maintain a steady supply of Hunter Guides, you must spend Character Pixels at Houdinii’s Magic Stage. This is a repeatable combat arena where players face waves of enemies. Higher difficulty tiers of the Magic Stage yield Elite Hunter Guides, which are far more efficient for leveling characters past Level 50.

Anomaly Hunts are specialized encounters that focus on specific boss mechanics. These are the primary source of the rare materials needed for Ascension. Because Character Pixels are a limited daily resource, you must balance your farming between EXP items and Ascension materials.

### Managing Your Resources: Beetle Coins and Character Pixels
Resource management is the biggest hurdle in the mid-to-late game. Beetle Coins may seem abundant in the first few hours, but the cost of leveling scales exponentially. 

*   **Beetle Coin Costs:** Leveling a character from 1 to 20 costs undisclosed Beetle Coins, while 70 to 80 can cost upwards of undisclosed.
*   **Character Pixels:** These regenerate at a rate of 1 every undisclosed minutes. It is generally recommended to prioritize Anomaly Hunts during the weekends if there are active drop-rate bonuses.

### Early Game Leveling Strategy: Who to Prioritize
In the early game, avoid the temptation to level every Esper you unlock. Resource depletion happens quickly once you reach Appraisal Level 3. Focus on a core team of four Espers that cover different elemental and role requirements (Main DPS, Sub-DPS, Support, and Sustenance/Healer).

Prioritize your Main DPS (such as Nanally or Byron) first, keeping them at the maximum possible level allowed by your current Appraisal Level. Your supports can generally function effectively even if they are 10 levels behind the DPS, provided their Arcs and Gear are well-maintained.

### The Impact of Character Level on Combat Stats and Scaling
Leveling up does more than just increase raw numbers. In NTE, certain combat mechanics scale based on the level difference between your Esper and the enemy Anomaly. If your character is significantly lower level than the target, you may suffer a "Level Penalty," which reduces the damage you deal and increases the damage you take.

Stat growth per level:
*   **HP:** Increases survivability against heavy-hitting anomaly AOE attacks.
*   **ATK:** Directly increases the damage of basic attacks, skills, and Ultimates.
*   **DEF:** Reduces the flat damage taken from physical and elemental sources.

### Leveling via Exploration and Quests
While Hunter Guides are the primary source of EXP, completing Main Story Quests and Side Commissions provides a small amount of character experience to the active party. More importantly, these activities are the fastest way to gain Hunter Level experience, which is necessary to unlock higher Appraisal Levels. 

Exploring Hesperia also rewards players with Beetle Coins and occasional Hunter Guides found in hidden containers or as rewards for clearing urban anomalies.

Character level is only one part of the power equation in Neverness to Everness. To truly maximize your team's potential, you must also look into Arcs, Consoles, and the Awakening system.

### How to Level Up Arcs (Weapons)
Arcs, the weapons equipped by Espers, require a separate leveling process. Instead of Hunter Guides, Arcs consume EXP Dyes. Arcs also have their own Ascension requirements, often necessitating materials like 'Golden Appleseed' or specific refined ores found in the industrial sectors of the city. Like characters, Arcs are capped at certain levels and must be "Enhanced" to continue progressing.

### Increasing Your Hunter Level Fast
Since your character level is gated by your Appraisal Level, increasing your Hunter Level (account level) is a top priority. The most efficient ways to gain account EXP include:
*   Completing Daily Commissions.
*   Clearing the Main Story chapters.
*   Opening supply crates scattered throughout Hesperia.
*   Completing "First Clear" rewards for Anomaly Pilgrimages.

### The Awakening System and Mind Shards
Once a character reaches their level cap, you can further enhance their power through the Awakening system. This does not increase the numerical level (e.g., from 80 to 81) but instead unlocks passive resonances and skill enhancements. Awakening requires Mind Shards, which are primarily obtained by pulling duplicate copies of an Esper from the gacha banners, such as "The Ichi-daime" banner. Each Awakening tier provides a significant boost to specific skills or provides team-wide buffs.

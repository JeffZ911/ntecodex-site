### What are Disk Sets in Neverness to Everness?

In the context of NTE, "Disk Sets" is a colloquial term carried over from other urban fantasy RPGs. The official system in Neverness to Everness consists of two primary components: **Modules** and **Cartridges**. 

*   **Modules:** These are the individual gear pieces that possess specific shapes (Type II, Type III, or Type IV). They are placed onto a character's Console grid.
*   **Cartridges:** This refers to the "set bonus" activated when you place enough Modules of the same type into the Console. For example, if you place four "Speedy Hedgehog" Modules into your grid, you activate the Speedy Hedgehog Cartridge set bonus.

### Understanding B-Rank (3-Star) Rarity

B-rank gear is identified by its blue color coding. In the hierarchy of NTE equipment, B-rank sits below A-rank (Purple) and S-rank (Orange). However, B-rank gear is the most efficient choice for players between Account Level 1 and 30. The primary reason for this is the resource economy; upgrading B-rank Modules requires significantly fewer materials than their higher-rarity counterparts, yet they provide enough of a stat boost to clear the "Beyond the Rails" challenges and early world bosses.

### Why B-Rank Gear is Essential for Early Progression

New players often make the mistake of hoarding resources for S-rank gear that they cannot yet farm efficiently. B-rank gear is essential because of its accessibility. You can obtain a full set of B-rank Modules within the first few hours of gameplay by participating in the "Rabbit Hole" activities in the New Herland District. 

Furthermore, the set bonuses in NTE are identical across rarities. A B-rank Speedy Hedgehog set provides the same 15% ATK buff as an S-rank version. The only difference lies in the primary stats (like flat HP or ATK) and the potential ceiling of the sub-stats. For a support character who only needs to trigger the set bonus, a B-rank set is often just as good as an S-rank set during the first month of a server's life.

To master the gearing system in Neverness to Everness, you must look beyond just the names of the sets and understand the spatial puzzle of the Console system. Your ability to maximize a character's power depends on how efficiently you can fit Modules into their grid.

### The Console & Module Mechanics Explained

Every character in NTE has a Console grid that expands as they level up. Modules come in different shapes, categorized as Type II, Type III, and Type IV. These names refer to the number of blocks the Module occupies on the grid, similar to Tetris pieces.

*   **Type II Modules:** Usually straight lines or small squares. They are easy to fit but have lower base stats.
*   **Type III Modules:** Often "L" or "T" shaped. These provide a balance between stat budget and grid space.
*   **Type IV Modules:** Large, complex shapes that offer the highest primary stats.

To activate a 4-piece Cartridge bonus, you don't necessarily need four physical items if you are using higher-tier Modules, but in the B-rank tier, you generally need to fit four individual Modules of the same set name into the grid. This requires careful planning to ensure you don't leave empty, unusable gaps on the Console.

### How to Farm and Upgrade B-Rank Cartridges

Farming for B-rank gear is straightforward compared to the late-game grind. The primary source is the **Rabbit Hole**, a series of combat trials located in the **New Herland District**.

1.  **Bronze Carrota Coins:** Clearing Rabbit Hole stages rewards you with Bronze Carrota Coins. These are the specific currency used at the "Vending Machines" near the district entrance to pull for B-rank Modules.
2.  **The "No RNG" Stat System:** One of the most player-friendly features of NTE is that B-rank gear shows all four of its sub-stats immediately upon acquisition. You do not need to level the item to "reveal" stats. Before spending resources to upgrade a Module to Level 10, check if it has desirable sub-stats like ATK%, Crit Rate, or Elemental DMG.
3.  **Recycling:** If you get a Module with poor stats or a shape that doesn't fit your Console, you can recycle it to recover a portion of the upgrade materials and Carrota Coins.

### Optimizing Your Console Grid (The Tetris Strategy)

As you acquire B-rank Modules, you will notice they come in various shapes even within the same set. To optimize your grid

*   **Prioritize Type IV for DPS:** Your main damage dealer should use Type IV Modules in the center of the grid to maximize their base ATK or Elemental DMG.
*   **Use Type II for Fillers:** Use the small 2-block Modules of the Speedy Hedgehog set on your supports to fill the edges of the grid, allowing you to hit the 4-piece bonus without blocking your high-stat pieces.
*   **Symmetry Matters:** The Console grid is symmetrical. Often, placing two "L" shaped Type III pieces mirroring each other is the most efficient way to leave a large 2x2 or 2x4 space in the center for your powerhouse Modules.

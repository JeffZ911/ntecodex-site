Exploring the supernatural urban landscape of Hethereau is a massive undertaking, but you don't have to face the anomalies of Neverness to Everness (NTE) alone. While the game starts as a solo journey to introduce you to the mechanics of Esper Zero and the Eibon shop, the co-op features eventually allow you to team up with friends for high-speed races and anomaly raids.

# How to Unlock Multiplayer in Neverness to Everness: Co-op Guide

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/how-to-unlock-multiplayer-neverness-to-everness/inline-1.webp)


Many new players entering Hethereau find themselves wondering exactly when the social features of the game become available. Because Neverness to Everness features a heavy narrative focus during its opening hours, the multiplayer icon is conspicuously absent from the initial menu. This leads to several common questions: How do you unlock the co-op and multiplayer features in Neverness to Everness? What are the specific level and story requirements to play with friends? Where is the multiplayer menu located within the NTE interface?

### The Social Experience in Hethereau

Hethereau is designed as a living, breathing city where supernatural anomalies coexist with everyday urban life. While the main story missions are tailored for a single-player experience to ensure players connect with characters like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) and [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte), the open world is built for interaction. Multiplayer allows up to four players to occupy the same instance of the city. In this shared space, you can show off your customized vehicles, tackle difficult world bosses in Anomaly Zones, or simply hang out at the local Maid Café to play a round of Mahjong. The social experience is a core pillar of the endgame, turning the city into a playground for groups of "Appraisers" to explore together.

### Why Multiplayer is Initially Locked

Neverness to Everness utilizes a gated progression system common in open-world RPGs to ensure players understand the fundamental gameplay loop before introducing the complexities of co-op. During the first hour of gameplay, you are introduced to the combat system, the "Esper" switching mechanics, and the basic navigation of the city. Opening multiplayer too early would allow players to bypass these essential tutorials or potentially get lost in high-level districts without the proper gear. By locking the feature behind a specific Hunter Level and story milestone, the developers ensure that every player in a co-op session has at least a baseline understanding of how to survive Hethereau’s more dangerous anomalies.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-unlock-multiplayer-neverness-to-everness/inline-2.webp)


Multiplayer in Neverness to Everness unlocks once you reach Hunter Level 8. This level serves as the primary gateway for most of the game's social and competitive features. However, reaching the level requirement is only half of the process; you must also complete the main story quest "Prologue II: Good Business, Better Treasure." This quest concludes the introductory arc of the game and establishes your role within the Eibon organization.

Once both requirements are met, the feature is accessed via the in-game smartphone menu on the second page of apps. It is important to verify your server settings before trying to connect, as both you and your friends must be on the same server region to play together. If one player is on the North American server and another is on the European server, you will not be able to find each other’s User IDs (UIDs).

### Quick Unlock Checklist

| Requirement | Details |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hunter Level** | Reach Level 8 |
| **Main Story Progress** | Complete "Prologue II: Good Business, Better Treasure" |
| **Server Region** | Both players must be on the same regional server |
| **Access Point** | In-game Smartphone -> Page 2 -> Multiplayer Icon |

### The Hunter Level 8 Threshold

Hunter Level 8 is the specific point where the game determines you have sufficient experience to join the wider community. This level is tied to your account-wide progression and is separate from the individual levels of your Espers. Reaching this threshold typically takes about 45 to 60 minutes of active play if you focus on the main objectives. As you gain Hunter EXP, you also increase your Appraisal Level, which dictates the difficulty and rewards of the world around you. 

### Required Story Progression

The story requirement, "Prologue II: Good Business, Better Treasure," is the final hurdle. This mission involves traveling to the Bridge Crossings area, syncing with the Wertheimer Tower to unlock the map, and eventually heading to the Eibon shop (often referred to as Abone). During this quest, you will encounter a boss fight against Taygedo. Defeating Taygedo and resting at the dormitory marks the official completion of the prologue. Immediately after the quest-clear notification appears, a pop-up will inform you that "Multiplayer" and "Friends" functions are now active on your device.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-unlock-multiplayer-neverness-to-everness/inline-3.webp)


Navigating the multiplayer interface for the first time can be slightly confusing since NTE uses a "smartphone" UI that hides several apps on secondary screens. To begin a session, follow these steps:

1.  Open your in-game phone by tapping the icon in the top right corner (or pressing the designated hotkey on PC/PS5).
2.  Swipe the screen to the left to access **Page 2** of the applications.
3.  Tap the **Multiplayer** icon, which is located directly next to the "Tutorial" app.
4.  From this menu, you can see a list of available worlds to join or search for specific friends.

### Adding Friends and Using UIDs

Every player in Neverness to Everness is assigned a unique 10-digit User ID (UID). You can find your own UID at the bottom of the main phone menu or within your profile settings. To add a friend, enter their UID into the search bar located at the top of the Multiplayer menu. Once the request is accepted, they will appear in your "Friends" list.

When starting a session, you have two primary options:
*   **Hosting (Invite to Join):** You act as the host, and friends join your version of Hethereau. Your world's progression (unlocked teleport points and world level) will be the standard for the session.
*   **Joining (Request Access):** You enter a friend's world. While you can help them with combat and exploration, you cannot progress your own main story missions while in their world.

### Co-op Activities: Combat, Racing, and Hobbies

Multiplayer supports up to 4 players in a single world instance. While the main story remains a solo affair, almost every other activity in Hethereau can be shared. 

| Activity | Multiplayer Support | Unlock Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **World Exploration** | Yes (4 Players) | Hunter Level 8 |
| **Anomaly Raids** | Yes (4 Players) | Hunter Level 8 |
| **Street Racing** | Yes (4 Players) | Hunter Level 17 & Tycoon Level 2 |
| **Mahjong** | Yes (4 Players) | Hunter Level 11 |
| **Tetrominoes** | Yes (4 Players) | Tycoon Level 8 |
| **Pink Paws Heist** | Yes (4 Players) | Tycoon Level 12 |
| **Main Story Quests** | No | Solo Only |

The **Pink Paws Heist** mode is a dedicated multiplayer challenge that has become a favorite for farming Fons (the primary currency). Located at the Pink Paws Bank HQ in the New Herland District, you can speak to the NPC Chiz to start a simulation heist. In co-op, players can split up to cover different floors of the bank, making it much easier to locate keys and loot before the 12-minute timer expires.

### Housing and City Tycoon

The "City Tycoon" system is deeply integrated with multiplayer. Once you unlock the **My House** feature, you can invite friends to visit your decorated apartment or estate. Friends can interact with certain furniture items and view your vehicle collection in the garage. Managing shops and businesses together is also a possibility as you raise your Tycoon Level, allowing a group of players to coordinate their efforts to maximize the city's economy.

### Multiplayer Restrictions: What You Cannot Do

While the co-op mode is expansive, there are several limitations to keep in mind:
*   **Story Blocking:** You cannot initiate or progress main "Episode" quests while in a multiplayer session. If you reach a story trigger, the game will prompt you to leave the group.
*   **Loot Sharing:** In modes like the Pink Paws Heist, loot is often calculated individually. If one player picks up a pile of Fons, it may disappear for others, meaning coordination is required to ensure everyone gets their share.
*   **Teleport Points:** You can only use the teleport points (Phone Booths) that the **host** has unlocked. If you are joining a new player's world, you may find your travel options limited.
*   **Oracle Stones:** Exploration collectibles like Oracle Stones are unique to each player. If you pick one up in a friend's world, it counts for your collection, but it will not count for theirs.

## Related

![Related](/img/how-to-unlock-multiplayer-neverness-to-everness/inline-4.webp)


If you are eager to reach the multiplayer stage, focusing on efficient leveling is the best strategy. The jump from the start of the game to Hunter Level 8 is relatively quick, but the subsequent climb to unlock advanced features like Racing (Level 17) requires more dedicated effort.

### Fastest Ways to Reach Hunter Level 8

The most efficient way to gain Hunter EXP is through the **Circle Bounty** (Daily Commissions). These tasks reset every 24 hours and provide a massive injection of experience for very little time investment. Additionally, clearing **Anomaly Zones** using your "Character Pixels" (stamina) will grant a steady stream of EXP. 

**Fast Hunter XP Sources:**
*   **Main Quests:** 1,000 - 2,000 EXP per mission.
*   **Circle Bounty (Dailies):** 400 EXP per task.
*   **Exploration Guide Phases:** 100 EXP per mini-goal.
*   **Side Quests (Blue Icons):** 150 - 500 EXP depending on length.

### Unlocking Racing and City Tycoon Hobbies

While basic co-op unlocks at Level 8, many of the "hobby" activities require you to engage with the **City Tycoon** system. This system tracks your economic impact on Hethereau. You increase your Tycoon Level by earning Fons, purchasing property, and completing business-related side quests. To unlock the competitive Street Racing mode, you must reach Tycoon Level 2 and Hunter Level 17. This ensures that players entering the racing scene have had enough time to customize their cars and understand the city's layout.

### Server Regions and Cross-Play Explained

Neverness to Everness features full cross-platform play between PC, PS5, and mobile devices. A player on an iPhone can seamlessly join a friend playing on a high-end PC. However, this cross-play is strictly bound by server regions. When you first create your account, you select a region (North America, Europe, Asia, etc.). 

Your character data is tied to that specific region. If you want to play with a friend, you must ensure you both select the same region at the login screen. While it is possible to change regions, your progress does not carry over, meaning you would have to start from Hunter Level 1 on the new server. Always double-check your friend's region before committing to a long-term save file.

The rewards earned during co-op, such as Fons and upgrade materials from Anomaly bosses, are vital for late-game progression. Participating in the bi-weekly Pink Paws Heist cycle can net you up to 1,000,000 Fons, which is essential for purchasing the S-Rank vehicles and luxury apartments that define the Hethereau lifestyle.

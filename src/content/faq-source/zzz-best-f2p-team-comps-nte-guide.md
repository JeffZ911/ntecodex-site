## Question Restated

*   **How do elemental synergies like the Esper Cycle affect F2P team building?**

Building a team in NTE is not just about raw power; it is about the "Esper Cycle," a system where switching between characters of specific elemental types triggers powerful reactions. For F2P players, leveraging these reactions is the only way to clear high-difficulty content like the "Unheard Shores" or the "New Herland" challenges without relying on limited-banner S-Rank pulls.

## Direct Answer

The best F2P starting team in Neverness to Everness consists of **Zero (Main Character), Mint, Adler, and Haniel**. This lineup is often referred to as the "Starter Four." It excels by triggering the **Blossom reaction** (Cosmos + Anima), which spawns autonomous turrets that deal consistent area-of-effect (AoE) damage while the rest of the team provides shielding and attack buffs.

As you progress through the main story and engage with the **City Tycoon** system, your team should transition into the **"Chiz Hypercarry"** composition. This team features **Chiz, Zero, Haniel, and Adler**. Chiz is a top-tier S-Rank Cosmos DPS who can be unlocked entirely for free by reaching Level 18 in the City Tycoon management mode.

To further optimize your endgame potential, use the **free S-Rank Selector** (granted after 40-50 pulls on the standard banner) to pick **[Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide)** or **Jiuyuan**. Sakiri is the recommended pick for most F2P players due to her massive 30% ATK buff and superior crowd control (CC) capabilities, which are vital for clearing waves of mobs in the open world.

## Detailed Explanation

The combat system in NTE revolves around the **Esper Meter**. Every character has a circular gauge next to their portrait that fills through basic attacks, skills, and parries. Once full, the portrait glows, signaling that an "Esper Ability" is ready. Swapping to that character at this moment triggers an elemental reaction based on the previous character's element.

### The 'Starter Four' Blossom Team

The "Starter Four" (Zero, Mint, Adler, Haniel) is the most reliable team for the first 20 hours of gameplay. The core of this team is the **Blossom reaction**, triggered by combining **Cosmos** (Zero) and **Anima** (Mint).

**Combat Rotation:**
1.  Start with **Zero** to build the Esper Meter. Zero’s skill is unique because it instantly fills the gauge to 100%.
2.  Swap to **Mint** to trigger the Blossom reaction. This spawns a "Vita Bud" turret that fires "Vita Pistils" at enemies, dealing AoE damage for several seconds.
3.  While the turrets are active, swap to **Adler** to provide the team with a shield. If the enemy is hit by Adler’s Incantation attacks while Mint’s Anima effects are active, you trigger **Hexed**, which deals follow-up damage based on 20% of the damage taken by the enemy in the last 12 seconds.
4.  Use **Haniel** to provide a team-wide ATK buff before Zero or Mint uses their Ultimate.

### The Chiz Hypercarry Transition

Once you reach City Tycoon Level 18, you unlock **Chiz**, an S-Rank Cosmos character who significantly outclasses Mint in terms of raw damage. Chiz utilizes a unique **"Grain Market"** mechanic. By using basic attacks, she accumulates "Grain" stacks. When she uses her charged skill at high Grain levels, she deals massive Cosmos damage.

The transition team (Chiz, Zero, Haniel, Adler) focuses on maximizing Chiz’s field time. In this setup, Zero acts as a "Quick-Swap" sub-DPS. Because Zero can fill the Esper gauge instantly, you can swap Zero in, use their skill, and immediately swap back to Chiz to trigger a reaction without disrupting Chiz’s Grain accumulation.

### Understanding the Esper Cycle and Elemental Lanes

The Esper Cycle is a wheel of six elements. Reactions only occur between **adjacent** elements on this wheel. If you swap between two elements that are opposite each other, no reaction occurs, and you lose a significant portion of your damage potential.

| Reaction Name | Elements Required | Combat Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blossom** | Cosmos + Anima | Spawns an AoE turret (Vita Bud). |
| **Remora** | Cosmos + Lakshana | Marks enemies and slows their movement/attacks. |
| **Hexed** | Anima + Incantation | Deals 20% follow-up damage based on recent hits. |
| **Scorch** | Incantation + Chaos | Applies a 15-second Damage-over-Time (DoT). |
| **Nova** | Chaos + Psyche | Attaches a bomb that deals burst damage after 5s. |
| **Stain** | Psyche + Lakshana | Increases damage taken from Psyche/Lakshana. |

**Trio Reactions:**
If you trigger two adjacent reactions within the same "Trio" (the top three elements or bottom three elements), you trigger a Triple Cycle. For example, triggering both Remora and Blossom results in **Charge**, which grants the active character a massive amount of Ultimate Energy every time the Blossom turret hits a Remora-marked target.

### F2P Combat Tips: Parrying and Phantom Swaps

To master F2P combat, you must look beyond basic skill rotations and utilize advanced mechanics:

1.  **Instant Meter Parrying:** Successfully parrying an enemy attack (indicated by a yellow or red glint) instantly fills the Esper Meter of the character currently on the field. This allows F2P teams to skip the "building phase" and go straight into high-damage reactions.
2.  **Phantom Swaps:** Some characters, like Chiz, can "channel" their skills even after you swap them out. If you start Chiz’s charged skill and immediately swap to Zero, Chiz will remain on the field for a few seconds to finish the attack. This allows two characters to deal damage simultaneously, a technique known as a Phantom Swap.
3.  **The 1 Million Fons Rule:** If you are using Chiz with her signature Arc, **Contemplative Cat**, always keep at least 1,000,000 Fons in your bank. The Arc provides a 2.5% Cosmos DMG bonus for every 100,000 Fons you hold, capping at 25%. Spending all your money on car upgrades in City Tycoon will directly lower your combat DPS.

### Rerolling: Is it Necessary?

Rerolling in Neverness to Everness takes approximately 20 minutes to reach the point where the gacha system unlocks. However, the community generally discourages rerolling for several reasons. First, the "no 50/50" gacha system ensures that players eventually get the characters they want without the frustration of "losing" a pity roll. Second, the game provides a generous amount of free S-Rank selectors and characters through gameplay milestones.

Most players find that starting with the guaranteed units—such as Mint and Zero—and working toward the free S-Rank Chiz provides a sufficiently strong foundation. If you do choose to reroll, your primary target should be a high-tier DPS character from the Standard "Fabricated Dice" banner to complement the support units given for free.

### The Scarborough Fair Gacha System Explained

The gacha system in NTE, known as Scarborough Fair, uses a board-game interface rather than a simple animation. Players use Solid Dice for limited banners and Fabricated Dice for the standard banner. As you "roll" the dice, your avatar moves across a board filled with rewards, including Arcs (weapons), Artifacts, and upgrade materials.

Landing on a character tile or reaching the hard pity threshold triggers the character acquisition. The pity system is structured to be more player-friendly than previous Hotta Studio titles.

### Combat Basics: Espers and the Cycle System

Combat in Hesperia relies on the "Esper Cycle" system. Every character (Esper) belongs to an element on the attribute wheel. Your team consists of four characters, and the goal is to switch between them to trigger reactions. When you switch to a character whose element is adjacent to the current character on the attribute wheel, you trigger a "Cycle Reaction," which deals massive burst damage and often applies crowd-control effects to anomalies.

The attribute wheel includes elements like Solid, Cosmos, and others. Mastering the order of your rotations is the difference between struggling against street-level anomalies and clearing high-threat zones with ease.

### Urban Life: Vehicles and Customization

Traversing Hesperia is primarily done through the vehicle system. Unlike many open-world games where cars are merely a mode of transport, NTE treats vehicles as a core progression pillar. Players can visit "E-Motors" for basic purchases or "Rampage Players" for high-end tuning. Customization includes performance parts (engines, tires, suspension) and aesthetic changes (paint, decals, neon underglow).

### The City Tycoon and Business Management

The City Tycoon system is the primary way to generate Fons, the game's main currency. Players can purchase and manage businesses ranging from convenience stores to high-end boutiques. Each business requires an "Operating Esper" to run. Placing characters with high "Management" stats into these roles increases your passive income.

Upgrading your City Tycoon level unlocks new districts of Hesperia and provides account-wide buffs. Reaching Tycoon Level 15 is a major milestone for beginners, as it unlocks the S-Rank character Chiz for free.

### Character Progression: Arcs, Consoles, and Artifacts

Character power in NTE is determined by three main factors:

1.  **Arcs:** These are the primary weapons. Each Arc has a unique passive ability that can drastically change how an Esper plays.
2.  **Consoles:** The gear interface where you equip Artifacts.
3.  **Artifacts:** These function like Tetris pieces. Each Artifact has a specific shape and stat profile. You must fit these pieces into the Console's grid to maximize stat bonuses. Matching colors or shapes can trigger "Set Resonances" for additional power.

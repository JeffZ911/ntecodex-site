## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/how-to-unlock-nte-daily-quests/inline-1.webp)


Accessing the daily gameplay loop is a priority for any player looking to optimize their account in Neverness to Everness. Because the game features a dense urban environment with various simulation elements, identifying which tasks contribute to your daily progression can be confusing for new players.

### The Importance of Dailies for Account Progression

In Neverness to Everness, your "Hunter EXP" serves as the primary gateway to higher-level content, weapon upgrades, and character ascension. Unlike the main story quests, which provide large but finite chunks of experience, the daily system offers a renewable source of 1,000 Hunter EXP every 24 hours. Without unlocking this system early, players often find themselves "level-gated," unable to progress through the later chapters of the Hethereau storyline because their account level is too low. Beyond experience, these tasks are the most reliable way to accumulate Annulith, the premium currency required for pulling on limited banners like "The Ichi-daime."

### Common Confusion: Daily Quests vs. Participation Tasks

New players often search for a traditional "Quest Log" to find their daily chores, but NTE utilizes a more modern "Participation" system. While there are specific daily missions called Circle Bounties, the bulk of your daily rewards comes from the Participation Task system. You do not always need to talk to an NPC to start these; many are tracked automatically as you engage with the world. Understanding the distinction between these automatic tasks and the manual commissions found in the city is vital for efficient play.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-unlock-nte-daily-quests/inline-2.webp)


Daily Quests in Neverness to Everness are officially unlocked after completing the Main Story Quest (MSQ) "Prologue - Part 2." This milestone occurs approximately two to three hours into the game, depending on how much time you spend exploring the initial districts of Hethereau.

Once you have completed the required story beat, you can access the "Exploration Guide" menu to view your active daily objectives. On PC, the default shortcut for this menu is the F4 key. On mobile devices, it is found by tapping the compass icon in the top-right corner of the HUD.

The daily reset in NTE occurs at 5:00 AM UTC+8 (Server Time). Completing the full daily set of Participation Tasks rewards the player with 1,000 Hunter EXP, a significant amount of Fons (the standard currency), and Annulith.

### The 'Prologue - Part 2' Milestone

To reach this milestone, you must follow the main objective marker through the introduction of the anomaly containment mechanics and your first encounter with the urban management systems. The "Prologue - Part 2" concludes after your initial visit to the Bureau of Anomaly Control, where you are formally registered as a Hunter. Once the dialogue concludes and the "System Unlocked" notification appears on your screen, the Exploration Guide becomes available.

### Where to Find the Exploration Guide

The Exploration Guide serves as your central hub for all activity tracking. Within this menu, you will see several tabs
1.  **Participation Tasks:** Your primary daily goal list.
2.  **Circle Bounty:** The seasonal battle pass objectives.
3.  **Weekly Commissions:** High-reward tasks that reset every Monday.
4.  **City Tycoon:** Management tasks related to your owned businesses.

Focusing on the Participation Tasks tab is the fastest way to see what you need to do immediately after logging in.

## Detailed Explanation

The daily system in Neverness to Everness is designed to reward both combat and "life-sim" activities. This hybrid approach ensures that players who prefer racing or business management can still progress their account level at the same rate as those who focus on clearing anomaly zones.

### Breaking Down Participation Tasks

The Participation system functions on a point scale. You need to reach 100 points daily to claim all rewards. You do not need to complete every single task listed in the Exploration Guide; instead, you can pick and choose the activities that fit your playstyle.

### Managing Character Pixels and City Energy

NTE uses a dual-stamina system to balance different gameplay loops. 

**Character Pixels** are your primary combat stamina. You have a maximum cap of 240 Pixels, which regenerate at a rate of 1 Pixel every 6 minutes (240 every 24 hours). Spending these Pixels on "Instanced Anomalies" or "Weapon Material Nodes" is the fastest way to cap your daily Participation points. If you are short on time, spending 180 Pixels will grant you 60 Participation points instantly, leaving only 40 points to be gained from other activities.

**City Energy** is a separate pool used for "City Tycoon" hobbies. This includes street racing, fishing at the docks, and certain high-stakes arcade games. Using City Energy does not directly grant Participation points for the "Spend Pixels" task, but the activities themselves (like completing a race) often have their own entries in the daily task list.

### The Circle Bounty Battle Pass System

The Circle Bounty is NTE’s version of a battle pass. While the Participation Tasks reset daily, the Circle Bounty tracks your progress over a 40-day season. However, there are "Daily Bounties" within this menu that provide Circle EXP. These often overlap with your Participation Tasks, such as "Clear 3 Combat Encounters." Always check the Circle Bounty tab to ensure you are maximizing your seasonal level while doing your daily routine.

### Daily NPC Interactions: Nacupeda’s Pool and The Witch’s House

There are two specific locations in Hethereau that offer daily buffs or items that are not explicitly listed in the F4 menu but are considered "best practice" for veteran players.

**Nacupeda’s Pool:** Located in the southwestern district of Hethereau, this landmark is home to the NPC Nacupeda. Interacting with the pool once per day allows you to receive a "Hydration Blessing," which provides a minor boost to your Character Pixel regeneration rate for two hours.

**The Witch’s House:** This shop offers a daily rotating stock of rare ingredients. While not a quest, buying out the limited stock of "Anomaly Spices" is necessary for high-level Cafe management in the City Tycoon mode.

### City Tycoon: Cafe Management and Passive Income

The "Cafe by Origen" is the first business you unlock. Daily management is required to keep the Fons flowing. You must
1.  Collect the accumulated profits (capped at 24 hours).
2.  Restock ingredients purchased from the city markets.
3.  Assign characters with the "Service" trait to the floor to increase efficiency.
Completing these steps takes less than two minutes but provides the bulk of your non-combat currency.

## Related

![Related](/img/how-to-unlock-nte-daily-quests/inline-4.webp)


Once you have mastered the daily routine, you must look toward weekly and monthly resets to ensure your characters remain viable for end-game content like the Realm of Greed.

### Weekly Resets and Anomaly Pilgrimages

Every Monday at 5:00 AM, the "Anomaly Pilgrimage" resets. These are world bosses that require Character Pixels to claim rewards but have a limited number of "High-Yield Drops" per week (usually three). You should prioritize these on Mondays or Tuesdays to ensure you don't forget them before the next reset.

**The Realm of Greed** is a tiered combat challenge. Your progress here determines your weekly Annulith income. Unlike daily tasks, the Realm of Greed requires high-level team compositions and specific elemental setups.

### Bonding Encounters and Character Gifting Limits

You can send up to 10 gifts daily to characters in your roster to increase their Affection level. It is highly recommended to prioritize Edgar in the early game, as his Affection rewards include unique weapon components. Higher Affection also unlocks "Bonding Encounters," which are short narrative scenes that grant one-time Annulith rewards.

### Upgrading Anomaly Furniture for Better Daily Drops

Your apartment in Hethereau isn't just for aesthetics. Certain pieces of "Anomaly Furniture" provide daily collectible items.
*   **The Hamster Ball:** Generates a small amount of Fons every 12 hours.
*   **The Cloud Anomaly:** Occasionally drops "Weather Shards" used for crafting.
Upgrading these items through the "Urban Deco" shop increases the quality of the daily drops.

### Advanced City Tycoon: Expanding Your Business Empire

As you progress past the Cafe by Origen, you will unlock car dealerships and boutiques. Each new business adds more tasks to your City Tycoon menu. Managing a full empire can take 10-15 minutes a day but results in millions of Fons, allowing you to purchase luxury vehicles and high-end apartment upgrades without ever needing to grind combat encounters.

### Ultimate NTE Daily, Weekly, and Monthly Routine Checklist

**Daily Checklist:**
- [ ] Log in (20 Participation Points)
- [ ] Collect Cafe by Origen profits and restock ingredients
- [ ] Visit Nacupeda’s Pool for the Hydration Blessing
- [ ] Spend 180+ Character Pixels on Material Nodes (60 Participation Points)
- [ ] Complete 1 City Hobby (Racing or Fishing)
- [ ] Send 10 gifts to Edgar or your preferred Main DPS
- [ ] Claim the 100-point Participation Reward (60 Annulith)

**Weekly Checklist:**
- [ ] Clear 3 Anomaly Pilgrimage Bosses
- [ ] Reach the highest possible floor in the Realm of Greed
- [ ] Complete 5 Special City Commissions from the Old Mailbox
- [ ] Buy the weekly limit of "Limited Tuning Parts" from the shop

**Monthly Checklist:**
- [ ] Purchase the 5 discounted "Standard Pulls" from the Annulith Exchange
- [ ] Complete the Circle Bounty seasonal track (if nearing the 40-day mark)

By following this structured approach, you will maintain a steady flow of Hunter EXP and resources, ensuring you are always ready for the next content update in Neverness to Everness.

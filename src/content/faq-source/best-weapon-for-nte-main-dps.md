## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/best-weapon-for-nte-main-dps/inline-3.webp)


The combat system in Neverness to Everness revolves around the "Arc" system. Unlike traditional RPGs where weapons are interchangeable, NTE Arcs are categorized into five distinct types: **Plasma, Solid, Gas, Liquid, and Synthesis**. An Esper can only equip an Arc that matches their specific Type. This restriction makes it vital to pull for Arcs that align with your favorite Main DPS characters.

### The 5 Arc Types and Their Roles

Each Arc Type generally follows a specific combat philosophy:
1.  **Plasma:** Primarily offensive, focusing on high-frequency attacks, burst damage, and boss-killing multipliers.
2.  **Solid:** Balanced types that often incorporate "Redirect" mechanics, defensive ignore, or summon-based utility.
3.  **Gas:** Often utility-heavy, focusing on Charge Efficiency, energy restoration, and team-wide ATK buffs.
4.  **Liquid:** Designed for sustained on-field presence, rewarding characters who do not swap out frequently.
5.  **Synthesis:** High-risk, high-reward weapons that often trigger effects based on HP consumption or specific elemental reactions.

### Top-Tier S-Rank Arcs: Stats and Passive Breakdown

#### Marching Beyond Time (S-Rank, Solid)
Released alongside the character Hotori, this Arc has become a staple for the Solid-type meta. At base level, it increases ATK by 16% (scaling up to 32% at Rank 5). Its unique mechanic, **Wastelab**, is triggered when the wearer uses a Redirect Skill. While in this state, the team generates "Wastetime" stacks whenever a Redirect or Support Skill is used. When the wearer finally unleashes their Ultimate, they consume these stacks to gain a massive boost to Ultimate CRIT DMG (up to 48% at max refinement) and a 12–24% DEF Ignore effect.

#### Fluff of Fleetness (S-Rank, Liquid)
Often obtained through the premium Battle Pass or specific high-level Anomaly commissions, Fluff of Fleetness is the definitive "selfish" DPS weapon. It carries a 44% CRIT DMG substat at level 80. Its passive, **Motor Candy**, grants the active character a 5% ATK boost every second they are on the field. This stacks up to 5 times (25% total ATK at Rank 1, or 45% at Rank 5). However, switching characters immediately resets this bonus, making it ideal only for Main DPS units like Mint who anchor the field for 10–15 seconds.

#### Hethereau's Keeper (S-Rank, Solid)
A versatile alternative for those who haven't pulled Marching Beyond Time, Hethereau's Keeper provides a flat 27.5% ATK substat. Its passive increases damage dealt to Bosses by 15–25% and summons **Officer Whisker**, an automated combat drone. Officer Whisker deals off-field damage equal to 100–200% of the wearer's ATK, providing a significant DPS floor even during the Esper's downtime.

#### Camellia Society (S-Rank, Synthesis)
This Arc is built for aggressive players. It grants CRIT Rate as a substat and increases CRIT DMG by 12–20% whenever the wearer’s HP is reduced without taking direct damage (e.g., through self-drain mechanics). It also unlocks the **Silent Garden** Arc skill, which consumes 5% of the wearer's current HP to trigger high-damage "Incantation" bursts like Harsh Rebuke.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/neverness-to-everness-disk-set-guide/inline-2.webp)


Disk Sets in Neverness to Everness (NTE) are the primary equipment system located within the "Console" tab of the Esper menu. This system consists of two distinct components: one central **Cartridge** and multiple **Modules**. The Cartridge acts as the "brain" of the set, providing a primary stat boost and defining the requirements for the 2-piece (Epic) and 4-piece (Legendary) set bonuses.

Modules are "Tetris-style" geometric pieces of varying shapes (Type I, II, III, and IV) that must be manually fitted into the Console’s grid. To activate a Cartridge's set bonuses, you must fit enough Modules of the matching set into the grid. For example, to trigger a 4-piece bonus, four separate Modules belonging to that specific set must be successfully placed within the limited grid space.

As of the current Version 1.0 meta, the best DPS sets are **Lost Radiance** (ideal for Crit-scaling characters) and **Devil’s Blood: Curse** (optimized for characters relying on the Scorch reaction). For Supports, **Speedy Hedgehog** is the universal choice due to its impact on Energy Regen and cooldown reduction. Farming for these items is concentrated in the **Rabbit Hole** (Anomaly Zones), where players spend Energy to clear combat challenges. Additionally, the **Rewind** system, fueled by **Bronze Carrotas**, allows players to re-roll specific Module attributes to minimize the impact of RNG.

The Console system is the most intricate part of character building in Neverness to Everness. It moves away from the "six-slot" gear model seen in many contemporary RPGs, instead challenging players to manage spatial constraints alongside stat optimization.

### Understanding the Console: Cartridges vs. Modules

Every Esper has a Console that serves as their equipment motherboard. This motherboard has a grid—the size of which is [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-24]—where all gear must be placed.

1.  **The Cartridge:** This is the core item. It occupies a central, non-removable position (conceptually) but can be swapped out for different set types. The Cartridge itself provides a massive Main Stat (such as Elemental DMG Bonus or ATK%) and lists the 2-piece and 4-piece passives that can be unlocked.
2.  **The Modules:** These are the individual "blocks" you collect. Each Module has its own Main Stat and a set of Substats. Modules come in four shapes, known as Type I, Type II, Type III, and Type IV. Because the grid space is limited, you cannot simply equip the "best" Modules; you must equip the Modules that actually fit together without overlapping.

### The Tetris Mechanic: How to Activate Set Bonuses

Activating a set bonus is not as simple as clicking "Equip All." You must engage with the Tetris mechanic. Each Module shape corresponds to a specific number of tiles on the Console grid:

*   **Type I:** Usually a compact 2x2 square or a small L-shape.
*   **Type II:** Often a 3-tile straight line or a T-shape.
*   **Type III:** Larger 4-tile shapes that are harder to place.
*   **Type IV:** Complex 5-tile or irregular shapes that offer the highest base stats but consume the most grid real estate.

To activate a 2-piece bonus, two Modules of the same set name as the Cartridge must be placed on the grid. To activate the 4-piece bonus, four Modules must be placed. The challenge lies in the fact that high-rarity (Legendary) Modules often have larger, more awkward shapes. Min-maxing involves finding "Type I" or "Type II" Modules with perfect substats so that you can fit all four required pieces while leaving room for "off-set" Modules that might have god-tier stats.

### Farming Guide: Rabbit Hole and the Rewind System

To obtain high-quality Disks, you must enter the **Rabbit Hole**. These are Anomaly Zones scattered across the city that function as repeatable combat instances. 

*   **Anomaly Tiers:** As your Urban Level increases, you unlock higher tiers of the Rabbit Hole. Tier 5 and above are the only reliable sources for Legendary (Gold) Modules and Cartridges.
*   **The Rewind System:** One of the most player-friendly features in NTE is the Rewind system. By using **Bronze Carrotas** (a currency earned through daily commissions and Urban Management), you can "Rewind" a Module. This allows you to reset the enhancement levels of a Module and recoup a portion of the experience materials, or in some cases, attempt to swap a single Substat.
*   **The Preview Feature:** Before committing resources to a Module upgrade, the "Preview" feature allows you to see the potential range of stat increases. This significantly reduces the RNG frustration common in gear-heavy RPGs.

When building your Espers, such as **Skia** or **[Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)**, you must prioritize specific stats on your Modules. While the Cartridge provides the bulk of your power, the cumulative Substats from four or five Modules define your ceiling.

Arcs (the weapons of NTE) often have passives that trigger based on your stats. The synergy between a Legendary Arc and a matching 4-piece Cartridge set is the key to tackling Version 1.0 end-game content.

### Advanced Combat Mechanics
Understanding Elemental Reactions is vital for choosing the right set.
*   **Scorch:** Triggered by Fire-based anomalies; significantly buffed by the **Devil’s Blood: Curse** set.
*   **Stain:** A debuff that increases the damage enemies take from subsequent hits.
*   **Nova:** A high-impact burst reaction that occurs when specific elements collide.

By tailoring your Cartridge and Modules to these reactions, you can clear Rabbit Hole instances much faster, leading to more efficient farming and faster progression through the City Tycoon rewards system.

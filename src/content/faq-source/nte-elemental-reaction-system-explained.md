## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-elemental-reaction-system-explained/inline-2.webp)


The elemental reaction system in NTE is formally called the "Esper Cycle." Unlike other open-world RPGs where any two elements might interact, NTE utilizes a hexagonal "Esper Wheel" where reactions only occur between adjacent elements.

There are six primary elements (Espers) in the game:
1.  **Lakshana** (Yellow/Gold)
2.  **Cosmos** (Blue/Silver)
3.  **Anima** (Green/Teal)
4.  **Incantation** (Red/Crimson)
5.  **Chaos** (Purple/Violet)
6.  **Psyche** (Pink/Magenta)

To trigger a reaction, you must fill an active character’s Esper Meter to 100% and then swap them out for a character whose element is adjacent on the wheel. These reactions are categorized into Duo Cycles (involving two adjacent elements) and Triple Cycles (involving a chain of three adjacent elements). Duo Cycles generally provide status effects, damage-over-time (DoT), or localized explosions, while Triple Cycles trigger powerful team-wide buffs like "Charge" or massive shield-shredding effects like "Discord."

### The Core Rule: Adjacency

The most critical factor to remember in NTE combat is the rule of adjacency. If you attempt to swap from a Lakshana character to an Incantation character, no elemental reaction will occur because they are opposite each other on the hexagonal wheel. You must follow the perimeter of the wheel. For example, Lakshana can only react with Cosmos or Psyche. This restriction makes team composition vital, as a "broken" wheel in your party lineup will significantly lower your total damage per second (DPS).

### Advanced Triple Cycles: Charge and Discord

Triple Cycles are the pinnacle of NTE combat. These are triggered by completing a three-element sequence in order around the wheel. These cycles do not just affect the enemy; they provide massive systemic advantages to your entire party.

#### The "Charge" Cycle (Top Trio)
**Sequence:** Lakshana → Cosmos → Anima (or reverse).
The Top Trio focuses on sustainability and energy. When you complete this cycle, you trigger **Charge**. This state grants the party 100% increased energy regeneration for 15 seconds and instantly refreshes the cooldown of the first character's Skill. In the current meta, using [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) (Anima) to close a Charge cycle is a popular strategy for maintaining high uptime on her powerful ultimate.

#### The "Discord" Cycle (Bottom Trio)
**Sequence:** Incantation → Chaos → Psyche (or reverse).
The Bottom Trio is designed for destruction. Completing this sequence triggers **Discord**. This reaction deals a massive blow to an enemy’s "Anomaly Bar" (the Break/Stagger gauge). Against bosses, a Discord cycle can often deplete 50% of a shield instantly. Furthermore, while Discord is active, all "Chaos" and "Incantation" damage ignore a portion of the enemy's defense.

### Managing the Esper Meter and Cycle Rate

To trigger these reactions, your characters must have a full Esper Meter. This meter is the circular gauge surrounding the character's icon on the right side of the screen.

**Esper Meter Generation Rates:**
*   **Normal Attacks:** Generates undisclosed energy per hit.
*   **Elemental Skills:** Generates roughly 25% to 40% of the meter, depending on the character's specific "Cycle Rate" stat.
*   **Elemental Ultimates:** Instantly fills 50% of the *next* character's meter in the party order.
*   **Perfect Parry:** This is the most efficient way to play. A successful parry instantly fills the active character's Esper Meter to 100%, allowing for an immediate reaction swap.

The "Cycle Rate" is a secondary stat found on weapons and gear. Investing in Cycle Rate allows your characters to fill their meters with fewer attacks, which is essential for "Quick-Swap" team compositions.

### Team Building Around the Esper Wheel

When building a team, you should aim for "Reaction Synergy." A common mistake is picking three powerful characters who cannot trigger cycles with each other.

**The "Blossom" Meta (Anima/Cosmos Focus):**
Currently, the strongest general-use composition involves pairing **Nanally** (Anima) with **Jiuyuan** (Cosmos). Because Anima and Cosmos are adjacent, swapping between them constantly triggers the Blossom reaction. Blossom is favored because it provides both AoE damage and self-healing, making it incredibly safe for clearing high-level "Urban Anomaly" zones.

**The "Insta-Cycle" Mechanic (Zero):**
The character **Zero** (Chaos) is unique due to his passive ability. Zero can bridge rotations that would otherwise be impossible. If Zero is in your party, his "Insta-Cycle" passive allows him to trigger a Duo Cycle even if his Esper Meter is only at 50%, provided the previous character exited the field via an Ultimate. This makes Zero the premier "Bridge" character for maintaining 100% reaction uptime.

### Pro Tips for High-Level Rotations

1.  **The Ultimate Lead:** Always start a boss fight by using an Ultimate on your support character. Because Ultimates grant energy to the next character, this jumpstarts your cycle without needing to farm energy through normal attacks.
2.  **Passive Stacking:** Characters like Nanally and Jiuyuan offer off-field buffs. Always check your character's "Resonance" passives to see if they reward specific cycles.
3.  **Breaking Shields with Discord:** If a boss has a heavy elemental shield, do not waste your Lakshana or Cosmos skills. Swap immediately to your Bottom Trio (Incantation, Chaos, Psyche) to force a Discord reaction. The Break damage from Discord is significantly higher than raw damage against shielded targets.

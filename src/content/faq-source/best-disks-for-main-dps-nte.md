### Defining 'Disks' in NTE: Arcs vs. Console Modules

New players often confuse "disks" with the various components in the gear menu. To clarify, Arcs (often called disks by the community due to their circular design) are the primary weapons. Each Esper can equip one Arc. These are categorized into five distinct types: Plasma, Liquid, Gas, Solid, and Synthesis. 

Conversely, the Console system utilizes Cartridges and Modules. While Arcs provide the foundation of your attack power and unique mechanics, the Console Modules act as the "Tetris" gear system that fills in your secondary stats like Crit Rate and Elemental Bonus. A Main DPS requires a high-rarity Arc that matches their innate element to unlock powerful passive resonances.

### Why Your Choice of Arc Disk Determines Endgame Viability

In NTE, [damage scaling](https://ntecodex.com/faq) is not linear. The game utilizes a "Type Resonance" mechanic where equipping an Arc that matches the Esper's type (e.g., a Liquid Arc on a Liquid Esper) grants a significant percentage-based multiplier to all damage dealt. If you equip an S-Rank Arc of the wrong type, you lose out on the [core passive](https://ntecodex.com/faq), often resulting in lower total damage than a well-matched A-Rank Arc.

Furthermore, Main DPS characters rely on specific triggers—such as Baicang’s HP consumption or Daffodil’s Break state transitions—which are only facilitated by top-tier Arcs. Without the correct disk, your Esper Cycles will feel sluggish, and your "Ultimate Redirect" skills will fail to hit the necessary damage thresholds for boss phases.

### Arc Disk Mechanics: Stats, Scaling, and Refinement

Every Arc Disk has a Primary Stat (usually ATK) and a Secondary Stat (Crit Rate, Crit DMG, Elemental Bonus, or Break Strength). For a Main DPS, you should almost always prioritize Arcs with Crit-based secondary stats.

Refinement is the process of consuming duplicate Arcs to upgrade the disk's passive ability. For S-Rank Arcs, R3 (Refinement 3) is widely considered the "sweet spot" for spenders, as it typically doubles the effectiveness of the base passive. For example, at R1, Camellia Society might grant 1% Crit DMG per 100 HP lost, while at R3, this value increases significantly, allowing Baicang to hit the Crit DMG cap much faster.

### Character-Specific Best-in-Slot (BiS) Breakdowns

**Baicang Build Deep Dive:**
Baicang functions as a Synthesis carry who thrives on the edge of defeat. His "Power Word" mechanic detonates based on the amount of HP he has lost during his field time. Camellia Society is essential because it provides the necessary Crit DMG to make those detonations hit for millions. Without this disk, Baicang’s damage falls off sharply in the late game, as his base multipliers aren't high enough to carry without the disk's stacking buff.

**Daffodil and the Break Meta:**
Daffodil is designed to punish enemies once their guard is broken. Youthful Fantasy triggers the "Black Book" effect, which is a separate instance of Chaos damage. In the current meta, Chaos damage ignores a portion of the enemy's flat defense, making it the most effective way to deal with armored bosses in the Anomaly zones.

**Nanally and the Anima Carry:**
Nanally uses anti-gravity mechanics to stay airborne, avoiding most ground-based boss attacks. Using "Ichi-daime's Authority" or the F2P "Day Off" allows her to maintain her combo meter. Her damage scales with the number of hits landed while in the air, so Arcs that provide attack speed or combo window extensions are highly valued.

### The 'Tetris' Gear System: Maximizing Console Modules

The Console is where you fine-tune your Main DPS stats. It uses a grid-based system where you must fit different shaped Modules (Type I, II, and III). 

To reach the competitive 50% Crit Rate threshold, you should aim for Type III Modules. In the current version, Type III Modules provide a flat 8% Crit Rate bonus per piece if they are part of a matching set. By carefully "Tetris-ing" these pieces into your Console grid, you can stack enough Crit Rate to allow your Arc Disk to focus entirely on Crit DMG or Elemental scaling.

For Cartridges, the "Crimson: Twin Butterflies" set is the current meta for Baicang, providing additional Synthesis damage. Daffodil players should opt for the "Diabolos" set, which increases damage dealt to enemies with low Break gauges.

### Farming Guide: Where to Find Upgrade Materials

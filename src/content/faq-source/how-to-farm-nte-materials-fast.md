## Detailed Explanation

### Managing the Dual Stamina System: Pixels vs. City Stamina

Character Pixels are your most valuable resource for combat progression. They regenerate at a rate of 1 Pixel every 6 minutes, totaling 240 Pixels over a 24-hour period. Because the cap is set at 240, you must spend them at least once per day to prevent waste. If you are short on time, using the Double Reward (80 Pixels) option allows you to empty a full bar in just three runs.

City Stamina, often referred to as Vigor, operates on a different logic. Unlike Pixels, which regenerate hourly, your Vigor pool is largely tied to weekly resets (refreshing every Monday at 04:00 server time). Vigor is spent on the "Urban" side of NTE, such as managing your business properties in the Tycoon system or participating in street races. Farming Fons through City Stamina is vital because high-level character upgrades require significant amounts of currency that Pixel-based farming alone cannot sustain.

### Anomaly Zones: The Core Hubs for Growth Materials

Anomaly Zones are the instanced domains where the bulk of your farming occurs. 
*   **Houdini’s Magic Stage:** This should be your primary focus during the first two weeks of play. Character level is the most significant gate for content difficulty. Stockpiling Hunter Guides ensures that when you pull a new character like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or Mint, you can instantly bring them to parity with your world level.
*   **Bubble Can Factory:** Arcs function as the "weapons" or "equipment" of NTE. Farming the Bubble Can Factory is necessary to level up these Arcs. Note that S-Rank Arcs require significantly more materials, so farming this zone becomes more important in the mid-to-late game.

### Unlocking and Maximizing Auto-Sweep with Mr. Yugardi

Mr. Yugardi, your Butler, is the most important NPC for farming efficiency. By navigating to the Butler menu, you can upgrade his "Service Level" using Arcane Threads—a currency obtained through missions and exploration milestones. 

Once Mr. Yugardi reaches Level 3, the Auto-Sweep function for Anomaly Zones is unlocked. This allows you to skip the combat phase of any domain you have previously cleared with a 3-star rating. At higher levels, Mr. Yugardi can also perform "Bulk Sweeps," allowing you to spend all 240 Pixels with a single button press.

### Best Exploration Characters for Fast World Farming

While domains provide the bulk of materials, character ascension requires specific "Mob Drops" found in the open world of Hethereau. To farm these fast, you need a team optimized for traversal:
*   **Nanally:** Her anti-gravity skill allows her to run directly up the sides of skyscrapers in the Bridge Crossings district, bypassing complex elevator puzzles or slow climbing.
*   **Hathor:** Essential for crossing the gaps between the floating blocks in the Anomaly-affected zones. Her gliding efficiency reduces Exploration Stamina consumption by [information unavailable as of 2026-05-22].
*   **Sakura:** Her [dash speed](https://ntecodex.com/guides/nte-vs-zzz-character-dash-speed-gear-guide) is currently the highest in the game for ground-level traversal, making her ideal for chasing down fleeing "Glitch Mobs."

### Farming Beetle Coins and Fons for the Urban Economy

Fons are the standard gold equivalent, but Beetle Coins are a specialized currency used for high-end car parts and apartment customizations. 
1.  **The Tycoon System:** Invest your Vigor into the "Convenience Store" and "Noodle Shop" properties early. These provide a passive income of Fons every hour.
2.  **Street Racing:** Participating in the "Hethereau Drift" weekly events is the fastest way to earn Beetle Coins. 
3.  **Part-Time Jobs:** If you have excess Vigor before the weekly reset, spend it on the "Delivery" mini-game near the Central Station for a quick Fons injection.

### Shop Resets and Exchange Strategy: Warp vs. Lost Pieces

The in-game exchange shop resets monthly. You should always prioritize purchasing the discounted "Warp Tickets" (pulls) using your "Lost Pieces" (stardust equivalent). After securing your pulls, spend the remaining currency on "Elite Hunter Guides" and "S-Rank Arc Refiners." Avoid buying basic materials like Dyes or common mob drops, as these are easily farmed for free using Character Pixels or exploration.

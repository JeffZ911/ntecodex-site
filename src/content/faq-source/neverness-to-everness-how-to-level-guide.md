## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/neverness-to-everness-how-to-level-guide/inline-1.webp)


Many new players entering the urban landscape of Hethereau find themselves confused by the layered progression systems. Unlike traditional RPGs where a single experience bar dictates power, this game splits growth into several distinct categories. Players often ask:

*   **What is the difference between Hunter Level and Esper Level in Neverness to Everness?** Hunter Level represents your overall account rank and determines which story chapters and features you can access. Esper Level is the individual strength of your characters, which is capped by your current account progression.
*   **How do I unlock higher level caps for my characters and weapons?** You cannot simply grind experience to reach max level; you must complete specific Breakthrough Quests that raise your Appraisal Level (World Level) at key milestones.
*   **What are the most efficient ways to farm EXP materials in Hethereau?** Efficiency depends on managing your Character Pixels (stamina) and targeting specific Anomaly Zones like Houdinii's Magic Stage for character EXP or the Bubble Can Factory for weapon materials.

### Understanding the Dual-Leveling System

The progression in Neverness to Everness functions through a "gate and key" mechanic. Your Hunter Level acts as the primary gate. As you earn Hunter EXP from quests and stamina consumption, your Hunter Level rises. However, your characters (Espers) have their own independent levels. An Esper cannot exceed the level cap dictated by your current Appraisal Level. This prevents players from over-leveling a single character to bypass the difficulty of world encounters and boss fights.

### The Role of Appraisal Levels

Appraisal Levels function similarly to "World Levels" in other open-world urban fantasies. Increasing your Appraisal Level makes enemies throughout Hethereau more difficult but also significantly improves the quality and quantity of loot dropped. Most importantly, reaching a new Appraisal Rank is the only way to "Ascend" your Espers and Arcs (weapons), allowing them to move past level caps such as 20, 40, or 60.

## Direct Answer

To level up fast in Neverness to Everness, you must prioritize activities that grant Hunter EXP to raise your account rank while simultaneously farming materials for your core team of Espers.

1.  **Hunter Level (Account Rank):** Complete all available Main Story quests (Urban Anomalies) first. Once the story is gated by level, complete your Daily Commissions and spend your **Character Pixels** (stamina) in Anomaly Zones. Every 10 Character Pixels spent typically yields 100 Hunter EXP.
2.  **Esper Level (Characters):** Navigate to the Esper development menu and use **Hunter Guides** (Rising, Senior, or Elite) along with **Beetle Coins**. You do not gain significant character level experience from open-world combat; manual leveling via these items is mandatory.
3.  **Appraisal Level (World Level):** When you hit Hunter Levels 20, 40, and 50, you will receive a **Breakthrough Quest**. Completing this quest is the only way to raise your Appraisal Level and bypass the current level caps for your characters.
4.  **Arc Level (Weapons):** Use **Dye** to increase the level of your Arcs. To ascend Arcs past level caps, farm materials from the **Bubble Can Factory** Anomaly Zone.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/neverness-to-everness-how-to-level-guide/inline-3.webp)


Progression in Hethereau is a marathon rather than a sprint. To maximize your efficiency, you must understand how each system feeds into the others.

### How to Increase Hunter Level (Account Rank)

Hunter Level (HL) is the most important metric for your account. It dictates your access to the narrative and high-tier farming zones.

*   **Main Story Quests:** These provide the largest chunks of Hunter EXP. You should follow the "Urban Anomaly" questline until you hit a level requirement.
*   **Daily Commissions:** These reset every 24 hours and offer a consistent stream of Hunter EXP and premium currency. They are the most time-efficient way to level up.
*   **Spending Character Pixels:** Whether you are farming for Hunter Guides or Arc materials, spending your stamina always contributes to your Hunter Level. Never let your Character Pixels sit at the 240 cap.
*   **Side Urban Stories:** These smaller quests found throughout the city districts provide moderate EXP and help flesh out the lore of Hethereau.

### Raising Appraisal Level and Breakthrough Quests

Your Appraisal Level determines the "ceiling" for your entire account. If you do not complete your Breakthrough Quests, your Hunter Level will stop increasing once it hits the cap for that rank.

### Esper Progression: Leveling, Ascension, and Abilities

Espers require three different types of "growth" to remain viable in late-game Anomaly Zones.

1.  **Leveling:** Using Hunter Guides to reach the current cap.
2.  **Ascension:** Using boss-specific materials (obtained from high-level Anomaly bosses) to "break" the level cap once your Appraisal Rank allows it.
3.  **Abilities:** You must also level up individual skills (Basic Attack, Skill, Ultimate). These require specific "Tactical Records" found in combat challenges.

**Hunter Guide EXP Values:**
*   **Rising Hunter Guide:** 1,000 EXP
*   **Senior Hunter Guide:** 5,000 EXP
*   **Elite Hunter Guide:** 20,000 EXP

Arcs are the primary source of Attack and Critical stats for your Espers. Leveling an Arc requires **Dye**, which comes in various rarities.

### The Console System: Cartridges and Modules

The Console System is the "gear" or "artifact" system of Neverness to Everness. It is unlocked after completing the early chapters in the Hethereau business district.

*   **Cartridges:** These act as the base for your Console. They provide primary stats like HP, ATK, or DEF.
*   **Modules:** These are "Tetris-style" blocks that you fit into the Cartridge grid. 
*   **Optimization:** The goal is to fit as many high-value Modules as possible into the grid. Modules have different shapes (L-shape, T-shape, squares), and matching specific Module colors can trigger "Resonance" bonuses for extra elemental damage or energy regeneration.

### City Tycoon Leveling: Fons and Life Skills

Separate from the combat-focused Hunter Level is the **City Tycoon** system. This represents your influence and business success within Hethereau.

*   **Fons:** The currency used for buying properties, customizing cars, and managing businesses.
*   **City Stamina:** Unlike Character Pixels, which regenerate daily, City Stamina is a weekly pool used for "Life Skill" activities like fishing, specialized crafting, and business management.
*   **Life Skills:** Leveling up skills like "Negotiation" or "Engineering" allows you to earn Fons more quickly and unlock exclusive vehicle upgrades.

### Efficient Stamina Management: Character Pixels vs. City Stamina

To level up fast, you must manage two different energy economies:

1.  **Character Pixels (Combat Stamina):**
    *   Regeneration: 1 every 6 minutes.
    *   Max Cap: 240.
    *   Priority: Use these for Hunter Guides and Ascension materials. Avoid using them for "Gold" (Beetle Coins) unless you are completely out, as the world exploration rewards usually provide enough for early levels.

2.  **City Stamina (Management Stamina):**
    *   Regeneration: Weekly reset.
    *   Priority: Use these to progress your business ventures. This does not help your Hunter Level directly but is the only way to afford high-end gear and car parts.

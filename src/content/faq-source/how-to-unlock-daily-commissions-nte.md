Many new Appraisers in Hethereau are looking for the 'Daily Commissions' system to start earning consistent rewards. As you navigate the neon-lit streets and confront the strange phenomena known as Anomalies, you will quickly realize that standard story rewards only go so far. To maintain a steady stream of premium currency and upgrade materials, accessing the daily gameplay loop is mandatory.

In Neverness to Everness (NTE), this system is officially known as 'Daily Quests' and 'Participation Tasks.' Unlike some open-world RPGs that grant daily objectives the moment you finish the tutorial, NTE ties its daily routine to specific narrative milestones within the city's districts. 

Players often wonder why the Exploration Guide or daily objectives menu is missing during the first few hours of gameplay. If you are currently exploring Bridge Crossings and cannot find a list of tasks to complete, it is likely because you have not yet reached the necessary chapter in the Appraiser’s Journey. This guide clarifies the exact story milestones and level requirements needed to access your daily routine.

### What are Daily Commissions in NTE?
Daily Commissions in NTE serve as the primary source of Hunter EXP and Annulith (the premium currency used for character and weapon banners). The system is comprised of two distinct but overlapping mechanics:
1.  **Daily Quests:** Four fixed tasks that refresh every 24 hours, focusing on combat or simple urban interactions.
2.  **Participation Tasks:** A broader checklist of activities—ranging from driving your vehicle to managing your business—that contribute to a daily "Participation" score.

### Why can't I see my Daily Quests yet?
If the "Exploration Guide" icon (located in the top-right of your HUD) only shows basic tutorials or world exploration percentages, you are still in the early tutorial phase of the Prologue. The daily system is locked behind the introduction of the Eibon Antique Shop, which serves as your operational base. You must progress the main narrative until the game formally introduces the concept of professional "Anomaly Hunting" for hire.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-unlock-daily-commissions-nte/inline-2.webp)


To unlock Daily Commissions (Daily Quests) in NTE, you must complete the Main Story Quest (MSQ) 'Prologue - Part 2'. This specific chapter involves the investigation of the 'Film Anomaly' in the Bridge Crossings district. 

The feature automatically becomes available in the 'Exploration Guide' menu once you finish the 'Film Anomaly' investigation and speak with the Eibon Antique Shop owner. After this conversation, a notification will appear on your screen announcing that "Daily Assignments" are now active. 

Daily Quests reset globally every day at 5:00 AM (UTC+8). There is no specific Hunter Level requirement to unlock this feature, but due to the length of the Prologue, most players reach this point around Hunter Level 10-12.

### The Unlock Milestone: Prologue Part 2
The 'Prologue - Part 2' is a significant story beat where you move beyond simple street-level encounters and begin working with the official Anomaly Hunter organizations. The climax of this segment is the 'Film Anomaly' quest, a scripted sequence where you must enter a distorted reality based on a haunted cinema. Upon successfully clearing this zone and returning to the Eibon Antique Shop, the owner will grant you access to the city’s official Hunter network, which populates your Exploration Guide with daily tasks.

### Where to Find the Daily Menu
Once unlocked, you can access your dailies by opening the main menu and selecting the **Exploration Guide**. Inside this menu, look for the tab labeled "Daily Quests" or "Participation." This interface tracks your progress toward the 100-point daily goal and lists the four specific quests assigned to you for the day.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-unlock-daily-commissions-nte/inline-3.webp)


The path to unlocking dailies involves following the 'Appraiser's Journey' through the early streets of Bridge Crossings. This initial phase of the game is designed to familiarize you with the dual nature of Hethereau: the mundane urban life and the supernatural Anomaly zones.

### Step-by-Step Story Progression Guide
To reach the unlock point efficiently, focus exclusively on the gold-colored Main Story Quest markers.
1.  **Complete Prologue - Part 1:** This involves meeting [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) and [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide) and learning the basic combat mechanics.
2.  **Enter Bridge Crossings:** Once you gain free access to the city, follow the marker to the Eibon Antique Shop.
3.  **Resolve the 'Film Anomaly' incident:** This quest serves as the final tutorial for the Anomaly Hunter mechanics and vehicle usage. During this quest, you will unlock your first vehicle, the **Rover A1**, which is required to navigate the city quickly.
4.  **Final Report:** Return to the Eibon Antique Shop. After the dialogue concludes, the "Exploration Guide" icon will flash, indicating that Daily Quests are now live.

### Understanding the 4 Core Daily Quests
Every day, the Eibon network assigns you four randomized quests. These are usually short tasks that take less than five minutes each. Completing these is the most efficient way to gain Hunter EXP early in the game.

**Table: The 4 Core Daily Quests and EXP Rewards**
| Quest Type | Objective Example | Hunter EXP Reward |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Combat | Defeat 5 Anomalies in Bridge Crossings | 250 EXP |
| Interaction | Purchase a beverage from a vending machine | 250 EXP |
| Exploration | Travel 1,000 meters in a vehicle | 250 EXP |
| Commission | Talk to a specific NPC in the central plaza | 250 EXP |
| **Total** | **4 Quests Completed** | **1,000 EXP** |

### The Participation Point System Explained
While the four Daily Quests provide raw EXP, the **Participation Point** system is where you earn your primary rewards, including Annulith (pull currency). You do not necessarily have to complete the four specific Daily Quests to earn Participation Points, though they are the fastest way to do so.

You earn points by engaging in various urban activities. Reaching 100 Participation Points is the primary goal for daily rewards like Annulith, Fons (gold), and Beetle Coins.

**Common Participation Tasks and Point Values:**
*   Complete a Daily Quest: 20 Points
*   Spend 30 Character Pixels (Stamina): 20 Points
*   Complete an Anomaly Zone challenge: 30 Points
*   Modify a vehicle at a garage: 10 Points
*   Collect revenue from a City Tycoon business: 10 Points
*   Win a street race: 15 Points

### Managing Character Pixels for Daily Completion
Character Pixels (Stamina) play a crucial role in your daily routine. Spending them in Anomaly Zones to farm for upgrade materials or Arcs is the fastest way to complete your daily activity requirements. Since spending 30 Pixels grants 20 Participation Points, using your entire daily cap of Pixels can often complete 60% to 80% of your daily requirements without even touching the specific Daily Quests.

### Best Daily Routine for Maximum Efficiency
To finish your dailies in under 15 minutes, follow this sequence:
1.  **Login and check the Old Mailbox:** Collect any overnight rewards or City Tycoon income.
2.  **Spend Character Pixels:** Run 2-3 Anomaly Zones for materials. This will usually net you 40-60 Participation Points.
3.  **Complete the 2 easiest Daily Quests:** Usually the "Interaction" or "Exploration" ones. This will push you to 100 points.
4.  **Claim Rewards:** Open the Exploration Guide and click the chests at the bottom to claim your Annulith.

## Related

Beyond the Exploration Guide, several other 'hidden' dailies provide essential progression materials that are not explicitly listed in the Participation menu. These "Urban Dailies" are vital for players looking to min-max their account progression.

### Nacupeda's Pool: Mhm! Coins and S-Rank Arcs
Located in the southwestern district of Bridge Crossings, Nacupeda's Pool is a landmark where players can interact with the water once per day.
*   **Action:** 'Make a Sincere Wish.'
*   **Rewards:** You can receive Mhm! Coins (used for specialized gacha machines) or rare furniture items for your apartment. There is a small chance to obtain high-tier Arcs (equipment) through this interaction.

### The Witch's House Fortunes
The Witch's House is a small shop tucked away in an alleyway near the Eibon Antique Shop. Visiting the NPC here daily allows you to receive a fortune reading.
*   **Combat Buffs:** Some fortunes provide a 5% damage increase to specific elements for 2 hours.
*   **Lost Tales:** Occasionally, the fortune will reveal a "Lost Tale," which is a hidden world quest that doesn't appear on the standard map.

### City Tycoon: Daily Cafe Management
The City Tycoon system is the business management side of NTE. Once you unlock the 'Cafe by Origen,' you must check in daily.
*   **Passive Income:** Collect Fons generated by the cafe over the last 24 hours.
*   **Restock:** Ensure ingredients are stocked so the business continues to run while you are offline.
*   **Employee Morale:** Interacting with the staff can provide small bonuses to the cafe's efficiency.

### Weekly Commissions vs. Daily Quests
Once you reach Tycoon Level 2, you can access the 'Old Mailbox' in your apartment to accept Weekly Special City Commissions. These are much longer than Daily Quests and often involve multi-stage investigations across Hethereau. While Daily Quests focus on Hunter EXP and Annulith, Weekly Commissions are the primary source of high-end vehicle parts and apartment customization unlocks.

**Additional Daily Checklist (NPCs and Shops):**
*   **The Convenience Store:** Purchase the daily discounted 'Pixel Soda' to restore 30 Character Pixels.
*   **The Garage:** Check for the "Daily Special" car paint or decal.
*   **Vending Machines:** Some machines have a once-per-day "Jackpot" chance when buying any drink.

By integrating these urban activities with your standard Exploration Guide tasks, you can maximize your Hunter's growth and fully experience the depth of life in Hethereau.

In the neon-drenched streets of Hethereau, your survival depends as much on your equipment as it does on your Esper’s innate abilities. Mastering the weapon system is the first step toward dominating the high-stakes combat of Neverness to Everness (NTE).

# Neverness to Everness Weapon Guide: Best Arcs and Gacha System

Navigating the equipment landscape in Neverness to Everness requires a firm understanding of Arcs—the specialized disks that serve as the game’s primary weapon system. Unlike traditional RPGs where you might equip a sword or a rifle, NTE uses these advanced data-disks to channel an Esper’s power, providing massive stat boosts and unique passive abilities that can define a character’s role in a team.

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/neverness-to-everness-weapon-guide/inline-1.webp)


When diving into the gear progression of Neverness to Everness, players typically find themselves asking several core questions:

*   **What are the different types of weapons (Arcs) in Neverness to Everness?** Understanding the five distinct categories—Plasma, Solid, Gas, Liquid, and Synthesis—is vital because Arcs are type-locked to specific Esper classes.
*   **How does the weapon gacha (Arc Research Program) and pity system work?** Players need to know how to efficiently spend their Tri-Keys and Analith to secure S-Rank Arcs without wasting resources.
*   **Which S-Rank Arcs are considered the best for the current meta?** identifying top-tier options like Ready-Ready or Hethereau's Keeper allows for focused investment.
*   **How can players upgrade and refine their Arcs to increase combat power?** Beyond just leveling, systems like Refinement (Mixing Levels) and Ascension are necessary for endgame content.

This guide provides a comprehensive breakdown of these systems to ensure your arsenal is ready for the anomalies lurking in the city.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/neverness-to-everness-weapon-guide/inline-2.webp)


Weapons in Neverness to Everness are officially known as **Arcs**. These are equippable disks categorized into five distinct types: **Plasma, Solid, Gas, Liquid, and Synthesis**. Each Esper is compatible with only one of these types, meaning you must match the Arc type to the character's class to equip it. Arcs provide three layers of power: a base ATK stat that scales with level, a secondary substat (such as CRIT Rate, ATK%, or Break Intensity), and a unique passive known as an **Arc Skill**.

The primary method for obtaining high-tier weapons is through the **Arc Research Program** (the weapon gacha), which utilizes a currency called **Tri-Keys**. This system features a generous pity mechanic: an S-Rank Arc is guaranteed within **60 pulls**, and the featured limited-time signature weapon is guaranteed by the **80th pull**.

As of the current meta, the most coveted S-Rank Arcs include **Ready-Ready** (the signature weapon for [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)) for its massive boss-killing potential, **Camellia Society** for high-risk CRIT builds, and **Hethereau's Keeper** for its unique summon-based utility.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/neverness-to-everness-weapon-guide/inline-3.webp)


### Arc Fundamentals: Stats, Substats, and Compatibility

Every weapon in Neverness to Everness is an Arc. When you view an Arc in your inventory, the first thing to check is its **Compatibility**. If an Esper is a Plasma-type, they can only equip Plasma Arcs. Equipping an Arc provides an immediate boost to your **Base ATK**, which is the foundation for all damage calculations.

Secondary substats vary significantly between Arcs. For damage dealers, you generally want Arcs that provide **CRIT Rate** or **CRIT DMG**. For supports or "Breakers," substats like **Break Intensity** or **Charge Efficiency** (which speeds up Ultimate Energy gain) are often more valuable.

The most transformative part of any Arc is its **Arc Skill**. These passives can range from simple ATK buffs to complex mechanics that summon companions or create zones on the battlefield. These skills are improved through **Refinement**, which requires consuming duplicate copies of the same Arc to increase its **Mixing Level** (up to Rank 5).

### The Five Arc Types and Their Strategic Roles

1.  **Plasma (Offense/Burst):** These Arcs are designed for your primary damage dealers. They typically feature high base ATK and skills that reward aggressive playstyles, such as boosting damage after a Redirect Skill or Ultimate.
2.  **Solid (Durability/Utility):** Solid Arcs are often used by characters who need to stay on the field for extended periods or provide team-wide utility. Many Solid Arcs, like **Day Off**, focus on energy restoration or defensive buffs.
3.  **Gas (Support/Energy):** Gas Arcs are the backbone of team synergy. They often provide buffs that persist even when the wearer is off-field, making them ideal for sub-DPS or support Espers.
4.  **Liquid (Skill-Based):** Liquid Arcs focus on enhancing the effectiveness of specific skills. They are frequently paired with characters who rely on frequent skill rotations rather than raw basic attacks.
5.  **Synthesis (Hybrid/Conditional):** These are the most complex Arcs. They often require specific conditions to be met—such as maintaining a certain HP threshold or using specific elemental reactions—to trigger their powerful effects.

### NTE Weapon Tier List: The Best S-Rank Arcs at Launch

#### Ready-Ready (Plasma)
Ready-Ready is widely considered the best-in-slot weapon for Nanally and other Plasma-type main DPS units. It features a high base ATK (570 at max) and a **CRIT Rate** substat. Its unique mechanic, the **Tiger Talisman**, is split into Left and Right components. Obtaining the Left Talisman (via Redirect Skill) and the Right Talisman (via Ultimate) within 15 seconds unlocks the **Commander Tiger Talisman**, which grants a massive 10% damage bonus specifically against Bosses.

#### Camellia Society (Synthesis)
This is a high-risk, high-reward weapon perfect for Synthesis characters like Baicang. It rewards the player for losing HP by granting stacks of **CRIT DMG**. More importantly, it unlocks the **Silent Garden** Arc Skill. This ability consumes 5% of the wearer's current HP to trigger one of two random Incantation attacks: **Harsh Rebuke** (multiple instances of high-damage leaves) or **Incessant Nagging** (piercing words that deal double damage on the final hit).

#### Hethereau's Keeper (Solid)
A versatile S-Rank Arc that provides a flat ATK boost and increased damage to Bosses. Its standout feature is the ability to summon **Officer Whisker**, a robotic companion that assists in combat. Officer Whisker deals steady damage based on the wearer's ATK, providing significant off-field DPS that helps whittle down elite enemy health bars.

#### Contemplative Cat (Gas)
This Arc features a unique scaling mechanic tied to the player's economy. It increases **Cosmos DMG** based on the amount of **Fons** (the primary in-game currency) the player currently holds. To maximize the bonus, players are encouraged to hold at least 1,000,000 Fons, making it a "greedy" but powerful choice for characters like Chiz.

### Arc Research Program: Understanding the Gacha and Pity System

The **Arc Research Program** (often found under the Scarborough Fair menu) is where you will spend your **Tri-Keys** to pull for new weapons.

*   **Pull Cost:** 1 Tri-Key costs **160 Analith**. Note that the system currently only allows for **10-pulls** (1,600 Analith per session); single pulls are not available.
*   **Drop Rates:** The base rate for an S-Rank Arc is **3%**, while A-Rank Arcs have a **7%** drop rate.
*   **Pity System:**
    *   **60 Pulls:** You are guaranteed to receive an S-Rank Arc.
    *   **80 Pulls:** If you have not yet received the featured limited-time Arc (such as the weapon on the "Tiger Special" banner), the 80th pull guarantees it.
    *   **Carry Over:** Pity progress carries over between limited weapon banners, allowing you to save your progress for future releases.

### Enhancing Your Arsenal: Leveling, Refinement, and Ascension

To keep your Arcs effective in late-game Anomalies, you must engage with three upgrade systems:

1.  **Leveling:** Uses standard enhancement materials to increase the Arc's level, directly boosting its Base ATK.
2.  **Refinement (Mixing Level):** By feeding duplicate copies of an Arc into the original, you increase its Mixing Level from 1 to 5. This significantly enhances the percentages and durations of the Arc's passive skills.
3.  **Ascension:** Every 10 or 20 levels, an Arc will hit a level cap. You must "Ascend" the weapon using materials gathered from the world or specific **Anomaly Commissions**. For example, higher-tier Arcs often require materials like "White Rose" or specific boss drops to progress.

### F2P Weapon Guide: Best A-Rank Arcs and Farmable Options

You do not need to spend money to get a powerful arsenal. Neverness to Everness is notably generous with farmable S-Rank Arcs.

*   **Farmable S-Ranks:** You can obtain Arcs like **Day Off**, **Song of the Whale**, and **Raging Flames** by completing specific world activities. **Day Off** is a fantastic choice for the protagonist, providing a massive ATK boost and an **Eclipse** zone that restores Ultimate Energy upon killing enemies.
*   **City Tycoon Rewards:** Progressing your City Tycoon rank to Level 21 rewards you with the **Contemplative Cat** S-Rank Arc for free.
*   **Best A-Rank "Filler" Arcs:**
    *   **Dangerous Game (Synthesis):** A solid bridge for hybrid characters.
    *   **Be Happy (Gas):** Provides reliable team-wide support buffs.
    *   **Us. (Plasma):** Offers a straightforward increase to Basic Attack damage, making it a perfect placeholder for main DPS units until you pull an S-Rank.

### Synergy with the 'Tetris' Gear System

Arcs do not work in isolation. They are designed to interact with the **Cartridge** and **Module** system (often referred to by fans as the "Tetris" system due to the block-fitting UI). For instance, if your Arc (like Ready-Ready) provides a high CRIT Rate, you should focus your Cartridge set bonuses on **CRIT DMG** (such as the **Fireflies and the Forest** set) to maximize your damage output through elemental reactions and critical hits.

High-intensity combat in Hethereau requires a constant flow of Ultimates and character swaps to keep up with the city's most dangerous anomalies. Mastering your energy regeneration is the only way to maintain momentum and ensure your team never stalls during a difficult boss encounter.

# Neverness to Everness: How to Increase Energy Regeneration

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/nte-how-to-increase-energy-regeneration/inline-1.webp)

In Neverness to Everness (NTE), players often find themselves waiting for long cooldowns or struggling to fill the meters required for their most powerful attacks. To optimize your performance, you must first distinguish between the three primary forms of "energy" used in the game: Ultimate Energy, the Esper Cycle Meter, and Character Pixels (Stamina). Each system has its own regeneration rules and specific methods for acceleration.

### What Kind of Energy Are You Looking For?
Identifying which resource is currently slowing your progression is the first step toward building an efficient team. If your characters are not casting their Ultimates frequently enough, you are lacking Ultimate Energy. If you cannot swap between characters to trigger reactions, your Esper Cycle Meter is the bottleneck. Finally, if you cannot enter the Anomaly Zone to farm materials, you are out of Character Pixels.

| Energy Type | UI Location | Primary Purpose | How to Regenerate |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ultimate Energy** | Top icon (Portrait) | Casts character's Ultimate ability. | Basic attacks, skills, and "Charge" reactions. |
| **Esper Cycle Meter** | Circular icon (Portrait) | Enables character swap reactions. | Attacking, skills, and successful Parrying. |
| **Character Pixels** | Top-right (Menu/Map) | Stamina for farming Anomaly Zones. | Time (1/6min), De-noise Solutions, Annuliths. |

### The Importance of Energy Management in High-Level Combat
In the endgame of NTE, particularly in the Rabbit Hole or high-tier Anomaly Zones, damage is not the only stat that matters. Energy regeneration dictates your "uptime"—the percentage of the fight where you are invulnerable during animations or dealing maximum damage through reactions. A team with high raw damage but low energy regeneration will often fail timed challenges because they cannot chain their abilities fast enough to maintain momentum. For characters like [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte) or [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte), whose damage is heavily weighted toward their Ultimate abilities, increasing energy regeneration is often more valuable than raw Attack stats.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-how-to-increase-energy-regeneration/inline-2.webp)

To increase Ultimate Energy regeneration, you should prioritize the "Energy Regen" (often listed as Charge Efficiency) substat on your Cartridges and Modules while prioritizing the "Charge" trio-reaction (Lakshana + Cosmos + Anima) in your team composition. This reaction creates a Blossom turret that restores Ultimate energy for every hit it lands on a target marked with Remora.

To increase Esper Cycle Meter speed, you must focus on the "Cycle Rate" stat through gear or character passives. Additionally, mastering the Parry mechanic is the fastest manual way to gain energy, as a successful parry instantly refills 100% of the active character's Esper Cycle Meter, allowing for immediate Support Skill (QTE) chains.

To recover Character Pixels (Stamina), you must rely on natural regeneration (1 Pixel every 6 minutes) or consume De-noise Solutions, which provide 60 Pixels per use. You can also use Annuliths for up to six refills per day, though the cost in premium currency increases after the third use.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-how-to-increase-energy-regeneration/inline-3.webp)


### Mastering the 'Charge' Reaction for Infinite Ultimates
The "Charge" reaction is the gold standard for energy-hungry teams in NTE. Unlike standard duo-reactions that provide immediate damage or crowd control, Charge is a trio-reaction requiring characters from the Lakshana, Cosmos, and Anima elements.

*   **Phase 1: Remora (Lakshana + Cosmos):** When you combine Lakshana and Cosmos, you apply the "Remora" effect, which slows enemies and marks them.
*   **Phase 2: Blossom (Cosmos + Anima):** Combining Cosmos and Anima spawns "Blossom" turrets that automatically fire bolts at nearby enemies.
*   **Phase 3: The Charge Trigger:** When a Blossom turret hits an enemy already marked by Remora, the "Charge" reaction activates. This generates a fixed amount of Ultimate Energy for the active character for every single bolt that connects.

By using characters like Chiz (Cosmos) and Nanally (Anima), you can maintain high Blossom uptime. If you include a Lakshana character like Hathor to maintain the Remora mark, your on-field DPS can effectively have their Ultimate ready almost as soon as the previous one ends. Chiz is particularly effective here, as her passives grant the active character 4 additional "Charge" energy per hit during this reaction.

### Cycle Rate vs. Energy Regen: Which Stat Matters More?
It is a common mistake to confuse "Energy Regen" with "Cycle Rate," but they govern two completely different systems:

*   **Energy Regen (Charge Efficiency):** This is a percentage multiplier applied to the Ultimate Energy you gain from all sources. If a character has a high Ultimate cost, you should aim for at least 130% Energy Regen through Cartridge substats.
*   **Cycle Rate:** This stat specifically affects the Esper Cycle Meter. A higher Cycle Rate means your Basic Attacks and Skills fill the swap-meter faster. For example, characters like the MC (Zero) and Fadia have built-in passives or skills that provide a "100 Cycle Rate" effect, allowing them to fill their meter instantly with a single skill use.

### How Parrying Becomes Your Primary Energy Source
While stats are important, the most effective way to increase energy regeneration manually in NTE is through the Parry mechanic. When an enemy prepares a parriable attack—indicated by a yellow circular icon or red closing circles—performing a Basic Attack, Skill, or Esper Cycle swap at the correct moment will trigger a parry.

A successful parry does more than just stun the enemy; it **instantly fills the active character's Esper Cycle Meter to 100%**. This is the most efficient way to rotate through your team. In boss fights, skilled players will intentionally bait parriable attacks to skip the slow process of building energy through normal combos, allowing for a continuous loop of swap-in reactions.

### Optimizing Consoles: Cartridge Substats for Energy
The Console system in NTE is a grid where you slot in Cartridges and Modules. To maximize energy, you need to look for specific set bonuses and substats. Unlike other games where gear stats are hidden until upgraded, NTE allows you to see all four substats on a Cartridge the moment you acquire it.

**Best Cartridge Sets for Energy Regeneration:**
*   **2-Piece Bonus:** Look for sets that provide "Charge Efficiency +12%." This is the primary stat for increasing Ultimate Energy gain.
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** Some support-oriented sets increase all allies' ATK by 15% for 20 seconds after the wearer casts an Ultimate. This is highly recommended for characters who can spam their Ultimates frequently.

| Stat to Prioritize | Recommended Value | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Energy Regen** | 130% - 150% | Ultimate-reliant DPS (e.g., Nanally, Hotori) |
| **Cycle Rate** | 110% - 125% | Swap-heavy Support (e.g., [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide), Mint) |
| **Cycle Intensity** | As high as possible | Reaction-based damage dealers |

When arranging your Console, remember that leveling a Cartridge only boosts the Main Stat. Your Substats, such as Energy Regen or Cycle Rate, stay exactly the same regardless of level. This means you should only invest resources into Cartridges that already have the energy stats you need in their base form.

### Managing Character Pixels and De-noise Solutions
Character Pixels are the stamina resource required to challenge the Anomaly Zone and farm materials. With a cap of 240 and a regeneration rate of 1 per 6 minutes, you generate a total of 240 Pixels every 24 hours.

To maximize your progression efficiency:
1.  **Avoid the Cap:** If you hit 240/240, you stop generating Pixels. Log in daily to spend them on "Houdini's Magic Stage" for EXP or "Bubble Can Factory" for Arc materials.
2.  **Use De-noise Solutions Wisely:** Each De-noise Solution restores 60 Pixels. Save these for moments when you have just unlocked a higher difficulty tier in the Anomaly Zone and want to farm the new, higher-quality rewards immediately.
3.  **Annulith Refills:** You can use Annulith to recover 60 Pixels up to 6 times per day. The first three refills cost 60 Annulith each, while the fourth through sixth refills cost 120 Annulith. For most players, stopping at three refills is the most efficient use of premium currency.

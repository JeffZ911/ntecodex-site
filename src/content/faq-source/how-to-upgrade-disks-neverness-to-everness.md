### Understanding the Terminology: Disks vs. Cartridges and Modules
While the community often uses "Disks" as a catch-all term for gear, the game technically differentiates between two types of equipment that occupy the Console
*   **Cartridges:** These function as the "brain" of your gear setup. They provide the primary Main Stat (such as % ATK, % CRIT Rate, or % Elemental Damage) and dictate the set bonuses your character receives.
*   **Modules:** These are smaller, Tetris-shaped pieces that provide flat bonuses (primarily Flat ATK and Flat HP). Their main purpose is to fill the grid space around the Cartridge to activate additional passive effects.

### The Role of the Console in Character Progression
The Console is the dedicated equipment interface for every character in NTE. Unlike traditional RPGs where you simply equip a sword or armor, the Console requires you to manage spatial efficiency. Upgrading these items does more than just increase numbers; it unlocks the potential for "Legend Effects," which can fundamentally change how a character like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or Mingze performs in combat.

1.  **Select the Component:** Choose either a Cartridge or a Module from the Console grid.
2.  **Enter the Enhance Menu:** Tap the "Enhance" button to open the upgrade interface.
3.  **Add Materials:** Use **Manhole EXP** materials (available in various rarities) or "fodder" gear (low-rarity Disks you no longer need) to fill the experience bar.

5.  **Hit Breakpoints:** Disks have a maximum level of 20. Significant power spikes occur every 5 levels (+5, +10, +15, +20), at which point a new sub-stat is either unlocked or an existing sub-stat is randomly upgraded.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-upgrade-disks-neverness-to-everness/inline-3.webp)


The upgrade system in Neverness to Everness is a multi-faceted process that involves managing resources, farming specific zones, and understanding the geometric constraints of the Console.

### The Console System: Cartridges vs. Modules
The Console is a grid-based system where spatial management is as important as the stats themselves. 
*   **Cartridges** occupy the central slot of the Console. They are the most resource-intensive to upgrade because they provide percentage-based stats that scale heavily into the late game. 
*   **Modules** come in various geometric shapes (L-shapes, squares, T-shapes). While their flat stats are lower than Cartridges, upgrading them is necessary to reach the "Sync Threshold" required for high-tier character passives.

### How to Level Up Cartridges (The "Artifact" Equivalent)
Cartridges are the primary source of your Esper's power. When you upgrade a Cartridge, the Main Stat increases linearly. For example, an S-Rank Cartridge with a % ATK main stat will see a significant jump in value at level 20 compared to its base level.

To level up Cartridges efficiently
*   Use **High-Density Manhole EXP** for the most significant gains.
*   Avoid using A-Rank Cartridges as fodder until you have a full set of S-Rank gear, as the EXP loss during "recycling" is roughly 20%.

### How to Level Up Modules (The "Tetris" Pieces)
Modules are upgraded similarly to Cartridges but require fewer Beetle Coins per level. The strategy with Modules is to prioritize those that have the best shapes for your current Console layout. If a Module has perfect sub-stats but a shape that blocks your other pieces, it may not be worth the investment until you can reorganize your entire grid.

### Sub-stat Mechanics: Unlocking and Refining
Every Disk starts with a set number of sub-stats based on its rarity. S-Rank (Gold) Disks typically start with 2 to 4 sub-stats. 
*   If a Disk starts with 4 sub-stats, every 5-level breakpoint (+5, +10, etc.) will randomly increase the value of one of those stats.
*   If a Disk starts with fewer than 4, the breakpoints will first unlock new slots until 4 are filled, then begin upgrading them.

To sustain a constant stream of upgrade materials, you must engage with two primary systems

### Activating Set Bonuses: Epic vs. Legend Effects
When you equip Disks of the same set (e.g., the "Neon Pulse" set), you unlock powerful bonuses.
*   **Epic Effects:** Usually activated by equipping 2 or 4 pieces of the same set. These provide flat stat boosts or minor passive triggers.
*   **Legend Effects:** These are the pinnacle of the Console system. To activate a Legend Effect, you must not only have the correct set pieces but also arrange your Modules in a specific pattern that "completes" the Cartridge's circuit. This often requires leveling up specific Modules to unlock the "Type II" passives mentioned in character-specific guides.

### Arc Upgrades: Enhancing Your Character's Weapon
While Disks provide the bulk of your stats, **Arcs** (the game's weapons) provide the base scaling. Upgrading an Arc requires **Dye** (for leveling) and **Ascension materials** (for breaking level caps). It is generally recommended to keep your Arc level equal to your character's level, while Disks can lag slightly behind until you find the perfect S-Rank pieces.

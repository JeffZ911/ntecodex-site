### Mastering the Esper Cycle and Elemental Reactions

The Esper Cycle is the heart of NTE combat. Unlike traditional action RPGs where swapping characters is merely a way to access different skills, swapping in NTE is a tactical trigger. When a character on the sidelines has their portrait glow, switching to them immediately activates their Esper Ability. This keeps the character on the field at all times, preventing "dead air" in your DPS rotation.

The elements in NTE are divided into two main groups on the Esper Wheel: the "Physical" side (Cosmos, Anima, Incantation) and the "Anomalous" side (Chaos, Psyche, Lakshana). When you trigger abilities in sequence, you create reactions. Duo Reactions occur between two elements, but the highest damage potential lies in Trio Reactions.

| Reaction Name | Elements Involved | Primary Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Discord | Chaos + Psyche + Lakshana | High burst damage and reduces enemy resistance to all elements. |
| Charge | Cosmos + Anima + Incantation | Increases the team's Energy Regeneration and boosts Basic Attack DMG. |
| — | Cosmos + Chaos | — |
| — | Anima + Psyche | — |

To maximize damage, you should aim to complete a Trio Reaction every time your Esper Cycle resets. This requires a team composition that covers at least three different elements that can interact. For example, a team featuring [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) (Anima) should be paired with characters who can provide the Cosmos and Incantation elements to consistently trigger the [Charge reaction](https://ntecodex.com/faq), keeping Nanally's high-damage skills fueled with energy.

### Optimizing the Console: Cartridges and the Module Tetris System

The Console is the primary gear system in Neverness to Everness, consisting of Cartridges and Modules. While Cartridges act as the "base" of your gear, providing primary stats like HP or ATK, the Modules are where the real optimization happens. Modules come in various geometric shapes—reminiscent of Tetris blocks—and must be fitted into a limited grid.

To increase team damage, you must prioritize "Set Bonuses." Each Module belongs to a specific set (e.g., the "Urban Vanguard" set or the "Neon Strike" set). Fitting a certain number of Modules from the same set into your Console grid activates powerful passive effects.

[Screenshot description: A screenshot of the NTE Console UI demonstrating how different shaped Modules fit into the grid to activate set bonuses. Aspect ratio: 16:9]

When placing Modules, keep these key damage stats in mind:
*   **ATK %:** Increases your base damage.
*   **CRIT Rate / CRIT DMG:** Essential for late-game scaling.
*   **Cycle Intensity:** Increases the damage dealt by Esper Abilities when swapping.
*   **Break Intensity:** Increases the speed at which you deplete the enemy's Break Meter.

The "Tetris" aspect adds a layer of strategy; you might have a high-stat Module that simply doesn't fit into your current grid without removing two others. Balancing the raw stats of individual Modules against the power of a 4-piece set bonus is the key to maximizing your Console's efficiency.

### Weapon (Arc) Progression: Enhancement, Ascension, and Mixing

Arcs are the weapons of NTE, and they come in five distinct types based on their physical properties: Solid, Plasma, Gas, Liquid, and Synthesis. Each type generally aligns with specific character archetypes.

### Combat Tactics: Parrying and Exploiting the Break Meter

Raw stats are only half the battle. To truly maximize team damage, you must master the Break Meter. Most elite enemies and bosses have a white bar beneath their health. This is the Break Meter. While this bar is active, the enemy takes reduced damage and is resistant to hitstun.

By using characters with high **Break Intensity**, you can deplete this meter. Once it reaches zero, the enemy enters a "Broken" state. In this state, the enemy is staggered and takes significantly increased damage—often a multiplier of 1.5x or 2.0x. This is your "burst window." You should save your Ultimates and high-cooldown skills for the moment an enemy breaks.

Additionally, mastering the Parry mechanic is essential for maintaining DPS. A successful parry not only prevents damage to your character but also deals a large amount of "Break Damage" to the enemy. This allows you to reach the Broken state faster, increasing your overall damage uptime throughout the fight.

### Understanding the NTE Gear System: Disks, Cartridges, and Modules

In Neverness to Everness, "Disks" are the primary equipment system used to enhance an Esper's stats and provide powerful passive effects. This system is split into two distinct components: Cartridges and Modules.

**Cartridges** function as the set-piece backbone of your build. Equipping two or four pieces of the same Cartridge set unlocks specific bonuses, such as increased Elemental DMG or specialized combat buffs like DEF-ignore. Unlike traditional gear, Cartridges are not "worn" in specific slots but are instead slotted into the **Console**, a 20-slot grid.

**Modules** are the individual components that fill the remaining space in your Console. While Cartridges provide the set bonuses, Modules provide the bulk of your raw stats. Each character has a preferred **Module Type** (e.g., Type I, Type II, or Type III). Matching a Module's type to the character's preference is mandatory for unlocking high-tier resonance bonuses, such as flat Crit Rate or Crit DMG boosts that do not appear on the items themselves.

### Top-Tier Cartridge Breakdown

**Lost Radiance** remains the gold standard for endgame progression. In the "Rabbit Hole" (the game's primary challenge mode), enemies possess high armor values. Flat Attack bonuses suffer from diminishing returns, whereas DEF-ignore allows your damage to remain consistent regardless of the enemy's level. For characters with high-cooldown, high-impact Ultimates, this set provides a window of extreme burst potential.

**Fireflies and the Forest** is specifically designed for the Anima element, which currently dominates the meta through characters like Nanally. The set rewards aggressive playstyles, stacking CRIT DMG every time an Anima-based skill deals damage. Because Nanally's kit features multi-hit properties, she can reach the 56% CRIT DMG ceiling almost instantly.

**Devil's Blood: Curse** is unique because its value is tied to your team composition. If you are running a Sub-DPS capable of applying constant **Stain** or **Nova** effects, the 36% total DMG bonus from this set often outperforms element-specific sets.

### Optimizing the Console Grid

The Console is a 20-slot grid where Cartridges and Modules must be placed. Cartridges usually take up 2 or 3 slots each, and you must fit four of the same set to get the full bonus. This leaves roughly 8 to 12 slots for Modules.

To optimize the grid:
1.  Place your 4-piece Cartridge set first.
2.  Fill every remaining empty slot with Modules of the character's preferred Type.
3.  Ensure no slots are left empty, as even a low-rarity Module is better than an empty space for triggering the Resonance bonuses.

### Substat Priority and Refinement Tips

Unlike many urban RPGs, NTE allows you to preview the potential stat ranges before committing resources to leveling a Disk. This reduces the "gear grind" but increases the importance of starting with the right base.

**Main DPS Substat Priority:**
1.  **CRIT DMG:** Since Crit Rate is often supplemented by Module Resonance, CRIT DMG is the highest priority substat for scaling.
2.  **CRIT Rate:** Aim for a 70% total Crit Rate (including Resonance and base stats).
3.  **Elemental DMG %:** Specifically matching the character's element (Anima, Incantation, etc.).
4.  **ATK %:** Useful, but secondary to Crit and Elemental modifiers.

**Sub-DPS Substat Priority:**
1.  **Energy Recharge:** Ensures Ultimates are ready on every rotation.
2.  **Elemental DMG %:** To contribute to team damage during off-field windows.
3.  **Effect Hit Rate:** Crucial for characters meant to apply **Stain** or **Remora** reactions.

### Reaction-Based Gear Scaling

The **Esper Cycle** system allows for reactions that significantly buff your gear's performance. For example, triggering **Nova** (Anima + Incantation) can trigger secondary effects on certain Disks like **Street Boxer** [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-24]. 

**Nova** and **Stain** are the current offensive favorites. **Nova** creates an explosion that scales with the character's ATK, making it perfect for the ATK-heavy Lost Radiance builds. **Stain** reduces enemy resistance, which multiplies the effectiveness of the CRIT DMG gained from the **Fireflies and the Forest** set.

### Recommended DPS Team Rotations

To maximize the 4-piece bonus of your Disks, your rotation must be precise. For a **Nanally** DPS team:
1.  **Sakiri (Support):** Use her abilities to group enemies. This ensures that when Nanally enters the field, her Anima attacks hit the maximum number of targets to stack the **Fireflies and the Forest** bonus quickly.
2.  **Sub-DPS:** Apply an element to prime a reaction (e.g., Incantation for Nova).
3.  **Nanally (Main DPS):** Use Ultimate to trigger **Lost Radiance** DEF-ignore or **Fireflies** stacks, then follow up with her Skill sequence.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-farm-disks-nte-20260520/inline-3.webp)


Farming in Neverness to Everness is a multi-layered process. It involves a mix of direct acquisition through missions and the indirect farming of currencies to use in the gacha and shop systems.

### The Arc Research Program (Gacha Mechanics)
The most common way to get new Arcs is the Arc Research Program. This is the dedicated weapon banner in NTE. A unique mechanic of this banner is that it does not allow for single pulls; you must use 10 Tri-Keys at once for a multi-pull. 

The drop rates for the Arc Research Program are as follows:
*   **S-Class Arcs:** 3%
*   **A-Class Arcs:** 7%
*   **B-Class Arcs:** 90%

While the 3% rate for S-Class items is relatively generous compared to other urban RPGs, the lack of single pulls means you must save up 1,600 Annulith before you can attempt to farm this banner. 

### Farming Tri-Keys and Annulith
Since Tri-Keys are the gateway to Arcs, your daily farming loop should focus on Annulith. You can acquire this currency through several consistent streams:
1.  **Circle Bounties:** These are your daily quests. Completing all four daily tasks provides a significant chunk of Annulith.
2.  **Anomaly Hunts:** These function as weekly boss challenges. Defeating high-level Anomalies for the first time each week rewards Annulith.
3.  **Achievements:** NTE has an extensive achievement system. Many of these are simple tasks, such as driving a certain distance or visiting specific shops in Hethereau, which reward small amounts of Annulith that add up over time.
4.  **Tycoon Level Rewards:** As you engage with the city's economy—buying cars, decorating your apartment, and managing businesses—your Tycoon Level increases. Each level milestone provides bulk rewards, including Tri-Keys and Annulith.

### Guaranteed S-Rank Disks from Missions
If you are looking for a guaranteed S-Rank Disk without spending currency, you must complete the "Eclipse" mission. This is a hidden-in-plain-sight questline located at the Anomaly Agency in central Hethereau.

**Step-by-Step Guide to the "Eclipse" Mission:**
1.  **Locate the NPC Eclixse:** Travel to the Anomaly Agency headquarters. Eclixse is usually found near the main terminal in the lobby.
2.  **Trigger the Mission:** You must have reached Hunter Level [information unavailable] to unlock this dialogue option. Speak with Eclixse about "Unregistered Arcs."
3.  **The Combat Trial:** You will be transported to a digital arena. You must defeat three waves of Anomalies within a strict time limit.
4.  **Claim Your Reward:** Upon completion, Eclixse will grant you the S-Rank Disk "Disclipse." This Arc is particularly strong for Shadow-element Espers and provides a significant boost to burst damage.

### World Exploration and Oracle Stones
Exploration is a viable, albeit finite, way to farm Disks. Scattered throughout the various districts of Hethereau are Oracle Stones. These floating geometric objects act as the primary collectible in the open world. 

When you interact with an Oracle Stone, you receive a small amount of Annulith. More importantly, high-tier chests guarded by elite "Urban Horrors" often contain purple-rarity A-Rank Disks. While you won't find S-Rank items in common chests, the A-Rank Arcs found during exploration are essential for gearing your secondary team members during the early-to-mid game.

### The Arc Shop and Hunter Exchange
Every time you use a Tri-Key in the Arc Research Program, you earn a secondary currency (often referred to as Arc Residue). This currency can be spent in the Arc Shop. 

The Arc Shop is the best way to bypass gacha RNG. It features a rotating selection of S-Rank Arcs. Currently, a specific S-Rank Arc costs 25 Tri-Keys' worth of residue. This means that even if you have bad luck with your pulls, every 250 pulls (or 25 multi-pulls) effectively guarantees you can pick exactly the S-Rank Disk you need from the shop.

### Optimizing Your Daily Farming Loop
To maximize your Disk farming, follow this daily routine:
*   Log in and complete all **Circle Bounties** (approx. 10 minutes).
*   Check the **Anomaly Agency** for any new bounties or "Anomaly Hunts" that have reset.
*   Spend your **Stamina** (Fons) in the Bubble Can Factory to get upgrade materials (this ensures your farmed Disks are actually usable).
*   If you have 1,600 Annulith, perform a multi-pull on the **Arc Research Program**.
*   Check the **Arc Shop** monthly to see if a "Best-in-Slot" Arc for your main Esper has appeared.

Farming the Disks themselves is only half the battle. Once you have acquired an S-Rank Arc, you need to maintain and upgrade it to keep up with the game's scaling difficulty.

### Disk Upgrade Materials
You cannot simply farm Disks and leave them at level one. To increase their power, you need Pigments and EXP Dyes. These materials are farmed in the **Bubble Can Factory**, a specialized instance that consumes your daily stamina.
*   **Pigments:** Used for "Ascending" an Arc to increase its level cap.
*   **EXP Dyes:** Used to provide the raw experience needed to level up the Arc.

Higher-level Bubble Can Factory stages yield more efficient rewards, so it is recommended to push your Hunter Level as high as possible before dumping all your stamina into these materials.

### Modules: The Other "Disk" System
A common point of confusion for new players is the difference between Arcs and Modules. While Arcs are your "weapons," Modules are the block-based gear pieces used in the **Console** system.

| Feature | Arcs (Disks) | Modules (Cartridges) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Use** | Weapon / Passive Ability | Stat Blocks / Set Bonuses |
| **Farming Location** | Arc Research Program / Missions | Rabbit Hole (End-game Dungeon) |
| **Currency** | Tri-Keys / Annulith | Stamina / Rabbit Hole Tokens |
| **Rarity** | S, A, B-Rank | Tier 1 through Tier 5 |

Modules are farmed in the **Rabbit Hole**, a multi-floor dungeon that gets progressively harder. While Disks provide the "soul" of your Esper's build through passive effects, Modules provide the "body" by filling out your base stats like Crit DMG and Elemental Mastery.

### Recommended Disks for Early Game
If you are struggling to find S-Rank Disks, do not ignore A-Rank (Purple) Arcs. Several A-Rank options are highly competitive when fully upgraded:
*   **Street Brawler (A-Rank):** Excellent for physical attackers; provides a stacking ATK buff every time you land a basic attack.
*   **Neon Pulse (A-Rank):** The go-to for support Espers, as it increases the rate at which your team gains Fons.
*   **Digital Ghost (A-Rank):** Great for evasion-heavy playstyles, providing a damage boost after a Perfect Dodge.

Focus on farming these A-Rank versions through exploration and the Arc Research Program while you save your Tri-Keys and Annulith for the specific S-Rank Arcs that appear in the monthly Arc Shop rotation.

## Direct Answer

Neverness to Everness utilizes a 90-pull hard pity system for S-Rank characters on both Limited and Standard boards. However, players rarely reach the 90th pull due to a "Board Modification" mechanic (soft pity) that triggers at the 70th pull. This modification drastically increases the S-Rank acquisition rate from a base of 1.87% to 19.59%.

Unlike many contemporary gacha games, NTE does not feature a "50/50" system for characters. If you trigger an S-Rank pull on a limited-time banner, you are guaranteed to receive the featured character. Weapon (Arc) pity is slightly lower, requiring 60 pulls for a guaranteed S-Rank and 80 pulls for a guaranteed featured Arc. While character and weapon pity carry over between banners of the same type, cosmetic milestones at 50, 120, and 200 pulls are banner-specific and do not transfer.

### Scarborough Fair: The Board Game Gacha Mechanics

The gacha system in Neverness to Everness is visually presented as "Scarborough Fair," a Monopoly-style board game. Instead of a simple animation of falling stars or crates, players roll dice to move a small mascot called a "Chuppa" across various tiles. Each tile represents a potential reward, including A-Rank characters, S-Rank characters, upgrade materials, or currency.

To participate, players spend Annulith, the primary premium currency. Each pull costs 160 Annulith. The type of dice used depends on the banner:
*   **Solid Dice:** Used for Limited-Time Character Boards (e.g., "The Ichi-daime" banner for Nanally).
*   **Fabricated Dice:** Used for the Standard Board (Permanent).
*   **Tri-Keys:** Used for the Arc Research Program (Weapon Gacha).

The Chuppa moves according to the dice results, but the "pull" is technically calculated the moment you click the button. The board movement is a stylized representation of the RNG outcome. If the Chuppa lands on a "Journey Together" tile, you have successfully recruited a character.

### Character Pity: Soft Pity vs. Hard Pity

The pity system in NTE is divided into two distinct phases: the standard board state and the "Board Modification" state.

From pull 1 to 69, you operate under the base S-Rank rate of 1.87%. This is notably higher than the 0.6% or 1.0% found in other major gacha titles. If you do not land on an S-Rank tile by your 70th pull, the game initiates "Board Modification." 

During Board Modification, the Scarborough Fair layout physically shifts. More S-Rank tiles are added to the board, and the probability of landing on a "Journey Together" tile jumps to 19.59% per pull. This "soft pity" ensures that the vast majority of players obtain their desired character between pulls 74 and 82. If you are extremely unlucky and reach 90 pulls, the game forces a landing on the featured S-Rank tile.

### The 'No 50/50' Rule: Why NTE is Different

The most significant advantage of the Neverness to Everness gacha system is the absence of the 50/50 mechanic. In many other games, hitting the highest rarity on a limited banner only gives you a 50% chance of getting the featured character, with the other 50% being a standard pool character.

In NTE, there are no "standard" S-Rank characters hidden on the Limited Board. If the board triggers an S-Rank reward, it is the featured character (such as Nanally). This removes the frustration of "losing" a pity and makes the 90-pull hard pity a true maximum cost for any character. This transparency allows for much stricter budget management for free-to-play players.

### Weapon (Arc) Pity and Tri-Key Mechanics

The Arc Research Program is where you pull for weapons, known in-game as Arcs. This system uses Tri-Keys rather than Dice. The pity thresholds here are lower than the character boards, reflecting the lower base rate.

*   **S-Rank Arc Pity:** You are guaranteed an S-Rank Arc every 60 pulls.
*   **Featured Arc Guarantee:** While the S-Rank rate is 1.5%, your first S-Rank pull only has a 25% initial chance of being the featured weapon. However, the system includes a hard guarantee at 80 pulls. If you have not received the featured Arc within 79 pulls, the 80th pull is guaranteed to be the specific featured weapon.

### Pity Carryover: What Transfers and What Resets?

A common concern is whether progress toward the 90-pull pity is lost when a banner ends. In Neverness to Everness, pity is persistent across banners of the same category.

1.  **Limited Character Boards:** If you perform 40 pulls on Nanally's banner and do not get an S-Rank, those 40 pulls will count toward the pity on the next limited banner (e.g., Hotori). You would only need 50 more pulls to hit the 90-pull hard pity on the new banner.
2.  **Arc Research:** Pity carries over between different weapon rotations.
3.  **Standard Board:** This has its own independent pity counter that never resets.

**Important Exception:** The Cosmetic Milestone track (the 50/120/200 pull rewards) does **not** carry over. These rewards are unique to the specific banner they are attached to. If you do 190 pulls on a banner and fail to reach the 200-pull outfit reward, you cannot use those 190 pulls to claim an outfit on the next banner.

### Cosmetic Milestones: The 200-Pull Guarantee

NTE introduces a separate reward track for "whales" or dedicated savers known as Cosmetic Milestones. These are separate from the character pity and do not reset when you pull an S-Rank character.

*   **50 Pulls:** Usually rewards a limited-edition Glider skin or City Accessory.
*   **120 Pulls:** Usually rewards a limited Vehicle skin (e.g., a custom paint job for your car).
*   **200 Pulls:** Rewards a premium Character Outfit for the featured S-Rank unit.

Because these rewards are tied to the specific banner "event," they are the only part of the gacha system that requires a "all-in" approach within a single banner period.

### Warp and Lost Pieces

When you pull duplicate characters or land on specific Warp tiles, you receive currency called "Lost Pieces."
*   **Duplicate A-Rank:** Provides a small amount of Lost Pieces and an "Awakening" material for that character.
*   **Duplicate S-Rank:** Provides a large amount of Lost Pieces and an "Awakening" material.

These Lost Pieces can be spent in the Exchange Shop to purchase Solid Dice, Tri-Keys, or even specific Awakening materials for S-Rank characters you already own. This acts as a secondary "spark" system, allowing players to eventually power up their favorite characters even if they have poor luck with the dice rolls.

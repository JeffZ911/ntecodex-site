## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/neverness-to-everness-elemental-reaction-guide/inline-2.webp)


Elemental reactions in NTE are formally known as "Esper Cycles." These are not triggered by simply applying two elements to a target; instead, they are triggered by swapping characters when the incoming character has a full Esper Meter. This creates a "Cycle" effect that originates from the character entering the field.

There are six core elements in Neverness to Everness: Cosmos, Anima, Incantation, Chaos, Psyche, and Lakshana. These elements are arranged in a fixed hexagonal "Esper Wheel." A reaction can only occur between neighboring elements on this wheel. 

The system is divided into two primary categories:
1.  **Duo Cycles:** These occur when you swap between two adjacent elements. There are six Duo Cycles: Remora, Blossom, Hexed, Scorch, Nova, and Stain.
2.  **Triple Cycles:** These occur when you successfully chain three elements from a specific "Trio" (either the Top Trio or the Bottom Trio). These trigger the most powerful effects in the game: Charge and Discord.

### The Esper Cycle Mechanic

The Esper Cycle relies on the "Swap Glow." When a character on your sidelines reaches a 100% Esper Meter, their portrait on the right side of the screen will glow with their elemental color. Swapping to that character immediately triggers a reaction based on the element of the character who was just on the field. If the two elements are not adjacent on the Esper Wheel, no reaction occurs, and you simply perform a standard swap attack.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/neverness-to-everness-elemental-reaction-guide/inline-3.webp)


To master combat in Neverness to Everness, you must internalize the layout of the Esper Wheel. Because reactions only occur between neighbors, your team composition is strictly dictated by the wheel's geometry.

### Understanding the Esper Wheel

The Esper Wheel is a fixed hexagonal loop. The order of elements is:
**Lakshana ↔ Cosmos ↔ Anima ↔ Incantation ↔ Chaos ↔ Psyche ↔ (Back to Lakshana)**

If your active character is an Anima element (like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)) and you swap to a Cosmos character (like Zero), you trigger a reaction because they are neighbors. However, if you swap from Anima to Psyche, no reaction will trigger because they are across the wheel from each other. This makes team synergy more rigid than in other games, but far more explosive when executed correctly.

### How to Trigger Reactions: The Swap Glow

You cannot trigger an Esper Cycle at will; you must first build the Esper Meter for the character you wish to bring in.
*   **Normal Attacks:** Provide the lowest amount of Esper Meter generation.
*   **Active Skills:** Provide moderate generation (usually [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-20]% per cast).
*   **Parrying:** A successful parry is the most efficient way to fight. Performing a "Perfect Parry" instantly grants the active character's teammates a massive boost to their Esper Meters, often filling them to 100% immediately.

Once the meter is full, the character portrait glows. Pressing the corresponding swap key (or tapping the portrait) executes the Esper Cycle.

### Triple Cycle Reactions: The Ultimate Combat Multiplier

Triple Cycles are the advanced form of the Esper Cycle. To trigger these, you must rotate through three specific elements in a row without breaking the chain or waiting too long between swaps. The wheel is divided into two halves:

#### 1. The Top Trio: Charge (Lakshana, Cosmos, Anima)
When you cycle through these three elements in any order (as long as they are neighbors, e.g., Lakshana -> Cosmos -> Anima), you trigger **Charge**.
*   **Effect:** Immediately restores a large portion of the entire team's Energy and grants a 20% Attack Speed buff to the active character for 15 seconds.
*   **Best For:** Teams that rely on their Ultimate abilities (Burst Espers) to deal damage.

#### 2. The Bottom Trio: Discord (Incantation, Chaos, Psyche)
When you cycle through these three elements in order (e.g., Incantation -> Chaos -> Psyche), you trigger **Discord**.
*   **Effect:** Deals massive Stagger damage (breaking the enemy's shield/posture) and shreds Physical Resistance by [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-20]%.
*   **Best For:** Boss fights where breaking the enemy's "Anomalous Guard" is required to deal real damage.

### Optimizing Team Composition for Reactions

Because you are limited to three characters per team, you must choose whether you want to focus on a single Duo Reaction or aim for a Triple Cycle.

*   **The "Double-Up" Strategy:** Run two characters of the same element and one neighbor. Example: Zero (Cosmos), [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte) (Cosmos), and Nanally (Anima). This allows you to trigger **Remora** constantly by swapping between the Cosmos and Anima characters.
*   **The "Trio" Strategy:** Run three different elements that form a half-circle on the wheel. Example: Nanally (Anima), Zero (Cosmos), and a Lakshana-element character. This allows you to trigger **Charge** consistently.

### The Role of Parrying in Reaction Speed

While you can build your Esper Meter through basic attacks, the "Cycle Rate" stat on your Arcs (gear) and weapons heavily influences how quickly you can trigger reactions. However, no amount of Cycle Rate compares to the **Parry Mechanic**.

In Neverness to Everness, parrying an incoming attack doesn't just protect you; it "overcharges" the Esper system. If you parry an attack with Nanally (Anima), and your sideline character Zero (Cosmos) is at 50% meter, the parry will often jump him to 100% instantly. This allows for rapid-fire reaction chains that can lock an enemy in a perpetual state of Remora or Scorch.

# How to Level Up Fast in Neverness to Everness (NTE): Ultimate Progression Guide

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/how-to-level-up-fast-nte-20260522/inline-1.webp)


Players arriving in the neon-soaked streets of Hethereau often find themselves gated by their Hunter Level. This account-wide progression metric determines which story chapters you can access and directly influences your Appraisal Level, which acts as the world difficulty tier. Understanding how to navigate these systems is the difference between a stalled experience and a smooth climb to the endgame.

The most common questions regarding progression include:

*   **What is the fastest way to increase Hunter Level in Neverness to Everness?** Speed is generally tied to a combination of high-yield story missions and the disciplined consumption of daily stamina.
*   **How do players unlock higher Appraisal Levels and world difficulty?** Appraisal Levels are tied to specific Hunter Level milestones and usually require a breakthrough trial to advance.
*   **What are the primary sources of Hunter EXP for account progression?** Sources range from one-time quest rewards to repeatable combat challenges and daily checklists.
*   **How does the dual stamina system (Character Pixels vs. City Stamina) affect leveling speed?** While Character Pixels are the primary driver for Hunter EXP, City Stamina fuels the economic side of the game, which provides secondary progression benefits.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-level-up-fast-nte-20260522/inline-2.webp)


The fastest way to level up in NTE is to prioritize **Main Episodes and Spinoff quests**, as these provide the largest one-time injections of Hunter EXP. Once the available story content is exhausted, your primary focus shifts to the daily gameplay loop.

Consistently spending all **240 daily Character Pixels** in Anomaly Zones is the most critical habit for steady growth. Because 1 Pixel spent equals 5 Hunter EXP, a full day’s natural regeneration yields 1,200 Hunter EXP. Combining this with the **100-point Participation Tasks** in the Exploration Guide provides the bulk of your daily account progression. Furthermore, advancing the **City Tycoon system** and clearing **Exploration Guide phases** offer significant secondary XP sources that bridge the gap between major story beats.

### Top 5 XP-Yielding Activities (Ranked by Efficiency)

1.  **Main Episodes:** The highest Hunter EXP per minute of gameplay.
2.  **Participation Tasks:** Daily objective completions that offer massive, repeatable EXP.
3.  **Anomaly Zones:** The most efficient way to convert Character Pixels into Hunter EXP.
4.  **Exploration Guide Phases:** One-time milestone rewards for completing specific game tasks.
5.  **Spinoff Quests:** Important side stories that provide substantial EXP and world-building.

## Detailed Explanation

Progression in Neverness to Everness is a two-pronged system. Your **Hunter Level** represents your overall account experience, while your **Appraisal Level** dictates the difficulty of the world and the quality of the loot dropped by enemies and Anomaly Zones. If you increase your Hunter Level without completing the Appraisal Level breakthrough quests, your XP will pool at the cap, and you will stop progressing until the trial is cleared.

### Prioritize Main Episodes and Spinoff Quests

Main Episodes are the backbone of the NTE experience. They are designed to lead you through the city of Hethereau while providing enough Hunter EXP to keep you near the intended level for each zone. Spinoff Quests, while optional, are equally important. These side stories often unlock new areas of the city or introduce mechanics that are essential for later gameplay.

When a new chapter is released, completing it should be your absolute priority. The Hunter EXP rewards for these missions are significantly higher than any repeatable activity. If you find yourself "level-gated" (unable to start the next chapter because your Hunter Level is too low), this is the game’s signal to look toward your Exploration Guide and Daily Tasks.

### Maximize Daily Participation Tasks

The Participation system is NTE’s version of daily commissions. By completing simple tasks—such as using a vending machine, defeating a certain number of enemies, or completing an Anomaly Zone—you earn points toward a daily total.

*   **20 Points:** Small Hunter EXP reward + Beetle Coins.
*   **40 Points:** Medium Hunter EXP reward + Upgrade materials.
*   **60 Points:** Medium Hunter EXP reward + City Stamina items.
*   **80 Points:** Large Hunter EXP reward + Annuliths (premium currency).
*   **100 Points:** Maximum Hunter EXP reward + Rare upgrade materials.

Aiming for 100 points every day is non-negotiable for fast leveling. These tasks usually take less than 15 minutes to complete and provide a consistent stream of XP that outpaces random world exploration.

### Efficient Character Pixels Management

Character Pixels are your primary energy resource for combat-related rewards. They regenerate at a rate of **1 Pixel every 6 minutes**, totaling 240 Pixels in a 24-hour window.

To level up fast, you must never let your Pixels sit at the 240 cap. Every minute spent at the cap is Hunter EXP lost. The conversion rate is fixed: **1 Character Pixel = 5 Hunter EXP**.

**Anomaly Zones** are the primary drain for your Pixels. "Houdini’s Magic Stage" is widely considered one of the most efficient combat-based XP farms due to its straightforward enemy waves and high density of rewards. When farming, look for the double-reward toggle if you have "De-noise Solution" items available; this allows you to spend double the Pixels for double the loot and XP in a single run, significantly cutting down on real-world time spent grinding.

### Clearing Exploration Guide Phases

The Exploration Guide acts as a roadmap for your journey through Hethereau. It is divided into phases, each containing a list of mini-tasks like "Upgrade an Arc to Level 20" or "Complete 3 Spinoff Quests."

Each mini-task typically rewards **100 Hunter EXP**. When you complete all tasks in a phase, you receive a large bundle of rewards, including Beetle Coins and sometimes Arcs or Character Shards. These phases are specifically designed to bridge the gaps between story chapters. If you are 500 EXP away from the next level, checking your Exploration Guide is often the fastest way to close that gap without waiting for stamina to regenerate.

### Advancing City Tycoon and Appraisal Levels

While the City Tycoon system focuses heavily on the management sim and economic side of NTE, it has a hidden impact on your leveling speed. Advancing your Tycoon Level unlocks efficiency milestones. For example, reaching **City Tycoon Level 16** unlocks a significant time-save buff that reduces the duration of certain passive resource collection tasks, allowing you to focus more on XP-generating activities.

Appraisal Levels are the "World Level" mechanic. Once you hit Hunter Level 20, 40, and 50, you must complete a breakthrough quest. These quests involve a series of combat trials against buffed versions of standard anomalies. Completing these is essential because higher Appraisal Levels increase the Hunter EXP drops from certain world activities and significantly improve the quality of materials you get from spending Pixels.

### How to Level Up Characters and Arcs

As your Hunter Level rises, you must keep your characters (Esper/Anomalists) and their Arcs (weapons) leveled up to match.

*   **Character Leveling:** Use **Beetle Coins** and specific EXP materials (often found in Anomaly Zones) to increase a character's level. You will hit caps that can only be bypassed by "Ascending" the character once you reach the required Appraisal Level.
*   **Arc Upgrades:** The **Bubble Can Factory** is the primary location for farming Arc ascension materials. Arcs provide the bulk of your base stats, so keeping them synchronized with your character level is vital for clearing breakthrough quests.

### Understanding the City Tycoon System

The City Tycoon system allows you to manage businesses and properties in Hethereau. This system uses **City Stamina** and rewards you with **Fons** (the primary economic currency). While Fons aren't used for Hunter EXP directly, they are required for purchasing high-end character upgrade materials and Arc components from the city shops. A healthy economy allows you to buy the materials you would otherwise have to spend Character Pixels to farm, effectively allowing you to redirect your Pixels into XP-heavy Anomaly Zones.

### Appraisal Level Breakthrough Quests

When you reach the Hunter Level cap for your current Appraisal Level, a "Breakthrough Quest" will appear in your mission log. These are typically solo combat challenges.

*   **Level 20 Breakthrough:** Focuses on basic elemental reactions and dodging mechanics.
*   **Level 40 Breakthrough:** Requires a well-built team with at least one healer or shielder.
*   **Level 50 Breakthrough:** A significant difficulty spike involving elite anomaly bosses with high resistance.

### Beyond the Rails and Daily Activities

For players looking for every possible edge, secondary activities provide minor but helpful boosts.

*   **Beyond the Rails:** This is the end-game combat progression mode. It functions on a monthly reward cycle. While it is more about testing your strength than leveling up, the rewards often include materials that save you stamina in the long run.
*   **The Witch's House and Nacupeda's Pool:** These locations offer daily minor activities. Interacting with NPCs here can trigger hidden questlines or provide temporary buffs that make farming Anomaly Zones faster. Always check these spots after the daily reset to see if any new "Urban Legend" quests have appeared, as these often reward small amounts of Hunter EXP.

By balancing your story progression with disciplined stamina usage and focusing on the Exploration Guide, you will find the path to high-level play in Neverness to Everness both efficient and rewarding. Keep your Arcs sharp and your City Tycoon businesses running to ensure you never fall behind the curve in Hethereau.

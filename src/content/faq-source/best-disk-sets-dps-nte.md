# What are the Best Disk Sets for DPS in Neverness to Everness?

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/best-disk-sets-dps-nte/inline-1.webp)


In Neverness to Everness (NTE), the equipment system is centered around the "Console," which utilizes Cartridges (sets) and Modules (stats). Unlike traditional gear systems, NTE uses a grid-based interface where players must fit geometric shapes into a specific layout to activate bonuses.

Players often ask: "What are the best disk sets for DPS characters?" to ensure they are maximizing their damage output in the endgame. Because the game’s combat relies heavily on elemental reactions like Blossom, Nova, and Scorch, choosing a set that synergizes with your character's element and playstyle is mandatory. This guide focuses on identifying the top-tier Cartridge sets and Module configurations for the current meta DPS units like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte), Mint, and Daffodill.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/best-disk-sets-dps-nte/inline-2.webp)


The absolute best DPS disk set for Anima-element characters (like Nanally and Mint) is "Fireflies and the Forest" due to its massive stacking CRIT DMG bonus that reaches up to 56% at maximum stacks. For Chaos-element DPS like Daffodill, the "Diabolos" set is the gold standard, providing essential Resistance Shred that allows her to bypass enemy defenses during Break windows.

Universal DPS options include "Shadow Creed," which is excellent for skill-heavy attackers who benefit from a flat 25% ATK boost, and "Crimson: Twin Butterflies" for characters relying on Incantation-based damage. To activate these bonuses, you must fit four specific Module shapes (Type II, III, or IV) into your character's Console to trigger the Legendary 4-piece effect.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/best-disk-sets-dps-nte/inline-3.webp)


### Understanding the Console System
Unlike traditional RPGs where you simply equip a chest piece or a ring, NTE uses a "Tetris" style grid called the Console. You equip one Cartridge, which serves as the base for your set bonuses, and then fill the remaining grid with Modules. Modules are the actual stat blocks that provide ATK, HP, and CRIT stats. 

The complexity arises from the shapes; Modules come in Type II (2 cells), Type III (3 cells), and Type IV (4 cells). To unlock the full power of a Cartridge, you must place the specific types of Modules required by that set's recipe. For example, a Legendary set might require two Type III modules and two Type IV modules to be present on the board. If you cannot fit these shapes into the character's unique Console layout, the 4-piece bonus will not activate.

### The 2-Piece vs. 4-Piece Power Spike
Most sets provide a flat 10% Elemental or ATK boost at the 2-piece (Epic) level. While this is helpful for early-game progression, the 4-piece "Legendary" effect defines the endgame meta. These effects often introduce stacking mechanics, DEF ignore, or Resistance Shred that scale exponentially with your character's base stats. For a main DPS, settling for a 2-piece bonus is generally considered a significant loss in potential damage.

### Character Specializations
Every character has a Module Type preference that provides "free" stats. This is a critical layer of optimization:
*   **Nanally:** Gains 6% CRIT Rate for every Type II Module equipped.
*   **Mint:** Gains 7.5% to 8% CRIT Rate for every Type III Module equipped.
*   **Daffodill:** Gains 10% Chaos DMG for every Type III Module equipped.

Because of these specializations, a "perfect" build involves matching the Cartridge requirements with the character's preferred Module type. Mint, for instance, performs best with the Fireflies and the Forest set because it allows her to slot multiple Type III modules, double-dipping into both the set's CRIT DMG and her own innate CRIT Rate bonuses.

### Top-Tier Set Breakdown

#### Deep Dive: Fireflies and the Forest
This is currently the most sought-after set for Anima DPS units. 
*   **2-Piece Bonus:** Anima DMG +10%.
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** Whenever a nearby enemy takes Anima DMG from the team, the wearer gains 8% CRIT DMG for 10 seconds. This stacks up to 7 times for a total of 56% CRIT DMG.
*   **Why it’s Meta:** Because characters like Nanally and Mint hit multiple times per second, they reach max stacks almost instantly. Furthermore, the effect remains active even if the character is off-field, making it viable for sub-DPS units who contribute to elemental reactions.

#### The Power of Diabolos
For Chaos characters like Daffodill, the Diabolos set is indispensable.
*   **2-Piece Bonus:** Chaos DMG +10%.
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** Ignores 12% of enemy Chaos RES. This value increases to 24% for 20 seconds after the wearer triggers a Nova or Scorch reaction.
*   **Why it’s Meta:** Chaos damage is primarily used for "Breaking" enemies. By ignoring resistance, Daffodill can shred shields faster and deal massive "Break DMG" once the enemy's guard is down.

#### Lost Radiance: The Universal Option
While Lost Radiance provides a Cosmos DMG bonus at 2 pieces, its 4-piece effect is universal.
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** Ignores 25% of enemy DEF for 20 seconds after the wearer casts an Ultimate.
*   **Why it’s Meta:** DEF ignore is one of the rarest and most powerful stats in NTE. For characters whose Ultimates have high uptime, this set can outperform element-specific sets in certain boss encounters.

### Stat Priority for Endgame DPS
In Neverness to Everness, substats are visible at Level 0, allowing you to "pre-screen" Modules before investing resources. For a DPS, the priority is consistently:
1.  **CRIT Rate:** Aim for at least 60-70% before buffs.
2.  **CRIT DMG:** Since sets like Fireflies provide a lot of this, ensure your CRIT Rate is high enough to make use of it.
3.  **Elemental DMG %:** Usually found as a Main Stat on Cartridges.
4.  **ATK %:** Important, but often yields diminishing returns compared to CRIT and DMG multipliers.
5.  **Cycle Intensity:** Essential for characters who rely on triggering frequent elemental reactions (like Nanally’s Blossom).

### How to Farm Legendary Cartridges and Modules
Farming in NTE is split into two distinct loops:

1.  **Cartridges (The Rabbit Hole):** You farm these using "Character Pixels" (the game's energy system) in specific domains. The Rabbit Hole is located in Cape Square, south of the New Herland District. Each level of the Rabbit Hole drops different sets; ensure you are farming the specific floor that lists your desired Cartridge.
2.  **Modules (The Rewind System):** Modules are not dropped directly by enemies. Instead, you farm "Carrota Coins" (Bronze, Silver, and Gold) from the Rabbit Hole and then use the "Rewind" function on the Console screen. This is a gear gacha where you spend coins to pull Modules. To get Legendary (Gold) Modules, you must reach Hunter Rank 30 and spend Gold Carrota Coins.

### Best Weapons for DPS Characters
Pairing your Disk sets with the right Arcs (weapons) is crucial for maximizing synergy. For example, Nanally's signature S-rank Arc, **Ready-Ready**, provides a massive boost to CRIT Rate and ATK, perfectly balancing the high CRIT DMG provided by the Fireflies set.

*   **Nanally:** Ready-Ready (S-Rank) - Increases ATK by 15% and boosts Basic Attack damage after using a skill.
*   **Mint:** Fluff of Fleetness (S-Rank) - Provides CRIT DMG and increases ATK every second while on-field.
*   **Daffodill:** Youthful Fantasy (S-Rank) - Boosts Chaos DMG and Break Intensity.
*   **Zero:** The Rain That Shook the World (S-Rank) - Massive Cosmos DMG increase and CRIT Rate.

### NTE Character Tier List
As of the current 2026 meta, Nanally remains the undisputed queen of single-target DPS, often sitting in the "EX" or "S+" tier. Mint is a highly capable A-rank character who can rival S-ranks when equipped with a 5-stack Fireflies set. Daffodill is considered the premier "Breaker" DPS, essential for clearing high-difficulty Anomaly bosses that feature thick shields.

### Elemental Reactions and Synergy Guide
The effectiveness of your Disk sets often depends on your team's ability to trigger reactions.
*   **Blossom (Anima + Cosmos):** Essential for Nanally teams. It creates Vita Buds that explode for AoE damage.
*   **Nova (Chaos + Psyche):** Triggers the secondary effect of the Diabolos set, increasing resistance shred.
*   **Scorch (Chaos + Incantation):** Another way to trigger Diabolos bonuses while dealing damage over time.

When building your Console, always consider if your team can consistently trigger the reactions required by your 4-piece Legendary effects. If your team cannot trigger Nova or Scorch, the Diabolos set loses half of its value, and you might be better off with a universal ATK set like Shadow Creed.

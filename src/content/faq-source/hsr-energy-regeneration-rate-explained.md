In Honkai: Star Rail, the difference between a character who feels clunky and one who feels unstoppable often comes down to a single stat: Energy Regeneration Rate (ERR). Managing your Ultimate uptime is the most effective way to maintain buffs, sustain your team, and maximize damage windows during difficult content like Memory of Chaos or Apocalyptic Shadow.

# HSR Energy Regeneration Rate Explained: Breakpoints & Guide

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/hsr-energy-regeneration-rate-explained/inline-1.webp)


What exactly is Energy Regeneration Rate (ERR) in Honkai: Star Rail? This stat functions as a multiplicative modifier that determines how much Energy a character receives from virtually every action they take on the battlefield. Unlike flat stats such as Attack or Defense, ERR directly dictates the "rotation" of a character—the specific sequence of Basic Attacks and Skills required to fill their Ultimate gauge.

How does the ERR stat impact how often a character can use their Ultimate? Without sufficient ERR, a character might find themselves just a few points short of their Ultimate after three turns, forcing them to take a fourth turn and delaying critical buffs or heals. By hitting specific "breakpoints," players can "shave off" an entire turn from their rotation, effectively increasing their character's power and efficiency by 25% to 33%.

Where can players find this stat within the character's relic and equipment screens? You can view your current ERR by opening the Character menu, selecting "Details," and scrolling down to the "Advanced Stats" section. By default, every character in the game displays a base Energy Regeneration Rate of 100%. Any bonuses from Planar Ornaments, Link Ropes, or Light Cones will be added to this base value (e.g., a 19.4% bonus will show as 119.4% in the menu).

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/hsr-energy-regeneration-rate-explained/inline-2.webp)


Energy Regeneration Rate (ERR) is a percentage-based multiplier that increases the amount of Energy a character gains from almost all actions, including using Skills, Basic Attacks, defeating enemies, and being hit by opponents. The base ERR for every character is 100%, meaning they receive the standard amount of energy (e.g., 20 from a Basic ATK) without any additional modifiers.

Increasing this stat allows characters to reach their Ultimate threshold in fewer turns, which is a concept known as "rotation breakpoints." For example, a character with a 120-energy Ultimate might require four turns to charge their gauge at 100% ERR. By increasing that stat to 119.4% or higher, they may be able to achieve the same result in only three turns, significantly improving their performance in timed battles.

The primary and most consistent source of ERR is the Link Rope (Planar Ornament) main stat. A 5-star Link Rope provides 19.4% ERR at the maximum level of +15. Because ERR cannot appear as a sub-stat on any relic piece, the Link Rope is considered one of the rarest and most valuable items in a player's inventory.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/hsr-energy-regeneration-rate-explained/inline-3.webp)


### How Energy is Generated: The Core Mechanics

Every character in Honkai: Star Rail has a Max Energy cap, ranging from as low as 90 to as high as 180. To use an Ultimate, the character must fill this gauge. Energy is generated through several standard actions, and the amount gained is then multiplied by your total Energy Regeneration Rate.

The formula for actual energy gained is:
**Total Energy Gained = Base Action Energy x (Total ERR% / 100)**

For instance, if a character with 120% ERR uses a Skill (30 base energy), they will receive 36 energy (30 x 1.2).

| Action Type | Base Energy Gained |
| :--- | :--- |
| Basic Attack | 20 |
| Skill | 30 |
| Ultimate Use (Refund) | 5 |
| Defeating an Enemy (Kill) | 10 |
| Follow-up Attack | 5 – 10 (Varies by character) |
| Being Hit (Single Target) | 10 – 15 (Varies by enemy) |
| Being Hit (AoE / Blast) | 2 – 5 |

### Calculating Your Rotations: The 3-Turn vs. 4-Turn Split

A "rotation" refers to the number of turns a character takes between each Ultimate. In high-level play, the goal is usually to achieve a 3-turn rotation (3T) for support characters.

Let’s look at a character with a 120 Energy requirement. After using their Ultimate, they are refunded 5 energy, leaving 115 to be gained. 
*   **At 100% ERR:** Using three Skills (30+30+30) and the 5 refund equals 95 energy. They are 25 points short and must take a 4th turn.
*   **At 119.4% ERR (Rope):** Three Skills now provide 107.46 energy (90 x 1.194). Combined with the 5 refund, the total is 112.46. They are still roughly 3 points short.
*   **Reaching the Breakpoint:** To achieve a guaranteed 3-turn rotation with only Skills, this character would need roughly 123% ERR. This is why players often combine an ERR Link Rope with a 5% ERR Planar set (like Sprightly Vonwacq) and specific Light Cones to bridge the gap.

### The Rarity of ERR: Why the Link Rope is Your Most Important Relic

Unlike stats like ATK% or HP%, which can appear on multiple relic slots and as sub-stats, Energy Regeneration Rate is restricted exclusively to the Link Rope's main stat. Furthermore, data mining and community testing suggest that ERR has a lower drop rate compared to other main stats on Planar Ornaments.

Because you cannot "farm" ERR through sub-stats, your ability to optimize a character's rotation is entirely dependent on finding the correct Link Rope. Many players prioritize using "Self-Modeling Resin" to craft ERR Link Ropes for their Harmony and Abundance characters, as the utility of a faster Ultimate far outweighs the benefit of better sub-stats on a different main stat.

### Light Cones and Relic Sets: Maximizing Your Recharge

While the Link Rope provides the bulk of your ERR, several other sources are vital for hitting specific thresholds:

1.  **Planar Ornament Sets:** Sprightly Vonwacq, Penacony Land of the Dreams, and Lushaka the Sunken Seas all provide a flat 5% ERR bonus. Penacony is particularly popular for mono-element teams, while Lushaka provides an additional ATK boost to the first character in the team lineup.
2.  **Harmony Light Cones:** "But the Battle Isn't Over" (Bronya's signature) is a staple for Harmony units, offering 10% ERR at S1. For players looking at newer options, "A Grounded Ascent" (Sunday's signature) provides 12% ERR and significant team-wide utility.
3.  **Abundance Light Cones:** "Post-Op Conversation" is the premier 4-star choice, offering up to 16% ERR for the wearer's Ultimate at S5. "Night of Fright" (Huohuo's signature) provides a flat 12% ERR.
4.  **The "Cogs" Method:** The 3-star Harmony Light Cone "Meshing Cogs" (and its 4-star counterpart "Memories of the Past") does not provide ERR%, but it grants flat energy (up to 8) when the wearer attacks or is hit. Because this flat energy is added to the "Base Energy" before the ERR multiplier is applied, it is often the key to achieving 3-turn or even 2-turn rotations on characters like Tingyun or [Ruan Mei](https://ntecodex.com/guides/hsr-memories-of-the-past-vs-signature-ruan-mei-gear-guide).

### Exceptions to the Rule: Acheron, Robin, and Fixed Energy

Not every character follows the standard ERR rules. As the game has evolved, several unique mechanics have been introduced:

*   **Acheron:** Acheron does not use Energy at all. Her Ultimate is charged by "Slashed Dream" stacks, which are gained when she uses her Skill or when any unit (ally or enemy) inflicts a debuff. Consequently, ERR stats and ERR Link Ropes are completely useless on Acheron.
*   **Robin:** Robin has a massive 160 Energy cost. While she benefits from ERR, her primary energy source is her Talent, "Tonal Resonance," which grants her a fixed 2 Energy whenever an ally attacks. undisclosed regarding whether future ERR-style buffs will interact with this fixed talent value.
*   **Firefly:** Firefly (SAM) features a unique energy mechanic where her non-enhanced Skill restores a fixed 60% of her Max Energy. She also starts the battle with 50% energy via her traces, allowing her to enter her "Complete Combustion" state almost immediately without heavy ERR investment.
*   **Fixed Energy Gains:** Energy provided by external sources—such as Tingyun’s Ultimate, Huohuo’s Ultimate, or the "Quid Pro Quo" Light Cone—is generally considered "flat energy." This energy is typically not increased by the receiving character's ERR stat. If Tingyun gives 50 energy to a DPS with 119% ERR, that DPS still only receives exactly 50 energy.

### Speed vs. Energy Regeneration Rate

Speed (SPD) and ERR are two sides of the same coin. While ERR increases the energy gained *per action*, Speed increases the *number of actions* taken within a certain timeframe. A character with high Speed but low ERR may take many turns but still struggle to keep their Ultimate active. Conversely, a character with high ERR but low Speed will have a very efficient rotation but may be too slow to react to enemy threats.

For most support characters, the "Golden Ratio" involves reaching at least 134 SPD (to act twice in the first cycle) while maintaining enough ERR to guarantee a 3-turn rotation. For specialized builds, reaching 160+ SPD alongside an ERR rope allows for "0-cycle" strategies in Memory of Chaos.

### Top Characters Who Need an ERR Link Rope

As a general rule, Support characters (Harmony, Abundance, and some Nihility) almost always prefer an ERR Link Rope over ATK%, HP%, or DEF%. Their primary value comes from their Ultimates.

*   **Tingyun:** Requires an ERR rope and often "Meshing Cogs" to maintain a consistent 3-turn rotation to battery the main DPS.
*   **[Ruan Mei](https://ntecodex.com/guides/hsr-memories-of-the-past-vs-signature-ruan-mei-gear-guide):** Needs an ERR rope to ensure her high-cost Ultimate (140) is available frequently to maintain her Resistance Penetration (RES PEN) field.
*   **Huohuo:** As a healer who also provides energy to the team, her Ultimate uptime is paramount.
*   **Robin:** Due to her 160 energy cost, every percentage of ERR is vital to ensure she can re-enter her "Concerto" state.
*   **Pela/Silver Wolf:** These Nihility supports need their Ultimates to apply Defense Shred. An ERR rope allows them to maintain 100% uptime on these debuffs.

### External Energy Sources: Batteries and Light Cone Procs

If you cannot find a high-quality ERR Link Rope, you can supplement your energy needs through "Batteries." These are characters or equipment that provide energy to others:

*   **Tingyun (Ultimate):** Grants 50–60 energy to a single target.
*   **Huohuo (Ultimate):** Grants energy equal to 15%–20% of each ally's Max Energy.
*   **Quid Pro Quo (Light Cone):** A free-to-play Abundance Light Cone that grants 8–16 energy to a random ally with less than 50% energy at the start of the wearer's turn.
*   **Shared Feeling (Light Cone):** Grants a small amount of energy to all allies when the wearer uses their Skill.

By combining these external sources with your character's internal ERR, you can overcome high energy costs and ensure your team's most powerful abilities are always ready when you need them.

### Understanding the Disk and Arc System in NTE

In Neverness to Everness, "Disks" are officially categorized as Arcs. These items serve as the primary weapon system for your characters. Unlike traditional RPG weapons, Arcs are digital records of supernatural phenomena that provide massive base stats and unique passive perks known as Arc Effects.

Every character in NTE possesses a specific "Adapter" type: Bose, Gas, Liquid, Plasma, or Solid. An Arc must match the character's Adapter type for the character to equip it and benefit from its stats. S-Rank Arcs are the highest tier of these items, offering significantly higher Base ATK and secondary stat growth compared to their A-Rank (4-star) counterparts. Understanding how to match these Arcs with your character's role—DPS, Support, or Hybrid—is the first step in mastering the urban combat of Hethereau.

### Best DPS Disks (S-Rank Arcs)

For players focused on maximizing damage, the following Arcs represent the current peak of the NTE meta:

**Raging Flames (Lakshana DPS)**
This Arc is specifically designed for high-frequency Lakshana attackers. Its primary mechanic revolves around stacking damage bonuses whenever the character uses a Redirect Skill or an Ultimate. In the hands of a character like Nanally, these stacks accumulate rapidly, leading to a massive increase in sustained DPS. The Redirect Skill bonus is particularly important because it rewards players for timing their counters and repositioning skills correctly, which is a core part of Nanally's high-skill-ceiling kit.

**Day Off (Cosmos DPS)**
While "Day Off" is the signature disk for the Main Character (MC), its raw power makes it a top-tier choice for any Cosmos-aligned DPS, such as Esper Zero. It provides a flat 30% ATK boost, which is one of the highest unconditional ATK modifiers in the game. Additionally, its energy restoration mechanic triggers upon killing an enemy, allowing your DPS to chain Ultimates together when fighting groups of Anomalies in the streets of Hethereau.

**Black Tome (Chaos DPS)**
The "Black Tome" introduces the "Seals" mechanic. When a character equipped with this Arc uses their skills, they generate Seals that can be unleashed by support skill chains. This summons the Tome itself to deal massive Chaos DMG in an area of effect. Beyond the raw damage, this Arc is highly valued for its Break Intensity stat. Chaos characters are often tasked with breaking enemy shields, and the Black Tome makes this process significantly faster.

### Best Support and Utility Disks

Support Arcs in NTE are often more impactful than DPS Arcs because their buffs apply to the entire team, effectively multiplying the damage of your primary carry.

**Good Boy's Grand Adventure**
Currently the most sought-after support Arc in the game, "Good Boy's Grand Adventure" offers a dual-threat utility package. First, it provides an 18% Charge Efficiency boost, which ensures your support unit can use their Ultimate on every rotation. Second, it grants a 16% ATK buff to all team members whenever the wearer uses a skill. This makes it the meta-defining choice for Sakiri or a support-oriented MC.

**Blow up the Crowd**
This Arc is designed for "off-field" supports. Its effects trigger even when the character holding the Arc is not the active unit on the field. This allows characters like Sakiri to provide continuous ATK buffs to the active DPS without needing to take up valuable field time.

**Hethereau's Keeper**
This is a unique S-Rank Arc that cannot be pulled from "The Fair" (the gacha system). Instead, it is a rare drop or reward associated with the 5-star Wanted Level boss, Officer Whisker. Its unique effect allows the user to summon a spectral version of Officer Whisker to the battlefield. This summon provides defensive utility and shreds enemy resistances, making it an excellent choice for hybrid or tankier characters who need to contribute to team damage.

### How to Obtain 5-Star Disks: Gacha vs. F2P Sources

Acquiring these powerful Arcs requires a mix of luck and resource management.

1.  **The Fair (Gacha):** Most 5-star disks are obtained through the Arc banners in "The Fair." Players use specialized currency to pull for these items. It is generally recommended to save your pulls for "The Ichi-daime" or similar limited-time banners that feature high-tier Arcs like Raging Flames.
2.  **The Arc Shop and Tri-Keys:** This is the primary "pity" mechanic for Arcs. Whenever you pull on an Arc banner, you accumulate Tri-Keys. These Tri-Keys can be spent in the Arc Shop to directly purchase specific 5-star disks. This is the most reliable way for F2P (Free-to-Play) players to guarantee a meta-defining Arc like Good Boy's Grand Adventure.
3.  **Wanted Level Bosses:** As mentioned with Hethereau's Keeper, certain Arcs are tied to world activities. Engaging with high-level supernatural threats in the open world can reward you with Arcs that are otherwise unavailable in the gacha.

### Optimizing Disk Stats and Substats

Once you have acquired an S-Rank Arc, you must focus on its substats to truly unlock its potential. Unlike the fixed main stats, substats are randomized when the Arc is acquired and upgraded.

*   **For DPS:** Prioritize Crit DMG and Crit Rate. Because S-Rank Arcs already provide high Base ATK, Crit stats offer the best scaling for your damage. If the character relies on elemental reactions, look for Elemental DMG (Lakshana, Cosmos, etc.) as a secondary priority.
*   **For Supports:** Charge Efficiency is the king of stats. Your goal is to reach the "breakpoint" where your support can use their Ultimate immediately upon switching in. After Charge Efficiency, look for ATK% or HP% depending on what the support's buffs scale with.
*   **For Breakers:** If you are using a Chaos character or a dedicated shield-breaker, Break Intensity is the most important substat. This allows you to stun bosses faster, creating windows for your DPS to deal massive damage.

The Arc system does not exist in a vacuum; it is part of the broader "Console" customization system in Neverness to Everness.

### NTE Cartridge and Module Guide

Your character's Console consists of the Arc (the disk), Cartridges, and Modules. While the Arc provides the base stats and primary passive, Cartridges function like armor sets or artifacts, providing set bonuses when multiple Cartridges of the same series are equipped. Modules are smaller slots that allow for fine-tuning specific stats like movement speed or elemental resistance. For a 5-star disk to perform at its peak, it must be paired with Cartridges that complement its effects. For example, Raging Flames should be paired with Lakshana-boosting Cartridge sets to maximize Nanally's output.

### The Impact of Awakening

Awakening is the process of using duplicate copies of the same S-Rank Arc to upgrade its Arc Effect. Each Awakening level significantly increases the percentages of the passive buffs. For "Good Boy's Grand Adventure," Awakening can push the team ATK buff even higher, potentially reaching [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-16]. While S-Rank Arcs are powerful at Awakening Level 0, high-refinement A-Rank disks can sometimes outperform them if you have managed to max out the A-Rank's Awakening levels. However, the raw Base ATK of a 5-star disk usually makes it the superior choice once you reach the level cap.

### Future-Proofing Your Account

As the game evolves, new Arcs will inevitably be introduced. However, Arcs that provide universal utility, like "Good Boy's Grand Adventure," are likely to remain in the meta for a long time. These are "safe" investments for your Tri-Keys. Conversely, Arcs that provide very specific niche bonuses may be power-crept by newer, more specialized releases. When choosing which 5-star disk to invest your resources into, consider how versatile the Arc is across different characters of the same Adapter type.

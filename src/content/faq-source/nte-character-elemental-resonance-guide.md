What is the elemental resonance system in Neverness to Everness (NTE)?

The elemental resonance system in NTE, officially titled the "Esper Cycle," is the core combat mechanic that dictates how different character elements interact. Unlike traditional RPGs where elements might simply deal extra damage to certain enemy types, NTE requires players to actively chain elements together to trigger specific combat phenomena.

How do character elements interact to trigger combat bonuses?

Interaction occurs through the Esper Meter. When a character’s meter is full, swapping them onto the field consumes the meter and applies their element to the target. If the previous character had already applied a compatible element, a reaction occurs. These reactions range from summoning autonomous turrets to shredding enemy shields.

What are the specific names and effects of every elemental reaction in the game?

The system is categorized into Duo Cycle reactions (two-element interactions) and Trio Cycle reactions (three-element chains). The primary reactions include Blossom, Hexed, Scorch, Nova, Stain, and Remora for Duos, while Charge and Discord represent the high-tier Trio reactions.

### The Six Esper Elements and the Wheel

The Esper Cycle operates on a "forward-flow" logic. The elements are arranged in a specific sequence: **Lakshana > Cosmos > Anima > Incantation > Chaos > Psyche**. 

To trigger a Duo reaction, you must follow this clockwise order. For example, applying Cosmos and then swapping to an Anima character will trigger a reaction. However, swapping from Anima back to Cosmos will not trigger a standard Duo reaction, as it moves against the flow of the Esper Wheel. This makes team order and swap timing the most important skills to master in NTE’s high-level content, such as the urban anomalies and boss rushes.

### Trio Reactions: The Peak of Combat Efficiency

Trio reactions are advanced chains that occur when you complete two Duo reactions in rapid succession. These are the most powerful effects in Neverness to Everness and are essential for clearing timed challenges.

**1. Charge (Lakshana > Cosmos > Anima)**
The Charge reaction is triggered by completing a Remora reaction followed immediately by a Blossom reaction. When Charge activates, the entire team receives a massive surge of Ultimate Energy. This is the premier strategy for "Burst Teams" that rely on frequent Ultimate usage to clear waves. Characters like Nanally benefit immensely from the Charge cycle, as it keeps her Anima-infused abilities active almost indefinitely.

**2. Discord (Incantation > Chaos > Psyche)**
Discord is triggered by completing a Scorch reaction followed by a Nova reaction. This reaction is specifically designed for boss "Breaking." When Discord triggers, it deals significant damage to the enemy's Break Meter (shield). Once the Break Meter is depleted, the boss enters a vulnerable state where they take increased damage and remain immobile. If you are struggling with high-HP bosses in the late-game districts, a Discord-focused team is the solution.

### Optimizing Meter Gain and Cycle Rate

You cannot trigger reactions if your Esper Meter is empty. Every character has a hidden "Cycle Rate" stat that determines how quickly they generate meter.

*   **Normal Attacks:** Provide steady, low-level meter gain.
*   **Esper Skills:** Provide a significant chunk of meter (usually 20-30% per cast).
*   **Perfect Parries:** This is the most skill-intensive method. Successfully parrying an enemy attack with the correct timing grants an instant 100% Esper Meter fill for the active character.
*   **Cycle Rate Buffs:** Certain Weapon Arcs and gear sets provide a "Cycle Rate" percentage increase. For a "Driver" character (the one you swap *into* most often), prioritizing this stat is better than raw damage.

### Building Elemental Lanes for Your Team

In NTE, you aren't just picking three strong characters; you are building an "Elemental Lane." A lane is a sequence of elements that allows every swap to be productive.

**The 3-Element Continuous Lane**
The most common setup. You pick three elements that follow each other on the wheel.
*   *Example:* Zero (Cosmos) -> Nanally (Anima) -> Baicang (Incantation).
*   *Rotation:* Zero applies Cosmos -> Swap to Nanally (Triggers Blossom) -> Nanally builds meter -> Swap to Baicang (Triggers Hexed) -> Swap back to Zero. 
*   *Note:* In this 3-man rotation, the jump from Incantation back to Cosmos does not trigger a reaction, creating a "dead swap."

**The 4-Element Advanced Lane (Tag-Team)**
As players unlock more character slots or use specific assists, 4-element lanes become viable.
*   *Example:* Lakshana Character -> Zero (Cosmos) -> Nanally (Anima) -> Baicang (Incantation).
*   This allows you to trigger Remora, then Blossom, then Hexed in a single continuous loop.

**Passive Stacking**
Every character in NTE possesses a unique Esper Passive. These passives often buff a specific reaction even when the character is not on the field. For instance, if you have both Nanally and Jiuyuan in a team (both Anima), their passives might both contribute to "Blossom Damage +20%." Even though you can only have one character active, the passive stacking makes the resulting Blossom turrets significantly more lethal.

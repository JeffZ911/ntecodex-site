# How to Trigger Discord Reaction in Neverness to Everness (NTE)

To trigger the [Discord reaction](https://ntecodex.com/faq) in NTE, you must apply both the Nova (Psyche + Chaos) and Scorch (Incantation + Chaos) effects to the same enemy simultaneously. This Trio Reaction significantly reduces the target's Break cap, allowing your team to stagger even the most resilient bosses with a single rotation.

### Understanding Trio Reactions in NTE

In Neverness to Everness, reactions are categorized based on how many elements are involved in the chain. Standard Duo Reactions occur when two elements interact, such as Nova (Psyche + Chaos) or Scorch. However, a Trio Reaction like Discord represents a higher tier of elemental synergy. It is not triggered by a single button press but by the strategic overlapping of two specific Duo Reactions. 

Discord is unique because it requires three distinct elemental affinities—Chaos, Psyche, and Incantation—to be applied in a specific sequence. Unlike Duo Reactions that might provide a flat damage burst or a minor status effect, Discord targets the underlying mechanics of the enemy's defense, specifically the Break cap.

### The Role of Discord in the Meta

As of the current version of the game, Discord has become the cornerstone of the "Break Meta." Many endgame bosses possess massive stagger bars that are difficult to deplete through standard attacks alone. By triggering Discord, players can bypass a significant portion of this requirement. This makes characters with Chaos, Psyche, and Incantation affinities highly sought after for high-difficulty content like the [Anomalous Zones](https://ign.com).

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-trigger-discord-reaction-nte/inline-2.webp)


Discord is an advanced Trio Reaction that functions as a powerful debuff against enemy defenses. It is triggered by stacking the Nova and Scorch Duo Reactions on a single target. 

To achieve this, you must follow a specific elemental formula:
1.  **Requirement 1:** [Trigger Nova](https://ntecodex.com/faq) by combining Psyche and Chaos.
2.  **Requirement 2:** Trigger Scorch by combining Incantation and Chaos.
3.  **The Result:** When both Nova and Scorch are active on the same enemy, the [Discord reaction](https://ntecodex.com/faq) triggers automatically.

The primary effect of Discord is a massive deduction from the enemy's Break cap. This is not merely damage to the stagger bar; it actually lowers the total amount of "stagger" required to put the enemy into a vulnerable state.

### The Discord Formula

The following table breaks down the elemental components required to achieve the [Discord reaction](https://ntecodex.com/faq):

| Duo Reaction | Psyche | Chaos | Nova |
| Duo Reaction | Incantation | Chaos | Scorch |
| **Trio Reaction** | **Nova (Duo)** | **Scorch (Duo)** | **Discord** |

### Why Discord is Essential for Boss Fights

In the current combat environment, particularly following the release of Lacrimosa, the ability to shred an enemy's Break cap has become more valuable than raw DPS in many scenarios. When an enemy's Break cap is reduced via Discord, they enter the "Execution" phase much faster. During this phase, your team can perform high-damage finishers that ignore standard defense ratings. Without Discord, players often find themselves chipping away at a boss's health for extended periods, risking team wipes during the boss's enrage timers.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-trigger-discord-reaction-nte/inline-3.webp)


Executing the Discord reaction requires a disciplined rotation and careful management of your team's Esper Meter, also known as Cycle Energy. Because the durations of the prerequisite Duo Reactions differ, the order in which you apply them is critical.

### Mastering the Rotation Timing

To [trigger Discord](https://ntecodex.com/faq) effectively, follow this step-by-step rotation:

1.  **Build Cycle Energy:** Begin the fight by using your characters' basic attacks, parries, or perfect dodges to fill the Esper Meter. You will need enough energy to perform at least two swaps within the Esper Cycle.
2.  **Apply Scorch First:** Start with an Incantation character like Baicang and swap to a Chaos character like Daffodil. This triggers Scorch. Scorch is the foundation of the Discord reaction because it has a relatively long duration of 15 seconds.
3.  **Apply Nova:** While the Scorch effect is still active on the enemy, swap from your Chaos character to a Psyche character such as Haniel or Fadia. This triggers Nova. 
4.  **The Discord Window:** The Nova effect only lasts for 5 seconds. Therefore, you must ensure that your Psyche character enters the field and applies their elemental damage while the 15-second Scorch timer is still ticking. Once both are active, the Discord icon will appear above the enemy's health bar, and their Break cap will visibly shrink.

### Best Characters for Discord Teams

Choosing the right "enablers" is the difference between a clunky rotation and a seamless one. 

*   **Chaos Enablers:** Daffodil was the original choice for most players, but the v1.1 update introduced Lacrimosa, who is currently the premier Chaos character for Discord teams. Her passive ability, Tomato Banquet, further amplifies the damage enemies take while under the Discord effect.
*   **Psyche Support:** Haniel provides excellent Psyche application through her off-field abilities, making the 5-second Nova window much easier to hit. Fadia is a strong alternative for players who prefer a more aggressive, on-field Psyche presence.
*   **Incantation Starters:** Baicang remains a top-tier choice for starting the Scorch reaction due to his rapid Incantation application and high energy generation.

### How to Fill the Esper Meter Instantly

If you find yourself struggling to maintain the rotation, the protagonist, [Esper Zero](https://ign.com), offers a unique solution. Their [Redirect Skill](https://ign.com) can immediately trigger an Esper Cycle regardless of your current energy levels, provided the skill is off cooldown. This is a "get out of jail free" card for when a boss enters a critical phase and you need to [trigger Discord](https://ntecodex.com/faq) immediately but lack the built-up energy.

### The Impact of Break Cap Reduction

The visual indicator of a successful Discord reaction is the enemy's Break meter turning a distinct color and shrinking in size. According to community testing on thegamer.com, the reduction is percentage-based, meaning it scales effectively against bosses with massive health pools. Once the meter is depleted, the enemy is incapacitated, allowing for a "Break Burst" where all incoming damage is significantly multiplied.

While Discord is the most popular Trio Reaction for shredding defenses, it is not the only high-level elemental interaction in Neverness to Everness. Depending on your team composition and the specific challenge you are facing, you might consider other elemental synergies.

### Discord vs. Charge: Which is Better?

The other major Trio Reaction in the current meta is Charge. While Discord focuses on the Break cap, Charge is triggered by combining Vita Pistils and Remora. 

*   **Discord:** Best for boss fights where staggering the enemy is the priority. It requires Chaos, Psyche, and Incantation.
*   **Charge:** Acts as an "energy battery" for the team. It rapidly regenerates Ultimate Energy for all party members. This is often preferred for "Ultimate Spam" team compositions that rely on high-cost bursts rather than the Break mechanic.

Most players find that Discord is superior for world bosses and high-tier anomalies, while Charge excels in wave-based content where clearing mobs with Ultimates is more efficient.

### Top Tier Discord Team Compositions

With the introduction of Lacrimosa in version 1.1, the "Gold Standard" Discord team has shifted. A highly effective team currently consists of:
1.  **Lacrimosa (Chaos):** The primary enabler and sub-DPS.
2.  **Baicang (Incantation):** The primary starter and energy generator.
3.  **Haniel (Psyche):** The secondary enabler who provides the Nova trigger.

This team benefits from Lacrimosa's Tomato Banquet passive, which scales off the number of reactions triggered, making the frequent Discord procs incredibly lethal.

### Common Mistakes When Triggering Trio Reactions

The most frequent error is applying Nova before Scorch. Because Nova only lasts 5 seconds, if you apply it first and then spend too much time swapping to your Incantation character to trigger Scorch, the Nova effect will likely expire before the overlap occurs. Always prioritize the longer-lasting Scorch (15 seconds) first to give yourself a comfortable window to execute the rest of the rotation.

Another common mistake is ignoring the Esper Cycle Wheel. Elements must be applied in an order that moves around the wheel; trying to jump across non-adjacent elements without a proper bridge character like [Esper Zero](https://ign.com) will result in failed reactions and wasted energy.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I trigger Discord with only two characters?
No, the Discord reaction requires three specific elemental affinities: Chaos, Psyche, and Incantation. Since each character in NTE typically only carries one primary elemental affinity, you must have a team of three characters that cover these three elements to trigger the reaction.

### What is the best Chaos character for a Discord team?
Currently, Lacrimosa is considered the best Chaos character for Discord teams due to her v1.1 buffs and the Tomato Banquet passive. However, Daffodil remains a very strong free-to-play friendly alternative for players who haven't pulled for limited banners.

### Does the Discord reaction do direct damage?
While the process of applying Scorch and Nova deals elemental damage, the Discord reaction itself is primarily a utility debuff. Its main function is to reduce the enemy's Break cap, which facilitates faster staggering rather than providing a single large burst of health damage.

### How do I know if Discord has been triggered?
When Discord is successfully applied, a specific purple and gold icon will appear under the enemy's health bar. Additionally, you will see a portion of the enemy's white Break meter disappear or change color, signifying that the total stagger requirement has been reduced.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-disk-set-bonuses-guide/inline-2.webp)


In Neverness to Everness, "Disks" are officially referred to as **Cartridges**. These items serve as the core of an Esper's build and are slotted into a 20-slot grid called the **Console**. Set bonuses are categorized into two tiers: **Epic (2-piece)** and **Legendary (4-piece)**.

Activating a 2-piece bonus is straightforward; you simply equip the Cartridge. However, activating the 4-piece Legendary effect requires you to place **Modules** (geometric, Tetris-like blocks) into the Console grid in specific shapes—such as L-shapes, Z-shapes, or 1x4 blocks—that match the Cartridge's unique requirements.

### The Core Gearing Loop: Cartridges vs. Modules

The gearing process in NTE is a two-step loop. First, you farm **Anomaly Zones**, specifically the **Rabbit Hole**, to acquire high-rarity Cartridges. These determine your build's ceiling. Second, you acquire Modules through the **Rewind** system using a currency called **Carrotas**. While the Cartridge provides the percentage-based stat boosts, the Modules provide the flat stats (Flat ATK, Flat HP) and act as the "keys" to unlock the Cartridge's full potential. You cannot activate a Legendary effect without the correct Module shapes, making the spatial management of your Console grid just as important as the stats themselves.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-disk-set-bonuses-guide/inline-3.webp)


The Console system is the heart of character customization in Neverness to Everness. Every Esper, regardless of rarity, begins with a **20-slot grid (4x5)**. This grid is where the interaction between Cartridges and Modules takes place.

### The Console System: Spatial Optimization

When you open an Esper's gear menu, you are presented with the Console. You can only equip **one Cartridge** per character. This Cartridge occupies a fixed 2x2 space in the center of the grid. The remaining 16 slots must be filled with Modules. 

Modules come in various geometric shapes. To unlock the Legendary (4-piece) effect of your equipped Cartridge, you must place Modules that satisfy the "Shape Requirement" listed on the Cartridge's detail page. For example, if a "Lost Radiance" Cartridge requires two L-shaped Modules and one 1x4 Module to be adjacent to it, the Legendary effect will remain locked until those specific shapes are fitted into the grid. This adds a layer of strategy where players must often choose between a Module with "perfect stats" and a Module with the "perfect shape."

### Activating Legendary Effects

Legendary effects are the most powerful passives in the game. They often change how a character is played. For instance, the **Lost Radiance** set's DEF ignore is essential for clearing high-level Anomaly Zones where enemy armor scaling becomes a significant hurdle. 

To activate these:
1.  **Check the Cartridge Requirements:** View the "Resonance" tab on your Cartridge.
2.  **Match the Geometry:** Use the Module inventory to find shapes that fit the empty spaces around the central Cartridge.
3.  **Color Coding:** Some high-tier Cartridges also require Modules of a specific color (Type) to be placed in specific quadrants of the grid.

### Optimizing the Console Grid: The Tetris Strategy

To maximize your character's power, you should follow the "Type-First" rule. If you are building **Nanally**, your priority is to fill as many of the 16 available Module slots as possible with **Type 2 (Red)** Modules. However, because you also need to satisfy the shape requirements for her Cartridge (likely **Lost Radiance** or **Vibrant Pulse**), you may have to use a Type 1 or Type 3 Module if it is the only one that fits the required shape.

The most efficient players keep a wide variety of "trash" Modules in their inventory specifically for their shapes, even if their sub-stats are poor. Activating the 4-piece Legendary effect is almost always more beneficial than having slightly better sub-stats on a Module that fails to complete the set.

### Where to Farm: Anomaly Zones and the Rewind System

Farming gear in NTE is divided into two distinct activities:

1.  **Anomaly Zones (The Rabbit Hole):** This is the primary source of Cartridges. Each "Floor" or "Zone" of the Rabbit Hole drops specific sets. For example, the "Neon District Anomaly" is the best place to farm the **Lost Radiance** set. You consume **Stamina** (Energy) to claim rewards here.
2.  **The Rewind System:** Modules are often obtained as side-drops in Anomaly Zones, but the **Rewind** system is how you perfect them. By using **Carrotas**—a currency earned through daily missions and recycling unwanted gear—you can "Rewind" a Module. This process allows you to reroll either its sub-stats or its geometric shape. 

### The Hunter Level 30 Breakpoint

Progression in NTE is gated by your **Hunter Level**. Before Hunter Level 30, it is advised not to spend your "Golden Carrotas" or heavy amounts of Stamina on farming. At **Hunter Level 30**, the drop rate for **Gold-tier (Legendary)** Cartridges and Modules increases significantly. This is the point where you should begin optimizing your Console grid for the "endgame." Prior to this, simply use whatever high-stat Modules you find and focus on leveling your Espers and Arcs (weapons).

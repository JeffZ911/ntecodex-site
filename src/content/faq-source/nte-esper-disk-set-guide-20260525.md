# Neverness to Everness (NTE) Esper Disk Set Guide

In the high-octane urban world of Neverness to Everness, your combat efficiency is determined by more than just your reflexes. While your choice of Arc (weapon) provides the foundation for your damage, the Esper Disk system—known in-game as the Console—is what defines your character's ceiling. Whether you are aiming to clear high-difficulty Anomaly Zones or push through the endgame challenges of Beyond the Rails, understanding how to pair Cartridges with the correct Modules is essential for any serious player.

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/nte-esper-disk-set-guide-20260525/inline-1.webp)


### Defining the Esper Disk and Cartridge System
In Neverness to Everness, "Esper Disks" is a community term often used to describe the dual-layered equipment system consisting of **Cartridges** and **Modules**. These items are slotted into a character’s **Console**, a grid-based interface that functions similarly to a spatial puzzle. 

A Cartridge acts as the "brain" of the set, providing a primary stat and defining the specific set bonuses available to the character. Modules are the "components" that fill the grid around the Cartridge. They provide the bulk of your sub-stats and are the keys required to unlock the Cartridge's powerful set effects. Unlike traditional RPG gear, you cannot simply equip a set; you must physically fit the required Module shapes into the Console grid to activate your bonuses.

### The Role of Gear in Character Progression
Gear in NTE is the primary driver of elemental scaling and utility. While leveling up your character increases base HP and ATK, the Console system provides multiplicative bonuses like Elemental DMG, CRIT Rate, and DEF Penetration. 

Optimizing your NTE Esper Disk set guide strategy is critical because the game’s "Esper Cycle" mechanic—the elemental reaction system—relies heavily on your stats to trigger meaningful damage. Without a well-fitted Console, even S-Rank characters like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or Zero will struggle against the damage sponges found in later Hunter Level activities.

### Why is optimizing your Esper Disk sets critical for endgame content like Beyond the Rails?
Beyond the Rails represents the pinnacle of NTE’s combat challenges as of May 2026. In this mode, enemies possess high resistance values and massive health pools that cannot be overcome by raw levels alone. Optimization allows you to:
*   **Maximize Set Bonuses:** Activating a 4-piece "Legendary" effect can increase your damage output by over 25% or provide team-wide buffs.
*   **Synergize with Character Passives:** Many characters, such as Mint or Zero, have unique passives that trigger based on the *type* of Modules you use (e.g., gaining CRIT Rate for every 3-cell Module equipped).
*   **Maintain Rotation Fluidity:** Sets like Speedy Hedgehog provide "Charge Efficiency," ensuring your Ultimate is ready as soon as your rotation cycles back to your support.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-esper-disk-set-guide-20260525/inline-2.webp)


Esper Disks, primarily referred to as "Cartridges" in-game, are the core equipment pieces that provide base stats and set bonuses. A full set consists of a Cartridge and matching Modules fitted into the character's Console grid. 

Set bonuses are divided into two tiers:
1.  **Epic (2-piece) Bonus:** Requires two Modules of the correct shape to be fitted into the Console.
2.  **Legendary (4-piece) Bonus:** Requires four Modules of the correct shape to be fitted into the Console.

These bonuses typically provide elemental DMG buffs, ATK increases, or specialized utility like DEF penetration. Farming for these pieces occurs in the **Rabbit Hole** Anomaly Zone, which consumes **Character Pixels** (stamina). High-rarity (S-Rank/Gold) gear only becomes efficiently farmable once you reach **Hunter Level 30**.

### Summary of Console Mechanics

| Feature | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Cartridge** | Provides 1 Main Stat and 4 Sub-stats. Determines the Set Bonus. |
| **Modules** | Provides 2 Fixed Main Stats (ATK/HP) and 4 Sub-stats. Used to activate Set Bonuses. |
| **Epic Effect** | 2-piece set bonus activated by matching 2 Module shapes. |
| **Legendary Effect** | 4-piece set bonus activated by matching 4 Module shapes. |
| **Console Grid** | A "Tetris-like" board where pieces must be manually placed and rotated. |
| **Farming Currency** | **Carrota Coins** (Bronze, Silver, Gold) are used for the Rewind gacha to get Modules. |

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-esper-disk-set-guide-20260525/inline-3.webp)


The Console system is one of the most distinct mechanics in Neverness to Everness. It moves away from the "equip and forget" style of gear, requiring players to engage in a spatial mini-game to maximize their character's potential.

### Understanding the Console: Cartridges vs. Modules
Every character in NTE has a unique Console layout. The board size and the placement of "blocked" cells vary, meaning a Module configuration that works for Zero might not fit on Nanally’s board.

*   **Cartridges:** These are obtained directly as drops from the Rabbit Hole. When you equip a Cartridge, it sits in a central slot and projects "required shapes" onto the board. For example, a set might require two "L-shaped" modules and two "I-shaped" modules to reach its Legendary tier.
*   **Modules:** These are obtained through the **Rewind** system—a specialized gacha located within the Console menu. You spend Carrota coins earned from Rabbit Hole runs to pull for Modules. Modules come in different "Types" (II, III, and IV), which refers to how many cells they occupy on the grid.

### Top-Tier Esper Disk Sets and Bonuses
As of Version 1.0, several sets have emerged as the "meta" choices for specific roles. Choosing the right set is the first step in any NTE Esper Disk set guide.

#### Lost Radiance (Cosmos DPS)
This is the premier set for Cosmos-element attackers like Zero. 
*   **2-Piece Bonus:** Cosmos DMG +10%.
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** Ignores 25% of enemies' DEF for 20 seconds after the wearer casts an Ultimate. 
*   **Why it’s good:** DEF ignore is a rare and powerful multiplier that scales exceptionally well in the late game.

#### Shadow Creed (Generalist / ATK Buffer)
A highly versatile set that works on almost any ATK-scaling character.
*   **2-Piece Bonus:** ATK +10%.
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** Increases wearer's ATK by 25% for 20 seconds after casting a Redirect Skill.
*   **Why it’s good:** It has a high up-time and is easier to farm early on than element-specific sets.

#### Fireflies and the Forest (Anima DPS)
The best-in-slot choice for Nanally and other Anima-focused damage dealers.
*   **2-Piece Bonus:** Anima DMG +10%.
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** Every time a nearby enemy takes Anima DMG from any team member, the wearer gains 8% CRIT DMG, stacking up to 7 times (56% total).
*   **Why it’s good:** Since Nanally triggers frequent follow-up attacks, she can maintain maximum stacks indefinitely.

#### Speedy Hedgehog (Support / Utility)
Essential for supports who need to cycle their Ultimates quickly.
*   **2-Piece Bonus:** +12% Charge Efficiency.
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** All allies gain +15% ATK for 20 seconds after the wearer casts an Ultimate.

### The Tetris Mechanic: Fitting Modules for Maximum Stats
Placing Modules isn't just about meeting the set requirements; it's about maximizing the "Type" bonuses.
*   **Type II (2 cells):** Provides lower base stats but is easy to fit into tight corners.
*   **Type III (3 cells):** The "sweet spot" for most builds. Characters like Mint gain 7.5% CRIT Rate for every Type III module on their board.
*   **Type IV (4 cells):** Provides the highest base ATK and HP stats but can be difficult to place without blocking other required shapes.

To optimize your board, you should first place the four Modules required for your Legendary set bonus. Once the bonus is active, fill the remaining empty cells with whatever Type III or Type IV modules have the best sub-stats.

### How to Farm: Rabbit Hole Locations and Carrota Coins
The Rabbit Hole is located in the **Cape Square** district of Hethereau, specifically on the southern side of the New Herland District. 

1.  **Select a Node:** Different nodes drop different Cartridge sets. Check the "Possible Rewards" screen before entering.
2.  **Spend Character Pixels:** Each run costs 40 Pixels. You can choose to spend 80 Pixels for a "Double Drop" run to save time.
3.  **Collect Carrota:** Upon clearing a Rabbit Hole stage, you will receive Carrota coins (Bronze, Silver, or Gold). 
4.  **Rewind for Modules:** Go to your character's Console menu and select "Rewind." Use your Gold Carrota for the "Hard" difficulty pull to ensure you receive S-Rank (Orange) Modules.

### Stat Priority: Crit Rate, Crit DMG, and Cycle Rate
When refining your Cartridges and Modules, look for these key stats in order:
1.  **CRIT Rate / CRIT DMG:** Essential for all DPS characters. Aim for a 1:2 ratio (e.g., 60% CRIT Rate to 120% CRIT DMG).
2.  **Elemental DMG Bonus:** This is usually found as the Main Stat on the Cartridge. Ensure it matches your character's element (Cosmos, Anima, Chaos, etc.).
3.  **Cycle Intensity / Rate:** This stat determines how quickly you can trigger Esper Cycle reactions. High Cycle Intensity is vital for quick-swap teams.
4.  **ATK%:** A solid secondary stat that scales your base damage.

### Best Sets for Meta Characters (Nanally, Zero, Jiuyuan)
*   **Nanally:** Best-in-slot is **Fireflies and the Forest**. Pair this with her signature Arc, **Ready-Ready**, from "The Ichi-daime" banner. Focus on CRIT DMG sub-stats, as her kit provides significant CRIT Rate naturally.
*   **Zero:** **Lost Radiance** is the top choice for a main DPS build. If using Zero as a reaction support to trigger "Blossom" for Nanally, **Speedy Hedgehog** is a viable alternative to keep the team's ATK buffed.
*   **Jiuyuan:** [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-25] regarding her specific signature set name, but she currently excels with **Fireflies and the Forest** or **Shadow Creed** in Anima-focused Blossom teams.
*   **Mint:** Always prioritize **Type III Modules** regardless of the set, as her passive CRIT Rate bonus from these pieces is too strong to ignore.

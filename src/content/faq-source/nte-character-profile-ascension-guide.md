### The Role of Arcs and Consoles in Character Growth

A high-level Esper is only as good as their equipment. The NTE profile system includes two major gear categories: Arcs and Consoles.

**Arcs (Weapons)**
Arcs must be leveled and ascended similarly to Espers. Using "Arc Enhancement Chips," you can increase the weapon's base ATK and secondary stat (such as Crit Rate or Energy Recharge). If you obtain a duplicate Arc, you can "Tune" it to increase the rank of its passive ability, providing massive power spikes.

**Consoles (Cartridges and Modules)**
The Console system is NTE's version of an accessory or artifact system. 
*   **Cartridges:** These provide primary stat boosts.
*   **Modules:** These act as sub-components that offer percentage-based increases to specific attributes like Chaos Damage or Psyche Resistance.
*   **Set Bonuses:** Equipping multiple Modules from the same "Brand" (e.g., the "Hethereau Customs" set) unlocks unique set bonuses that can fundamentally change how an Esper plays, such as reducing cooldowns after a successful dodge.

### Advanced Profile Systems: Awakenings and Resonance

Once an Esper reaches their level cap, you can further enhance them through the Awakening and Resonance systems.

**Awakenings**
This system utilizes duplicates of the character obtained through the "The Ichi-daime" banner or standard recruitment. Each Awakening level (up to 6) grants a significant boost to a specific skill or provides a new utility, such as energy restoration for the entire team.

**Resonance**
The Resonance tree is a talent map unique to each Esper. By spending regional materials and Beetle Coins, you can path through this tree to customize your build. Some nodes offer flat stat increases, while "Major Resonance" nodes can add new properties to your Ultimate skill, such as adding a lifesteal effect or increasing the area of effect.

## How do weapon stats and scaling work in Neverness to Everness (NTE)?

![How do weapon stats and scaling work in Neverness to Everness (NTE)?](/img/nte-weapon-stats-scaling-guide-20260523/inline-1.webp)


Weapon stats in Neverness to Everness are tied to specialized equipment known as Arcs. Unlike traditional RPG weapons, Arcs are digitalized combat disks that provide three layers of power: Base ATK, a secondary Substat, and a unique Passive Effect. Scaling occurs through three distinct progression paths: Enhancement (Leveling), Ascension (Breakthrough), and Mixing (Refinement). To maximize an Arc's performance, players must match the Arc’s type—Solid, Plasma, Gas, Liquid, or Synthesis—with the corresponding Esper’s compatibility.

Materials for scaling these weapons are primarily gathered through Anomaly Zones and city exploration. Leveling requires Pigments (Dye), while breaching level caps requires Ascension materials found in the Bubble Can Factory. Furthermore, the Mixing system allows players to consume duplicate Arcs to enhance the potency of the weapon's passive ability, scaling from Tier 1 (T1) up to Tier 5 (T5).

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-weapon-stats-scaling-guide-20260523/inline-3.webp)


The depth of the Arc system lies in how it interacts with an Esper’s kit. Because Arcs are type-locked, you cannot equip a Plasma Arc like Ready-Ready on a Solid-type character like Hotori. This restriction ensures that weapon passives are tightly balanced around the specific mechanics of their intended users, such as Nanally’s Tiger Talisman system or Zero’s energy-hungry Ultimate.

### The Anatomy of an Arc: Base ATK vs. Substats

Every Arc features a Base ATK stat that increases with every level. This value is added to the Esper's base stats before percentage-based modifiers are applied, making high-rarity Arcs like Marching Beyond Time significantly more valuable for endgame damage.

Substats provide the necessary specialized scaling for specific builds. Current S-rank Arcs typically feature one of the following:
*   **CRIT Rate:** Essential for characters who rely on frequent hits, like Nanally.
*   **Charge Efficiency:** Increases the rate at which Ultimate Energy is gained, vital for the Main Character (Zero).
*   **CRIT DMG:** High-end scaling for Espers with naturally high critical hit chances.

For example, the S-rank Arc **Day Off** provides a massive 33% Charge Efficiency at level 80, allowing Zero to maintain high uptime on the Eclipse field. Meanwhile, **Ready-Ready** provides a 24% CRIT Rate boost, which is the foundation for Nanally’s "Commander Tiger Talisman" damage spikes.

### How to Level Up and Ascend Arcs

To increase an Arc’s level, you must use Pigments (often referred to as Dye in the community). These come in different tiers, providing varying amounts of Arc EXP. Pigments are primarily farmed in the **Houdinii’s Magic Stage** Anomaly Zone.

Leveling is gated by the Ascension system. Once an Arc reaches its current cap (e.g., Level 20, 30, 40), you must perform a Breakthrough. This requires Beetle Coins and specific materials from the **Bubble Can Factory**. The complexity and rarity of these materials increase as you approach the Level 80 cap.

### Arc Mixing: Maximizing Passive Potential

Arc Mixing is the refinement process where you sacrifice a duplicate of the same Arc to increase its Mixing Intensity. Most Arcs can be mixed up to five times (T5). This does not change the Base ATK or Substat, but it significantly boosts the Passive Effect.

Taking **Marching Beyond Time** as an example, the T1 passive provides a 16% ATK bonus and a 24% Ultimate CRIT DMG boost when consuming Wastetime stacks. At T5, the ATK bonus scales to 32%, and the Ultimate CRIT DMG boost increases to 48%, effectively doubling the weapon's specialized offensive output.

The weapon system is intrinsically tied to the gacha mechanics and character progression. Understanding how to acquire these Arcs is just as important as knowing how to level them.

### Best Arcs for Starting Characters

For new players, focusing on the Main Character (Zero) and the first featured S-rank character (Nanally) is the most efficient path. 
*   **Zero (MC):** Always prioritize getting **Day Off**. Since it is free and provides Charge Efficiency, it ensures Zero can use her Ultimate frequently to trigger team reactions.
*   **Nanally:** If you pulled Nanally from **The Ichi-daime** banner, her best-in-slot is **Ready-Ready**. If you are F2P, the A-rank Arc **Us.** is a solid Plasma alternative that boosts Basic Attack damage.
*   **Hotori:** As a Solid-type DPS, she benefits most from **Marching Beyond Time**. If that is unavailable, **Blow up the Crowd** is a high-tier alternative that provides off-field ATK buffs for the team.

### Understanding the Arc Research Program Pity System

The Arc Research Program is the dedicated banner system for weapons. It uses a currency called **Tri-Keys**. 
*   **S-Rank Pity:** You are guaranteed an S-rank Arc within 60 pulls.
*   **Featured Guarantee:** There is an 80-pull hard guarantee for the specific featured Arc (e.g., the "Tiger Special" banner for Ready-Ready).
*   **Standard Pool:** Arcs like **Youthful Fantasy** and **Contemplative Cat** are always available in the standard rotation and can be pulled using Fabricated Dice on the standard board if you land on the correct tiles.

### How to Farm Weapon Upgrade Materials Efficiently

Efficiency in NTE is determined by how you spend your **Character Pixels** (stamina). 
*   **Bubble Can Factory:** This should be your primary destination for Ascension materials. The drops are type-specific, so check the daily rotation to ensure you are farming for the correct Arc type (Solid, Plasma, etc.).
*   **Houdinii’s Magic Stage:** Use this for Pigments (Dye) and Shell Credits (Beetle Coins).
*   **City Tycoon & Apartments:** Once you reach higher Hunter Levels, purchasing properties like the **Golden Capital** (2 million Fons) allows you to place furniture like the **Intact Crate**. This furniture generates Beetle Coins passively, which is essential for covering the high costs of Level 70–80 upgrades.
*   **Scrap and Exchange:** You can dismantle unwanted B-rank Arcs to receive a small amount of upgrade materials, though it is generally better to use them as fodder for Enhancement early on.

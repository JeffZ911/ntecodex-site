## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-unlock-shiyu-defense-zzz/inline-3.webp)


The path to Shiyu Defense has evolved as the game has expanded. While early versions of the game allowed entry at lower levels, current balancing ensures that players are sufficiently geared before tackling these high-pressure combat scenarios.

### Inter-Knot Level and Proxy Reputation Requirements

The most significant barrier to entry is the Inter-Knot Level (IK Level). While Shiyu Defense originally became available at IK Level 20 during the launch window, the Version 2.5 update adjusted this requirement. For all players starting after this update, the unlock threshold is now IK Level 35. This change was implemented to ensure that Proxies have access to higher-level Agent promotions and Disk Drive tuning, which are essential for clearing the initial Stable Nodes.

In addition to the level requirement, you must complete the Reputation Rank-Up trial to become a "Certified Proxy." This rank-up quest is triggered by Shepherd and typically involves a combat trial that mirrors the difficulty of the early Shiyu stages. If you are at the correct level but the Shiyu Defense commission hasn't appeared, check your "Proxy Handbook" to ensure your reputation is up to date.

### Navigating Scott Outpost and the HDD System

Shiyu Defense is physically located at Scott Outpost, a frontline military base situated on the edge of the Hollows. You can access this area by opening the world map and selecting the Scott Outpost icon. 

Once you arrive, head toward the large, grey military tent in the center of the camp. Inside, you will find two primary NPCs: Ray, who handles [Hollow Zero](https://ntecodex.com/guides/zzz-3-0-hollow-zero-tactical-buff-comparison) operations, and Roland, who manages the Shiyu Defense frontier. To officially "unlock" the mode, you must follow the quest marker to Roland. He will provide a brief explanation of how the Hollow Deep Dive (HDD) system has been configured to simulate high-intensity combat data. The console itself is a distinct terminal separate from the main Hollow Zero entrance, though they sit side-by-side.

### Stable Nodes vs. Critical Nodes: What's the Difference?

Shiyu Defense is divided into different "Node" categories, each serving a different purpose in your endgame progression.

| Node Type | Reset Frequency | Difficulty | Reward Type |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stable Nodes** | Permanent (One-time) | Progressive (Low to High) | Polychromes, Master Copies |
| **Disputed Nodes** | Permanent (One-time) | Survival-Focused | Polychromes, Plating Agents |
| **Critical Nodes** | Bi-monthly (1st and 16th) | Very High | Polychromes, Ether Medals |
| **Ambush Nodes** | Event-based | Variable | — |

**Stable Nodes** consist of 10 stages. These are designed as a "gear check" to see if your teams are ready for the true endgame. Once you clear a Stable Node stage with an S-Rank, you claim the rewards, and they do not refresh.

**Critical Nodes** are the rotating challenge floors. These unlock only after you have cleared Stable Node Stage 10. These stages reset on the 1st and 16th of every month, providing a consistent source of Polychromes for active players.

### Team Building for Shiyu Defense: Elemental Weaknesses and Buffs

As you progress through the stages, the complexity of team building increases. Each stage features a "Frontline Resonance" or "Stage Buff" that provides massive bonuses to specific elements or combat styles (such as Daze application or Chain Attack damage).

One of the most important milestones in Shiyu Defense is the transition from one team to multiple teams:
*   **Stages 1–6:** Require only one team of three Agents.
*   **Stages 7–10 (Stable):** Require two separate teams. You cannot use the same Agent on both teams for a single stage.
*   **Critical Node Stage 5+:** As of recent updates, the final tiers of Critical Nodes now require three distinct teams to clear the various "fronts" of the simulation.

To succeed, you should consult a zzz tier list to identify which Agents excel in high-pressure environments. Building teams around elemental synergy (e.g., an all-Electric team for enemies weak to Shock) is often more effective than simply using your highest-level characters.

### Scoring and Ranking: How the S-Rank Timer Works

Your rewards in Shiyu Defense are tied to your clear speed. Each stage has three time-based objectives. To earn an S-Rank and the maximum Polychrome reward, you typically need to finish the encounter with more than 300 seconds (5 minutes) remaining on the clock.

In **Disputed Nodes**, the scoring system shifts from speed to survival. Instead of a timer, your rank is determined by how many times your Agents are defeated or how much total damage your team takes. This mode favors defensive Agents and those with strong parry or dodge-counter mechanics.

### Shiyu Defense Rewards and Reset Schedule

The primary motivation for clearing Shiyu Defense is the Polychrome yield.

*   **Stable Nodes:** 300 Polychromes per stage (3,000 total).
*   **Critical Nodes:** Up to 600–700 Polychromes per reset cycle, depending on the current patch's stage count.
*   **Materials:** You will also earn Master Copies (used for tuning Disk Drives), Plating Agents (for leveling Disk Drives), and specialized currency that can be spent in the Scott Outpost shop.

The Critical Nodes reset exactly at 4:00 AM server time on the 1st and 16th of each month. Any stages not cleared before this time will be lost as the nodes rotate to new enemy configurations and buffs.

### Shiyu Defense vs. Hollow Zero

While both modes are located at Scott Outpost, they offer very different experiences. Hollow Zero is a roguelike mode where you collect "Resonia" (buffs) as you explore a grid-based map. It is more about long-term endurance and managing corruption levels. Shiyu Defense is pure combat; there are no exploration buffs, so your success depends entirely on your Agents' stats, your gear, and your mechanical skill.

### Tips for Clearing Stable Node Stage 10 to Unlock Critical Nodes

Stable Node 10 is the final gatekeeper. To clear it, you need two well-invested teams. Focus on:
1.  **Leveling your DPS:** Ensure your primary damage dealers are at the maximum level allowed by your current Proxy Reputation.
2.  **Disk Drive Sets:** Equip 4-piece and 2-piece sets that complement your Agent's kit.
3.  **Bangboo Selection:** Don't overlook your Bangboo. In multi-team stages, having a Bangboo that matches your team's element (like Sharkboo for Ice teams) can significantly reduce clear times. Check a guide for best team comps for Shiyu Defense to see which Bangboo synergy works best for your roster.

### Best Agents for Early Shiyu Defense Stages

For players just starting their Shiyu journey, "A-Rank" Agents can be incredibly potent. Characters like Nicole are essential for grouping enemies, while Ben Bigger can provide the survivability needed for Disputed Nodes. If you are struggling to maintain high DPS, learning how to level up Inter-Knot fast will help you unlock higher character level caps, making the early stages much easier to brute-force.

### How to Reset Critical Node Progress

If you find that a run is going poorly—perhaps an Agent was defeated or you missed a crucial dodge—you can reset the stage at any time. Simply pause the game and select "Restart Challenge." This will put you back at the beginning of the current half of the stage with your health and energy restored to what they were at the start of that specific floor. Note that you cannot change your team composition mid-stage; to change characters, you must exit to the Shiyu Defense menu and start the stage over from the first half.

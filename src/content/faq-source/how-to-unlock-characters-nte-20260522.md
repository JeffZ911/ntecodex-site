# How to Unlock Characters in Neverness to Everness: A Complete Guide

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/how-to-unlock-characters-nte-20260522/inline-1.webp)


Acquiring new characters is the central progression loop for any Appraiser in Neverness to Everness (NTE). Since the game's launch on April 29, 2026, many players have looked for the most efficient ways to expand their roster without necessarily opening their wallets. The process involves a mix of narrative progression, engagement with life-simulation systems, and the unique board-game-inspired gacha known as Scarborough Fair.

### The Importance of Character Variety in NTE

Character variety is not just about aesthetic preference; it is a mechanical necessity. Each Esper belongs to one of six elemental categories: Cosmos, Incantation, Chaos, Lakshana, Anima, or Psyche. Enemies in the open world and within anomalous realms often possess specific elemental shields that can only be efficiently shattered by matching elements. Furthermore, characters provide unique utility for exploration, such as [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)’s ability to walk on vertical surfaces or [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte)’s time-manipulation skills that assist in solving environmental puzzles.

### Overview of Rarity: S-Class vs. A-Class Espers

Characters are divided into two main rarities: S-Class and A-Class. S-Class Espers are the premium units with high base stats and transformative ultimate abilities. These are typically the "chase" units on banners. A-Class Espers are more common but serve as the backbone of most teams, providing essential buffs, healing, or elemental application. While S-Class units are harder to obtain, NTE provides several guaranteed paths to secure them, ensuring that even free-to-play players can field a competitive team.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-unlock-characters-nte-20260522/inline-2.webp)


Unlocking characters in Neverness to Everness is accomplished through three primary avenues: completing Main Story chapters, progressing through the City Tycoon management system, and participating in the Scarborough Fair gacha. At launch, the game is remarkably generous, offering 6 characters entirely for free through gameplay milestones and login rewards.

The gacha system, Scarborough Fair, uses two types of dice: Fabricated Dice for the permanent Standard Board and Solid Dice for the rotating Limited Boards. Unlike many other urban RPGs, NTE does not use a 50/50 system for limited banners; the first S-Rank you land on a limited board is guaranteed to be the featured character. Additionally, a one-time S-Rank Selector is granted to every player after completing 50 pulls on the Standard Board.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-unlock-characters-nte-20260522/inline-3.webp)


### Free Story and Milestone Characters

The initial hours of NTE provide a solid foundation for your team. You begin the game as **Zero**, the protagonist and a Cosmos-element S-Rank character. Shortly after starting the prologue mission "A New Destination – Head to Eibon Antique Shop," you unlock **Mint**, an Anima-element A-Rank who serves as an early-game damage dealer.

As you progress through the first major narrative arc involving the Eibon Antique Shop, you will encounter a boss known as the Viewfinder. Defeating this enemy and completing the subsequent story missions unlocks **Adler**. For those who participated in pre-registration or joined during the launch window, **Haniel** is available via the in-game mail system. **Aurelia**, a Psyche-element support, is granted simply for logging into the game for three consecutive days.

The most significant free unlock is **Chiz**, an S-Rank Cosmos character. Unlike the others, she is tied to the City Tycoon system rather than the main questline.

### The Scarborough Fair Gacha System Explained

Scarborough Fair is a departure from traditional "pull" mechanics. It is presented as a Monopoly-style board game where your "pulls" are actually dice rolls. You move a game piece (a Chuppa) across a board filled with various tiles.

*   **Fabricated Dice:** Used for the "Strange Encounters" Standard Board.
*   **Solid Dice:** Used for Limited-time Event Boards (e.g., Nanally or Hotori).
*   **Tiles:** Landing on a tile grants the item displayed. These include upgrade materials, Warp Pieces (shop currency), Arcs (weapons), and characters.
*   **Journey Together Tiles:** These tiles feature a character's portrait. Landing on one immediately unlocks that character.

Each dice roll costs 160 Annulith. A "10-pull" costs 1,600 Annulith and results in 10 dice rolls that move you cumulatively around the board.

### Pity Mechanics and Board Modification

NTE uses a distinct pity system that integrates with its board-game aesthetic. The base rate for an S-Rank character is 1.87%. However, the game features a mechanic called **Board Modification** that acts as "soft pity."

Once you reach 70 pulls without landing on an S-Rank character, Board Modification triggers. This transforms the board, replacing common material tiles with S-Rank character tiles and increasing the S-Rank acquisition rate to 19.59%. This state persists until you land on an S-Rank tile. If you are exceptionally unlucky and do not land on an S-Rank by pull 89, pull 90 is a **Hard Pity** guarantee, which will force your piece onto the featured character's tile.

| Metric | Value |
| :--- | :--- |
| Base S-Rank Rate | 1.87% |
| Base A-Rank Rate | 11.67% |
| Soft Pity (Board Modification) | 70 Pulls |
| Hard Pity (Guaranteed S-Rank) | 90 Pulls |
| S-Rank Rate at Soft Pity | 19.59% |
| 50/50 Mechanic | None (100% Featured Guarantee) |

### The 50-Pull S-Rank Selector Strategy

One of the most important milestones for a new account is the 50-pull mark on the Standard Board. After 50 rolls, the game grants a one-time S-Rank Selector. This allows you to choose one of the six permanent S-Rank Espers to join your team.

| Character | Element | Role |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Baicang** | Incantation | DPS / Crowd Control |
| **Daffodil** | Chaos | Sub-DPS / Buffer |
| **Fadia** | Psyche | Tank / Shield Support |
| **Hathor** | Lakshana | Sustained DPS |
| **Jiuyuan** | Anima | Healer / Support |
| **[Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide)** | Incantation | Burst DPS |

The best strategy is to wait until you have performed your initial free pulls and perhaps your first limited banner attempts before using this selector. For example, if you pulled Nanally (Anima), you might want to avoid picking Jiuyuan (Anima) and instead select Fadia or Baicang to diversify your elemental coverage.

### Limited Event Banners: Nanally and Beyond

Limited-time boards feature exclusive characters that cannot be obtained from the Standard Board. The launch banner, "The Ichi-daime," featured **Nanally**, while the second phase banner, "Misty Tipsy Style," features **Hotori**.

Acquiring these characters requires Solid Dice. Because there is no 50/50 system, you are guaranteed the featured character the moment you hit an S-Rank tile. If you stop pulling on a banner before hitting pity, your progress carries over to the next limited banner. If you were at 60/90 pity on Nanally's board, you will start at 60/90 pity on Hotori's board.

### Unlocking Chiz via City Tycoon Level 18

**Chiz** is a powerful S-Rank character who is essentially the reward for engaging with the game's life-simulation mechanics. To unlock her, you must reach Level 18 in the City Tycoon system.

1.  **Unlock the Mode:** Complete the story mission "Good Morning, Hethereau" after the Viewfinder boss fight.
2.  **Earn Fons:** Fons is the currency for this mode. You earn it by managing the Cafe by Origen, completing city delivery missions, and participating in street races.
3.  **Manage City Stamina:** Unlike combat stamina (Pixels), City Stamina only resets weekly. Use it on high-return activities like the Pink Paws Heist (unlocked at Level 10).
4.  **Complete Tasks:** Each Tycoon level requires specific tasks, such as buying an apartment or inviting a character to your home.
5.  **Claim Chiz:** Once you hit Level 18, Chiz becomes playable. Continued progression in City Tycoon (up to Level 45) provides additional copies of Chiz and her signature weapon.

For a more in-depth look at optimizing your city management, consult a City Tycoon progression guide.

### NTE Reroll Strategy

Rerolling in Neverness to Everness is possible but time-consuming due to the length of the prologue. If you are determined to start with a specific limited character like Nanally or Hotori, you will need to play for approximately 30 to 45 minutes to reach the point where the Scarborough Fair unlocks. Given the generous 50-pull selector and the free Chiz, rerolling is generally only recommended for players who want to maximize their account's power for the Neverness to Everness tier list rankings immediately.

### Unlocking Arcs (Weapons)

Characters are only half of the equation; they also require Arcs. Arcs are obtained through the **Arc Research Program**, which functions differently than character banners. This system uses **Tri-Keys** instead of dice. You can only perform 10-pulls on the Arc banner. While character banners have no 50/50, the Arc banner has a 25% chance of granting the featured weapon on your first S-Rank pull. If you miss the featured weapon, the next S-Rank Arc is guaranteed to be the featured one. To find the right gear for your team, look for guides on the best weapons for Nanally or other specific Espers.

### Resource Management: Annulith and Dice

Annulith is the most precious resource in Hethereau. It is primarily earned through:
*   First-time exploration of Anomaly Zones.
*   Completing Hunter Commissions.
*   Achievement rewards and event participation.

Always prioritize converting Annulith into Solid Dice for limited banners. Standard Fabricated Dice are often given out as rewards for leveling up your Hunter Rank or through the City Tycoon exchange shop, so spending Annulith on the Standard Board is usually considered a waste of resources.

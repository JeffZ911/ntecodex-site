## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-farm-disks-neverness-to-everness/inline-2.webp)


Farming Disks in Neverness to Everness is a two-step process involving the Rabbit Hole domain and the Rewind system. To obtain the best gear, you must travel to Cape Square in the New Herland District and enter the Rabbit Hole. Each successful run costs 40 Character Pixels and rewards you with Cartridges and Carrota Coins.

Once you have farmed Carrota Coins, you must use the "Rewind" menu, accessible via the Character > Console screen. Here, you spend your coins to roll for Modules—the "Tetris-style" pieces that slot into your character's Console to provide stat boosts. While the Rabbit Hole provides the base Cartridge, the Rewind system is how you acquire the specific Modules needed to complete a build. For players seeking immediate power spikes, completing Anomaly Commissions such as "Eclipse" or "Raging Flames" will grant specific S-Rank Disks without the need for repetitive grinding.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-farm-disks-neverness-to-everness/inline-3.webp)


The Disk system in NTE is more complex than traditional gear systems due to its spatial puzzle mechanics. To farm effectively, you must understand the interplay between the items you drop in the world and the items you "roll" in the menu.

### Understanding Cartridges vs. Modules

A complete Disk setup consists of one Cartridge and several Modules. The Cartridge acts as the foundation; it is a physical "CD" that you slot into the center of the character's Console. This item determines the set bonus (e.g., a 2-piece or 4-piece effect). However, a Cartridge alone provides very few stats.

Modules are the geometric shapes that you must arrange around the Cartridge in a grid. These pieces provide the bulk of your Attack, Crit Rate, and Elemental Damage stats. Farming Disks is essentially the act of farming the Cartridge from the world and then farming the Modules via the Rewind system to fit the available space in your character’s unique grid.

### The Rabbit Hole Farming Loop

The Rabbit Hole is the central hub for Disk progression. Located in the neon-lit alleys of Cape Square, this domain offers different "Tracks" or stages based on the set bonuses you are looking for. 

1.  **Select the Track:** Each day, different Cartridge sets are featured. Ensure you are farming the day that corresponds to your character's needs (e.g., Physical DMG sets vs. Anomaly DMG sets).
2.  **Spend Character Pixels:** A single reward claim costs 40 Character Pixels. 
3.  **Collect Carrota Coins:** In addition to the Cartridge, you will receive Carrota Coins. These are the exclusive currency used to generate Modules.

### How to Use the Rewind System

The Rewind system is where the "gacha" element of gear farming resides. You do not drop Modules directly from enemies in the Rabbit Hole; instead, you drop the currency to create them. Inside the Character Console menu, you can select "Rewind" to spend your Carrota Coins.

The rarity of the Module you receive depends on the tier of the coin used. 

**Carrota Coin Tiers and Module Rarities**

| Coin Type | Module Rarity Potential | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Bronze Carrota Coin | B-Rank to A-Rank | Early game leveling and fodder |
| Silver Carrota Coin | A-Rank to S-Rank | Mid-game building (Hunter Level 20+) |
| Gold Carrota Coin | Guaranteed S-Rank | End-game optimization (Hunter Level 30+) |

The Rewind system also determines the "shape" of the Module. Because each character has a different grid layout, you may need to Rewind multiple times to get a high-stat Module that actually fits into the remaining empty blocks of your Console.

### S-Rank Disk Locations: Anomaly Commissions

If you are struggling with the Rabbit Hole or lack the Character Pixels to farm, Anomaly Commissions are your best alternative. These are high-difficulty world boss encounters or investigation missions that reward "Signature Disks."

**Anomaly Commissions and S-Rank Rewards**

| Commission Name | Target Anomaly / Location | S-Rank Disk Reward |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Raging Flames | Ghost Rider (Near Witch's House) | Raging Flames (Hator Signature) |
| Eclipse | — | Eclipse Set (Shadow/Dark DMG) |
| Y-Full Fantasy | Urban Legend: The Neon Cat | Y-Full Fantasy (Support/Energy) |
| Camellia Society | The Secret Garden District | Camellia Society (Healing/HP) |

The Ghost Rider anomaly is particularly notable for early S-Rank farming. Located near the Witch's House, this boss requires high mobility to defeat but provides a massive power boost for fire-element characters like Hator.

### Hunter Level Milestones for Better Drops

Your efficiency in the Rabbit Hole is capped by your Hunter Level. Spending Character Pixels at low levels is often seen as inefficient because S-Rank (Gold) Disks have a very low drop rate.

*   **Hunter Level 1-19:** Focus on B-Rank and A-Rank Disks. Do not spend Gold Carrota Coins yet.
*   **Hunter Level 20-29:** A-Rank Disks become guaranteed. This is the time to start refining your Module shapes.
*   **Hunter Level 30+:** S-Rank Disks begin dropping consistently from the Rabbit Hole. This is the "true" start of the end-game farm.

### Fons and Beetle Coin Management

Dropping a high-tier Disk is only the beginning. You must upgrade it using Fons (the primary currency) and specialized enhancement materials. S-Rank Disks are notoriously expensive to max out, often requiring hundreds of thousands of Fons. If you run out of Fons while farming Disks, consider running the "Fons Distillation" challenges found in the industrial sector of New Herland.

Additionally, Beetle Coins—the premium-adjacent currency—can sometimes be used in the city's specialty shops to buy specific Module shapes if you are having bad luck with the Rewind system.

### Connecting Disks to Arc Builds

While Disks provide the stats and set bonuses, Arcs serve as your weapons. A perfect Disk set will underperform if it does not synergize with your equipped Arc. For example, if you are farming the "Raging Flames" Disk set for Hator, you should pair it with an Arc that boosts Fire DMG or Crit DMG to maximize the set's percentage-based scaling.

### Character Life Skills

Certain characters possess Life Skills that directly impact the Disk farming loop. Before you engage with the Rewind system, check if you have a character in your roster with a skill that reduces the Carrota Coin cost or increases the chance of a "Double Rewind" (obtaining two Modules for the price of one). Using these characters as your "Active City Avatar" while in the menu can save a significant amount of resources over time.

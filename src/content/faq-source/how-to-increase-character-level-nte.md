# How to Increase Character Level in Neverness to Everness (NTE)

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/how-to-increase-character-level-nte/inline-1.webp)


What is the process for leveling up Espers (characters) in Neverness to Everness? Players often find themselves hitting sudden walls where enemies become significantly stronger, necessitating a clear understanding of how to boost character stats. Beyond just gaining experience, you need to know what materials are required to increase character stats and level caps, as well as how account-wide systems like Hunter Rank and Appraisal Level affect character progression. Because NTE uses a "World Level" system, your individual character power is directly tied to your overall account progression.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-increase-character-level-nte/inline-2.webp)


To increase a character's level in NTE, you must use Hunter Guide EXP materials and Beetle Coins in the character menu. Unlike traditional RPGs where characters gain significant experience from combat, the vast majority of your Esper's growth comes from consuming these specific items.

Characters hit level caps every 10–20 levels, requiring "Ascension" (also known as Breakthrough) to continue progressing. This process consumes specific Anomaly drops found in the open world and rare materials obtained from defeating bosses in Anomaly Hunts. Furthermore, your maximum character level is strictly gated by your Appraisal Level. This means even if you have enough materials, you cannot raise an Esper to level 80 until you have reached the required Hunter Rank and completed the associated World Elevation Quest to raise your Appraisal Level.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-increase-character-level-nte/inline-3.webp)


Character progression in Neverness to Everness is a multi-layered system designed to ensure your power level matches the difficulty of the urban environment. While the game features a massive open world, the strength of your Espers is determined in the character management screen rather than through passive exploration.

### Using Hunter Guides for Experience

Hunter Guides are the primary source of character EXP. These are categorized into three distinct tiers, each providing a different amount of experience points. When you open the Esper menu and select "Level Up," you can manually select these guides or use the "Quick Add" feature to automatically fill the bar to the next cap.

| Item Name | EXP Value | Rarity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Hunter Guide: Rising | 1,000 | Green (2-Star) |
| Hunter Guide: Senior | 5,000 | Blue (3-Star) |
| Hunter Guide: Elite | 20,000 | Purple (4-Star) |

Every level gained requires a corresponding amount of Beetle Coins. As the level of your Esper increases, the Beetle Coin cost per level rises exponentially. It is common for players to run out of Beetle Coins before they run out of Hunter Guides in the mid-to-late game, making currency management a vital part of the leveling process.

### The Ascension System: Breaking Level Caps

Once an Esper reaches a milestone level (such as 20, 40, 50, or 60), their level bar will turn gold, and they will no longer gain experience. To continue leveling, you must perform an Ascension. This process does more than just raise the level cap; it provides a significant "stat spike" to the character's base Health, Attack, and Defense.

Ascension requires materials from two primary sources:
1.  **Common Anomaly Drops:** These are fragments and essences dropped by standard enemies (Anomalies) found wandering the streets of Hethereau. Higher Ascension tiers require higher-tier versions of these drops, which can be crafted using the Synthesis bench in the city.
2.  **Boss Materials:** Starting at the level 40 cap, Ascension requires unique drops from Anomaly Hunts. These are specialized boss encounters located throughout the map that require Character Pixels (stamina) to claim rewards. Each Esper belongs to a specific element or faction that determines which boss you need to farm.

### Hunter Rank and Appraisal Level Requirements

The most important restriction on your character level is the Appraisal Level. This functions as NTE's "World Level." If your Appraisal Level is too low, the game will prevent you from ascending your characters, regardless of how many materials you have.

Your Appraisal Level is tied to your Hunter Rank (your account level). At specific Hunter Rank milestones, you will be prompted to complete a "World Elevation Quest." This is a combat trial that tests your current team's strength. Once completed, your Appraisal Level increases, the enemies in the open world become tougher, the loot quality improves, and your character level cap is raised.

### Where to Farm Leveling Materials

Efficiently leveling your characters requires knowing where to spend your Character Pixels. Since Character Pixels regenerate over time, you should prioritize the following locations:

*   **Houdinii's Magic Stage:** This is the primary source for Hunter Guides. These challenges consist of waves of enemies that must be defeated within a time limit. Higher difficulty stages yield more Elite Hunter Guides.
*   **Anomaly Hunts:** These are the boss arenas. You should only farm these when you are approaching a level cap (e.g., your character is level 39 and needs to hit level 40).
*   **Anomaly Pilgrimage:** These nodes provide Beetle Coins. While you get Beetle Coins from almost every activity in the game, the Pilgrimage is the fastest way to replenish your reserves if you are aggressively leveling multiple characters.
*   **Open World Exploration:** Common Ascension materials (like Anomaly fragments) do not cost Character Pixels to collect. You can obtain these simply by defeating enemies in the streets or completing random urban events.

### Resource Management: Prioritizing Your Main Carry

In the early stages of Neverness to Everness, the game provides an abundance of Hunter Guides and Beetle Coins. However, this supply thins out significantly once you reach Hunter Rank 30. To avoid getting stuck with a weak team, focus your resources on your "Main Carry" first.

Your Main Carry is usually your primary damage dealer (DPS). Keep this character at the maximum level allowed by your current Appraisal Level. Your support characters and healers can typically function effectively while being 10 to 15 levels below your main DPS. This strategy allows you to save Beetle Coins for Arcs and Consoles, which are equally important for overall combat power.

## Related

![Related](/img/how-to-increase-character-level-nte/inline-4.webp)


Leveling your Espers is only one part of the progression puzzle in NTE. To truly master the game's challenges, you must also look at your account level and the equipment your characters use.

### How to Increase Hunter Rank Fast

Since your character level is gated by your Hunter Rank, increasing your account level is a top priority. The most effective way to gain Hunter Rank EXP is by completing the Main Story Quests (Urban Chronicles). These provide massive chunks of experience and unlock new areas of Hethereau.

Once you have exhausted the available story content, you can increase your Hunter Rank by:
*   **Spending Character Pixels:** Every point of stamina spent on Houdinii's Magic Stage or Anomaly Hunts grants Hunter Rank EXP.
*   **Daily Commissions:** These short tasks provide a consistent daily source of experience.
*   **Side Stories and Requests:** Helping the citizens of Hethereau with their paranormal problems offers moderate experience rewards and often unlocks new shops or services.

### Leveling Arcs and Consoles

Even a level 100 Esper will struggle if they are equipped with level 1 weapons. In NTE, weapons are known as **Arcs**, and equipment sets are known as **Consoles**.
*   **Arcs:** These require "Arc EXP Materials" and Beetle Coins to level up. Like characters, they have level caps that require "Refinement" using duplicate copies of the weapon or specialized materials.
*   **Consoles:** These are the gear pieces that provide set bonuses. Leveling Consoles requires sacrificing other Consoles or using "Console Data" chips. Prioritize Consoles with percentage-based stats (like ATK% or Crit Rate%) for your damage dealers.

### Understanding Character Bond and Life Skills

While not directly tied to combat level, Bond Levels and Life Skills represent the "urban life" aspect of Neverness to Everness. 
*   **Bond Levels:** You increase Bond by including characters in your party during exploration, giving them gifts, or interacting with them at the tea shop. Increasing Bond unlocks unique character voice lines, lore entries, and occasionally cosmetic rewards or small stat-boosting "Talents."
*   **Life Skills:** Characters have unique abilities related to the city, such as "Street Racing," "Bargaining," or "Anomalous Research." Improving these skills can make navigating the city easier or provide discounts at various stores, but they do not increase the raw combat level of the Esper.

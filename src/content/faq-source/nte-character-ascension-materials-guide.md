## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-character-ascension-materials-guide/inline-2.webp)


Character ascension in Neverness to Everness (NTE) requires three main resource types: **Hunter Guides** (for EXP), **Whispers** (common monster drops), and **Boss Materials** (obtained from Anomaly Hunts). Unlike standard RPGs, ascension is strictly gated by your **Hunter Rank**; you must reach **Hunter Rank 10** before you can perform your first breakthrough to raise a character's level cap beyond 30.

The primary currency for all character upgrades is **Beetle Coins**, which are earned through commissions and city activities, while **Fons** are typically reserved for urban life skills and shop purchases. Farming for these materials occurs primarily in **Anomaly Zones** and **Houdinii's Magic Stage**, both of which consume a daily regenerating stamina resource called **Character Pixels**.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-character-ascension-materials-guide/inline-3.webp)


The progression system in Neverness to Everness is a dual-layered mechanic that ties individual character strength to your overall account progress. To maximize your efficiency, you must balance your "Character Pixels" (combat stamina) and "City Stamina" (weekly life-sim stamina) while targeting specific districts in Hethereau for rare drops.

### Unlocking Ascension: The Hunter Rank 10 Requirement

You cannot ascend any character until you reach **Hunter Rank 10**. In NTE, your Hunter Rank (HL) determines your **Appraisal Level (AL)**, which acts as the ultimate gate for character and Arc (weapon) levels. 

The progression follows a specific pattern:
*   **HL 10 (AL 1):** Unlocks the first breakthrough, allowing characters to reach Level 30.
*   **HL 20 (AL 2):** Requires a "Hunter Level Limit Ascension" quest at Houdinii's Magic Stage to reach Level 40.
*   **HL 30 (AL 3):** Unlocks Level 50 cap.
*   **HL 40 (AL 4):** Unlocks Level 60 cap.
*   **HL 50 (AL 5):** Unlocks Level 70 cap.
*   **HL 60 (AL 6):** Unlocks the final Level 80 cap.

If you reach the level cap but haven't raised your Hunter Rank, any EXP earned by that character is lost. Prioritize completing the **Circle Bounty** (daily tasks) and main story missions to push your Hunter Rank forward.

### Character EXP: Hunter Guides and Houdinii's Magic Stage

To level up characters between breakthroughs, you use **Hunter Guides**. These are consumed from the character menu and provide fixed amounts of experience. While you can find small quantities through world exploration, the only reliable way to mass-produce them is by clearing **Houdinii's Magic Stage**, located in the northern part of Hethereau near **Iris Bridge**.

| **Elite Hunter Guide** | 20,000 | Houdinii's Magic Stage (High Difficulty) |

### Common Materials: The Whisper Hierarchy

Whisper materials are the "common" drops required for every breakthrough tier. These are dropped by standard enemies (Oddities) found throughout Hethereau. As your Appraisal Level increases, enemies will begin dropping higher-tier versions of these materials.

#### Whisper Material Tiers and Rarity
| Tier Name | Rarity Color | Drop Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Lost Whisper** | Green (B-Rank) | Low-level Oddities (AL 1+) |
| **Obscure Whisper** | Blue (A-Rank) | Mid-level Oddities (AL 3+) |
| **Paradoxical Whisper** | Purple (S-Rank) | High-level Oddities (AL 5+) |

To farm these efficiently, use the **Exploration Guide** on your in-game smartphone. Navigate to the **Enemy Shadow Elimination** tab and use the "Track" feature to pinpoint specific clusters of enemies on your map.

### Elite Materials: Anomaly Hunts and Boss Drops

High-level breakthroughs (typically level 40 and above) require unique drops from elite Anomaly bosses. These bosses are found in specific **Anomaly Pilgrimage** zones or as unique world encounters that require specific conditions to trigger.

#### Boss Drop Sources and Associated Anomaly Hunts
| Boss Name | Location | Key Requirement | Primary Drop Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Headless Rider** | Coastal Highway Z42 | 8:10 PM (In-game time) + Must be in a Car | Physical/Speed-based Espers |

The **Headless Rider** is a unique encounter: you must set your in-game clock to **8:10 PM**, drive to the intersection of **Hankaku Street** and **Fox Window Street** in a four-wheeled vehicle, and engage in a high-speed chase before the actual boss arena opens.

### Currency and Costs: Managing Beetle Coins

Every level-up and breakthrough consumes **Beetle Coins**. While the costs are negligible in the early game, they escalate sharply at higher levels. For example, ascending a character from Level 50 to 60 costs approximately **100,000 Beetle Coins**, in addition to 16 Boss Materials and 12 regional specialty items.

The most effective way to maintain a healthy Beetle Coin balance is through the **City Tycoon** system. Increasing your Tycoon Level by managing businesses and completing "Hethereau Hobbies" provides massive Beetle Coin injections—reaching Tycoon Level 8, for instance, grants a one-time bonus of 7,500 Beetle Coins, alongside recurring passive income.

### Stamina Management: Optimizing Character Pixels

Your primary bottleneck is **Character Pixels**. You possess a maximum cap of **240 Pixels**, which regenerates at a rate of 1 Pixel every 6 minutes (full recovery in 24 hours). 

**Optimization Tips:**
*   **Daily Spend:** Never let your Pixels sit at 240. Spend them on Houdinii's Magic Stage for EXP even if you don't immediately need it; you will need thousands of Hunter Guides for your secondary team later.
*   **De-noise Solutions:** These items restore 60 Pixels. Save them for "Double Reward" events or for the day you unlock a new Appraisal Level to jump-start your farming.
*   **Double Rewards:** A standard Anomaly run costs 40 Pixels. Using the "Double Reward" option costs 80 Pixels and saves real-world time while providing identical loot-per-stamina efficiency.

### Alternative Sources: The Mall and Exchange Shops

If you are short on a specific material and have exhausted your Pixels, visit the **Mall**.
*   **Hunter Exchange:** Spend Beetle Coins here for Rising and Senior Hunter Guides.
*   **Mode Shop:** Uses **Peril Hunt Medals** (earned from high-risk commissions). This is one of the few places to buy high-tier ascension materials directly.
*   **Redeem Codes:** Developers frequently release codes (e.g., `NTE0429` or `NTENANALLYGO`) that provide free Elite Hunter Guides and Beetle Coins.

### Understanding the Esper Cycle and Elemental Wheel

The combat system in Neverness to Everness revolves around the Esper Wheel, a diagram of six elemental types: Lakshana (Gold), Cosmos (Silver), Anima (Green), Incantation (Red), Chaos (Purple), and Psyche (Azure). Analysis of the "Esper Wheel" mechanics shows that adjacent elements on the wheel trigger powerful elemental reactions, while non-adjacent elements do not interact.

For example, an Anima (Green) character like Nanally can trigger a reaction if swapped with a Cosmos (Silver) or Incantation (Red) character. This makes "Cycle Rate" one of the most important stats in the game. A high Cycle Rate allows for faster Esper Meter generation, leading to tighter rotations and more frequent triggers of "Trio-Esper Reactions." These triple cycles occur when all three members of a team belong to adjacent elements (e.g., Silver-Green-Red), providing a massive damage multiplier to the final character in the sequence.

### S-Tier Character Deep Dives: Nanally, Sakiri, and Hotori

**Nanally (Anima/Plasma)**
Nanally remains the gold standard for Main DPS units in Hethereau. Her kit is built around her Anima element and her compatibility with Plasma-type Arcs. Her most defining combat mechanic is the "Underboss" summon, a spectral entity that mimics her attacks and provides independent damage scaling. Beyond her combat prowess, Nanally is highly valued for her exploration utility. She possesses a unique wall-walking ability that allows players to navigate the verticality of Hethereau's skyscrapers without relying on vehicles or stamina-heavy climbing. This makes her a permanent fixture in most exploration teams.

**Sakiri (Incantation)**
Sakiri serves as the premier support and crowd control unit. Her signature ability, "Devour Whole," creates a localized singularity that pulls in smaller enemies and applies a significant defense shred. When Sakiri is swapped out, she leaves behind a team-wide ATK buff that scales based on her total Incantation power. Her role in the meta is to set the stage for a Main DPS, grouping enemies together so that Nanally or Jiuyuan can maximize their Area of Effect (AoE) damage.

**Hotori (Skill Mimic)**
The newest addition to the S+ tier, Hotori, has shifted the meta with her "Skill Recording" mechanic. Hotori does not rely solely on her own abilities; instead, she can replicate the active skill of the ally she swapped in from. This effectively allows a team to double the output of a high-impact ability. If Hotori swaps in after Sakiri, she can cast a second "Devour Whole," extending the duration of enemy displacement and debuffs. This flexibility makes her the most versatile Sub-DPS currently available.

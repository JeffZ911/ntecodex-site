## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-character-database-analysis-guide/inline-2.webp)


The Neverness to Everness (NTE) character database currently features 16 playable units as of the 1.0 launch, categorized into S-Class and A-Class rarities. S-Class characters are the premier units, often serving as the backbone of high-level teams, while A-Class units provide essential utility and are frequently accessible for free through story progression or side activities.

Top-tier characters in the current meta include [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) (S-Class Anima), [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte) (S-Class Cosmos), and [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide) (S-Class Incantation). The game utilizes a unique "Archetype" system—Solid, Liquid, Gas, Plasma, and Synthesis—that dictates weapon (Arc) compatibility and influences energy generation. Analysis shows that S-Class characters are obtained via the "Scarborough Fair" board-game style gacha system, which features a 90-pull hard pity and no 50/50 mechanic, making character acquisition more predictable than in traditional gacha titles.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-character-database-analysis-guide/inline-3.webp)


The depth of NTE combat stems from the intersection of character kits and the elemental Esper system. Success in endgame modes like Beyond the Rails depends on exploiting these synergies.

### S-Class Powerhouses: Nanally and Hotori

Nanally is currently the strongest Main DPS in the game due to her "Underboss" follow-up attack mechanic. When Nanally activates her Ultimate, she summons the Underboss, which coordinates strikes with her Basic Attacks, effectively doubling her damage instances. Her Redirect Skill triggers the "Ichi-daime’s Authority" state, granting a 30% Crit DMG bonus for 12 seconds and enabling unique anti-gravity movement. This allows her to fight from walls and ceilings, bypassing many ground-based enemy attacks.

Hotori serves as a premier support and burst DPS hybrid. Her unique mechanic allows her to "record" the skills used by her teammates and replay them, duplicating their effects. Her Ultimate provides a "Time Stop" window, which is invaluable in timed endgame content for maximizing damage without the clock ticking down. While Nanally provides raw sustained damage, Hotori provides the mechanical leverage to clear the hardest "Stops" in the Beyond the Rails gauntlet.

### A-Class Essentials: Mint, Adler, and Haniel

Mint is often considered the "face" of NTE and is given to players early in the story. While she is an A-Class unit, her "Minty Whirlwind" (hold Basic Attack) transforms her into a mobile tornado that deals continuous Anima damage. This makes her the most efficient early-game farmer for clearing groups of enemies. Her A1 Ascension is highly recommended as it resets her Redirect Skill cooldown upon a successful Critical Riposte.

Adler and Haniel are the pillars of F2P (Free-to-Play) team building. Adler provides consistent shields and Incantation DoT (Damage over Time), making him a reliable sustainer. Haniel, a pre-registration reward, is a Psyche-element support who provides team-wide ATK buffs based on her own base ATK attribute. Her buffs scale exceptionally well when paired with high-investment DPS units.

### Understanding Elements and Archetypes

Characters are divided into six elements that form the Esper Circle. Adjacent elements on this circle can trigger duo reactions, while specific combinations of three elements trigger powerful trio reactions.

| Reaction | Elements Involved | Primary Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blossom** | Cosmos + Anima | Summons a turret that fires AoE projectiles. |
| **Remora** | Lakshana + Cosmos | Marks enemies and significantly slows their movement/attacks. |
| **Hexed** | Anima + Incantation | Records damage over 12s and unleashes a massive burst. |
| **Scorch** | Incantation + Chaos | Applies a 15-second Damage over Time (DoT) effect. |
| **Nova** | Chaos + Psyche | Attaches a bomb to the enemy that explodes after a delay. |

| **Charged** | Lakshana + Cosmos + Anima | Generates massive Ultimate Energy when Blossom hits a Remora target. |
| **Discord** | Incantation + Chaos + Psyche | Deals massive Break/Stagger damage to enemies with Scorch and Nova. |

Archetypes (Solid, Liquid, Gas, Plasma, Synthesis) determine which Arcs (weapons) a character can equip. Furthermore, Archetypes influence how characters manage their Esper Meter. For instance, Plasma characters like Nanally often excel at generating energy through follow-up attacks, while Solid characters like Hotori may rely more on "Cycle Rate" stats to fill their meters via basic attack strings and parries.

### The Gacha System: Board Game Pity Mechanics

The "Scarborough Fair" gacha system is a departure from traditional pull-based mechanics. Players spend Solid Dice (limited) or Fabricated Dice (standard) to move a piece across a board. Rewards are determined by the tile the piece lands on.

Key features include:
*   **90-Pull Pity:** You are guaranteed the featured S-Class character by the 90th roll.
*   **No 50/50:** If you land on an S-Class character tile on a limited banner, it is guaranteed to be the featured unit.
*   **Board Modification:** After 70 rolls without an S-Class, the board transforms, turning several tiles into S-Class character tiles, significantly increasing your chances of a "soft pity" win.
*   **Pity Carry-over:** Progress toward the 90-pull guarantee carries over between limited banners (e.g., from Nanally to Hotori).

### Combat Systems: Esper Cycles and Critical Ripostes

Combat revolves around the Esper Meter, a circular gauge next to character portraits. Filling this meter allows you to trigger an "Esper Attack" by swapping to that character. Parrying an enemy's attack (indicated by a double red circle) instantly fills the meter.

Critical Ripostes are another core mechanic. When an enemy flashes red, performing a dodge at the exact moment triggers a "Critical Dodge," granting invincibility frames. Following up with a Basic Attack executes a "Critical Riposte," which deals high damage and shreds the enemy's Break bar. For characters like Mint, this riposte is essential for resetting skill cooldowns and maintaining a high-tempo offensive loop.

# Wuthering Waves Echo Fusion System FAQ: Neverness to Everness Anomaly & Gear Guide

Neverness to Everness (NTE) does not use the [Wuthering Waves](https://ntecodex.com/guides/wuthering-waves-2-6-vs-2-5-meta-gear-guide-20260706) [Echo Fusion system](https://ntecodex.com/faq). Instead, players upgrade Arcs (weapons) and collect Equipment (Cartridges) through Anomaly Commissions and the City Tycoon system. Progression in Hethereau focuses on urban management and tactical gear refinement rather than capturing and fusing monster souls.

### Understanding the Echo Fusion Query in NTE

The query stems from the shared "monster-collecting" DNA of modern gacha RPGs. In Hethereau, you encounter various supernatural phenomena, but you do not "equip" these monsters in the same way you equip Echoes. Instead, the focus shifts to how these encounters provide materials for your Espers, such as [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or Mint. The "fusion" aspect that players expect is replaced by more traditional weapon enhancement and a unique urban progression system.

### Key Differences Between NTE and Wuthering Waves Mechanics

The primary difference lies in the source of power. In *[Wuthering Waves](https://ntecodex.com/guides/wuthering-waves-2-6-vs-2-5-meta-gear-guide-20260706)*, your Echoes provide active skills and set bonuses. In Neverness to Everness, your active skills are inherent to the character and their equipped Arc. While you still hunt monsters, the rewards are tangible items like Annulith and Fons, which are used to strengthen the characters directly or fuel the city's economy.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/wuthering-waves-echo-fusion-system-faq-nte/inline-3.webp)


The progression in Hethereau is a multi-layered experience that combines traditional RPG gear slots with an urban simulation. To maximize your efficiency, you must understand how Arcs, Cartridges, and the City Tycoon system interact.

### How Arcs Replace the Echo System

Arcs are the core of an Esper's loadout. Unlike Echoes, which are farmed by killing any mob in the overworld, Arcs are primarily obtained through the gacha system or as rare drops from high-level Anomaly Commissions. Arcs are categorized by their physical state: **Plasma, Synthesis, Solid, Gas, and Liquid**.

Each character is compatible with specific types. For example, Nanally utilizes Arcs that complement her high-mobility playstyle. These weapons do not just provide raw stats; they often change the behavior of the Esper's abilities. High-tier Arcs, such as those found in limited banners like The Ichi-daime, offer unique passive effects that are essential for endgame content.

### The Role of Anomaly Hunting in Gear Progression

While you don't fuse the Anomalies themselves, hunting them is the only way to progress your gear. By visiting the Bureau and accepting commissions, players track down supernatural threats across Hethereau. Successful hunts reward players with:
*   **Hunter EXP:** Increases your Bureau rank, unlocking harder missions.
*   **Annulith:** A primary currency/material for upgrading the level of your Arcs.
*   **Fons:** A specialized energy source required for character ascension.
*   **Cartridges:** These are the "artifact" equivalent in NTE. They fit into specific equipment slots and provide set bonuses, such as increased elemental damage or faster energy regeneration.

### Upgrading Arcs: Materials and TeaRealm T-Fusion

Upgrading your gear in NTE requires a steady supply of materials gathered from the city. A common point of confusion for new players is the item **TeaRealm T-Fusion**. Despite the "Fusion" in its name, this is actually a premium consumable food item found within the city's various shops. While it doesn't fuse your weapons, consuming TeaRealm T-Fusion provides significant temporary buffs to your Esper's combat capabilities, making it easier to clear difficult Anomaly Commissions.

To actually level up an Arc, you will use standard enhancement materials. When you reach a level cap, you must use specific materials—often tied to the Arc's type (e.g., Solid or Liquid)—to "break through" to the next tier.

### Esper Cycles: The Core Combat Mechanic

Combat in NTE is built around six distinct Esper types: **Anima, Chaos, Cosmos, Incantation, Lakshana, and Psyche**. Instead of relying on a monster's active skill (like a [Wuthering Waves Echo](https://ntecodex.com/faq)), players trigger "Cycles" by switching between characters and layering elemental effects.

*   **Anima & Chaos:** Often serve as the primary damage dealers or "Main DPS" roles.
*   **Cosmos & Psyche:** Focus on crowd control and spatial manipulation.
*   **Incantation & Lakshana:** Usually provide utility, healing, or defensive shields.

Understanding the synergy between these types is more important than any individual gear piece. A well-timed cycle can clear a room of Anomalies faster than a single high-stat Arc.

### Urban Progression: City Tycoon and Vehicle Stats

A unique pillar of Neverness to Everness is the **City Tycoon** system. Your progression isn't limited to combat; you also manage businesses and properties within Hethereau. This system provides a passive income of materials and currency that can be reinvested into your characters.

Furthermore, **Vehicle Tuning** is a major stat-sink for endgame players. You can modify your cars with different parts, such as:
*   **LSD (Limited Slip Differential):** Affects handling and cornering during high-speed chases.
*   **Suspension:** Impacts how the vehicle handles the varied terrain of the city.
*   **Tires:** Different compounds affect grip and top speed.

While car stats don't directly increase your Esper's attack power, they are vital for completing timed delivery missions and urban challenges that reward high-tier Cartridges and Arc enhancement materials.

### Commonly Asked Questions about NTE Progression

Many players wonder if there is a way to "reroll" the substats on their Cartridges, similar to tuning Echoes in *Wuthering Waves*. Currently, the process for optimizing Cartridges involves farming new drops from higher-level Anomaly Commissions rather than using a specific calibration fluid. Official details on a dedicated "reroll" mechanic have not been published by Hotta Studio as of the current patch.

Another frequent question concerns the "Monster Book" or collection rewards. While the game tracks your encounters with various Anomalies through the Bureau's records, these are primarily for lore and one-time milestone rewards rather than a repeatable [fusion system](https://ntecodex.com/faq).

### NTE vs. Wuthering Waves: Which System is More F2P Friendly?

The F2P friendliness of NTE's gear system is often debated. Because Arcs are tied to the gacha, getting a character's "signature" weapon can be more difficult than farming a high-tier Echo in *Wuthering Waves*. However, the **Awakening** system in NTE allows players to use generic materials to upgrade character power over time, which can be more forgiving than needing multiple pulls of the same character to unlock their full potential.

Additionally, the City Tycoon system allows dedicated F2P players to "buy" their way into high-level gear by managing their urban resources effectively, providing a path to power that doesn't rely solely on combat or gacha luck.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is there any way to fuse items in Neverness to Everness?
While there is no "Echo Fusion," NTE features a crafting system where players can combine lower-tier materials to create higher-tier ascension items. This is done at specific workbenches within the Bureau or in player-owned properties.

### What are Cartridges in NTE?
Cartridges are the primary equipment pieces that provide set bonuses and substats. They are categorized by their manufacturer and provide different bonuses depending on how many pieces of the same set you have equipped on an Esper.

### How do I get more Annulith for upgrading Arcs?
Annulith is primarily earned through Anomaly Commissions and as a byproduct of your businesses in the City Tycoon system. High-level street races and urban challenges also provide significant amounts of this currency.

### What is the best way to farm for S-Rank Arcs?
The most reliable way to obtain S-Rank Arcs is through the limited-time banners using the gacha system. However, players can also find them as extremely rare drops from World Boss Anomalies or as rewards for reaching the highest ranks in the Bureau's commission system.

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/nte-how-to-increase-union-level/inline-1.webp)


In Neverness to Everness (NTE), players often search for the "Union Level," which is the terminology used for account-wide progression in many other titles. However, it is important to clarify that in NTE, this system is officially referred to as the **Hunter Level**. This level acts as the primary gatekeeper for your experience in Hethereau.

The Hunter Level represents your overall account standing. It determines your access to new Main Story Episodes, side activities, and high-tier character development features. Most importantly, your Hunter Level dictates your Appraisal Level (often called World Level), which scales the difficulty of enemies and the quality of the loot they drop.

Increasing your Hunter Level is the only way to unlock character level caps through Ascension. Without a high enough Hunter Level, your Espers will remain stuck at lower levels, making it impossible to clear late-game Anomaly Zones or high-difficulty bosses.

### What is Hunter Level in NTE?

Your Hunter Level is the numerical value displayed on the top left of your main menu and on your in-game phone profile. It tracks the total Hunter EXP you have accumulated from all activities. Unlike individual Esper levels, which apply only to specific characters, the Hunter Level applies to your entire account. 

### Why is Increasing Your Level Important?

As you reach specific Hunter Level milestones, you can increase your Appraisal Level. Higher Appraisal Levels improve the drop rates for rare materials and Arcs (weapons) in the open world and within instanced challenges. Furthermore, story progression is strictly gated by this level; for instance, certain Spinoffs and later Episodes will not trigger until you reach the required Hunter Level.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-how-to-increase-union-level/inline-2.webp)


The most efficient way to increase your Union Level (Hunter Level) is through a combination of Main Story Episodes and Daily Participation Tasks. While the story provides the largest chunks of EXP at once, the Daily Participation Tasks provide a consistent 1,000 Hunter EXP every 24 hours, making them the most important routine for long-term growth.

To level up quickly, you should focus on:
*   **Completing Main Story Episodes:** These offer between 500 and 2,000 Hunter EXP per chapter.
*   **Daily Participation:** Four simple tasks that guarantee 1,000 EXP daily.
*   **Spending Character Pixels:** This is your combat stamina. Spending Pixels in Anomaly Zones like "Houdinii’s Magic Stage" converts stamina directly into Hunter EXP.
*   **Exploration:** Activating Check-In Points (Teleports) and finding "Gifts from 21" (chests) provides smaller but frequent EXP boosts.
*   **Appraisal Breakthroughs:** You must complete trial quests at levels 20, 40, and 50 to unlock the level cap and continue gaining EXP at the full rate.

## Detailed Explanation

Progression in Neverness to Everness is designed to be a marathon rather than a sprint, but understanding where your EXP comes from allows you to optimize your daily playtime.

### Maximizing Daily Participation Tasks

Daily Participation Tasks are unlocked shortly after completing Prologue Part 2. These tasks are refreshed daily at the server reset time. They are designed to be completed in under 15 minutes and are the single most important source of Hunter EXP for players looking to keep up with the world difficulty.

Each of the four tasks provides 250 Hunter EXP, totaling 1,000 EXP per day. The tasks typically involve logging in, spending a set amount of Fons (the standard currency), consuming a portion of your Character Pixels, and reaching a specific activity point threshold through combat or exploration.

### Stamina Efficiency: Spending Character Pixels

Character Pixels are the primary stamina system for combat-related rewards. They regenerate at a rate of 1 Pixel every 6 minutes, giving you a total of 240 Pixels over a 24-hour period.

Every 10 Pixels spent in an Anomaly Zone or against a world boss yields a fixed amount of Hunter EXP. To maximize efficiency, never let your Pixels sit at the cap (currently 240). If you are short on time, you can quickly burn Pixels in Anomaly Zones.

*   **Houdinii’s Magic Stage:** Primarily for Esper EXP materials and Hunter EXP.

Spending 40 Pixels at once usually provides a more significant "chunk" of progress toward your daily activity goals while yielding the same EXP-to-Pixel ratio as smaller spends.

### The Appraisal Level Gate System

One of the most critical mechanics to understand is the Appraisal Level Breakthrough. When you reach Hunter Levels 20, 40, and 50, your Hunter EXP bar will fill up but you will not level up further. Instead, you will receive a "Breakthrough Quest" in your mission log.

You must complete this specific combat trial to increase your Appraisal Level. If you do not complete the trial, any further Hunter EXP you earn will be penalized, often reducing your gains to only 10% of their original value. This system ensures that your account level does not outpace your actual combat ability, as a higher Appraisal Level makes world enemies significantly tougher.

### Exploration: Finding Gifts from 21 and Anomalies

For players who have exhausted their daily tasks and story missions, exploration is the only remaining way to grind Hunter EXP. 

1.  **Check-In Points:** These are the fast-travel nodes scattered throughout Hethereau. Activating a new Check-In Point grants 50 Hunter EXP.
2.  **Gifts from 21:** These are the standard treasure chests of NTE. They are often guarded by Anomalies (enemies) or hidden behind light environmental puzzles. Each "Gift from 21" provides 10 Hunter EXP.
3.  **Anomalous Investigations:** These are more complex world puzzles or elite enemy encounters. Completing an investigation provides a higher amount of EXP, typically ranging from 20 to 100 depending on the difficulty.

### Tycoon Level and the Hunter Exchange

Neverness to Everness features a secondary progression system called the City Tycoon system. While your Tycoon Level is separate from your Hunter Level, the two are linked through "City Stamina."

By engaging in Hethereau Hobbies—such as urban racing, delivery missions, or managing your cafe—you earn Tycoon EXP. While these activities do not provide direct Hunter EXP in large quantities, spending City Stamina contributes to your overall account progression and unlocks the **Hunter Exchange**. The Hunter Exchange is a shop where you can use currency earned from Tycoon activities to buy Hunter EXP boosters and Ascension materials, effectively allowing you to "buy" progress through gameplay.

Increasing your Hunter Level is just the first step. Once you have reached a new level, you must utilize that progress to strengthen your team.

### Character Ascension and Level Caps

Your Espers (characters) are strictly limited by your current Hunter Level. Even if you have enough EXP items to reach level 80, you cannot do so until your account reaches Hunter Level 50 and you complete the Appraisal Level 6 breakthrough.

Ascension requires specific materials found in Anomaly Zones. It is recommended to farm these materials as soon as you reach a new Appraisal Level, as the difficulty of world content will increase immediately upon completing a breakthrough quest.

### Arc (Weapon) Progression

Similar to characters, your Arcs (weapons) are capped by your Appraisal Level. Arcs provide the bulk of your Esper's base stats, including Attack and Critical Rate. If you find your damage output falling behind as you level up your Hunter Level, it is likely because your Arcs are under-leveled. Always prioritize leveling the Arcs for your main DPS characters first.

### Claiming Your Hunter Rewards

A common mistake new players make is forgetting to claim their level-up rewards. On your in-game phone, there is a dedicated "Hunter Rewards" menu. For every Hunter Level gained, you can claim a variety of rewards.

**Hunter Reward Highlights:**
*   **Annuliths:** The premium currency used for pulling on banners like "The Ichi-daime."
*   **Standard Pulls:** Vouchers for the permanent recruitment banner.
*   **Ascension Materials:** Rare items needed to break character level caps.
*   **Fuel Refills:** Items that restore 60 Character Pixels instantly.

| Levels 21–40 | Advanced Ascension Mats, 4-Star Arc Boxes, Fuel Refills |

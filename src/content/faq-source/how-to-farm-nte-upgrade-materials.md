## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-farm-nte-upgrade-materials/inline-3.webp)


The progression systems in Neverness to Everness are deeply interconnected. While you may focus on leveling a specific Esper, you will quickly find that their effectiveness is capped by their Arc level, their Module quality, and even the bonuses provided by your urban standing.

### Farming Character EXP and Ascension Materials

Character leveling is the most straightforward progression path. You use Hunter Guides to increase an Esper's base level. These guides come in three primary tiers: Rising, Senior, and Elite.

The most efficient place to farm these is **Houdinii’s Magic Stage**. This stage scales with your City Level, meaning the rewards improve as you progress through the main story. A single run typically costs 20 Character Pixels.

Once an Esper reaches a level cap (starting at level 20), you must perform an Ascension (or Breakthrough). This requires materials dropped by world anomalies found throughout Hethereau. Common materials include:
*   **Lost Whispers:** Dropped by basic humanoid anomalies.
*   **Blurred Numerals:** Found after defeating glitch-type enemies in the industrial sectors.
*   **Suspended Delusions:** Rare drops from elite anomalies in the New Herland District.

### Arc (Weapon) Enhancement and Mixing

Arcs serve as the primary "weapons" for your Espers. Enhancing them requires **Dyes**, which function as weapon EXP. These are farmed in the **Kaleidoscope** stage. Like Hunter Guides, Dyes have different rarities that provide varying amounts of EXP.

Ascending an Arc to unlock higher level caps requires specialized materials from the **Bubble Can Factory**. This domain focuses on combat challenges that reward items like the undisclosed and various industrial components.

Mixing (refining) an Arc requires duplicate copies of the same Arc or specialized "Universal Mixing Components" which are often limited to event rewards or high-level shop exchanges.

### The Module Rewind System: Farming Carrota

Modules are the most complex part of an Esper's build, functioning similarly to equipment sets in other RPGs. To obtain high-tier Modules, you must clear **The Rabbit Hole** located in the New Herland District.

Completing Rabbit Hole stages rewards you with **Carrota**, a unique currency used in the **Rewind System**. The Rewind system is a specialized gacha mechanic for gear:
*   **Bronze Carrota:** Used for basic Module rolls.
*   **Silver Carrota:** Guarantees at least a rare (Blue) or higher Module.
*   **Gold Carrota:** Used for high-end Rewinds with a chance for Epic (Purple) or Legendary (Gold) Modules.

Each Module has a "Cartridge" slot. Farming the correct Cartridge requires targeting specific days of the week in the Rabbit Hole, as the available sets rotate regularly.

### Anomaly Pilgrimages: Weekly Boss Material Guide

For high-level character and Arc breakthroughs, you must challenge **Anomaly Pilgrimages**. These are boss encounters that require 40 to 60 Character Pixels to claim rewards.

Key bosses currently in the rotation include:
*   **Headless Rider:** Drops materials required for physical and high-mobility Espers.
*   **Morphix:** Provides materials for Espers specializing in elemental manipulation.
*   **Swallowtail:** A high-difficulty boss in the central plaza area that drops elite breakthrough items.

These bosses have weekly reward limits. It is highly recommended to clear your maximum number of Pilgrimages every week, even if you do not currently have an Esper that needs those specific materials, as these become the primary bottleneck in the late game.

### Urban Farming: Fons and Vehicle Modifications

Unlike combat materials, urban resources are farmed through gameplay systems like **City Tycoon** and **Commissions**. Fons are the primary currency used for almost everything in Hethereau, from buying snacks to purchasing high-end real estate.

**Vehicle Customization** is a major part of the NTE experience. To upgrade your car's performance, you need to visit **Rampage Players** shops. There are currently three main locations:
1.  **Old Town Branch:** Focuses on basic engines and tires.
2.  **Central Hethereau Hub:** Sells mid-tier transmissions and nitro kits.
3.  **New Herland Performance Center:** Stocks the highest-tier racing parts.

Parts at these shops cost a combination of Fons and **Beetle Coins**. Beetle Coins are earned through street racing, delivery missions, and finding hidden collectibles throughout the city.

### Advanced Efficiency: The Butler and Hamster Ball Systems

To minimize the daily grind, Neverness to Everness includes several "quality of life" farming systems.

The **Butler** is an AI assistant that can be configured to "auto-farm" stages you have already cleared with a 3-star rating. You can assign your Character Pixels to the Butler, and it will complete the runs over time, even while you are exploring the city or offline. This is best used for Hunter Guides and Dyes.

The **Hamster Ball** is a passive reward generator located in your home base. It accumulates small amounts of Fons, basic EXP materials, and occasionally rare items like undisclosed. The capacity of the Hamster Ball increases as you level up your City Tycoon rank.

When starting out, focus your Hunter Guides on a single "Carry" Esper to level 40 before spreading resources across a full team of four. This allows you to clear higher-level stages of Houdinii’s Magic Stage earlier, increasing your overall farming efficiency.

### Best S-Rank Arcs for Beginners

While Arcs are often obtained through the gacha system, some can be earned through high-level city achievements. The "Standard Resonance" concept often discussed in community forums refers to the baseline effectiveness of Arcs when matched with the correct Esper element.

Always prioritize ascending Arcs that provide "Anomaly Damage" or "Pixel Regeneration" stats, as these significantly reduce the time spent in combat encounters during your farm routes.

### Hethereau District Exploration Guide

Farming isn't just about combat; it's about exploration. Throughout the various districts of Hethereau, you will find **Oracle Stones**. These are collectible items that, when turned in at the central plaza, increase your maximum Character Pixel cap.

Increasing your stamina cap is the single most important thing you can do for long-term farming efficiency. A higher cap means more runs of the Bubble Can Factory or the Rabbit Hole before needing to wait for a recharge or using a undisclosed item to restore Pixels.

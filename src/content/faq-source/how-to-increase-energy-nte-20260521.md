Efficiently managing resources in the urban supernatural world of Hethereau is the difference between a stalled account and a high-tier endgame build.

# How to Increase Energy in Neverness to Everness (NTE)

Navigating the various systems in Neverness to Everness (NTE) requires a clear understanding of its distinct "Energy" pools. Unlike many urban RPGs that use a single stamina bar, NTE splits your progression into three primary categories: Character Pixels for combat rewards, City Stamina for the Tycoon economy, and Ultimate Energy for combat performance. This guide explains how to increase each limit and restore your reserves to maintain peak efficiency.

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/how-to-increase-energy-nte-20260521/inline-1.webp)


Players often ask "how to increase energy" referring to three different mechanics that govern how much content they can clear per day or week. Because Character Pixels, City Stamina, and Ultimate Energy operate on different timers and scaling systems, a one-size-fits-all approach does not exist.

Character Pixels act as your primary farming stamina. You consume these to collect materials from Anomaly Zones to upgrade your characters and Arcs. City Stamina is tied specifically to the Hethereau Hobbies and Tycoon systems, allowing you to earn Fons (currency) through activities like street racing and fishing. Finally, Ultimate Energy is the combat resource that dictates how often you can unleash your character's most powerful abilities.

### What is Character Pixels?
Character Pixels are the gatekeeper for character progression. Every material needed for leveling up, ascending Arcs, or obtaining Cartridges (equipment) is found in Anomaly Zone stages, which cost Pixels to enter. This resource regenerates naturally over time, following a 24-hour cycle to reach its default maximum capacity.

### What is City Stamina?
City Stamina is the engine of your in-game wealth. It is used exclusively for non-combat "Tycoon" activities. Whether you are delivering packages, participating in high-stakes races, or fishing in the city's waterways, you spend City Stamina to earn Fons. Unlike Pixels, this resource does not regenerate hourly; it is a weekly allowance that resets every Monday.

### What is Ultimate Energy?
Ultimate Energy is a combat-only resource. It is generated during active encounters through basic attacks, skills, and specific elemental reactions known as Esper Cycles. Increasing your "Cycle Rate" stat and mastering the Esper Cycle Meter are the primary ways to maximize your Ultimate uptime.

| Resource Type | Primary Usage | Base Cap | Regeneration / Reset |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Character Pixels** | Anomaly Zones (Materials/EXP) | 240 | 1 per 6 minutes (Daily) |
| **City Stamina** | Tycoon Hobbies (Fons/Racing) | 200 (Rank 5) | Weekly Reset (Monday 5 AM) |
| **Ultimate Energy** | Casting Ultimate Abilities | 100 [approximate] | Combat Actions / Reactions |

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-increase-energy-nte-20260521/inline-2.webp)


To increase energy in Neverness to Everness, you must target the specific recovery items and progression milestones associated with each system.

*   **To increase Character Pixels:** Allow them to regenerate naturally (1 every 6 minutes), use a **De-noise Solution** to restore 60 Pixels instantly, or spend **Annulith** (premium currency) for up to 6 refills per day. To increase the maximum cap beyond 240, purchase the Eden apartment and upgrade the **Dream Weaver's Knot** furniture.
*   **To increase City Stamina:** Raise your **Tycoon Level**. The cap increases at specific milestones (Rank 5, 10, 16, and 23). To restore City Stamina, use the **Refresh Recharge** item or spend Annulith. Note that City Stamina resets to full every Monday at 5:00 AM (UTC+8).
*   **To increase Ultimate Energy generation:** Equip Arcs or Cartridges with the **Cycle Rate** stat. In combat, trigger the **Charge** reaction (a combination of Blossom and Remora) to gain 10 Ultimate Energy per hit.
*   **To increase Movement Stamina:** Level up the **Witch's House** (located southwest of Hankaku Street) to Rank 3. This increases your maximum sprint and climb duration.

### Quick-Reference: Recovery Items and Sources
*   **De-noise Solution:** Restores 60 Character Pixels. Obtained from events, Twitch Drops, and Battle Pass rewards.
*   **Refresh Recharge:** Restores 100 City Stamina. Obtained from Tycoon Level milestones and Hunter Level rewards.
*   **Annulith:** Premium currency used to refill Pixels (60-120 cost) or City Stamina.
*   **Fons:** Used to upgrade Tycoon Level, which indirectly increases your weekly City Stamina cap.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-increase-energy-nte-20260521/inline-3.webp)


Maximizing your energy efficiency requires deep engagement with the city's infrastructure and the combat's elemental reaction systems.

### Mastering Character Pixels (Farming Stamina)
The 240-cap for Character Pixels is reached exactly 24 hours after hitting zero. Because regeneration stops once you hit the cap, you should spend your Pixels daily to avoid "capping out." 

A standard run in an Anomaly Zone costs 40 Pixels. However, once you have cleared a stage, you can select the "double reward" option for 80 Pixels to save time. If you are in a rush to progress, the Annulith refill system is your primary tool. The cost of refilling 60 Pixels scales based on how many times you have done it that day:
*   **Refills 1–3:** 60 Annulith each.
*   **Refills 4–6:** 120 Annulith each.

For long-term growth, focus on increasing the cap. The **Dream Weaver’s Knot** furniture, found in the Eden apartment, is the most reliable way to expand your storage, allowing you to stay away from the game for longer than 24 hours without losing potential regeneration.

### Scaling City Stamina via Tycoon Levels
City Stamina is arguably more important for account-wide wealth than Pixels. Every point of City Stamina spent translates to 1,000 Fons. Since Fons are required for everything from buying luxury cars at the Regalia dealership to upgrading your properties, managing this weekly budget is vital.

Your City Stamina cap scales with your Tycoon Rank:
*   **Rank 5:** Increases cap to 200; unlocks property ownership (Wiener Apartments).
*   **Rank 10:** Increases cap to 350; unlocks the **Pink Paws Heist** (weekly event for 1 million Fons).
*   **Rank 16:** Increases cap to 500. This is a critical breakpoint. At Rank 16, you unlock a time-save mechanic where stamina costs and rewards are increased by 100%, allowing you to burn through your weekly allotment twice as fast.
*   **Rank 23:** Increases cap to 700.

To reach these ranks quickly, participate in Hethereau Hobbies. Fishing is currently considered the fastest way to burn stamina, as one fish caught equals one stamina point spent. Deliveries and Racing offer higher engagement but take slightly longer per stamina point.

### Optimizing Combat Energy and Ultimate Charge
Ultimate Energy management is dictated by your team composition and the **Esper Cycle Meter**. To increase the speed at which you charge Ultimates, you must focus on the **Cycle Rate** stat. Characters like **Fadia** have exceptionally high natural Cycle Rate generation on their basic attacks, making them excellent "batteries" for the team.

The most effective way to generate energy in combat is through the **Charge** Trio-Esper reaction. This requires a team with Cosmos, Anima, and Lakshana elements.
1.  Trigger **Blossom** (Cosmos + Anima) to spawn a Vita Bud that fires Vita Pistils.
2.  Trigger **Remora** (Lakshana + Cosmos) to slow the enemy.
3.  When these coexist, the **Charge** reaction activates. Every time a Vita Pistil hits an enemy affected by Remora, your active character gains 10 Ultimate Energy.

Characters like **[Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)** (from "The Ichi-daime" banner) amplify this further by increasing the number of Vita Pistils spawned from 5 to 10, effectively doubling the energy gain from the reaction. Additionally, performing a **Perfect Parry** instantly fills a significant portion of both the Esper Cycle Meter and the Ultimate gauge, making defensive play a core part of energy management.

### Movement Stamina and Exploration Efficiency
While often overlooked, movement stamina dictates how quickly you can traverse Hethereau to reach Anomaly Zones or safehouses. The **Witch's House** system is the primary way to upgrade this. By collecting birds throughout the city and trading them for Oracle Stones, you can reach Level 3 at the Witch's House. This milestone not only increases your maximum movement stamina but also makes birds visible on your mini-map, accelerating future upgrades.

Specific furniture items also provide QoL (Quality of Life) boosts to movement. For example, the **replica ghost car** furniture at Fangan Villa reduces the durability loss of your vehicles, allowing you to spend more time driving and less time at repair shops.

## Related

Managing your energy is only the first step. Once you have a steady supply of Character Pixels and City Stamina, you must decide where to spend them for the best return on investment.

### NTE Tycoon Leveling Guide
To raise your Tycoon Level and increase your City Stamina cap, you need to accumulate Fons. The most efficient loop involves collecting passive income from the **Cafe by Origen** (unlocked at Rank 4) and reinvesting those Fons into higher-tier properties like Skyview Halls or the 8 million Fon Fangan Villa. Increasing your Tycoon Level is the only way to eventually unlock the S-Rank character **Chiz** for free at Rank 18.

### Best Arcs for Energy Recharge (Cycle Rate)
If your Ultimates are charging too slowly, look for Arcs that provide flat Cycle Rate bonuses. Arcs like **Moment of the Galaxy** or **Stellar Veil** are highly valued for support characters because they allow the team to cycle through Trio-Esper reactions more frequently. Characters like **Zero** benefit from these as they can use their "Instant Cycle" trait to fill the Esper Meter immediately upon casting a skill.

### Anomaly Zone Farming Priority
When spending Character Pixels, follow this priority list based on your Hunter Level:
1.  **Houdini's Magic Stage:** Farm this early for character and weapon EXP materials.
2.  **Bubble Can Factory:** Once your characters reach level 40, shift focus here for Arc ascension materials.
3.  **Rabbit Hole:** Reserved for the late game. This is where you farm for high-rarity Cartridges and Console pieces. Due to the RNG nature of equipment stats, only spend Pixels here once your character levels and Arcs are maxed out.

### Why Dendro Reactions Dominate the Meta

Dendro reactions dominate because they scale primarily with Elemental Mastery (EM) and character level, rather than strictly relying on high-investment weapon refinements or perfect artifact substats. Reactions like Hyperbloom and Burgeon are "transformative," meaning their damage is calculated based on a fixed multiplier that ignores the enemy's Defense, scaling only with the trigger character's EM and Level 90 stats. This allows players to achieve 30,000+ damage per seed trigger with relatively basic gear.

### Key Elemental Synergies to Understand

To master these teams, one must understand the "Dendro Core" system. When Dendro meets Hydro, a Bloom reaction occurs, dropping a Dendro Core. This core can then be interacted with by Electro (Hyperbloom) or Pyro (Burgeon). Alternatively, Dendro and Electro create the Quicken aura, which acts as a debuff on the enemy. Attacking a Quickened enemy with Dendro causes Spread, while attacking with Electro causes Aggravate. Both reactions add a flat damage bonus to the attack, which can then benefit from Critical Hit stats, making them the preferred choice for late-game carries like Alhaitham or Tighnari.

### The Hyperbloom Gold Standard: Nahida and Kuki Shinobu

Hyperbloom is widely considered the most efficient reaction in Genshin Impact due to its homing nature and high damage floor. The reaction occurs when an Electro attack hits a Dendro Core (produced by Bloom). 

The key to this team is the "trigger" character—usually Kuki Shinobu or Raiden Shogun. Because the damage scales entirely off the Electro character's stats, they must be built with maximum Elemental Mastery (EM). A Level 90 Kuki Shinobu with 1,000 EM and the Flower of Paradise Lost artifact set can trigger Hyperblooms for upwards of 35,000 damage every 1.5 seconds. Nahida is the preferred Dendro slot because her Elemental Skill, All Schemes to Know, provides the most consistent off-field Dendro application in the game while sharing up to 250 EM with the active character.

### Catalyze Mastery: Alhaitham and Yae Miko Spread Comps

Unlike Hyperbloom, which is a transformative reaction, Spread and Aggravate function as "additive" reactions. They add a flat amount of damage to the base attack before multipliers like Crit DMG and DMG% bonuses are applied. 

Alhaitham is the premier Spread DPS. His kit involves maintaining Chisel-Light Mirrors to infuse his attacks with Dendro. When paired with an Electro sub-DPS like Yae Miko or Fischl, he can trigger Spread on almost every mirror projection. This team thrives on a "Quickbloom" hybrid setup, where a slow Hydro applier (like Furina) allows Alhaitham to trigger both Spread and Hyperbloom simultaneously, maximizing his total output.

### Burning and Burgeon: Kinich, Emilie, and Thoma

For a long time, Burning was considered a "utility" reaction used mainly to maintain a Pyro aura for Melt teams. This changed with Emilie and Kinich.
*   **Emilie:** Her Elemental Skill, Lumidouce Case, levels up when Burning occurs nearby. At Level 2, the Case deals significantly higher damage and gains increased AoE. Her passive also grants a stacking DMG bonus (up to 72% at max stacks) as long as enemies are Burning.
*   **Kinich:** As a Natlan Dendro DPS, Kinich’s kit rewards the player for maintaining the Burning aura. He uses a grappling mechanic to circle enemies, firing high-damage shots that scale when the target is afflicted by Burning or Burgeon.

To support these two, Mavuika has become the go-to Pyro enabler, offering more consistent off-field Pyro application than Xiangling without the heavy Energy Recharge requirements.

### Natlan Support Integration: Mavuika and Xilonen

The 2026 meta heavily features Natlan characters who bring "Nightsoul" mechanics into Dendro teams. Mavuika provides not just Pyro application but also significant ATK and DMG% buffs that benefit Burning and Burgeon carries. Meanwhile, Xilonen has emerged as a top-tier support for Dendro teams that utilize an Electro or Hydro sub-DPS. Her ability to shred Elemental RES (Resistance) by 36% for elements she interacts with makes her a viable alternative to Kazuha, especially in teams where Geo resonance or specific Natlan artifact sets (like Obsidian Codex) are utilized.

### Understanding Internal Cooldowns (ICD) and Energy Recharge

Internal Cooldown (ICD) determines how often a character can apply an element to a target. Nahida is prized because her Tri-Karma Purification has no ICD, meaning she applies Dendro every time the reaction is triggered. In contrast, characters like the Dendro Traveler have standard ICD, meaning they only apply Dendro every third hit or every 2.5 seconds.

Energy Recharge (ER) is also a critical factor. Dendro Traveler and Baizhu both have high-cost Bursts (80 energy). Without at least 180% - 220% ER, these characters will fail to maintain their Dendro application, causing the team's total DPS to plummet. Always prioritize meeting ER thresholds before stacking EM or Crit stats on your support characters.

### Team Rotations for Maximum DPS

To ensure the correct character triggers the reaction (and thus uses their high EM stat), follow this general sequencing:
1.  **Dendro Application:** Use Nahida's Skill or Traveler's Burst to mark enemies.
2.  **Hydro Application:** Use Xingqiu or Yelan's Burst to start generating Dendro Cores.
3.  **Buffs/Shields:** Switch to your defensive support (Zhongli, Baizhu, or Xilonen).
4.  **Electro/Pyro Trigger:** Switch to your EM-trigger (Kuki Shinobu or Thoma) and activate their off-field skill.
5.  **Main DPS:** Bring in Alhaitham or Kinich to drive the team and provide additional elemental application.

# How to Upgrade Weapons in Neverness to Everness (NTE)

In Neverness to Everness, weapons are referred to as Arcs, and they serve as the primary source of raw stats and passive combat effects for your Espers. Unlike traditional gear systems that rely on random substat rolls, Arcs follow a deterministic progression path. To maximize your combat effectiveness against high-level anomalies, you must master the three pillars of Arc development: Enhancement, Ascension, and Mixing.

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/how-to-upgrade-weapons-nte-guide/inline-1.webp)


Players often find themselves hitting a wall when tackling the harder Anomaly Zones in Hethereau. This usually leads to three core questions regarding gear progression:

*   **What is the process for upgrading weapons (Arcs) in Neverness to Everness?** You must navigate to the "Develop" menu within the Esper interface to access the enhancement and ascension screens.
*   **Which materials are required for leveling and ascending weapons?** Progression requires a combination of Dye (EXP items), Beetle Coins (currency), and specific drops from Anomaly Zones.
*   **How do you unlock higher level caps for your gear in NTE?** Your ability to upgrade Arcs is strictly gated by your Hunter Rank and the resulting Appraisal Level of your account.

Understanding the interplay between these systems is vital for efficient resource management, especially since the stamina resource, Character Pixels, is shared between weapon upgrades and character ascension.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-upgrade-weapons-nte-guide/inline-2.webp)


In NTE, weapons are called Arcs. You upgrade them through three main methods: Enhancement, Ascension, and Mixing.

*   **Enhancement:** This is the basic leveling process. You use "Dye" (available in various tiers of rarity) and "Beetle Coins" to increase the Arc's level, which directly boosts its base attributes like ATK and HP.
*   **Ascension:** Once an Arc reaches a level cap (milestones occur at levels 20, 40, 50, 60, 70, and 80), you must perform an Ascension. This requires specific materials harvested from world bosses or the Bubble Can Factory Anomaly Zone.
*   **Mixing:** This is the refinement system. By consuming duplicate copies of the same Arc, you increase its "Mixing Intensity." This progression runs from T1 to T5 and significantly boosts the weapon's unique passive ability.
*   **Gating:** All upgrading is gated by your Hunter Rank (Appraisal Level). If your Appraisal Level is too low, the game will prevent you from ascending your Arcs further, regardless of how many materials you have.

### Quick Reference Table: Arc Upgrade Types & Materials

| Upgrade Type | Primary Materials | Primary Purpose | Scaling Limit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Enhancement** | Dye (various rarities), Beetle Coins | Increases Base ATK and Substats | Capped by current Ascension tier |
| **Ascension** | Anomaly Zone Drops, Boss Materials | Breaks level caps (20, 40, 50, etc.) | Capped by Appraisal Level |
| **Mixing** | Duplicate Arcs | Increases Passive Ability (T1 to T5) | Max Tier 5 (5 total copies) |

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-upgrade-weapons-nte-guide/inline-3.webp)


The Arc development system is housed within the "Develop" menu of each character's Esper interface. To reach it, open your character roster, select an Esper, click on their equipped Arc, and select the "Develop" button. This interface is divided into the three tabs mentioned above.

Managing your upgrades requires a careful balance of your Character Pixels. Character Pixels are your combat stamina, which regenerates at a rate of 1 Pixel every 6 minutes, capping at 240. Since every run of an Anomaly Zone costs Pixels, you must prioritize whether you need EXP (Dye) or Ascension materials on any given day.

### Leveling Up Arcs (Enhancement)

Enhancement is the most frequent upgrade you will perform. The primary material used here is Dye. Dye comes in different rarities, with higher-tier Dye providing significantly more EXP than the common variants found during early exploration.

To enhance an Arc:
1.  Open the Develop menu and stay on the Enhancement tab.
2.  Select the Dye materials you wish to consume.
3.  Pay the required amount of Beetle Coins.

Beetle Coins act as the "gold" or "mora" equivalent for gear progression. While they are plentiful in the early game, the cost of leveling an S-Rank Arc from 70 to 80 is exponentially higher than the 1 to 20 range. If you run out of Beetle Coins, you can farm the "Coinicles" stage within Houdini's Magic Stage Anomaly Zone.

### Breaking Level Caps (Ascension)

Ascension is the process of "limit breaking" your Arc so it can reach the next set of levels. You cannot level an Arc past its current milestone until you perform an Ascension.

**Arc Level Milestones and Ascension Requirements**

| Milestone | Appraisal Level Required | Primary Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Level 20 | Appraisal Level 1 | Bubble Can Factory (Low Tier) |
| Level 40 | Appraisal Level 3 | Bubble Can Factory (Mid Tier) |
| Level 50 | Appraisal Level 4 | Anomaly Hunt Bosses |
| Level 60 | Appraisal Level 5 | Anomaly Hunt Bosses |
| Level 80 | Appraisal Level 7 | [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-19] |

Ascension materials are more specialized than Dye. For A-Rank Arcs, you typically need common drops from enemies found in Hethereau. For S-Rank Arcs, you will often need rarer items like "Golden Appleseed" or "Harmony," which are typically obtained through the Bubble Can Factory or by exchanging Mhm! Coins in the Hunter Exchange.

### Increasing Passive Power (Mixing)

Mixing is the only way to improve the unique passive effect of an Arc. For example, an S-Rank Arc like "Fluff of Fearlessness" has a passive called "Popping Candy" that grants an ATK bonus after casting an Ultimate. At T1 (the base level), this bonus is 25%. By Mixing four duplicate copies into the original, you reach T5, which increases that bonus to 45%.

**Strategic Advice for Mixing:**
*   **Early Game:** Do not mix your A-Rank duplicates immediately if you are short on weapons. It is better to have two separate A-Rank Arcs equipped on two different characters than one T2 Arc and one character with no weapon at all.
*   **F2P Strategy:** Focus on reaching T5 with A-Rank Arcs first. A T5 A-Rank Arc often outperforms a T1 S-Rank Arc in specific niche builds.
*   **Locking Gear:** Always lock your T5 Arcs. The interface allows you to accidentally use a T5 Arc as Mixing material for a T1 Arc, which results in a massive loss of resources.

### Where to Farm Upgrade Materials

Almost all Arc materials are tied to the Anomaly Zone system. You can access these zones via the world map or the "Anomagram" menu.

1.  **Houdini's Magic Stage (Kaleidoscope):** This is the premier location for farming Dye. It provides the highest EXP-to-Pixel ratio for leveling weapons.
2.  **Bubble Can Factory:** This zone is dedicated to Ascension materials. Different nodes within the factory drop different types of materials, so check the "Source" button on the Ascension screen to find exactly which node you need.
3.  **Anomaly Commissions:** These are one-time world challenges that often reward S-Rank Arcs or large quantities of Beetle Coins.
4.  **Hunter Exchange:** If you are a few materials short of an Ascension and don't want to spend Character Pixels, check the Mall. The Hunter Exchange allows you to use currency earned from daily activities to buy specific upgrade items.

### The Role of Hunter Rank and Appraisal Level

Your Hunter Rank is your account level. As you gain Hunter EXP from quests and spending Pixels, your Hunter Rank increases. At specific intervals (Rank 20, 40, 50), you will receive an "Ascension Quest" to raise your Appraisal Level.

The Appraisal Level acts as a world tier. Raising it makes enemies in the open world harder and increases the quality of drops in Anomaly Zones. Crucially, it also dictates the maximum level your Arcs can reach. If you find that the "Ascend" button is greyed out despite having the materials, check your Appraisal Level; you likely need to complete your next account-level milestone quest.

## Related

![Related](/img/how-to-upgrade-weapons-nte-guide/inline-4.webp)


Weapon progression does not exist in a vacuum. To truly optimize your characters, you must understand how Arcs interact with other systems like the Console and the gacha economy.

### Best S-Rank Arcs to Upgrade First

When deciding where to spend your limited resources, prioritize S-Rank Arcs that offer universal stats like CRIT Rate or CRIT DMG. "Fluff of Fearlessness" is a standout choice for many DPS characters because its CRIT Rate substat scales up to 22% at Level 80, and its Ultimate-based passive is easy to trigger.

Another high-priority investment is "The Day Off," which is widely considered the best-in-slot option for Esper Zero. Because S-Rank Arcs require significantly more Beetle Coins and rarer Ascension materials than A-Rank gear, focus on one "Main DPS" Arc at a time rather than spreading your materials across your entire four-person team.

### How to Get More Tri-Keys for Arc Pulls

Tri-Keys are the currency used for the "Arc Research Program," which is the dedicated weapon banner. Unlike character banners that use Dice, Arcs require these specialized keys.

*   **Annulith Processing:** You can convert Annulith (premium currency) into Tri-Keys at a rate of 160 per key.
*   **Hunter Exchange:** A limited number of Tri-Keys are available for purchase each month using Fons or other secondary currencies.
*   **Events:** Most limited-time events, such as those accompanying the "The Ichi-daime" banner, provide Tri-Keys as milestone rewards.

The Arc banner features a pity system where you are guaranteed the featured S-Rank Arc within 80 pulls. If you are a free-to-play player, it is generally recommended to save your Tri-Keys until you pull a character whose signature Arc significantly changes their playstyle.

### NTE Character Leveling vs. Weapon Leveling

A common mistake is over-investing in character levels while neglecting Arcs. In Neverness to Everness, a character's base stats are relatively low compared to the massive ATK and substat boosts provided by a high-level Arc.

If you are low on Character Pixels, prioritize your Arc levels first. A Level 40 character with a Level 60 Arc will almost always deal more damage than a Level 60 character with a Level 40 Arc. Furthermore, Arcs are transferable. If you pull a better Esper later, you can simply move your high-level Arc to the new character, whereas the resources spent on character levels are permanently tied to that specific Esper.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-get-more-astrites-wuthering-waves/inline-3.webp)


### Daily Activity and the Guidebook

The Guidebook is your most reliable tool for steady progression. By completing simple tasks such as spending Waveplates, synthesizing items, or defeating a specific number of enemies, you earn Activity Points.

There are five reward milestones at 20, 40, 60, 80, and 100 points. Reaching the 100-point milestone grants a total of 60 Astrites per day. Over a 30-day month, this adds up to 1,800 Astrites, which is enough for 11 pulls. This should be your absolute priority every time you log in.

### World Exploration: Chests, Puzzles, and Blobflies

The open world of Solaris-3 is filled with hidden treasures. Supply Chests are the most numerous source of one-time Astrites. They are categorized into four rarities, each providing a different amount of currency.

| Chest Type | Astrite Reward |
| :--- | :--- |
| Basic Supply Chest | 5 Astrites |
| Medium Supply Chest | 10 Astrites |
| Advanced Supply Chest | 20 Astrites |
| Premium Supply Chest | 40 Astrites |

Beyond chests, you will encounter "Blobflies"—small, golden flying creatures that flee when approached. Destroying a Blobfly usually rewards 10 Astrites. Environmental puzzles, such as the Magnetic Cube or Signal Consoles, also reward chests upon completion. To make exploration efficient, use the **Lootmapper** gadget, which reveals nearby chests on your mini-map once you have reached the required level with the Pioneer Association.

### Sonance Caskets and the Relic Merchant

As you explore, you will find Sonance Caskets floating in hard-to-reach places. These are regional collectibles that you can turn in to Chenpi, the Relic Merchant in Jinzhou. Turning in these caskets increases your Delivery Level, which provides tiered rewards including Astrites, Lustrous Tides, and stamina-boosting items.

| Delivery Level | Astrite Reward |
| :--- | :--- |
| Level 1-5 | 50 Astrites per level |
| Level 6-10 | 100 Astrites per level |

### Data Bank and Echo Collection

[Wuthering Waves](https://ntecodex.com/guides/tactical-simulacrum-event-rewards-wuthering-waves-gear-guide) features a unique system where you capture "Echoes" from defeated monsters. Every time you capture a new type of Echo or find a higher rarity version of an existing one, you gain Data Bank experience.

Leveling up your Data Bank is one of the most lucrative one-time Astrite farms. Each level usually rewards 40 Astrites. Since there are 20 levels (and potentially more in future updates), this system provides a massive bank of currency as you fill out your Echo Gallery.

### Tower of Adversity: The Endgame Grind

The Tower of Adversity is the primary endgame combat mode. It is divided into three sections: the Stable Zone, the Experimental Zone, and the Hazard Zone.

*   **Stable Zone:** A one-time challenge consisting of several floors. Completing this with full stars yields 800 Astrites.
*   **Experimental Zone:** A more difficult one-time challenge that yields 1200 Astrites for a full clear.
*   **Hazard Zone:** This is the "resetting" portion of the tower. It consists of three towers (Tower of Echoing, Tower of Resonating, and Central Tower). You can earn up to 700 Astrites per cycle by earning "Crests" (stars) based on your clear speed. This zone typically resets every two weeks, making it essential for long-term farming.

### Depths of Illusive Realm (Roguelike Mode)

The Depths of Illusive Realm is a roguelike game mode that offers a separate progression system. By completing runs and leveling up your "Illusive Consciousness," you earn currency that can be spent in the Illusive Store. This store typically contains over 1,000 Astrites, along with upgrade materials and Echoes. This mode refreshes with major game updates, providing a significant seasonal boost to your pull savings.

### Main, Companion, and Side Quests

Story content is a major driver of Astrite income. Main Quests (Yellow) provide the largest chunks, often rewarding 60 to 100 Astrites per chapter. Companion Stories (Purple) focus on specific Resonators and offer similar rewards. Side Quests (Blue) vary in length; some are short and offer 20 Astrites, while multi-part world quests can offer hundreds of Astrites across several stages.

### Tactical Holograms and First-Time Boss Clears

Tactical Holograms are high-difficulty boss encounters found in the open world. Each boss has multiple difficulty tiers (I through VI). Clearing a tier for the first time rewards you with Astrites and high-tier Echoes. These are permanent additions to the world and do not reset, but they serve as a challenging way to "burst" farm currency when you are close to a pity milestone.

### Pioneer Association Exploration Progress

In Jinzhou, you can speak to Maqi at the Pioneer Association to claim rewards based on your total exploration percentage for each region (e.g., Central Plains, Desorock Highland).

*   **20% Exploration:** 50 Astrites
*   **40% Exploration:** 50 Astrites
*   **60% Exploration:** 50 Astrites
*   **80% Exploration:** 50 Astrites
*   **100% Exploration:** 50 Astrites

Completing every region to 100% provides a substantial total, encouraged by the use of the Lootmapper and Casket Sonar gadgets.

### Synthesis and Cooking Proficiency Levels

Non-combat activities also contribute to your wealth. As you synthesize potions or cook meals, you gain proficiency points for those specific skills. Reaching new Proficiency Levels rewards you with small amounts of Astrites (usually 10-20 per level) and various recipes. While not a primary source, it is a nice bonus for players who engage with the game's crafting systems.

### Limited-Time Events and Login Rewards

Kuro Games frequently runs limited-time events. These can range from combat challenges to exploration-based minigames. Most major events provide between 400 and 1,000 Astrites. Additionally, "7-Day Sign-in" events often appear during new patch launches, granting Radiant Tides or Astrites simply for logging into the game.

### Redemption Codes and Mail Compensation

Always keep an eye on your in-game mail. ### What to Spend Astrites On

The most efficient use of Astrites is purchasing **Radiant Tides** (for limited character banners) or **Forging Tides** (for limited weapon banners). You should generally avoid spending Astrites on the Standard Banner (Lustrous Tides), as the game provides many of these for free through leveling up and the Guidebook.

One controversial use of Astrites is "Waveplate Refreshes." You can spend Astrites to restore 60 Waveplates up to six times per day. The cost increases with each refresh (60, 120, 150, etc.). For F2P players, this is generally discouraged, as those Astrites are better spent on new characters.

### Understanding the Pity System

Knowing how many Astrites you need is essential for planning. [Wuthering Waves](https://ntecodex.com/guides/tactical-simulacrum-event-rewards-wuthering-waves-gear-guide) features a "pity" system that guarantees a 5-star result after a certain number of pulls.

| Pull Count | Astrite Cost | Guarantee |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 Pull | 160 Astrites | N/A |
| 10 Pulls | 1,600 Astrites | Guaranteed 4-star or higher |
| 80 Pulls | 12,800 Astrites | Guaranteed 5-star (Pity) |
| 160 Pulls | 25,600 Astrites | Guaranteed Limited 5-star (Hard Pity/Guarantee) |

Note that the weapon banner in Wuthering Waves is 100% guaranteed for the featured weapon at 80 pulls, unlike the character banner which has a 50/50 mechanic.

### Managing Your Pull Currency

To manage your currency effectively, always keep a "reserve" of at least 12,800 Astrites if there is a future character you desperately want. Because the Hazard Zone in the Tower of Adversity resets every two weeks, your goal should be to build two or three strong teams to clear as many floors as possible, as this is your only infinite, recurring source of "free" Astrites in the late game.

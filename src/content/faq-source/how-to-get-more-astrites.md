## Detailed Explanation

Farming Annulith requires a mix of active gameplay and passive management. Because NTE features a "No 50/50" rule on limited banners, every piece of currency you save brings you closer to a guaranteed S-rank character, making efficiency more rewarding than in many other gacha titles.

### Daily and Weekly Tasks

The bread and butter of your Annulith income is the **Circle Bounty** system. This functions as the game's combined daily quest and Battle Pass mechanic. By completing four simple daily tasks—such as logging in or spending a small amount of Fons—you earn 1,000 Experience Points, which is exactly enough to raise your Bounty Rank by one level. 

While the individual tasks provide Beetle Coins and Hunter EXP, the daily completion chest is the primary source of recurring Annulith. Missing even one day can significantly hamper your long-term pulling power. Weekly missions offer a larger experience boost and additional materials, ensuring you reach the higher tiers of the seasonal pass where Solid Dice and larger Annulith caches are stored.

### Story Missions and Side Quests

During the early game, the **Main Story** (often referred to as "Anomaly Hunter" missions) is the single fastest way to accumulate Annulith. Major story beats, such as the "Good Morning Hethereau" and "Relax Time" quests, unlock core systems and provide hundreds of Annulith upon completion. 

Beyond the main narrative, keep an eye out for **Urban Legends** and side stories marked by blue and white exclamation points on your map. Quests like "Seeing is Believing" are known to provide substantial one-time rewards. While these are not repeatable, they are essential for building the initial stash needed to pull for launch characters like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte).

### Exploration and World Interaction

Hethereau is a massive city filled with "Oddities" and hidden rewards. Exploration is tracked through several specific systems:

*   **Chests:** Scattered throughout districts like Bridge Crossings and Unheard Shores, you will find **Apprentice Chests** and **Hero Chests**. While most contain upgrade materials, higher-tier chests frequently award small amounts of Annulith.
*   **Oracle Stones:** There are 21 Oracle Stones hidden across the city. Collecting these and leveling up your progress at the Witch's House rewards you with 50 Annulith per level, totaling approximately 600 Annulith for full completion.
*   **Gifts from "21":** Finding these hidden collectibles provides additional milestones in your Exploration Guide, which trigger Annulith and Fabricated Dice rewards.
*   **Anomagram / Anomaly Commissions:** Using the Anomagram to track and contain supernatural entities is a core gameplay loop. Each unique commission provides between 10 and 60 Annulith as a one-time reward.

### The City Tycoon System

The **City Tycoon** system is a unique progression mechanic where you manage businesses and properties in Hethereau. By earning Fons and spending **City Stamina** (which resets weekly), you increase your Tycoon Level. Every few levels, you unlock rewards in the **Hunter Exchange**, including Annulith and **Tri-Keys**. 

Leveling your Tycoon rank also unlocks hobbies like racing, fishing, and "Pink Paws Heists." These activities often have their own achievement tracks that reward premium currency for hitting specific milestones, such as reaching a certain fishing level or clearing a race within a time limit.

### Events and Community Milestones

Hotta Studio frequently distributes Annulith via the in-game mailbox for server maintenance, bug fixes, and seasonal events. Understanding how to spend your hard-earned Annulith is just as important as knowing how to get it. The [gacha system](https://ntecodex.com/faq) in Neverness to Everness differs significantly from other titles in the genre.

### How to Spend Annulith Wisely

The golden rule for most players is to **only spend Annulith on Solid Dice**. Because the game provides a steady supply of Fabricated Dice through Hunter Level rewards and the Circle Bounty free track, using Annulith on the standard banner is generally considered inefficient. Furthermore, you should prioritize character banners over the **Arc Research Program** (weapon banner) in the early game, as many S-rank Arcs can be obtained for free by completing specific High-Risk Anomaly Commissions.

### Neverness to Everness Pity System Explained

The summoning system, **[Scarborough Fair](https://ntecodex.com/faq)**, is a board game where you roll dice to move a piece called a **Chuppa** across tiles. 
*   **Soft Pity:** At 70 pulls without an S-rank, the board undergoes a "Modification." The tiles change, and the S-rank rate jumps to approximately 19.59%.
*   **Hard Pity:** You are guaranteed an S-rank character by your 90th pull.
*   **No 50/50:** Unlike [Wuthering Waves](https://ntecodex.com/guides/tactical-simulacrum-event-rewards-wuthering-waves-gear-guide) or Genshin Impact, if you pull an S-rank on a limited banner (such as Nanally's "The Ichi-daime" banner), it is **100% guaranteed** to be the featured character. There is no risk of losing to a standard pool unit.

### Secondary Gacha Currencies: Warp and Lost Pieces

When you land on specific tiles or pull duplicate characters, you earn secondary currencies:
*   **Warp Pieces:** These are earned from S-rank duplicates or specific Hero Chest tiles. They can be traded in the shop for an unlimited number of pulls or even specific standard S-rank characters like [Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide) or Daffodil.
*   **Lost Pieces:** These are more common and are earned from A-rank or B-rank duplicates. They are primarily used to buy a limited number of discounted Solid and Fabricated Dice each month (usually 5 of each), along with essential upgrade materials.

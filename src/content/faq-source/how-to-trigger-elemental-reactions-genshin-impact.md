# How to Trigger Elemental Reactions in Genshin Impact: A Complete Guide

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/how-to-trigger-elemental-reactions-genshin-impact/inline-1.webp)


To master combat in [Genshin Impact](https://ntecodex.com/guides/wuthering-waves-vs-genshin-impact-storage-solutions), players must understand the specific mechanics behind elemental interactions. This involves knowing how to combine the seven elements—Pyro, Hydro, Anemo, Electro, Dendro, Cryo, and Geo—to produce secondary effects that enhance damage or provide utility. Many players ask: what are the specific steps to trigger an elemental reaction? How do different elements like Pyro, Hydro, and Dendro interact with each other in complex chains? Furthermore, does the sequence of applying elements change the outcome or damage of the reaction?

### Understanding the Core Combat Loop

The core combat loop revolves around switching between four party members to apply multiple elements to a single target. Because most characters have a primary element associated with their Vision, they can only apply one type of elemental status at a time. To trigger a reaction, you typically use one character's Elemental Skill or Burst to apply an initial element, then swap to a second character to hit the enemy with a different element. This "swap-and-strike" rhythm is what defines efficient gameplay.

### The Importance of Elemental Synergy

Elemental synergy is not just about dealing more damage; it is about controlling the battlefield. Some reactions, like Frozen, stop enemies in their tracks, while others, like Overloaded, provide knockback to keep aggressive foes at bay. Understanding synergy allows you to build teams where the characters' abilities complement each other. For example, a "Vaporize" team relies on a Hydro character providing a constant status so a Pyro character can deal massive multiplier damage. Without this synergy, characters function in isolation, significantly lowering the team's total damage output (DPS).

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-trigger-elemental-reactions-genshin-impact/inline-2.webp)


To trigger an elemental reaction, you must apply one element to a target to create an "Aura" and then hit that target with a second, different element to "Trigger" the effect. The first element lingers on the enemy for a short duration, indicated by a small icon above their head. While this icon is present, any subsequent hit with a reactive element will immediately cause the reaction to occur.

Elemental reactions occur instantly upon the second application, provided the elements are compatible. For instance, hitting a Pyro-affected enemy with Hydro will always trigger Vaporize. Reactions can also be triggered by environmental factors. Standing in rain applies the Hydro status to everyone, including enemies, making it easier to trigger Electro-Charged or Frozen without using a Hydro character's abilities. Similarly, burning grass applies Pyro, and bodies of water allow for widespread Cryo or Electro application.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-trigger-elemental-reactions-genshin-impact/inline-3.webp)


The mechanics of elemental reactions are divided into several categories based on how the damage is calculated and how the elements interact over time.

### Amplifying Reactions: Maximizing Raw Damage

Amplifying reactions are the most sought-after for "nuke" builds because they multiply the damage of the hit that triggers the reaction. These include Vaporize and Melt. The order of application is vital because it determines the multiplier.

*   **Vaporize:**
    *   **Forward Vaporize:** Applying Hydro to a Pyro aura. This deals 2.0x damage.
    *   **Reverse Vaporize:** Applying Pyro to a Hydro aura. This deals 1.5x damage.
*   **Melt:**
    *   **Forward Melt:** Applying Pyro to a Cryo aura. This deals 2.0x damage.
    *   **Reverse Melt:** Applying Cryo to a Pyro aura. This deals 1.5x damage.

While "Forward" reactions deal more damage per hit, they consume the underlying "Aura" much faster. Most players prefer "Reverse" reactions (like using Xiangling's Pyro Burst on enemies affected by Xingqiu's Hydro) because the Hydro aura stays on the enemy longer, allowing for multiple reactions in a row.

### Transformative Reactions: AoE and Utility

Transformative reactions deal a separate instance of damage that is calculated based on the character's Level and Elemental Mastery (EM), ignoring the character's Attack or CRIT stats.

*   **Swirl:** Triggered when Anemo hits Pyro, Hydro, Electro, or Cryo. It deals damage of the infused element and spreads that element to nearby enemies.
*   **Overloaded:** Pyro + Electro. Causes an explosion that deals Pyro damage and can break Geo shields or heavy armor.
*   **Superconduct:** Cryo + Electro. Deals AoE Cryo damage and reduces the enemy's Physical Resistance by 40% for 12 seconds. This is essential for Physical damage dealers like Eula or Razor.
*   **Electro-Charged:** Hydro + Electro. Deals Electro damage over time and can jump between multiple wet targets.
*   **Crystallize:** Geo + any element (except Anemo/Dendro). Creates a shard. Picking it up grants a shield of that element.
*   **Shatter:** Occurs when a "Heavy" attack (Claymores, Plunging Attacks, or Geo damage) hits a Frozen enemy. It breaks the freeze and deals Physical damage.

### The Dendro Revolution: Bloom and Catalyze Explained

The introduction of Dendro added multi-step reactions that require more specific sequences.

*   **Bloom:** Dendro + Hydro. This creates a Dendro Core. Only 5 cores can exist at once.
    *   **Hyperbloom:** Hit a Dendro Core with Electro. The core turns into a homing missile dealing Dendro damage.
    *   **Burgeon:** Hit a Dendro Core with Pyro. The core explodes for massive AoE Dendro damage.
*   **Catalyze (Quicken):** Dendro + Electro. This applies a "Quicken" aura to the enemy (visualized by green lightning).
    *   **Aggravate:** Hit a Quickened enemy with Electro for a flat damage bonus.
    *   **Spread:** Hit a Quickened enemy with Dendro for a flat damage bonus.
*   **Burning:** Dendro + Pyro. Deals continuous Pyro damage over time.

### Advanced Mechanics: Internal Cooldown (ICD) and Gauge Theory

You cannot trigger a reaction with every single hit of an ability due to Internal Cooldown (ICD). Most abilities follow a "Standard ICD" rule: an element can only be applied once every 2.5 seconds or every 3 hits of the same attack. 

For example, if Xingqiu fires three rain swords rapidly, usually only the first sword applies Hydro. The next two hits will deal damage but won't apply the element again until the 2.5-second timer resets or the 3-hit count is reached. Some characters, like Xiangling (Pyronado) or Collei (Burst), have "No ICD" on certain abilities, meaning every single hit applies an element, making them elite reaction triggers.

Elemental Gauge Theory refers to the "strength" of an elemental application, measured in Units (U). 
*   **1U (Weak):** Lasts about 9.5 seconds as an aura.
*   **2U (Strong):** Lasts about 12 seconds.
*   **4U (Very Strong):** Usually reserved for specific Bursts like Beidou's initial hit or Nahida's skill.
A "Strong" aura can support multiple "Weak" trigger reactions before it is fully consumed.

### How Elemental Mastery and Character Level Affect Damage

For transformative reactions (Swirl, Bloom, Overloaded), your Attack stat does not matter. The damage is determined by:
1.  **Character Level:** The base damage of these reactions scales exponentially as a character moves from Level 80 to Level 90.
2.  **Elemental Mastery (EM):** This stat provides a percentage bonus to [reaction damage](https://ntecodex.com/guides/nte-chaos-vs-psyche-reaction-damage-gear-guide).
3.  **Enemy Resistance:** Reactions are still affected by how much resistance an enemy has to that specific element.

In contrast, Amplifying reactions (Melt/Vaporize) use your character's standard damage (Attack, CRIT, Damage Bonus) and then multiply it by the reaction bonus (2.0x or 1.5x) plus a small bonus from EM.

Beyond the reactions themselves, your party composition significantly influences how often and how effectively you can trigger these effects.

### Top Characters for Consistent Elemental Application

To trigger reactions reliably, you need "Enablers"—characters who can apply elements while they are not the active character on the field.

*   **Hydro:** Xingqiu and Yelan are the gold standard for off-field Hydro application via their Elemental Bursts. Kokomi provides wide AoE Hydro with her jellyfish.
*   **Dendro:** Nahida is the premier Dendro enabler, applying the element every time a reaction is triggered on a marked enemy. Dendro Traveler and Collei are accessible F2P alternatives.
*   **Pyro:** Xiangling is almost unique in her ability to apply high-frequency Pyro off-field with her Pyronado. Bennett is often paired with her to provide an Attack buff and Pyro particles.
*   **Anemo:** Kazuha and Sucrose are used to trigger Swirl, which reduces [enemy resistances](https://ntecodex.com/guides/zzz-3-0-disputed-node-enemy-resistances-gear-guide) if they are wearing the Viridescent Venerer artifact set.

### Common Mistakes in Elemental Rotations

One of the most frequent errors is ignoring the "Aura" strength. If you apply a weak Hydro aura and hit it with a strong Pyro attack, the Hydro will be instantly vaporized and gone. If you reverse this, the Hydro might stay, allowing for a second Pyro hit to also vaporize. 

Another mistake is neglecting Character Level on Bloom or Swirl triggers. Because these reactions scale heavily with level, a Level 70 Kazuha will deal significantly less Swirl damage than a Level 90 Kazuha, even if their Elemental Mastery is the same. Finally, failing to account for ICD can lead to "missing" reactions. If you attack too quickly with an ability that has a long ICD, you will see many hits with no reaction text appearing, resulting in a loss of potential damage.

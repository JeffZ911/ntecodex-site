### Defining Energy Recharge (Charge Efficiency) in NTE

Energy Recharge, officially labeled as Charge Efficiency within the Neverness to Everness interface, is the primary attribute that governs the rate at which an Esper restores their Ultimate Energy. Every time an Esper performs an action that generates energy—such as landing a Basic Attack or successfully executing a Redirect Skill—the base amount of energy produced is passed through the Charge Efficiency multiplier.

This stat is represented as a percentage. By default, every Esper begins with a base Charge Efficiency of 100%. This means they receive exactly the amount of energy the game’s internal logic assigns to a specific action. Increasing this stat through Arcs, Consoles, and specific Esper Talents allows that character to reach their "Ultimate" (typically mapped to the Q key or the top icon on mobile/controller) much faster than their baseline performance would allow.

### The Role of Ultimates in the Combat Loop

Ultimates in NTE are the most powerful tools in an Esper’s kit. They do not merely deal high damage; they often provide essential utility such as massive Break Meter reduction, team-wide buffs, or elemental application necessary for high-tier reactions. Because Ultimates are gated by an energy cost rather than a simple cooldown, the Charge Efficiency stat effectively acts as a "cooldown reduction" mechanic. If you can fill your energy bar in 10 seconds instead of 20, you have effectively doubled the frequency of your most powerful ability.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-energy-recharge-stat-explained/inline-2.webp)


In Neverness to Everness, "Energy Recharge" is officially known as the Charge Efficiency stat. It functions as a percentage-based multiplier applied to all Ultimate Energy gained during combat. While the community often uses the term "Energy Recharge" due to its prevalence in the genre, you must look for "Charge Efficiency" when upgrading your Consoles or selecting Arcs to ensure you are boosting the correct attribute.

The mechanic follows a straightforward linear scaling system. A base Charge Efficiency of 100% means you gain energy at the standard rate. If you increase this stat to 150% through gear and buffs, your Esper will fill their Ultimate (Q) meter 50% faster than they would at base. This is calculated using a simple formula:

**Total Energy Gained = Base Energy Value × Charge Efficiency %**

Energy is not passively generated over time in NTE; it must be earned through active participation in combat. The primary methods for generating energy include:
*   **Basic Attacks:** Small amounts of energy per hit, varying by character.
*   **Redirect Skills:** Significant energy chunks granted upon successful activation.
*   **Successful Parries:** Rewards defensive play with immediate energy influx.
*   **Elemental Reactions:** Specific reactions, most notably the "Charge" reaction, provide massive energy boosts to the active character.

## Detailed Explanation

To master the combat of Neverness to Everness, you must look beyond the surface-level damage numbers and understand the underlying engine that powers your rotations. Charge Efficiency is that engine.

### Understanding the Energy Formula and Scaling

Every action in NTE has a hidden "Base Energy Value." For example, a standard Redirect Skill might have a base value of 15 energy. If your [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) has a Charge Efficiency of 140%, she will actually receive 21 energy (15 × 1.40) upon using that skill. This scaling is vital because Ultimate costs vary significantly between characters. An Esper with a high energy cost (such as 100 units) requires significantly more Charge Efficiency to feel "smooth" compared to a character with a low cost of 40 or 60 units.

### Ultimate Energy vs. the Esper Cycle Meter

One of the most common mistakes for new players in Hethereau is confusing the two primary combat gauges.
1.  **Ultimate Energy Meter:** This is the circular icon located at the top of your skill cluster. It tracks the progress toward your Esper's Ultimate ability. This is the only meter affected by the Charge Efficiency stat.
2.  **Esper Cycle Meter:** This is the horizontal bar (often 100 units) located at the bottom of the UI. This meter governs character swapping and the "Cycle" mechanics. It is affected by the **Cycle Intensity** stat, not Charge Efficiency.

While they are separate, they are linked in practice. Using an Ultimate often generates a surge in the Esper Cycle Meter, allowing for a quick swap to a follow-up character.

### The Power of the 'Charge' Elemental Reaction

While gear is important, the most efficient way to generate energy in the heat of battle is through the "Charge" Trio Reaction. This reaction occurs when you combine the **Blossom** effect (created by Cosmos + Anima) with the **Remora** effect (created by Lakshana + Cosmos).

When the "Charge" reaction is triggered, the active character enters a brief state where every hit landed on the affected enemy restores a flat amount of energy. This effect scales with Charge Efficiency. In multi-target scenarios, a single "Charge" proc can fully refill an Esper’s Ultimate meter in a matter of seconds, making it the premier choice for "Energy Battery" team compositions.

### Character Breakpoints: Who Needs Energy Recharge Most?

Not every character needs to stack Charge Efficiency. Understanding the energy cost tiers is essential for efficient building.

*   **Low Cost (40-60 Energy):** Characters like Chiz often have lower costs. They can usually function perfectly fine with 100-110% Charge Efficiency, relying on basic combat to fill their meters.
*   **Medium Cost (80 Energy):** This is the standard for many sub-DPS characters. They typically require 120-130% Charge Efficiency to ensure their Ultimate is ready every time they swap in.
*   **High Cost (100+ Energy):** Espers like **Mint** and **Nanally** fall into this category. Because their damage is heavily back-loaded into their Ultimates, they require high ER breakpoints, typically between **130% and 160%**. Without this, their rotations feel "clunky," leaving them standing on the field performing weak basic attacks while waiting for their meter to fill.

### How Energy Uptime Affects the Break Phase

The "Break" mechanic is central to defeating bosses in NTE. When an enemy's Break Meter is depleted, they take significantly increased damage and are incapacitated. Ultimates are the primary tool for shredding this meter. If your energy uptime is low, you will find yourself unable to "Break" the enemy during critical windows, such as when they are preparing a massive area-of-effect attack. High Charge Efficiency ensures you always have a "Break tool" ready when the enemy exposes their weakness.

### Building an 'Energy Battery' Team in NTE

An "Energy Battery" is a character specifically included in the team to generate energy for a "Hungry" main DPS. 
*   **Jiuyuan:** Her Redirect Skill generates a higher-than-average amount of base energy particles which can be picked up by the next character in the rotation.
*   **Zero:** With his **A2 (Awakening 2)** unlocked, Zero provides a flat energy restoration to the entire team whenever he triggers a successful Parry.

By pairing a battery like Zero with a high-cost DPS like Nanally (currently featured in "The Ichi-daime" banner), you can lower the Charge Efficiency requirements on Nanally’s gear, allowing you to invest more heavily in Crit Rate and Attack power.

### Stamina Management and Rotations

Finally, it is important to note that energy-heavy rotations often place a strain on your Stamina. Many energy-generating actions, such as Hold Attacks or frequent Dodging to trigger Parries, consume the Stamina bar. If you focus purely on Charge Efficiency but neglect Stamina management, you may find yourself with a full Ultimate meter but no Stamina left to dodge an incoming lethal blow. Balancing your "Energy Economy" with your "Stamina Economy" is the hallmark of a veteran Hethereau explorer.

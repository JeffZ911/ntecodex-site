## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/mira-character-profile-skills-nte/inline-2.webp)


As of the current May 2026 version of Neverness to Everness, there is no playable character named Mira in the official roster. Despite various community rumors and search trends, "Mira" does not appear as an S-Rank or A-Rank Esper in the game’s launch window or the subsequent Version 1.0 Phase 2 update.

The search for a "Mira character profile" in NTE is likely the result of cross-game nomenclature confusion. Mira serves as a primary currency in Mirroria within Hotta Studio's previous title, *Tower of Fantasy*, and is also the name of a progression system (Mira Level) in the recently released *Infinity Nikki*. Because Hotta Studio developed both *Tower of Fantasy* and *Neverness to Everness*, veteran players may be inadvertently mixing terminology between the two titles.

Players searching for Mira are most likely looking for the current top-tier S-Rank characters: **Nanally**, the premier Anima DPS from the launch banner "The Ichi-daime," or **Hotori**, the Cosmos Sub-DPS featured in the Phase 2 banner "Misty Tipsy Style."

### Clarifying the 'Mira' Confusion in NTE

The absence of a character named Mira in Neverness to Everness has not stopped the name from trending in community hubs. This confusion stems from two primary sources:

1.  **Hotta Studio Legacy:** In *Tower of Fantasy*, Mira is the specific currency used in the cyberpunk city of Mirroria. Since NTE features a similar urban-supernatural aesthetic and is developed by the same studio, many players have mistakenly applied the term to the game's characters or currency systems (which actually use terms like Fons and Annulith).
2.  **Cross-Genre Competition:** *Infinity Nikki*, another high-profile title released in a similar timeframe, utilizes "Mira" as a core ranking and progression mechanic. The overlap in player bases between these open-world titles has led to a blending of search terms.

In NTE, the actual currency used for character pulls is **Fabricated Dice** (Standard) and **Solid Dice** (Limited), while the premium currency is known as **Annulith**.

### NTE Character System: Elements and Arcs

To understand why a character like "Mira" would fit into a specific niche, one must understand the Esper Cycle. Characters in Neverness to Everness are categorized by six elemental attributes and five weapon (Arc) types.

**The Six Elements (Esper Types):**
*   **Cosmos:** Associated with silver/light; focuses on time and space manipulation.
*   **Anima:** Associated with green/life; focuses on summons and follow-up attacks.
*   **Lakshana:** Associated with gold/order; focuses on marks and slowing enemies.
*   **Incantation:** Associated with red/force; focuses on raw power and damage amplification.
*   **Chaos:** Associated with purple/dark; focuses on Damage over Time (DoT).
*   **Psyche:** Associated with azure/mind; focuses on debuffs and vulnerability.

**Elemental Reactions (Duo Cycles):**
Combat in NTE relies on swapping characters to trigger reactions between neighboring elements on the Esper Wheel.
*   **Cosmos + Anima = Blossom:** Generates an automated AoE turret.
*   **Anima + Incantation = Hexed:** Deals bonus damage based on 20% of damage taken by the enemy in the last 12 seconds.
*   **Incantation + Chaos = Scorch:** Applies a 15-second Damage over Time effect.
*   **Chaos + Psyche = Nova:** Attaches a bomb to the enemy that detonates after 5 seconds.
*   **Psyche + Lakshana = Stain:** Increases the target's vulnerability to Psyche and Lakshana damage.
*   **Lakshana + Cosmos = Remora:** Marks the target and slows their movement/attack speed.

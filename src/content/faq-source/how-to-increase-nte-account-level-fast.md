Maximizing your Hunter Level is the key to unlocking the full potential of Hethereau's supernatural urban sprawl and reaching the endgame cartridge farming loop. This guide details the most efficient strategies to skyrocket your account level and access higher-tier rewards in Neverness to Everness (NTE).

# How to Increase NTE Account Level Fast: Hunter Leveling Guide

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/how-to-increase-nte-account-level-fast/inline-1.webp)


Players often look for the most efficient ways to bypass the early-game progression gates in Neverness to Everness. The primary questions surrounding account progression include:

*   What is the fastest way to level up an account in Neverness to Everness?
*   How do I unlock higher Appraisal Levels and increase the level caps for my Espers?
*   Which daily activities provide the most Hunter Level XP for the time invested?

Understanding the relationship between Hunter Level and Appraisal Level is vital for ensuring your team remains powerful enough to handle the anomalies found throughout the city.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-increase-nte-account-level-fast/inline-2.webp)


To increase your NTE account level (Hunter Level) fast, prioritize completing Episode missions (Main Story) and Daily Tasks found in the Exploration Guide. These provide the largest single chunks of XP. You should also consistently spend your Character Pixels (stamina) on Anomaly Zone stages, such as Houdini’s Magic Stage, which converts stamina into XP at a fixed ratio.

Engaging with the City Tycoon system is another critical path; reaching Tycoon Level milestones grants massive XP rewards and eventually unlocks the 5-star character Chiz. Finally, you must complete Appraisal Level ascension quests at Hunter Levels 20, 40, and 50. Failure to complete these quests will cap your XP and prevent your Espers from leveling further.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-increase-nte-account-level-fast/inline-3.webp)


The Hunter Level serves as the primary gate for almost all content in Hethereau. It dictates which districts you can enter, which high-tier Arcs (weapons) you can equip, and when you can begin farming Legendary (gold) Consoles and Cartridges. Rushing to Hunter Level 30 is a common goal for competitive players, as this is the threshold where high-efficiency gear farming truly begins.

### Prioritizing Episode and Spinoff Missions

Episode missions are the backbone of your initial leveling experience. They offer the highest XP density and often unlock essential features like the City Tycoon system or specific fast-travel nodes. If you find yourself "level-gated" (where the next story mission requires a higher Hunter Level than you currently possess), turn your attention to Spinoffs. These are character-focused side stories that offer significant XP and help flesh out the lore of characters like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) or Jiuyuan.

### Maximizing Daily Tasks and Participation Tasks

The Exploration Guide (accessible via the F1 key or the in-game phone) is your most important tool for consistent growth. Daily Tasks reset every 24 hours and can provide up to 1,500 Hunter XP. These tasks are usually simple, such as:
*   Upgrading an Arc or Console once.
*   Spending a set amount of Fons (currency).
*   Completing a specific number of Anomaly Zones.
*   Participating in a City Hobby (like racing or delivery).

Participation Tasks within the Exploration Guide act as a permanent checklist. Each small milestone in these phases rewards 100 Hunter XP. Completing an entire phase often grants premium rewards like Annulith or character ascension materials.

### Managing Character Pixels and City Stamina

Neverness to Everness utilizes two separate stamina systems: Character Pixels and City Stamina. For the purpose of increasing your Hunter Level, Character Pixels are the priority.

*   **Character Pixels:** These are consumed when entering Anomaly Zones (dungeons) to farm materials. The standard conversion is 5 XP for every 1 Pixel spent. A standard run typically costs 40 Pixels (yielding 200 XP), while a double-reward run costs 80 Pixels (yielding 400 XP).
*   **Regeneration:** You gain 1 Character Pixel every 6 minutes, totaling 240 Pixels every 24 hours.
*   **Efficiency Tip:** Never let your Pixels sit at the 240 cap. If you are short on time, run "Houdini’s Magic Stage." It is a quick combat encounter that allows you to dump Pixels rapidly for guaranteed Hunter XP and upgrade materials.

### Leveling Up the City Tycoon System

The City Tycoon system is a management simulation where you buy properties, manage cafes, and run businesses in Hethereau. Each time you increase your Tycoon Rank, you receive a payout of 300 to 500 Hunter XP. 

To level up your Tycoon Rank, you must meet two criteria: a specific amount of Fons (total earned) and completion of Tycoon Objectives. Objectives include tasks like unlocking "The Cafe by Origen" at Level 4 or the "Pink Paws Bank Heist" at Level 11. Because the XP from Tycoon Ranks scales up as you progress, it remains a viable leveling method even in the mid-to-late game.

### Exploration XP: Ravens, Phone Booths, and Towers

While exploration provides smaller increments of XP, it is cumulative. 
*   **Signal Towers:** Activating these reveals the map and grants 100 XP.
*   **Phone Booths:** These serve as fast-travel points and healing stations, granting 50 XP each.
*   **Ravens:** These are collectibles hidden throughout the city. Turning them in at the Witch’s House rewards 200 XP per turn-in.
*   **Gifts from "21":** Finding the hidden entities known as "21" provides small XP rewards and Beetle Coins.

### Handling Appraisal Level Ascension Quests

At Hunter Levels 20, 40, and 50, your XP bar will stop progressing until you complete an Ascension Quest. These quests are found in the "Tutorial Quests" section of your mission log. If you continue to play without completing the ascension, your earned XP is heavily penalized (often reduced to 10% of its original value) or capped entirely. Always prioritize these quests the moment they become available to ensure no XP is wasted.

### Hunter Level vs. Appraisal Level

It is a common point of confusion for new players to mistake Hunter Level for the level of their characters. Your Hunter Level is your account-wide progress. Your Appraisal Level is the "World Level" that scales enemy difficulty. 

If you find that the open world has become too difficult after an Appraisal Level increase, the game allows you to manually lower your Appraisal Level by one tier starting at Level 3. This reduces enemy strength but also lowers the quality of the loot dropped. There is a 24-hour cooldown on changing this setting.

### Unlocking the 5-Star Character Chiz through Tycoon

One of the greatest incentives for pushing your account level and Tycoon Rank is the S-Rank character Chiz. 
*   **Unlock:** Chiz joins your roster for free once you reach City Tycoon Level 18.
*   **Utility:** Beyond being a powerful DPS, Chiz has a passive ability that generates Fons when she defeats enemies in the open world. This makes her the premier character for sustaining your Tycoon business expenses.
*   **Maxing:** You can obtain all of her Awakening (A1-A6) copies and her signature weapon by reaching the maximum Tycoon Level of 45.

### Resource Management for New Players

To maintain a fast leveling pace, you must manage your Annulith and De-noise Solutions effectively. 
*   **De-noise Solutions:** These items restore 60 Character Pixels instantly. Save these for days when you have unlocked a new, higher-tier Anomaly Zone to maximize the value of the materials you receive alongside the XP.
*   **Annulith Refreshes:** You can spend 60 Annulith to restore 60 Character Pixels up to 6 times per day. For the first three refreshes, the cost remains 60 Annulith, but it increases to 120 Annulith for the final three. F2P players should generally avoid refreshing more than once or twice a day to save Annulith for limited character banners like "The Ichi-daime."
*   **Early Game Focus:** Before Hunter Level 30, avoid spending Pixels on farming Consoles (artifacts). The drop rates for high-quality gear are poor at low Appraisal Levels. Instead, spend all Pixels on character and Arc ascension materials to ensure you can clear the Ascension Quests easily.

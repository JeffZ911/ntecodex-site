### Why Team Composition Matters in NTE
In NTE, you do not simply switch characters; you utilize the "Lane" system. When you swap characters during an attack animation, the previous character leaves a "Phantom" on the field for a brief duration. This allows multiple elemental applications to occur simultaneously. If your team composition is poorly optimized, your Phantoms will not trigger reactions, leaving your total damage output significantly lower than the game's difficulty curve expects.

### The Role of the Esper Cycle in Starter Teams
The Esper Cycle is the mechanical heart of NTE. Elements are arranged in a specific order where adjacent elements on the wheel generate the most "Cycle Energy." For starter teams, focusing on a "Lane" of at least two characters whose elements are compatible ensures that you can trigger reactions like Blossom or Scorch every few seconds. This constant flow of reactions is necessary for clearing high-density mob groups found in the city’s urban anomalies.

### Understanding the Esper Cycle: Elements and Reactions
The Esper Cycle is more than a simple rock-paper-scissors mechanic. It functions as a sequence. When you trigger a reaction, you gain "Cycle Points." Reaching 100 Cycle Points allows the entire team to enter a "Synchronized State," where swap cooldowns are removed for 8 seconds. 

Starter teams must prioritize elements that build these points quickly. Cosmos and Psyche are considered "Universal Conductors," meaning they can bridge the gap between more volatile elements like Incantation and Chaos. This is why Zero (Cosmos) and Haniel (Psyche) are featured in almost every recommended beginner build.

### Deep Dive: The F2P Powerhouse (Zero, Haniel, Edgar)
This team excels because it covers all three pillars of NTE combat: sustained DPS, damage amplification, and survivability.

**Character Roles:**
*   **Zero (Cosmos):** The Engine. Zero’s basic attack string has high "Phantom Retention," meaning his phantom stays on the field longer than most characters, allowing for easier reaction triggers.
*   **Haniel (Psyche):** The Multiplier. Haniel’s Ultimate, "Ethereal Echo," grants the active character a [exact value pending official confirmation] bonus to their primary element's damage.
*   **Edgar (Cosmos):** The Anchor. Edgar’s healing scales with his Max HP, and his presence provides the "Cosmos Resonance" buff to Zero.

**Step-by-Step Rotation:**
1.  Start with **Zero** and perform his full basic attack combo to build "Cycle Energy."
2.  Swap to **Haniel** mid-combo. Zero’s Phantom will continue the attack.
3.  Use **Haniel’s Skill** to apply the Psyche mark.
4.  Immediately swap back to **Zero**. The interaction between Zero’s Cosmos and Haniel’s Psyche mark triggers "Resonance," granting Zero a shield.
5.  If health is low, swap to **Edgar**, use his burst heal, and swap back to Zero to continue the cycle.

### The Blossom Strategy: Utilizing Zero and Mint
Mint is an A-Rank Anima character often given for free during the "Hethereau Welcome" event. Her synergy with Zero is the most efficient way to handle early-game AoE.

The Blossom reaction creates "Anima Blooms" on the battlefield. Each Bloom fires three shots of Anima damage before disappearing. If you swap between Zero and Mint rapidly, you can saturate the battlefield with these turrets. This strategy is particularly effective for "Defend the Objective" missions where enemies approach from multiple directions.

### Advanced Starter Synergy: Triggering Discord with Adler and Daffodil
Discord is a Trio Reaction, making it harder to execute but significantly more powerful. It requires three different elements to be present on the target within a 3-second window.

Adler (Incantation) and Daffodil (Chaos) are both obtainable through the standard "Urban Recruitment" banner. When paired with a Psyche unit like Fadia or Haniel, they form the "Discord Breaker" team. This team is less about sustained damage and more about "Burst Windows." You apply all three elements, [trigger Discord](https://ntecodex.com/faq) to break the boss, and then dump all your Ultimates while the boss is incapacitated.

Mastering the Anomaly system is the difference between struggling against elite Ethereals and melting them in seconds. This guide breaks down exactly how to trigger Attribute Anomalies, optimize your buildup stats, and execute the devastating Disorder combo.

# How to Trigger Anomaly in Zenless Zone Zero

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/zzz-how-to-trigger-anomaly/inline-1.webp)


Understanding the Anomaly system is vital for any Proxy looking to tackle late-game challenges like Shiyu Defense or the Battle Tower. But what exactly is an Attribute Anomaly in Zenless Zone Zero? Simply put, it is a powerful status effect and damage burst triggered when an enemy is subjected to enough elemental pressure.

To master this system, players need to answer several key questions:
*   How do you initiate the Anomaly buildup process during fast-paced combat?
*   What are the visual indicators that tell you an Anomaly is about to "pop"?
*   Why is the Anomaly system considered the premier damage engine for endgame content compared to traditional Crit-based builds?

Triggering an Anomaly is not just about spamming attacks; it is about managing a hidden meter and timing your character swaps to maximize the resulting damage and debuffs.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/zzz-how-to-trigger-anomaly/inline-2.webp)


Triggering an Anomaly in Zenless Zone Zero requires dealing consistent Attribute damage—Fire, Electric, Ice, Physical, or Ether—to an enemy until their Anomaly Buildup gauge is full. This circular gauge is located to the right of the enemy's health bar. As you land elemental attacks, the gauge fills with a color corresponding to that element (e.g., yellow for Electric, red for Fire).

Once the gauge is completely filled, the corresponding Attribute Anomaly triggers. This results in an immediate burst of damage and applies a unique status effect, such as Burn or Shock, which persists for a set duration.

Key rules for triggering Anomalies include:
*   **Gauge Ownership:** While any character can contribute to the buildup, the character who lands the hit that fills the final 1% of the gauge is considered the "trigger owner." However, the final damage calculation is weighted based on the stats of every character who contributed to that specific gauge.
*   **Role Specialization:** While all Agents can build Anomaly, specialized "Anomaly" class Agents like Grace Howard, [Jane Doe](https://ntecodex.com/guides/yanagi-vs-jane-doe-anomaly-comparison), and Burnice White possess significantly higher Anomaly Mastery, allowing them to fill the gauge much faster than Attack or Stun roles.
*   **Internal Cooldowns:** Most Anomalies have a short internal cooldown (typically 3 seconds) before the same attribute can be triggered again on the same target.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/zzz-how-to-trigger-anomaly/inline-3.webp)


The Anomaly system is a multi-layered mechanic involving buildup, scaling, and elemental interactions. To maximize your DPS, you must understand how these hidden numbers translate into battlefield results.

### Understanding the Anomaly Buildup Gauge

The Anomaly Buildup gauge is a circular icon that appears next to an enemy's health bar once you begin dealing Attribute damage. Every attack in an Agent's kit has a hidden "Anomaly Buildup" value. Generally, EX Special Attacks, Chain Attacks, and Ultimates provide the highest amount of buildup, while Basic Attacks provide a steady stream of pressure.

The gauge does not decay immediately, but it will slowly reset if the enemy is not hit with that specific attribute for a prolonged period. In multi-element teams, you can actually see multiple colors filling the same circle, indicating the progress of different potential Anomalies.

### Attribute Breakdown: From Burn to Corruption

Each of the five attributes in Zenless Zone Zero provides a unique "pop" effect and a follow-up debuff. Choosing the right element for the right enemy is crucial, as enemies have varying resistances.

| Attribute | Anomaly Name | Primary Effect (The "Pop") | Secondary Debuff/DoT |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Physical** | Assault | Massive Physical DMG burst (713% ATK base). | **Flinch:** Increases Daze taken by 7.5% for 10s. |
| **Fire** | Burn | Interrupts organic enemies. | **Burn DoT:** Deals 50% ATK every 0.5s for 10s (1000% total). |
| **Electric** | Shock | Interrupts mechanical enemies. | **Shock DoT:** Deals 125% ATK every 1s for 10s (1250% total). |
| **Ice** | Freeze / Shatter | **Freeze:** Immobilizes the target. | **Shatter:** Deals 500% ATK burst when Freeze ends; **Frostbite:** +10% Crit DMG taken. |
| **Ether** | Corruption | Interrupts Ethereal enemies. | **Corruption DoT:** Deals 62.5% ATK every 0.5s when hit for 10s (1250% total). |

*Note: Special characters like Hoshimi Miyabi (Frost) and Tsukishiro Yanagi (Electric) have unique modifiers that enhance these base effects, such as Miyabi's "Frostburn" which increases buildup from all attributes by 20%.*

### Anomaly Mastery vs. Anomaly Proficiency

Two primary stats govern the effectiveness of your Anomaly triggers. Mixing these up is a common mistake that can ruin an Agent's performance.

1.  **Anomaly Mastery:** This stat determines the **speed** of buildup. 1 point of Anomaly Mastery is equivalent to a 1% increase in buildup rate. If an enemy requires 100 units of buildup to trigger an Anomaly, an Agent with 150 Mastery only needs to deal 66.6 units of raw attribute pressure to fill the gauge. This stat is usually found on Disk Drive slot 6.
2.  **Anomaly Proficiency:** This stat determines the **damage** of the trigger. 1 point of Anomaly Proficiency increases the final Anomaly damage by 1%. For DoT effects like Burn or Shock, this scales every tick of damage. This stat is usually found on Disk Drive slot 4.

**The Scaling Formula:**
Anomaly damage scales based on the following factors:
*   **Base ATK:** The combined attack stat of the contributing Agents.
*   **Agent Level:** Level provides a massive hidden multiplier (Level Coefficient). A Level 60 Agent deals significantly more Anomaly damage than a Level 50 Agent, even with identical gear.
*   **Anomaly Proficiency:** The primary damage multiplier.
*   **Enemy Resistance/Defense:** Higher-level enemies mitigate more damage unless you have PEN (Penetration) stats.

### Mastering the Disorder Combo

Disorder is the "reaction" mechanic of Zenless Zone Zero. It occurs when you trigger a second, different Attribute Anomaly while a previous one is still active on the enemy.

When Disorder triggers:
1.  The remaining damage from the first Anomaly (the DoT ticks of Burn, Shock, or Corruption) is instantly front-loaded into a single burst.
2.  An additional "Disorder Bonus" damage instance is applied.
3.  A significant amount of Daze is dealt to the enemy, often leading to an immediate Stun.

For example, if you apply **Burn** (Fire) and then immediately trigger **Assault** (Physical), the remaining 8 seconds of Burn damage are "consumed" and dealt instantly alongside the Assault burst. This is why "Disorder Teams" (using two different Anomaly elements) are currently the meta for high-difficulty bosses.

### Snapshotting and Trigger Ownership

A common point of confusion is which character's stats are used for the Anomaly damage. Zenless Zone Zero uses a **weighted contribution system**.

If Grace (Electric) deals 90% of the buildup and Rina (Electric) deals the final 10%, the resulting Shock damage will be calculated using 90% of Grace's stats (ATK, Proficiency, Level) and 10% of Rina's. This prevents players from "cheating" the system by building up the gauge with a weak character and only swapping to a high-stat character for the final hit.

However, certain buffs only apply if the character is on-field during the "pop." Advanced players use **swap-canceling** (switching characters during a long animation) to ensure their main Anomaly DPS is the one active when the gauge hits 100%, allowing them to benefit from on-field buffs like those from the *Chaos Jazz* or *Freedom Blues* Drive Discs.

### Enemy Resistances

Every time you trigger a specific Anomaly on an enemy, they gain a permanent 2% resistance to that specific attribute's buildup. This effect caps at 20% (after 10 triggers). This means that in very long fights, it becomes progressively harder to "spam" the same Anomaly. This mechanic further encourages the use of Disorder teams, as you can alternate between two different elements to delay the impact of this resistance scaling.

## Related

The Anomaly system does not exist in a vacuum; it interacts heavily with the Stun mechanic and your gear choices.

### How Anomaly Interacts with Stun

While Anomaly and Stun (Daze) are separate systems, they are highly synergistic.
*   **Increased Damage:** Stunned enemies take increased damage from all sources, including Anomaly pops and DoT ticks.
*   **Daze Buildup:** Triggering an Assault (Physical) Anomaly applies the Flinch debuff, which makes the enemy 7.5% easier to Stun.
*   **Freeze-Stun Locking:** The Ice Anomaly (Freeze) actually pauses the enemy's Stun decay meter. If you Freeze an enemy right as they are about to recover from a Stun, you can extend your damage window significantly.

### Optimizing Gear for Anomaly Agents

To trigger Anomalies effectively, you must equip the correct Drive Discs. As of May 2026, the following sets are the gold standard for Anomaly builds:

*   **Freedom Blues (4-Piece):** Reduces the enemy's Anomaly Buildup RES to the equipper's attribute by 20% for 8 seconds after an EX Special Attack hits. This is essential for fast triggers.
*   **Chaos Jazz (4-Piece):** Provides a 15% Fire/Electric DMG bonus and increases off-field EX Special/Assist DMG by 20%. This is the premier set for off-field Anomaly triggers like Burnice or Yanagi.
*   **Rainforest Gourmet (4-Piece):** Increases the equipper's ATK for every 10 Energy consumed, which is excellent for Anomaly Agents who rely on spamming EX Special Attacks.

For main stats, always prioritize **Anomaly Proficiency** on Slot 4 and **Anomaly Mastery** on Slot 6. Slot 5 should typically be an **Attribute DMG %** cup or **PEN Ratio** if you are running a high-penetration team with Rina.

### Top Tier Disorder Team Comps

The meta has shifted toward dual-Anomaly compositions that prioritize constant Disorder procs.

1.  **The "Fire-Electric" Burn/Shock Team:** Consists of **Burnice White** (Off-field Fire) and **Tsukishiro Yanagi** (On-field Electric). Burnice applies Scorched/Burn from the background, while Yanagi triggers Shock and her unique "Polarity Disorder," which deals massive damage without removing the base Anomaly.
2.  **The "Frost-Electric" Team:** Uses **Hoshimi Miyabi** (Frost) and **Yanagi**. Miyabi's Frostburn increases the buildup speed for Yanagi's Electric attacks, leading to rapid-fire Disorder triggers.
3.  **The "Physical Hyper-Carry":** Features **Jane Doe** paired with a support like **Caesar King**. Jane Doe is unique because her Physical Anomaly (Assault) can actually Crit based on her Anomaly Proficiency, allowing her to bridge the gap between Anomaly and traditional Attack roles.
4.  **The "Ether-Delusion" Team:** Features **Aria** and **Nangong**. This team focuses on the Corruption Anomaly and utilizes Aria's "Fandom Power" to sustain high-uptime Ether pressure.

As the game moves into the 2.8 update and beyond, new characters like **Roscaelifer** are expected to introduce even more complex Anomaly interactions, potentially adding a sixth attribute or new "Rupture" mechanics. For now, focusing on Mastery for trigger frequency and Proficiency for trigger power remains the most effective strategy for any Proxy.

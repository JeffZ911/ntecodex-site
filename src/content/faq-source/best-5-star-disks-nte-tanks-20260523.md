What are the best 5-star (S-Rank) Disks for tank characters in Neverness to Everness (NTE)? To answer this, we must first look at the dual-gear system that defines character builds in Hethereau. In NTE, "Disks" is the collective term for two distinct but interconnected components: Cartridges and Modules. While Cartridges provide the primary set bonuses (2-piece and 4-piece effects), Modules function as the "Tetris-style" grid pieces that fill the Disk's internal board, providing massive raw stat boosts.

The current 1.0 meta features two primary tank and sustain characters: Fadia and Adler. Fadia is a high-HP damage-sharer who absorbs hits for the team, while Adler focuses on DEF-scaling shields and crowd control. Choosing the right S-Rank gear is vital because 5-star Disks offer significantly higher base stat ceilings and more complex set bonuses compared to their B-Rank and A-Rank counterparts.

### Understanding NTE's Gear System: Cartridges vs. Modules

Cartridges are the circular components of the Disk system. You can equip up to four, and they dictate the "Set Bonus." For tanks, these bonuses often revolve around HP percentages, Shield Strength, or team-wide damage mitigation. Modules, on the other hand, are the geometric blocks (ranging from Type 1 to Type 4 shapes) that you fit into the Module Grid. S-Rank Modules are particularly valuable because they possess more "affixes" (substats) and higher main stat values, allowing you to fine-tune a tank’s survivability.

### Who are the Primary Tanks in NTE?

Currently, Fadia and Adler dominate the defensive meta. Fadia is often considered the "Immortal" tank due to her "All Eyes on Me" passive, which links her HP pool to her teammates, effectively taking a portion of the damage they receive. Adler is the premier "Shielder," utilizing his DEF stat to create barriers that negate incoming damage entirely. Understanding which tank you are building is the first step in selecting your S-Rank Disks, as a Fadia build focused on DEF is far less effective than one focused on pure HP.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/best-5-star-disks-nte-tanks-20260523/inline-3.webp)


The power of 5-star Disks in Neverness to Everness lies in how their unique effects interact with character passives. Let's look closer at the mechanics that make these sets the "best-in-slot" options for the current 1.0 roster.

### Fadia Build: The Crimson Twin Butterflies Meta

Fadia’s kit revolves around her ability to absorb damage for her allies. The Crimson Twin Butterflies set is designed to facilitate this. The 2-piece bonus gives a straightforward 15% Max HP boost. However, the 4-piece bonus is where the real power lies: every time the wearer takes damage (including damage shared via passives), they gain a "Butterfly Stack." Each stack increases Max HP by 4%, and this can stack up to 10 times.

At maximum stacks, Fadia gains a total of 55% Max HP from the set alone (15% base + 40% from stacks). Because Fadia’s damage-sharing efficiency scales directly with her total HP, this set allows her to protect her team through even the most aggressive boss phases in the Rabbit Hole. Without this S-Rank set, Fadia often struggles to survive the "shared damage" from high-damage AoE attacks.

### Adler Build: Maximizing Shield Strength and DEF

Adler functions differently. He is a proactive tank rather than a reactive one. His shields scale off his total DEF, meaning every point of DEF you gain via S-Rank Modules directly increases the "health" of the shield he provides to the team. The S-Rank Disk set providing +20% Shield Effect is essential because this multiplier is applied *after* the DEF calculation.

In practice, an Adler equipped with 5-star DEF-mainstat Cartridges and the Shield Effect set can create barriers that absorb upwards of 12,000 damage. In the current 1.0 meta, this is enough to completely negate the "Urban Collapse" mechanic used by several world bosses.

### Optimizing the Module Grid for Survivability

The Module Grid is the "Tetris" board located in the center of your Disk menu. To maximize a tank's stats, you must focus on Type 4 modules. These are the largest pieces and offer the highest density of defensive stats. For an S-Rank Module grid, you want to aim for a layout that prioritizes "HP%" blocks for Fadia and "DEF%" blocks for Adler.

A common mistake is filling the grid with smaller Type 1 blocks to save space. However, S-Rank Type 4 blocks have a higher chance of rolling "Gold" tier affixes. For a tank, an optimized grid should have at least three Type 4 blocks focused on their primary scaling stat, with the remaining gaps filled by Type 2 "Energy Recharge" blocks to ensure Ultimate uptime.

### Disk Set Synergies with Defensive Arcs

Disks do not exist in a vacuum; they must be paired with the correct Arcs (weapons). For Fadia, pairing the Crimson Twin Butterflies set with the "Umbrella" Arc is a powerful combination. The Umbrella Arc provides additional damage reduction when the wielder's HP is above 70%, which is easily maintained thanks to the HP stacking from her Disks.

For Adler, the "Good Boy's Grand Adventure" Arc is the premier choice. This Arc increases the wearer's DEF by a percentage whenever they generate a shield. When combined with the S-Rank Shield Effect Disk set, Adler becomes an immovable wall, providing shields that refresh faster than the enemies can break them.

Tanks in NTE are rarely just "meat shields"; they provide specific elemental and mechanical synergies. Fadia is a staple in "Immortal Discord" teams. This composition usually features Fadia, a high-damage Anomaly character, and a healer. Fadia’s role is to ensure the squishy DPS characters don't get one-shot while they build up Discord stacks on the enemy.

Adler, conversely, excels in "Mono Scorch" setups. His shields provide the stability needed for Scorch-based DPS characters to stay in close range and apply burn effects. Because Adler’s shields also provide a small amount of Interruption Resistance, he allows your DPS to ignore minor enemy attacks and complete their full combo strings.

### Where to Farm S-Rank Disks

S-Rank Disks do not drop early in the game. You must reach Hunter Level 30 before the "Rabbit Hole" domains begin dropping Golden (S-Rank) Cartridges. Specifically, the "Neon Graveyard" Rabbit Hole is the primary farming location for the Crimson Twin Butterflies set.

For the DEF and Shield-focused sets, you will need to clear the "Industrial District: Sector 7" domain. These challenges are difficult and often require a well-built 4-star (A-Rank) set before you can successfully farm the 5-star versions. It is recommended to use your "Resonance Energy" (stamina) on these domains during the weekend when drop rates for S-Rank Modules are slightly increased.

### Budget 4-Star Alternatives

If you haven't reached Hunter Level 30 yet, don't ignore your A-Rank Disks. The "Steel Shell" A-Rank set is a fantastic placeholder for Adler, providing a 12% DEF boost that can carry you through the mid-game. For Fadia, the "Vitality Spring" A-Rank set offers a 10% HP boost and a small amount of healing when she takes damage, serving as a "lite" version of the Crimson Twin Butterflies set until you can farm the S-Rank version.

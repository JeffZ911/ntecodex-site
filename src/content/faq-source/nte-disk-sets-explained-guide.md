Mastering the gear system in Neverness to Everness (NTE) is the difference between struggling against urban anomalies and effortlessly clearing the most difficult Anomaly Zones in Hethereau. Unlike traditional gacha equipment systems, NTE utilizes a unique "Console" interface that blends stat optimization with a Tetris-like spatial puzzle.

# Neverness to Everness (NTE) Disk Sets Explained: The Ultimate Gear Guide

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/nte-disk-sets-explained-guide/inline-1.webp)


Understanding the equipment system in Neverness to Everness requires looking at the Console as a two-part machine: the Cartridge and the Modules. Players often ask how these components interact to form what the community calls "Disk Sets." 

Key questions this guide covers include:
* **What are Disk Sets (Cartridges and Modules) in Neverness to Everness?**
* **How do you activate 2-piece and 4-piece set bonuses?**
* **Where is the best place to farm high-tier gear in Hethereau?**
* **How does the 'Tetris' Console system impact character stats?**

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-disk-sets-explained-guide/inline-2.webp)


In Neverness to Everness, "Disk Sets" refers to the Console system, which consists of a base Cartridge and several fitted Modules. Every Esper has a unique Console grid where you must arrange these components to unlock their full potential.

* **Cartridges** act as the "brain" of your gear. They provide the primary set bonuses—an Epic 2-piece bonus and a Legendary 4-piece bonus—and carry random main stats such as Elemental DMG%, ATK%, or CRIT Rate.
* **Modules** are "Tetris-like" blocks that provide flat ATK and HP as their primary stats. To unlock the Cartridge’s set effects, you must slot specific Module types (Type II, III, or IV) into the Console grid.
* **Set Activation** is not just about quantity; it requires matching the specific shapes and types required by the Cartridge while fitting them into the character's unique grid layout.
* **Farming** is primarily done in Anomaly Zones, specifically the **Rabbit Hole** located in Cape Square. This costs **Character Pixels** (stamina). S-Rank (Orange) gear becomes available for farming once you reach Hunter Level 30.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-disk-sets-explained-guide/inline-3.webp)


The Console system is designed to remove the "hidden RNG" found in many other urban RPGs. In NTE, you can see every sub-stat on a piece of gear the moment it drops, allowing you to decide immediately if it is worth the resources to upgrade.

### Understanding Cartridges vs. Modules

To build an effective Esper, you must distinguish between the two components of the Console. 

**Cartridges** are the primary source of percentage-based scaling. They come in three rarities: B-Rank (Blue), A-Rank (Purple), and S-Rank (Orange). A Cartridge has one main stat and four sub-stats. When you level a Cartridge to its cap of +20, only the main stat increases. The sub-stats are "unlocked" or activated every five levels (+5, +10, +15, and +20), but their numerical values remain fixed from the moment the item is acquired.

**Modules** are the physical pieces you fit into the grid. Every Module has two fixed main stats: Flat ATK and Flat HP. While their main stats are consistent, their sub-stats are randomized. Modules are categorized by their "Type," which refers to how many grid cells they occupy:
* **Type II:** 2-cell blocks (often horizontal or vertical bars).
* **Type III:** 3-cell blocks (L-shapes or small corners).
* **Type IV:** 4-cell blocks (large T-shapes, squares, or long bars).

### How to Solve the Console Module Puzzle

Activating a 4-piece set bonus isn't as simple as clicking "equip." You must manually (or via auto-equip) fit the required Modules into the character’s Console grid. 

Each Cartridge has a "Requirement" list. For example, the **Lost Radiance** set requires one Type II (Horizontal), two Type III (L-shapes), and one Type IV (Vertical) to activate its Legendary 4-piece effect. If you use a Type II (Vertical) instead of a Horizontal one, the piece will still provide its individual stats, but the set bonus will remain inactive.

Furthermore, every Esper has a unique grid layout. A layout for a character like **Zero** might be wide and rectangular, making horizontal Modules easy to place, while **[Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)** might have a more fragmented grid that rewards more compact Type III pieces. Empty spaces in the grid should always be filled with "filler" Modules to maximize flat ATK and HP, even if they don't contribute to a set bonus.

### Main Stats and Sub-stats Priority

Because sub-stats do not increase when leveling, your "perfect" gear hunt in NTE is focused on finding a Cartridge or Module that drops with high initial rolls. 

**Recommended Main Stats for Cartridges:**
* **DPS:** CRIT Rate, CRIT DMG, or Elemental DMG% (matching the Esper's element).
* **Support/Healer:** HP% or Charge Efficiency.
* **Tank:** DEF% or HP%.

**Sub-stat Priority:**
In NTE, the main stat and sub-stats can overlap. This means a Cartridge can have ATK% as a main stat and ATK% as a sub-stat, allowing for extreme specialization. For most damage dealers, the priority is **CRIT Rate > CRIT DMG > ATK% > Universal DMG%**.

### Farming Guide: Rabbit Hole and Anomaly Zones

To acquire Cartridges and the currency needed for Modules, you must spend **Character Pixels** in Anomaly Zones. The most important zone for gear is the **Rabbit Hole**, located on the skyscraper rooftops of Cape Square in the New Herland district.

| Stage | Hunter Level Req. | Primary Rewards |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stage 1** | Level 10 | B-Rank (Blue) Cartridges, Bronze Carrota Coins |
| **Stage 2** | Level 20 | A-Rank (Purple) Cartridges, Silver Carrota Coins |
| **Stage 3** | Level 30 | S-Rank (Orange) Cartridges, Gold Carrota Coins |
| **Stage 4** | Level 35 | Increased S-Rank Drop Rate, Gold Carrota Coins |
| **Stage 5** | Level 40+ | Guaranteed S-Rank Drops, High-tier Upgrade Mats |

**The Rewind System:**
Modules are not dropped directly in the Rabbit Hole. Instead, you earn **Carrota Coins** (Bronze, Silver, and Gold) from the dungeon clears. You then go to the Console menu and use the **Rewind** feature. This is a targeted "gacha" for Modules. You can spend Gold Carrota Coins on "Hard Rewind" to pull specifically for S-Rank Modules of certain shapes or types.

### Best Disk Sets for DPS, Support, and Tanks

Choosing the right set depends on the "Esper Cycle" (the elemental reaction system). 

* **Best for Cosmos DPS (Zero, Chiz):** **Lost Radiance**. The DEF ignore is unparalleled for high-end bossing.
* **Best for Psyche DPS (Aurelia):** **Devil's Blood: Curse**. This set is specifically designed to reward teams that [trigger Nova](https://ntecodex.com/faq) or Stain reactions, doubling the damage bonus.
* **Best for Supports ([Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide), Nanally):** **Speedy Hedgehog**. Since supports usually cycle their Ultimates to buff the team, the 15% team-wide ATK buff is essential.
* **Best for Tanks (Fadia):** **Tiny Big Adventure**. This set rewards characters who stay on the field and absorb hits, providing a massive Max HP boost that often scales with a tank's utility.

### Synergizing Disks with the Esper Cycle

Set bonuses like **Devil's Blood: Curse** and **Street Boxer** specifically mention "Nova," "Stain," and "Remora." These are elemental reactions triggered by swapping between Espers of different elements. For example, combining Cosmos and Anima triggers a **Blossom Effect**, while Cosmos and Lakshana triggers **Remora**. 

When building your Console, always look at your team's reaction potential. If your team cannot trigger Stain, the **Devil's Blood** set loses half of its effectiveness. In these cases, a generalist set like **Shadow Creed** (ATK +25% after skill) may actually provide higher consistent DPS.

### Top 5 Beginner Mistakes in Gear Farming

1.  **Ignoring Module Shapes:** Many players equip an S-Rank Module because it has "good stats," but if it doesn't match the Cartridge's shape requirement, you lose the 4-piece set bonus, which is usually worth more than any single sub-stat.
2.  **Farming S-Rank Gear Too Early:** Do not spend all your Character Pixels on gear before Hunter Level 30. The jump from A-Rank to S-Rank is significant; use your early stamina on character and Arc ascension materials instead.
3.  **Leveling Sub-par Modules:** Since sub-stats never increase, a level 1 Module with bad stats will be a level 20 Module with bad stats. Only invest "Fons" (currency) and upgrade materials into pieces that have at least two desirable sub-stats.
4.  **Forgetting the "No-Overlap" Rule for Buffs:** Sets like **Speedy Hedgehog** and **Lost Radiance** state that their 4-piece effects "do not stack." Running two characters with Speedy Hedgehog on the same team is a waste of a set bonus.
5.  **Neglecting the City Tycoon System:** High-level Module upgrades require a significant amount of Fons. If you aren't managing your properties and businesses in Hethereau, you will quickly run out of the funds needed to hit +20 on your gear.

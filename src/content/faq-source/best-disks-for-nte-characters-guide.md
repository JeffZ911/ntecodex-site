# Best Disks for NTE Characters: The Ultimate Cartridge & Module Guide

*   Which gear sets are considered meta for DPS, Support, and Shielder roles in Version 1.0?

### Defining Disks, Cartridges, and Modules in NTE

In Neverness to Everness, "Disks" is the common community term for **Cartridges**. These are the primary gear items that define your character's set bonuses. Each character has a "Console" grid where you equip one Cartridge and then fill the surrounding slots with **Modules**.

Modules are the "Tetris blocks" of NTE. They provide the raw stats—such as Flat ATK, HP, and various sub-stats—while the Cartridge dictates the set effects. Activating these effects is not as simple as clicking "equip"; you must physically fit enough Modules of the correct type into the Console grid to trigger the 2-piece (Epic) and 4-piece (Legendary) bonuses.

The "Best Disk" for any given character depends heavily on their element and combat role. In the current Version 1.0 meta, three Cartridge sets stand above the rest: **Fireflies and the Forest** for Anima-based damage dealers, **Lost Radiance** for Cosmos-elemental DPS, and **Speedy Hedgehog** for dedicated Supports.

*   **For Anima DPS ([Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte), Mint, Jiuyuan):** "Fireflies and the Forest" is the undisputed best-in-slot. It provides a massive 56% Crit DMG boost at maximum stacks, which is easily maintained by Anima characters' high hit-rate abilities.
*   **For Supports ([Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide), Haniel):** "Speedy Hedgehog" is mandatory for high-tier rotations. It grants a 15% ATK buff to the entire team for 20 seconds after the wearer uses their Ultimate.
*   **For Cosmos DPS (Chiz, Zero, [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte)):** "Lost Radiance" is the preferred choice. Its 4-piece bonus allows the wearer to ignore 25% of an enemy's DEF for 20 seconds after casting an Ultimate, which is essential for bypassing the high resistances found in endgame Hethereau Anomaly challenges.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/best-disks-for-nte-characters-guide/inline-3.webp)


The complexity of the NTE gear system lies in the interaction between the Cartridge's requirements and the character's Console layout. Unlike other gacha games where you simply equip a set, NTE requires you to solve a spatial puzzle to maximize your stats.

### Nanally & Mint: The Anima Crit Meta

Anima characters like Nanally and Mint thrive on multi-hit combos. Because "Fireflies and the Forest" gains a stack of Crit DMG every time a nearby enemy takes Anima damage, these characters can reach the 7-stack maximum (56% Crit DMG) almost instantly.

For **Nanally**, focus on Modules that provide Crit Rate and Anima DMG. Her Console specialization rewards the use of **Type II Modules**, granting her 5% Crit Rate for every Type II piece equipped. 

**Mint**, on the other hand, has a specialization for **Type III Modules**, gaining 8% Crit Rate for each one. To optimize Mint, you should prioritize fitting as many Type III blocks as possible while still completing the Fireflies and the Forest set requirements.

### Sakiri & Haniel: Maximizing Support Utility

Supports in NTE are defined by their ability to generate "Cycle Energy" and provide team-wide buffs. **Speedy Hedgehog** is the gold standard here because of its 12% Charge Efficiency (2-piece) and 15% team ATK buff (4-piece).

**Sakiri** is particularly potent with this set because her Ultimate, *Feast of Gluttony*, already increases team ATK based on her own Base ATK. Combining her natural kit with the Speedy Hedgehog bonus makes her an indispensable asset for any burst-damage window. For Sakiri, prioritize **Cycle Rate** and **Break Intensity** sub-stats to ensure she can cast her Ultimate as frequently as possible.

### Chiz & Zero: The Cosmos DEF-Ignore Build

The Cosmos element currently lacks a stacking Crit DMG set like Anima, but **Lost Radiance** makes up for it with raw penetration. The 25% DEF-ignore effect is a multiplicative damage increase, meaning it becomes more valuable the higher the enemy's level.

**Chiz** benefits from this significantly because her gameplay revolves around her **Redirect Skill** and the "Grain Market" state. By casting her Ultimate to trigger the Lost Radiance bonus, her subsequent Redirect Skill attacks (which scale with Grain) bypass a quarter of the enemy's armor. Both Chiz and **Zero** have a Type III Module specialization, granting them 10% ATK for every Type III piece equipped.

### Adler: Shield Scaling and Kingdom's Guard

**Adler** is the premier defensive support in Version 1.0. His effectiveness scales directly with his DEF stat, making **Kingdom's Guard** the only viable choice for a pure tank build. The 2-piece bonus provides a flat 15% DEF increase, while the 4-piece bonus boosts all Shield effects by 20%.

Adler's Console specialization is one of the strongest in the game: he gains 12% DEF for every **Type III Module** equipped. A fully optimized Adler build will utilize a Kingdom's Guard Cartridge and four Type III Modules, resulting in a character that is nearly unkillable and provides massive shields to the rest of the team.

### Optimizing the Console: The Tetris Module Guide

Every character in NTE has a unique grid shape. Some are long and narrow, while others are square. This means a Module that fits perfectly in Nanally's Console might be impossible to use in Sakiri's.

*   **Type II Modules:** Occupy 2 cells. These are the most flexible and are often used to "fill the gaps" in a build.
*   **Type III Modules:** Occupy 3 cells (usually L-shaped or I-shaped). These are the most common requirement for character specializations.
*   **Type IV Modules:** Occupy 4 cells (Tetris "T" or "Z" shapes). These provide the highest base stats but are the hardest to fit.

The key strategy is to prioritize your character's **Specialization Modules** first. If Mint needs Type III pieces for Crit Rate, place those in the grid first. Then, look at your Cartridge's 4-piece requirement. Most S-Rank Cartridges require a specific combination, such as one Type II, two Type III, and one Type IV Module.

### Farming Disks: Character Pixels and Rewind Strategy

Farming for the perfect gear setup involves two distinct loops

1.  **The Rabbit Hole:** Located in Cape Square, this Anomaly Zone is where you spend **Character Pixels** (40 per run) to obtain Cartridges. Higher difficulty stages reward Purple (A-Rank) and Gold (S-Rank) Cartridges.
2.  **The Rewind System:** Accessed through the character's Console menu, this is where you spend **Carrota Coins** to pull for Modules.

To optimize your farming, do not waste Carrota Coins on "Easy" or "Normal" Rewind modes once you reach Hunter Level 30. Save your **Gold Carrota Coins** for "Hard" mode to guarantee S-Rank Modules. Use the **Module Selection** feature during 10-pulls to target specific shapes (like Type III) that your character specialization requires.

If you receive a Module with great sub-stats but the wrong shape, or vice versa, you can use **Carrota Coins** to "Rewind" or reroll specific aspects of the piece. However, use this mechanic sparingly, as Carrota Coins are a finite resource in the early game.

### How Disks Impact Elemental Reactions

The NTE combat system is built on **Esper Cycles**—reactions triggered by combining different elements. Your choice of Cartridge can significantly impact the damage of these reactions

*   **Blossom (Cosmos + Anima):** Since this creates a turret that deals steady damage, using **Fireflies and the Forest** on your Anima character will allow the turret's hits to rapidly stack your Crit DMG bonus.
*   **Hexed (Anima + Incantation):** This reaction deals follow-up damage. An Incantation character using **Crimson: Twin Butterflies** will see a massive ATK boost as the Hexed procs count toward the set's stacking requirement.
*   **Nova (Chaos + Psyche):** This is a high-burst reaction. Using **Diabolos** on your Chaos DPS (like Lacrimosa) is essential here, as the set's RES-ignore bonus doubles after a Nova reaction is triggered.

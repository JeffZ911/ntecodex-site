### Understanding the Three Stamina Systems in NTE

Neverness to Everness utilizes a tri-part stamina system, each color-coded to help players distinguish between different gameplay loops. 

1.  **Exploration Stamina (Yellow):** This bar appears when you sprint, swim, or climb. It dictates how long you can move at high speeds or scale the skyscrapers of Hesperia before falling or slowing down.
2.  **Character Pixels (Blue):** This is the traditional "gacha energy" or "resin" equivalent. It is consumed when claiming rewards from Anomaly containment zones, boss encounters, and material domains. 
3.  **City Stamina (Purple):** Exclusive to the City Tycoon system, this resource is used to manage your businesses, run storefronts, and participate in urban management events.

### Upgrading Exploration Stamina at the Witch's House

The Witch's House serves as the central hub for all movement-related upgrades. Located in a secluded corner southwest of the bustling Hankaku Street district, this location becomes accessible early in the main story. 

To increase your yellow stamina bar, you must collect Oracle Stones. These stones are not purchased; they are earned by locating and capturing "Birds"—ethereal anomalies that manifest as glowing avian figures throughout Hesperia. These birds often require short platforming challenges or puzzles to reach.

A major progression milestone occurs at **Witch's House Level 3**. Upon reaching this level, the game unlocks a radar function that reveals the general locations of nearby Birds on your mini-map. This significantly accelerates the process of gathering Oracle Stones for higher-level stamina upgrades. Each level of the Witch's House provides a fixed increase to your total yellow stamina, allowing for longer climbs up the city's massive vertical architecture without needing to rest on ledges.

### Increasing Character Pixel Caps via Property and Furniture

Character Pixels (Blue Stamina) regenerate at a standard rate of 1 every 6 minutes, totaling 240 Pixels per 24-hour period. While the base cap is sufficient for casual play, endgame farming requires a larger "bank" to avoid over-capping while offline.

The primary way to increase this cap is through the **Property System**. By visiting Hesperia Realty, you can purchase the **Eden Apartment**. This property acts as more than just a social space; it provides a baseline increase of +20 to your Character Pixel cap.

Once you own a property, you can install **Anomaly Furniture**. These are specialized items that provide functional buffs to your account. 
*   **Little Cloud:** A furniture item that adds +10 to your Character Pixel storage.
*   **Dream Weaver's Knot:** A high-end anomaly item that, when upgraded to Level 5, provides a substantial +30 increase to the cap.

By combining the Eden Apartment with a fully optimized set of furniture, players can push their Character Pixel cap to 280 or higher, providing more flexibility for when they choose to spend their energy.

### Scaling City Stamina through Tycoon Ranks

City Stamina (Purple) functions differently than the other two types. It operates on a weekly reset rather than a rapid timer. This stamina is the lifeblood of the "City Tycoon" business simulation mode, where you manage shops and generate passive income.

Your maximum City Stamina is tied directly to your **Tycoon Rank**. As you complete business orders and upgrade your storefronts, you gain Tycoon XP.

### Stamina Recovery Items and Annulith Refills

If you run out of Character Pixels (Blue) and wish to continue farming, you have two primary options: consumables and premium refills.

1.  **De-noise Solution:** This is the standard recovery consumable in NTE. Using one De-noise Solution instantly restores 60 Character Pixels. These are typically earned through battle pass rewards, event shops, or as compensation for maintenance.
2.  **Annulith Refills:** You can spend Annuliths (the premium currency) to refill 60 Character Pixels. Note that the cost in Annuliths increases with each subsequent refill within the same day. Most players recommend a maximum of 1-2 refills per day to maintain currency efficiency.
3.  **City Stamina Refills:** Unlike Character Pixels, City Stamina is much harder to refill once the weekly allotment is spent. Players must wait for the weekly reset or use rare "Tycoon Permits" which are occasionally distributed during business-themed events.

Not all characters interact with the stamina system in the same way. Choosing the right character for exploration can make your existing stamina bar feel much larger.

Her banner, "The Ichi-daime," is a high priority for players focused on urban exploration.

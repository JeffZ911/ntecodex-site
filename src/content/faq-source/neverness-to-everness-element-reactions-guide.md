## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/neverness-to-everness-element-reactions-guide/inline-2.webp)


Elemental reactions in Neverness to Everness are referred to as Esper Cycles. These reactions are not triggered by simply hitting an enemy with different elements, but by swapping between characters once their individual Esper Meters are full. There are six primary elements, or Esper Types: Lakshana, Cosmos, Anima, Incantation, Chaos, and Psyche.

The fundamental rule of the Esper Cycle is adjacency. Reactions only occur between elements that are positioned next to each other on the hexagonal Esper Wheel. These reactions are categorized into two tiers:

*   **Duo Reactions:** Triggered by swapping between two adjacent elements. These provide tactical status effects, such as Remora, which significantly slows enemy movement, or Blossom, which summons Lebenstamm (AoE Turrets) to the battlefield.
*   **Trio Reactions:** Triggered by completing a sequence of three elements within the same Trio group (Top or Bottom). Reactions like Charge provide massive energy restoration, while Discord deals immense [Break damage](https://ntecodex.com/guides/hsr-3-0-super-break-damage-calculation-gear-guide) to deplete enemy shields.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/neverness-to-everness-element-reactions-guide/inline-3.webp)


The combat system in Neverness to Everness revolves around the "Esper Wheel," a hexagonal interface that dictates which elements can react with one another. Unlike other open-world RPGs where any two elements might react, NTE requires a specific sequence based on the wheel's layout.

### The Six Esper Types and the Esper Wheel

The Esper Wheel is divided into two distinct groups: the Top Trio and the Bottom Trio. Understanding this division is essential for triggering the high-impact Trio reactions.

*   **Top Trio:** Lakshana, Cosmos, and Anima. These elements often focus on battlefield manipulation, defensive utility, and sustained area-of-effect damage.
*   **Bottom Trio:** Incantation, Chaos, and Psyche. These elements generally lean toward offensive pressure, status ailments, and high-velocity strikes.

Reactions occur when you swap from an "Active" character with a full Esper Meter to a "Standby" character. If the standby character's element is adjacent to the active character's element on the wheel, a Duo Cycle reaction is triggered automatically upon entry.

### Trio Cycle Reactions: Mastering Charge and Discord

Trio Reactions are the ultimate goal of a rotation. To trigger a Trio Reaction, you must rotate through all three characters in a team that match one of the Trios on the wheel (e.g., a team consisting of Lakshana, Cosmos, and Anima).

| Trio Group | Reaction Name | Trigger Condition | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Top Trio (L/C/A) | Charge | Complete a full 1-2-3 swap sequence within 15 seconds. | Instantly restores 50% Energy to all party members and increases Cycle Rate. |
| Bottom Trio (I/C/P) | Discord | Complete a full 1-2-3 swap sequence within 15 seconds. | Deals massive "Break" damage to the target's Stability bar and increases Crit Damage. |

Triggering Discord is the most effective way to "Stun" bosses in the late-game Anomalous Zones. Once the Stability bar is broken, the enemy enters a vulnerable state where they take increased damage from all sources.

### The Combat Loop: Meter Management and Parrying

The speed at which you can trigger these reactions depends entirely on your Esper Meter management. You cannot swap to trigger a reaction unless the incoming character has a glowing, full meter.

| Action | Esper Meter Generation | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Normal Attack (Full String) | 25% | Varies slightly by weapon (Arc) type. |
| Elemental Skill | 40% | Usually has a cooldown of 8–12 seconds. |
| Successful Parry | 100% | Instantly fills the meter for the active character. |
| Perfect Dodge Counter | 35% | Triggers a slow-motion window for follow-up attacks. |

Mastering the Parry mechanic is the fastest way to cycle through your team. A successful parry not only protects you from damage but allows for an immediate reaction swap, keeping the momentum of the Esper Cycle going without having to grind out normal attacks.

### Advanced Stats: Cycle Rate vs. Cycle Intensity

To optimize your elemental damage, you must balance two key stats found on your Arcs (weapons) and gear:

1.  **Cycle Rate:** This stat acts as a multiplier for Esper Meter generation. High Cycle Rate is essential for "Support" characters who need to swap in, trigger a reaction, and swap out quickly.
2.  **Cycle Intensity:** This stat directly increases the damage and effectiveness of the reactions themselves. For example, higher Cycle Intensity makes the Blossom turrets hit harder and increases the slow percentage of the Remora effect.

Generally, your Main DPS should focus on Cycle Intensity, while your Sub-DPS and Support should prioritize Cycle Rate to ensure the wheel is always turning.

Beyond the basic mechanics of the Esper Wheel, high-level play involves integrating your team's elemental needs with their weapon types and resonance levels.

### Top Meta Team Compositions

**The "Infinite Blossom" Team (Top Trio Focus)**
*   **Core:** Nanally (Anima) + Cosmos Support + Lakshana Utility.
*   **Strategy:** This team focuses on keeping three Lebenstamm turrets active at all times. By cycling through the Top Trio, you trigger the [Charge reaction](https://ntecodex.com/faq), which provides the energy needed to spam Elemental Bursts, further accelerating the meter gain.

**The "Discord Strikeforce" (Bottom Trio Focus)**
*   **Core:** Sakya (Chaos) + Incantation Control + Psyche Speedster.
*   **Strategy:** Designed for boss killing. The goal is to trigger Remora to slow the boss, followed by a full rotation to [trigger Discord](https://ntecodex.com/faq). This setup can deplete a boss's Stability bar in under 20 seconds if executed with perfect parries.

### Weapon (Arc) Compatibility for Elemental DPS

The type of Arc a character wields changes how they interact with their element. There are five primary Arc types:

*   **Plasma:** High hit-rate, excellent for building Esper Meter quickly. Best for Psyche and Chaos characters.
*   **Solid:** High impact, provides bonus Break damage. Synergizes well with the [Discord reaction](https://ntecodex.com/faq).
*   **Gas:** Focuses on AoE and lingering field effects. Ideal for Anima and Cosmos characters.
*   **Liquid:** Provides healing or lifesteal based on [reaction damage](https://ntecodex.com/guides/nte-chaos-vs-psyche-reaction-damage-gear-guide).
*   **Synthesis:** A versatile type that can adapt its bonus based on the current Esper Cycle active on the field.

When choosing an Arc, ensure the sub-stat complements the character's role in the Esper Cycle. A Nanally build, for instance, should favor a Gas-type Arc with high Cycle Intensity to maximize her Blossom damage.

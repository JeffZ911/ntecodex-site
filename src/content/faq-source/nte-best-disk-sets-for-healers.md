# NTE Best Disk Sets for Healers: Top Cartridges for Support Espers

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/nte-best-disk-sets-for-healers/inline-1.webp)


When building your team in Neverness to Everness (NTE), one of the most common questions players ask is: what are the best disk sets (known in-game as Cartridges) for healer characters? Unlike traditional RPGs where any "magic" gear might suffice, NTE uses a specific Cartridge and Module system that rewards matching set bonuses to an Esper's specific scaling stat—usually HP or Charge Efficiency.

Players often look for clarity on which Cartridge sets provide the highest Healing Bonus and HP scaling for support Espers. Understanding how the 2-piece and 4-piece set bonuses impact the survivability of your team is the difference between clearing a Level 50 Anomaly Zone and a total party wipe. Furthermore, specific characters like Edgar, Fadia, and Jiuyuan have unique mechanics that make certain sets significantly better than others. This guide breaks down the meta-defining healer gear to help you farm the right Anomaly Zones efficiently.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-best-disk-sets-for-healers/inline-2.webp)


The current meta for healers in Neverness to Everness revolves around four primary Cartridge sets, each serving a different niche within the survival role. The absolute best disk set for pure healers is **Thea's Night Tavern**, which provides a 10% HP boost and a 20% Healing Bonus, making it the gold standard for throughput. For HP-scaling supports like Fadia who act as "drain tanks," the **Tiny Big Adventure** set is the top choice, offering up to 40% Max HP through its unique stacking mechanics.

If your healer also serves as the primary team buffer, **Speedy Hedgehog** is the meta choice; it increases Charge Efficiency by 12% and grants a 15% ATK buff to all allies for 20 seconds after the wearer uses an Ultimate. Finally, for shield-based survival supports like Adler, **Kingdom's Guard** is the recommended set to boost shield strength by 20%.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-best-disk-sets-for-healers/inline-3.webp)


The gear system in NTE is divided into Cartridges and Modules. While Cartridges provide the set bonuses, the Modules (Type I, II, III, and IV) determine the base stats and how those bonuses are activated within your character's Console.

### The Core Healing Set: Thea's Night Tavern
Thea's Night Tavern is the most straightforward set for any character whose primary job is restoring the party's health bar. The 2-piece bonus provides a flat 10% HP increase, which is the standard scaling stat for most healers. However, the 4-piece Legendary effect—a 20% Healing Bonus—is why this set is mandatory for dedicated healers like Edgar. 

In NTE, Healing Bonus is a multiplicative stat. It takes the final calculated heal (based on the Esper's HP or ATK) and increases it by a flat percentage. This is significantly more efficient than simply stacking more HP, as you eventually run into diminishing returns with raw stat stacking. For Edgar, whose healing is stationary and relies on keeping allies within his field, the 20% boost ensures that even quick swaps into his zone provide a full health reset.

### The HP-Scaling Alternative: Tiny Big Adventure
Tiny Big Adventure is a specialized survival set designed for "Survival-type" Espers who benefit from having a massive health pool. Fadia is the prime example of a character who prefers this over Thea's Night Tavern. Her kit involves redirecting damage from allies to herself, essentially acting as a sponge.

The 4-piece mechanic for Tiny Big Adventure is unique: whenever the wearer's HP decreases (which happens constantly for Fadia), they gain a 4% Max HP increase. This stacks up to 10 times, allowing for a 40% total increase in Max HP. The most powerful part of this set is that casting an Ultimate instantly grants all 10 stacks. This allows Fadia to enter a fight, trigger her Ultimate, and immediately reach her maximum health potential, which in turn scales her damage and self-healing.

### Utility Support Sets: Speedy Hedgehog and Kingdom's Guard
Not every "healer" slot is filled by a pure medic. In high-level play, many players opt for a "Support-type" Esper who provides utility. 

*   **Speedy Hedgehog:** This is arguably the most used set in the NTE meta. The 12% Charge Efficiency allows healers to maintain 100% uptime on their healing Ultimates. Since many healers have high energy costs, the 4-piece effect (15% ATK buff to the whole team) turns your healer into a powerful offensive buffer.
*   **Kingdom's Guard:** While not a healing set in the traditional sense, it is the Best-in-Slot (BiS) for shielders like Adler. Since shields prevent damage rather than reacting to it, the 20% Shield Strength bonus is vital for surviving "one-shot" mechanics in Anomaly Pilgrimage runs.

### Optimizing the Console: Modules and Substats
To activate these 4-piece effects, you must slot the corresponding Modules into the character's Console. In NTE, Modules are categorized into Types II, III, and IV. 

*   **Type II Modules:** Usually focus on Offensive stats or Charge Efficiency. 
*   **Type III Modules:** Primarily provide HP and DEF stats.
*   **Type IV Modules:** Often contain the rarest main stats, including Healing Bonus and Shield Strength.

When farming for healers, your main stat priority on Type IV Modules should always be **Healing Bonus**. For all other slots, prioritize **HP%** and **Charge Efficiency**. Unlike DPS characters, healers do not need Crit Rate or Crit DMG, allowing you to focus entirely on tankiness and energy regeneration.

### Best Healer Characters and Their Gear Needs
*   **Edgar (Cosmos, A-Rank):** As the primary dedicated healer available at launch, Edgar's kit is purely focused on sustain. He needs Thea's Night Tavern to compensate for his stationary healing style. If your team is mobile, increasing his Healing Bonus is the only way to ensure his brief windows of healing are impactful.
*   **Fadia (Psyche, S-Class):** Fadia is a "vampire" style martial artist. She doesn't just heal; she absorbs. Because her damage scales with her Max HP, Tiny Big Adventure is her undisputed BiS. 
*   **Jiuyuan (Anima, S-Class):** While Jiuyuan is primarily a sub-DPS, her first Awakening (A1) allows her to provide team-wide healing. Since she is often used in "Blossom" teams (Anima + Cosmos), she is the best candidate for Speedy Hedgehog to keep the team's ATK high.

### Synergizing with Arcs (Weapons)
Cartridges only provide half of the build. To maximize sustain, you must pair them with the right Arcs.

1.  **Call of the Twisted City:** This is the best Arc for pure healing throughput. It provides a massive boost to the wearer's HP and increases healing effectiveness after using a Skill.
2.  **Eternal Vaults:** Fadia’s signature Arc. It provides HP and Mental DMG, which perfectly complements the Tiny Big Adventure set.
3.  **Your Happiness is Priceless:** A flexible session weapon that restores a percentage of the team's HP whenever the wearer triggers an Esper Cycle reaction.

### Best Team Compositions for Healers
In the current Version 1.0 meta, healers shine best in "Immortal" or "Charge" team compositions. 
*   **The Immortal Team:** Fadia + Edgar + Zero. This comp uses Fadia to absorb damage and Edgar to keep Fadia’s health bar topped off. It is virtually impossible to die with this setup in open-world exploration.
*   **The Charge Team:** Jiuyuan + [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) + Edgar. This team focuses on the **Charge** reaction (Anima + Lakshana + Cosmos), which accelerates energy gain. In this team, Edgar should run Speedy Hedgehog to maximize the ATK buff uptime.

### Farming Guide for Healer Cartridges
To get S-Rank (Orange) Cartridges, you must farm high-level **Anomaly Zones**. Thea's Night Tavern and Tiny Big Adventure are typically found in the "Evernight" and "Industrial District" zones. It is recommended to save your stamina for Level 45+ zones, as these have a guaranteed drop rate for Legendary Modules, which are required to activate the 4-piece set bonuses.

### Shielders vs. Healers: Which Does Your Team Need?
NTE allows for a flexible survival slot. If you find yourself getting "one-shot" by bosses like the Hethereau Centurion, a shielder like Adler with the **Kingdom's Guard** set is superior because shields can exceed your maximum HP. However, for long-form content like Anomaly Hunts where chip damage adds up over time, a healer like Edgar with **Thea's Night Tavern** is indispensable. Most top-tier players eventually run one of each in their secondary teams for the Anomaly Pilgrimage.

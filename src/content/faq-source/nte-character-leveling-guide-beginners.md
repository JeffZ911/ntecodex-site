## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-character-leveling-guide-beginners/inline-2.webp)


Character leveling in NTE is a multi-tiered process involving base leveling with Hunter Guides and Beetle Coins, followed by Ascension (also known as Breakthrough) to raise the level cap. Unlike traditional RPGs where you can grind indefinitely, your characters' maximum level is strictly gated by your account's Appraisal Level. This Appraisal Level acts as a world tier and increases at specific Hunter Level milestones: 20, 30, 40, 45, 50, and 55.

To reach the current level cap of 60, players must first reach Hunter Rank 10 to unlock the Ascension feature. From there, you must complete specific Appraisal Level quests at levels 20, 40, and 50 to unlock the higher level ceilings for your Espers. The primary loop involves consuming Hunter Guides (available in Rising, Senior, and Elite tiers) and Beetle Coins (Shell Credits) for immediate stat gains, then farming Anomaly Hunts for boss-specific materials once a character hits their current level ceiling.

## Detailed Explanation

The progression system in Neverness to Everness is designed to prevent players from out-leveling the story content too quickly while ensuring that every activity in Hethereau contributes to your team's power.

### The Account Gating: Hunter Level and Appraisal Level

In NTE, your individual Esper levels are secondary to your Hunter Level (your account rank). You earn Hunter XP by completing main story chapters, side urban myths, and daily missions. However, the true regulator of power is the Appraisal Level.

Every time you hit a Hunter Level milestone, your Appraisal Level can be increased. Raising your Appraisal Level makes world enemies tougher and increases the rarity of loot dropped in the open world and from Anomaly Hunts. Most importantly, it is the only way to raise the level cap of your Espers.

### Core Leveling: Hunter Guides and Beetle Coins

The most basic form of progression is increasing an Esper’s base level. This is done via the "Info" tab in the character menu. Unlike some older titles where characters gain significant XP from killing overworld mobs, NTE relies heavily on "Hunter Guides."

*   **Hunter Guide (Rising):** Best for the early levels (1-20).
*   **Hunter Guide (Senior):** The standard mid-game leveling resource.
*   **Hunter Guide (Elite):** Reserved for pushing characters from level 50 to 60 due to the high XP requirements.

Every time you use a Guide, you must also spend a corresponding amount of Beetle Coins (Shell Credits). Always ensure you are participating in "Houdinii’s Magic Stage" challenges, as these are the most stamina-efficient way to stock up on these guides.

### Breaking the Ceiling: The Ascension System

Once an Esper reaches their current level cap (e.g., level 20), they cannot gain more XP until they undergo Ascension. This process requires specific materials dropped by world bosses found in Anomaly Hunts.

To unlock Ascension, you must reach Hunter Rank 10. Each character element requires different elemental shards, and each specific character requires a unique boss drop. For example, [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) requires materials from the "Glitch-Vein Horror" boss, while Zero requires drops from "Cosmic Singularity" encounters. Farming these bosses consumes stamina, so plan your daily sessions around which character you intend to Ascend next.

### The Console System: Cartridges and Modules Explained

The Console System is NTE’s version of a gear or artifact system, but with a unique spatial puzzle twist. Each Esper has a "Console" with a Tetris-like grid.

1.  **Cartridges:** These are the central pieces of the Console. They provide set bonuses (e.g., 2-piece or 4-piece bonuses) that significantly alter playstyles.
2.  **Modules:** These are the "blocks" you place around the Cartridge. Modules come in various shapes (squares, L-shapes, T-shapes).
3.  **Optimization:** Your goal is to fit as many high-stat Modules as possible into the grid without overlapping. Higher-rarity Consoles have larger grids, allowing for more complex stat combinations.

Modules provide flat stats like ATK, HP, and Crit Rate. Matching the shape of a Module to the specific "Socket" colors on the grid can provide additional Resonance bonuses.

### Esper Abilities and Skill Progression

Leveling your character's base stats is only half the battle. You must also upgrade their active and passive abilities. Skill progression is gated by materials found in "Houdinii’s Schemes" and "Anomaly Pilgrimages."

Skills have their own level caps tied to the character's Ascension level. A level 20 character can usually only upgrade skills to level 2, while a level 60 character can max them out. Prioritize upgrading the "Main Ability" and "Ultimate" of your primary damage dealers first, as the resource cost for level 5 and level 6 skills is significantly higher.

### Arc Optimization: Matching Weapons to Espers

Arcs are the primary weapons in NTE. They are categorized into five types: Solid, Liquid, Gas, Plasma, and Synthesis. Each Esper has a specific compatibility with these types based on their element and combat role.

| Character | Element | Recommended Arc Type |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Nanally | Anima | Solid |

Leveling an Arc requires "Pigments," which function similarly to Hunter Guides but for weapons. Equipping a "Solid" Arc on an Anima character like Nanally provides a secondary stat boost that is not available if you equip a non-matching type.

### The Awakening System: Mind Shards and Resonance

If you obtain a duplicate of a character through the gacha system, you receive "Mind Shards." These are used in the Awakening menu. Unlike many other games where duplicates follow a linear path, NTE uses a 6-node board.

You can choose which of the 6 nodes to activate first, allowing for some customization. For instance, one node might boost Ultimate damage, while another might reduce skill cooldowns. Reaching 3 nodes and 6 nodes unlocks "Resonance" tiers, which provide massive team-wide buffs or unique mechanical changes to the character’s kit (such as adding an extra charge to their dash).

### F2P Progression Tips: City Tycoon and Resource Management

One of the most unique aspects of NTE character growth is the "City Tycoon" integration. By managing your cafe, participating in street races, and completing urban delivery missions, you earn "City Tycoon Stamina."

This stamina is separate from your combat stamina. You can spend it to unlock free S-Rank characters like Chiz and purchase their specific upgrade materials from the City Shop. For F2P (Free to Play) players, this is the most reliable way to max out a high-tier character without relying on gacha luck.

**Daily and Weekly Farming Checklist:**
*   **Daily:** Complete 4 Urban Commissions for Hunter XP.
*   **Daily:** Spend combat stamina on Houdinii’s Magic Stage for Hunter Guides.
*   **Weekly:** Clear the Anomaly Pilgrimage for high-level skill upgrade materials.
*   **Weekly:** Max out City Tycoon rewards to stock up on Beetle Coins and free Mind Shards.

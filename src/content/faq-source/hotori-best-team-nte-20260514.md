Since her release on April 29, 2026, she has redefined the meta by allowing players to compress an entire team’s worth of skill damage into a single, frozen ten-second window.

# Hotori Best Team NTE: The Ultimate Guide to Team Compositions

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/hotori-best-team-nte-20260514/inline-1.webp)


What is the best team for Hotori in Neverness to Everness (NTE)? Many players are looking for the optimal lineup to maximize her Cosmos-element damage and her signature time-stop Ultimate. Specifically, which characters provide the best synergy for the "Blossom" reaction, and how can you build a team that makes the most of her "Present Replay" recording mechanic? This guide analyzes the current meta to identify the top-performing compositions for both high-end spenders and free-to-play (F2P) Appraisers.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/hotori-best-team-nte-20260514/inline-2.webp)


The absolute best team for Hotori is the **"Eternal Blossom"** composition. This team consists of **Hotori (Cosmos/Sub-DPS)**, **[Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) (Anima/Main DPS)**, **Jiuyuan (Anima/Sub-DPS)**, and **Zero (Cosmos/Reaction Support)**. This lineup is widely considered the gold standard because it maximizes the Blossom reaction (Cosmos + Anima), which spawns Vita Buds that deal continuous AOE damage and regenerate team energy.

Hotori’s primary synergy lies with Anima-element characters. When Hotori triggers a Blossom reaction, it creates a "Vita Bud" turret. With Jiuyuan on the team, the number of simultaneous Vita Buds is doubled and the field cap is increased to six, creating a screen-clearing field of projectiles. For F2P players who may not have pulled Nanally or Jiuyuan, a highly effective alternative includes Hotori, Zero, Mint (Anima), and Haniel (Psyche/Buff).

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/hotori-best-team-nte-20260514/inline-3.webp)


To master Hotori, you must look past her "Support" classification. While the game labels her as a Support due to her healing and buffing capabilities, her damage ceiling is arguably the highest in the current 1.0 patch. This is due to what the community calls the **"Support-DPS Paradox."** Hotori doesn't deal consistent damage throughout the fight; instead, she records the high-impact skills of her teammates and "replays" them all at once during her Ultimate, "World's Tide," while time is stopped.

### The Blossom Reaction: Why Anima is Essential
In Neverness to Everness, the elemental wheel dictates that Cosmos reacts with Anima to trigger **Blossom**. This reaction is the backbone of Hotori’s best teams. Triggering Blossom spawns a Vita Bud near the target. These buds fire "Vita Pistils" (projectiles) at nearby enemies. 

The synergy becomes "broken" when you pair Hotori with **Jiuyuan**. Jiuyuan’s passive ability, "Seize the Moment," enhances the Blossom reaction, allowing for two buds to spawn per trigger instead of one and raising the maximum bud count from three to six. When Hotori activates her Ultimate, these Vita Buds continue to fire even while the rest of the world is frozen, allowing you to stack massive damage without the enemy being able to retaliate.

### Recording Efficiency with "Present Replay"
Hotori’s most complex mechanic is her Redirect Skill, **Present Replay**. When activated (costing 60 Non-Closed Timepiece energy), Hotori enters a 5-second recording window. During this time, you must switch to your allies and use their Support or Redirect skills. Hotori can record up to three timings.

Teammates like **Nanally** and **Zero** are mandatory for high-level play because their skills have high "per-hit" value. Nanally, the "Ichi-daime" limited unit, provides follow-up attacks that mirror the active character's rhythm. When Hotori replays Nanally’s recorded skill during "World's Tide," the damage is calculated using Hotori's own Cosmos DMG bonuses and DEF ignore stats, often resulting in a burst that can one-shot world bosses.

### Hotori Optimal Team Rotation

1.  **Step 1 (Setup):** Start with **Hotori** on the field to build "Non-Closed Timepiece" energy. Use her basic umbrella attacks until the gauge reaches at least 60 (ideally 120).
2.  **Step 2 (Recording):** Activate **Present Replay**. You now have 5 seconds. Immediately swap to **Jiuyuan** and use her skill to mark enemies with "Lethal Rose Pact."
3.  **Step 3 (Synergy):** Swap to **Nanally** and activate her skill to enter the "Ichi-daime's Authority" state. 
4.  **Step 4 (The Burst):** Swap back to **Hotori** and unleash her Ultimate, **"World's Tide."** 
5.  **Step 5 (Katana Phase):** Time is now stopped for 10 seconds. Hotori draws her katana. All recorded skills from Jiuyuan and Nanally replay automatically. Spam Hotori’s katana attacks to build stacks for the final finisher.

### The 'Eternal Blossom' Meta Team
This team is the peak of the current NTE meta. By combining Hotori, Nanally, and Jiuyuan, you are essentially creating a feedback loop of Anima and Cosmos damage. Nanally acts as the primary driver, dealing sustained damage while Hotori is off-field. Jiuyuan provides the "Lethal Rose Pact" marks, which increase the damage enemies take from the Blossom buds. Once Hotori’s timepiece is full, the team transitions into a burst phase that the current endgame content, such as the Pink Paws Heist high-risk anomalies, simply cannot survive.

### The 'Time-Stop Burst' Quick-Swap Team
If you prefer a faster playstyle, the combination of **Hotori, Zero, and Sakiri** focuses on "Quick-Swap" mechanics. Zero’s ability to instantly charge the Esper Meter means you can trigger reactions on demand. Sakiri is included here for her ability to group enemies. By grouping them before Hotori’s time-stop, you ensure that every instance of recorded AOE damage hits every single target. This team is less about the Blossom reaction and more about raw Cosmos and Lakshana burst damage.

### F2P and Starter Team Options
For players just starting their journey in Hethereau, the team of **Hotori, Zero, Mint, and Haniel** is exceptionally reliable. Zero is given to every player for free and serves as a perfect Cosmos battery for Hotori. Mint provides the necessary Anima element to trigger Blossom, and Haniel (available via pre-registration milestones or the standard board) provides a flat ATK buff that helps Hotori’s umbrella attacks deal respectable damage during the energy-recharge phase.

### Why Anima is Hotori's Best Elemental Partner
While Hotori can react with Lakshana to trigger **Remora** (which slows enemies), the slow effect is redundant because Hotori already stops time with her Ultimate. Blossom, however, provides independent damage sources (the buds) that function autonomously. This elemental synergy is the only one that allows for "uncontested" damage during the 10-second freeze, as the buds do not require Hotori to stop her katana combo to trigger.

### Maximizing the Recording Window with Sakiri and Zero
Advanced players use **Sakiri** to prep the battlefield. Sakiri’s "Redirect" skill can be recorded by Hotori. When replayed during the Ultimate, it pulls enemies into the center of Hotori’s katana slashes. **Zero** is used to bridge the gap between Ultimates. Because Hotori needs time to build her timepiece energy, Zero can stay on the field to maintain DPS pressure without sacrificing the team's reaction potential.

### Best Arcs and Cartridges for Team Synergy
To make a Hotori team truly viable for endgame content, you must equip the correct Arcs (weapons) and Cartridges (gear). Her signature Arc, **"Marching Beyond Time,"** is mathematically non-negotiable for high-tier play. It features a "Wastelab" mechanic that converts the skills used by your allies during the recording window into "Wastetime" stacks. When Hotori uses her Ultimate, these stacks are consumed to provide up to 24% DEF ignore and a massive boost to her Ultimate Crit DMG.

For gear, the **"Lost Radiance"** 4-piece Cartridge set is the standard. The 2-piece set increases Cosmos DMG by 15%, while the 4-piece set allows Hotori to ignore 25% of enemy DEF for 10 seconds after using her Ultimate. When combined with her A6 awakening (which adds another 30% DEF ignore during time-stop), Hotori can effectively reduce a boss's armor to zero.

### Hotori vs. Other Cosmos Characters
Compared to other Cosmos units like **Zero** or **Edgar**, Hotori occupies a unique niche. Zero is a "Main DPS" who wants to stay on the field and slash enemies with a sword. Edgar is a "Survival/Healer" who focuses on keeping the team alive with "Liquid" archetype abilities. Hotori is the only character who can "borrow" the power of her teammates. This makes her more future-proof; as stronger Anima or Lakshana characters are released in future patches (such as the rumored Chaos or Psyche characters in version 1.1), Hotori will only get stronger by recording their more powerful skills.

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Does Hotori’s time-stop pause the mission timer?**
Yes. In timed challenges like the **Pink Paws Heist**, activating "World's Tide" pauses the actual stage timer for the duration of the effect. This makes Hotori essential for achieving "S-Rank" times on difficult levels.

**Can I use Hotori with Chaos or Psyche characters?**
While you can, it is not optimal. Chaos and Psyche do not have a direct "Duo Cycle" reaction with Cosmos. You would miss out on the Blossom or Remora effects, significantly lowering your overall team DPS.

**What is the best Awakening level for Hotori?**
Hotori is very functional at A0 (no duplicates). However, **A1** is a major power spike as it increases her damage during Time Stop by 12% for each recorded skill. **A6** is her "whale" milestone, granting the entire team a 30% ATK buff and allowing Hotori to ignore 30% of enemy DEF during her Ultimate.

**How do I build energy for the Non-Closed Timepiece faster?**
Energy is built passively while Hotori is on the field, but you can accelerate it by performing "Critical Ripostes" (perfect dodges followed by a basic attack). Each successful riposte grants a chunk of energy, allowing you to reach your Ultimate much faster.

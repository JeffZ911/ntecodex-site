Navigating the gacha mechanics of Teyvat requires more than just luck; it requires a firm grasp of the hidden math that governs every wish you make. Understanding the pity system is the single most important factor in managing your Primogems and ensuring you actually get the characters you want.

# How Does the Pity System Work in Genshin Impact?

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/how-does-pity-system-work-genshin/inline-1.webp)


The "pity system" is a foundational mechanic in [Genshin Impact](https://ntecodex.com/guides/genshin-impact-vs-honkai-star-rail-battery-drain-gear) designed to ensure that players eventually receive high-rarity items, even if they have poor luck. Without this system, a player could theoretically spend thousands of Primogems and never see a 5-star character. For Free-to-Play (F2P) players and low spenders, the pity system is the only reliable way to plan for future character releases and manage a limited budget of Intertwined Fates.

This system guarantees specific outcomes after a certain number of attempts. If you have not pulled a 5-star item within a set number of "Wishes," the game's internal logic forces one to appear. Similarly, it guarantees 4-star items at regular intervals. However, the system is not identical across all banners, and recent updates, specifically Version 5.0, have introduced significant changes to how these guarantees function.

### The Basics of Genshin's Gacha System

Genshin Impact uses a "Wishes" system where players spend currency—either Intertwined Fates for limited banners or Acquaint Fates for the standard banner—to receive a random assortment of weapons and characters. Most pulls result in 3-star weapons, which serve as "filler." The real goal for most players is the 4-star and 5-star rewards.

The pity system tracks every pull you make on each specific banner type. Every time you fail to pull a high-rarity item, your "pity count" increases. Once you hit a specific threshold, your odds of success skyrocket until a high-rarity item is guaranteed.

### Why Understanding Pity Saves Primogems

Knowing your current pity count prevents "rage pulling" and helps you decide when to stop. For example, if you know you are 75 pulls into a banner without a 5-star, you are in the "soft pity" range where a 5-star is imminent. If you don't actually want the currently featured 5-star character, you should stop immediately to avoid "wasting" your guarantee. By tracking these numbers, you can effectively "save" a guaranteed pull for a character you truly desire, rather than leaving it to chance.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-does-pity-system-work-genshin/inline-2.webp)


The pity system is a safety net that guarantees a 5-star item after a set number of pulls: 90 for character banners and 80 for weapon banners. If you reach these "Hard Pity" numbers without seeing a gold flash, the very next pull is 100% guaranteed to be a 5-star.

Key rules of the system include:
*   **4-Star Guarantee:** A 4-star item (character or weapon) is guaranteed at least once every 10 pulls.
*   **Pity Reset:** The moment you pull a 5-star item, your 5-star pity count resets to zero. The same applies to 4-star pity when you pull a 4-star item.
*   **Carryover:** Pity carries over between banners of the same type. If you pull 50 times on a limited character banner and it ends, you start the next limited character banner with 50 pity already built up.

| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

| **Weapon Event Wish** | 10 Pulls | 63 Pulls | 80 Pulls |

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-does-pity-system-work-genshin/inline-3.webp)


While the hard numbers (90 and 80) are the absolute guarantees, the internal mechanics of the pity system are more nuanced. The community has discovered through millions of data points that the probability of pulling a 5-star is not linear.

### Character Event Wish: Soft Pity and Capturing Radiance

On a Character Event Wish banner, the base chance of pulling a 5-star is 0.6%. However, once you reach pull 74, you enter "Soft Pity." From pull 74 to 89, the probability of pulling a 5-star increases dramatically with each subsequent wish. Most players will see their 5-star character between pulls 76 and 81. It is statistically rare to actually reach the 90th pull.

As of Version 5.0, a new mechanic called **Capturing Radiance** was introduced. Previously, when you pulled a 5-star, you had a flat 50% chance of getting the featured character and a 50% chance of getting a standard character (the "50/50 rule"). With Capturing Radiance, if the game determines you are about to "lose" the 50/50, there is a chance a special animation will trigger, turning that loss into a win. This effectively raises the 50/50 win rate from 50% to approximately 55%.

### The 50/50 Rule and Guarantee Carryover

The 50/50 rule is the most famous aspect of the Genshin gacha.
1.  When you pull a 5-star on a limited banner, there is a 50% (now ~55% with Capturing Radiance) chance it is the featured character.
2.  If you "lose" and receive a standard character (like Jean, Diluc, or Dehya), your next 5-star is **100% guaranteed** to be the featured character.
3.  This "guaranteed status" carries over. If you lose a 50/50 on one banner and stop pulling, your first 5-star on the *next* limited banner will be the featured character.

### Weapon Banner: Epitomized Path and the 5.0 Update

The Weapon Banner (Epitome Invocation) operates differently. It has a lower hard pity (80) and a lower soft pity (63). The base 5-star rate is also slightly higher at 0.7%.

In the Weapon Banner, you face a "75/25" rule. There is a 75% chance of pulling one of the two featured 5-star weapons and a 25% chance of pulling a standard 5-star weapon. To prevent players from pulling the "wrong" featured weapon indefinitely, the **Epitomized Path** system exists.

**Major Change in Version 5.0:** Previously, you needed 2 "Fate Points" to guarantee your chosen weapon (meaning you could fail twice before the third was guaranteed). As of Version 5.0, you only need **1 Fate Point**.
*   If you pull a 5-star weapon that is NOT your chosen weapon, you receive 1 Fate Point.
*   Your next 5-star weapon is then 100% guaranteed to be the one you selected.
*   **Warning:** Fate Points **do not** carry over between banner rotations. If the banner ends, your Fate Point disappears, though your numerical pity (e.g., 40/80) still carries over.

### Chronicled Wish: Rules for the Newest Banner Type

The Chronicled Wish is a special banner that appears occasionally, featuring a mix of older characters and weapons from a specific region.
*   It uses a "Fate Point" system similar to the weapon banner.
*   You select a "Designated" character or weapon.
*   If you pull a 5-star that isn't your choice, you get 1 Fate Point, making the next 5-star guaranteed.
*   Pity count carries over to the next Chronicled Wish, but the Fate Point does not.

### Standard Wish and Beginner's Wish Differences

The **Standard Wish (Wanderlust Invocation)** does not have a 50/50 rule because there is no "featured" character. It pulls from a permanent pool of 5-star characters and weapons. While there is no official "balance" rule, data suggests the game tries to alternate between giving you weapons and characters over long periods, though this is not a hard guarantee like the 90-pull pity.

The **Beginner's Wish** (the Noelle banner) is unique. It has a 20% discount on pulls and disappears after 20 wishes. It does not share pity with any other banner and does not have a 5-star pity system beyond the basic rates.

### 4-Star Pity and Rate-Up Mechanics

Every 10 pulls, you are guaranteed at least one 4-star item.
*   If you pull a 4-star on pull 1 through 9, the pity resets.
*   On limited banners, there is a 50/50 rule for 4-stars. If the 4-star you pull is not one of the three featured "rate-up" characters, the next 4-star is guaranteed to be one of them.
*   This 4-star guarantee also carries over between banners.

## Related

![Related](/img/how-does-pity-system-work-genshin/inline-4.webp)


Understanding the mechanics is the first step; managing them is the second. Many players find themselves confused because the game does not provide a live "pity counter" on the screen.

### How to Track Your Pity in 2025

To check your current pity, you must navigate to the "Wish" menu and click on the "History" button at the bottom.
1.  Select the banner type you want to check (Character, Weapon, or Standard).
2.  Count the number of pulls starting from the most recent 5-star you received.
3.  Each page shows 5 entries. If your last 5-star was 10 pages ago, and it was the third item on that page, your pity is $(10 \times 5) - 3 = 47$.
4.  **Note:** The History tab can take up to an hour to update after you make wishes, and it only stores data for the last 6 months. If you haven't wished in over half a year, your history will appear blank, though your internal pity count remains intact.

### Single Pulls vs. 10-Pulls: Which is Better?

There is a common myth that 10-pulls (multi-pulls) have better odds than single pulls. This is false. The game calculates each pull individually. Doing a 10-pull is simply a way to speed up the process.

In fact, doing single pulls is technically more "efficient" when you are close to pity. If you do a 10-pull and get your desired character on the 2nd wish, the remaining 8 wishes are still consumed. While they contribute to your next pity, you might have preferred to save those Primogems for a different banner entirely.

### The Risks of "Building Pity"

"Building pity" is the practice of wishing on a banner you don't like to get closer to the 90-pull guarantee for a future banner, usually while chasing a specific 4-star character. **This is generally discouraged.**

Because "Soft Pity" can trigger early, and even the 0.6% base rate can result in a 5-star at any time, you risk "resetting" your pity on a character you do not want. If you are 60 pulls in and have a "guaranteed" status saved for a future Archon, pulling on a different banner just to get a 4-star constellation could result in an accidental 5-star, wiping out your guarantee and 60 pulls of progress.

### Primogem Management for F2P Players

For F2P players, the most efficient way to use the pity system is to always assume the worst-case scenario. Never assume you will win the 50/50.
*   Save at least 160–180 pulls (approx. 25,600–28,800 Primogems) before your favorite character's banner arrives.
*   Only use Intertwined Fates on limited banners.
*   Never spend Primogems on the Standard Wish (Wanderlust Invocation); only use the free Acquaint Fates provided by the Battle Pass, character ascensions, and the Paimon’s Bargains shop.
*   With the Version 5.0 weapon banner changes, the weapon banner is more accessible than ever, but it still requires a significant investment (up to 160 pulls) to guarantee a specific result.

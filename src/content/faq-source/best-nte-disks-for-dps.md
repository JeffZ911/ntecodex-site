In Neverness to Everness, the term "Disks" is frequently used by the player base to describe the two primary pillars of character builds: Arcs (the weapon system) and Cartridges (the gear or relic system). ### What are Disks in Neverness to Everness?

Arcs are the primary "Weapon Disks" that dictate a character's base Attack (ATK) and provide unique passive abilities. These are categorized into three physical states: Plasma, Solid, and Gas. Each state interacts differently with character kits, often favoring specific combat styles such as rapid-fire projectiles or heavy melee strikes.

Cartridges, on the other hand, function as the "Gear Disks." These are equipped in sets to provide stat boosts and powerful set bonuses. Unlike traditional gear systems, Cartridges in NTE are slotted into the Console, where their effectiveness is further amplified by the Module system. To maximize a DPS character, you must align the Arc’s passive triggers with the Cartridge’s set requirements.

### Why DPS Optimization Matters

The combat in NTE revolves around the "Esper Cycle," where triggering elemental reactions like Blossom, Scorch, and Hex is essential for shredding enemy resistances. If your DPS Disks are not optimized, you will find it increasingly difficult to trigger these reactions before the enemy’s "Stability Bar" recovers. In the current endgame meta, specifically within the high-level Urban Anomalies, raw ATK is no longer sufficient. High-tier builds now focus on DEF-ignore mechanics, Crit ratios, and Elemental DMG bonuses to bypass the scaling armor of boss-level entities.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/best-nte-disks-for-dps/inline-3.webp)


The complexity of NTE’s gear system lies in the interaction between the physical state of the Arc and the "Tetris" layout of the Console Modules. Understanding these layers is vital for moving beyond basic builds into endgame-ready optimization.

### Arc Passives and Refinement Priorities

Arcs in Neverness to Everness are more than just stat sticks; they define the character's combat rhythm. For example, characters like Nanally rely on Arcs that enhance her "Anima" state. When selecting an Arc, you must match the physical type—Plasma, Solid, or Gas—to the character’s innate scaling. 

Refining Arcs (using duplicate copies) is particularly impactful in NTE. Unlike other games where refinement only offers marginal gains, NTE Arcs often unlock secondary passive tiers at Refinement Level 3 (R3). For DPS Arcs, these tiers usually involve "Execution" mechanics, where damage is increased by [information unavailable] against enemies below 30% HP. If you are a F2P player, focusing on refining A-rank Arcs like undisclosed can often outperform a base R1 S-rank Arc.

### Cartridge Set Mechanics: 2-Piece vs. 4-Piece

The Cartridge meta is currently dominated by the **Lost Radiance** set. The reason for its supremacy in Version 1.0 is the 25% DEF-ignore mechanic. In NTE, defense scaling for enemies in the "Deep Anomaly" floors is exponential. While a flat ATK set like **Urban Vanguard** (often used in the early game) provides a visible boost to your stat sheet, it falls off significantly when facing armored bosses.

For elemental specialists, the **Fireflies and the Forest** set is the high-ceiling alternative. This set requires the player to maintain "stacks" by consistently dealing Anima damage. At maximum stacks, it provides a 56% Crit DMG buff. However, maintaining this requires a team composition that can provide high "Esper Particle" generation to ensure the main DPS never drops their elemental infusion.

### Mastering the Console: Module Types and Shapes

The "Console" is where your Disks are actually powered up. It uses a grid-based system where you must fit "Modules" of various shapes (Type II, III, and IV). 
*   **Type II Modules:** Generally provide offensive stats like Crit Rate and ATK.
*   **Type III Modules:** Focus on utility, such as Cooldown Reduction and Energy Regen.
*   **Type IV Modules:** These are the rarest and provide massive boosts to Elemental DMG and Armor Penetration.

The "Tetris" aspect comes from the fact that certain Cartridges and Arcs grant bonus stats if you fill specific patterns in the Console. For instance, Nanally gains a 6% Crit Rate bonus for every Type II module placed in a vertical column. If your Module layout is messy, you lose out on these "Resonance" bonuses, which can account for up to 20% of your total damage output. Using **Carrota Coins** to "Rewind" or reshuffle Module shapes is a necessary part of the endgame grind to ensure a perfect fit.

### Optimal Main Stats and Substats for DPS

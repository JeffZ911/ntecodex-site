## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/neverness-to-everness-disk-system-guide/inline-2.webp)


The "Disk System" in Neverness to Everness (NTE) refers to the dual-layered equipment architecture consisting of Arc Disks (weapons) and the Console/Cartridge system (gear/artifacts). Every Esper in your squad requires both a high-level Arc Disk and a fully optimized Console grid to be viable in late-game content.

Arc Disks provide the primary stats, specifically ATK and CRT, along with unique passive abilities that can fundamentally change how an Esper plays. These are categorized into five distinct types based on their physical properties: Gas, Liquid, Plasma, Solid, and Synthesis. Each type generally aligns with specific Esper roles, such as DPS, Support, or Control.

The Console system is where NTE departs from gacha norms. It uses a unique "Tetris" grid where players must manually slot geometric "Modules" into "Cartridges." By fitting these shapes together, you activate powerful 2-piece (Epic Effect) and 4-piece (Legend Effect) set bonuses.

A standout feature of the NTE gear loop is its high sub-stat transparency. Unlike many other titles where sub-stats are hidden or randomized upon leveling, NTE allows players to see all stats on a piece of gear before investing any resources. This significantly reduces the "gear treadmill" frustration and allows for more intentional character building.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/neverness-to-everness-disk-system-guide/inline-3.webp)


### Understanding Arc Disks and Mixing

Arc Disks are the cornerstone of your Esper's offensive capabilities. They are primarily obtained through the Scarborough Fair gacha system (using the Dice Board to target specific S-Rank Arcs), the Arc Shop, or as rare drops from world-tier threats like the Headless Biker.

The progression of an Arc Disk is divided into two main paths: Leveling and Ascension.

**Leveling and Materials:**
To increase the level of an Arc Disk, you must consume "Dye," which is the primary EXP material in NTE. Dye is most efficiently farmed in the Kaleidoscope stage, located within Houdini's Magic Stage Anomaly Zone. Along with Dye, you will need a steady supply of Beetle Coins, the universal currency of Hethereau.

**Arc Ascension:**
Your progress is gated every 10 levels, with a maximum cap of level 80. To break these level ceilings, you must perform an Ascension. This requires specialized materials found in the Bubble Can Factory, most notably:
*   **Golden Appleseed:** Used for mid-to-high tier ascensions.
*   **Harmony:** A rare material required for the final tiers of growth.

### The Console Grid: Mastering the Tetris Mechanic

The Console system is NTE's version of artifacts, but with a spatial twist. Each character has a grid (the size of which increases as you level up) where you must fit geometric "Modules." These Modules come in three primary types, each taking up different amounts of space and offering different stat weights.

| Module Type | Grid Space Requirement | Typical Shapes | Stat Potency |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Type I** | 1x1 Square | Single Block | Low |
| **Type II** | 2x1 or 1x2 Blocks | Dominos | Medium |
| **Type III** | 4 Blocks | L-shape, T-shape, 2x2 Square | High |

**The Cartridge System:**
A "Cartridge" acts as the housing for your Modules. When you equip four Modules belonging to the same set within your Console grid, you activate the "Legend Effect." Equipping two pieces activates the smaller "Epic Effect." The challenge lies in the fact that higher-rarity Modules (Gold) often have awkward shapes, forcing you to choose between a perfect 4-piece set bonus or a mix of high-stat individual Modules.

**The "Tetris" Passive Synergy:**
Some characters have innate passives that scale based on the specific geometry of your Console. For example, the popular Esper [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) gains a significant Crit Rate bonus for every Type II module successfully slotted into her grid. This makes her build path very specific, as you must prioritize domino-shaped modules even if a square-shaped Type III module has slightly better sub-stats.

### Farming Strategy: Rabbit Hole vs. Anomaly Zones

Managing your stamina, known as "Character Pixels," is vital for steady progression. Early in the game (Hunter Level 1-30), your priority should not be farming perfect gear.

**Early Game (HL 1-30):**
Focus on clearing the "Rabbit Hole" challenges to gain basic materials and unlock exploration milestones. Do not spend excessive Character Pixels farming gold-rarity Consoles until you reach the Level 8 Exploration Breakpoint. Before this point, the drop rates for high-tier gear are too low to justify the cost.

**Mid-to-Late Game:**
Once you hit the Level 8 Breakpoint, transition your focus to Houdini's Magic Stage Anomaly Zone. This is where you will find the most potent Cartridge sets.

**Early-Game "Force Multiplier" Gear Sets:**
*   **Speedy Hedgehog:** Provides a massive boost to movement speed and initial burst damage, perfect for clearing overworld mobs quickly.
*   **Lost Radiance:** Focuses on energy regeneration, allowing your Espers to use their ultimates more frequently during boss encounters.

### Sub-stat Transparency and Optimization

One of the most player-friendly aspects of the NTE Disk system is sub-stat transparency. When a Module drops, all of its sub-stats are immediately visible. You do not need to level it up to "reveal" what stats it has.

**Sub-stat Refinement:**
If you find a Module with the perfect shape and set but mediocre stats, you can use "Manhole" materials to refine them. Refinement allows you to reroll or slightly increase the values of existing sub-stats. However, Manhole materials are scarce, so they should be reserved for Type III gold modules with at least two desirable stats (e.g., Crit Damage and ATK%).

**Optimization Priority:**
1.  **Set Bonus:** Always aim for at least the 2-piece Epic Effect.
2.  **Shape Synergy:** Ensure the modules satisfy character-specific passives (like Nanally's Type II requirement).
3.  **Sub-stats:** Fine-tune with Manhole materials only after the first two conditions are met.

## Related

![Related](/img/neverness-to-everness-disk-system-guide/inline-4.webp)


The Disk system does not exist in a vacuum; it interacts with several other core game mechanics that you should be aware of as you progress through Hethereau.

### Esper Cycle and Elemental Reactions
Your gear stats directly influence the "Esper Cycle." This is the rhythm of swapping between characters to trigger elemental reactions. Certain Cartridge sets provide bonuses to "Swap-skill Cooldown Reduction," which is essential for teams relying on rapid-fire reactions. If your gear doesn't include enough Energy Regen or Cooldown Reduction, your rotation will feel sluggish.

### City Tycoon System
Upon reaching Level 16, players unlock the "City Tycoon" system. This is a management mini-game that, when leveled up, provides a permanent buff that can double your stamina efficiency for gear farming. It is highly recommended to prioritize reaching Level 16 before doing heavy "refinement" farming, as the Beetle Coin discounts and material bonuses from City Tycoon are substantial.

### Scarborough Fair Gacha and the Dice Board
When pulling for Arc Disks in the Scarborough Fair, pay close attention to the "Dice Board." This mechanic allows you to set a "Path" toward specific S-Rank Arc Disks. If you are building Nanally, you should target her signature Arc Disk, undisclosed, to maximize her synergy with the Type II modules mentioned earlier.

### Character Synergies and Arc Pairings
Choosing the right Arc for the right Esper is crucial:
*   **Nanally:** Best paired with high-crit Gas-type Arcs. Her banner, "The Ichi-daime," usually features Arcs that complement her fast-paced playstyle.
*   **[Sakiri](/characters/sakiri-nte-free-5-star-guide):** Often utilizes Plasma-type Arcs to enhance her elemental spread capabilities. undisclosed remains her best-in-slot weapon for general play.
*   **Lacrimosa:** As a more utility-focused Esper, she thrives with Liquid-type Arcs that provide team-wide buffs or increased healing potency.

By balancing the raw power of Arc Disks with the spatial puzzle of the Console grid, you can create a highly optimized team capable of tackling the toughest challenges Neverness to Everness has to offer. Focus on reaching the Level 8 Exploration Breakpoint before committing heavily to refinement, and always keep an eye on your character-specific module shape requirements.

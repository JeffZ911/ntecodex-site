# Neverness to Everness (NTE) Weapon Pity System Explained

The weapon acquisition system in Neverness to Everness (NTE) is distinct from its character recruitment counterpart, offering a higher base rate for top-tier items but introducing a unique "25/75" probability check. Known as the Arc Research Program, this system is the primary method for obtaining S-Rank Arcs—the powerful weapons that define a character's build and potential. Unlike many contemporary urban RPGs, NTE employs specific restrictions on how players can spend their currency on weapons, making strategic planning essential for F2P and low-spending players alike.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-weapon-pity-system-explained/inline-2.webp)


The NTE weapon pity system, officially titled the Arc Research Program, guarantees an S-Rank Arc every 60 pulls (equivalent to 6 multi-pulls). However, obtaining an S-Rank does not immediately guarantee the featured limited weapon. Instead, there is a 25% chance that the S-Rank Arc obtained at the 60-pull mark is the featured limited weapon.

If you do not receive the featured weapon at the 60-pull pity (the "25/75" loss), the game's internal tracking ensures that the featured limited Arc is 100% guaranteed by the 80th pull (8 multi-pulls total). A critical distinction in the Arc Research Program is the 10-pull restriction: weapon banners in NTE only allow multi-pulls, meaning single pulls are not an option for Arcs.

Pity progress is permanent and carries over to future Arc Research Program rotations. If you are halfway to a guarantee when a banner expires, your progress will remain intact for the next featured weapon banner.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-weapon-pity-system-explained/inline-3.webp)


The Arc Research Program is designed to be more accessible in terms of raw drop rates compared to the character banners, but it requires a higher volume of pulls to navigate the probability checks. To master this system, you must understand the relationship between Arcs, the currency required to obtain them, and the specific math behind the 25/75 mechanic.

### Understanding Arcs
In the world of Hethereau, Arcs serve as the primary equippable weapons. These are not merely stat sticks; they provide significant passive abilities that can fundamentally change how a character interacts with the game's "Anomaly" mechanics. Arcs are categorized by rarity:
*   **S-Rank:** The highest tier, often featuring unique visual effects and character-specific synergies.
*   **A-Rank:** Mid-tier weapons that provide solid stat boosts and are guaranteed every 10 pulls.
*   **B-Rank:** Common weapons primarily used as enhancement material or temporary placeholders.

### The 10-Pull Restriction
One of the most discussed aspects of the Arc Research Program is the inability to perform single pulls. Unlike the character banner (Scarborough Fair), which allows players to spend single Solid Dice, the weapon banner requires Tri-Keys and only permits pulls in batches of ten. This means you must always have at least 1,600 Annulith (or 10 Tri-Keys) to interact with the banner. This restriction prevents "pity sniping," where a player might do single pulls once they are close to 60 to save currency. In NTE, you must commit to the full 10-pull block.

### S-Rank Rates and Probabilities
The base rate for an S-Rank Arc is 3%, which is significantly higher than the character base rate of 0.99%. This higher base rate means that players will often see S-Rank weapons appearing before they ever hit the 60-pull pity threshold. While this sounds strictly beneficial, the "25/75" mechanic is the balancing factor.

### The 25/75 Mechanic Explained
When you trigger an S-Rank drop—whether through raw luck or by hitting the 60-pull pity—the game performs a "featured check." In the Arc Research Program, you have a 25% chance for the S-Rank to be the featured limited Arc and a 75% chance for it to be a standard S-Rank Arc from the permanent pool.

This is a departure from the 50/50 systems found in other popular gacha titles. Because the base rate is higher (3%), the developers implemented a lower initial "win" rate for the featured item.

### The 80-Pull Absolute Guarantee
To protect players from extreme streaks of bad luck, NTE includes an "Absolute Guarantee." If the 25% check fails at the 60-pull mark (or at any point you pull a non-featured S-Rank), the game tracks this loss. Your next S-Rank is then guaranteed to be the featured limited Arc. Furthermore, the game caps the total number of pulls to reach this guarantee at 80. 

Essentially, even if you are incredibly unlucky, you will never spend more than 80 pulls (12,800 Annulith) to get the specific limited weapon you want.

### Currency Breakdown: Tri-Keys and Annulith
Tri-Keys are the exclusive currency for the Arc Research Program. While you can occasionally earn them through gameplay, most players will convert Annulith into Tri-Keys.
*   **1 Tri-Key:** 160 Annulith
*   **10-Pull (1 Multi):** 1,600 Annulith
*   **S-Rank Pity (60 Pulls):** 9,600 Annulith
*   **Full Guarantee (80 Pulls):** 12,800 Annulith

### A-Rank Pity
Just like the character banners, you are guaranteed at least one A-Rank item every 10 pulls. In the Arc Research Program, this can be an A-Rank Arc or an A-Rank character. This ensures that even if you don't hit the 3% S-Rank rate, you are still receiving "Purple" rarity items to strengthen your account or convert into shop currency.

### How Pity Carryover Works for Arcs
Pity progress in NTE is user-friendly. If a banner ends while you are at 40/60 pity, your progress does not reset. When the next limited weapon banner arrives (for example, moving from [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)'s weapon to a future character's weapon), you will start at 40/60. 

The "guaranteed featured" status also carries over. If you pulled on a banner, hit an S-Rank, but "lost" the 25% check and received a standard weapon, your very next S-Rank on *any* future limited weapon banner is guaranteed to be the featured one.

## Related

![Related](/img/nte-weapon-pity-system-explained/inline-4.webp)


Beyond the Arc Research Program, Neverness to Everness features several other gacha and progression systems that interact with your resource management.

### NTE Character Pity and Board Mechanics
The character banner, Scarborough Fair, operates on a much simpler logic than the weapon banner. It features a 90-pull hard pity with no 50/50 check. When you hit an S-Rank on a limited character banner, it is always the featured unit. This makes character acquisition very predictable. Additionally, the "Board" mechanics allow for further character enhancement through duplicates, though the base pity remains focused on the initial 90-pull threshold.

### How to Get More Tri-Keys
While Annulith is the primary source, savvy players can acquire Tri-Keys through various in-game exchanges.
*   **Fair Exchange:** Use the currency gained from pulling duplicates (Lost Pieces) to buy a limited number of Tri-Keys each month.
*   **Hunter Exchange:** Spend Warp Pieces earned from missions and world activities to supplement your Tri-Key stash.
*   **Urban Leveling:** Progressing your city reputation and completing "Anomaly" investigations often rewards Tri-Keys at specific milestones.

### Cosmetic Pity and Milestone Rewards
NTE is unique in that it separates cosmetic items from the main character and weapon pity tracks. Limited banners often feature separate side-pity systems for high-end cosmetics:
*   **Gliders:** Guaranteed at 50 pulls on specific banners.
*   **Vehicles:** Guaranteed at 120 pulls.
*   **Character Skins:** Guaranteed at 200 pulls.

These tracks do not interfere with your Arc Research Program pity, allowing you to build progress toward a weapon while potentially sniping a vehicle or skin if you are pulling on a consolidated "Celebration" banner.

### Standard Banner (Strange Encounters)
The permanent banner, Strange Encounters, is where you will spend your standard Tri-Keys. This banner features a one-time beginner selector at 50 pulls, allowing you to choose a specific standard S-Rank Arc to kickstart your account. It also offers a 20% discount on the first five multi-pulls, meaning you only spend 8 Tri-Keys for every 10 items received until the discount is exhausted.

By understanding that the weapon system favors higher drop rates (3%) at the cost of a lower initial featured guarantee (25%), you can better manage your Annulith. Always ensure you have at least 12,800 Annulith if you want to be absolutely certain of walking away with a featured S-Rank Arc.

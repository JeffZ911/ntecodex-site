### Understanding the Arc System in NTE
Arcs are not merely stat sticks; they are specialized equipment that must align with an Esper’s specific Archetype. In NTE, every character is categorized into one of five matter-based types: Solid, Liquid, Gas, Plasma, or Synthesis. An Esper can only equip an Arc that matches their internal type. For example, a Plasma-type character like [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) can only utilize Plasma Arcs. This system forces players to build a diverse collection of weapons rather than relying on a single "universal" blade or gun.

### The Importance of S-Rank Weapon Stats
S-Rank Arcs represent the pinnacle of gear in NTE. While A-Rank (4-star) and B-Rank (3-star) Arcs are useful for early progression, S-Rank versions offer significantly higher Base ATK scaling and more potent substats. More importantly, S-Rank Arcs feature unique "Arc Skills"—transformative passive abilities that can introduce new mechanics into a character's kit, such as summoning combat companions, creating energy-restoring zones, or granting massive damage multipliers against specific enemy types like world bosses.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-best-5-star-weapon-stats-guide/inline-2.webp)


The best 5-star weapons in NTE are currently **Ready-Ready** (Plasma), **Marching Beyond Time** (Solid), and **Camellia Society** (Synthesis). These Arcs are considered "Best-in-Slot" for the game's top DPS units due to their high Base ATK and synergistic passive effects.

Top-tier S-Rank Arcs typically feature a Base ATK ranging from 512 to 666 at maximum level (Level 80). They are paired with powerful substats such as CRIT Rate (up to 24%), CRIT DMG (up to 24% or higher via passives), or ATK% (up to 27.5%). Signature Arcs provide transformative 'Arc Skills'—for instance, Ready-Ready significantly boosts Basic Attack damage and provides a massive multiplier against Bosses through the Tiger Talisman mechanic.

These weapons are primarily obtained through the **Arc Research Program** banner using a currency called Tri-Keys. The gacha system features a 60-pull soft pity for any S-Rank Arc and an 80-pull hard pity to guarantee the featured limited weapon.

When evaluating an S-Rank Arc, look for two primary scaling models. The "High ATK" model, exemplified by Camellia Society and Ready-Ready, reaches a Base ATK of 666. For most DPS Espers, the CRIT Rate substat is often more valuable than the raw ATK difference, as it allows for more flexible "Cartridge" (artifact) builds.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-best-5-star-weapon-stats-guide/inline-3.webp)


### S-Rank Plasma Arcs: Offensive Powerhouses
Plasma Arcs are designed for aggressive, frontline damage dealers. **Ready-Ready** stands as the current gold standard for this category, serving as the signature weapon for Nanally. 

*   **Stats and Scaling:** At Level 80, it provides a massive 666 Base ATK and a 15% ATK boost (at T1 refinement).
*   **The Tiger Talisman Mechanic:** This weapon introduces the Left and Right Tiger Talismans. Casting a Redirect Skill grants the Left Talisman, while an Ultimate grants the Right. Combining both within 15 seconds unlocks the "Commander Tiger Talisman," which increases damage to Bosses by up to 20% at maximum refinement.
*   **Combat Synergy:** Because it also increases Basic Attack and Critical Riposte damage by 15% (stacking up to 2 times), it is the perfect match for characters who rely on sustained melee combos.

Other notable Plasma options include **Raging Flames** and **Stellar Veil**, which focus on elemental-specific damage bonuses for Lakshana and Psyche, respectively.

### S-Rank Solid Arcs: Balanced and Burst Damage
Solid Arcs generally offer the most versatile stat spreads in NTE. **Marching Beyond Time**, the signature weapon for [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte), is the premier choice for Cosmos-element burst dealers.

*   **The Wastelab Effect:** This Arc rewards strategic skill rotations. Using a Redirect Skill enters the "Wastelab" state. While active, allies generate "Wastetime" stacks when they use skills. When the wearer eventually uses their Ultimate, they consume these stacks to gain a 24% Ultimate CRIT DMG boost, with an additional 8% per stack.
*   **DEF Penetration:** If you consume the maximum of three stacks, the weapon grants 12% DEF Ignore, allowing your Ultimate to shred through armored elite enemies.

For players who prefer utility, **Day Off** is a top-tier Solid Arc. It provides a flat 30% ATK increase and features the "Eclipse" Arc Skill, which creates a 40-second zone where every enemy defeated restores 6 to 10 Ultimate Energy. This is essential for maintaining "Ultimate uptime" during wave-based encounters like the Pink Paw Heist.

### S-Rank Synthesis Arcs: High-Risk, High-Reward Mechanics
Synthesis Arcs often feature complex triggers that require specific playstyles. **Camellia Society** is the most prominent example, often used as the Best-in-Slot for Baicang.

*   **Risk Management:** The passive effect, "Silent Garden," increases CRIT DMG by 12% for 5 seconds whenever the wearer's HP is reduced without taking damage (such as through self-sacrifice mechanics). This can stack up to 4 times for a 48% CRIT DMG boost.
*   **Active Burst:** The Arc Skill itself consumes 5% of the wearer's current HP to unleash one of two random attacks: "Harsh Rebuke" (multi-hit Incantation DMG) or "Incessant Nagging" (piercing damage with a double-damage final hit). This makes it a high-skill weapon that requires the player to manage their health carefully.

### S-Rank Gas and Liquid Arcs: Support and Energy Utility
Gas and Liquid Arcs typically lean toward team utility and energy management. 

*   **Good Boy's Grand Adventure (Gas):** This Arc is a staple for support Espers. It increases Charge Efficiency by 18% and provides a team-wide ATK buff of 10% for 20 seconds after the wearer casts an Ultimate. If the Ultimate also controls or crowd-collects enemies, the ATK buff increases by an additional 6%.
*   **Contemplative Cat (Gas):** An interesting niche weapon that increases Cosmos DMG based on the amount of "Fons" (currency) the player is currently holding, rewarding players who hoard their resources.
*   **Liquid Arcs:** These are often used by characters like Mint or Edgar. Many focus on "Redirect Skill" cooldown reduction or energy refunding, ensuring that the team's rotation remains fluid.

### How to Read Arc Stats: Base ATK vs. Substat Priority
In Neverness to Everness, the "Base ATK" of a weapon is added to the character's base stats before percentage modifiers from Cartridges are applied. This means a weapon with 666 Base ATK makes every "ATK +10%" bonus on your gear significantly more effective than a weapon with 512 Base ATK.

However, the community consensus is that **CRIT Rate substats** generally outweigh raw ATK in the endgame. Since CRIT Rate is harder to find on Cartridges, an Arc like Marching Beyond Time (24% CRIT Rate) allows you to focus your other gear entirely on CRIT DMG and Elemental DMG, leading to a higher total damage output.

### The Arc Research Program: Pity and Pull Rates Explained
Acquiring S-Rank Arcs requires navigating the "Arc Research Program" gacha. 

*   **Base Rate:** The base chance to pull an S-Rank Arc is 3%, which is relatively high for the genre.
*   **Soft Pity:** If you have not pulled an S-Rank by pull 50, the rates begin to climb, with a guarantee at 60 pulls.
*   **Guaranteed Featured:** When you pull an S-Rank, there is a 25% chance it is the featured limited weapon. If you do not get the featured weapon, your **next** S-Rank is guaranteed to be the featured one, with a hard limit of 80 total pulls to secure the specific weapon you want.
*   **Currency:** Pulling requires **Tri-Keys**. One Tri-Key allows for a 10-pull (granting 10 Arcs). These can be purchased for 1,600 Anulith or earned through the Fair Exchange and Lost Exchange shops.

### Best 4-Star Alternatives for Every Type
For F2P players or those who haven't lucked into a signature S-Rank, several A-Rank Arcs perform admirably at high refinement (T5)

*   **Umbrella (Synthesis):** A fantastic defensive option that provides HP and shields.
*   **Shiny Days (Plasma):** Provides a reliable ATK boost and is a great placeholder for Nanally.
*   **The Forgotten (Solid):** Offers solid DMG bonuses and is easily obtainable through early-game missions.
*   **Song of the Whale:** A unique S-Rank that is actually farmable for free by defeating world bosses. It grants 20% ATK and heals the user upon defeating "Broken" enemies.

### Arc Refinement: Is it Worth the Cost?
Refinement in NTE is called "Mixing." By consuming a duplicate of the same Arc, you can increase its rank from T1 to T5. This does not increase the base stats (ATK/CRIT), but it significantly enhances the passive effect. For example, the ATK boost on Ready-Ready increases from 15% at T1 to 30% at T5. For most F2P players, it is better to keep two separate S-Rank Arcs for different characters rather than mixing them, unless you have a surplus of Tri-Keys.

### How to Farm Tri-Keys for the Weapon Banner
Tri-Keys are the lifeblood of the Arc Research Program. While you can buy them with Anulith (the premium currency), you can also farm them through
1.  **Fair Exchange Shop:** Use the currency earned from pulling on character banners to buy limited Tri-Keys each month.
2.  **Hunter Exchange:** As you increase your Hunter Level by completing commissions and world activities, you unlock the ability to purchase Tri-Keys using Fons (standard in-game gold).
3.  **City Tycoon:** Progressing through the urban management simulation mode provides milestone rewards, including Tri-Keys and even free S-Rank Arcs like those for the character Chiz.

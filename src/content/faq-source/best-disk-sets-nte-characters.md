### What are the best Disk Sets for characters in Neverness to Everness?
In the community and across early guides, players often refer to "Disk Sets" when discussing character equipment. However, in the official Neverness to Everness (NTE) interface, these are known as **Cartridges**. These items function as the primary gear system, providing both flat stat increases and powerful set bonuses when equipped in specific combinations.

### Understanding the Equipment System
The NTE equipment system is divided into three distinct layers
1.  **The Console:** The base interface where equipment is slotted. Each character has a unique Console grid (typically 5x5 or 6x6) that unlocks as they level up.
2.  **Modules:** These are the individual "Tetris-like" pieces you place onto the Console. Each Module has a shape (I, L, T, O, S, or Z) and belongs to a specific Type (I, II, III, or IV).
3.  **Cartridges (Disk Sets):** When you fill a Module with a specific Cartridge "data," you contribute toward a set bonus. Activating these bonuses requires equipping either 2 or 4 Modules of the same Cartridge set.

### Which sets are currently dominating the May 2026 meta?
As of May 22, 2026, the meta has stabilized around sets that prioritize **Crit Damage**, **Defense Ignore**, and **Cycle Intensity**. Because NTE emphasizes the "Esper Cycle"—a mechanic where switching characters and triggering elemental reactions is key—sets that provide team-wide buffs or faster Ultimate energy generation are currently seeing the highest usage in endgame content like the Anomaly Investigations.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/best-disk-sets-nte-characters/inline-3.webp)


The depth of the NTE build system lies in the interaction between the Console grid and the Module shapes. Simply having the right set isn't enough; you must optimize the "Tetris" placement to fit the highest-rarity Modules into your character's Console.

### The Console and Module Tetris System
Each character's Console has a limited amount of space. Modules come in various shapes, and high-tier Modules (Gold/S-Rank) often have awkward dimensions that require careful planning.
*   **Module Type I:** Usually focuses on flat ATK and HP.
*   **Module Type II:** The most sought-after type for DPS characters, as these can roll **Crit Rate** and **Crit DMG** as primary stats.
*   **Module Type III:** Focuses on **Elemental Damage Bonus** (e.g., Anima DMG%, Cosmos DMG%).
*   **Module Type IV:** Focuses on **Cycle Intensity** and **Resonance**.

To activate a 4-piece set bonus, you must slot four Modules of that set into the Console. The challenge is fitting four Type II or Type III Modules into the grid while leaving room for the necessary "filler" pieces that provide sub-stats like ATK% or Flat Defense.

### Anima Powerhouse: Fireflies and the Forest
This set is designed for characters with high attack frequency.
*   **2-Piece Bonus:** Increases Anima DMG by 15%.
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** When an Anima attack hits an enemy, the character gains a "Firefly" stack. Each stack increases Crit DMG by 8%, stacking up to 7 times (56% total Crit DMG).
This set is tailor-made for **Nanally**. Her rapid-fire pistol shots can reach maximum stacks in less than three seconds, making her the highest burst-damage dealer in the current version of the game.

### Support Meta: Speedy Hedgehog and Team Utility
Speedy Hedgehog is the backbone of the "Quick-Swap" meta.
*   **2-Piece Bonus:** Increases Cycle Intensity by 20%.
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** After casting an Ultimate (Esper Skill), the entire team receives a 20% ATK increase for 12 seconds.
This effect does not stack with itself, so you should only run one Speedy Hedgehog set per team. Usually, this is placed on **Sakiri** or **Haniel**, who can cycle their Ultimates frequently to maintain 100% uptime on the ATK buff.

### Cosmos and Chaos: Specialized Elemental Sets
**Lost Radiance** is the premier choice for **Hotori**.
*   **2-Piece Bonus:** Increases Cosmos DMG by 15%.
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** Attacks ignore 25% of the target's Defense.
Because Hotori’s kit already includes built-in resistance shred, the Defense Ignore from Lost Radiance creates a multiplicative damage effect that allows her to "melt" boss health bars regardless of their level.

**Diabolos** is the primary set for **Daffodil**.
*   **2-Piece Bonus:** Increases Chaos DMG by 15%.
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** Increases Break Efficiency by 30%. When an enemy is in a "Broken" state, the wearer's Chaos DMG is further increased by 20%.

### Optimizing Sub-stats: Crit, ATK, and Cycle Intensity
When farming Modules, the primary stat is fixed based on the Module Type, but the sub-stats are randomized. For a perfect endgame build, you should look for the following priority
1.  **Crit Rate / Crit DMG:** Essential for all DPS characters.
2.  **Cycle Intensity:** The most important stat for supports and sub-DPS characters to keep the Esper Cycle moving.
3.  **ATK%:** A universal scaling stat.
4.  **Resonance:** Increases the damage of elemental reactions triggered during character swaps.

### Farming Strategy: Rewind and Karota Coins
Farming the perfect Module shape and stat combination is the main endgame loop in NTE. 
*   **Anomaly Investigations:** These are the primary source of high-tier Modules.
*   **The Rewind Feature:** If you find a Module with perfect sub-stats but the wrong "Tetris" shape (e.g., a Z-shape when you need an I-shape), you can use the Rewind feature. This costs **Karota Coins**, which are earned by dismantling lower-tier Modules.
*   **Target Farming:** Use the "Data Filtering" option at the Investigation Terminal to increase the drop rate of specific sets like Fireflies and the Forest or Lost Radiance.

Building a powerful roster in Neverness to Everness (NTE) requires a strategic approach to resource management and a clear understanding of the city’s complex progression systems. Since the game’s release on April 29, 2026, many players have found that power scaling is gated not just by character levels, but by account-wide milestones and a unique gear system.

# Neverness to Everness (NTE) Character Progression Guide for Beginners

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/nte-character-progression-guide-beginners-20260522/inline-1.webp)


What are the primary ways to increase character power in Neverness to Everness?  
How do Hunter Level and Appraisal Level affect Esper progression?  
What materials are needed for character leveling and where can they be farmed?  
How does the unique "Tetris" gear system (Consoles and Modules) work?

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/nte-character-progression-guide-beginners-20260522/inline-2.webp)


Character progression in NTE is a multi-layered system involving Esper Leveling, Arc (Weapon) Enhancement, and the Console (Gear) system. Your account's Hunter Level and Appraisal Level act as the primary gates for all character growth, determining the maximum level your Espers and Arcs can reach. 

To build your team efficiently, focus on leveling your main DPS to the current cap first, followed by upgrading their Arc and fitting high-stat Modules into their Console grid. You should prioritize "guaranteed" upgrades like levels and abilities before engaging with high-RNG systems. Avoid farming high-end gear (Consoles) until Hunter Level 30, when Legendary (Orange) rarity becomes available in the Rabbit Hole Anomaly Zone.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/nte-character-progression-guide-beginners-20260522/inline-3.webp)


The core progression loop in Neverness to Everness revolves around spending Character Pixels (the game's primary stamina resource) in Anomaly Zones to gain EXP and materials. These activities increase your Hunter Level, which eventually unlocks Appraisal Level breakthroughs. Without raising your Appraisal Level, your characters will hit "soft caps" that prevent further stat increases.

### Understanding Hunter Level vs. Appraisal Level

Hunter Level represents your overall account progress, earned by completing story episodes, side quests, and spending Character Pixels. Appraisal Level, however, functions as the world difficulty and reward tier system. Every 10 Hunter Levels, you will typically encounter a breakthrough point that requires an "Appraisal Ascend" quest to raise the level cap for your entire roster.

| Hunter Level | Appraisal Level | Esper Max Level | Key Unlocks |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 10 | 1 | 20 | Ascension system unlocked |
| 20 | 2 | 40 | Ascension Quest: Part 1 |
| 30 | 3 | 40 | S-Rank Console farming starts |
| 40 | 4 | 50 | Ascension Quest: Part 2 |
| 45 | 5 | 60 | [Information not yet publicly available as of 2026-05-22] |
| 50 | 6 | 70 | Difficulty V Anomaly Zones |

### Esper Leveling and Ascension Mechanics

To increase an Esper's level, you must consume Hunter Guides and Beetle Coins in the "Develop" menu. Hunter Guides come in three tiers, providing varying amounts of experience.

*   **Rising Hunter Guide:** +1,000 character EXP.
*   **Senior Hunter Guide:** +5,000 character EXP.
*   **Elite Hunter Guide:** +20,000 character EXP.

Once an Esper reaches their current level cap (e.g., Level 20 at Appraisal Level 1), you must perform an Ascension to unlock the next 10 or 20 levels. Ascension requires specific materials farmed from Anomaly Hunts and world bosses. Spreading these materials across too many characters early on is a common mistake; focus on a core team of four to ensure you have enough resources for your primary damage dealers.

### The Arc System: Enhancement, Ascension, and Mixing

Arcs are the weapon system in NTE. They provide a base ATK stat and a unique passive effect. Upgrading an Arc involves three distinct processes

1.  **Enhancement:** Raising the Arc's level using materials called "Dye" (Light, Colorless, or Chaotic Dye) and Beetle Coins.
2.  **Ascension:** Breaking the level cap of an Arc. This requires materials like Iron Appleseed, Silver Appleseed, or Golden Appleseed, along with Drama Cores (Beginner, Master, or Collector’s). Arcs can reach a maximum level of 80.
3.  **Mixing:** A duplicate upgrade system (T1 to T5). By consuming duplicate copies of the same Arc, you increase the "Mixing Intensity," which significantly strengthens the weapon's passive ability.

### The Console System: Solving the 'Tetris' Gear Puzzle

The Console system is NTE's alternative to traditional RPG equipment or "artifacts." Each character has a Console grid with a unique layout where you must fit geometric shapes called Modules.

*   **Modules:** These are Tetris-like blocks (Type II, III, and IV) that provide stat boosts. Type II modules occupy two squares, while Type IV modules occupy four. 
*   **Cartridges:** These are the "core" pieces of the Console. The Cartridge you equip determines the set bonuses available to that character.
*   **Set Bonuses:** To activate a Cartridge's 2-piece or 4-piece bonus, you must fit Modules of specific shapes or types into the grid.

Unlike many other gacha games, the sub-stats on Modules are previewable before you spend materials to level them up to their maximum of +20. This allows you to plan your build with precision. To obtain new Modules, you must use the "Rewind" gacha located within the Console menu, which consumes Carrota coins (Bronze, Silver, or Gold).

### Upgrading Esper Abilities and Awakenings

Beyond raw stats, you can enhance an Esper's combat performance through Ability upgrades and Awakenings.

*   **Esper Abilities:** These are the skills used in the "Esper Cycle" rotation. Upgrading them requires materials found in Houdinii's Schemes, such as Nestling's Longing or Resonance of Faith.
*   **Awakenings:** These are unlocked by obtaining duplicate copies of a character. Each Awakening tier provides massive utility boosts, such as reduced cooldowns or increased elemental damage. For example, [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte)'s early Awakenings significantly improve her vertical exploration capabilities and DPS.

### Stamina Management: Where to Spend Character Pixels

Character Pixels are your most valuable resource for progression. They regenerate at a rate of 1 Pixel every 6 minutes, with a cap of 240. Because every reward claim in an Anomaly Zone also grants Hunter Level EXP, you should never let your Pixels remain capped.

| Anomaly Zone | Primary Farmed Material | Location |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Houdinii's Magic Stage** | Hunter Guides (Character EXP), Dyes (Arc EXP) | Bridge Crossings, southeast of Green Wall Slope |
| **Bubble Can Factory** | Appleseed and Drama Cores (Arc Ascension) | New Herland District, under Iris Bridge |
| **Houdinii's Schemes** | Ability Upgrade Materials (Resonance, Faith) | Miguel District, near St. Torres Avenue |
| **Rabbit Hole** | Cartridges and Carrota (Console/Modules) | Cape Square, New Herland (Skyscraper roof) |

### City Tycoon: The Passive Progression Engine

While Character Pixels govern combat growth, the City Tycoon system manages your economic growth. This "life-sim" mode uses a separate weekly stamina pool and is essential for generating "Fons," the currency used for high-level upgrades and purchasing items from the Hunter Exchange.

Key City Tycoon milestones include
*   **Level 4:** Unlocks the "Cafe by Origen," your primary source of passive Fon generation.
*   **Level 11:** Unlocks the "Pink Paws Bank Heist," providing a major Fon injection every two weeks at zero stamina cost.
*   **Level 16:** A massive efficiency milestone where city stamina costs and rewards are increased by 100%, effectively doubling your progression speed.
*   **Level 18:** Grants the S-Rank character Chiz for free. Chiz is invaluable for progression because her passive generates Fons whenever she defeats enemies in the open world.

### The Esper Cycle: Elemental Adjacency

Progression is not just about stats; it is also about mastering the "Esper Cycle." There are six elements in NTE: Lakshana, Cosmos, Anima, Incantation, Chaos, and Psyche. These elements are arranged on a hexagonal wheel. 

Reactions only trigger when you swap between "adjacent" elements on the wheel. For example, swapping from a Cosmos character to an Anima character triggers a reaction because they are neighbors. Swapping from Cosmos to Chaos (opposite sides) will not trigger a reaction. Mastering these "Duo" and "Triple" cycles is necessary for clearing high-level Anomaly Pilgrimages where enemies have massive health pools.

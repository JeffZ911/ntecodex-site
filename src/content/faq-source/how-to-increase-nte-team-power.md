# How to Increase Team Power in Neverness to Everness

## Question Restated

![Question Restated](/img/how-to-increase-nte-team-power/inline-1.webp)


Understanding the mechanics behind team strength is the difference between clearing high-level Anomaly Zones and hitting a progression wall. Players often ask: what are the primary ways to increase combat power in Neverness to Everness? While leveling up characters is the most visible method, it is only one piece of a much larger puzzle.

Total team strength is a calculation derived from three main pillars: Esper progression, Arc (weapon) efficiency, and the spatial optimization of the Console system. To reach the endgame, you must understand how character levels and breakthroughs interact with your Hunter Level. Furthermore, the gear system—comprising Cartridges and Tetris-shaped Modules—provides the bulk of your late-game stat growth. Finally, power isn't just about raw numbers; it is about how your team synergy facilitates the Esper Cycle, allowing you to trigger reactions like Discord and Blossom to bypass enemy defenses.

## Direct Answer

![Direct Answer](/img/how-to-increase-nte-team-power/inline-2.webp)


Increasing team power in Neverness to Everness (NTE) requires a multi-layered approach that balances individual character growth with account-wide progression. The most immediate way to boost power is by leveling your Espers using Hunter's Manuals and performing Breakthroughs to raise their level caps. Simultaneously, you must equip and enhance Arcs (the game's weapon system) using Dye and Bubble Cans, while "Mixing" duplicate Arcs to maximize their unique passive abilities.

For significant stat spikes, you must engage with the Console system. This involves equipping Cartridges and strategically placing Tetris-like Modules onto a grid to activate set bonuses and preferred style stats. True combat efficiency is then achieved by mastering the Esper Cycle, which involves matching Esper elements (such as Cosmos, Anima, and Incantation) to trigger high-damage reactions. All of these systems are ultimately gated by your Hunter Level and Appraisal Level, which dictate the maximum potential of your roster.

## Detailed Explanation

![Detailed Explanation](/img/how-to-increase-nte-team-power/inline-3.webp)


The progression architecture in Neverness to Everness is designed to prevent players from over-leveling a single character while neglecting their account rank. To efficiently scale your power, you must advance through the following systems in parallel.

### Esper Leveling and Breakthroughs

The foundation of your power lies in your Espers. Each Esper has base stats that increase linearly with their level. To level up, you consume Hunter's Manuals, which come in various tiers providing different amounts of experience. However, leveling is not infinite; it is halted by Breakthrough points (often referred to as Ascension).

Breakthroughs require specific materials gathered from regional bosses located throughout Hethereau and the surrounding urban districts. Performing a Breakthrough not only raises the level cap but also provides a significant jump in base Attack (ATK), Health (HP), and Defense (DEF).

| Hunter's Manual (Advanced) | Leveling XP | Anomaly Zone Rewards |
| Shell Credits | Currency | General Gameplay / Events |

Beyond base levels, you must invest in Skill Upgrades. Each Esper has a talent tree that enhances their Active Skills, Ultimate, and Passive traits. Investing in these talents increases the damage multipliers of your abilities, which is often more impactful than raw stat increases in the late game.

### Optimizing Arcs (Weapons) and Mixing

Arcs serve as the primary weapons for your Espers, providing both a massive boost to base ATK and a secondary stat (such as Critical Rate or Energy Recharge). Upgrading an Arc involves two processes: Enhancement and Mixing.

Enhancement requires Dye (or Pigments) to increase the Arc's level. Similar to Espers, Arcs have level caps that must be bypassed using Bubble Cans. These materials are primarily farmed in specialized combat challenges or purchased with Beetle Coins.

Mixing is the refinement process. If you obtain a duplicate of an Arc, you can "Mix" it into your primary version. This can be done up to five times (T5). Each level of Mixing significantly enhances the Arc's unique passive effect. For example, a T1 Arc might provide a 10% damage boost after using a skill, while a T5 version of that same Arc could increase that boost to 25% and add a secondary utility effect.

### The Console System: Cartridges and Modules

The Console system is the most complex gear mechanic in Neverness to Everness and offers the highest ceiling for power customization. Think of the Console as a motherboard where you install Cartridges and Modules.

1.  **Cartridges:** These are the "base" gear pieces that define the main stats for your Esper. Each Cartridge has a specific type and slot.
2.  **Modules:** These are Tetris-shaped pieces that you must fit into the grid provided by the Cartridge. Each Module has its own stats and belongs to a specific Type (I, II, III, or IV).
3.  **Spatial Optimization:** You cannot simply equip the best Modules; you must fit them into the grid. This requires strategic planning to ensure you are maximizing the available space while activating "Preferred Style" bonuses. If a Module's type matches an Esper’s preferred style, they receive an additional percentage-based stat bonus.

To optimize your Console, you will eventually use the Rewind System. By spending Carrota Coins in the Rewind menu, you can re-roll the sub-stats on high-tier Modules. This is the primary method for "min-maxing" your team power once you have reached the level cap.

### Mastering Esper Cycles and Team Synergy

Raw stats can only take you so far if you do not understand the Esper Cycle. Combat in NTE is built around six elements: Cosmos, Anima, Incantation, Chaos, Psyche, and Lakshana. When you use abilities from different elements in quick succession, you trigger elemental reactions that deal massive bonus damage or provide crowd control.

| Reaction Name | Elements Required | Primary Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blossom** | Anima + Cosmos | High damage over time (DoT) and AOE explosion. |
| **Discord** | Incantation + Chaos + Psyche | Massive shield-breaking damage and stun. |
| — | Chaos + Lakshana | — |
| — | Psyche + Cosmos | — |

A team with lower raw power but high synergy will often outperform a "whale" team with no elemental cohesion. For example, triggering Discord is essential for late-game bosses that regenerate shields rapidly. If your team cannot [trigger Discord](https://ntecodex.com/faq), your numerical Team Power becomes irrelevant against those specific mechanics.

### Hunter Level and Appraisal Level Gating

Your progression is strictly tied to your account rank, known as the Hunter Level. Almost every power-up mechanic is gated by this level:
*   **Esper Level Caps:** You cannot raise an Esper to level 50 if your Hunter Level is too low.
*   **Arc Level Caps:** Arcs are similarly restricted.
*   **Appraisal Level:** Every 10 Hunter Levels, you undergo an Appraisal. Increasing your Appraisal Level is the only way to unlock higher-tier Breakthroughs and access the most difficult Anomaly Zones, which drop the highest-quality Modules and Cartridges.

If you find yourself unable to increase your team power, check your Hunter Level. You may need to complete side quests, urban activities, or daily tasks to gain the account experience necessary to unlock the next tier of progression.

### Understanding the Arc and Disk System

In Neverness to Everness, "Disks" are the physical, equippable form of Arcs, which serve as the game’s primary weapon system. Unlike traditional RPGs where weapons might be swords or staves, Arcs are supernatural objects that resonate with an Esper’s power. Every Arc has three core components:
1.  **Base ATK:** This scales with the Arc's level and provides the foundation for your character's damage.
2.  **Substat:** A secondary stat such as CRIT Rate, ATK%, HP%, or Break Intensity.
3.  **Arc Skill:** A unique passive ability that can be enhanced through the "Mixing" system.

3-star (B-rarity) Arcs are the most common drops from the undisclosed gacha and are frequently rewarded for completing urban commissions in Hethereau. While their base stats are lower than S-rank Arcs, they are much easier to upgrade to their maximum potential.

### Top 3-Star Disks for Damage Dealers (DPS)

For players focusing on raw damage output, **Oraora!** stands as the king of the B-rarity category. Its "Ora Puncher" effect is specifically designed for characters who spend a lot of time on the field performing Basic Attacks. At base level, each hit increases Basic Attack DMG by 2% for 10 seconds. Because these stacks are independent, a fast-attacking character like Nanally can easily maintain the maximum 10 stacks, resulting in a consistent 20% damage increase (rising to 36% at Max Mixing).

Another strong offensive contender is **The Forgotten**. This Solid-type Arc is unique because it provides a massive 20% ATK boost as long as you stay above half health. This makes it an ideal choice for "glass cannon" builds where you prioritize dodging and shielding to stay healthy. If you do take a hit and drop below 50% HP, the Arc automatically switches to a 20% DEF boost, providing a safety net that helps you survive until a healer can intervene.

### Best 3-Star Disks for Support and Sustain

Support characters in NTE often struggle with Energy regeneration or survivability in the early game. **Mind Royale** solves the energy problem by rewarding players for using Redirect Skills—the game's unique parry and counter mechanic. By restoring up to 18 Energy per trigger, Mind Royale allows supports to cycle their Ultimates much faster than they would with higher-rarity Arcs that lack energy-related passives.

For tankier characters or dedicated shielders, **Umbrella** is the gold standard for B-rarity Synthesis Arcs. Its passive effect, which strengthens shields by up to 18%, scales exceptionally well when paired with Espers who create shields based on their own DEF or HP stats. This Disk ensures that your team can withstand the heavy "Anomaly" attacks found in Hethereau’s high-level zones.

### The Importance of Arc Type Compatibility

One of the most critical mechanics in NTE is the Arc Type restriction. Characters are strictly categorized into one of five types: **Plasma, Solid, Gas, Liquid, or Synthesis**. You cannot equip a Plasma Arc on a Solid-type character. 

This restriction means that even if you have a Max-Mixed "Oraora!", you can only use it if you have a Plasma-type DPS on your team. This makes it vital to keep at least one high-quality 3-star Disk for every Arc Type. For example:
*   **Gas Type:** Look for **Be Happy**, which restores 12% to 20% HP upon defeating an enemy, making it great for mob clearing.
*   **Liquid Type:** **Shiny Days** is your go-to for breaking enemy stagger bars.
*   **Solid Type:** **Time Bandit** is excellent for utility, as it summons the companion "Picky" to help unlock objects in the open world while boosting Break Intensity.

### How to Maximize 3-Star Arcs via Mixing

The "Mixing" system is the process of consuming duplicate copies of an Arc to increase the power of its passive skill. In Neverness to Everness, an Arc can reach a maximum Mixing level of T5.

A Max-Mixed (T5) 3-star Arc will often outperform a base (T1) 4-star Arc. For instance, a T5 **Oraora!** provides a 36% Basic Attack DMG bonus, whereas many T1 A-rank Arcs only provide a 15% to 20% generic damage boost. Because 3-star Disks are so common, you should never settle for a T1 version; always aim to combine your duplicates to reach the maximum threshold as quickly as possible.

### Early Game Character Pairings

To get the most out of your 3-star Disks, pair them with the following starting characters:
*   **Nanally (Plasma):** Always equip **Oraora!**. Her rapid-fire "Ichi-daime" style attacks build stacks almost instantly.
*   **Zero (Solid):** Pair with **The Forgotten** or **Time Bandit**. Zero benefits from the ATK/DEF swap of The Forgotten, especially when using his self-healing passives to stay above the 50% HP threshold.
*   **Chiz (Gas):** Use **Be Happy** or **Watch Your Heads!** (if available). Chiz relies on high critical uptime, and the utility of Gas-type Arcs helps maintain her presence on the field.
*   **Adler (Synthesis):** **Umbrella** is his best early-game option, significantly increasing the durability of the shields he provides to the team.

### Transitioning to A-Rank Arcs

As you progress through the main story and increase your Appraisal Level, you will eventually start replacing your 3-star Disks with 4-star (A-rank) Arcs. Key A-rank Arcs to look for include **Reality Refuge** for Solid-type defenders and **Clear Skies** for Liquid-type supports. However, do not dismantle your Max-Mixed 3-star Arcs immediately. Keep them for your secondary and tertiary team members in the "Anomaly Investigation" endgame modes, where you are required to field multiple teams.

### The Console Gear System: Cartridges vs. Disks

It is important to distinguish between **Disks (Arcs)** and **Cartridges**. While Disks act as your weapon, the **Console Gear System** utilizes Cartridges and Modules to provide smaller, incremental stat boosts. 
*   **Disks/Arcs:** Provide the main passive skill and the largest stat chunk.
*   **Cartridges:** Function like armor or accessories, providing set bonuses (e.g., 2-piece or 4-piece effects).
*   **Modules:** Slot into your Console to further refine stats like CRIT Rate or Elemental DMG.

A common mistake for new players is focusing entirely on their Disk level while ignoring their Cartridges. For a balanced build, ensure your Cartridge set bonuses complement the passive effect of your 3-star Disk.

### Farming Materials for Arc Development

To level up your 3-star Disks, you will need a steady supply of **Beetle Coins** and **Elite Hunter Guides**. These can be farmed through:
*   **Daily Commissions:** The most reliable source of XP materials.
*   **[Scarborough Fair](https://ntecodex.com/faq) undisclosed:** Using undisclosed to pull for new Arcs often yields "fodder" Arcs that can be used to level up your primary Disks.
*   **Hethereau Field Bosses:** Defeating anomalies in the open world frequently drops Arc ascension materials.

By efficiently managing your resources and focusing on the top-tier 3-star Disks like Oraora! and Mind Royale, you can successfully navigate the early challenges of Neverness to Everness without needing to spend heavily on the [gacha system](https://ntecodex.com/faq).

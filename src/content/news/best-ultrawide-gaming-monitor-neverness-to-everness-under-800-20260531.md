## What Happened

![What Happened](/img/best-ultrawide-gaming-monitor-neverness-to-everness-under-800-20260531/inline-1.webp)


Neverness to Everness (NTE) launched on April 29, 2026, setting a new graphical benchmark for urban open-world gacha games. Developed by Hotta Studio using Unreal Engine 5, the game presents a dense, supernatural metropolis that relies heavily on peripheral awareness. Players quickly discovered that the standard 16:9 aspect ratio feels restrictive when moving through the verticality of Hesperia and using the high-speed driving mechanics.

The demand for 21:9 and 32:9 displays surged post-launch as players sought to maximize their field of view (FOV) for Esper combat and city exploration. In a city where threats can emerge from alleyways or skyscrapers, the extra horizontal space provided by an ultrawide monitor offers a distinct advantage in tracking Anomaly entities during chaotic encounters.

Initial launch version 1.0.8 faced criticism for "cinematic cropping" on ultrawide displays. This issue caused the top and bottom of the frame to be cut off rather than expanding the sides, leading to a community-driven search for the best hardware and software settings to mitigate visual issues. This has led many players to look for monitors with high pixel density and specific scaling capabilities to handle the current state of the NTE engine.

### The Hesperia Immersion Factor

Hesperia is not just a backdrop; it is a character in itself. The city utilizes Lumen global illumination and Nanite virtualized geometry to create a level of detail rarely seen in the gacha genre. When viewing the city in 21:9, the scale of the "Bridge Crossings" district becomes much more apparent. The wider aspect ratio allows players to see the transition from street-level storefronts to the tops of glowing billboards without constant camera adjustments.

The immersion factor is most noticeable during the game's weather transitions. Seeing a supernatural storm roll across the entire width of an ultrawide panel while your character, such as Nanally, stands on a rooftop provides a sense of scale that 16:9 displays simply cannot replicate.

### Why 16:9 Falls Short in Urban Gachas

In traditional 16:9 setups, the camera in Neverness to Everness often feels "zoomed in," especially during high-speed driving. When taking a Porsche through the tight corners of the city, the limited FOV can make it difficult to anticipate oncoming traffic or upcoming turns. This restriction also affects the combat experience. Many Esper abilities have wide area-of-effect (AoE) ranges that extend beyond the edges of a standard screen, forcing players to rely on off-screen indicators rather than visual cues.

The 21:9 aspect ratio provides approximately 34% more horizontal screen real estate. In the context of NTE, this means seeing more of the environment during Anomaly investigations and having a better view of the Esper Meter and other UI elements without them cluttering the central action.

## Player Reactions

![Player Reactions](/img/best-ultrawide-gaming-monitor-neverness-to-everness-under-800-20260531/inline-3.webp)


The [Reddit community r/NevernessToEverness](https://www.reddit.com) has been vocal about the "fish-eye" effect on certain VA panels when driving at high speeds. Some users report that the extreme curvature of budget monitors can distort the image at the edges, making the city streets look warped. This has led to a preference for monitors with more subtle curves or higher-quality panels that maintain color accuracy at wide angles.

Discord users have shared various "ini file" tweaks to fix the 1.0.8 resolution bugs, though many are waiting for official optimization in the 1.1 update. The community consensus is that while the game is beautiful, the technical hurdles for ultrawide users are currently an obstacle to full enjoyment.

Positive feedback centers on the increased visibility of S-Class characters like Lacrimosa and Chaos during chaotic elemental combat. Players using 21:9 displays have noted that they can better track Chaos's teleportation patterns, as he often moves to the edges of a standard 16:9 frame.

### The 'Cinematic Cropping' Controversy

The "cinematic cropping" issue has been the most discussed topic in hardware circles. Instead of providing a wider view, the game's initial launch version simply cut the top and bottom of the 16:9 image to fit the 21:9 aspect ratio. This resulted in players seeing less of the world than those on standard monitors. Hotta Studio acknowledged this feedback shortly after launch, promising a fix that would implement "Hor+" scaling, which expands the horizontal field of view instead.

### Community Fixes and Workarounds

Until the official patch arrives, players have turned to community-made tools and manual configuration changes. By editing the `Engine.ini` file located in the game's local folders, some users have successfully forced a proper FOV. However, these fixes can sometimes cause the UI to misalign, particularly the Officer Whisker glider icons and the mini-map, which can get pushed too far into the corners or overlap with other menus.

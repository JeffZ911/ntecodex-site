## Key Changes

NTE has introduced significant departures from the traditional gacha model, most notably through its "Scarborough Fair" system. This system replaces the standard "press button, see animation" pull mechanic with an interactive board-game interface.

### Scarborough Fair: The Board Game Gacha

In Scarborough Fair, players use "Solid Dice" or "Fabricated Dice" to move a game piece—the mascot Chuppa—across a board filled with reward tiles. 
- **Solid Dice:** Provide a random movement of 1 to 6 spaces.
- **Fabricated Dice:** Allow players to choose a specific number of spaces to move, providing a layer of strategy when landing on high-value tiles.

Each tile on the board represents a reward, ranging from upgrade materials and A-class character shards to the coveted S-class character tile. This visual representation provides transparency that is often missing in other gacha games, as players can see exactly how far they are from the next major reward.

### Understanding Board Modification (Soft Pity)

The "Board Modification" mechanic serves as the game's soft pity system. Upon reaching 70 pulls without an S-class character, the board undergoes a visual and mechanical transformation. The drop rate for S-class tiles jumps from the base 1.87% to a staggering 19.59%. Furthermore, more S-class tiles are physically added to the board, making it statistically difficult to miss them as Chuppa moves.

### The End of the 50/50: Guarantee Mechanics

The most revolutionary change in NTE is the complete removal of the 50/50 mechanic found in games like Genshin Impact or Honkai: Star Rail. On a limited character banner, if you land on an S-class tile, you are guaranteed to receive the featured character. There is no chance of "losing" to a standard pool character. This change has been a major talking point in the community, as it removes the primary source of frustration for gacha players. Hard pity is set at 90 pulls, meaning that even if a player has the worst possible luck with dice rolls, they will obtain the featured S-class character by their 90th pull.

## Player Reactions

Since the April 29 launch, community sentiment has been largely positive, specifically regarding the monetization and pull mechanics.

### Community Praise for Gacha Transparency

The removal of the 50/50 system has been hailed as a landmark move for the genre. On platforms like Reddit and the official Discord, players have expressed that the 100% guarantee makes spending money—or even just saving free currency—feel much more rewarding. The "Board Modification" visual effect, where the board glitches and glows gold, has also been praised for its aesthetic flair and the excitement it generates.

### Nanally Meta Performance Feedback

In terms of gameplay, Nanally is currently viewed as a top-tier DPS. Her Plasma element interacts favorably with most early-game enemies, and her electromagnetic adhesion is being used in creative ways for "vertical exploration." Players have discovered they can skip certain platforming puzzles by using her skills to stick to walls and bypass obstacles. Early meta-analysis from high-level players suggests that her signature Arc, "Ready-Ready," is a significant power spike, leading many to recommend pulling for the weapon if Nanally is your primary damage dealer.

### The 'Slow Roll' Debate: Board Game vs. Instant Pulls

Not all feedback is purely positive. A vocal segment of the player base finds the board-game animation to be slower than the traditional "10-pull" skip buttons found in other games. While the "Fabricated Dice" add strategy, the time it takes for Chuppa to walk across the tiles can add up during long sessions of mass pulling. Hotta Studio has acknowledged this feedback, stating they are looking into a "Turbo Mode" for the Scarborough Fair board in a future update.

**Pros and Cons of the Scarborough Fair System**

*   **Pros:**
    *   No 50/50 chance; 100% guarantee on featured S-class.
    *   High base S-class rate (1.87%) compared to industry standards.
    *   Visual transparency of rewards on the board.
    *   Strategic use of Fabricated Dice to target specific tiles.
*   **Cons:**
    *   Pulling process is slower than traditional menus.
    *   Learning curve for understanding tile types and board movement.
    *   Fabricated Dice are rarer than Solid Dice, requiring careful management.

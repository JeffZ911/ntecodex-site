## What Happened

Neverness to Everness (NTE) successfully launched on April 29, 2026, introducing players to the urban supernatural world of Hethereau. The game immediately distinguished itself from other open-world titles by blending high-speed vehicular traversal with a complex "Esper Cycle" combat system. Within the first 48 hours, the title reportedly generated over $14 million in revenue across PC, PlayStation 5, and mobile platforms, signaling a massive commercial success for Hotta Studio and Perfect World Games.

The Version 1.0 cycle featured [Nanally](/characters/nanally-guide-nte) and [Hotori](/characters/hotori-best-team-nte) as the debut S-Rank limited characters. Nanally, an Anima-faction unit using the Plasma element, headlined the launch window with her "Colucci Howling Technique," which allowed players to walk on vertical surfaces and ceilings. This was followed by Hotori, the owner of the Eibon Antique Shop, whose "Misty Tipsy Style" banner introduced time-stop mechanics that functioned both in high-intensity combat and during open-world city exploration.

### The Road from Launch to 1.1

The transition from the launch version to the first major update has been defined by rapid community growth and technical hotfixes. While the 1.0 experience was praised for its atmosphere and "GTA-meets-Anime" vibe, early adopters noted performance hurdles on mobile devices and some inconsistencies with the Unreal Engine 5 Lumen reflections. Hotta Studio has used the weeks following the April 29 launch to address these concerns while simultaneously teasing the next chapter of the Hethereau story.

Official social media teasers have shifted focus toward the Bureau of Anomaly Control’s ETD-4 and 6th Platoon members. This "drip marketing" campaign confirmed that the characters seen in the game's initial trailers—specifically those involved in the investigation of the "Black Tome" anomaly—would be the centerpieces of the 1.1 update.

### Drip Marketing: Lacrimosa and Chaos Confirmed

Hotta Studio began 'drip marketing' for Version 1.1 shortly after launch, confirming the arrival of fan-favorite characters from the prologue. Lacrimosa, who many players controlled briefly during the opening tutorial, was officially revealed as the Phase 1 character for 1.1. Her reveal was met with significant enthusiasm, particularly due to her quirky personality and her affinity for "Red Jelly" (often referred to by fans as the Tomato Jelly mission). 

Shortly after Lacrimosa's announcement, the studio revealed Chaos, an S-Rank Lakshana-element unit. This caused a stir in the community due to the potential confusion between the character's name and the existing "Chaos" elemental damage type. Chaos is a member of the ETD 6th Platoon and is expected to lead the narrative expansion into the Miguel District.

## Key Changes

The NTE 1.1 patch release date is expected to be June 3, 2026, following the conclusion of Hotori's banner. This update marks the first time the playable map will expand beyond the initial launch districts, introducing two massive new zones: the Miguel District and the New Herland District.

### New Characters: Lacrimosa and Chaos

The primary draw of the 1.1 update is the introduction of two heavy-hitting S-Rank characters. Lacrimosa, a Chaos-element DPS from the ETD-4 division, utilizes a unique weapon-swapping mechanic. She alternates between her "Frying Pan" (Tomato Metal) and "Red Jelly" (Tomato Percussion) modules to stack the "Nightmare" debuff on enemies. This debuff deals continuous Chaos damage over time (DoT) and can be detonated for massive burst damage.

Chaos, arriving in Phase 2, is a Lakshana-element unit from the ETD 6th Platoon. Unlike the more eccentric Lacrimosa, Chaos is described as a calm and intimidating operative. While his full kit details remain [information unavailable], early leaks suggest he specializes in high-stagger attacks and crowd control, making him a potential staple for clearing high-level Anomaly Commissions.

### Hethereau Map Expansion: Miguel and Herland Districts

The 1.1 update significantly increases the vertical and horizontal scale of Hethereau. The Miguel District is a lakeside area surrounded by mountains, offering a blend of traditional architecture and modern skyscrapers. Players can explore the Shuiling Lake region and the iconic Skytower, which serves as a new landmark for high-altitude gliding.

The New Herland District, by contrast, is the gleaming financial core of the city. It features the densest concentration of corporate towers in the game, including the Wetheimer Tower, which players must activate to clear the fog of war in the region. This district is home to the most lucrative Anomaly Hunter contracts and high-end luxury properties.

### Quality of Life and Technical Optimizations

Version 1.1 brings the game's engine up to Unreal Engine 5.5 standards, introducing several technical upgrades:
*   **UE5 Lumen Enhancements:** Improved dynamic lighting and reflections on glass-fronted skyscrapers in the New Herland District. This addresses community feedback regarding "low poly" reflections seen at launch.
*   **Model Rendering:** More detailed hair meshes and improved facial/eye shading for all characters.
*   **Driving Physics:** A complete overhaul of vehicular handling to reduce the "floaty" feel reported during the 1.0 cycle.
*   **First-Person Perspective:** The addition of a toggleable first-person mode for companion interactions, dates, and exploring purchasable apartments.

## Player Reactions

The community response to the 1.1 announcements has been a mix of intense hype and strategic skepticism. The "Lacrimosa Hype Train" is in full swing, largely driven by the character's prominent role in the game's marketing and her unique "Nightmare" stacking mechanics.

### The Lacrimosa Hype Train

Massive community hype for Lacrimosa stems from her role in the "Tomato Jelly" mission and her appearance in the early combat tutorials. On platforms like Reddit and Discord, players have already begun theory-crafting teams. One popular strategy involves pairing Lacrimosa with "Bang," as leaks suggest a secret synergy where swapping to Bang mid-rotation triggers an immediate follow-up attack.

### The 'Chaos' Naming Controversy

There is ongoing confusion regarding the naming convention of the character 'Chaos' vs. the 'Chaos' elemental damage type. Players have pointed out that having a character named Chaos who belongs to the Lakshana element is counter-intuitive for new players. Some community members have jokingly suggested that the character should be renamed to avoid "elemental identity theft," while others argue it fits the game's "supernatural anomaly" theme where names rarely match reality.

### Monetization and the Porsche Collaboration

Mixed feelings have emerged regarding the "aggressive monetization" mentioned in recent investor talks. Specifically, the high-end Porsche vehicle collaboration—featuring the Porsche 918 Spyder and 911 Turbo S—is expected to be locked behind premium currency or high-cost gacha boards. While some players are excited to drive licensed luxury cars through Hethereau, F2P (Free-to-Play) users are concerned that the most desirable "Lifestyle" features are becoming increasingly pay-walled.

## What Happened

The global launch of Neverness to Everness (NTE) on April 29, 2026, has fundamentally changed the daily routine of gacha gamers. Unlike traditional mobile titles designed for short bursts, NTE’s "Urban RPG" framework encourages massive time investments. Players are currently spending unprecedented hours in the city of Hethereau, balancing high-speed [Porsche 918 Spyder](https://www.ign.com/articles/neverness-to-everness-is-a-new-open-world-supernatural-rpg-from-the-developers-of-tower-of-fantasy) racing with the complex City Tycoon property management system.

This shift toward long-form gameplay has led to a surge in "grinder fatigue." Community hubs like r/NevernesstoEverness and the official Discord have reported a 40% increase in threads regarding the "NTE Hunch"—a specific type of lower back strain caused by leaning forward during intense Anomaly commissions. The unique "board game" gacha mechanic, while innovative, requires more continuous interaction than a standard "single-click" pull system, keeping players tethered to their desks for longer durations.

Health experts within the gaming community have specifically flagged the "Appraiser's Slump." This posture issue arises from the hybrid nature of the game: players switch between the high-intensity combat required to defeat Anomalies and the relaxed, lean-back nature of urban exploration and business management.

### The Rise of "Hethereau Hunch"

The "Hethereau Hunch" is more than just a meme; it is a physical manifestation of the game's demanding "Appraiser" role. When players engage with characters like Nanally in high-stakes combat, the tendency is to "turtle"—pulling the chin toward the screen and rounding the shoulders. Over a three-hour session of farming Annuliths, this leads to significant tension in the trapezius and rhomboid muscles. 

The complexity of the City Tycoon mode exacerbates this. Managing properties requires navigating dense menus and spreadsheets, often leading to "static loading," where the muscles stay contracted in one position for hours. Without a chair that promotes micro-movements, players find themselves stiff and sore by the time they finish their daily commissions.

### Why NTE Grinding is Harder on Your Back Than Other Gachas

Traditional gacha games often allow for "auto-battle" or "skip" functions that let players step away from their hardware. NTE's core loop is built on manual exploration and physics-based interactions. Whether you are drifting through Hethereau in a licensed Porsche or investigating supernatural phenomena, the game demands active input. 

Furthermore, the "board game" gacha system requires players to navigate a physicalized reward space. This isn't just clicking a button; it’s a mini-game that keeps the player engaged for several minutes per multi-pull. When you multiply this by the frequency of pulls during a limited banner like "The Ichi-daime," the cumulative time spent in a potentially poor posture adds up faster than in any other title in the genre.
